## What AI is (in normal words)

AI is a tool that can:
- understand your text
- generate text
- summarize
- organize
- suggest options
- create templates
- help you think through decisions

AI is *not*:
- guaranteed correct
- a replacement for food safety
- a replacement for your real judgment
- a replacement for testing in real life

Think of AI like a fast assistant.

It can help you:
- get started
- structure your work
- create drafts
- improve clarity
- reduce time spent on planning

But you still decide what’s true and what’s best.

## Step 1 — Create your AI “Business Brief” (your permanent prompt)

This is the most powerful thing you can do.

A Business Brief is a short text you paste into AI at the start of a session.
It helps AI give you answers that match your real life.

### Your Business Brief (copy/paste template)

> **My business:** I run a home kitchen selling local pickup orders.  
> **My weekly model:** I do pickup on [DAY] in the window [TIME].  
> **My max capacity:** [X] orders or [X] portions per week.  
> **My signature dish:** [DISH].  
> **My banker dish (easy, stable):** [DISH].  
> **My add-ons:** [ADD-ONS].  
> **My constraints:** I have [HOURS] total time available per week. I have [EQUIPMENT LIMITS].  
> **My goal right now:** [stable income / reduce stress / more repeat orders / improve quality].  
> **My tone:** warm, simple, professional, not salesy.  
> **Important:** give me simple steps, checklists, and copy/paste templates. Avoid long theory.

Save this in one note. Reuse it every week.

## Step 2 — Build your “Weekly AI Session” (15 minutes)

This is how you create stability.
A small weekly session creates a big long-term effect.

### Weekly AI Session (15 minutes)

1) Paste your Business Brief
2) Answer these 6 questions (short):
   - What day is pickup this week?
   - What is max orders?
   - What are the 2–3 menu items?
   - Any constraints this week (time, money, events)?
   - What went wrong last week (one sentence)?
   - What is one improvement you want this week?

3) Ask AI for:
   - a weekly plan
   - a shopping list
   - a prep timeline
   - a packing checklist
   - customer messages

This is your system.

## Step 4 — Shopping list + backup planning with AI

Shopping stress is a major energy drain.

### Prompt: Shopping SOP builder (copy/paste)

> Build a shopping list for this week’s menu.  
> Organize by categories (proteins, veg, dry goods, spices, packaging).

## Common AI mistakes (and quick fixes)

1) **Believing AI without checking**
Fix: AI drafts. You verify.

2) **Asking vague questions**
Fix: give constraints (time, capacity, menu).

3) **Using AI to sound like a robot**
Fix: always include “tone: warm, simple, human”.

4) **Trying to use AI for everything**
Fix: start with 2–3 use cases.

5) **Copy/paste without adapting**
Fix: edit scripts to match your real voice.

6) **No loop**
Fix: after pickup day, write one note and improve next week.

## The 5 prompt patterns that make AI useful (every time)

Most people get weak results because their prompts are too vague.

Use these patterns and your outputs become much better.

### Pattern 1 — “Act as” + “Goal” + “Constraints”
Example:
> Act as a practical small-business coach for home kitchens.  
> My goal: stable income without stress.  
> Constraints: 6 hours prep + 5 hours cook/pack, max 25 orders.  
> Give me a simple plan in bullets.

### Pattern 2 — “Ask me only the minimum”
AI can overwhelm you with questions.
Tell it to stay minimal.

Example:
> Ask me only the minimum inputs you need.  
> Then produce a short answer.

### Pattern 3 — “Give me 3 options”
This prevents “one perfect answer” thinking.

Example:
> Give me 3 options: safe, normal, stretch.  
> Then recommend one.

### Pattern 4 — “Output format”
If you want checklists, say so.

Example:
> Output as a printable checklist with checkboxes.

### Pattern 5 — “My tone”
This keeps the writing human.

Example:
> Tone: warm, simple, confident. No hype. No corporate language.

These five patterns will upgrade your AI results instantly.

## Data and privacy (small business friendly)

This section is important, but we keep it calm.

### The simple rule
**Do not paste sensitive personal customer data into AI.**

Avoid:
- full names + addresses together
- phone numbers
- personal stories or health details
- payment details

Instead, use:
- “Customer A”
- “Order #12”
- “a customer said…”

### What is safe to share
- your menu items
- general complaints (without identity)
- your own processes
- your own numbers in summary form

### Why this matters
Trust is the foundation of food business.
Protect it.

## When AI gives you a bad answer (how to fix it in 10 seconds)

Sometimes AI will respond with:
- too much text
- too many options
- generic advice
- unrealistic schedules
- “corporate” language

Here is how you fix it fast.

### Fix 1 — “Make it shorter”
> Make it 50% shorter. Only include what I must do this week.

### Fix 2 — “Use my constraints”
> Use my real constraints: [time], [capacity], [equipment]. Remove anything unrealistic.

### Fix 3 — “More practical, less theory”
> Remove theory. Give me a checklist and a timeline.

### Fix 4 — “Warm, human tone”
> Rewrite in a warm, simple tone. No hype. No corporate language.

### Fix 5 — “Ask me one question”
> Ask me one question to improve your plan, then update the plan.

These five fixes make AI feel like a real assistant.
