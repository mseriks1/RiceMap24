## Big idea (positive and powerful)

People don’t order from a home kitchen because they saw one photo.

1) **Hungry**  
2) **Safe**

Trust is what turns “looks good” into “I will order”.

The good news:

- more first-time orders

- your kitchen becomes a local (or global) name

## The 7 strongest trust signals (use these)

### Signal 1: Clean visuals (background + tools)
A clean counter and tidy tools instantly increase trust.
Messy background reduces trust.

### Signal 2: Portion proof (value + honesty)
Show a box photo sometimes.
Customers feel safe because they see what they get.

### Signal 3: Packing proof (professionalism)
Boxes lined up neatly = “this is real business”.

### Signal 4: Consistency (same style, repeated)
Consistency builds confidence.
If you look stable, you feel safe.

### Signal 5: Human vibe (optional but powerful)
A smile holding the dish.
Or hands cooking.
Humans build trust.

### Signal 6: Clear ordering system (DM ORDER)
When ordering is easy, customers feel safe.
Confusion creates doubt.

### Signal 7: Reviews / customer proof
A screenshot of “so good!” can convert strangers.

If you focus on these 7, your trust will grow fast.

## Hygiene and safety cues (simple, not scary)

You don’t need to talk about “food safety” like a textbook.
You just need to show small clean cues:

- washed hands (1 second clip)
- clean apron (optional)
- hair tied back (optional)
- clean cutting board
- fresh ingredients

These cues make customers comfortable.

You don’t need to show your full kitchen.
Close-up shots with clean surfaces are enough.

## How to stand out from generic take-away (signature identity)

Trust is about safety.
Brand is about memory.

To stand out, you need:
- **Signature dish** (your tradition, your “flag”)
- **Banker dish** (easy first-time choice)
- A simple story line

### Your simple story line (one sentence)
Use a sentence you can repeat everywhere:
- “Homemade [Cuisine] food, cooked like we cook for family.”
- “Small-batch home kitchen in [Area].”
- “Taste of home, made fresh.”

One sentence is enough.
Repeat builds brand.

## Messaging brand (tone = trust)

Your messages should feel:
- friendly
- short
- clear

Avoid long explanations.

### 3-message order flow (copy/paste)
1) “Thanks! 😊 Which dish and how many portions?”  
2) “Pickup area: [Area]. Pickup time: [Day + time window]. OK?”  
3) “Confirmed ✅ Thank you! ❤️”

Fast, calm, professional.

## Reviews and proof (how to get it)

Reviews are trust rockets.

Ask when:
- customer says “so good”

### Review ask (copy/paste)
“Thank you ❤️ If you have 10 seconds, a 1-line review helps a lot 😊”

You can post:
- screenshot (hide name if needed)

Proof helps strangers trust you.

## The “familiarity effect” (why trust grows with repetition)

Humans trust what feels familiar.
This is why brands repeat the same message.

When customers see you repeatedly:
- same style photos
- same kitchen name
- same “DM ORDER” line
- same signature dish

They feel:
“I know this kitchen.”

That feeling creates orders.

So you don’t need new ideas every day.
You need repetition with small variations.
