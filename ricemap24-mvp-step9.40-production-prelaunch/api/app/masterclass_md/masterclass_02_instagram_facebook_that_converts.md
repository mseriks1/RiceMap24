## Why Instagram & Facebook are powerful for RiceMap24 kitchens

TikTok is discovery.  
Instagram and Facebook are often **conversion**.

That means:
- People see your content
- Then they click your profile
- Then they decide: “Do I trust this kitchen?”
- Then they DM you “ORDER”

Facebook also has a huge advantage:
**local groups** (neighborhood groups, community groups, apartment groups).
These groups are full of people who actually live near you and buy local.

Instagram is strong for:
- beautiful photos
- stories (daily “mini reminders”)
- Reels (reuse your TikTok)

Facebook is strong for:
- local groups
- older audiences with money
- community trust
- simple sharing

You can use both without doing double work.

## The Instagram profile checklist (your storefront)

Most customers will check your profile before ordering.
So your profile must answer 4 questions fast:

1) **What do you sell?**
2) **Where are you located?**
3) **How do I order?**
4) **Can I trust this?**

### Profile photo
Use a clear photo:
- your best dish close-up OR
- you smiling holding the dish

### Name
Keep it simple:
- “[Kitchen Name] • Homemade [Cuisine]”

### Bio (copy/paste template)
Use one of these:

**Bio A (simple):**
Local home kitchen in **[Area/City]**  
Homemade **[Cuisine]** food  
DM **ORDER** to reserve ✅

**Bio B (trust + value):**
Fresh homemade **[Cuisine]** in **[Area]**  
Small batches • clean packing  
DM **ORDER** ✅

### Link
If you have a RiceMap24 listing link, use it.
If not, DM is enough.

### Highlights (optional, but powerful)
Make 3 highlights:
- **Menu**
- **Pickup**
- **Reviews**

Even if you have only 1 story inside each, it looks professional.

## Facebook Groups (local goldmine for home kitchens)

This is one of the best growth tools for your business.

Why groups work:
- people are local (near you)
- people trust community posts
- people actively look for recommendations

### Group rules (very important)
- Always check group rules first.
- Some groups allow selling posts only on certain days.
- Be respectful. Don’t spam.

### How to post in a group (the friendly way)
Your post should feel helpful, not pushy.

**Best group post structure:**
1) Friendly hello
2) One photo
3) One sentence: what you sell + where you are
4) One sentence: how to order (DM “ORDER”)

**Group post template (copy/paste):**
Hi everyone 😊  
I’m a local home kitchen in **[Area]** (RiceMap24).  
This week I’m cooking **[Dish]**.  
If anyone wants a portion, just DM me **ORDER** ❤️

### The group post timing (simple)
- Post once
- Then comment once later (“thanks for the support” / “still available”)
Do not post every day.

### Pro tip: ask a question
Questions bring comments → more reach.
Example:
“Do you like mild or spicy?”

## The DM system (make ordering smooth)

Instagram and Facebook convert best when your DM replies are fast and friendly.

### The 3-message DM flow (copy/paste)
1) “Thanks! 😊 Which dish and how many portions?”  
2) “Pickup area: [Area]. Pickup time: [Day + time window]. OK?”  
3) “Confirmed ✅ Thank you! ❤️ I’ll message you when it’s ready.”

### How to respond when people ask price
“DM ‘ORDER’ 😊 I’ll send menu + price.”

### How to respond when people ask delivery
If you don’t deliver:
“Pickup in [Area] 😊 DM ‘ORDER’ and I’ll share the details.”

Keep it calm. Keep it short.

## How to get repeat orders (without being pushy)

Repeat orders are easier than new orders.
People already trust you.

### Simple repeat system (friendly)
1) After pickup: “Thank you ❤️ Hope you enjoy!”
2) A week later: “We’re cooking again on [Day]. Want me to reserve?”

That is enough.

### VIP feeling (optional)
Send to your best customers:
“VIP heads-up ❤️ We’re cooking again on [Day]. Want early reservation?”

People love feeling special.

## Photography basics (make your photos look premium)

You don’t need a fancy camera.

### Light
- window light is best
- turn off harsh yellow light if possible

### Background
- clean table
- one plate/board
- no mess in background

### Angles
- close-up for texture
- top-down for boxes
- 45-degree for bowls

### The “wipe lens” trick
Always wipe your phone lens before shooting.
This alone makes photos look sharper.

## Extra (very useful): Facebook group strategy that feels natural (not salesy)

Many people are afraid to post in groups. They think:
- “People will think I’m spamming.”
- “What if someone is rude?”

You can avoid that by using a friendly, community style.

### The “community-first” rule
Your post should feel like:
- “Hi neighbors, I cook at home and share food”
Not:
- “Buy now!!!”

### The best group post styles (choose one)

**Style A — Simple announcement**
- one photo
- one dish
- DM ORDER

**Style B — Ask a friendly question**
Example:
“Do you like mild or spicy?”
This invites conversation.

**Style C — Give a small value**
Example:
“Here is a simple tip: always reheat dumplings with steam for best texture.”
Then show your dumplings photo and say DM ORDER.

This style feels helpful and people respect it.

### What to do if someone comments something negative
Stay calm and kind.
A good reply:
“Thanks for your comment 😊 We are a small home kitchen. If it’s not for you, no worries. Have a great day!”

Never argue. Your calmness makes you look professional.

### One group is enough
You do not need 20 groups.
One good group can bring many customers over time.
