## The customer experience funnel: DM → Confirm → Pickup → Enjoy → Repeat

A smooth experience has 5 steps:

1) DM ORDER  
2) Confirm dish + portions  
3) Confirm pickup/delivery details  
4) Hand over food smoothly  
5) Follow up (thank you)

If any step is messy, repeat orders drop.
If all steps are smooth, repeat orders grow.

---

## Part 1 — Messaging (make ordering feel easy)

Messaging is the first customer experience.
If messaging is confusing, customers lose confidence.

### Your goal
Make ordering feel like:
- “3 messages and done.”

### The 3-message order flow (copy/paste)
1) “Thanks! 😊 Which dish and how many portions?”  
2) “Pickup area: [Area]. Pickup time: [Day + time window]. OK?”  
3) “Confirmed ✅ Thank you! ❤️ I’ll message when it’s ready.”

This flow works worldwide and is friendly.

---

## Fast replies (trust signal)

Fast replies build trust.
Customers think:
- “They are reliable.”
- “My order will be handled.”

Use templates so you can reply quickly even when busy.

### Saved replies you should have
- Price question reply
- Location/pickup reply
- Delivery reply
- Sold out reply
- Allergy/ingredient reply

## Scripts for common questions (copy/paste)

### Price?
“DM ORDER 😊 I’ll send the menu + price.”

### Location?
“We’re in [Area]. DM ORDER and I’ll share pickup details 😊”

### Delivery?
“Pickup in [Area] 😊 (Delivery is [yes/no] depending on the day.)”

Short, honest, calm.

## Part 2 — Pickup flow (make it smooth)

Pickup is where trust becomes real.

A smooth pickup makes customers feel:
- safe
- respected
- confident

### The 4 rules of smooth pickup
1) **Clear time window** (example: 17:00–19:00)  
2) **Clear meeting point** (door, lobby, outside)  
3) **Short waiting time** (be ready)  
4) **Friendly handover** (smile)

If pickup is messy, customers hesitate next time.
If pickup is smooth, customers reorder easily.

---

## Pickup messages that reduce confusion

### Before pickup (same day)

Message me when you are outside ❤️”

### When food is ready
“Ready ✅ See you soon 😊”

### If they are late (calm)
“No worries 😊 Just tell me when you’re on the way.”

Clear messages reduce stress for both sides.

## Delivery flow (optional, keep it realistic)

Delivery can be great, but only if you can do it calmly.

If you deliver:
- use one delivery window
- deliver only in a small area
- charge a delivery fee (time + fuel)

If delivery creates stress, start with pickup only.
Pickup-only kitchens can still grow strong.

## Part 3 — Packaging (trust in the customer’s hands)

Packaging is the first physical impression.
It must look:
- clean
- organized
- safe
- leak-proof (if possible)

### The 5 packaging rules
1) Use consistent container types  
2) Wipe edges (clean look)  
3) Separate sauces (cups)  
4) Label spicy level (optional)  
5) Pack like a professional (neat)

Packaging that looks good makes customers proud.
Proud customers share photos.

## “Less back-and-forth” DM technique (super useful)

If you want fewer long chats, use “two-choice questions”.

Instead of:
“What do you want?”
Ask:
“Do you want **A or B**?”

Examples:
- “1 or 2 portions?”
- “Mild or spicy?”
- “Pickup 17–18 or 18–19?”

Two choices help customers decide faster.
Faster decisions = more orders.
