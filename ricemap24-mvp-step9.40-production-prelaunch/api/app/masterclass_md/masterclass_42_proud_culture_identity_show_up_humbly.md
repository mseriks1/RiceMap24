# Part 4 — Your story as a business asset (without oversharing)

You don’t need to share everything.
You need to share the right 10%.

Think of your story in 3 layers:

## Layer 1 — Simple origin (1 sentence)
“I’m from [country/region], and I’ve always loved cooking.”

## Layer 2 — Meaning (2–3 sentences)
“These dishes remind me of home.  
They’re the food my family made for gatherings and weekends.”

## Layer 3 — Now (1 sentence)
“Now I cook them here in [city] and share them with local customers.”

That’s a full story.
Simple, respectful, powerful.

### The rule
Share what builds trust and connection.
Keep private details private.

# Part 6 — How to show up on camera (without feeling exposed)

You don’t have to become an influencer.
You can show up lightly.

### The 4 levels of showing up
1) **Hands only** (food + voiceover optional)
2) **Voice only** (no face, just your voice)
3) **Partial** (face briefly, simple hello)
4) **Full** (talking to camera)

Start at Level 1 or 2.
Move slowly.

The goal is not to “perform.”
The goal is to be present.

### The easiest on-camera script (10 seconds)
“Hi 😊 I’m [Name].  
I’m cooking [dish] from [country/region] for this weekend pickup.  
Menu link in RiceMap24.”

That’s it.

# Part 17 — Cultural translation (how to share culture so locals feel invited)

Sometimes people hesitate because they fear:
- “people won’t understand”
- “people will think it’s too different”
- “locals won’t like it”

The solution is cultural translation:
You keep authenticity, but you explain gently.

### The bridge method (one sentence)
- “This is a dish from [country] — it’s like [local reference], but with [your twist].”

Examples:
- “It’s like a cozy stew bowl, but with ginger-garlic and rich soy comfort.”
- “It’s like a sharing tray for family weekends — mild base, spice optional.”

This makes your culture accessible without watering it down.

# Part 19 — Handling negativity (calm strategies)

If you show up, you might get:
- weird comments
- insensitive jokes
- negativity

This is unpleasant, but you can handle it calmly.

### The 3-level response system
1) Ignore (most small negativity)  
2) Short boundary (if needed)  
3) Remove/block (if disrespectful)

Boundary line examples:
- “We keep this space respectful. Thanks.”
- “We focus on food and community. Have a good day.”

Don’t debate your identity online.
Protect your peace.
Your energy is a business resource.
