## The simple local growth funnel: See → Scan/DM → Order → Repeat

Local growth is simple:

1) People SEE you (flyer, poster, group post, partner counter)  
2) They SCAN or DM (QR or “DM ORDER”)  
3) They ORDER (first order)  
4) They REPEAT (VIP + follow-up)

Local growth works best when you keep the action simple:
**DM “ORDER”** is perfect.

## The 3 local growth channels (the big 3)

### Channel 1: Partner spots (borrow an audience)
Partner spots are places where your customers already go:
- Asian grocery store
- hair salon / barbershop
- gym / sports club
- café / small shop
- community center

Why it works:
- you borrow their foot traffic
- people trust the location
- it feels natural (“local community”)

### Channel 2: Flyers / noticeboards / QR cards (offline visibility)
This is classic marketing and it still works:
- apartment noticeboards
- condo boards
- community boards
- local shops
- gyms

Why it works:
- people see it repeatedly
- it reaches people not active on social media
- it feels real and local

### Channel 3: Local groups (Facebook + WhatsApp)
Local groups are a goldmine:
- neighborhood FB groups
- apartment groups
- community WhatsApp chats
- school parent groups (if allowed)

Why it works:
- people are local
- people buy from recommendations
- one good post can bring many orders

## Channel 1 — Partner spots (step-by-step)

### Step 1: Choose 1 partner type (keep it simple)
Start with one:
- Asian grocery store
- salon
- gym

Pick the one closest to your customers.

### Step 2: What to offer the partner (make it easy)
Partners don’t want extra work.
Offer something simple:
- “I will bring a small QR card / flyer”
- “No work for you”
- “Just place it near the counter”
- “I will pick it up in 7 days”

### Step 3: Partner message (copy/paste)
Hi! 😊 I run a small local home kitchen in **[Area]** (RiceMap24).  
Can I place a small QR card / mini flyer near your counter for 7 days?  
It helps local families find homemade food, and it’s no work for you.  
Thank you so much ❤️

### Step 4: If they say yes (great!)
Place:
- one small counter card (A5 or smaller)
- one QR card

Then do one story on your own account:
“Now available near [Partner] ❤️ DM ORDER”

### Step 5: If they say no
No problem. Smile and leave.
Try the next place.
Local growth is a numbers game: 5 asks → 1–2 yes.

## Channel 2 — Flyers & noticeboards (step-by-step)

This is the easiest “offline campaign”.

### Step 1: Choose 2 places
Examples:
- your apartment board
- a gym board
- a community board

### Step 2: Use a clean flyer message (short)
A good flyer needs:
- kitchen name
- cuisine type (optional)
- area
- DM ORDER
- QR to listing (optional)

### Flyer text (copy/paste)
HOMEMADE FOOD (LOCAL)  
[Kitchen Name] — [Cuisine]  
Pickup: [Area]  
Order: DM “ORDER”  
RiceMap24 (scan QR)

### Step 3: One flyer is enough
Don’t place flyers everywhere.
Do 1–2 good places and keep it clean.

### Step 4: Combine with online
After you place the flyer, post a story:
“Local pickup in [Area] — DM ORDER”

This makes offline + online work together.

---

## Channel 3 — Local groups (Facebook/WhatsApp) (step-by-step)

Groups can bring customers fast if you do it respectfully.

### Step 1: Pick 1–2 groups only
Choose groups where:
- people are active
- rules allow posts
- audience is local

### Step 2: Follow rules
Some groups allow selling posts only on certain days.
Respect that.
It builds trust.

### Step 3: Your best group post structure
- friendly hello
- one photo
- one sentence: local home kitchen in [Area]
- one sentence: dish + DM ORDER

### Group post template (copy/paste)
Hi everyone 😊  
I’m a local home kitchen in **[Area]** (RiceMap24).  
This week I’m cooking **[Dish]**.  
If anyone wants a portion, just DM me **ORDER** ❤️

### Step 4: Add one question (optional)
A question invites comments → more reach.
Example:
“Do you like mild or spicy?”

### Step 5: One reminder only
Do not spam.
You can do one reminder comment:
“Thank you for the support ❤️ still available today.”

## Partner strategy (deeper): how to choose the best partners

Not all partners are equal.
Choose partners where your customers already are.

### Best partner categories for home kitchens
1) **Asian grocery stores** (perfect match)
2) **Hair salons** (people wait and talk)
3) **Gyms / sports clubs** (hungry customers, routine)
4) **Nail salons** (community, sharing)
5) **Community centers** (local trust)

### Partner “fit” test (quick)
Ask:
- Do people here buy food-related things?
- Are people here my target (families, workers, locals)?
- Is this place near my pickup area?
- Is there space for a small card?

If yes, it’s a good partner.

---

## How to make partners say YES (practical)

Partners say no when they fear:
- extra work
- messy flyers
- too much selling

So you must make it easy and respectful.

### The 5-part YES formula
1) “I’m local”
2) “No work for you”
3) “Small, clean card”
4) “Only for 7 days”
5) “I will remove it”

This feels safe for them.

### What to bring
- 1 small counter card
- 3–5 small QR cards
- tape or stand (clean)
- a smile 😊

### Bonus: offer something nice (optional)
You can say:
“I can also share your shop in my story.”
That makes it win-win.

---

## Flyer strategy (deeper): what makes a flyer work

A flyer works when:
- it is simple
- it has a clear next step
- it looks clean and trustworthy

### Flyer “design” rules (no designer needed)
- big space (not crowded)
- one main photo (optional)
- very short text
- clear “DM ORDER”
- QR only if it helps

If the flyer looks messy, people ignore it.
Clean = premium.

### Where flyers work best
- apartment boards
- laundry rooms
- elevators (if allowed)
- gyms
- community boards
- partner stores

Choose 1–2 places and do it well.

---

## Group strategy (deeper): how to be loved in groups

The secret to groups is: **be human, not salesy**.

### The “3 posts per month” maximum rule
For one group, a good rhythm is:
- 1 helpful post or campaign post
- 1 comment update
- 1 thank you (optional)

That’s enough.

### The best group content types
- “We’re a local home kitchen in [Area]”
- “Here’s what we’re cooking”
- “Here’s a small tip (reheat dumplings with steam)”
- “Mild or spicy?”

Groups love friendly questions and real photos.

## Troubleshooting (quick fixes)

### “I feel shy to ask partners”
Start with the smallest ask:
“Can I place one small QR card for 7 days?”

Most people say yes more than you think.

### “My flyer doesn’t get results”
Fix:
- make it shorter
- add a better photo
- place it in a better location (higher traffic)
- add DM ORDER bigger

### “People in groups complain about selling”
Fix:
- follow rules
- post less
- be more helpful and friendly

### “I get DMs but people don’t order”
Fix:
- reply faster
- send 1–2 clear options
- confirm pickup details quickly
