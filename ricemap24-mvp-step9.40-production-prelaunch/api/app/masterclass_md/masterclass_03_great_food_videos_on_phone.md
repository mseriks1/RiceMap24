## The 5-step video formula (the RiceMap24 way)

This is the exact formula you can use every week.

### Step 1: Hook (0–2 sec)
Start with the best moment:
- final dish close-up
- sauce pour
- steam
- crunch
- sizzle

### Step 2: Satisfy (2–12 sec)
Show 2–4 satisfying moments:
- stir
- flip
- chop
- noodle lift
- dumpling steam lid open

### Step 3: Trust (12–18 sec)
Show clean signals:
- clean counter
- clean hands
- packing box
- portion in box

### Step 4: Reward (18–25 sec)
Show final dish again:
- close-up
- slow pan
- garnish

### Step 5: Action (last 2 sec)
Make ordering easy:
- on-screen text: DM “ORDER”
- caption ends with DM “ORDER”

If you do this formula, you always have a selling video.

## What the algorithm “wants” from your video (simple and useful)

Platforms push videos that people watch longer.

They look at:
- watch time
- completion rate (did people watch to the end?)
- replays (did people loop it?)
- shares and saves
- comments

That’s why food videos work:
- they are satisfying
- they create loops naturally
- they make people rewatch sauce pours and crunch

Your goal is not “perfect editing”.
Your goal is making people watch.

Food loops are powerful.

## The “watch time” tricks for food videos (very effective)

If you want more reach, focus on watch time.

Here are easy watch-time tricks:

### 1) Cut faster than you feel comfortable
A food video should move.
If a clip feels “a little too long”, cut it shorter.

### 2) Repeat the best moment
Show the sauce pour or crunch twice:
- once at the start (hook)
- once near the end (reward)

### 3) Use “micro suspense”
Simple suspense lines:
- “Wait for it”
- “Watch the sauce”
- “Final reveal”
(Only 1–2 words, not a paragraph.)

### 4) Keep the ending strong
A strong ending increases completion rate.
End with your best close-up again.

### 5) Use a simple rhythm
Example: 1 sec + 1 sec + 2 sec + 1 sec + 2 sec…
A rhythm keeps people watching.
