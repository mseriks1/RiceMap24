# Business Coach #20-A — Category Drift Over Time (Packaging, Ingredients, Transport) And How To Fix It
## How to spot slow profit leaks early—and protect net without sacrificing quality

Most profit problems don’t arrive with a bang.

They arrive quietly.

One month is fine.  
The next month is fine.  
Then one day you realize:

“I’m selling more… but net isn’t improving.”

This is often caused by **category drift**—costs creeping up slowly over time:
- packaging creep
- ingredient waste creep
- transport creep

This month is about spotting drift early, using your Accounting & Insights tools, and fixing it with calm owner moves.

Drift is not failure.
Drift is simply a signal that one lever needs attention.

---

## 1) What “category drift” means (simple definition)

Category drift means:
**One cost category grows faster than it should.**

It can happen because:
- you buy packaging more often in small amounts
- you add dish variety and waste increases
- you do extra shopping/transport runs
- your supplier prices rise and you don’t notice
- your process becomes messy

Drift is dangerous because it’s slow.
It can steal net profit without you noticing.

---

## 2) Why categories are the key to catching drift

If you only log “expenses,” you can see total cost.
That’s helpful.

But categories turn total cost into a map.

When you categorize expenses, you can see:
- which category grew
- which category stayed stable
- which category is the hidden leak

A simple category set is enough:
- Ingredients
- Packaging
- Transport
- Equipment
- Other

The goal is not perfect accounting.
The goal is visibility.

---

## 3) How to check drift with time ranges and comparisons

Use your Accounting & Insights views like an owner:

### Step A: Choose a time range
Good ranges for drift:
- This month
- Last 30 days
- This quarter
- Custom (before/after)

### Step B: Compare Now vs Prev
Compare:
- Last 30 days vs previous 30 days
- This month vs previous month
- This quarter vs previous quarter

Then watch:
- income change
- expense change
- net change

If expenses rise faster than income, drift is likely.

---

## 4) The “big three drift categories” and how to fix them

### A) Packaging drift
Signs:
- packaging expenses rise faster than income
- you keep buying “just a little more”
- too many container types appear
- spills/texture issues cause you to upgrade without strategy

Fix (owner move):
- choose one standard per dish type
- bulk-buy the standard instead of random purchases
- redesign packaging for texture without adding unnecessary cost
- log packaging consistently so you can see improvement

Packaging is not a cost only.
It’s reputation protection.
So the goal is **best value**, not cheapest.

---

### B) Ingredients drift
Signs:
- ingredient expenses rise faster than volume
- more waste/spoilage
- too many ingredients for too many variations
- last-minute shopping increases

Fix (owner move):
- focus the menu around anchors
- overlap ingredients across dishes intentionally
- simplify “versions” of the same dish
- improve storage and labeling habits

Ingredient drift is often a sign of too much variety or weak prep rhythm.
Simplification usually fixes it fast.

---

### C) Transport drift
Signs:
- fuel or transport costs rise
- extra runs increase
- you shop more often than planned
- you do unplanned deliveries

Fix (owner move):
- batch supply runs
- plan one purchasing rhythm per week
- consolidate trips
- tighten pickup windows (reduces chaos)
- avoid “special trips” caused by too many menu variations

Transport drift is often a symptom of planning drift.

---

## 5) The best way to fix drift: one lever at a time

Drift can feel annoying because it’s not one big mistake.

But the fix is simple:
**Choose one lever and protect it for 30 days.**

Examples:
- “Packaging standard locked for 30 days.”
- “Menu focus: anchors stay, rotation limited.”
- “One supply run rhythm per week.”

Then compare:
- Last 30 days vs previous 30 days

This is how owners improve net without pressure:
test → compare → refine.

---

## 6) Drift is also a signal for smart investment

Sometimes drift is solved by investment:
- a better storage solution (reduces spoilage)
- a better sealing solution (reduces refunds/spills)
- a better packaging standard (protects texture)

The key is to invest with purpose:
- reduce friction
- protect quality
- protect net

That is owner-level discipline.

## Packaging drift: the hidden cause is “too many options”

Packaging drift often comes from:
- buying small quantities repeatedly
- testing too many container types at once
- upgrading reactively after one complaint

The owner fix is:
- decide one standard per dish type
- buy that standard in planned quantities
- redesign the dish if needed (separate sauce, venting) instead of buying random packaging

When you standardize, cost stabilizes and quality improves.

---

## Ingredient drift: the hidden cause is “too many variations”

Ingredient drift often comes from:
- too many variants of the same dish
- too many special requests accepted
- too many items that use unique ingredients

Owners protect net by:
- keeping anchors stable
- limiting rotations
- choosing new items that share ingredients
- labeling and storage discipline

This is not limiting creativity.
It is building a business you can repeat.

---

## Transport drift: the hidden cause is “weak rhythm”

Transport drift usually means:
- unplanned shopping runs
- supply runs triggered by “we ran out”
- extra delivery trips
- ingredient chasing (special items)

Owners fix transport drift with rhythm:
- one planned supply run schedule
- consolidate trips
- menu focus to reduce surprise ingredients
- tighter pickup windows to reduce chaos

Rhythm reduces both time cost and money cost.
