# Business Coach #27-A — Pricing Updates Without Fear (Use Costs, Anchors, And Net Targets)
## A calm owner method to adjust prices, protect profit, and keep customers trusting you

Pricing is one of the most emotional parts of running a food business.

Owners think:
- “If I raise prices, customers will leave.”
- “If I don’t raise prices, I’m working too hard for too little.”
- “Costs keep changing. I can’t keep up.”

Here’s the truth:

**A professional kitchen adjusts pricing sometimes.**  
Not aggressively. Not randomly. Calmly.

This month is about how to do pricing updates the RiceMap24 owner way:
- based on real costs
- based on anchor dishes
- based on net clarity
- based on customer trust

No drama. No guessing. No fear.

---

## 1) The real pricing goal: protect net, protect quality, protect trust

Price is not only “what people pay.”

Price is what allows you to deliver:
- quality ingredients
- stable packaging
- consistent portions
- safe food handling
- calm operations

So pricing is not greed.
Pricing is protection.

A strong owner does not price to “be nice.”
A strong owner prices to be sustainable.

Sustainable kitchens last.
Unsustainable kitchens burn out.

---

## 2) The three signals that prices may need adjustment

You don’t need a spreadsheet to know when a change is needed.
Look for these signals in your Monthly overview and comparisons:

### Signal A: Income rises, but net stays flat
Often means costs are drifting faster than sales.

### Signal B: You feel “busy but not rewarded”
Often means your margin is too thin or your menu is too complex.

### Signal C: One cost category is creeping up
Packaging, ingredients, or transport drift can silently steal profit.

When these signals appear, the owner response is not panic.
It is a calm adjustment.

---

## 3) Use your categories to understand cost pressure

If you use categories (Ingredients / Packaging / Transport), you already have the foundation.

When you compare periods (Now vs Prev), ask:
- “Which category moved?”
- “Is the change temporary or permanent?”
- “Is this drift, or a planned investment?”

This matters because pricing updates should match reality:
- if ingredient costs are permanently higher
- if packaging standards improved (and should stay)
- if transport became more expensive due to distance or deliveries

Categories turn pricing into a business decision, not an emotional one.

---

## 4) Anchor dishes: the safest place to lead pricing

Your Top dishes list shows what people actually buy and repeat.

This is important because price changes are easiest when:
- the dish is loved
- the dish is consistent
- the dish is your identity

Anchor dishes are not fragile.
Anchor dishes are your strongest product.

So if you ever need to adjust prices, start by thinking about anchors:
- do they protect net profit?
- do they require expensive ingredients?
- do they require special packaging?
- do they take the most time?

An anchor dish should not be underpriced.
Underpricing an anchor hurts everything.

---

## 5) A calm pricing update method (the owner approach)

Here is a method that keeps pricing professional and customer-friendly.

### Step 1: Choose a time lens
Use:
- Last 30 days
- This month
- This quarter

### Step 2: Read the headline
- Income
- Expenses
- Net

### Step 3: Identify the pressure
- cost drift? (which category)
- menu complexity?
- packaging change?
- transport change?

### Step 4: Choose the smallest effective adjustment
Not a huge jump.
Just enough to protect sustainability.

### Step 5: Apply it with clarity
Customers don’t need a long explanation.
They need confidence.

---

## 6) Price adjustments can be “micro” and still powerful

Many owners think price changes must be big.

Often they don’t.

Small increases can protect net dramatically over time, especially if you sell anchors frequently.

You can also adjust in indirect ways:
- adjust add-ons / sides
- adjust lunch lane items
- adjust premium versions
- adjust packaging upgrade options (if offered)

The goal is:
protect net without breaking trust.

---

## 7) The trust rule: consistency beats apologies

If you change prices, don’t apologize like you did something wrong.

Be calm and confident.

Customers trust owners who feel professional.

Professional means:
- prices are clear
- quality is consistent
- the experience is reliable

Your best customers don’t return because you were the cheapest.
They return because you were trustworthy.

## 8) Link pricing to net targets (simple and motivating)

If you’ve done profit planning before, you know the simple truth:

**Target income = expenses + target net**

If net is consistently below what you want to keep, something must change:
- cost control
- focus
- volume
- pricing

Pricing is one of the levers you control.

And pricing updates can be calm, small, and professional.

## A practical example: when packaging costs rise

You compare:
- Last 30 days vs previous 30 days

You see:
- Income stable
- Packaging expense up
- Net slightly down

Owner move is not panic.

It’s one of these calm options:
- standardize packaging per dish type (reduce random purchases)
- switch to a better-value packaging standard
- make a micro price adjustment on the dishes that use the expensive packaging
- simplify menu so packaging variety drops

This is how pricing stays connected to reality:
a small change that protects sustainability.

---

## A practical example: anchor dish is underpriced

You notice your anchor dish sells constantly, but net is flat.

Ask:
- Does this dish use expensive ingredients?
- Does it require special packaging?
- Does it take the most time?

If yes, it may be underpriced.

A calm owner adjustment:
- small price update
- or a premium version option (extra topping/sauce)
- or a small add-on structure that increases basket value

The key: protect the dish’s repeatability and your profit.

## 10) Two pricing strategies that keep customers calm

If you want pricing updates to feel smooth, these two strategies work well:

### Strategy 1: “Anchor protection” pricing
Keep anchor dishes:
- consistent
- available
- priced sustainably

Anchor protection means:
you would rather raise an anchor slightly than let quality drift.

Because customers return for anchors.
Anchors are the core of trust.

### Strategy 2: “Lane-based” pricing
If you have lanes (lunch, desserts, drinks), treat them differently:
- Lunch: repeat frequency + portability
- Desserts: delight + basket value
- Drinks: add-on margin + simplicity

Lane pricing prevents one area from subsidizing another.
It keeps the business healthy.

## 11) The “price change communication” that feels professional

You usually don’t need a long explanation.
But you can keep it simple and confident.

A professional message sounds like:
- “We’ve updated prices to keep quality high.”
- “We’re using better packaging to protect texture.”
- “We’re keeping ingredients fresh and consistent.”

Short. Calm. Confident.

The tone matters more than the details.

## 12) The “margin leak” checklist (pricing-related)

If net is flat, pricing may not be the only issue.
But pricing is often connected to these margin leaks:

- portion sizes drift upward over time  
- packaging types multiply (cost rises)  
- too many special requests (extra ingredients/time)  
- too many dish variations (waste)  
- transport runs increase (hidden cost)  

If you see these, your owner response can be:
- standardize portions (scale/measuring rule)
- standardize packaging
- clarify boundaries for special requests
- simplify menu
- consolidate transport

Sometimes you protect net without raising prices.
Sometimes you do a micro price update.
Both are professional choices.

---

## 13) How to use comparisons as your “price test”

If you change prices (even slightly), you don’t need to guess the outcome.

Use:
- Last 30 days vs previous 30 days
or
- This month vs previous month

Watch:
- income change
- expenses change
- net change

If net improves and repeat behavior stays stable, your pricing update worked.

If net improves but volume drops too much, adjust calmly:
- revise the change
- shift value into bundles or add-ons
- improve surprise value and experience

Pricing is not one decision forever.
Pricing is a controlled system you steer.
