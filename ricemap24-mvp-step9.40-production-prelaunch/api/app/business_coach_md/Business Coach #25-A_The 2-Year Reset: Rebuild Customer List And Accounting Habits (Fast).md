# Business Coach #25-A — The 2-Year Reset: Rebuild Customer List And Accounting Habits (Fast)
## Back to basics—without shame. Just clarity, control, and a fresh start.

Two years is a long time in a real business.

If you’ve been operating for a while, it’s completely normal that some habits get lighter:
- you stop logging every expense
- you forget to link customers on income entries
- you stop using categories
- your customer list becomes incomplete
- you rely more on memory than systems

This is not failure.
This is reality.

But here’s the truth:
**the businesses that last are the ones that can reset.**

A reset is not going backwards.
A reset is an upgrade:
you return to the foundations—now with experience.

This month is a practical “Back to Basic” reset for two core systems in RiceMap24:

1) **Customer List system** (capture, tags/notes, recognition)  
2) **Accounting & Insights system** (income/expense, categories, trends, exports)

The goal is simple:
**rebuild clean habits in a fast, realistic way.**

No heavy tasks.
Just a clean owner standard again.

## 3) Reset part 1: Customer List — make it an asset again

Your customer list is not a contact list.
It is your stability engine.

A clean customer list helps you:
- recognize repeat customers
- reduce messaging friction
- build warm professionalism
- understand who drives stability
- link income to customers in the ledger

### The “reset” version of customer list habits
Keep it very simple:

**When a customer is new, you save them. When they return, you recognize them.**

That alone creates a different business.

---

## 4) Customer List reset: the 4 fields that matter most

You can treat the customer list like a “service memory.”

The fields that matter most are:

### 1) Name (clear and searchable)
Consistency helps.
Avoid multiple versions of the same person.

### 2) Contact (where relevant)
Whatever your kitchen uses: phone, message handle, etc.

### 3) Tag (small set)
Use a small tag set that changes behavior:
- VIP
- Office
- Family
- Allergy-aware
- Mild / Spicy (if relevant)

Too many tags becomes noise.
Small set becomes power.

### 4) Note (one line)
Notes should be short:
- “Prefers mild. No peanuts.”
- “Office order. Sometimes needs receipt.”
- “Pickup usually 17:30.”
- “Orders for 4. Likes extra sauce.”

One line saves you three messages later.

## 5) Reset part 2: Accounting — rebuild the ledger habit

Accounting in RiceMap24 is not about being “an accountant.”
It is about owning your money story.

The reset is very simple:

- income gets logged
- expenses get logged
- categories used for the big three
- exports saved monthly

That is enough to rebuild clarity.

---

## 6) The “big three” categories (the reset set)

If you do nothing else, categorize expenses into:
- Ingredients
- Packaging
- Transport

Those three usually explain most drift in a home kitchen.

Optional categories:
- Equipment
- Other

This gives you a clear map of where money goes.

## 7) The linking reset: connect Customer List + Ledger

This is the key connection you mentioned:

When you log income, choose the customer when possible.

That connection unlocks:
- Top customers insights
- stability learning over time
- more professional business memory

When you also add dish lines (when relevant), you unlock:
- Top dishes insights
- better product strategy
- clearer winner dishes

You don’t need to do it every time.

But a reset goal is:
**link customers often, add dish lines sometimes.**

That’s enough for insights to wake up again.

## 8) Reset the insight views: Monthly overview + comparisons

Once logging becomes consistent again, your insights become meaningful again.

Use these owner views:
- Monthly overview (Income/Expenses/Net)
- Last 30 days vs previous 30 days
- This month vs previous month
- This quarter vs previous quarter

This is the calm improvement loop:
log → compare → choose one lever → repeat.

## 9) Reset the archive: monthly exports (light but powerful)

If you want a clean restart habit:
- export Transactions CSV monthly
- save by month folder (YYYY-MM)

Optional:
- export Customers CSV monthly (your customer asset)

This creates:
- tax-ready habits
- bank-ready story
- peace of mind

Most owners underestimate how powerful “organized exports” feels.
It makes the business feel real.

## 10) The “two-year reset” owner standard (fast and repeatable)

Here is the clean reset standard:

## 11) The reset has “modes” (choose the one that fits your reality)

A reset doesn’t have to look the same for every owner.

Choose the mode that matches your current situation:

### Mode 1: Light reset (busy season)
- log income consistently
- log major expenses (ingredients/packaging/transport)
- capture new customers
This keeps the business real without pressure.

### Mode 2: Standard reset (most owners)
- log income + link customer often
- log expenses + big three categories
- use dish lines sometimes (multi-item orders)
- export monthly
This unlocks insights again.

### Mode 3: Deep reset (when you want a serious upgrade)
- standard reset, plus:
- tighten naming consistency for dishes
- tighten packaging standards per dish type
- use comparisons monthly and quarterly
This is how you create big leaps.

The important message:
**Any reset is better than none.**

## 12) Reset the “naming hygiene” (so Top lists become clear again)

Two-year businesses often have messy naming:
