# Business Coach #18-A — Quarterly Review: Compare Periods And Lead With Clarity
## How to use quarter thinking, time ranges, and comparisons to understand your business in a bigger picture

Monthly tracking is powerful.  
Quarterly thinking is leadership.

A month can be noisy:
- one holiday
- one slow week
- one big order
- one supplier change

A quarter smooths the noise and shows the truth:
- what is actually improving
- what is drifting
- what is stable
- what needs one correction

This month is about using **quarterly review** to lead with calm clarity—using tools you already have in RiceMap24:
- Monthly overview (Income / Expenses / Net)
- Comparisons (Now vs Prev)
- Time ranges (This quarter, Last 30 days, Custom)
- Top dishes and Top customers (if linked)
- Expense categories (if used)
- Exports (for archiving and confidence)

The point is not to become a finance person.
The point is to become an owner who can say:

**“I understand my business direction.”**

---

## 1) What a “quarter” review really is

A quarter is simply a 3‑month period.

Quarterly review means:
- You step back from daily stress
- You look at patterns
- You compare periods
- You learn faster, with less emotion

It’s like moving from “how did today go?” to:
**“Is my business becoming stable?”**

That question is the owner question.

---

## 2) The three-quarter questions (simple and powerful)

A quarterly review can be very simple if you ask the right questions:

### Question 1: Is income becoming more stable?
Not just “higher.” More stable.

Stability looks like:
- fewer extreme swings
- repeat customers showing up
- top dishes staying consistent

### Question 2: Are expenses controlled—or drifting?
Drift is the enemy.

Drift means:
- packaging cost quietly rising
- ingredient waste increasing
- transport runs becoming chaotic

### Question 3: Is net improving over time?
Net is your freedom number.

Net is not a judgement.
Net is direction.

## 3) How to do quarter comparisons inside RiceMap24 (the practical view)

Inside Accounting & Insights, use time ranges like:
- **This quarter**
- **Custom** (if you want exact dates)
- **All time** (for big-picture confirmation)

Then use Comparison:
- **Now vs Prev**

For quarter review, the cleanest comparison is:
- Current quarter vs previous quarter

This gives you:
- Now (current quarter totals)
- Prev (previous quarter totals)
- Change
- %

This is where clarity becomes real.

If you don’t have exact “quarter” buttons in your specific UI, Custom ranges are perfect:
- Q1 range dates
- Q2 range dates
- Q3 range dates
- Q4 range dates

Quarter thinking is not dependent on labels.
It’s dependent on comparing periods.

---

## 4) How to interpret the quarter like an owner (without overthinking)

Here’s a calm interpretation method:

### Step A: Read the headline
- Income
- Expenses
- Net

### Step B: Identify the main driver
Ask:
- “Did income change the most, or expenses?”
- “Did net change because of growth, or because of cost control?”

### Step C: Choose one lever
Not ten.

One lever per quarter is enough:
- tighten packaging standard
- reduce ingredient waste
- consolidate transport runs
- focus the menu around top dishes
- improve order-level tracking so insights become clearer

Owners improve one thing at a time.
That is how businesses become stable.

---

## 5) Quarter review becomes powerful when you add “why” using details

If you use:
- expense categories
- customer linking for income
- dish lines for income

…then your quarter review becomes more intelligent.

Now you can see:
- whether packaging costs rose faster than income
- whether one dish became a true anchor
- whether top customers became more stable
- whether growth came from repeat buyers or one-off spikes

This is not “analysis for fun.”
This is how you stop guessing.

---

## 6) The “quarterly stability map” (how to use Top lists)

If Top customers and Top dishes are available and linked, use a quarter lens:

### Top customers (quarter)
Ask:
- “Are the same names returning?”
- “Is the base growing?”
- “Are office customers becoming a stable segment?”

### Top dishes (quarter)
Ask:
- “Are 1–2 dishes clearly anchoring income?”
- “Did a seasonal dish spike and disappear?”
- “Which dish is stable enough to protect as an anchor?”

Quarter lens helps you avoid overreacting to a one-week trend.

---

## 7) Exports: your quarterly archive and confidence

Quarter review becomes even stronger when you have an archive.

A simple archive strategy:
- export transactions CSV each month
- save in a folder structure (month folders)
- once per quarter, save a “Quarter” folder with the three monthly exports

This is not busywork.
This is future-proofing.

It helps when you:
- talk to an accountant later
- apply for a kitchen rental
- want to prove growth
- plan investments

Clean archives create owner confidence.

---

## 8) Your quarterly owner decision (the calm version)

A quarterly review should end with one decision:

**“What is the one improvement I will protect for the next quarter?”**

Examples:
- “One packaging standard per dish type.”
- “Expense categories used consistently.”
- “Anchor dishes stay stable; creativity rotates.”
- “Link customers on income entries more often.”
- “Use dish lines for multi-item orders.”

One decision per quarter.
That is leadership.

That is how your kitchen becomes a stable business.

## A practical quarter rhythm you can repeat

If you want a clean rhythm, use this cycle:

1) Month 1: Build clarity (log consistently, link customers/dishes more often)  
2) Month 2: Improve one lever (packaging, waste, focus, etc.)  
3) Month 3: Review the quarter and choose the next lever  

This makes growth predictable.

Predictable growth is calmer than intense growth.

## Your final reminder

Quarter reviews are not for perfection.
They are for direction.

Direction is what makes your business feel real.

## Making quarterly review practical (a short template)

If you want a clean template, run your quarter review like this:

### Part 1: Headline (30 seconds)
- Income
- Expenses
- Net

### Part 2: Compare to previous quarter (2 minutes)
- What changed the most: income or expenses?
- Did net improve or decline?

### Part 3: Explain the “why” (3 minutes)
Use what you have:
- categories (packaging/ingredients/transport)
- top dishes (if dish lines exist)
- top customers (if customer linking exists)

Ask:
- “Did costs drift?”
- “Did an anchor dish stay stable?”
- “Did repeat customers increase?”

### Part 4: One decision (1 minute)
Choose one lever for the next quarter.
Only one.

This template makes quarterly review simple and repeatable.

## Quarter review is also your investment filter

Quarter review helps you decide whether you should invest.

If net is improving and stable:
- investing in better packaging or a small tool can be safe

If net is unstable:
- your investment may be in simplification first (menu focus, waste reduction, transport consolidation)

This is how owners avoid “stress upgrades.”

You upgrade when your business supports it.

## Quarter review and “growth story”

A strong quarter story is not always “we grew fast.”
A strong quarter story is:

- “We became more stable.”
- “We reduced cost drift.”
- “We protected our anchor dish.”
- “We increased repeat behavior.”

Those stories create businesses that last.
