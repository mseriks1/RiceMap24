# Business Coach #45-A — Capacity Planning And Bottleneck Map (Grow Without Breaking)
## The most practical growth skill: find your bottleneck and design around it

Most small food businesses don’t stop growing because demand is impossible.

They stop growing because the owner hits a bottleneck:
- time bottleneck
- prep bottleneck
- cooling/storage bottleneck
- pickup window bottleneck
- packaging bottleneck
- delivery/logistics bottleneck
- mental bottleneck (too many decisions)

When you don’t know your bottleneck, growth feels random and stressful.

When you know your bottleneck, growth becomes design:
- you protect what matters
- you simplify what drains you
- you choose one upgrade that unlocks capacity

This month is about creating a Bottleneck Map.
Not a spreadsheet.
A practical owner map.

---

## 1) The principle: your bottleneck decides your income ceiling

Your income ceiling is often set by one limiting factor.

If you fix the wrong thing, nothing changes.
If you fix the bottleneck, everything gets easier.

So the first growth question is not:
“How do I market more?”

It’s:
**“What is currently limiting my capacity to deliver quality orders?”**

That’s the bottleneck.

---

## 2) The 7 common bottlenecks (and how to recognize them)

### Bottleneck A: Time bottleneck
Signals:
- you always feel rushed
- you can’t add more orders without stress
- mistakes increase when volume rises

### Bottleneck B: Prep bottleneck
Signals:
- chopping/portioning takes too long
- you start late and chase the day
- you can’t batch effectively

### Bottleneck C: Storage/cooling bottleneck
Signals:
- fridge space limits ingredients and prep
- you avoid certain lanes because storage is tight
- you can’t do meal packs without space

### Bottleneck D: Pickup window bottleneck
Signals:
- too many customers arrive same time
- handoff becomes chaotic
- messaging increases during pickup

### Bottleneck E: Packaging bottleneck
Signals:
- you run out
- you buy emergency packaging
- packing station chaos slows you down

### Bottleneck F: Delivery/logistics bottleneck
Signals:
- delivery takes too much time
- you do too many runs
- transport costs drift upward

### Bottleneck G: Decision fatigue bottleneck
Signals:
- too many menu items and variations
- you feel mentally exhausted
- you procrastinate decisions

Any business can have multiple bottlenecks, but usually one is dominant.

---

## 3) The bottleneck map (simple structure)

Make a simple map:

1) Identify your dominant bottleneck (choose one)  
2) Identify the “second bottleneck” (backup limiter)  
3) Decide one upgrade that unlocks the bottleneck  
4) Decide one simplification rule to protect the upgrade  

That’s it.

---

## 4) Bottleneck unlockers (examples of upgrades that change everything)

### If time is the bottleneck:
- simplify menu to fewer repeatable winners
- create an assembly rhythm
- limit customization
- use one rotation slot only

### If prep is the bottleneck:
- batch core components
- reduce ingredient variety
- create a core pantry system
- upgrade one tool that speeds prep

### If storage is the bottleneck:
- consider a second fridge/freezer (if financially justified)
- redesign menu toward overlap ingredients
- reduce unique items that take space
- focus on formats that store well

### If pickup is the bottleneck:
- clearer windows
- staggered pickup slots
- pickup-ready zone system
- fewer last-minute changes

### If packaging is the bottleneck:
- standardize packaging types
- keep minimum stock levels
- build a packaging station layout

### If logistics is the bottleneck:
- consolidate deliveries
- shift more orders to pickup
- create one delivery window only
- partner with a pickup point (sometimes)

### If decision fatigue is the bottleneck:
- adopt a lane (lunch, office, family)
- use the offer stack
- use menu engineering categories
- remove low-value dishes

The idea:
unlock the bottleneck, then protect it.

---

## 5) The “unlock and protect” rule

When you unlock a bottleneck, you must protect the unlock.

Example:
You buy a second fridge (unlock storage).
But if you keep adding random dishes, you fill it and the bottleneck returns.

So you protect the unlock with a rule:
- fewer unique ingredients
- one lane focus
- controlled rotation only

Growth is not one purchase.
Growth is design plus discipline.

---

## 6) The strongest capacity strategy: raise average order value

Here’s a powerful insight:

Sometimes you don’t need more orders.
You need bigger orders.

Average order value (AOV) can be a “capacity unlocker” because:
- fewer pickups needed for the same income
- fewer messages and handoffs
- calmer logistics

So your bottleneck strategy can be:
- bundle design
- add-on structure
- office packs
- family packs

This is how you increase income without breaking.

## 7) Your 90-day capacity plan (simple)

If you want a real plan:
- choose one bottleneck
- choose one unlock
- protect it for 90 days
- measure stress and net profit direction

This is enough to create a different business.

## 8) The “bottleneck conversation” you should have with yourself

Ask:
- What part of the process do I secretly dread?
- Where do mistakes happen when volume rises?
- What causes emergency runs?
- What creates the most messaging?

The answers reveal the bottleneck faster than any spreadsheet.

---

## 9) Final reminder

Bottlenecks are not personal failures.
They are design problems.

Design problems can be solved.

---

## 10) Bottlenecks and money: the “profit per hour” lens

A bottleneck is not only about stress.
It’s also about profit per hour.

If your bottleneck is time, and you solve it, you often:
- reduce mistakes
- reduce waste
- increase output
- increase AOV (because you can deliver bundles smoothly)

So bottleneck work is one of the most profitable upgrades you can make.

---

## 11) Bottleneck solutions can be “rules,” not purchases

Many owners think bottlenecks require buying something.

Often, the cheapest unlock is a rule:

- one lane focus
- one rotation slot only
- fixed pickup windows
- order cutoff time
- simplified menu list in busy weeks

Rules can unlock more capacity than equipment.

---

## 12) The “bottleneck stack” (what happens when you unlock one)

When you unlock the main bottleneck, a second bottleneck appears.
That’s normal.

That’s why growth is iterative:
- unlock
- protect
- observe
- unlock next

This is how real businesses scale.

---

## 13) A practical example: storage is the bottleneck

If storage is the bottleneck:
- you avoid prep
- you avoid meal packs
- you avoid certain ingredients

Unlock options:
- second fridge/freezer (if worth it)
- reduce ingredient variety with overlap
- redesign menu toward formats with simpler storage

Then protect the unlock with menu discipline.

---

## 14) A practical example: pickup is the bottleneck

If pickup is the bottleneck:
- chaos happens at the same time each day
- messaging increases
- mistakes happen at handoff

Unlock options:
- staggered pickup slots
- clearer windows
- one “pickup-ready” zone
- reduce customization during pickup hours

Then protect the unlock by keeping the rules consistent.

---

## 15) Your 30-day bottleneck experiment (no heavy planning)

A calm approach:
- choose one bottleneck
- choose one unlock move
- protect it for 30 days
- compare stress level and net direction

If it worked, keep it.
If it didn’t, adjust.

---

## Final reminder

Your bottleneck is not a weakness.
It’s a map.
It tells you exactly what to improve next.

## Extra examples (to make this easier to apply)

- Choose one change, not five.
- Keep it stable for a period.
- Measure using simple signals (repeat behavior, AOV, stress, net direction).
- Keep what works, remove what doesn’t.

This is how small businesses learn fast without chaos.


---

## 16) Bottleneck “swap” strategy (replace effort with structure)

If you want a powerful growth mindset:

Replace effort with structure.

Examples:
- Instead of “cook faster” → simplify the menu and lock stations
- Instead of “message more” → tighten ordering clarity and windows
- Instead of “buy more ingredients” → increase overlap and reduce waste
- Instead of “deliver more” → consolidate and raise AOV

This is how growth becomes lighter.

---

## 17) The bottleneck score (choose the right bottleneck)

If you’re unsure which bottleneck is dominant, score each 1–5:
- Time stress
- Prep burden
- Storage limitation
- Pickup chaos
- Packaging chaos
- Delivery/logistics burden
- Decision fatigue

The highest score is your bottleneck.
Then choose one unlock.

Simple. Honest. Useful.
