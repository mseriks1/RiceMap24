# Business Coach #07-A — Expense Categories And Profit Levers (Packaging, Ingredients, Transport)
## How to see where money goes — and improve profit without raising stress

When owners say “I want higher profit,” they often imagine one thing:

Raising prices.

Sometimes price changes are needed. But the calmest profit improvements often come from something else:

**Expense clarity.**

Because many home kitchen businesses don’t lose money from one big mistake.  
They lose money from small, repeated leaks:

- packaging that gets a little more expensive
- ingredients that spoil
- extra trips (transport)
- small purchases that aren’t tracked
- “just this once” spending that becomes a habit

The solution isn’t to become strict or cheap.

The solution is to become clear.

That’s what categories are for.

---

## Why categories matter inside Accounting & Insights

If you only track totals, you can see:
- Income
- Expense
- Net

That’s already helpful.

But when you add simple categories to expenses, you unlock a higher level of owner clarity:

- Which costs are increasing?
- Which costs are stable?
- Which costs are “worth it” because they protect quality?
- Which costs are silently eating net profit?

Categories are how your ledger becomes a map.

And once you have a map, you can improve profit with confidence.

---

## The “3 profit levers” for most home kitchens

You mentioned the most important ones already.

These three levers are where most owners can gain the most:

1) **Packaging**
2) **Ingredients**
3) **Transport**

If you track these three consistently, you will almost always see an opportunity to improve net profit—without sacrificing taste.

---

## Packaging: premium feel, controlled cost

Packaging is not “just containers.”

Packaging is:
- product protection
- brand trust
- temperature control
- texture protection
- spill protection
- customer confidence

It’s worth investing in good packaging.

But packaging is also one of the easiest costs to lose control of—because it’s purchased often, in small amounts.

### What to do inside the ledger
When you log a packaging expense, categorize it clearly as **Packaging** (or similar).

Over time, you’ll be able to see:
- how packaging costs rise as volume rises
- if packaging cost per order is creeping up
- if a new packaging choice increased cost without improving experience

A calm owner doesn’t guess.
A calm owner checks the map.

---

## Ingredients: quality stays high, waste goes down

Ingredients are the heart of your product.

This isn’t about lowering quality.
It’s about **reducing waste and surprise purchases**.

When you categorize ingredient purchases as **Ingredients**, you can gradually see:
- how ingredient costs follow volume
- if a dish is too expensive to produce regularly
- whether your purchasing rhythm is efficient

This becomes even more powerful when you combine it with dish lines on income entries.
Then you can start thinking like a product owner:
- which dishes pay best
- which ingredients drive most cost
- where small tweaks can protect net profit

---

## Transport: the hidden time and money cost

Transport is often underestimated.

It includes:
- fuel
- extra trips
- delivery runs
- pickup logistics

Even if customers pick up, transport can still be a real cost:
- buying supplies
- shopping runs
- special ingredient trips
- occasional deliveries

Categorizing transport helps you see a cost that is otherwise invisible.

And once you can see it, you can reduce it:
- batch shopping
- plan supply runs
- combine trips
- simplify logistics

This is profit improvement without more work.

---

## Simple category set (keep it practical)

A clean category set that works across most kitchens:

- Ingredients
- Packaging
- Transport
- Equipment
- Other

That’s enough.

You’re not building a corporate accounting system.
You’re building owner clarity.

---

## The “profit improvement” mindset that stays positive

When you look at expenses, do it with this mindset:

**“I’m not restricting myself. I’m protecting my net.”**

Because net profit is what gives you:
- stability
- upgrades
- growth options
- less stress
- the ability to invest in better tools or packaging

This is not about being cheap.

This is about being free.

---

## What this unlocks inside your Insights views

Once expenses have consistent categories, several useful things become easier:

- month-to-month comparisons become more meaningful  
- you can explain why a month was different  
- you can spot which cost driver moved  
- you can plan smarter purchasing and packaging  
- you build stronger confidence in your numbers  

It’s a simple upgrade that makes your whole accounting system feel more “alive.”

---

## A strong owner line for this month

If you want a clean principle:

**“I track expenses in simple categories so I can protect profit without sacrificing quality.”**

That’s how you run a calm, profitable business:
You keep taste high—and you keep your numbers clear.
