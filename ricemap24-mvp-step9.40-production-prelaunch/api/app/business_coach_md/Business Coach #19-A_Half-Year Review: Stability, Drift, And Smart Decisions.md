# Business Coach #19-A — Half-Year Review: Stability, Drift, And Smart Decisions
## How to read 6 months of data, compare periods, and make one strong owner decision

Quarter reviews give direction.  
Half-year reviews give **identity**.

Six months is long enough to see what is real:
- what repeats
- what drifts
- what is seasonal
- what is stable
- what is worth investing in

A half-year review is not complicated.
It’s simply a bigger lens.

This month is about using your Accounting & Insights tools to run a calm half-year review:
- income, expenses, net
- comparisons (Now vs Prev)
- categories (if used)
- top dishes and top customers (if linked)
- exports (for archiving)

The goal is one thing:
**make one smart decision for the next 6 months.**

---

## 1) Why half-year review is so powerful

Monthly numbers can swing.
Quarters smooth some noise.
Half-year smooths even more.

This helps you see:
- real demand patterns
- whether net is improving
- whether costs are drifting
- whether your anchor dishes are stable
- whether your customer base is becoming stronger

Half-year review is where owners stop “trying.”
They start “building.”

---

## 2) The 6-month headline (keep it simple)

Start with the headline:

- Total Income (6 months)
- Total Expenses (6 months)
- Total Net (6 months)

Then compare:
- 6 months vs previous 6 months (if available)
or
- Q1+Q2 vs Q3+Q4 style periods (depending on your timeline)

If your UI doesn’t have a “half-year” button, use Custom ranges.

The labels don’t matter.
The comparison does.

---

## 3) The three half-year truths (what to look for)

### Truth 1: Stability
Is income becoming more predictable?

Signs:
- top customers repeating
- top dishes staying consistent
- fewer extreme swings

### Truth 2: Drift
Are costs quietly rising?

Common drifts:
- packaging creep
- ingredient waste
- extra transport runs
- too many dish variations (hidden cost)

This is why categories matter.
They make drift visible.

### Truth 3: Direction
Is net improving?

Net improving is a signal:
your systems are working.

Net flat is a signal:
one lever needs attention.

Net falling is a signal:
drift or complexity is stealing profit.

Signals are not judgement.
Signals are guidance.

---

## 4) How to use comparisons for half-year clarity

Use:
- This year (if needed)
- This quarter
- Custom ranges

Then compare Now vs Prev.

Half-year comparisons are especially powerful when you do:
- first 3 months vs next 3 months
or
- first half vs second half

This shows whether your improvements are working.

---

## 5) Use top lists with the half-year lens (if linked)

### Top dishes (6 months)
Ask:
- Which dishes stayed in the top list across months?
- Which dishes spiked and disappeared?
- Which dish is your true anchor?

A true anchor dish is a gift:
- it simplifies prep
- it reduces waste
- it stabilizes income

### Top customers (6 months)
Ask:
- Are repeat customers forming a base?
- Is the base growing?
- Are office customers becoming a stable segment?

Stability comes from the base.

---

## 6) The half-year “smart decision” framework (one decision only)

A half-year review should end with one decision:

**What is the one improvement that will make the next 6 months easier and more profitable?**

Examples:
- “Reduce packaging cost drift with one standard per dish type.”
- “Protect the anchor dish and make it repeatable every time.”
- “Build a lunch lane to create weekday stability.”
- “Consolidate transport runs to reduce time and cost.”
- “Increase order-level linking so insights become smarter.”

Choose one.
Protect it.
Measure it.

That is ownership.

---

## 7) Archive it (exports) so you can trust your story

A half-year archive is simple:
- save monthly exports
- place them in a “Half-year” folder

Clean records create confidence.
Confidence makes decisions easier.

## A practical half-year review template (use it every time)

If you want a simple template, use this:

1) Headline totals (Income/Expenses/Net) for the last 6 months  
2) Compare first 3 months vs next 3 months  
3) Identify one drift (packaging/ingredients/transport/complexity)  
4) Identify one anchor (dish or customer segment)  
5) Choose one decision for the next 6 months  

That’s it.

Half-year review should feel light, not heavy.

---

## A final calm reminder

Half-year review is not about judging yourself.
It’s about seeing your direction.

Seeing direction is how owners become confident.

---

## Where half-year review becomes “real”: trend + comparison together

Half-year review is strongest when you combine:
- a long time range (6 months)
- a comparison (Now vs Prev)

This lets you see:
- whether growth is real or just one month
- whether expenses are drifting slowly
- whether net is improving or stuck

If you can’t compare “6 months vs previous 6 months,” do:
- first 3 months vs next 3 months

That comparison is often the cleanest learning tool.

---

## The “drift detection” section (what owners often miss)

Most profit problems are not dramatic.
They are drift.

Drift examples:
- packaging cost creeping up because of small purchases
- ingredient waste rising due to too much variety
- extra transport runs because planning is loose
- more refunds/complaints because packaging or texture is unstable

If you have expense categories, check:
- which category grew faster than income?

If you don’t have categories, start using them going forward.
Half-year review will become much easier next time.

---

## A half-year review also reveals your capacity truth

Six months shows capacity:
- which weeks you handled well
- where stress increased
- where quality slipped
- where the business felt smooth

Capacity truth is important for decisions like:
- adding a lunch lane
- investing in storage
- investing in packaging upgrades
- bringing in a helper later

Owners don’t only track money.
They track what they can sustain.

---

## The “one decision rule” (why this matters)

Half-year review is not a list of ten goals.
It is one decision.

One decision is powerful because:
- it changes behavior consistently
- it is measurable
- it becomes a real upgrade

The best half-year decisions are usually about:
- simplifying
- stabilizing
- protecting an anchor
- reducing one drift

## Use half-year review to decide what NOT to do

One of the most valuable outcomes of a half-year review is clarity about what to stop.

Half-year review can reveal:
- too many dish variations that increase waste
- a packaging choice that looks nice but hurts net
- a process that creates transport chaos
- a dish that sells sometimes but is too hard to repeat

Owners grow by subtraction as much as by addition.

A strong decision can be:
**“We will simplify this.”**

Simplification is not weakness.
It is professionalism.
