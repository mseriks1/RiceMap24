# Business Coach #16-A — Order-Level Tracking: Linking Customers, Dishes, And Income
## The fastest way to unlock smarter insights in Accounting & Insights

When you start linking **customers** and **dishes** to income entries, your ledger changes.

It stops being only a money timeline.
It becomes a business intelligence tool.

Because you are no longer recording “money came in.”
You are recording:

- *who* bought  
- *what* they bought  
- *when* they bought  
- and how that behavior repeats over time  

This month is about **order-level tracking**—in a calm, realistic way.

You don’t need perfect detail on every sale.
You need a method that works in real life.

---

## 1) Why linking matters (and what it unlocks)

### Link income to a customer
Unlocks:
- Top customers ranking
- Stability learning (“who drives repeat behavior?”)
- Better understanding of customer segments

### Link income to dishes (dish lines)
Unlocks:
- Top dishes ranking
- Product strategy learning (“what should be anchor?”)
- Better understanding of what sells during different periods

When you link both, you unlock the strongest insight:
**which dishes drive your best customers.**

That is the business sweet spot:
product + customer stability.

---

## 2) Where this happens: the Ledger entry flow

When you add an income entry in the Ledger, you typically choose:

- Date
- Type: Income
- Customer (optional)
- Multi-line (optional)
- Lines (dish lines) (optional)
- Note (optional)

The key is not to fill everything every time.
The key is to use a simple priority:

1) Link the customer when you can  
2) Add dish lines when the order includes multiple items or when it matters  
3) Keep notes short and only when useful  

This keeps the system lightweight.

---

## 3) Single-item order vs multi-item order (how to think)

### Single-item order
If the customer ordered one main dish, you can:
- link the customer
- record the dish name (or one dish line)
- record the amount

This is enough for top lists.

### Multi-item order
If the customer ordered multiple items, multi-line is your friend:
- each dish line has qty and amount
- totals sum automatically
- you keep the order as one entry

Multi-line entries create better insight:
- they reflect real orders
- they build top dishes accuracy
- they make trends more meaningful

---

## 4) A calm “minimum standard” that works long-term

If you want an owner standard that stays realistic:

- **Always log income**
- **Often link the customer**
- **Sometimes use dish lines**

That alone can unlock powerful insights within a few months.

Remember:
insight does not require perfection.
Insight requires patterns.

Patterns come from consistency.

---

## 5) The connection to comparisons and trends

Once customers and dishes are linked, your comparisons become smarter.

Now when you compare “Last 30 days vs previous 30 days,” you can ask:

- “Did my top dish change?”
- “Did my top customers become more stable?”
- “Did net improve because I focused on winners?”
- “Did a new dish enter the top list?”

This makes improvement visible.

Visible improvement is motivating.

---

## 6) How to avoid overcomplication

Order-level tracking should never feel like a burden.

Use this rule:

**Track detail when it helps decisions.**

Examples of when detail helps:
- you are testing a new dish
- you are refining packaging for one dish
- you are improving a dish to become an anchor
- you are studying what sells to offices vs families
- you are learning why net is flat

When you don’t need detail, keep it simple:
income + customer link.

That keeps the system sustainable.

## A practical example: one order becomes three insights

Imagine a customer buys:
- Dish A (qty 1)
- Side B (qty 2)

If you log this as multi-line with the customer selected, you gain three benefits at once:

1) The income contributes to your month totals (Income/Net).
2) The customer becomes more accurate in Top customers.
3) The dish and side become more accurate in Top dishes.

That is why order-level tracking is powerful:
one entry supports multiple insights.

---

## How to keep the dish list clean (so insights stay readable)

If you want Top dishes to stay useful, keep dish naming consistent.

A simple standard helps:
- same spelling every time
- avoid too many “versions” in the name
- if you change the dish, keep the core name and note the version in a note if needed

This keeps your top lists clean.

Clean lists create clear decisions.

The goal is to track enough to learn what works.

Learning is what increases profit and calm.

## The “three insight doors” you unlock with one income entry

When you create an income entry, you always unlock Door 1:

### Door 1: Money clarity
Income contributes to:
- Monthly totals (Income/Expenses/Net)
- Comparisons (Now vs Prev)
- Trend views

When you also link a customer, you unlock Door 2:

### Door 2: Customer stability
The entry contributes to:
- Top customers
- segment clarity (office/family/VIP patterns)

When you also add dish lines, you unlock Door 3:

### Door 3: Product strategy
The entry contributes to:
- Top dishes
- which products are anchors vs seasonal
- what actually pays you back over time

That’s why order-level tracking is so powerful:
one entry powers three types of insight.

---

## A realistic “when to use detail” rule

To keep this sustainable, use detail in these moments:

- **Big orders** (office/group)  
  → link customer + dish lines (you want accurate insight)

- **New dish tests**  
  → dish lines + note (so you can compare before/after)

- **Your anchor dish**  
  → dish lines often (because this dish drives identity and profit)

- **When net feels flat**  
  → detail helps you diagnose where the business is leaking

On simple everyday sales, keep it light:
income + customer link.

---

## Keeping the customer link clean (so Top customers is accurate)

Top customers is only as accurate as the linking.

If you see “missing” customers in top lists, it often means:
- income entries were logged without selecting the customer
- customer data exists but isn’t linked consistently

The calm fix:
- save the customer once
- choose them from the list when logging income next time

You don’t need to backfill everything.
You need to improve accuracy going forward.

---

## Keeping the dish list clean (so Top dishes stays readable)

Top dishes becomes messy when dish naming varies:
- “Curry Chicken”
- “Chicken Curry”
- “Curry Chicken (spicy)”
- “Curry Chicken v2”

A simple owner naming standard keeps the list useful:
- choose one main name
- keep it consistent
- if you change the recipe, keep the name and add a short note like “v2” only in a note field (not the dish name)

Clean naming creates clean insight.
Clean insight creates calm decisions.

## One more example: diagnosing “busy but net is flat”

If you are busy but net stays flat, order-level tracking helps you identify why.

Look for:
- Are your top dishes changing toward higher-cost items?
- Are you selling more items that need expensive packaging?
- Are you doing too much variety (waste and extra purchases)?
- Are transport expenses rising with extra runs?

When customers and dishes are linked, this diagnosis becomes faster and calmer.

You don’t need to guess.
You can see.

That is owner power.
