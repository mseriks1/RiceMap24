# Business Coach #08-A — Top Dishes And Top Customers: Make Data Work For You
## How your ledger becomes a clear “what to cook” and “who to serve” compass

When your Accounting & Insights starts showing **Top dishes** and **Top customers**, something big happens:

Your business stops being a feeling.  
It becomes a compass.

You no longer have to guess:
- what drives income
- what creates repeat behavior
- which dishes are true winners
- which customers create stability

This month is about using those insights in a practical, owner-level way.

Not with heavy theory—just with clear thinking.

---

## Why “Top lists” are worth more than you think

Many owners spend months guessing:

- “Should I add more dishes?”
- “Which dish should be my focus?”
- “Are people ordering what I think they’re ordering?”
- “Who are my best customers really?”

Top lists answer those questions with reality.

And reality is power.

Top lists help you do three profitable things:

1) **Focus production on winners**
2) **Protect quality by simplifying decisions**
3) **Build stability by supporting the right customers**

That’s how you grow without burning out.

---

## How the system learns: entries → insight

Top dishes and top customers don’t appear by magic.

They come from the way you enter income in the ledger.

### For Top customers
Link income entries to a **Customer** in your register when you can.

That creates the connection needed to rank customers by value over time.

### For Top dishes
Use **Dish (line)** entries (or dish name) when you can.

That creates the connection needed to show which dishes drive revenue.

You don’t need to do it perfectly.
But if you do it often enough, the system becomes smarter and your overview becomes meaningful.

---

## Top dishes: what to do with the list (owner mindset)

When you see your top dishes, don’t treat it like a trophy list.

Treat it like a production strategy tool.

A strong owner asks:

- “Which dish should be my anchor?”
- “Which dish is easy to produce consistently?”
- “Which dish has the best quality-to-effort ratio?”
- “Which dish creates the strongest repeat behavior?”

Sometimes the top dish is not the dish you personally love most.

That’s okay.

Your job as an owner is to balance:
- pride (taste and culture)
- consistency (repeatable quality)
- profit (net stability)

If a dish ranks high and is easy to repeat with high quality, it’s an owner’s gift.

That dish should become your “always available” anchor.

---

## Top customers: stability is built here

Top customers are not just “good customers.”

They are your stability base.

Top customers often have patterns:
- they order regularly
- they order larger baskets
- they trust you more
- they require fewer messages
- they recommend you naturally

A strong owner doesn’t chase everyone equally.

They protect the experience for the customers who already love the kitchen.

This doesn’t mean you ignore new customers.
It means you recognize that stability comes from a small group.

Even 10–20 strong repeat customers can change your entire month.

---

## Using time comparisons to understand growth
If you combine top lists with time comparison views (Last 30 days / This month / This quarter), you can think like a real business owner:

- “Are my winners staying consistent?”
- “Did a new dish climb into the top list?”
- “Did my customer base become more stable?”
- “Did my net improve with focus?”

You can also use custom ranges to compare:
- a special week
- a holiday period
- a pop-up event
- a new product launch

This is where your business becomes measurable and predictable.

---

## What to do when there is “No data yet”
If Top dishes / Top customers show “No data yet,” it simply means:

You haven’t linked enough entries to customers and dishes yet.

It’s not a problem.
It’s a roadmap.

When you start using:
- Customer (optional) on income entries  
- Dish lines when useful  

…those sections will wake up.

And once they do, you’ll feel a major shift:
You won’t need to guess what to focus on.
