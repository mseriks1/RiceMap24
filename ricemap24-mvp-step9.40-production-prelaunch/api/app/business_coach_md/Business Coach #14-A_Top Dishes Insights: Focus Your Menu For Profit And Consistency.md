# Business Coach #14-A — Top Dishes Insights: Focus Your Menu For Profit And Consistency
## How to use “Top dishes” to simplify decisions and grow net profit

One of the fastest ways to make a home kitchen business feel calmer is also one of the most profitable:

**Focus your menu.**

Not because variety is bad.
But because focus creates:
- repeatable quality
- smarter prep
- less waste
- smoother purchasing
- clearer brand identity
- better net profit

That’s exactly what **Top dishes** is for.

Top dishes is not just a list.
It’s a menu strategy compass built from your real sales.

This month is about turning that list into owner decisions.

---

## 1) How Top dishes becomes accurate (what powers it)

Top dishes becomes meaningful when income entries include dish information.

That can happen in two common ways:
- Dish name recorded on the income entry
- Dish lines used (multi-line), where each dish line has qty and amount

Dish lines are especially powerful when orders include multiple items.

You don’t need to do this perfectly every time.
But when you do it consistently enough, Top dishes becomes a real mirror of your business.

And once it’s a mirror, it becomes a tool.

---

## 2) What Top dishes really tells you

Top dishes answers three questions owners normally guess:

1) **What do people actually buy?**  
2) **What do people repeat?**  
3) **What drives stable income?**

Sometimes your personal favorite dish is not the top dish.
That’s not a disappointment.
That’s information.

Owners don’t take it personally.
Owners use it to build a stable business.

---

## 3) The “anchor dish” strategy (focus without losing creativity)

A strong home kitchen often has:
- 1–2 anchor dishes (always available)
- 1–2 rotating dishes (seasonal, creative, limited)
- optional add-ons or sides (simple)

Your Top dishes list helps you decide what deserves anchor status.

Anchor dishes should be:
- consistently ordered
- easy to produce reliably
- easy to package without losing texture
- strong in customer satisfaction

When you anchor the right dish, your whole business becomes easier.

---

## 4) Top dishes + expenses = real profit thinking

Top dishes shows revenue performance.
But owners also care about net profit.

If you are logging expenses with categories (ingredients/packaging/transport), you can combine insights:

- “This dish sells well—does it also protect net?”
- “Does it require expensive packaging?”
- “Does it increase waste?”
- “Does it take too much time to produce?”

This is not complicated accounting.
It’s product owner thinking.

And it’s how you avoid the trap:
being busy with a dish that doesn’t pay you back.

---

## 5) Use time ranges to see if a dish is stable or seasonal

Top dishes becomes smarter when you change the time lens:

- Last 30 days: what is winning now
- This month: what drives current performance
- This quarter: what stays strong over time
- Custom: what changed after a new version of a dish

This helps you see:
- whether a dish is a stable anchor
- whether it was a temporary spike
- whether changes improved results

Owners don’t guess.
Owners compare.

---

## 6) A practical menu focus decision (calm, not extreme)

Here is a strong owner move when Top dishes reveals clarity:

**Stop trying to push everything.**
Build around what already works.

Then refine:
- improve packaging
- improve sauce balance
- improve portion consistency
- reduce waste
- protect the eating moment

This is how you grow without burnout:
focus → consistency → repeats → stability.

**“I use Top dishes as a compass—so I focus my menu, protect quality, and improve profit.”**

That’s how a home kitchen becomes a real, stable business.

Imagine your Top dishes list shows:
1) Dish A (clear winner)

A calm owner move is:
- Dish A becomes the anchor (always available)

Consistency increases repeats.
Repeats increase stability.
Stability protects net profit.

Top dishes helps you escape the trap by giving permission to focus:
**“I will build around what customers already prove they want.”**

That is not limiting.

## Where to “see” the insight and what to look for

When you open Accounting & Insights and view **Top dishes**, you are looking at a ranking generated from your recorded income entries (especially when dish lines are used).

Two things matter when you interpret the list:

1) **Consistency:** does the same dish appear across multiple periods?
2) **Concentration:** do 1–2 dishes dominate the list?

If one dish dominates, that is not a weakness.
That is a gift:
a clear anchor for purchasing, prep, and quality.

---

## The 4 dish questions that create owner clarity

When you see a dish in your top list, ask:

1) **Can I produce it consistently without stress?**  
2) **Does it package well without losing texture?**  
3) **Does it create repeat behavior (people crave it again)?**  
4) **Does it protect net profit (ingredients + packaging + time)?**

If a dish scores high on these, it belongs in your anchor set.

If a dish sells well but fails one area (e.g., it becomes soggy), your move is not to remove it instantly.
Your move is to redesign the product:
- change packaging
- separate sauce
- adjust texture components
- make it more repeatable

This is how owners turn a popular dish into a stable profit engine.

## A focused menu is a “system upgrade”

A focused menu is not just fewer choices.
It’s an operating system upgrade:

- fewer ingredients to manage
- fewer packaging types to stock
- faster prep rhythm
- easier quality control
- less waste
- more consistent customer experience

This is why focus often increases both pride and profit.

## The “rotating slot” strategy (keep creativity alive)

Many owners fear focus because they fear losing creativity.

The rotating slot solves that:
- Keep anchors stable (your winners)
- Keep 1–2 rotating dishes for creativity and seasonal pride

This keeps the business calm and keeps the owner inspired.

---

## Your Owner Standard (repeatable)

**“Anchors stay stable. Creativity rotates.”**

That is a professional menu strategy.

## Using Top dishes with time comparisons (the owner method)

If you compare:
- Last 30 days vs previous 30 days

…you can see whether your top dish is becoming stronger or fading.

If a dish rises into the top list after you improved packaging or consistency, that’s proof your improvement worked.

If a dish drops, it may signal:
- seasonality
- competition
- inconsistency
- a change in ingredient quality
- a packaging problem

Owners don’t panic.
Owners adjust one lever and measure again.
