# Business Coach #03-A — Net Profit Made Simple (Your Money Scoreboard)
## Income, expenses, and the one number that gives you control

A profitable home kitchen business isn’t built by luck. It’s built by **clarity**.

Not “complex accounting.” Not spreadsheets you never open. Just a simple ability to answer one question with confidence:

**Am I actually making money?**

This month is about making that question easy—without stress.

You are going to hear a lot of words in the business world: margins, cash flow, KPIs, forecasts. Those words can feel intimidating when your main job is already big: cooking, serving, and showing up for customers.

So here’s the simple truth:

**You don’t need business language. You need a scoreboard.**

And in a home kitchen business, your scoreboard is:

**Net Profit = Income − Expenses**

That’s it.

---

## Why “Net” matters more than sales

Sales are exciting. They feel like success.

But sales alone don’t tell you the truth.

You can have a busy month and still feel drained, confused, and financially stuck—because you’re not looking at what stays after expenses.

Net profit is what stays.

Net profit tells you:
- if your business is sustainable
- if your pricing and costs match reality
- if you’re growing in a healthy way
- if you’re building freedom or building stress

And best of all:

**Net gives you control.**

Because once you can see net, you can improve it—without working harder.

---

## The simple money model (made for home kitchens)

Think of your business in three boxes:

### 1) Income (money in)
This is what customers pay.

If money came in, it belongs here.

### 2) Expenses (money out)
This is what you spend to run the business.

If money went out for the business, it belongs here.

Common examples:
- ingredients
- packaging
- transport
- tools/equipment (small purchases count)
- anything you buy specifically for orders

### 3) Net (what stays)
This is the result.

**Net is your business oxygen.**

It’s what allows you to:
- reinvest
- upgrade
- grow calmer
- build stability
- take time off without fear

## Your money routine (keep it small, keep it consistent)

The most important thing is not doing “a lot.”

The most important thing is doing a little, consistently.

Here’s the standard that builds real clarity:

- **If money came in, log it.**
- **If money went out, log it.**
- **Let the system show you net.**

RiceMap24’s Accounting & Insights is designed to make this lightweight. It doesn’t need to feel like homework. It can feel like a quick “business close.”

Just like you close your kitchen after cooking, you close your business after selling.

## What changes when you track net (real-life benefits)

When owners start tracking net, they usually notice these improvements quickly:

### 1) Less emotional stress
You stop wondering.

You don’t need to “feel” if the business is going well. You can see it.

### 2) Better decisions
Instead of guessing:
- you adjust one cost
- you improve one routine
- you make one smart pricing move
- you focus on dishes that pay

### 3) More confidence in growth
Growth becomes exciting, not scary, because you can measure it.

### 4) The beginning of real insights later
Top customers and top dishes become meaningful only when income and expenses are recorded consistently.

Net is the bridge from effort to insight.

## “What if my numbers aren’t perfect?”

Perfect numbers are not required for a strong business.

**Consistent numbers are.**

You are not trying to impress an accountant.  
You are trying to build a calm, profitable business.

That means:
- log what you can
- keep it simple
- get better month by month

Even “good enough” numbers create real clarity.

And clarity creates confidence.

## Small ways to improve net (without working more)

Once you can see net, you can improve net in smart, simple ways.

Here are three “owner levers” that don’t require more cooking hours:

### 1) Packaging awareness
Packaging is often underestimated.

When you track packaging as an expense consistently, you can spot patterns:
- where costs creep up
- which packaging choices increase expense without improving experience
- what feels premium without being expensive

### 2) Ingredient consistency
Not in the “don’t use good ingredients” way.

In the “plan your purchasing and reduce waste” way.

Waste is a hidden expense. Tracking helps you notice it.

### 3) Dish profitability
Some dishes are loved but expensive to produce.

Some are simple, fast, and profitable.

When your income logs include dish names sometimes, you gradually build a clearer picture of which dishes pay you back.

This is not about removing joy from cooking.

It’s about building **freedom** from your effort.
