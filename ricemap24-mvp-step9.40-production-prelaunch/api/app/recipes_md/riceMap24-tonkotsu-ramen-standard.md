## Ingredients (Broth batch: ~8 portions)

> **Important:** Tonkotsu depends on **bone cleaning + long boil + constant rolling boil** for emulsification.  
> Use the linked Serious Eats recipe for **exact bone types and ratios** and detailed technique notes.

### Broth (per source)
- Pork bones (neck bones, femur, trotters, etc.) per source
- Water (enough to cover; plus topping up)
- Aromatics (onion, garlic, ginger, scallions) per source timing

### Serving (per bowl)
- Ramen noodles (fresh preferred)
- Tare (seasoning concentrate) of choice *(shoyu/miso/shio — see note)*
- Aromatic oil (optional)
- Toppings: chashu, soft-boiled egg, nori, scallions, mushrooms, etc.

## Production Plan (Home-Kitchen Friendly)

### Day 0 (Evening): Soak
1. Rinse bones under cold water.
2. Soak bones overnight in cold water in the fridge (or 6–8 hours), changing water once if possible.

### Day 1: Clean + boil hard
#### 1) Parboil (removes impurities)
1. Drain soak water.
2. Cover bones with fresh water in a large pot.
3. Bring to a boil and boil **10–15 minutes**.
4. Drain, rinse bones thoroughly, and scrub off any dark residue.

#### 2) Main boil (emulsify)
5. Return cleaned bones to the pot.
6. Cover with fresh water (at least several cm above bones).
7. Bring to a **rolling boil** and maintain it (not a gentle simmer).
8. Top up with hot water as needed so bones stay submerged.
9. Boil **10–12 hours** (per source), keeping the boil vigorous to emulsify fat and collagen into the broth.

#### 3) Add aromatics late (cleaner flavor)
10. Add aromatics (onion/garlic/ginger/scallions) at the time window recommended by the source (often the last 1–2 hours).

#### 4) Strain
11. Strain broth through a fine strainer.
12. Optional: strain again through cheesecloth for smoother broth.

## Fast “Service Setup” (Per Bowl)
Tonkotsu broth is **not** seasoned by itself—you season with **tare**.

### Per-bowl assembly workflow
1. Boil ramen noodles according to package timing.
2. In serving bowl, add tare (amount depends on tare strength; start small).
3. Add hot tonkotsu broth and stir to dissolve tare.
4. Add drained noodles.
5. Add toppings and aromatic oil.

## Quality Checks
- **Broth color:** Opaque, creamy, off-white.
- **Mouthfeel:** Rich, slightly sticky (collagen).
- **Aroma:** Pork-forward but not “dirty” (cleaning step matters).

---

## Scaling & Ops Notes (10+ portions)
- Broth is easiest to scale by making **bigger bone batches** (same method, larger pot).
- This is best as a **weekly batch** item rather than daily.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended (Critical)
Ramen quality depends on separation:
- Container A: **Hot broth** (sealed, very secure lid)
- Container B: **Noodles** (lightly oiled to prevent sticking)
- Container C: **Toppings + tare cup**

Label: “Add tare to bowl, pour in hot broth, add noodles, then toppings.”

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** after packaging.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Broth:** Refrigerate up to 3 days or freeze up to 2–3 months.
- **Reheat:** Bring to a boil; whisk/stir to re-emulsify.
- **Noodles:** Cook fresh for best texture (don’t store cooked noodles long).
