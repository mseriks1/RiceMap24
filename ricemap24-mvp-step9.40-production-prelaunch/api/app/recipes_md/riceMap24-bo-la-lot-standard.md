# Bò Lá Lốt (Vietnamese Grilled Beef Wrapped in Wild Betel Leaves) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Vietnamese classic that looks and smells amazing: juicy, peppery beef rolls wrapped in fragrant **lá lốt** leaves and grilled until glossy with light char.  
Not the “usual” pho/bánh mì takeaway—perfect as a premium **skewer/roll platter** or **rice vermicelli set** for home-kitchen sellers.

**Source recipe (reference):** Viet World Kitchen — “Grilled Beef in Wild Betel/Piper Lolot Leaves (Thịt Bò Nướng Lá Lốt)”  
https://www.vietworldkitchen.com/blog/2016/06/beef-wild-betel-leaf-recipe-la-lot.html

- **Style:** Leaf-wrapped beef rolls, grilled
- **Yield:** ~26–30 rolls (depending on leaf size)

## Ingredients (approx. 26–30 rolls)

### Beef filling (from reference recipe)
- **450 g** ground beef (chuck preferred)
- **1/4 cup** finely chopped scallion (green + white)
- **2 tsp** fish sauce
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **3/4 tsp** ground black pepper
- **2–3 tsp** Madras curry powder *(optional but very common in lá lốt filling)*
- **1–2 Tbsp** club soda / sparkling water *(keeps filling tender and light)*

### Leaves + grilling
- **~115 g** lá lốt (wild betel / piper lolot) leaves with stems attached (enough for 26–30 large leaves)
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (for brushing or pan/grill)

### Optional “wow” finish (recommended for RiceMap24 photos)
- **Scallion oil:** 3 Tbsp neutral oil + 2 Tbsp chopped scallions + pinch salt (warm oil, pour over scallions)
- **Crushed roasted peanuts** + **fried shallots** (sprinkle)

---

## Dipping Sauce Options (pick one)
### Option A — Nuoc Cham (bright, classic)
- 3 Tbsp fish sauce
- 3 Tbsp lime juice
- 3 Tbsp sugar
- 6 Tbsp water
- 1–2 garlic cloves (minced)
- 1 chili (sliced)

### Option B — Mam Nem “lite” (stronger funk; optional)
If you want a more “authentic” punch, offer as an optional add-on only.

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)

### 1) Leaf prep
1. Rinse leaves and pat completely dry.
2. Trim thick stems if needed (keep some stem to help rolling).

### 2) Make filling (2 minutes)
1. In a bowl, combine ground beef with scallion, fish sauce, salt, pepper, curry powder, and club soda.
2. Mix until tacky/sticky (this helps it hold together).
3. Rest 5–10 minutes.

---

## Rolling (Consistency Rules)
1. Place a leaf shiny side **down** (matte side up).
2. Put **1–1.5 Tbsp** filling near the wider end.
3. Shape filling into a small “finger” log.
4. Roll tightly into a cigar.  
   - If leaf is small, overlap 2 leaves.

**Skewer option:** thread 4–6 rolls per skewer for easy turning and great presentation.

---

## Cooking Method (Grill or Grill Pan)

### Heat
- **Medium-high** (you want char without burning leaves instantly).

### Grill steps
1. Lightly brush rolls with neutral oil (or oil the grill grates).
2. Grill, turning frequently, until leaves are wilted with light char and beef is cooked through:
   - **8–12 minutes** total, depending on roll size and grill heat.
3. Rest 2 minutes before serving.

### Doneness check
- Beef should be cooked through (no raw pink in the center).

## Portioning (Per order guideline)
- Snack: **6 rolls**
- Meal set: **8–10 rolls** + noodles/rice
- Dip: **25–35 ml** per order

---

## Scaling for Production (10 meal orders)
Assume 1 meal order = 9 rolls average → 90 rolls total.
- Make **3×** the base recipe (yields ~80–90 rolls depending on leaf size).
- Assemble rolls ahead and keep chilled; grill in batches.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rolls in a **vented** container (or crack lid 2–3 min before sealing).
- Dip in a sealed cup.
- If doing vermicelli set: keep noodles and herbs separate; rolls in their own container.

**Holding time:** Best within 1–2 hours. Reheat rolls briefly in a hot pan or air fryer for best aroma.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Uncooked rolls: refrigerate up to 24 hours (covered).
- Cooked rolls: refrigerate up to 2 days; reheat in air fryer 190°C for 4–6 minutes.

---
