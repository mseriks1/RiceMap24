# Bao Buns (Gua Bao-Style) — Choose Protein (Pork / Chicken / Tofu) — RiceMap24 Standard

A premium handheld Asian genre: **soft steamed bao buns** with a bold filling, pickles, and sauce.  
This is a “choose protein” standard designed for **home kitchens**, **pickup**, and **2/4/6-pack** sales.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Bao buns (handheld)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Taiwanese/Chinese gua bao-inspired
- **Yield:** 8 bao buns (with scaling included)
- **Active time:** ~30 minutes
- **Total time:** ~60 minutes (faster if using store-bought bao)
- **Skill level:** Medium (assembly + moisture control)

---

## Two production routes (pick one)
### Route A (fastest, recommended for RiceMap24)
- Use **store-bought frozen bao buns** (steam 8–10 min).  
You focus on premium fillings/sauces.

### Route B (from scratch buns)
- Higher effort; not necessary for MVP menus.

This recipe assumes **Route A** (store-bought buns) for consistent home-kitchen success.

---

## Why bao works for RiceMap24
- **Premium & photogenic:** looks like restaurant street food.
- **High margin:** buns are cheap, perceived value is high.
- **Easy upsells:** extra bun, extra sauce, “double protein”.
- **Great packaging:** 2/4/6 bun boxes.

## Ingredients (8 bao buns)

## A) Bao buns
- **8** frozen bao buns (gua bao style)

---

## B) Pickles + crunch (premium identity)
### Quick cucumber pickles (5 min)
- **1 cucumber**, thin slices
- **2 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** sugar
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- Optional chili flakes

### Crunch add-ons (choose 1–2)
- Crushed roasted peanuts
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Fried shallots (premium)
- Nori strips (fusion)

---

## C) Protein filling (choose ONE)

### Option 1: Hoisin-Ginger Pork (fast, rich)
- **600 g** pork shoulder/belly, thin strips
- **2 Tbsp** hoisin
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** brown sugar
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **2 cloves** garlic, grated
- **1 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil

Cook: sear pork, add sauce, reduce to glossy.

---

### Option 2: Teriyaki Chicken (banker)
- **650 g** chicken thighs, sliced
- Teriyaki glaze:
  - 90 ml soy + 60 ml water + 2 Tbsp honey + 1 Tbsp vinegar + ginger/garlic + slurry

Cook: sear chicken, glaze to sticky.

---

### Option 3: Crispy Tofu (vegan-capable)
- **600 g** firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- **2 Tbsp** cornstarch + salt/pepper
- Fry or air-fry until crisp
- Toss in hoisin-soy-lime or teriyaki glaze

---

## D) Sauce (choose ONE main sauce)
### 1) Kewpie/Gochujang Mayo (spicy creamy)
- 120 ml mayo + 1 Tbsp gochujang + 1 tsp vinegar + 1 tsp honey

### 2) Sesame Miso Mayo (umami)
- 120 ml mayo + 1 Tbsp white miso + 1 tsp sesame oil + lime

### 3) Peanut-Lime Sauce (rich)
- peanut butter + hoisin + lime + water (thick)

---

## E) Fresh finish
- Sliced scallions
- Cilantro (optional)

---

## Equipment
- Steamer or pot + steaming rack
- Skillet/wok
- Mixing bowls
- Tongs

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make quick pickles (5 minutes)
Mix vinegar, sugar, salt and toss cucumber. Drain lightly before use.

---

## 2) Cook protein (10–15 minutes)
Follow your chosen protein option.  
**Key:** keep sauce thick/glossy (not watery) so buns don’t get soggy.

---

## 3) Steam bao buns (8–10 minutes)
Steam from frozen until soft and hot.

---

## 4) Assemble bao buns (RiceMap24 build)
Per bun:
1. Spread **1–2 tsp** sauce inside bun.
2. Add **70–90 g** hot protein.
3. Add pickles.
4. Finish with scallions + crunch topping.

---

## Portioning
- Protein: 70–90 g per bun
- Sauce: 10–15 g per bun
- Pickles: small pinch
- Crunch: small pinch

---

## Batch for 20 buns (party)
- Bao buns: 20
- Protein: 1.6–1.8 kg
- Sauce: 3×
- Pickles: 3 cucumbers

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- **Keep buns and fillings warm**, but avoid steam buildup:
  - Vent container 20–30 seconds before sealing.
- Pack **crunch toppings separate** if using fried shallots/peanuts.
- Offer extra sauce cup as upsell.

**Sell formats:** 2-pack / 4-pack / 6-pack / 8-pack.

---

## Storage
- Cooked protein: 2 days refrigerated
- Pickles: 2 days refrigerated
- Bao buns best same day (re-steam to refresh)

---

## Allergens
- Wheat (bao)
- Soy (soy sauce/hoisin)
- Egg (mayo sauces)
- Sesame/peanuts optional
