# Thai Panang Curry Chicken (Gaeng Panang Gai / แกงพะแนงไก่) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Panang curry is a Thai crowd-pleaser: creamy coconut curry with a richer, slightly sweeter profile than green curry, usually finished with **makrut lime leaves** and a drizzle of coconut cream.  
For RiceMap24, it’s a strong **banker** because it’s fast, scalable, photogenic, and delivers very well.

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Coconut curry + chicken + optional vegetables
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~35–40 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (balance paste + coconut; keep sauce smooth)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **High demand**: familiar Thai curry profile, widely liked.
- **Great delivery**: sauce protects chicken; reheats well.
- **Consistent**: easy to portion and price.
- **Upsells**: extra chicken, extra sauce cup, add vegetables, “spice levels,” add rice.

---

## Choose Your Chicken (Pick ONE)
### Option A (recommended banker): Boneless chicken thigh
- **1.2 kg** boneless chicken thighs, sliced into bite-size pieces

### Option B (leaner): Chicken breast
- **1.0–1.1 kg** breast, thin slices (cook gently to avoid dryness)

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Curry base
- **100–140 g** Panang curry paste *(about 6–8 Tbsp)*  
  - Start at 100 g if your paste is very spicy/salty.
- **800 ml** coconut milk (full-fat)
- **200 ml** chicken stock or water (adjust thickness)
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil **OR** use “cracked” coconut cream method (below)

## B) Seasoning (Thai balance)
- **2–3 Tbsp** fish sauce (to taste)
- **1–2 Tbsp** palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- **1–2 Tbsp** tamarind paste *(optional, adds depth and gentle tang)*
- **6–8** makrut/kaffir lime leaves, very finely sliced *(highly recommended)*
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** peanut butter (very small amount; boosts body/“Panang” richness)

## C) Vegetables (optional but good for photos)
Pick 1–3:
- **1 red bell pepper**, thin strips
- **250 g** green beans, cut 4–5 cm
- **1 small zucchini**, half-moons

## D) Finish (signature look)
- **120 ml** coconut cream (or thick top of coconut milk), for drizzle
- **1–2** red spur chilies, thin slices (garnish)
- Optional: chopped cilantro

## To serve
- Jasmine rice: **250 g cooked** per order

---

## Equipment
- Large pot/Dutch oven (5–6 L)
- Knife + cutting board
- Ladle
- Small bowl for balancing seasoning

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Slice chicken.
2. Slice peppers/beans if using.
3. Finely slice makrut lime leaves (remove central rib for best texture).
4. Have fish sauce + sugar ready for final balancing.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

### Step 1 — Fry paste for “restaurant” flavor (3–5 minutes)
**Option 1 (best): crack coconut cream**
1. Add **~200 ml** thick coconut cream to pot over medium heat.
2. Cook 3–5 minutes, stirring, until it thickens and looks slightly oily (“cracks”).
3. Add Panang curry paste and fry 2–3 minutes until very fragrant.

**Option 2 (simple): oil method**
1. Add **2 Tbsp oil** to pot over medium.
2. Add curry paste and fry 2–3 minutes until fragrant.

### Step 2 — Build curry sauce (5 minutes)
1. Add remaining coconut milk and **200 ml** stock/water.
2. Bring to a gentle simmer (avoid hard boil).

### Step 3 — Cook chicken (8–12 minutes)
1. Add chicken.
2. Simmer gently until just cooked:
   - Thigh: **8–10 minutes**
   - Breast: **6–8 minutes**
3. Skim excess oil if desired (optional).

### Step 4 — Add vegetables (5–8 minutes)
Add in order:
1. Green beans (3–4 min)
2. Bell pepper/zucchini (2–3 min)

### Step 5 — Balance seasoning (2 minutes)
Start with:
- **2 Tbsp fish sauce**
- **1 Tbsp sugar**
- **1 Tbsp tamarind** (optional)

Taste and adjust:
- Too salty/strong → add a splash of coconut milk or water
- Too flat → a bit more fish sauce
- Needs roundness → a bit more sugar
- Needs depth → tamarind or a **very small** spoon of peanut butter

### Step 6 — Finish (30 seconds)
1. Stir in sliced makrut lime leaves.
2. Turn off heat.
3. Drizzle coconut cream over the top (or package separately for a premium look).
4. Top with red chili slices.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sauce:** smooth, creamy (not split), rich orange-red.
- **Chicken:** tender, not dry.
- **Aroma:** paste fragrance + makrut lime leaf “lift.”
- **Taste:** spicy-salty-sweet balanced.

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Chicken thigh: ~2.0 kg
- Curry paste: ~170–240 g (adjust heat)
- Coconut milk: ~1.35 L
- Stock/water: ~350 ml
- Veg + garnishes: scale 1.7×

**Ops tip:** Make curry base + chicken in a big pot. Add vegetables in a finishing batch so veg stays bright for photos.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: Panang curry (leak-proof)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Optional cup: extra sauce / coconut drizzle
- Optional garnish cup: chili + lime leaf (add after reheat)

Label: “Reheat gently (don’t hard-boil). Add garnish last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently to a simmer; avoid hard boil (coconut can split).
- If thick, loosen with a splash of water/stock.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Coconut
- Peanuts (if using peanut butter)
