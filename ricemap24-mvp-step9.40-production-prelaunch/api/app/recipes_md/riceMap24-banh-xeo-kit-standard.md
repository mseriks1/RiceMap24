# Bánh Xèo (Vietnamese Crispy Sizzling Crepes) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Crispy turmeric rice-flour crepes stuffed with shrimp/pork, bean sprouts, and herbs—served with lettuce wraps and nuoc cham.  
This is visually striking and not the most common “takeaway default.” It can be sold as a **kit** (crepes + herb wrap + sauce) for home-kitchen sellers.

**Source recipe (reference):** Hungry Huy — “Banh Xeo (Vietnamese Sizzling Crepes)”  
https://www.hungryhuy.com/banh-xeo-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Crispy crepes + fillings + lettuce wrap + dipping sauce
- **Yield:** ~6–8 crepes (depends on pan size)
- **Active time:** ~30–40 min
- **Total time:** ~60 min (best with batter resting)
- **Skill level:** Medium (pan technique for crisp edges)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24
- **Looks amazing:** golden crepes, green herbs, colorful dipping sauce.
- **Premium “experience” product:** customers build lettuce wraps.
- **Modular:** choose protein, extra herbs, extra sauce, add “kit” options.
- **Batch-friendly:** batter can be mixed ahead; fillings prepped.

## Equipment
- Nonstick skillet or crepe pan (20–28 cm ideal)
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Ladle
- Spatula
- Knife + cutting board
- Optional: squeeze bottle for batter

---

## Ingredients

## A) Batter (base)
Use the Hungry Huy recipe as the reference for exact ratios. Core components:
- Rice flour
- All-purpose flour (small amount)
- Turmeric powder (color)
- Coconut milk
- Water (or sparkling water)
- Salt
- Green onion (sliced)
- Optional: batter rest 30–60 minutes for better crisp

> RiceMap24 tip: make batter the day before and keep chilled; whisk before use.

---

## B) Filling — Choose your protein (Pick ONE)
Classic combos are shrimp + pork, but it’s very flexible.

### Option A — Shrimp + Pork (classic)
- Shrimp (peeled)
- Thin sliced pork belly/shoulder

### Option B — Chicken
- Thin sliced chicken thigh

### Option C — Vegetarian
- Mushrooms + tofu

### Option D — “Seafood mix”
- Shrimp + squid + scallops (small amounts, quick cook)

> Keep filling portions modest: too much filling = soggy crepe.

---

## C) Vegetables + herbs (the “wow” plate)
- Bean sprouts (lots)
- Lettuce leaves (for wrapping)
- Fresh herbs: mint, cilantro, Thai basil
- Cucumber (optional)
- Pickled carrot/daikon (optional)

---

## D) Nuoc Cham (dipping sauce)
- Fish sauce + water + sugar + lime + garlic + chili  
Use your standard nuoc cham from earlier recipes.

---

## Prep (20 minutes)
1. Mix batter and rest (30–60 minutes recommended).
2. Prep protein and slice thin for fast pan cooking.
3. Wash and dry lettuce + herbs.
4. Make nuoc cham and portion into cups.
5. Keep bean sprouts ready.

---

## Cooking Method (Crispy Crepe Workflow)

> Goal: **thin batter, hot pan, enough oil, fast steam, then crisp.**

### 1) Pre-cook filling lightly (per crepe)
1. Heat pan over medium-high with a small splash of oil.
2. Add a small portion of protein and cook until mostly done.
3. Add a handful of bean sprouts on top (they steam inside crepe).
4. Move filling to one side of the pan.

### 2) Pour batter and “sizzle”
1. Add a bit more oil (key for crisp).
2. Stir batter and ladle into pan.
3. Swirl quickly to coat thinly.
4. Cover 30–45 seconds to steam and set.

### 3) Crisp and fold
1. Remove lid and cook uncovered until edges are crisp and the bottom is golden.
2. Fold crepe in half over the filling.
3. Slide onto a rack or plate (rack keeps it crisp).

Repeat for remaining crepes.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Crisp edges:** brittle, not soft.
- **Color:** golden with turmeric hue.
- **Interior:** sprouts still fresh-crisp; not watery.
- **Sauce:** nuoc cham served separately.

## RiceMap24 Serving Formats

### Format A — “Bánh Xèo Kit” (best for delivery)
- Container A: 2 crepes (keep vented, don’t seal hot)
- Container B: lettuce + herb pack (dry)
- Cup C: nuoc cham
- Optional: pickles + cucumber

### Format B — Dine-now Plate (pickup)
- Crepe on rack + herb platter + sauce cup

**Suggested pricing tiers**
- 1 crepe + kit
- 2 crepe + kit (most popular)
- Family pack: 6 crepes + large herb kit + 3 sauces

---

## Portioning (Per order)
- 2 crepes is a satisfying meal for most customers.
- Nuoc cham: 25–35 ml per crepe.
- Herb pack: generous (it’s part of the experience).

---

## Scaling for Production (10 orders)
- Make batter for ~25–30 crepes.
- Pre-portion fillings (protein + sprouts) into small cups for speed.
- Cook crepes to order for best crisp.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended (Critical)
- Use vented containers or crack lids briefly—steam destroys crisp.
- Keep lettuce/herbs separate and dry (paper towel helps).
- Sauce sealed separately.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Crepes are best fresh; they soften as they sit.
- Re-crisp in a hot dry pan for 30–60 seconds per side if needed.

---

## Allergens
- Shellfish (if shrimp)
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Gluten possible (small flour in batter)
