# Bulgogi (Korean BBQ Beef) Rice Bowl — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean favorite: thin-sliced beef marinated in a sweet-savory soy-garlic sauce, quickly cooked and served over rice. Great as a “mild crowd-pleaser” bowl with optional spice and veggie add-ons.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Bulgogi (Korean BBQ beef)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/bulgogi

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Marinated Beef • Quick Stir-Fry/Grill
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min  
- **Total time:** ~45–90 min *(includes marinating; longer is better)*
- **Skill level:** Easy (slicing + fast cook)

---

## Equipment
- Large skillet, grill pan, or BBQ
- Mixing bowl (marinade)
- Tongs
- Cutting board + knife
- Measuring spoons/cups

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Bulgogi depends on the **exact soy/sugar/fruit (if used) balance** and the cut of beef.  
> Use the linked Maangchi recipe for **exact measurements** and optional additions (e.g., pear).

### A) Beef
- Thinly sliced beef (ribeye/sirloin/brisket-style slices), amount per source

### B) Marinade / Seasoning set (per source)
- Soy sauce
- Sugar (and/or honey)
- Garlic
- Green onion
- Sesame oil
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Black pepper
- Optional: grated Korean pear (tenderizer + sweetness), per source
- Optional: onion, carrot, mushrooms, etc. per source

### C) To serve
- Cooked rice: **~250 g cooked rice per bowl** (4 bowls)
- Optional greens: lettuce, cucumber, quick sautéed onions, or kimchi

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Beef slicing:** If slicing yourself, partially freeze beef **20–30 minutes** so you can slice very thin.
2. **Mix marinade:** Combine all marinade ingredients per the source recipe.
3. **Marinate:** Toss beef with marinade and refrigerate:
   - **Minimum:** 30 minutes  
   - **Best:** 2–8 hours (or overnight if the source recommends)
4. Optional veggies: Slice onions/mushrooms and keep ready.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Cook bulgogi (fast, high heat)
1. Preheat a skillet/grill pan over **medium-high heat**.
2. Add a small amount of oil only if your pan needs it (many cuts render enough).
3. Add marinated beef in a thin layer (cook in **batches**; avoid crowding).
4. Cook quickly, stirring and spreading:
   - until beef is cooked through and lightly caramelized  
   - about **2–4 minutes** per batch depending on thickness and pan heat
5. If using onions/mushrooms, cook them with the beef (or sauté separately for cleaner texture).

> **Caramelization tip:** Crowd the pan = steaming. Keep batches small for browning.

---

## Bowl Assembly (RiceMap24 format)
1. Rice in base (hot).
2. Bulgogi on top.
3. Optional add-ons:
   - kimchi
   - cucumber salad
   - extra sesame
   - spicy sauce cup (gochujang on the side)

**Serve note:** “Mix gently” (optional).

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef:** Tender, not dry (don’t overcook).
- **Flavor:** Sweet-savory with sesame aroma; not overly salty.
- **Texture:** Some browned edges (caramelization) for depth.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** for beef + marinade by **2.5×**.

### Batch notes
- Marinate in large sealed containers (food-safe).
- Cook in **multiple small batches** for consistent browning.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Single bowl works well: rice + beef on top.
- Include kimchi/cucumber in a side compartment if available.
- Offer “spice level” via a separate gochujang sauce cup.

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Cooked bulgogi:** Refrigerate up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Quick hot-pan toss is best; microwave works if gentle.
- **Marinated raw beef:** Follow the source guidance and safe food handling; don’t hold too long.
