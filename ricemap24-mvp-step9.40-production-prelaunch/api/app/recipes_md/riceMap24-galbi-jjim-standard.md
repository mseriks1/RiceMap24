# Galbi Jjim (Korean Braised Beef Short Ribs) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean special-occasion braise: beef short ribs simmered until nearly falling off the bone, glazed in a sweet-savory soy sauce, with “rounded” radish and carrot pieces and optional chestnuts/jujubes.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Braised beef short ribs (Galbi-jjim: 갈비찜)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/galbi-jjim

## Ingredients (Serves 4 — from source)

### A) Seasoning sauce + vegetables
- **4 dried shiitake mushrooms**, washed and soaked in **2½ cups** cold water for **3–4 hours**, until soft
- **2 cups** mushroom soaking water *(measure after soaking; top up with cold water if short)*
- **⅓ cup** soy sauce
- **½ cup** mirim (mirin) **or** water
- **¼ cup** sugar (white or brown)
- **½ tsp** ground black pepper
- **8 garlic cloves**, minced
- **1 tsp** minced peeled ginger
- **1 medium Korean radish** (**8–12 oz / 225–340 g**) **or** daikon, peeled
- **2 large thick carrots**, peeled

### B) Beef short ribs + add-ins
- **2 lb** beef short ribs (**8 pieces**), rinsed and soaked in cold water **30 min–1 hr**, changing water a couple of times
- **12** peeled chestnuts (frozen pre-peeled or fresh peeled)
- **2 Tbsp** rice syrup
- **8 large** dried jujubes, pitted

### C) Garnish (optional)
- **12** pine nuts, tips removed
- **12** ginkgo nuts, shelled and cooked

---

## Prep (Do this before cooking)

### 1) Soak mushrooms (3–4 hours)
1. Soak dried shiitake mushrooms in **2½ cups** cold water until soft.
2. Drain through a strainer set over a bowl.
3. Measure out **2 cups** of soaking water. If short, top up with cold water to reach 2 cups.

### 2) Make seasoning sauce (5 minutes)
In a medium bowl, mix:
- **2 cups** mushroom soaking water
- **⅓ cup** soy sauce
- **½ cup** mirin (or water)
- **¼ cup** sugar
- **½ tsp** black pepper
- **8 cloves** minced garlic
- **1 tsp** minced ginger

Set aside.

### 3) Prep vegetables (10–15 minutes)
1. **Mushrooms:** Remove stems and quarter caps.
2. **Radish “balls”:** Cut radish into **1½-inch cubes**, then round edges with a paring knife to make ~**1-inch balls**. Make **10–12** pieces.
3. **Carrot “balls”:** Same method as radish. Make **10–12** pieces.

### 4) Soak ribs (30–60 minutes)
1. Rinse ribs and soak in cold water **30 min–1 hr**.
2. Change water 1–2 times. Drain.

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Blanch ribs (cleans flavor)
1. Fill a large pot halfway with water and bring to a boil.
2. Add drained ribs to boiling water.
3. Blanch **5 minutes**, stirring a few times.
4. Drain and rinse ribs thoroughly with cold water.
5. Wash the pot clean (important for a clean-tasting braise).

### 2) First braise (20 minutes)
1. Return ribs to the clean pot.
2. Add the **seasoning sauce**.
3. Cover and cook over **medium-high** heat for **20 minutes**.

### 3) Add vegetables + long simmer (60 minutes)
1. Add mushrooms, chestnuts, radish balls, and carrot balls.
2. Cover, reduce heat to **low**, and cook **1 hour**, stirring occasionally.

### 4) Add jujubes + rice syrup (10 minutes)
1. Stir in **jujubes** and **2 Tbsp rice syrup**.
2. Cover and simmer **10 minutes**.

### 5) Tenderness check + optional extra time
- Meat should be close to falling off bone.  
- Check by poking with a wooden skewer: it should go through easily.
- If still tough, add ~**1 cup water** and cook **15–30 minutes** more (thicker ribs take longer).

### 6) Reduce to glaze (final 3–7 minutes)
1. Increase heat to **high**.
2. Spoon sauce from the bottom over the meat and vegetables.
3. Reduce until sauce is glossy and coats the ribs and vegetables (not watery).

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Meat:** Very tender; nearly falling off bone.
- **Sauce:** Thick, glossy glaze; not soupy.
- **Veg:** Rounded pieces tender but intact (not broken).

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per plate/bowl)
This is traditionally served family-style, but you can portion:

- **Per portion:** 2 ribs + 2–3 radish balls + 2–3 carrot balls + mushrooms + sauce
- **Serve with:** steamed rice (separate container recommended for delivery)

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Source serves 4. Multiply by **2.5×** for ~10 portions.

### Quick scale targets (10 portions)
- Short ribs: **5 lb** (~**20 pieces**, depending on size)
- Dried shiitake: **10**

- Soy sauce: **~0.83 cup** (≈ **200 ml**)
- Mirin: **1.25 cups** (≈ **300 ml**)
- Sugar: **0.625 cup** (≈ **125–140 g**, depends on granule)
- Black pepper: **1.25 tsp**
- Garlic: **20 cloves**
- Ginger: **2.5 tsp**
- Radish: **~560–850 g**
- Carrots: **~5 large**
- Chestnuts: **30**
- Rice syrup: **5 Tbsp**
- Jujubes: **20**

### Batch notes
- Use a wide heavy pot for even simmering and reduction.
- Glaze step is easiest in smaller batches (avoid overcrowding).

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Pack ribs + vegetables + sauce in a leak-proof container.
- **Rice separate** (prevents sogginess).
- Add note: “Warm spices & sweet-savory glaze. Reheat gently.”

**Holding time:** Excellent; flavors deepen after resting.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **3 days**.
- Reheat gently on the stove or microwave.  
  If sauce thickens too much, loosen with a splash of water and stir.

## Allergens & Dietary Notes

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Don’t skip soak + blanch: it prevents off smells and keeps the braise clean.
- Reduce at the end for the signature “glazed” finish.
- Thick ribs take longer—plan buffer time.
