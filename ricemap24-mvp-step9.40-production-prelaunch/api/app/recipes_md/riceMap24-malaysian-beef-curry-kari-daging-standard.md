## Ingredients (Serves 6) — from source
- **2 Tbsp** canola oil   
- **1 stem** fresh curry leaves, removed from stem   
- **2** star anise  
- **1 onion**, sliced  
- **2.2 lb** beef chuck steak, cut into chunks   
- **1 large tomato**, chopped  
- **4 Tbsp** Malaysian curry powder   
- **1** lemongrass stalk (whole, bruised)  
- **400 ml** canned coconut milk
- **2 tsp** salt 
- **1 tsp** white pepper  
- **1 tsp** sugar  
- **1 tsp** shrimp paste 
- Water: **around 4 cups** (to cover) 

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Beef:** Cut chuck into **4 cm** chunks (uniform = even tenderness).
2. **Onion:** Slice.
3. **Tomato:** Chop.
4. **Lemongrass:** Bruise the stalk (smash with the back of a knife to release aroma).
5. **Curry leaves:** Strip from the stem.
6. Measure curry powder, coconut milk, salt/pepper/sugar, shrimp paste.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed, Repeatable)

### 1) Bloom aromatics (5 minutes)
1. Heat pot over **moderate/medium** heat with **2 Tbsp canola oil** until hot.  
2. Add **curry leaves** and **star anise**; let splutter **30 seconds**.   
3. Add sliced onion and sauté **5 minutes** to soften slightly.  

### 2) Add core ingredients (2 minutes)
Add to the pot:  
- beef chunks  
- chopped tomato  
- **4 Tbsp** Malaysian curry powder  
- bruised lemongrass stalk  
- **400 ml** coconut milk  
- salt, white pepper, sugar  
- shrimp paste citeturn2view0  

Stir to coat beef with spice and coconut.

### 3) Add water + simmer low and slow (2 hours)
1. Add **around 4 cups water** to cover.  
2. Bring to a boil.
3. Reduce heat to **low**, partially cover, and cook **2 hours** until beef is tender.
4. Stir every 20–30 minutes to prevent sticking and to keep coconut from catching on the bottom.

**Tenderness check**
- Beef should be **fork-tender**. If still chewy at 2 hours, keep simmering 15–30 minutes more and add a splash of water if needed.

### 4) Final balance (5 minutes)
- Taste and adjust salt/pepper.
- If sauce is too thin: simmer uncovered 5–10 minutes to reduce slightly.
- Remove star anise and lemongrass before packing (recommended).

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef:** Fork-tender, not stringy.
- **Sauce:** Fragrant, coconut curry gravy that coats beef.
- **Aroma:** Curry leaf + star anise + lemongrass clearly present.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Curry in leak-proof container.
- **Rice separate** (best texture).
- Note: “Reheat gently. Sauce thickens when chilled—add a splash of water if needed.”

**Holding time:** Excellent; curry improves after resting (often better next day).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **4–5 days** (per typical curry handling; source also notes it holds well).   
- Freeze up to **3 months**; reheat until piping hot.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes
- **Contains:** Shrimp paste (shellfish), coconut
- **May contain:** Gluten (some curry powders vary—check label)
