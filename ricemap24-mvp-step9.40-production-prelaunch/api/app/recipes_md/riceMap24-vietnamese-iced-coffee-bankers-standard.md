# Vietnamese Iced Coffee (Cà Phê Sữa Đá) — RiceMap24 “Banker Drink” Standard

A true bestseller drink: strong coffee + sweetened condensed milk served iced.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 because it’s **high-margin**, **fast**, and **repeat-order friendly**.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Iced coffee
- **Cuisine vibe:** Vietnamese
- **Yield:** 1 drink (with batch scaling included)
- **Active time:** 3–5 minutes (if coffee concentrate is ready)
- **Total time:** 5 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy

## Why this is a RiceMap24 “Banker”
- **Very popular flavor profile:** sweet + strong.
- **Fast to serve:** pour + stir + ice.
- **High margin:** condensed milk + coffee concentrate.
- **Easy upsells:** extra shot, less sweet, oat milk version.

---

## What you need for consistent results
You want a **strong coffee concentrate** so the drink doesn’t taste watery when iced.

### Two best options
1) **Espresso / strong moka pot**
2) **Cold brew concentrate** (best for batch)

This card includes both.

---

## Ingredients (1 drink)

## Option A (Fastest): Espresso method
- **60 ml** double espresso (or very strong brewed coffee)
- **35–45 g** sweetened condensed milk (about 2–3 Tbsp)
- **180–220 g** ice
- Optional: **30–60 ml** cold water (if you want it slightly less intense)

## Option B (Best for batch): Cold brew concentrate
- **90 ml** cold brew concentrate
- **35–45 g** sweetened condensed milk
- **150–200 g** ice

---

## Standard Sweetness Levels (pick one)
- **Less sweet:** 25–30 g condensed milk
- **Classic:** 35–45 g
- **Extra sweet:** 50–60 g

---

## Step-by-Step (Single Drink)

### 1) Add condensed milk
- Put condensed milk in the bottom of the cup.

### 2) Add coffee
- Pour espresso or concentrate over condensed milk.

### 3) Stir
- Stir **10–15 seconds** until fully blended.

### 4) Add ice
- Fill cup with ice and stir once more.

**Taste check:** it should be strong and sweet, not watery.

---

## Batch: Cold Brew Concentrate (Recommended for Home Kitchens)

### Concentrate ratio (simple)
- **1 part coffee grounds : 5 parts water** (by weight)

Example batch:
- **200 g** coarse coffee grounds
- **1,000 g (1 L)** cold water

#### Method
1. Combine in a container, stir.
2. Steep **12–16 hours** in fridge.
3. Strain through fine sieve + filter.
4. Store concentrate chilled **up to 5 days**.

**Portion per drink:** 90 ml concentrate.

---

## Batch for 10 drinks (classic sweetness)
- Coffee concentrate: **900 ml**
- Condensed milk: **400 g**
- Ice: **1.8–2.2 kg**

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
### Best practice
- Use a **sealed cup** (or bottle) and keep it cold.

### Two packaging styles
1) **Cup with ice (ready-to-drink)**  
   - Best for quick pickup.
2) **Bottle without ice + separate ice cup**  
   - Best for longer pickup times (prevents watery coffee).

**Label suggestion:** “Shake or stir before drinking.”

---

## Variations / Upsells
- **Extra shot:** +30 ml espresso (or +45 ml concentrate)
- **Less sweet:** reduce condensed milk (offer a toggle)
- **Oat milk version:** replace part of condensed milk with vanilla oat milk (still sweet)
- **Salted version:** tiny pinch of salt (boosts caramel note)
- **Cinnamon dust:** tiny sprinkle on top (optional)

---

## Quality Checks
- Coffee is strong enough to stand up to ice.
- Condensed milk fully blended (no streaks).
- Sweetness consistent with your menu option.

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (condensed milk)
