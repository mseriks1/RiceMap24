# Tea-Smoked Chicken (茶熏鸡) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Chinese technique: chicken is first cooked, then **tea-smoked with rice and sugar** in a wok/pot, producing a deep, aromatic smoky flavor.  
This is **not typical takeaway**—it’s a signature “special” that looks premium and sells well sliced over rice with a dipping sauce.

**Source recipe (reference):** Viet World Kitchen — “Tea Smoked Chicken Recipe”  
https://www.vietworldkitchen.com/blog/2015/03/tea-smoked-chicken-recipe.html

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (tea-smoking technique)
- **Style:** Cook → smoke in wok/pot → slice
- **Yield:** 4–6 servings
- **Active time:** ~25 min
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (safe indoor smoking + heat control)

---

## Equipment (Important)
- Large wok or deep pot with tight lid
- Heavy-duty aluminum foil (line wok and lid)
- Rack/steamer rack (to keep chicken above smoking mixture)
- Tongs
- Thermometer (recommended)
- Ventilation: strong kitchen fan + open window

---

## Ingredients (4–6 servings)

### A) Chicken
- **1 whole chicken** (1.3–1.6 kg) OR **1.5 kg** bone-in pieces
- **1 Tbsp** salt
- **1 Tbsp** Shaoxing wine (optional)
- **3 slices** ginger
- **2** scallions (smashed)

### B) Smoking mix (in foil packet)
- **1/4 cup** uncooked rice
- **1/4 cup** sugar
- **2 Tbsp** black tea leaves (or green tea)
- Optional aromatics (adds premium aroma):
  - dried tangerine/orange peel
  - star anise
  - cinnamon stick

### C) Finish (photogenic + sellable)
- Scallion oil (optional)
- Sesame seeds (optional)
- Cucumber salad side (excellent pairing)

### D) Simple dipping sauce (choose one)
- **Soy + black vinegar + chili oil**, OR
- **Ginger-scallion sauce** (very premium, not spicy)

---

## Prep Plan (Best workflow)
1) Cook chicken (poach/steam/roast) until fully done  
2) Air-dry 10 minutes (skin dries = better smoke finish)  
3) Smoke in wok/pot (10–20 minutes)  
4) Rest and slice

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Cook chicken (choose method)
### Option A — Gentle poach (juicy, easiest)
1. Put chicken in a pot with ginger, scallions, salt (and Shaoxing wine if using).
2. Add water to cover.
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
4. Simmer until thickest part reaches **74°C**.

### Option B — Roast (more browned skin)
- Roast at 180–190°C until fully cooked, then smoke.

> The smoking step adds flavor; chicken must be fully cooked first.

---

## 2) Dry the chicken (10 minutes)
- Place cooked chicken on a rack to air-dry 10 minutes.
- Pat skin dry if needed. Dry skin takes smoke better.

---

## 3) Set up the smoker (wok/pot)
1. Line the wok/pot and lid with foil (makes cleanup easy).
2. Mix rice + sugar + tea + optional aromatics and place at the bottom (on foil).
3. Place a rack above the mix so the chicken sits **1 inch / 2.5 cm** above it.
4. Put chicken on the rack, breast side up.

---

## 4) Smoke (10–20 minutes)
1. Heat the wok/pot over **medium-high** until smoke begins to appear.
2. Once you see steady smoke, reduce heat to **medium/medium-low** to prevent burning the sugar.
3. Cover tightly and smoke:
   - **10 minutes** for light smoke
   - **15–20 minutes** for deeper smoke

> Safety: keep ventilation on. Do not leave unattended (sugar can scorch).

---

## 5) Rest and slice (10 minutes)
1. Turn off heat and let the chicken sit covered 5 minutes.
2. Remove chicken and rest 10 minutes.
3. Slice for serving.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Smoke aroma:** tea + caramel + gentle spice, not burnt sugar.
- **Skin:** lightly browned, dry, aromatic.
- **Meat:** juicy, not dried out.

## RiceMap24 Serving Formats

### Format A — Tea-Smoked Chicken Rice Plate (best seller)
- 250 g cooked rice
- 180–220 g sliced chicken
- Small dip cup (soy-vinegar-chili)
- Optional cucumber salad side

### Format B — Party platter
- Sliced chicken on a board, sesame + scallion oil, 2 dips

### Upsells
- Extra dip cup
- Extra chicken
- Add cucumber salad / pickles
- “Spicy chili oil” add-on

---

## Scaling (10 rice plates)
- Cook 2–3 chickens (depending on size).
- Smoke in batches (one at a time) to keep smoke clean (avoid sugar burn).

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Chicken sliced and fanned for presentation.
- Rice separate.
- Dip cups sealed.
- Optional garnish cup (sesame/scallion greens) to add right before eating.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate cooked chicken up to 2 days.
- Reheat gently (steam or low oven); avoid microwaving too hot (dries).
- Smoke aroma is best same day.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (if using soy dip)
