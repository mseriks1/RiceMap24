# Bò Kho (Vietnamese Spicy Beef Stew) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Vietnamese beef stew/soup built on lemongrass, star anise, fish sauce, and tomato—served over wide rice noodles (or with baguette).  
This card is **highly repeatable**: exact ingredient list, clear timeline, and delivery packaging.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Bo Kho: Spicy Vietnamese Beef Stew with Noodles”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/bo-kho-spicy-vietnamese-beef-stew/

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Braised beef chunks (stew/soup)
- **Yield (source):** Serves **8**
- **Active time:** ~25–35 min  
- **Total time:** ~2 hr 30–3 hr (including marinate + simmer)
- **Skill level:** Medium (simmer + reduction control)

## Ingredients (Serves 8) — from source

### For the beef (marinade)
- **2 to 2½ lb** boneless beef chuck **or** brisket, cut into **1½-inch chunks**
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **3 Tbsp** ginger, minced
- **5 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **2½ tsp** five-spice powder
- **1½ tsp** brown sugar

### For the rest of the stew
- **3 Tbsp** oil
- **1 stalk** lemongrass, cut into 3-inch lengths (after removing tough woody parts)
- **2 stalks** lemongrass, minced (after removing tough woody parts)
- **8 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 onion**, sliced thinly
- **4 Tbsp** tomato paste
- **8 cups** water
- **2 cups** pure coconut water/juice
- **2** star anise
- **1 tsp** ground black pepper
- **1 tsp** chili powder
- **1 tsp** ground annatto (optional)
- **1 Tbsp** paprika
- **8 large carrots**, peeled and cut on the bias into **1½-inch chunks**
- **1 tsp** salt
- **3 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **3 Tbsp** chili oil (or to taste)
- Wide rice noodles or egg noodles

### Garnish / serve
- **¼ cup** coarsely chopped cilantro leaves
- **½ cup** Thai basil leaves
- **½ cup** thinly sliced raw onion
- Lime wedges

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Beef:** Cut to 1½-inch (4 cm) chunks.
2. **Lemongrass:**  
   - 1 stalk into 3-inch lengths (for oil infusion)  
   - 2 stalks minced (for stew base)
3. **Carrots:** Peel and cut on the bias into 1½-inch chunks.
4. **Onion:** Slice thinly.
5. **Measure everything** before you start—this stew is straightforward once mise en place is done.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

### 1) Marinate beef (30 minutes)
1. In a bowl, combine beef chunks with:
   - minced garlic (2 cloves)
   - minced ginger (3 Tbsp)
   - fish sauce (5 Tbsp)
   - five-spice powder (2½ tsp)
   - brown sugar (1½ tsp)
2. Mix thoroughly until every piece is coated.
3. Marinate **30 minutes** at room temperature (or longer refrigerated).

---

### 2) Build aromatic base
1. Heat **3 Tbsp oil** in a large pot over **high heat**.
2. Add the **lemongrass stalk pieces** (3-inch lengths) to infuse the oil for **1 minute**.
3. Add **minced lemongrass (2 stalks)** and **8 cloves minced garlic**. Cook **2 minutes**.

> Keep it moving—garlic burns fast on high heat. If it browns too quickly, reduce heat slightly.

---

### 3) Onion + beef browning
1. Add sliced onion; cook until translucent.
2. Add all marinated beef. Brown evenly on all sides.

---

### 4) Tomato paste + first simmer (1 hour)
1. Add **4 Tbsp tomato paste**.
2. Stir and cook **uncovered** for **5 minutes**.
3. Add:
   - **8 cups water**
   - **2 cups coconut water**
   - **2 star anise**
   - **1 tsp black pepper**
   - **1 tsp chili powder**
   - **1 tsp ground annatto (optional)**
   - **1 Tbsp paprika**
4. Bring to a boil.
5. Reduce heat to **medium-low**, cover, and simmer **1 hour**.

> Target: small steady bubbles, not a hard boil.

---

### 5) Add carrots + seasoning (40 minutes)
1. After 1 hour, add:
   - carrots
   - **1 tsp salt**
   - **3 Tbsp soy sauce**
   - **3 Tbsp chili oil** (or to taste)
2. Cover and simmer **40 minutes** more, until carrots and beef are tender.

---

### 6) Serve (noodle + garnish workflow)
1. Fish out and remove the big lemongrass stalk pieces and any star anise you can find.
2. Cook noodles per package instructions.
3. Portion noodles into bowls and ladle stew over.
4. Garnish with cilantro, Thai basil, raw onion, and lime wedges.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Service)
- **Beef:** Fork-tender; if still tough, simmer longer (add water if liquid is low).
- **Broth:** Spicy-sweet-savory with lemongrass aroma; adjust chili oil + salt at the end.
- **Carrots:** Tender but intact (not mush).
- **Aroma:** Star anise should be noticeable but not overpowering.

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Source serves 8. Multiply by **1.25×** for ~10 portions.

**Ops tip:** Broth and beef hold well; cook noodles closer to service to prevent sogginess.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality = separate components**
- Container A: beef + carrots
- Container B: hot broth (sealed)
- Container C: noodles (separate)
- Container D: garnish kit (dry)
- Optional: chili oil cup

Label: “Combine noodles + beef, pour broth, add garnish and lime last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **3 days** (flavor improves).
- Reheat gently to a simmer; add water if reduced.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes
- **Contains:** Soy (soy sauce), fish (fish sauce)
- **Gluten:** Possible (soy sauce unless GF)
- **Spicy:** Adjustable via chili oil
