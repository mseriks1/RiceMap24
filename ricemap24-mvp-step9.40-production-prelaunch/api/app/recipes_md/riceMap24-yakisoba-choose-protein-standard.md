# Yakisoba (Japanese Stir-Fried Noodles) — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Japanese street-food classic: stir-fried noodles with crisp vegetables and a sweet-savory yakisoba sauce.  
Perfect for RiceMap24/home kitchens because it scales well and supports **multiple protein choices** with one master sauce.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Yakisoba (Japanese Stir-Fried Noodles)”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/yakisoba/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Japanese
- **Style:** Wok/skillet noodle stir-fry
- **Yield:** 3–4 portions (depends on noodle pack size; see portioning)
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min  
- **Total time:** ~25–30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (high-heat timing)

---

## Choose Your Protein (Pick ONE)
Target: **~350–450 g total protein** for 3–4 bowls.

### Option A — Pork belly (classic)
- 350–450 g sliced pork belly

### Option B — Chicken thigh slices
- 350–450 g boneless chicken thigh, thinly sliced

### Option C — Beef (wok strips)
- 350–450 g beef sirloin/flank, sliced thin across the grain

### Option D — Shrimp
- 350–450 g peeled shrimp (raw)

### Option E — Tofu (vegetarian)
- 350–450 g firm tofu, pressed and sliced/cubed

> Keep the protein weight consistent so pricing stays predictable.

---

## Master Yakisoba Sauce (⅓ cup / 80 ml)
Whisk until sugar dissolves:
- **¼ cup** Worcestershire sauce
- **4 tsp** oyster sauce
- **4 tsp** ketchup
- **2 tsp** soy sauce
- **2 tsp** sugar

> This is the key “street-food” taste. Make in bulk and portion per batch.

---

## Vegetables (flexible base)
Use the classic yakisoba set (swap based on what home kitchens have):
- Onion (sliced)
- Carrot (julienned)
- Cabbage (shredded)
- Mushrooms (shiitake or button)
- Bell pepper (optional)
- Bean sprouts (optional, add last)
- Scallions (finish)

---

## Noodles
- **Pre-steamed yakisoba noodles** (common packs are 3 servings per package)
  - If noodles are stuck together: loosen under warm water quickly and drain well.
- If substituting: fresh ramen-style noodles can work (parboil briefly, drain well).

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Mix sauce** (⅓ cup / 80 ml). Set aside.
2. **Slice protein** thin (3–4 mm) for fast cooking.
3. **Prep vegetables** (keep sprouts/scallions separate for the end).
4. **Loosen noodles** if needed; drain well (water makes yakisoba soggy).

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable Workflow)

> Best practice: cook **2 bowls at a time** for better heat and less steaming.

### 1) Sear protein (2–4 minutes)
1. Heat wok/skillet over **high heat** until hot.
2. Add a little oil.
3. Add protein and stir-fry:
   - Pork/chicken/beef: until mostly cooked and lightly browned.
   - Shrimp: until just pink/opaque (remove early).
   - Tofu: sear until edges are lightly golden (remove early).
4. Remove protein to a plate (you’ll add it back later).

### 2) Stir-fry vegetables (2–3 minutes)
1. Add a touch more oil if needed.
2. Add onion + carrot first; stir-fry 30–60 seconds.
3. Add cabbage + mushrooms; stir-fry until crisp-tender.

### 3) Noodles + sauce + finish (2–3 minutes)
1. Add noodles and toss to combine.
2. Pour in yakisoba sauce and toss continuously until noodles are evenly coated and glossy.
3. Return protein to the pan and toss 30–60 seconds to heat through.
4. Add bean sprouts (if using) and toss 10–15 seconds.
5. Finish with scallions; serve immediately.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Noodles:** glossy, not watery; slightly browned edges are good.
- **Veg:** crisp-tender (not limp).
- **Protein:** tender (don’t overcook; add back at end).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl)
- Noodles: 1 portion (based on your noodle pack)
- Protein: **100–140 g**
- Vegetables: **120–180 g**
- Sauce: **~1.5–2 Tbsp** per bowl (adjust after test batch)

---

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
- Make **3–4×** sauce (portion into 10 cups).
- Cook in **small batches** (2 bowls per batch) to keep noodles from steaming.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Use a **wide container** (noodles clump in tall cups).
- Optional: sauce cup for “extra sauce” upsell.
- If adding sprouts: add them last or keep separate for crunch.

**Holding time:** Best within 1–2 hours.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce), oyster sauce (shellfish), wheat (noodles), Worcestershire (varies by brand)
