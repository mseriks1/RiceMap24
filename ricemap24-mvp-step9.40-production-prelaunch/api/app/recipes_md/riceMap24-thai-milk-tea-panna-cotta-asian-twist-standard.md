# Thai Milk Tea Panna Cotta Cups (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

A Western classic (panna cotta) with a strong Asian signature: **Thai milk tea** (cha yen) flavor, served as neat single-serve cups.  
Perfect for pickup/takeaway: stable, chilled, no melting.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Panna cotta cups
- **Asian twist:** Thai milk tea (black tea + spices + condensed milk vibe)
- **Yield:** 10 cups (120–150 ml each)
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Chill time:** 4–6 hours
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup-friendly:** cups with lids; clean portioning.
- **Premium look:** caramel-tea color + optional dark tea jelly top.
- **High-margin upsells:** boba add-on, extra condensed drizzle, larger cup.
- **Batch-friendly:** make a pot, fill cups.

---

## Equipment
- Saucepan
- Whisk
- Fine strainer (recommended)
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale recommended)
- 10 small dessert cups with lids

---

## Ingredients (10 cups)

## A) Thai tea infusion
- **800 ml** heavy cream (or 600 ml cream + 200 ml whole milk)
- **4–5 Tbsp** Thai tea mix (or strong black tea)
- Optional spices (small amounts):  
  - 1 star anise OR 1 small cinnamon stick (optional)
- **60–80 g** sugar (adjust)
- **120 ml** sweetened condensed milk (adjust to taste)
- Pinch of salt

## B) Gelatin
- **12 g** gelatin powder + **60 ml** cold water  
  OR 6 gelatin sheets (approx. 12 g)

## C) Optional toppings (pick 1–2)
- Condensed milk drizzle
- Tea jelly top (see below)
- Boba pearls (only if pickup is quick; boba hardens in fridge)
- Toasted sesame brittle shards (very premium crunch)

---

## Optional: Tea Jelly Top (looks amazing)
- **250 ml** strong brewed Thai tea
- **20 g** sugar
- **3 g** gelatin (or 1.5 sheets)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Bloom gelatin (5 minutes)
1. Sprinkle gelatin over cold water in a small bowl.
2. Let stand 5 minutes.

---

## 2) Infuse the cream (8 minutes)
1. In a saucepan, combine cream (and milk if using) + Thai tea mix + optional spice.
2. Heat on medium until steaming (do not boil).
3. Turn off heat and steep **5 minutes**.
4. Strain through a fine strainer back into the saucepan.

---

## 3) Sweeten + set (3 minutes)
1. Add sugar, condensed milk, and pinch of salt.
2. Warm gently until sugar dissolves.
3. Add bloomed gelatin and whisk until fully dissolved (no lumps).
4. Taste and adjust sweetness.

---

## 4) Pour into cups (2 minutes)
- Pour into 10 cups (120–150 ml each).
- Chill 4–6 hours until set.

---

## 5) Optional tea jelly top (extra premium)
1. Bloom 3 g gelatin in 15 ml water.
2. Warm strong tea + sugar, dissolve gelatin.
3. Cool to room temp, then pour a thin layer on top of set panna cotta.
4. Chill 1–2 hours.

---

## Quality Checks
- Clean set; panna cotta should wobble gently.
- Clear tea flavor + condensed-milk sweetness.
- No grainy gelatin lumps (strain if needed).
