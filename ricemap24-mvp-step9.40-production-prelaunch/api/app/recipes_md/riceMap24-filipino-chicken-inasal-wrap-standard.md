# Filipino Chicken Inasal Wrap (Calamansi + Lemongrass + Garlic) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A Filipino-inspired wrap built for lunch sales: **chicken inasal-style** (calamansi/lemongrass/garlic) with a bright slaw and garlic rice option.  
Fusion (wrap format), but the flavor profile is distinctly Filipino.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Filipino-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Chicken inasal (Visayas)
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~35–45 minutes (includes quick slaw)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **New + unique** (Filipino flavor is underrepresented).
- **Very sellable** (grilled chicken + citrus + garlic).
- **Pickup-friendly** (slaw can be separate; sauce optional).
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra inasal glaze cup, add garlic rice side.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Chicken (inasal-style)
- **700 g** boneless chicken thighs (preferred), sliced or kept as fillets
- Salt + pepper
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (for pan-sear)

### Inasal marinade / glaze
- **90 ml** calamansi juice *(or use 60 ml lime + 30 ml orange as substitute)*
- **60 ml** coconut vinegar *(or rice vinegar)*
- **4 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** grated ginger
- **1 stalk** lemongrass, very finely minced *(or 1 Tbsp lemongrass paste)*
- **1.5 Tbsp** brown sugar
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce (optional; adds color/salt)
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper
- Optional: **1/2 tsp** turmeric (traditional color hint)

> Marinate **20–60 minutes**.

---

## B) “Atsara-style” quick slaw (Filipino pickle vibe)
This gives the wrap the Filipino identity + crunch.

- **250 g** shredded cabbage
- **1 carrot**, julienne
- **1 cucumber**, julienne (optional)
- **2 Tbsp** vinegar (coconut/rice)
- **1 Tbsp** sugar
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- Optional: a few raisins (classic atsara vibe, optional)

Toss and rest 10–15 minutes, then drain lightly.

---

## C) Garlic sauce (optional but very good)
- **120 ml** mayo
- **2 cloves** garlic, grated
- **1 tsp** calamansi/lime juice
- Pinch salt

(If you prefer more Filipino feel: add 1 tsp vinegar instead of citrus.)

---

## D) Wrap build
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps
- Lettuce leaves (recommended moisture barrier)
- Optional: sliced scallions
- Optional: chili oil or sliced chili (if you want heat)

**Optional “premium add-on” side**
- Garlic rice (sinangag) as a side box (very on-brand)

---

## Equipment
- Skillet or grill pan
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (optional)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Marinate chicken (20 minutes)
1. Mix calamansi, vinegar, garlic, ginger, lemongrass, sugar, pepper (+ soy, turmeric).
2. Toss chicken to coat.
3. Rest 20–60 minutes.

---

## 2) Make quick atsara slaw (10 minutes)
1. Toss cabbage, carrot (+ cucumber).
2. Add vinegar, sugar, salt.
3. Rest 10 minutes, then drain lightly.
4. Optional: add a few raisins for classic sweetness.

---

## 3) Cook chicken (10–12 minutes)
1. Heat skillet medium-high with oil.
2. Cook chicken in batches until browned and cooked through.
3. Optional: reduce leftover marinade in pan briefly to make a glaze (boil 1–2 min).

**Quality target:** caramelized edges, citrus-garlic aroma.

---

## 4) Make garlic sauce (1 minute)
- Mix mayo + garlic + calamansi + salt.

---

## 5) Assemble wraps
Per wrap:
1. Warm tortilla briefly.
2. Add lettuce barrier.
3. Spread 1–2 Tbsp garlic sauce (optional).
4. Add chicken (160–200 g cooked).
5. Add atsara slaw.
6. Finish with scallions or chili (optional).
7. Roll tight.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- Chicken: 160–200 g cooked
- Slaw: 60–90 g
- Sauce: 25–30 g

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Chicken: 1.8 kg
- Marinade: 2.5×
- Slaw: 600 g cabbage + 4 carrots
- Sauce: 300 ml mayo + garlic

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- Wrap A: tortilla + lettuce + chicken (rolled)
- Cup B: atsara slaw (customer adds) for max crunch
- Cup C: garlic sauce (optional)

**Label:** “Add slaw last. Extra calamansi available.”

---

## Storage
- Cooked chicken: 2 days refrigerated
- Slaw best same day
- Sauce 2–3 days refrigerated

---

## Allergens
- Soy (if soy sauce used)
- Egg (mayo)
- Wheat (tortilla)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce)
