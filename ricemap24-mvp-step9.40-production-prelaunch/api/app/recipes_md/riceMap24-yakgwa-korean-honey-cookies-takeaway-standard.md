# Yakgwa (Korean Honey Cookies / 약과) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A traditional Korean deep-fried honey cookie soaked in ginger syrup.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** no melting, giftable look, stays good for days, and sells well as **2-pack / 4-pack / 8-pack**.

**Reference recipe:** Maangchi — Yakgwa (Korean honey cookies)  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/yakgwa

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Korean
- **Style:** Fried pastry cookies soaked in honey-ginger syrup
- **Yield:** ~18–24 cookies (depends on size/mold)
- **Active time:** ~45–60 min
- **Total time:** ~3–6 hours (includes soaking + drying)
- **Skill level:** Medium (frying temp control + syrup soak)

---

## Why it’s great for RiceMap24
- **Shelf-stable dessert** (room temp), no refrigeration needed.
- **Premium look:** glossy amber syrup sheen + sesame/pine nut garnish.
- **High-margin add-on:** easy multipacks and gift boxes.
- **Batch-friendly:** fry once, soak, dry, sell for several days.

## Equipment
- Pot or deep pan for frying
- Thermometer (strongly recommended)
- Mixing bowl
- Rolling pin
- Cookie cutter or small mold (flower/round)  
- Slotted spoon
- Wire rack + tray (drying)
- Saucepan (syrup)

---

## Ingredients

## A) Cookie dough
- All-purpose flour
- Sesame oil (key flavor)
- Honey
- Rice wine (soju or cheongju) *(or dry sherry if needed)*
- Ginger juice (or grated ginger strained)
- Baking soda (small amount)
- Pinch salt
- Optional: cinnamon (very small, for warmth)

> Yakgwa’s signature flavor is sesame oil + honey + ginger.

---

## B) Syrup (soak)
- Honey
- Rice malt syrup (or more honey as substitute)
- Water
- Ginger slices or minced ginger

---

## C) Garnish (for “premium bakery” look)
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Chopped pine nuts (optional but very premium)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Set up wire rack for draining cookies.
2. Heat frying oil gradually (yakgwa starts at lower temp).
3. Make syrup and keep warm (not boiling).

---

## Step-by-step Method (Repeatable)

## 1) Make syrup (5–8 minutes)
1. In a saucepan, combine honey + rice malt syrup + water.
2. Warm until fully combined (do not boil hard).
3. Add ginger and turn off heat.
4. Keep warm while you fry.

**Syrup target:** pourable and glossy.

---

## 2) Mix dough (10 minutes)
1. In a bowl, combine flour + salt + baking soda (and a pinch of cinnamon if using).
2. Add sesame oil + honey + rice wine + ginger juice.
3. Mix until a dough forms. Knead briefly until smooth.
4. Rest 10–15 minutes (covered) so flour hydrates.

**Dough texture target:** firm but pliable, not sticky.

---

## 3) Roll and cut (10–15 minutes)
1. Roll dough to about **8–10 mm** thickness (yakgwa is thicker than cookies).
2. Cut with molds/cutters.
3. Optional (traditional layering): score/cut shallow lines on the surface for texture and syrup absorption.

**Consistency tip:** keep sizes uniform for even frying.

---

## 4) Fry low, then higher (critical)
Yakgwa is usually fried in two stages: low heat to cook through, then higher heat to color.

### Oil temperatures
- Stage 1: **120–140°C**
- Stage 2: **150–165°C**

### Frying workflow
1. Fry cookies in small batches at stage-1 temp until they **float** and feel cooked through (time varies by size).
2. Raise temp to stage-2 and continue frying until golden.
3. Drain briefly on rack.

> Do not rush: if oil is too hot early, outside browns before inside cooks.

---

## 5) Soak in syrup (30–60 minutes)
1. While cookies are still warm, place them into warm syrup.
2. Soak until well infused (turn once or twice).
3. Remove to a rack to drip and dry.

---

## 6) Dry and finish (1–3 hours)
1. Let cookies sit on rack until surface is glossy but not dripping.
2. Sprinkle sesame seeds and/or pine nuts while slightly tacky.

**Best selling texture:** syrupy-chewy inside with a lightly firm outer layer.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Inside:** cooked and tender, not raw/doughy.
- **Outside:** golden, glossy, not oily.
- **Syrup:** absorbed; cookie feels “juicy” when bitten.

## RiceMap24 Menu Formats (High-performing)
- **2-pack** (impulse add-on)
- **4-pack** (most popular)
- **8-pack gift box** (premium)
- Optional “Hangwa mix” box (yakgwa + another baked item)

**Portioning suggestion**
- Small yakgwa: 35–45 g each
- Large yakgwa: 55–70 g each

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Let cookies dry before boxing (prevents sticking).
- Use parchment between layers.
- Best box: small bakery clamshell or paper dessert box with divider.

**Label suggestion:** “Best at room temperature. Keep airtight.”

---

## Storage
- Airtight at room temp: **3–5 days**
- Refrigerated: up to **1 week** (bring to room temp before eating)
- Freeze: **1 month** (thaw room temp)

---

## Allergens
- Wheat (flour)
- Sesame (sesame oil, sesame seeds)
