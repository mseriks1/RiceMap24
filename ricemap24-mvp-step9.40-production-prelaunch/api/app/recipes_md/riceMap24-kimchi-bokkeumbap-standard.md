# Kimchi Bokkeumbap (Korean Kimchi Fried Rice) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A fast, high-margin Korean fried rice using well-fermented kimchi. Ideal for a “spicy comfort bowl” and easy to batch for home-kitchen service.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Kimchi fried rice (Kimchi-bokkeumbap)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/kimchi-bokkeumbap

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Fried Rice • Spicy
- **Yield:** ~3 portions *(Maangchi base uses 3 cups cooked rice)*
- **Active time:** ~10 min  
- **Total time:** ~15–20 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (best results with cold day-old rice)

---

## Equipment
- Wok or large nonstick skillet
- Spatula
- Mixing bowl (optional)
- Measuring cups/spoons

---

## Ingredients (≈ 3 portions)

### Core
- **3 cups** cooked rice *(best: cold, day-old for separated grains)*
- **1 cup** chopped kimchi *(aged/sour is best)*
- **1/4 cup** kimchi juice *(from the kimchi container)*
- **1/4 cup** water
- **2–3 Tbsp** gochujang *(adjust to heat and saltiness of kimchi)*
- **3 tsp** toasted sesame oil
- **1 tsp** vegetable oil
- **1** green onion, chopped
- **1 Tbsp** roasted sesame seeds
- **1 sheet** gim (roasted seaweed), shredded into strips

*(Ingredient list follows the Maangchi recipe linked above.)*

---

## Prep (5–10 minutes)
1. **Rice:** If using fresh rice, spread on a tray to cool and dry slightly (reduces clumping).
2. **Kimchi:** Chop into small pieces (1–2 cm). Reserve **kimchi juice**.
3. **Gim:** Shred into thin strips (scissors work well).
4. **Green onion:** Chop; set aside for the final finish.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Start the pan
1. Heat a wok/skillet over **medium-high heat**.
2. Add **1 tsp vegetable oil** and swirl to coat.

### 2) Fry the kimchi (build flavor)
3. Add chopped kimchi and stir-fry for **1–2 minutes** until it smells fragrant and begins to soften.

### 3) Make the spicy base
4. Add **kimchi juice (1/4 cup)** and **water (1/4 cup)**.
5. Add **gochujang (2–3 Tbsp)** and stir until dissolved into a uniform red sauce.
6. Let it bubble for **30–60 seconds** so the sauce thickens slightly.

### 4) Add rice and fry
7. Add the cooked rice (3 cups). Break up clumps with the spatula.
8. Stir-fry **3–5 minutes**, pressing and tossing, until:
   - grains are separated,
   - rice is evenly coated,
   - excess liquid evaporates and the rice looks glossy rather than wet.

> If it looks too wet, keep the heat up and stir-fry longer.  
> If it’s too dry, add a small splash of water (1–2 Tbsp) and toss.

### 5) Finish
9. Turn off heat. Add **3 tsp toasted sesame oil** and toss to coat.
10. Add chopped green onion and toss briefly.
11. Plate and top with **sesame seeds** and **gim**.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Texture:** Rice should be separated, not mushy.
- **Moisture:** Not soupy; sauce should be absorbed/coating.
- **Flavor:** Tangy-spicy; adjust gochujang next batch if needed.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is ~3 portions. Multiply by **3.33×** for ~10 portions.

### Ingredients (≈ 10 portions)
- Cooked rice: **10 cups**
- Chopped kimchi: **3 1/3 cups**
- Kimchi juice: **~0.83 cup** *(≈ 200 ml)*
- Water: **~0.83 cup** *(≈ 200 ml)*
- Gochujang: **6 2/3 to 10 Tbsp** *(taste/heat dependent)*
- Toasted sesame oil: **10 tsp** *(= 3 Tbsp + 1 tsp)*
- Vegetable oil: **3 1/3 tsp** *(≈ 1 Tbsp + a bit)*
- Green onion: **3–4**
- Roasted sesame seeds: **3 1/3 Tbsp**
- Gim: **3–4 sheets**

### Batch notes
- Cook in **2 batches** to keep heat high and prevent steaming.
- Use **cold rice** for consistent texture.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Pack as a **single bowl** (rice is the main).
- Keep **gim** separate (small paper cup) so it stays crisp.

**Holding time:** Best within **2 hours** (gim added at serving).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Microwave (medium-high) or quick pan toss.  
  Add a teaspoon of water if rice is dry.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Sesame (sesame oil, sesame seeds)
- **May contain:** Fish/shellfish (many kimchi recipes include fish sauce or shrimp)
- **Gluten:** Possible (some gochujang brands)

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- “Old, sour kimchi” is a feature, not a bug—it makes the dish.
- Cold rice = better separation.
- Add sesame oil **off heat** to keep aroma fresh.
