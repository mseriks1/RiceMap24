# Agedashi Tofu (Japanese Crispy Tofu in Dashi Broth) — RiceMap24 Side Order Standard

A restaurant-style Japanese side: **crispy-fried tofu** served in a light, savory **dashi broth**, topped with grated daikon, scallions, and optional bonito flakes.  
Great for add-on sales because it feels premium, looks beautiful, and pairs with almost any bowl.

**Reference recipe:** Just One Cookbook — Agedashi Tofu  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/agedashi-tofu/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Side order / appetizer
- **Yield:** 4 side portions (or 8 small add-on cups)
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~30 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (crisp frying + hot broth)

---

## Best Ways to Sell It (RiceMap24)
- **Side order (standard):** 4 tofu cubes + toppings + broth
- **Add-on cup (small):** 2 tofu cubes + broth
- **Premium upgrade:** extra grated daikon + extra broth cup
- **Vegetarian option:** use kombu-shiitake broth (no bonito)

## Equipment
- Small pot (broth)
- Deep pan/pot for frying (or shallow fry in skillet)
- Paper towels / rack (drain)
- Knife + cutting board
- Small grater (for daikon)
- Slotted spoon/tongs

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Tofu
- **600 g** silken tofu (or soft tofu), drained
- **80–100 g** potato starch (katakuriko) or cornstarch (for coating)
- Neutral oil for frying

> Silken/soft gives the iconic contrast: crispy outside, silky inside. Handle gently.

---

## B) Broth (Tentsuyu-style)
- **240 ml** dashi (see quick dashi below)
- **30 ml** soy sauce (2 Tbsp)
- **30 ml** mirin (2 Tbsp)

### Quick Dashi Options
**Option 1 (fastest):** instant dashi powder + water (follow package)  
**Option 2 (better):** kombu dashi (10–15 min)  
**Option 3 (vegetarian):** kombu + dried shiitake soak broth

---

## C) Toppings (make it photogenic)
- **Grated daikon** (about 1/2 cup)
- **2 scallions**, thinly sliced
- Optional: **katsuobushi** (bonito flakes)
- Optional: grated ginger
- Optional: chili threads or shichimi togarashi

---

## Prep (10 minutes)

### 1) Drain tofu properly (important)
1. Remove tofu from package and drain.
2. Wrap gently in paper towels.
3. Place on a tray with a small weight (plate + light can) for **10 minutes**.

> Too much water = soggy coating.

### 2) Make broth (5 minutes)
1. Combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin in a small pot.
2. Warm gently (do not boil hard). Keep hot.

### 3) Prep toppings
- Grate daikon and squeeze lightly (leave it moist, not dripping).
- Slice scallions.

---

## Cooking Method (Crispy + Silky)

### 1) Cut + coat tofu
1. Cut tofu into **12–16 cubes** (about 4 cubes per portion).
2. Spread potato starch in a tray.
3. Coat tofu cubes gently on all sides. Shake off excess.

### 2) Fry tofu
**Oil temp target:** 170–180°C

1. Heat oil.
2. Fry tofu in small batches (don’t crowd).
3. Fry **2–3 minutes** until pale-golden and crisp.
4. Lift out to a rack/paper towels.

> Don’t over-brown—agedashi tofu should be lightly golden.

---

## Serve (Restaurant Style)
1. Place tofu cubes in a shallow bowl.
2. Ladle hot broth around (not directly on top if you want maximum crisp).
3. Top with grated daikon + scallions + optional bonito flakes.
4. Serve immediately.

## Quality Checks
- **Outside:** crisp coating
- **Inside:** silky tofu
- **Broth:** savory, lightly sweet, not too salty
- **Look:** clean broth, bright toppings

---

## Portioning (Side Order)
- Standard side: 4 tofu cubes + 60–80 ml broth + toppings
- Small add-on cup: 2 cubes + 40–50 ml broth + toppings

---

## Scaling (10 side orders)
- Tofu: **1.5 kg**
- Starch: **250 g** (adjust)
- Broth: **600–800 ml**
- Toppings: scale as needed

**Ops tip:** Keep broth hot in a thermos pot. Fry tofu in small batches for crisp consistency.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended (Critical)
To keep crisp:
- Container A: fried tofu cubes (vented)
- Cup B: hot broth (sealed)
- Cup C: toppings (daikon + scallions)

Label: “Pour broth around tofu, add toppings, eat right away.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- If needed: re-crisp tofu in air fryer 190°C for 3–5 minutes (broth reheated separately).

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Fish (if using bonito/standard dashi)
- Gluten possible (some soy sauces)
