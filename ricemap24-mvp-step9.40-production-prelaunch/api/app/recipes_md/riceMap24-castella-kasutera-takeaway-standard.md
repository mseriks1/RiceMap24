# Japanese Castella Cake (Kasutera / カステラ) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A classic Japanese **honey sponge cake** with a fine, bouncy crumb.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** no melting, travels well, slices cleanly, and actually gets better after resting (great for next-day sales).

**Reference recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Castella Cake (カステラ)”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/castella/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Japanese
- **Style:** Honey sponge loaf (sliced dessert)
- **Yield:** 1 loaf (8–12 slices depending on thickness)
- **Active time:** ~20–25 min
- **Total time:** ~60–75 min (plus resting time)
- **Skill level:** Medium (egg foam + bake control)

---

## Why it’s great for RiceMap24
- **Shelf-stable** for pickup (room temp).
- **Photogenic:** clean yellow crumb + optional coarse sugar base.
- **High-margin:** sell by slice, 4-slice box, or whole loaf.
- **Batch-friendly:** bake multiple loaves, portion, and sell next day.

## Equipment
- Loaf pan (recommended 9×5 inch / 23×13 cm) OR an 8-inch square pan
- Parchment paper (line fully; castella is sticky)
- Mixing bowls
- Hand mixer / stand mixer (recommended)
- Fine sieve (for flour)
- Spatula (folding)
- Kitchen scale (strongly recommended for consistent results)
- Oven thermometer (optional but helpful)

---

## Ingredients (1 loaf)

### Batter
- **160 g** bread flour *(or all-purpose flour if needed; bread flour gives the classic bouncy crumb)*
- **4 large eggs**, room temperature
- **140 g** sugar
- **2 Tbsp** honey
- **1 Tbsp** warm water (to thin honey)
- Optional: **1–2 Tbsp** coarse sugar (zarame) for the bottom crust

### Pan
- Neutral oil or butter (lightly grease under parchment)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)

### 1) Pan setup (important)
1. Line the pan fully with parchment (bottom + sides).
2. If using coarse sugar, sprinkle it evenly on the parchment bottom.

### 2) Flour
- Sift bread flour at least once (twice is even better).

### 3) Honey mix
- Stir honey with 1 Tbsp warm water until smooth.

### 4) Oven
- Preheat to **160–170°C** (depends on your oven; follow reference timing and adjust after a test bake).
- Place rack in the middle.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

### 1) Warm eggs (optional but helps volume)
- If eggs are cold, place them (in shells) in warm water 5 minutes, then dry.

### 2) Make the egg foam (most important step)
1. Combine eggs and sugar in a mixing bowl.
2. Whip with a mixer until the mixture becomes pale, thick, and ribbon-like.
   - **Ribbon test:** batter falls in a ribbon that sits on the surface for 2–3 seconds before disappearing.

> Under-whipping = dense cake. Over-whipping = unstable foam. Aim for the ribbon stage.

### 3) Add honey
- Pour in honey mixture and whisk briefly (low speed) just to combine.

### 4) Fold in flour
1. Sift flour into the bowl in 2–3 additions.
2. Fold gently with a spatula (bottom to top) until no dry streaks remain.
3. Do not overmix (you’ll deflate the foam).

### 5) Pour and remove large bubbles
1. Pour batter into lined pan from a low height (helps minimize bubbles).
2. Tap pan on the counter 2–3 times to release large bubbles.
3. Optional: run a skewer through batter in a few slow figure-8 motions.

---

## Bake (40–55 minutes)
1. Bake until the top is deep golden and a skewer comes out clean.
2. If top browns too fast, tent lightly with foil for the last 10–15 minutes.

**Doneness check:**
- Skewer mostly clean
- Top is richly browned
- Cake springs back when gently pressed

---

## Cooling + “Best Next Day” Rest (very important)
1. Remove from oven and drop the pan once from a very low height (helps prevent shrink).
2. Cool 10 minutes in pan.
3. Lift out using parchment and cool completely on a rack.
4. Wrap the cooled cake tightly in plastic wrap.
5. Rest **at least 8–12 hours** (overnight) before slicing for best texture and clean slices.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Crumb:** fine, moist, bouncy (not crumbly).
- **Top/bottom:** evenly browned; bottom may have crunchy sugar if used.
- **Slices:** clean edges after resting.

## RiceMap24 Menu Formats (High-performing)

### Portioning
- Slice thickness: **2–2.5 cm** for premium “bakery” slices.
- Target slice weight: **80–120 g** depending on loaf size.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Slice only after resting (better look).
- Use parchment between slices.
- Keep airtight to prevent drying.

**Label suggestion:** “Best at room temperature. Even better the next day.”

---

## Storage
- Airtight at room temp: **2–3 days**
- Refrigerated: **up to 1 week** (bring to room temp before eating)
- Freeze slices: **1 month** (thaw at room temp)

---

## Allergens
- Wheat (flour)
- Egg
- Honey
