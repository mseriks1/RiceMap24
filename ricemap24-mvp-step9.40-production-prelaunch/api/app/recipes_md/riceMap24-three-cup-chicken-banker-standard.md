# Three Cup Chicken (三杯鸡 / San Bei Ji) — Taiwanese-Style “Banker Dish” — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A famous Taiwanese/Chinese-style “banker”: chicken braised in a powerful 1:1:1 base of **soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and sesame oil**, finished with lots of **garlic, ginger, and Thai basil**.  
For RiceMap24, it’s perfect because it’s **one-pan**, **high aroma**, **photogenic**, and delivers well over rice.

**Reference recipe (recommended):** The Woks of Life — Three Cup Chicken (San Bei Ji)  
https://thewoksoflife.com/three-cup-chicken/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Taiwanese / Chinese
- **Style:** Braised-glazed chicken (one-pan) served with rice
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~45–55 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (reduce sauce to a glaze)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **High repeat-order potential:** sweet-salty, garlicky, basil aroma.
- **Batch-friendly:** one pot, consistent portioning.
- **Delivery-friendly:** sauce glazes chicken (not watery), reheats well.
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra sauce glaze, add mushrooms, add egg, add greens.

---

## Choose Your Chicken (Pick ONE)
### Option A (best banker): Bone-in chicken thighs/drumsticks
- **2.0 kg** bone-in chicken pieces  
More flavor and stays juicy after reheat.

### Option B (faster prep): Boneless chicken thigh
- **1.5 kg** boneless thighs, large chunks

> Banker choice: **bone-in thighs**.

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) The “Three Cups” sauce base
Traditional idea: equal parts soy, wine, sesame oil.  
For consistent RiceMap24 results (less greasy), use:

- **180 ml** light soy sauce
- **180 ml** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- **120 ml** toasted sesame oil *(slightly less than equal parts to avoid excess oil)*
- **60–90 g** rock sugar (or 4–6 Tbsp sugar), to taste

## B) Aromatics (this dish uses a lot — that’s the identity)
- **25–30 cloves** garlic, smashed (yes, lots)
- **60 g** ginger, thick slices (about a big hand’s worth)

## C) Basil + finish
- **2–3 cups** Thai basil leaves (holy basil also works)
- **2–3** long red chilies, sliced (optional, for color and mild heat)
- Optional: 1 tsp black pepper

## D) Oil for browning (optional)
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (only if your pan is dry)

## To serve
- Jasmine rice: **250 g cooked** per order

---

## Equipment
- Clay pot, wok, or heavy skillet with lid
- Tongs/spatula
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring cup
- Ladle

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Smash garlic (leave cloves mostly whole).
2. Slice ginger thick.
3. Mix sauce base in a jug: soy + wine + sesame oil + sugar.
4. Pick Thai basil leaves.
5. Portion rice and prepare containers.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Brown chicken (8–10 minutes)
1. Heat pot over **medium-high**.
2. Add chicken skin-side down (no need for much oil).
3. Brown until golden on both sides (work in batches if needed).
4. Remove excess rendered fat if there’s too much (leave ~1 Tbsp).

> Browning adds depth and helps the final glaze look premium.

---

## Step 2 — Add aromatics (2 minutes)
1. Add smashed garlic and ginger.
2. Stir 1–2 minutes until fragrant (don’t burn garlic).

---

## Step 3 — Braise in “three cups” (25–35 minutes)
1. Pour in sauce mixture.
2. Bring to a simmer.
3. Cover partially and simmer:
   - Bone-in pieces: **25–35 minutes** until cooked through
   - Boneless chunks: **15–20 minutes**

Stir occasionally so nothing sticks.

**Doneness check:** chicken reaches **74°C** at thickest point.

---

## Step 4 — Reduce to a glossy glaze (8–12 minutes)
1. Remove lid fully.
2. Increase heat to medium-high.
3. Simmer until sauce reduces and becomes glossy and sticky, coating chicken.
4. Stir more often near the end to avoid burning sugar.

**Glaze target:** thick enough to cling; you should see oil droplets but not a pool of oil.

---

## Step 5 — Finish with basil (30 seconds)
1. Turn off heat.
2. Add Thai basil and chili slices.
3. Toss just until basil wilts and becomes intensely fragrant.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sauce:** glossy glaze (not watery).
- **Aroma:** garlic + ginger + sesame + basil.
- **Chicken:** juicy, not dry.
- **Look:** shiny dark glaze with bright green basil.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Chicken + glaze: **300–380 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Basil garnish: visible on top (important for photos)

**Upsells**
- Extra chicken (double)
- Extra glaze cup
- Add mushrooms (braise them in the last 10 minutes)
- Add greens side

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Chicken bone-in: ~3.4 kg
- Soy: ~300 ml
- Wine: ~300 ml
- Sesame oil: ~200 ml
- Sugar: ~120–150 g
- Garlic: ~45 cloves
- Ginger: ~100 g
Cook in a wide pot to reduce faster.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: chicken + glaze (leak-proof; not watery)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Optional cup: extra glaze
- Optional garnish cup: extra basil (add after reheat)

Label: “Reheat gently. Add basil garnish last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat gently; add a splash of water if glaze becomes too thick.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Sesame (sesame oil)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce, Shaoxing)
