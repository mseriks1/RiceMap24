# Filipino Salpicao-Style Garlic Pepper Stir-Fry — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Filipino-inspired “salpicao” style dish: **lots of garlic**, a **Worcestershire–soy** sauce, and a bright **lime finish**.  
This version is **European-friendly** (savory, garlicky, peppery—no strong fermented aromas) and very photogenic (glossy sauce + scallion greens).

**Source recipe (base):** Christopher Kimball’s Milk Street — *Stir-Fried Garlic Beef with Scallions*  
https://www.177milkstreet.com/recipes/stir-fried-garlic-beef

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino-inspired
- **Style:** Fast stir-fry, thin sauce (great over rice)
- **Yield:** 4 servings
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min
- **Total time:** ~35 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (high-heat sear + quick reduction)

---

## Choose Your Protein (Pick ONE, ~450 g / 1 lb)
Keep the same weight for consistent food cost.

### Option A — Beef (original-style)
- 450 g beef sirloin tips or tri-tip, cut into **2.5 cm (1-inch)** chunks

### Option B — Chicken
- 450 g chicken thigh, cut into 2.5 cm chunks (or thin strips)

### Option C — Pork
- 450 g pork tenderloin/loin, cut into 2.5 cm chunks

### Option D — Shrimp
- 450 g large shrimp, peeled and deveined  
  *(Cook faster; see timing.)*

### Option E — Tofu (vegetarian)
- 450–500 g firm tofu, pressed and cubed (2.5 cm)  
  *(Sear to golden first.)*

---

## Equipment
- 30 cm / 12-inch skillet or wok
- Tongs / spatula
- 2 mixing bowls (protein + sauce)
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring spoons

---

## Ingredients (4 servings)

### Protein seasoning (base)
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1/2 tsp ground black pepper (plus more to taste)

### Main sauce (mix in a bowl)
- 5 Tbsp soy sauce
- 6 Tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 Tbsp packed brown sugar

### Aromatics + veg (highly recommended for looks)
- 3 Tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 8 medium garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 bunch scallions: whites chopped, greens thinly sliced (keep separate)
- 1 medium red bell pepper, thinly sliced
- Lime wedges, to serve

> Photo tip: Red pepper + scallion greens make the bowl look “fresh” and premium.

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Cut protein** into 2.5 cm chunks (or shrimp ready).
2. In a bowl, toss protein with **1 Tbsp soy sauce + 1/2 tsp black pepper**.
3. In a second bowl, mix **5 Tbsp soy + Worcestershire + brown sugar** until sugar dissolves.
4. Slice garlic, scallions, and bell pepper. Keep scallion greens separate.
5. Prepare rice (serve with steamed rice).

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Repeatable)

### Step 1 — Sear protein (high heat)
1. Heat skillet over **high**.
2. Add **2 Tbsp oil** and heat until barely smoking.
3. Add protein in a **single layer** (work in 2 batches if needed).

**Timing by protein**
- Beef chunks: sear **1–2 min** without stirring until browned; stir briefly and remove (not fully cooked is okay).
- Chicken/pork chunks: sear **2–3 min** total until mostly cooked; remove.
- Shrimp: sear **30–60 sec** until just turning pink; remove early.
- Tofu: sear **2–3 min per side** to golden; remove.

Transfer seared protein to a plate.

### Step 2 — Garlic + veg + sauce reduction
1. Reduce heat to **medium**.
2. Add remaining **1 Tbsp oil**.
3. Add garlic slices, scallion whites, and bell pepper; cook **~30 seconds** until fragrant.
4. Pour in the Worcestershire–soy mixture **plus any juices** from the protein plate.
5. Bring to a simmer, scraping browned bits.
6. Simmer **2–4 minutes** until a spatula leaves a trail in the sauce (slightly thickened).

### Step 3 — Return protein + finish
1. Return protein to skillet.
2. Toss **~30 seconds** (or until heated through / shrimp fully pink).
3. Turn off heat.
4. Stir in scallion greens and add **1/2 tsp black pepper** (or to taste).
5. Serve with lime wedges.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Garlic:** fragrant, not burnt (medium heat step).
- **Sauce:** glossy, slightly thick but still pourable for rice.
- **Protein:** tender (don’t overcook; especially beef/shrimp).
- **Finish:** lime wedge makes it bright and “restaurant.”

- Add fried egg (very popular in “rice bowl” menus)

---

## Optional Premium Add-on: Garlic Chips (looks amazing)
1. Slice extra garlic thin.
2. Fry in a small pan with oil over **low** heat until golden.
3. Drain well and sprinkle on top at service.

Keep separate for delivery so it stays crisp.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
Multiply everything by **2.5×**.
- Protein: ~**1.1–1.25 kg** (depending on portion size)
- Sauce: 2.5× all sauce ingredients
- Garlic: 2.5×
- Cook in **small batches** to keep sear quality.
