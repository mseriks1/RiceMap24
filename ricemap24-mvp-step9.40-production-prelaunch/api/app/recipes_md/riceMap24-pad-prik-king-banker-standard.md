# Pad Prik King (Thai Red Curry Stir-Fry with Chicken & Long Beans) — “Banker Dish” — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Thai “dry curry” stir-fry: chicken and long beans coated in red curry paste with a sweet-salty balance, finished with makrut (kaffir) lime leaves.  
For RiceMap24, this is a fantastic **banker** because it’s fast, scalable, super “Thai,” and delivers well (not soupy, stays vibrant).

Reference recipe used for structure: Hot Thai Kitchen (Pad Prik King / Red Curry Chicken Stir-Fry).

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Core
- **3–4 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **100–130 g** Thai red curry paste *(about 6–8 Tbsp; varies by brand)*
- **450–600 g** yardlong beans (sitaw) **or** green beans, cut 4–5 cm
- **6 makrut/kaffir lime leaves**, very finely sliced (remove center rib)

## B) Seasoning (Thai balance)
Start here, then taste-adjust:
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **2 Tbsp** oyster sauce
- **1.5–2 Tbsp** palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- **3–6 Tbsp** water/stock (to loosen paste and glaze)

## C) Optional add-ins (nice for sales + photos)
- 1 red bell pepper, thin strips
- 1–2 red spur chilies, thin slices (garnish)
- Crispy fried egg add-on (very popular)

## To serve
- Jasmine rice: **250 g cooked** per order

---

## Equipment
- Wok or large skillet
- Spatula/tongs
- Knife + cutting board
- Small bowl for seasoning mix
- Optional: mortar & pestle (if making fresh paste, not required)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Slice chicken.
2. Cut beans into 4–5 cm pieces.
3. Finely slice makrut lime leaves (thin ribbons look premium).
4. Mix seasoning in a bowl: fish sauce + oyster sauce + sugar + water.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

### Step 1 — Fry curry paste (1–2 minutes)
1. Heat wok over **medium-high**.
2. Add oil.
3. Add red curry paste and stir-fry 60–90 seconds until very fragrant (don’t burn).

> If paste sticks, add 1–2 Tbsp water and keep stirring.

### Step 2 — Cook chicken (6–8 minutes)
1. Add chicken.
2. Stir-fry until mostly cooked and coated in paste.
   - Thigh: ~6–8 minutes
   - Breast: ~4–6 minutes

### Step 3 — Add beans + glaze (3–5 minutes)
1. Add long beans/green beans.
2. Stir-fry 2–3 minutes.
3. Pour in the seasoning mixture.
4. Stir-fry until sauce reduces and clings (glossy, not watery).

**Bean texture target:** tender-crisp (still green, not dull).

### Step 4 — Finish (20 seconds)
1. Add sliced makrut lime leaves and toss 10–20 seconds.
2. Turn off heat.
3. Optional: top with red chili slices for color.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Coating:** glossy red curry glaze, not soupy.
- **Chicken:** tender, not dry.
- **Beans:** still green and crisp-tender.
- **Aroma:** curry paste fragrance + citrusy lime leaf lift.

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Stir-fry: **220–280 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Optional fried egg: 1 (upsell)

**Upsells**
- Extra chicken (double protein)
- Extra sauce/glaze cup (high-margin)
- Add fried egg
- Add extra beans or bell pepper
- Spice level: Mild / Medium / Hot (via paste amount)

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Chicken thigh: ~2.0 kg
- Beans: ~800–1,000 g
- Curry paste: ~170–220 g (adjust heat)
- Seasoning: 1.7×

**Ops tip:** Cook chicken in batches if your pan is small; finish each batch with beans + glaze so beans stay crisp and bright.

- Container B: rice (separate)

## Storage & Reheat
- Best same day.
- Refrigerate up to 2 days.
- Reheat quickly in a hot pan or microwave briefly (overheating softens beans).

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Shellfish (oyster sauce)
- Gluten possible (oyster sauce brand)
