# Chả Cá (Vietnamese Turmeric & Dill Fish) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Hanoi-style fish dish that looks incredible: golden turmeric fish with heaps of dill and scallions, served with rice vermicelli and a bright **nuoc cham** dipping sauce.  
It’s not the “default takeaway” everywhere (like pho/bánh mì), but it’s extremely sellable as a premium bowl set.

**Source recipe (reference):** Marion’s Kitchen — Vietnamese Turmeric & Dill Fish “Cha Ca”  
https://www.marionskitchen.com/vietnamese-turmeric-dill-fish-cha-ca/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese (Hanoi-style)
- **Style:** Pan-seared turmeric fish + herbs + vermicelli + nuoc cham
- **Yield:** 4 servings
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~30–45 min (plus optional marinating)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (fish timing + herb finish)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24
- **Looks premium:** golden fish + bright green dill/scallions + red chili + glossy nuoc cham.
- **Fast service:** noodles + sauce can be prepped, fish cooks quickly.
- **Upsells:** extra fish, extra nuoc cham, extra herbs, peanuts, “spicy” chili cup.

---

## Equipment
- Large nonstick or stainless pan
- Mixing bowl (marinade)
- Knife + cutting board
- Kettle (for vermicelli)
- Small bowl (nuoc cham)
- Tongs/spatula

---

## Ingredients (Serves 4)

### A) Noodles
- **180 g** dried rice vermicelli

### B) Fish + finish
- **800 g** white fish fillets, cut into **4 cm** wide strips (firm white fish works best)
- **2 Tbsp** vegetable oil (for pan)
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1** long red chili, finely sliced
- **3** spring onions (scallions), cut into batons
- **3 sprigs** dill, cut into large pieces (remove thick stems)

### C) Marinade
- **1/3 cup** sliced spring onion
- **3** garlic cloves
- **1/4 cup** fish sauce
- **1 Tbsp** turmeric
- **1 tsp** curry powder
- **1 tsp** sugar
- **2 Tbsp** vegetable oil
- **2 Tbsp** finely chopped dill

### D) Nuoc Cham Dressing (dipping sauce)
- **3 Tbsp** white sugar
- **3 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **2 Tbsp** white vinegar
- **2** garlic cloves, finely chopped
- **1** long red chili, deseeded and finely chopped
- **2 Tbsp** lime juice

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Marinate fish (10–30 minutes recommended)
1. Blitz or finely chop the marinade aromatics:
   - sliced spring onion + garlic
2. Mix with fish sauce, turmeric, curry powder, sugar, oil, and chopped dill.
3. Coat fish strips evenly and rest 10–30 minutes (refrigerated if longer).

> This gives the signature color and fragrance. If you’re in a rush, even 10 minutes helps.

### 2) Nuoc cham (5 minutes)
1. Stir sugar, fish sauce, vinegar until sugar dissolves.
2. Add chopped garlic and chili.
3. Add lime juice.
4. Taste: it should be bold (sweet–salty–sour). Adjust lime/sugar if needed.

### 3) Vermicelli (2 minutes)
1. Soak vermicelli in **just-boiled water** for about **2 minutes** until tender.
2. Drain well and portion into 4 bowls.

> Drain well so bowls don’t get watery.

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Repeatable)

### 1) Pan-cook fish (6–10 minutes total)
1. Heat pan over **medium-high**.
2. Add 2 Tbsp oil.
3. Add fish (don’t crowd; do in batches if needed).
4. Cook until just cooked through and golden on the outside.  
   - Typical timing: **2–3 minutes per side** depending on thickness.

### 2) Finish with herbs + scallions (30–60 seconds)
1. Add scallion batons and dill pieces to the pan.
2. Toss gently 30–60 seconds until herbs wilt slightly and become fragrant.
3. Squeeze 1 Tbsp lime juice over and toss.

> Don’t overcook dill—it should stay bright and aromatic.

---

## How to Serve (Bowl Set)
Per bowl:
1. Vermicelli base
2. Top with fish + dill/scallions
3. Spoon nuoc cham over, or serve in a small cup (recommended for delivery)
4. Garnish with sliced red chili (optional)

Optional extras (highly recommended for a premium look):
- Roasted peanuts (crush and sprinkle)
- Extra herbs (mint/cilantro)
- Lime wedge

---

## Quality Checks
- **Fish:** tender, flakes easily, not dry.
- **Color:** golden yellow (turmeric), not pale.
- **Herbs:** bright and fragrant (not browned).
- **Nuoc cham:** balanced sweet–salty–sour, not one-note.

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Fish: **180–220 g**
- Vermicelli: **45 g dry** (≈ 1 portion)
- Herb finish: generous (this is the identity)
- Nuoc cham: **25–35 ml** cup

---

## Scaling for Production (10 orders)
Multiply recipe by **2.5×**:
- Vermicelli: 450 g dry
- Fish: 2.0 kg
- Marinade: 2.5×
- Nuoc cham: 2.5× (bottle and portion into 10 cups)

**Ops tip:** Pre-make nuoc cham and portion it. Marinate fish in trays and cook in batches.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
To keep it looking good:
- Container A: vermicelli
- Container B: fish + dill/scallions (warm)
- Cup C: nuoc cham (sealed)
- Optional cup: crushed peanuts + extra herbs

Label: “Pour nuoc cham over noodles and fish. Add peanuts last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best eaten fresh.
- If needed: reheat fish gently (pan or microwave low). Add fresh dill/scallions after reheating if possible.
- Nuoc cham keeps 3–5 days refrigerated.

---

## Allergens
- Fish
- Fish sauce (fish)
- Chili
