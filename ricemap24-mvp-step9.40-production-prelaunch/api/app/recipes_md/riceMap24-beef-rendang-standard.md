# Beef Rendang (Indonesian/Malaysian Dry Coconut Beef) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A rich, aromatic, slow-simmered beef dish cooked in coconut milk and a spice paste until the sauce reduces and the meat is coated in a thick, almost dry glaze. Excellent for premium rice boxes and next-day flavor.

**Source recipe:** Rasa Malaysia — “Beef Rendang (The Best!)”  
https://rasamalaysia.com/beef-rendang-recipe-rendang-daging/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Indonesian / Malaysian • Slow Simmer • Coconut & Spice
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 45 min (prep 15 min + cook 1 hr 30 min)
- **Skill level:** Medium (spice paste + long simmer; stirring to prevent scorching)

---

## Equipment
- Stew pot / Dutch oven with lid (heavy-bottomed recommended)
- Blender or food processor (for spice paste)
- Cutting board + knife
- Wooden spoon/spatula
- Measuring spoons/cups or scale

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)  
*(Measurements below are from the source recipe.)*

### Main pot
- **1 1/2 lb** boneless beef short ribs, cut into cubes *(~680 g)*
- **5 Tbsp** cooking oil
- **1** cinnamon stick (about 2-inch length)
- **3** cloves
- **3** star anise
- **3** cardamom pods
- **1** lemongrass, cut into 4-inch length and pounded *(this is separate from the paste)*
- **1 cup** thick coconut milk / coconut cream
- **1 cup** water
- **2 tsp** tamarind pulp, soaked in warm water for juice; discard seeds
- **6** kaffir lime leaves, very finely sliced
- **6 Tbsp** kerisik (toasted coconut)
- **1 Tbsp** sugar (or palm sugar), to taste
- **Salt**, to taste

### Spice paste (blend until fine)
- **5** shallots
- **1 inch** galangal
- **3** lemongrass (white part only)
- **5** cloves garlic
- **1 inch** ginger
- **10–12** dried chilies, soaked in warm water and seeded

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Soak chilies:** Cover dried chilies with warm water until softened. Seed and drain.
2. **Cut beef:** Cube beef (avoid cutting too small; it can break apart after long cooking).
3. **Tamarind juice:** Soak tamarind pulp in warm water; squeeze to release juice; discard seeds.
4. **Slice kaffir lime leaves:** Very thinly (chiffonade).
5. **Make kerisik (if needed):** Toast grated coconut in a dry pan/wok, stirring until golden brown. *(Use ready-made kerisik if available.)*
6. **Blend spice paste:** Chop paste ingredients, then blend in a food processor until fine.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Fry the spice paste + whole spices
1. Heat **5 Tbsp oil** in a heavy stew pot over **medium heat**.
2. Add the **spice paste**, then add **cinnamon, cloves, star anise, cardamom**.
3. Stir-fry until aromatic (you should smell the spices clearly and see oil starting to separate slightly).

### 2) Add beef + aromatics
4. Add beef cubes and the **pounded lemongrass** (the 4-inch piece).
5. Stir for **about 1 minute** to coat and seal lightly.

### 3) Add liquids and simmer (medium heat)
6. Add **coconut milk**, **tamarind juice**, and **water**.
7. Simmer on **medium heat**, stirring frequently, until the meat is almost cooked.

### 4) Add finishing aromatics + toasted coconut
8. Add **kaffir lime leaves**, **kerisik**, and **sugar/palm sugar**. Stir well to blend.

### 5) Slow simmer to “dry” finish (low heat)
9. Reduce heat to **low**, cover with lid, and simmer **1 to 1 1/2 hours**, until:
   - beef is **very tender**, and  
   - the gravy has thickened and reduced (rendang is **not** watery).
10. Stir often near the end to prevent scorching.
11. Taste and adjust with **salt** and more **sugar** if needed.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Texture:** Meat is fork-tender; sauce is thick and clings to beef (not soupy).
- **Aroma:** Strong lemongrass + kaffir lime + toasted coconut notes.
- **Color:** Deep brown-red with oil sheen; not pale.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Main pot (10 portions)
- Beef: **3.75 lb** (~**1.7 kg**) boneless beef short ribs
- Cooking oil: **12.5 Tbsp**
- Cinnamon sticks: **2–3**
- Cloves: **7–8**
- Star anise: **7–8**
- Cardamom pods: **7–8**
- Pounded lemongrass: **2–3 stalks**
- Coconut cream: **2.5 cups**
- Water: **2.5 cups**
- Tamarind pulp: **5 tsp**
- Kaffir lime leaves: **15**
- Kerisik: **15 Tbsp**
- Sugar: **2.5 Tbsp**, to taste
- Salt: to taste

### Spice paste (10 portions)
- Shallots: **12–13**
- Galangal: **2.5 inches**
- Lemongrass (white part only): **7–8 stalks**
- Garlic: **12–13 cloves**
- Ginger: **2.5 inches**
- Dried chilies: **25–30**, soaked & seeded

### Batch notes
- Use a wide pot for better evaporation.
- In the final 30 minutes, stir more often to avoid burning (coconut + kerisik can stick).

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rice in base + rendang on top.
- Include **extra sauce** only if it’s still thick (rendang should be “dry” style).
- Great as a **premium bowl** because flavors improve after resting.

**Holding time:** Excellent; often better after **overnight** rest.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3–4 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer or microwave. Add a splash of water only if it has become too dry.

---

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Rendang is **not** curry: reduce until thick and clinging.
- Stir frequently, especially late in cooking, to prevent scorching.
- This is a perfect “cook today, sell tomorrow” dish.
