# Taiwanese Pineapple Cakes (Fengli Su / 凤梨酥) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A premium, shelf-stable Asian baked dessert: buttery shortbread “bars” filled with tangy pineapple jam.  
Perfect for **pickup/takeaway** because it travels well at room temperature, looks giftable, and keeps for days.

**Reference recipe:** Omnivore’s Cookbook — “Pineapple Cake (凤梨酥), A Simplified Version”  
(Use the reference for exact ingredient nuances if you want it identical.)

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Taiwanese
- **Style:** Baked shortbread cookie bars with pineapple filling
- **Yield:** ~12–16 small cakes/bars (depends on size)
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min
- **Total time:** ~2–3 hours (includes cooling + optional chill)
- **Skill level:** Medium (filling reduction + cookie handling)

---

## Why it’s great for RiceMap24
- **No melting / no refrigeration required** (best at room temp).
- **Looks premium**: golden glossy tops; perfect for photos.
- **High-margin add-on**: sell as 2-pack / 4-pack / gift box.
- **Batch-friendly**: bake once, sell for several days.

## Equipment
- Saucepan (for pineapple jam)
- Fine grater (for pineapple)
- Mixing bowl + spatula
- Baking tray + parchment
- Small scale (recommended for consistent portions)
- Optional: small square molds (nice) but not required

---

## Ingredients

### A) Pineapple filling (jam)
- 1 large ripe pineapple (or canned pineapple chunks, drained well)
- Sugar (adjust to pineapple sweetness)
- Lemon juice (brightness and set)
- Optional: pinch of salt
- Optional: small knob butter (richer filling)

> Goal: a **thick, scoopable paste** that won’t leak during baking.

### B) Shortbread dough
- Unsalted butter (softened)
- Sugar (powdered or fine)
- Egg yolk (richer texture)
- All-purpose flour
- Milk powder (optional but very “Taiwan bakery”)
- Pinch of salt

> Goal: dough that’s soft and workable, not sticky.

### C) Egg wash (for glossy top)
- 1 egg yolk + 1 tsp water (or milk)

---

## Prep Notes (Important)
- The filling must be **cool** before assembly.
- Portioning is the secret to a professional look: same size, same bake.

---

## Step 1 — Make Pineapple Filling (45–70 min depending on moisture)
1. **Prep pineapple**
   - Fresh: peel, remove core, grate finely (or mince in processor).
   - Canned: pulse briefly and drain excess liquid well.
2. **Cook down**
   - Add pineapple to a saucepan on medium heat.
   - Add sugar (start modest; you can add more later).
   - Simmer, stirring often, until liquid reduces.
3. **Finish**
   - Add lemon juice near the end.
   - Keep cooking until it becomes a thick paste that holds shape when you drag a spoon through.
4. **Cool completely**
   - Spread on a plate/tray to cool faster.

**Consistency check:** You should be able to roll the filling into balls without it sticking heavily to your hands.

---

## Step 2 — Make Shortbread Dough (10 min)
1. Cream softened butter + sugar until smooth.
2. Mix in egg yolk.
3. Add flour + salt (and milk powder if using).
4. Mix just until a dough forms (don’t overwork).
5. If too soft/sticky: chill 15–20 minutes.

---

## Step 3 — Portion + Assemble (15–25 min)
**Recommended sizing (consistent + sellable):**
- Dough per cake: **30–35 g**
- Filling per cake: **18–22 g**

1. Roll filling into balls (same weight each).
2. Divide dough portions (same weight each).
3. Flatten dough in your palm, wrap around filling, pinch seam closed.
4. Shape into a neat rectangle/bar:
   - With molds: press into molds.
   - Without molds: gently press into a tidy brick shape.

Place on a parchment-lined tray.

---

## Step 4 — Egg wash + Bake (20–28 min)
1. Brush tops lightly with egg wash.
2. Bake at **175°C** (convection 165°C) until golden.
3. Let cool 10 minutes on tray, then move to rack.

**Bake target:** golden top, lightly browned edges, firm enough to handle.

---

## Step 5 — “Next Day is Best” (optional but real)
Like many shortbread-style bakes, pineapple cakes often taste best after resting overnight:
- butter flavor settles
- filling firms up
- texture becomes more “bakery”

---

## Quality Checks
- **Filling:** thick, not leaking.
- **Crust:** crumbly but not dry.
- **Top:** evenly golden, glossy from egg wash.
- **Shape:** consistent (very important for selling).

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Cool completely before boxing (prevents condensation).
- Use paper cups or small dividers so cakes don’t rub and lose gloss.
- Include a small label: “Best at room temp.”

---

## Storage
- Airtight container at room temp: **3–5 days**
- Refrigerated: up to **1 week** (bring to room temp before serving)
- Freeze: **1–2 months** (thaw at room temp)

---

## Allergens
- Wheat (flour)
- Dairy (butter, milk powder)
- Egg (yolk/egg wash)
