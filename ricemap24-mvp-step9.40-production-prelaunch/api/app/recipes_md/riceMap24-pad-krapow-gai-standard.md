# Pad Krapow Gai (Spicy Thai Basil Chicken) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A fast Thai street-food style basil chicken stir-fry: finely chopped chicken, aromatics, a sweet-salty sauce, and basil stirred in at the end.

**Source recipe:** Allrecipes — “Spicy Thai Basil Chicken (Pad Krapow Gai)” (Chef John).  
https://www.allrecipes.com/recipe/257938/spicy-thai-basil-chicken-pad-krapow-gai/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai • Stir-Fry • Rice Bowl
- **Yield:** 2 portions
- **Active time:** ~15 min prep + ~10 min cook
- **Total time:** ~25 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (high-heat timing)

---

## Equipment
- Wok or large skillet (preferably heavy-bottomed)
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Cutting board + knife
- Measuring spoons/cups or scale

---

## Ingredients (2 portions)

### Sauce (whisk in a bowl)
- **80 ml** chicken broth *(⅓ cup)*
- **1 Tbsp** oyster sauce
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce *(“or as needed” per source; start with 1 Tbsp)*
- **2 tsp** fish sauce
- **1 tsp** white sugar
- **1 tsp** brown sugar

### Stir-fry
- **2 Tbsp** vegetable oil
- **454 g** boneless, skinless chicken thighs *(1 lb)*, coarsely chopped  
  - *Tip:* For best texture, mince/chop very fine with a knife (or use ground chicken to save time).
- **¼ cup** sliced shallots
- **4 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2 Tbsp** minced Thai chilies, serrano, or other hot pepper *(adjust to heat tolerance)*
- **1 cup** very thinly sliced basil leaves *(Thai/holy basil if available; sweet basil works)*

### To serve
- **2 cups** hot cooked rice

---

## Prep (Do this before turning on the heat)
1. **Make the sauce:** Whisk broth + oyster sauce + soy sauce + fish sauce + sugars until smooth.
2. **Chicken:** Chop chicken very fine (small pieces cook faster and cling to sauce better).
3. **Aromatics:** Slice shallots, mince garlic, mince chilies.
4. **Basil:** Slice leaves very thinly (ribbons). Keep separate—goes in at the end.

> Stir-fries move fast. Once the pan is hot, you won’t have time to prep.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Sear the chicken (high heat)
1. Heat a large skillet/wok over **high heat** until very hot.
2. Add **2 Tbsp oil**.
3. Add chicken and stir-fry until it **loses raw color**, **2–3 minutes**.

### 2) Aromatics + caramelization
4. Add shallots, garlic, and chilies. Keep cooking on **high heat** until some juices **caramelize** on the bottom of the pan, **2–3 minutes**.

### 3) Two-stage saucing (for better glaze)
5. Add about **1 Tbsp** of the sauce mixture. Cook and stir until it **starts to caramelize**, about **1 minute**.
6. Pour in the **remaining sauce**. Scrape the bottom to **deglaze**.
7. Cook and stir until sauce **glazes** the meat, **1–2 minutes**. Remove from heat.

### 4) Basil finish (off heat)
8. Stir in basil until **wilted**, about **20 seconds** (residual heat is enough).
9. Serve immediately with hot rice.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce glaze:** Should cling lightly to chicken, not watery → cook 30–60 sec longer next time (high heat).
- **Chicken texture:** Juicy, not dry → avoid overcooking during the glaze step.
- **Heat level:** Adjust chilies up/down; keep sauce ratios the same.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Cook in **2–3 batches** (or use a very large wok) to keep high heat and good caramelization.

### Ingredients (10 portions = 5×)
**Sauce**
- Chicken broth: **400 ml** *(1⅔ cups)*
- Oyster sauce: **5 Tbsp**
- Soy sauce: **5 Tbsp**
- Fish sauce: **10 tsp** *(3 Tbsp + 1 tsp)*
- White sugar: **5 tsp**
- Brown sugar: **5 tsp**

**Stir-fry**
- Vegetable oil: **10 Tbsp** *(≈ 150 ml)*
- Chicken thighs: **2,270 g** *(2.27 kg)*
- Shallots: **1¼ cups**
- Garlic: **20 cloves**
- Minced chilies: **10 Tbsp**
- Basil: **5 cups** thinly sliced

**To serve**
- Hot cooked rice: **10 cups**

### Batch notes
- Whisk sauce once (large bowl), then portion per batch.
- Keep the pan **smoking hot** before chicken goes in.
- Don’t add basil until **off heat**.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Best:** Rice in base + stir-fry on top, basil mixed in just before packing.
- **Optional add-on:** Fried egg (Kai Dao) packed separately if you offer it.

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** for peak basil aroma.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Cool quickly; store up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Medium power microwave or quick hot-pan toss. Avoid long reheats (basil dulls).

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
