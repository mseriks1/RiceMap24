# Kanom Tuay (Thai Coconut Pudding Cups / ขนมถ้วย) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A classic Thai **two-layer steamed coconut pudding**: sweet golden base + salty coconut top.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** served in small cups, no melting, keeps well chilled, and sells great as **2-pack / 4-pack / 6-pack**.

**Reference recipe:** Hungry in Thailand — “Thai Coconut Pudding Recipe (Kanom Tuay)” (updated May 16, 2025)  
https://hungryinthailand.com/thai-coconut-custard-dessert/

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Steamed dessert cups (two layers)
- **Yield (reference):** ~20 small cups

## Equipment
- Steamer with lid
- Small heatproof cups (Thai ceramic cups are traditional)
- 2 mixing bowls
- Whisk
- Fine mesh strainer/sieve (important for smooth layers)
- Spoon/ladle
- Optional: towel under steamer lid (reduces condensation drips)

---

## Ingredients (≈20 cups)

## A) Sweet bottom layer (golden)
- **1/2 cup** coconut sugar (or palm sugar)
- **1/2 cup** rice flour
- **2 Tbsp** tapioca starch
- **1/2 cup** water
- **1/2 cup** coconut milk
- **2 pandan leaves** (optional)

## B) Salty top layer (white)
- **1 cup** coconut milk
- **1/4 cup** water
- **3 Tbsp** rice flour
- **1/4 tsp** salt

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Set up steamer and bring to a steady steam.
2. Place empty cups in the steamer and warm them **10 minutes** (helps reduce sticking).
3. Measure all ingredients and prepare two bowls: one for each layer.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Make the sweet bottom layer
1. In a bowl, combine coconut sugar + water (+ pandan leaves if using).
2. Press/mix with your hand or spoon until the sugar dissolves as much as possible.
3. Add **1/2 cup coconut milk** and mix well.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk **rice flour + tapioca starch**.
5. Slowly pour the sugar-coconut mixture into the flour while whisking until smooth.
6. **Strain** through a sieve into a clean bowl (removes lumps).

### Fill and steam bottom layer
7. Remove warmed cups carefully (they’re hot) and place them back in the steamer tray.
8. Spoon **just over 1 Tbsp** bottom layer batter into each cup.
9. Steam **10 minutes** while you make the top layer.

**Bottom-layer set check:** surface looks set, not wet.

---

## 2) Make the salty top layer
1. In a clean bowl, whisk **3 Tbsp rice flour + 1/4 tsp salt**.
2. Gradually whisk in **1/4 cup water** and **1 cup coconut milk** until smooth.
3. **Strain** into a clean bowl.

### Fill and steam top layer
4. Once the bottom layer is set, gently spoon top layer onto each cup
   - Fill to just below the rim (traditional cups have a blue line).
5. Steam **5–10 minutes** until set.

**Top-layer set check:** surface looks firm and slightly matte, not liquid.

> Note from the reference: cracking on the top can happen; avoid over-steaming to limit cracks.

---

## Cool and Serve
1. Cool cups completely before boxing (prevents condensation).
2. Serve at room temp or chilled (chilled is best for clean layers and takeaway).

---

## Quality Checks
- **Bottom layer:** sweet, soft, lightly chewy (from tapioca).
- **Top layer:** creamy and slightly salty (key balance).
- **Texture:** no gritty flour lumps (strain both layers).
- **Look:** clean two layers.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Keep chilled for best stability.
- Pack cups in a tight dessert tray so they don’t tip.
- Add label: “Best chilled. Enjoy within 3 days.”

---

## Storage
- Refrigerated: **up to 3 days** for best freshness (reference guidance).
- Do not freeze (texture changes).

---

## Allergens
- Coconut
- Gluten-free (rice flour/tapioca starch)
