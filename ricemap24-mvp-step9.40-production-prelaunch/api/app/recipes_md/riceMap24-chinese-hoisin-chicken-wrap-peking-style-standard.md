# Chinese “Peking Duck” Style Chicken Wrap (Hoisin + Cucumber + Scallion) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A Chinese-inspired wrap based on the classic Peking duck flavor combo—adapted for home kitchens using **crispy chicken** (or roast chicken) instead of duck.  
It’s premium, photogenic, and perfect for lunch pickup.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Chinese-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Peking duck-style flavors
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~30–35 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Very recognizable flavor combo:** hoisin + cucumber + scallion.
- **Premium feel** without needing actual duck.
- **Pickup friendly:** keep sauce and crunchy veg separate if needed.
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra hoisin cup, add crispy wonton strips.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Chicken (choose one)
### Option 1 (premium crispy): Crispy chicken strips
- **700 g** chicken thigh/breast strips
- Salt + pepper

Coating:
- **120 g** flour
- **60 g** cornstarch (extra crisp)
- 1 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp pepper
Wet dip:
- 2 eggs beaten (or buttermilk)

Fry oil (or air fryer method)

### Option 2 (fast): Roast/rotisserie chicken
- **650–700 g** shredded chicken (warm)

---

## B) Sauce (the identity)
- **150 g** hoisin sauce
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** sesame oil (optional)
- Optional: 1 tsp honey (if your hoisin is not sweet enough)

Mix and portion into cups.

---

## C) Crunch + veg (classic Peking combo)
- **1 cucumber**, thin matchsticks
- **6–8 scallions**, thin strips
- Optional: shredded lettuce (barrier)
- Optional: cilantro (fusion but works)

---

## D) Wrap base (choose)
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps  
OR for more authentic feel:
- **8–12** Chinese pancakes / thin crepes (if you can source them)

---

## E) Optional “premium crunch”
- Crispy wonton strips (store-bought or fry thin wrapper strips)
- Sesame seeds

---

## Equipment
- Fryer/air fryer or skillet
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Mix sauce (2 minutes)
- Stir hoisin + soy + vinegar (+ sesame oil).

---

## 2) Prep veg (5 minutes)
- Cut cucumber and scallions into thin strips. Keep dry.

---

## 3) Cook chicken (10–12 minutes)
### Crispy method (best)
1. Heat oil to 175–180°C.
2. Coat chicken: egg → flour/cornstarch mix.
3. Fry in batches until golden and cooked (3–5 min).
4. Drain well.

### Air fryer
- Spray lightly with oil; air fry 200°C until crisp, flipping once.

### Roast chicken method
- Warm shredded chicken briefly and toss with a little sauce.

---

## 4) Assemble wraps
Per wrap:
1. Warm wrap briefly.
2. Add lettuce barrier (optional).
3. Spread 1–2 Tbsp hoisin sauce.
4. Add chicken (160–200 g cooked).
5. Add cucumber + scallion strips.
6. Add optional crispy wonton strips.
7. Roll tight.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- Chicken: 160–200 g
- Sauce: 30–40 g
- Cucumber + scallion: generous handful
- Crunch: small handful (optional)

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Chicken: 1.8 kg
- Sauce: 2.5× (about 375 g hoisin + seasonings)
- Cucumbers: 3
- Scallions: 2 bunches

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- Wrap A: chicken wrap (rolled, parchment)
- Cup B: extra hoisin sauce (upsell)
- Cup C: crunchy wonton strips (optional) to keep crisp

**Label:** “Add crunchy strips last (optional).”

---

## Allergens
- Wheat (wraps, flour)
- Egg (if using egg dip)
- Soy (hoisin/soy)
- Sesame (optional)
- Gluten possible (brands)
