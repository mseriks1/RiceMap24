# Kimbap (Korean Seaweed Rice Rolls) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Kimbap is a Korean roll made with seasoned rice and fillings wrapped in roasted seaweed (gim). It’s perfect for lunch boxes, grab-and-go, and “variety roll” upsells.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Kimbap (Korean seaweed rice rolls)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/gimbap

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Grab-and-Go • Rice Roll
- **Yield:** ~6 rolls (portion as 6–12 servings depending on slices/sets)
- **Active time:** ~35–50 min  
- **Total time:** ~50–60 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (component prep + rolling)

## Ingredients (≈ 6 rolls)

> **Important:** Kimbap quality depends on **rice seasoning**, **filling balance**, and **tight rolling**.  
> Use the linked Maangchi recipe for **exact amounts** (rice, sesame oil, salt, fillings).

### A) Rice (seasoned)
- Cooked short-grain rice (amount per source)
- Sesame oil (per source)
- Salt (per source)

### B) Gim (seaweed)
- Roasted seaweed sheets (gim), 6 sheets

### C) Classic fillings (per source)
- Carrot (julienned, sautéed)
- Spinach (blanched + seasoned)
- Eggs (thin omelet strips)
- Yellow pickled radish (danmuji)
- Imitation crab sticks / ham / fish cake (optional, per source variants)

### Optional add-ons (operator choice)
- Cooked bulgogi strips
- Tuna-mayo (separate “tuna kimbap” variant)
- Cucumber (quick roll; keep dry)

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Cook rice + cool slightly  
2) Prep and season spinach  
3) Sauté carrots  
4) Cook egg sheet + slice  
5) Prepare danmuji + protein sticks  
6) Season rice  
7) Roll and slice

---

## Component Prep (Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Rice
1. Cook short-grain rice and let it cool slightly (warm, not hot).
2. Season with sesame oil + salt per source. Mix gently (don’t mash).

## 2) Spinach
1. Blanch spinach briefly.
2. Rinse, squeeze dry, and season per source (sesame oil/salt/garlic if used).

## 3) Carrots
1. Sauté julienned carrots with a little oil until softened but still bright.
2. Season lightly (salt).

## 4) Eggs
1. Beat eggs, cook a thin omelet sheet.
2. Slice into long strips.

## 5) Other fillings
- Cut danmuji into long sticks.
- Prep crab/ham/fish cake sticks per source.

---

## Rolling Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)
1. Place a gim sheet shiny-side down on the bamboo mat.
2. Spread a thin layer of rice over the gim, leaving ~2 cm uncovered at the top edge.
3. Lay fillings in a neat line across the lower third.
4. Roll tightly using the mat, pressing to shape as you go.
5. Seal the end (a few grains of rice help stick).

## Slicing
1. Lightly oil the knife with sesame oil.
2. Slice each roll into even pieces (6–10 slices depending on your serving format).

---

## Quality Checks
- **Roll tightness:** Slices hold shape, not falling apart.
- **Rice layer:** Thin and even (not too thick).
- **Fillings:** Balanced, colorful, not wet.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 roll sets)
Because filling prep is the time sink, scale with a “filling bar” approach:
- Make large batches of carrots/spinach/eggs.
- Cook rice in batches and season consistently (weigh sesame oil + salt).

Multiply all **exact source amounts** by your target roll count / 6.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Pack slices in a flat container.
- Include a small note: “Best eaten fresh. Keep cool.”
- Optional: small cup of kimchi or pickles.

**Holding time:** Best within **4–6 hours** chilled (rolls dry out over time).

---

## Storage & Food Safety Notes
- Keep refrigerated if holding more than 1 hour.
- Avoid very wet fillings (cucumber) unless eaten quickly.
- Kimbap is best day-of; rice firms up overnight.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Egg, sesame
- **May contain:** Fish/shellfish (crab sticks), soy (some fillings)
- **Gluten:** Possible depending on fillings (check labels)
