# Chicken Tinola (Filipino Ginger Chicken Soup) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Tinola is a Filipino everyday favorite: comforting **ginger-garlic chicken soup** with leafy greens, often served with rice.  
It’s a great **banker** for RiceMap24 because it’s simple, affordable, widely liked, and very easy to batch-cook and deliver (broth + chicken in one container, rice separate).

**Reference recipes:** Kawaling Pinoy (Tinolang Manok) and Panlasang Pinoy (Tinola).

## Choose Your Chicken (Pick ONE)
### Option A (classic, best flavor): bone-in pieces
- **1.5 kg** chicken thighs/drumsticks/leg quarters (bone-in, skin-on)

### Option B (faster prep): boneless thigh
- **1.2 kg** boneless chicken thigh, cut into chunks

> Bone-in gives the best broth and feels more “home cooking.”

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Aromatics
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1 large onion**, sliced
- **6 cloves** garlic, minced
- **50–60 g** ginger, sliced into thin coins (about a big thumb)

## B) Soup base
- **2.2–2.5 L** water (or light chicken stock)
- **2–3 Tbsp** fish sauce (patis), to taste  
  - Substitute: salt if needed

## C) Vegetables (choose 2)
Traditional tinola uses green papaya or chayote plus leafy greens.

### Option 1 (most common)
- **1 medium chayote**, peeled and sliced into wedges  
  OR **1 small green papaya**, peeled and cubed

### Option 2 (easy EU substitution)
- **1 zucchini**, thick half-moons (still tasty, not “traditional”)

### Leafy greens (add at the end)
- **2 bunches** spinach, bok choy, pechay, or malunggay leaves (if available)

## D) Optional “banker upgrades”
- 1–2 bird’s eye chilies (whole) for a gentle heat (don’t split)
- 1 calamansi or lime wedge per bowl (brightness)

---

## Equipment
- Large pot (5–6 L)
- Knife + cutting board
- Ladle
- Containers + sauce cups (lime wedge)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Slice onion, mince garlic.
2. Slice ginger thin.
3. Prep chayote/papaya (or zucchini).
4. Rinse greens and keep aside.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable)

## Step 1 — Sauté aromatics (5 minutes)
1. Heat pot over medium.
2. Add oil.
3. Add onion and cook 2–3 minutes until softened.
4. Add garlic and ginger; cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.

## Step 2 — Brown chicken lightly (5 minutes)
1. Add chicken pieces.
2. Cook 4–5 minutes, turning, until the outside turns opaque and lightly browned.
3. Add fish sauce and cook 1 minute (this deepens flavor).

## Step 3 — Simmer broth (25–35 minutes)
1. Add water/stock.
2. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
3. Simmer until chicken is cooked and broth tastes “round”:
   - Bone-in: **30–35 minutes**
   - Boneless: **20–25 minutes**
4. Skim foam if needed for a clearer broth.

## Step 4 — Add vegetables (10 minutes)
1. Add chayote/papaya (or zucchini).
2. Simmer until tender:
   - Chayote/papaya: **8–12 minutes**
   - Zucchini: **5–7 minutes**

## Step 5 — Add greens last (1–2 minutes)
1. Add leafy greens.
2. Turn off heat once wilted (keeps color bright).

---

## Final Seasoning
Taste and adjust:
- Need more savory depth → a splash more fish sauce
- Too strong → add a little water
- Needs brightness → serve with calamansi/lime wedge

---

## Quality Checks
- **Broth:** ginger-forward but balanced; not greasy.
- **Chicken:** tender, not dry.
- **Vegetables:** cooked but not mushy.
- **Greens:** bright green.

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **1.7×** (since base makes ~6).
- Chicken: ~2.5 kg bone-in
- Water: 4.0–4.2 L
- Ginger: ~90–100 g
- Fish sauce: 4–5 Tbsp (adjust)

**Ops tip:** Make a large pot. Portion into containers with chicken pieces evenly distributed.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently to a simmer (avoid hard boil).

## Allergens
