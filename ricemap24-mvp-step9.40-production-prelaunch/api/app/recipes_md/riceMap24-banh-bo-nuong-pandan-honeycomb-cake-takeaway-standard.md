# Bánh Bò Nướng (Vietnamese Pandan Honeycomb Cake) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A striking Vietnamese baked cake with a bouncy “honeycomb” interior and a pandan-coconut aroma.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** no melting, travels well, looks amazing when sliced, and sells well as **slice boxes** or **mini loaf** portions.

**Reference recipe:** Viet World Kitchen — “Pandan and Coconut Tapioca Cake (Bánh Bò Nướng)”  
https://www.vietworldkitchen.com/blog/2010/12/pandan-and-coconut-tapioca-cake-recipe-banh-bo-nuong.html

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Baked pandan-coconut honeycomb cake
- **Yield:** 1 cake (8-inch round or 9×5 loaf) = 8–10 slices
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min
- **Total time:** ~60–75 min (includes baking + cooling)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (temperature + mixing for honeycomb)

---

## Why it’s great for RiceMap24
- **Shelf-stable for pickup:** best at room temp; doesn’t melt.
- **“Wow” factor:** green color + honeycomb structure sells on photos and videos.
- **High-margin format:** sell by slice, 4-slice box, or whole cake.
- **Batch-friendly:** bake 2–3 loaves and portion.

---

## Equipment
- 8-inch round pan (or 9×5 loaf pan)  
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Fine sieve (optional; helps smooth batter)
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale recommended)
- Oven thermometer (recommended; temp accuracy matters)
- Cooling rack

---

## Ingredients (1 cake)

### Batter
- **250 g** tapioca starch (tapioca flour)
- **200–220 g** sugar
- **400 ml** coconut milk (full-fat)
- **6 large eggs** (room temperature)
- **2 tsp** baking powder *(single-acting preferred if available)*
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **1–2 tsp** pandan extract (or pandan paste)  
  - Optional: add a few drops of green food color if your pandan is weak

### Optional aroma upgrade (nice for sales)
- **1 tsp** vanilla extract OR 1 packet vanilla sugar

---

## Preheat + Pan Prep (Critical)
1. Preheat oven to **175°C** (convection 165°C) for at least **20 minutes**.
2. Grease pan thoroughly and line bottom with parchment (round pan) or line loaf with parchment sling.
3. Optional: warm the pan in the oven for 2 minutes, then grease again (helps initial lift).

---

## Mixing Method (Smooth, Honeycomb-Friendly)

### 1) Combine dry
1. In a bowl, whisk tapioca starch + sugar + baking powder + salt.

### 2) Add wet
2. Add coconut milk and whisk until smooth.
3. Add eggs one at a time, whisking gently until fully incorporated.
4. Add pandan extract (and vanilla if using).

**Mixing rule:** whisk until smooth but avoid whipping tons of air.

### 3) Strain (recommended)
- Pour batter through a fine sieve into a second bowl to remove lumps.
- Rest batter **10 minutes** (bubbles rise; texture improves).

---

## Baking (The honeycomb happens here)
1. Pour batter into prepared pan.
2. Bake at **175°C** until the top is deeply golden and a skewer comes out clean:
   - Round pan: **40–50 minutes**
   - Loaf pan: **45–60 minutes**
3. If top browns too fast, tent loosely with foil for the last 10–15 minutes.

**Doneness check:** center should feel springy; skewer mostly clean (a few moist crumbs are ok).

---

## Cooling + Honeycomb Set
1. Cool in pan **10 minutes**.
2. Unmold onto a rack and cool completely before slicing (**60 minutes**).
3. Best slicing: refrigerate 30 minutes after cooling for cleaner slices.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Honeycomb:** visible holes in cross-section (not dense).
- **Texture:** bouncy, slightly chewy, not gummy.
- **Aroma:** pandan + coconut forward.
- **Color:** evenly green-gold, not gray.

---

## RiceMap24 Menu Formats (High-performing)
- **Single slice** (impulse dessert add-on)
- **2-slice box** (most common)
- **4-slice box** (family add-on)
- **Whole cake** (premium pre-order)

### Portioning
- Slice weight target: **90–120 g** per slice (depending on your price point).

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Cool fully before boxing (prevents condensation).
- Use parchment between slices.
- Add label: “Best at room temp. Can be chilled for a firmer chew.”

---

## Storage
- Room temp (airtight): **1 day** (best texture)
- Refrigerated: **3–4 days** (chewier, still good)
- Freeze slices (wrapped): **1 month** (thaw room temp)

---

## Allergen Notes
- Egg
- Coconut
