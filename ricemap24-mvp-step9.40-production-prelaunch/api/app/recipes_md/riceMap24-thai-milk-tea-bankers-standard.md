# Thai Milk Tea (Cha Yen / ชาเย็น) — RiceMap24 “Banker Drink” Standard

A proven bestseller: strong Thai tea + creamy sweetness, served iced.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 because it’s **high-margin**, **fast to serve**, and extremely popular alongside spicy food.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Iced milk tea
- **Cuisine vibe:** Thai
- **Yield:** 1 drink (with batch scaling included)
- **Active time:** 3 minutes (if tea concentrate is ready)
- **Total time:** 5 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this is a RiceMap24 “Banker”
- **Very popular flavor profile:** sweet, creamy, aromatic tea.
- **Fast to serve:** pour + stir + ice.
- **Batch-friendly:** brew concentrate once, serve all day.
- **Upsells:** boba add-on, less sweet, extra cream top.

---

## Ingredients (1 drink)

## A) Tea base (must be strong)
Choose one:
- **180 ml** Thai tea concentrate (chilled)  
OR
- **180 ml** strong black tea + Thai tea mix (if you don’t have concentrate)

## B) Sweet + creamy
- **30–40 g** sweetened condensed milk (2 Tbsp)  
- **20–30 ml** evaporated milk OR whole milk (for creamy lift)

## C) Ice
- **180–220 g** ice

## Optional finish
- Pinch of salt (tiny, boosts flavor)
- Whipped cream top (not necessary, but sells)

---

## Sweetness Levels (menu toggles)
- **Less sweet:** 20–25 g condensed milk
- **Classic:** 30–40 g
- **Extra sweet:** 45–55 g

---

## Step-by-Step (Single Drink)

### 1) Build in cup
1. Add condensed milk to cup.
2. Add tea concentrate and stir 10 seconds.

### 2) Add creamy lift
- Add evaporated milk/whole milk and stir again.

### 3) Add ice
- Fill with ice and stir once more.

**Taste check:** strong tea should still be present, not just “sweet milk.”

---

## Batch Setup (Recommended for Home Kitchens)

## A) Thai tea concentrate (for 10 drinks)
You want it strong so it survives ice dilution.

- **2 L** water
- **80 g** Thai tea mix *(or follow the brand’s “strong brew” ratio)*

Method:
1. Bring water to near-boil, turn off heat.
2. Steep Thai tea mix **8 minutes** (do not oversteep; bitterness).
3. Strain well.
4. Chill completely.

Portion per drink: **180 ml** concentrate.

---

## Batch for 10 drinks (classic sweetness)
- Thai tea concentrate: **1,800 ml**
- Condensed milk: **350–400 g**
- Evaporated/whole milk: **250 ml**
- Ice: **1.8–2.2 kg**

---

## Boba Add-on (upsell)
**Note:** boba hardens in the fridge. Best for quick pickup windows.
- Add **50–70 g cooked boba** per drink.
- Offer “boba only if pickup within 30–45 min” for best quality.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
### Best options
1) **Bottle without ice + separate ice cup** (best if pickup is long)
2) **Cup with ice** (best for immediate pickup)

**Label suggestion:** “Shake or stir before drinking.”

---

## Shelf Life
- Tea concentrate: **2–3 days** refrigerated
- Once mixed with dairy: best same day

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (condensed milk, evaporated milk)
