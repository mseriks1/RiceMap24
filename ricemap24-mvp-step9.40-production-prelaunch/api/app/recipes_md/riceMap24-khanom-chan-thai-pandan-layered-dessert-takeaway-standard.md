# Khanom Chan (Thai Pandan Layered Rice Sweet) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A classic Thai steamed dessert with a chewy, bouncy texture—cut into clean little squares that look amazing in a dessert box.  
**Pickup/takeaway friendly:** no melting, holds well chilled, and slices neatly the next day.

**Reference recipe:** Serious Eats — “Pandan Khanom Chan (Layered Rice Sweets)” (Updated Apr 1, 2025)

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Steamed, gelled starch dessert (2-layer version)
- **Yield:** 1 x 9×9-inch / 23×23 cm pan (cut 16–25 pieces)
- **Active time:** ~30 minutes
- **Total time:** ~3 hours steaming + **overnight chill**
- **Skill level:** Medium (steaming setup + patience)

## Equipment
- Steamer setup (large pot/wok + rack + tight lid)
- **9×9-inch** baking pan (metal or heatproof glass)
- Blender (for pandan juice) or pandan extract
- Mixing bowls + whisk
- Fine strainer
- Ladle
- Foil or clean towel (to prevent lid condensation dripping)

---

## Ingredients (1 pan)

### A) Pandan syrup base
- **10 pandan leaves**, chopped  
  *(OR 2–3 Tbsp natural pandan extract)*
- **4 cups water**, divided
- **3 cups sugar**
- **2 1/2 tsp kosher salt**, divided

### B) Batters
- **4 cups (1 lb / 454 g) glutinous rice flour**, divided
- **1 cup coconut milk**

> This 2-layer version uses:
> - **Coconut layer** (thicker/creamier)
> - **Pandan-syrup layer** (thinner/saltier)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Lightly oil the 9×9 pan (or line the bottom with parchment for easy lift).
2. Set up your steamer: bring water to a steady simmer/boil.
3. Wrap lid with a towel or place foil under lid so condensation doesn’t drip onto the surface.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Make pandan syrup (10 minutes)
### If using pandan leaves
1. Blend chopped pandan leaves with **2 cups water** until the water turns deep green (about 2 minutes).
2. Strain into a saucepan, pressing out liquid and discarding pulp.
3. Add remaining **2 cups water** to the saucepan.

### If using extract
- Combine extract with **4 cups water** in the saucepan.

### Cook syrup
4. Add **3 cups sugar** and **2 tsp** of the salt.
5. Bring just to a simmer, stirring until sugar dissolves.
6. Remove from heat and cool 5 minutes (warm is fine; not boiling hot).

---

## 2) Make the two batters (10 minutes)
1. Divide glutinous rice flour **equally** into two large bowls (2 cups flour each).
2. Add the remaining **1/2 tsp salt** to one of the bowls (this becomes the saltier pandan layer).

### Coconut layer bowl
3. Add **1 cup coconut milk** and **2 1/4 cups pandan syrup**.
4. Whisk until completely smooth (no visible dry flour).

### Pandan-syrup layer bowl
5. Add the **remaining pandan syrup** to the salted flour bowl.
6. Whisk until smooth.

> **Important:** Batter will settle quickly—whisk again before every pour.

---

## 3) Steam layer 1 (coconut layer) — about 45 minutes
1. Pour the coconut batter into the pan (tap pan gently to level).
2. Place pan into steamer.
3. Steam until gel is mostly set and a “skin” forms on top: **about 45 minutes**.

**Set check:** surface looks firm, not wet; gentle jiggle is OK.

---

## 4) Steam layer 2 (pandan layer) — about 1 hour 45 minutes
1. Whisk the pandan layer batter again.
2. Slowly pour pandan batter evenly over the set coconut layer (use a ladle and pour gently to avoid breaking the first layer).
3. Steam until fully set: **about 1 hour 45 minutes**.

**Final set check:** the entire slab feels firm and elastic, not liquid.

---

## 5) Chill overnight (essential for clean cuts)
1. Cool at room temperature 30–45 minutes.
2. Cover and refrigerate **overnight** (or at least 8–12 hours).

---

## Cutting (Clean “bakery cubes”)
1. Lightly oil a sharp knife (or dip in warm water and wipe dry).
2. Cut into:
   - **4×4 grid** = 16 large cubes (premium)
   - **5×5 grid** = 25 smaller cubes (snack box)
3. Wipe the knife between cuts for perfect edges.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Texture:** bouncy-chewy, not grainy.
- **Layers:** distinct, clean separation.
- **Surface:** smooth (no condensation pits).
- **Cut:** sharp cubes that hold shape.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Keep chilled for best texture and clean edges.
- Place cubes in paper cups or a divider tray.
- Add label: “Keep chilled. Best within 3 days.”

---

## Storage
- Refrigerated (covered): **3–4 days** best texture.
- Freeze: possible, but texture may change slightly (test first).

---

## Allergen Notes
- Coconut
- Gluten-free (glutinous rice flour contains no gluten)
