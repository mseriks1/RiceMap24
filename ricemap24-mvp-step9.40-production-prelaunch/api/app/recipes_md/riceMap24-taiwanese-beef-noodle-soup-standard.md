- Measuring cups/spoons (scale optional)

## Ingredients (from source; 6–8 servings)

### Broth + beef
- **3 Tbsp** vegetable/canola/peanut oil
- **2 lb** boneless beef shank **or** chuck, cut into **1-inch cubes**
- **6** large slices fresh ginger root
- **6 cloves** garlic, smashed
- **2–3** small red chilies (e.g., Thai chilies), roughly chopped
- **1** large plum tomato, roughly chopped
- **2 Tbsp** sugar
- **1 Tbsp** Sichuan chili bean sauce (doubanjiang)
- **1 cup** Chinese rice wine
- **1 Tbsp** Sichuan peppercorns
- **2** whole star anise
- **1/4 cup** dark soy sauce
- **1/2 cup** light soy sauce
- **2 1/2 quarts** water

### Noodles + greens
- **2 lb** Asian wheat flour noodles (any width you like)
- Fresh spinach leaves, baby bok choy, broccolini, or other small greens (optional, to taste)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Beef:** Cut into 1-inch (2.5 cm) cubes. Pat dry for better browning.
2. **Aromatics:** Slice ginger, smash garlic, chop chilies, chop tomato.
3. Measure soy sauces, rice wine, sugar, doubanjiang, spices.
4. Put a large pot of water on standby for noodles (cook noodles near serving time).

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Brown beef (2 batches; ~10 minutes)
1. Heat **1 Tbsp oil** in a large pot over **medium-high** heat until shimmering.
2. Add **half** the beef in a single layer.
3. Cook **without moving** until well browned on first side, about **4 minutes**.
4. Stir and continue cooking until browned all over, about **10 minutes total** for the batch.
5. Transfer browned beef to a bowl.
6. Repeat with **1 more Tbsp oil** and remaining beef.

> Browning is key for a deep broth. Don’t crowd the pot.

---

## 2) Aromatics + tomato + sugar base (2–3 minutes)
1. Add remaining **1 Tbsp oil** to the same pot; heat until shimmering.
2. Add ginger, garlic, and chilies; cook **30 seconds** until fragrant.
3. Add chopped tomato; stir **1 minute**.
4. Add sugar; stir **30 seconds** until dissolved.
5. Add doubanjiang; stir until mixture just begins to bubble.

---

## 3) Build broth + long simmer (2.5 hours)
1. Return browned beef to the pot; stir to coat.
2. Add rice wine; cook **1 minute**, scraping up any browned bits.
3. Add star anise, Sichuan peppercorns, dark soy, light soy, and water.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a **gentle simmer** and cover.
5. Simmer until beef is tender, about **2 1/2 hours**.

> Keep it at a steady simmer (small bubbles). If it boils hard, beef can tighten.

---

## 4) Optional: strain for cleaner broth
1. Remove beef cubes to a bowl (tongs).
2. Strain soup through a mesh strainer/colander to catch ginger, peppercorns, etc.
3. Return desired solids (and beef) to the broth.
4. Add greens to wilt in the hot soup (optional).

---

## 5) Noodles + assemble bowls
1. Cook noodles according to package directions.
2. Drain well.
3. Divide noodles among bowls.
4. Ladle hot broth + beef over noodles.
5. Add greens and serve immediately.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef:** Tender and easy to bite (if tough, simmer longer).
- **Broth:** Dark, savory, slightly spicy, aromatic.
- **Noodles:** Chewy, not overcooked.
- **Greens:** Wilted but still bright.

## Storage & Reheat
- Broth + beef: refrigerate up to **3 days**; reheat to a boil for service.
- Noodles: best cooked fresh; cooked noodles can clump.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes
- **Contains:** Wheat (noodles), soy (soy sauces), fermented bean paste (doubanjiang)
- **Gluten:** Likely (soy sauces unless GF)
