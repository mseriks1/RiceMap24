# Sichuan-Style Smashed Cucumber Salad (拍黄瓜) — RiceMap24 Side Order Standard

A high-impact, low-cost side that sells instantly: **crisp smashed cucumbers** in a garlicky, black-vinegar, sesame-chili dressing.  
Perfect next to dumplings, noodles, rice bowls, fried chicken, ramen, and grilled meats.

**Reference recipe:** Serious Eats — Sichuan-Style Smashed Cucumber Salad  
https://www.seriouseats.com/sichuan-style-smashed-cucumber-salad-recipe

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Chinese (Sichuan) • Side order / add-on
- **Yield:** 3–4 side portions
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Total time:** ~25 min (includes salting/draining)
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this is great for “mer-salg”
- **Looks great:** glossy dressing + sesame + chili + herbs.
- **Fast and cheap:** cucumbers + pantry sauce.
- **Keeps well:** can be prepped ahead and held chilled.
- **Customers love “cool + spicy” next to hot food.**

---

## Equipment
- Cutting board + knife
- Rolling pin / meat mallet (to smash)
- Colander + bowl
- Mixing bowl
- Measuring spoons

---

## Ingredients (3–4 portions)

### Cucumbers
- **1 large English cucumber** (or 2 smaller Persian cucumbers)
- **Kosher salt** (for draining)

### Dressing
- **2 cloves garlic**, minced (about 2 tsp)
- **1 tsp** black vinegar (Chinkiang) *or* rice vinegar
- **2 tsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** chili oil (or to taste)
- **1/2 tsp** sugar (optional, balances)
- **1/2 tsp** sesame oil (optional)
- Optional “Sichuan kick”: a pinch of toasted, ground Sichuan peppercorn

### Finish (photo + crunch)
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Chopped cilantro (optional)
- Thin-sliced scallions (optional)

---

## Method (Precise, Repeatable)

### 1) Smash + cut (3 minutes)
1. Wash cucumber and peel only the thickest/toughest areas if needed.
2. Place cucumber on board and smash it along its length with a rolling pin until it cracks and splits.
3. Cut into bite-size chunks (about 3–4 cm pieces).

> Smashing creates cracks so dressing penetrates and flavor “sticks.”

### 2) Salt + drain (10 minutes)
1. Toss cucumber pieces with **1/2 tsp kosher salt**.
2. Put in a colander over a bowl and let drain **10 minutes**.
3. Shake off excess liquid (do not rinse unless you oversalted).

### 3) Mix dressing (2 minutes)
1. In a bowl, mix garlic + vinegar + soy sauce + chili oil.
2. Add sugar/sesame oil/Sichuan peppercorn if using.

### 4) Toss and finish (2 minutes)
1. Add drained cucumbers to dressing and toss.
2. Finish with sesame seeds and herbs.
3. Serve chilled or cool.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Texture:** crisp, not watery.
- **Flavor:** tangy + garlicky + spicy, not overly salty.
- **Look:** glossy, with visible chili and sesame.

---

## Portioning (Side Order)
- **Single side:** 150–180 g
- **Family side:** 350–450 g

---

## Scaling (10 side orders)
- Cucumbers: **10 English cucumbers**
- Dressing: multiply by **10×**
- Keep cucumbers drained and dry before tossing.

**Ops tip:** Make dressing in a squeeze bottle. Toss cucumbers in batches to keep them crisp.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Best: pack cucumbers and dressing in the same container **only if delivered quickly**.
- For best crispness: pack dressing in a small cup and toss before eating.

Label: “Shake well before eating” or “Pour sauce and toss.”

---

## Storage
- Holds **24 hours** in fridge (best texture same day).
- If prepped ahead, keep cucumbers drained and dress close to service.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Sesame (if used)
