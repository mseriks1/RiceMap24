# Chicken Adobo (Filipino Soy-Vinegar Braised Chicken) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Filipino staple: chicken braised in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaf, and peppercorns. Deep flavor, excellent holding, and great for rice boxes.

**Source recipe:** Panlasang Pinoy — “Chicken Adobo Recipe”  
https://panlasangpinoy.com/filipino-food-chicken-adobo-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Filipino • Braise • Rice Bowl
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~10–15 min  
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (low-and-slow simmer)

---

## Equipment
- Heavy pot or deep skillet with lid
- Tongs
- Measuring cups/spoons
- Cutting board + knife

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Main
- **2 lb** chicken (about **900 g**), cut into serving pieces

### Braise base
- **3/4 cup** soy sauce
- **1/2 cup** vinegar *(white vinegar per source)*
- **1 cup** water
- **3 cloves** garlic, crushed
- **3** dried bay leaves
- **1 tsp** whole peppercorn
- **2 Tbsp** cooking oil
- **1 Tbsp** sugar *(optional, “optional” per source)*

*(Ingredient list and measurements are from the source recipe linked above.)*

### To serve
- Cooked rice: **~250 g cooked rice per bowl** (4 bowls)

---

## Prep (5–10 minutes)
1. **Chicken:** Pat dry for better browning. Cut into evenly sized pieces if needed.
2. **Garlic:** Crush cloves (smash with side of knife).
3. Measure soy sauce, vinegar, and water.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Braise
1. In a pot, combine chicken, **soy sauce**, and **garlic**. Mix well.
2. Add **bay leaves** and **peppercorns**.
3. Pour in **water** and bring to a boil over **medium-high heat**.
4. Reduce to **low**, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender, about **30 minutes**.

### 2) Add vinegar (do not stir at first)
5. Add **vinegar**. Let it simmer **2–3 minutes without stirring** (classic technique to “cook off” the sharp edge).
6. After 2–3 minutes, stir gently and continue simmering **10 minutes** (uncovered or partially covered), letting flavors deepen.

### 3) Optional: reduce + glaze
7. If you want a richer sauce for rice boxes, simmer uncovered a few extra minutes to reduce slightly.
8. Optional sweetness: add **1 Tbsp sugar** during the last simmer if you want a slightly rounder profile.

### 4) Optional: pan-fry for color (popular finish)
9. Heat **2 Tbsp oil** in a pan. Lightly brown the chicken pieces, then pour some adobo sauce over to glaze.  
   *(Follow the source’s finish steps if you want the browned version.)*

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Chicken:** Tender, not dry; falls off bone easily (if bone-in).
- **Sauce:** Salty-tangy balance; not harshly acidic.
- **Aroma:** Garlic + bay + peppercorn clearly present.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Chicken: **2250 g**
- Soy sauce: **1.875 cups** *(= 1 cup + 14 Tbsp)*
- Vinegar: **1.25 cups**
- Water: **2.5 cups**
- Garlic: **8 cloves** (crushed)
- Bay leaves: **7–8**
- Whole peppercorn: **2.5 tsp**
- Cooking oil (for optional browning): **5 Tbsp**
- Sugar (optional): **2.5 Tbsp**

### Batch notes
- Braise in a **wide pot** for even simmering.
- Taste at the end; small adjustments are easier than changing early.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rice in base; chicken on top.
- Add **2–3 Tbsp sauce** over chicken; include extra sauce cup as an upsell.

**Holding time:** Excellent. Flavor improves after resting **30–60 minutes**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3–4 days**.
- **Reheat:** Low simmer or microwave. Add a splash of water if sauce is too reduced.

---

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Vinegar goes in *after* the first simmer—don’t stir for the first 2–3 minutes.
- Adobo is a great “next-day” dish: consider cooking ahead for peak flavor.
