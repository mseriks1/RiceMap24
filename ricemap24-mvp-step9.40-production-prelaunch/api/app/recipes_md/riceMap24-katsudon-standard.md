# Katsudon (Japanese Pork Cutlet & Egg Rice Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Japanese comfort bowl: crispy pork cutlet simmered briefly with onions in a sweet-savory dashi sauce, finished with softly set eggs over steamed rice.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Katsudon”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/katsudon/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Donburi • Crispy Cutlet Bowl
- **Yield:** 2 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~35–45 min (includes frying)
- **Skill level:** Medium (frying + egg timing)

---

## Equipment
- Frying pan or pot for shallow/deep frying
- Medium skillet with lid (for simmer step)
- Wire rack (best for keeping cutlet crisp)
- Thermometer (optional but recommended)
- Bowls + whisk (for eggs)
- Cutting board + knife

---

## Ingredients (2 portions)

### Pork cutlets (tonkatsu-style)
- **2** center-cut boneless pork chops, pounded to **~1 cm** thick
- Salt + pepper
- Flour (for dusting)
- **1** egg (for breading)
- **1 cup** panko breadcrumbs
- Neutral oil (for frying)

### Simmer sauce + topping
- **1/2 cup** dashi stock *(or chicken stock)*
- **2 tsp** sugar
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 tsp** mirin
- **2** large eggs (for the topping)
- **1** medium onion, thinly sliced
- **1** scallion, chopped

### To serve
- **2 servings** steamed white rice

*(Measurements and ingredient list above are from the source recipe linked at the top.)*

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Pound pork:** Place between plastic wrap and pound to ~1 cm thick for even cooking.
2. **Set up breading station:**
   - Plate 1: flour
   - Bowl: **1 egg**, beaten
   - Plate 2: **1 cup panko**
3. **Slice onion** thinly; chop scallion.
4. **Beat 2 eggs** for the topping (separate bowl). Don’t over-whisk—just combine.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## A) Fry the pork cutlets (tonkatsu-style)
1. Season pork with salt and pepper.
2. Dredge each cutlet in flour (shake off excess) → dip in beaten egg → coat in panko.
3. Heat oil for frying to about **175°C / 350°F** (medium-high).
4. Fry cutlets until golden and cooked through. Transfer to a **wire rack** to stay crisp.
5. Slice each cutlet into strips (about 2–3 cm wide).

> **Crispness tip:** Keep cutlets on a rack, not paper towel, to avoid steaming the crust.

---

## B) Simmer + egg finish (the donburi step)
1. In a medium skillet, combine:
   - **1/2 cup dashi**
   - **2 tsp sugar**
   - **1 Tbsp soy sauce**
   - **2 tsp mirin**
2. Add the sliced onion. Bring to a simmer over **medium heat** and cook until onion softens (about **4–6 min**).
3. Lay sliced cutlet pieces on top of the onions.
4. Pour the **2 beaten eggs** evenly over the cutlet and onions.
5. Cover with a lid and cook until eggs are **just set but still soft** (about **30–90 seconds**, depending on heat and pan).

> **Stop point:** Turn off heat when eggs are still slightly glossy. They’ll finish setting from residual heat.

---

## Serve
1. Add hot rice to two bowls.
2. Slide the cutlet/onion/egg mixture on top.
3. Garnish with chopped scallion. Serve immediately.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Packing/Serving)
- **Cutlet:** Still crunchy at edges; not soggy.
- **Eggs:** Soft-set (not rubbery).
- **Sauce:** Savory-sweet; lightly coats, not soupy.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply by **5×**.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Pork chops: **10**
- Panko: **5 cups**
- Breading egg: **5 eggs**
- Simmer sauce:
  - Dashi/stock: **2.5 cups**
  - Sugar: **10 tsp**
  - Soy sauce: **5 Tbsp**
  - Mirin: **10 tsp**
- Topping eggs: **10 large eggs**
- Onions: **5 medium**
- Scallions: **5**
- Rice: **10 servings**

### Batch notes
- Fry cutlets first; hold on wire racks.
- Do the simmer/egg step in **small batches** (2 portions at a time) for best egg texture.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality:** Pack components in two compartments:
- Compartment 1: rice  
- Compartment 2: katsudon topping (cutlet + egg + onion)  
This preserves crispness much better than stacking everything in one box.

Include a note: **“Egg is soft-set; stir gently before eating.”**

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days** (quality drops; cutlet softens).
- **Reheat:** Microwave gently. For best crispness, re-crisp cutlet briefly in air fryer/oven and then add topping.

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Egg, wheat (panko/flour), soy (soy sauce)
- **May contain:** Fish (if dashi is fish-based)
- **Alcohol:** Mirin (typically cooked off)
