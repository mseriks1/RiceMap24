# Ube Vanilla Milkshake — RiceMap24 “Banker Drink” Standard

A high-impact, high-margin milkshake: vibrant **ube (purple yam)** + vanilla.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 because it’s visually striking, tastes like dessert, and sells well as an add-on.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Milkshake
- **Asian twist:** Ube (Filipino bakery flavor)
- **Yield:** 1 shake (with batch scaling included)
- **Active time:** 3–4 minutes
- **Total time:** 4 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this is a RiceMap24 “Banker”
- **Visual “wow”:** purple sells.
- **Strong dessert association:** easy upsell.
- **Fast to serve:** blend and go.
- **Upsells:** extra ube, coconut, white chocolate drizzle, larger size.

---

## Choose your Ube Base (Pick ONE)
### Option A (best): Ube halaya (ube jam)
Most home kitchens can source this from Asian stores.

### Option B: Ube extract + sweetener
Easier if you can’t get ube jam (color + flavor, less “real” texture).

> Banker choice: **ube halaya**.

---

## Ingredients (1 shake)

## Classic size (≈450–500 ml)
- **250 g** vanilla ice cream (about 2 big scoops)
- **180–220 ml** cold milk (whole milk preferred)
- **60–80 g** ube halaya (ube jam)  
- **1/2 tsp** vanilla (optional)
- Pinch of salt (tiny, boosts flavor)

### Optional “premium” add-ons
- **1 Tbsp** coconut cream (richer, tropical)
- **1 Tbsp** white chocolate sauce (dessert vibe)
- Whipped cream top (if you want)

---

## Sweetness Control
Ube halaya is sweet already. Start with **60 g** and taste.
- Too sweet → add more milk or a little ice.
- Not sweet enough → add 1 tsp sugar or more ube jam.

---

## Step-by-Step (Single Shake)

1) Add milk to blender first (helps blending).  
2) Add ice cream + ube halaya + optional coconut cream.  
3) Blend **20–30 seconds** until smooth and thick.  
4) Taste, adjust thickness:
   - Too thick → add 30 ml milk
   - Too thin → add 1 scoop ice cream  
5) Pour into cup and seal.

---

## Batch for 10 shakes (classic size)
- Vanilla ice cream: **2.5 kg**
- Milk: **2.0 L**
- Ube halaya: **700 g** (range 600–900 g depending on intensity)
- Coconut cream (optional): **200 ml**

**Ops tip:** Pre-portion ube into 10 cups (70 g each) to speed service.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Use a **thick shake cup** with tight lid.
- Use a **wide straw** (boba straw works).
- Keep cold.

**Label suggestion:** “Keep cold. Best within 30–60 minutes.”

---

## Storage
Milkshakes are best fresh.
- If you must hold: keep in freezer 10–15 minutes, then blend briefly before serving.

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (ice cream, milk)
- Coconut (if used)
