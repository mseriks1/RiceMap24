# Kung Pao Chicken (Gong Bao Ji Ding) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Sichuan classic: tender chicken stir-fried with dried chilies, Sichuan peppercorn, scallions, and peanuts in a glossy, savory-sweet sauce.

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### A) Chicken + velvet marinade
- **700 g** boneless, skinless chicken thighs *(best texture; breast works but is less forgiving)*
- **10 g** soy sauce (≈ 2 tsp)
- **10 g** Shaoxing wine (≈ 2 tsp) *(or dry sherry)*
- **5 g** cornstarch (≈ 2 tsp)
- **2 g** kosher salt (≈ 1/3 tsp)
- **1 g** ground white pepper (≈ 1/4 tsp)
- **10 g** neutral oil (≈ 2 tsp)

### B) Stir-fry sauce (mix in a bowl)
- **45 g** light soy sauce (≈ 3 Tbsp)
- **30 g** Chinkiang black vinegar (≈ 2 Tbsp)
- **25 g** sugar (≈ 2 Tbsp)
- **60 g** chicken stock or water (≈ 1/4 cup)
- **10 g** cornstarch (≈ 1 Tbsp) *(for glossy thickening)*
- **5 g** sesame oil (≈ 1 tsp)

### C) Aromatics + spice + veg
- **60 g** scallions (about 6), cut into 3–4 cm pieces; whites/greens separated if you want cleaner control
- **20 g** ginger, minced (about 1 Tbsp)
- **20 g** garlic, minced (about 1 Tbsp)
- **12–18** dried red chilies *(adjust heat; keep whole)*
- **1–2 tsp** Sichuan peppercorns, lightly toasted and crushed *(optional but authentic “ma” tingle)*

### D) Nuts
- **120 g** roasted peanuts *(unsalted preferred; salt to taste at end)*

### E) Frying oil
- **30–45 ml** neutral oil (2–3 Tbsp), divided as needed for wok batches

> Notes:
> - Sauce ratios and technique align to the cited Serious Eats recipe; small brand differences exist (soy/vinegar strength).  
> - If your soy sauce is very salty, reduce soy slightly and adjust at the end.

---

## Prep (Do this before heating the wok)

### 1) Cut chicken (5 min)
1. Cut chicken into **2–2.5 cm cubes** for fast, even cooking.
2. Pat lightly dry if very wet.

### 2) Velvet marinade (10 min)
1. Mix soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, cornstarch, salt, white pepper, and oil.
2. Toss chicken until evenly coated.
3. Rest **10–15 minutes** (room temp is fine).

### 3) Mix sauce (2 min)
1. Whisk all sauce ingredients until cornstarch dissolves.
2. Set beside the stove (stir-fry moves fast).

### 4) Prep aromatics (3 min)
- Mince garlic + ginger.
- Cut scallions.
- Count chilies; crush Sichuan peppercorn (optional).

---

## Stir-Fry Method (Precise, Repeatable)

> Rule: **High heat + fast timing**. Everything should be within arm’s reach.

### 1) Heat the wok
1. Heat wok/skillet over **high heat** until lightly smoking (about 2–3 minutes).

### 2) Fry chilies + peppercorn (20–30 sec)
2. Add **1 Tbsp oil**.
3. Add dried chilies (and Sichuan peppercorn if using).  
4. Stir constantly **20–30 seconds** until fragrant (do not burn).

### 3) Cook chicken (3–5 min)
5. Add chicken in a single layer (cook in 2 batches if your pan is small).
6. Stir-fry until chicken is mostly cooked and lightly browned, **3–5 minutes**.

### 4) Aromatics + scallions (30–60 sec)
7. Add garlic + ginger; stir **15–20 seconds**.
8. Add scallions; toss **20–40 seconds** until just softened.

### 5) Sauce + glaze (45–90 sec)
9. Stir sauce bowl again (cornstarch settles fast).
10. Pour sauce into wok; toss continuously until it thickens and coats chicken, **45–90 seconds**.

### 6) Finish with peanuts (10 sec)
11. Add peanuts, toss briefly, and remove from heat.

### Final taste check
- **Too salty:** a splash of water or a touch more sugar/vinegar.
- **Not bright enough:** a small splash more black vinegar.
- **Not spicy enough:** add more chilies next batch (don’t cook longer; sauce can over-thicken).

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Chicken:** Tender, not dry (thighs + velvet step).
- **Sauce:** Glossy and coating, not watery.
- **Peanuts:** Crunchy (add at end).
- **Aroma:** Chili + vinegar + sesame note.

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply everything by **2.5×**.

### Targets (10 portions)
- Chicken thighs: **1750 g**
- Peanuts: **300 g**
- Sauce: **2.5×** all amounts (mix in a large jug; whisk before each use)

### Ops tips
- Pre-portion sauce into **10 cups** so staff can dump-and-go.
- Cook in **small batches** for wok char; overcrowding makes it soggy.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rice in base; Kung Pao on top.
- If customers dislike chilies: include a note “Contains whole dried chilies (remove before eating).”
- Keep extra peanuts in a small cup for premium crunch (optional).

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** (sauce thickens as it cools).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **2 days**.
- Reheat in a hot pan with a splash of water to loosen sauce, or microwave and stir well.

- Don’t burn chilies: 20–30 seconds is enough.
- Add peanuts last to preserve crunch.
