# Mochi Donut Muffins (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

A “fusion banker” dessert: mochi donut texture (chewy, bouncy) baked as **muffins** for easier home-kitchen production and pickup sales.  
Not traditional Asian dessert—this is a modern Asian-inspired bakery item that travels great.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Baked mochi donut muffins
- **Asian twist:** Mochi chew (glutinous rice flour) + optional matcha/black sesame
- **Yield:** 12 muffins
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Total time:** ~35–40 min
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup-friendly:** muffins/mini cakes, no melting.
- **Unique texture:** chewy “mochi” bite stands out vs normal muffins.
- **Batch-friendly:** bake 12 at once.
- **Upsells:** flavors (matcha, ube, black sesame), glaze options, 2/4/6-pack.

---

## Equipment
- Muffin tin (12 cup)
- Paper liners (recommended)
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale recommended)
- Oven

---

## Ingredients (12 muffins)

## A) Base batter
- **200 g** glutinous rice flour (sweet rice flour)
- **60 g** sugar
- **1 tsp** baking powder
- **1/4 tsp** salt
- **2 large eggs**
- **240 ml** whole milk (or coconut milk for tropical vibe)
- **60 g** unsalted butter, melted (or neutral oil)
- **1 tsp** vanilla

## B) Flavor options (choose ONE)
### Option 1 — Classic vanilla (no add-in)
- Just base batter.

### Option 2 — Matcha
- **1–1.5 Tbsp** matcha powder (sifted)

### Option 3 — Black sesame
- **2 Tbsp** black sesame paste (or 30 g ground toasted black sesame)

### Option 4 — Ube (purple yam)
- **120 g** ube halaya (ube jam) OR 1–2 tsp ube extract + extra sugar to taste

## C) Optional glaze (pickup-friendly)
### Simple vanilla glaze
- **120 g** powdered sugar
- **2–3 Tbsp** milk
- Optional: a pinch of salt

### Matcha glaze
- Add **1 tsp matcha** to glaze

### Black sesame topping
- Sprinkle sesame seeds on wet glaze

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Prep
1. Preheat oven to **180°C**.
2. Line muffin tin with paper liners.

---

## 2) Mix batter (10 minutes)
1. Whisk dry: glutinous rice flour, sugar, baking powder, salt.
2. Whisk wet: eggs, milk, melted butter, vanilla.
3. Combine wet into dry and whisk until smooth.

Add your chosen flavor:
- Matcha: whisk in sifted matcha
- Black sesame: whisk in paste
- Ube: fold in ube jam (don’t overmix)

**Batter texture:** thick but pourable.

---

## 3) Bake (18–22 minutes)
1. Fill liners 3/4 full.
2. Bake until tops puff and a toothpick comes out mostly clean:
   - **18–22 minutes** (depends on oven)

---

## 4) Cool + glaze (optional)
1. Cool 10 minutes.
2. Dip tops in glaze or drizzle.
3. Let glaze set 10 minutes before boxing.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Texture:** chewy and bouncy, not crumbly.
- **Top:** lightly golden, not hard.
- **Flavor:** clear matcha/sesame/ube note if used.

## Storage
- Room temp (airtight): 1–2 days best chew
- Refrigerated: 3 days (bring to room temp for best texture)
- Freeze: 1 month (thaw room temp)

---

## Allergens
- Egg
- Dairy (milk/butter)
- Gluten-free (glutinous rice flour contains no gluten)
