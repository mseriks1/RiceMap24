# Pancit Batil Patung (Tuguegarao) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A regional noodle specialty from Tuguegarao, Cagayan: **fresh miki noodles with sautéed meat/veg toppings + a side bowl of “batil” (egg-drop beef stock)**.  
This dish feels unique (less “tourist-common”) and works great for RiceMap24 as a premium noodle bowl with an optional lechon topping.

**Source recipe:** Panlasang Pinoy — “Pancit Batil Patung Recipe”  
https://panlasangpinoy.com/pancit-batil-patung-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino (Cagayan / Tuguegarao)
- **Style:** Noodles + toppings + egg-drop stock (“batil”)
- **Yield (source):** 4 portions (as written)
- **Active time:** ~20 min (if stock is ready)  
- **Total time:** 1 hr+ *(stock can be long-simmered; see workflow)*
- **Skill level:** Medium (two components + assembly)

---

## What makes it sellable (RiceMap24)
- **Distinctive**: not adobo/sinigang; customers remember it.
- **Modular**: you can offer add-ons (extra egg, extra lechon, extra batil).
- **Batch-friendly**: stock can be made ahead; toppings cook fast.

---

## Equipment
- Stock pot (for batil stock)
- Wide pan or wok (for noodles + toppings)
- Small saucepan (to finish egg-drop batil)
- Colander/strainer
- Ladle
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Noodles + toppings
- 6 oz minced beef (or carabeef)
- 12 oz fresh miki noodles
- 3–4 oz pork liver, sliced
- 1 medium red onion, cubed
- 1 1/2 cups mung bean sprouts
- 3/4 cup chopped green onions
- 1 cup shredded cabbage
- 1 cup carrot, julienned
- 3 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
- 3 Tbsp cooking oil
- 1 egg (for poaching on the noodles)
- 6 oz lechon carajay, chopped (optional but highly recommended topping)

## B) Batil (stock + egg-drop)
- 1 lb beef bones with meat
- 1 beef cube
- 6–8 cups water (top up as needed)
- 1 stalk celery, chopped
- 1 medium carrot, cubed
- 1 small onion, cubed
- 1 egg (for egg-drop batil)

## C) Serving dip (essential)
- Chopped onion
- Soy sauce
- Vinegar
- Optional: hot chili slices

---

## Workflow Plan (Recommended)
1) Make **batil stock** (best ahead)  
2) Cook toppings + noodles in a wok  
3) Poach an egg in the noodle broth  
4) Turn the wok broth into **egg-drop batil** in a saucepan  
5) Assemble bowl + serve with dip

---

## 1) Make Batil Stock (best ahead)
### Quick version (minimum)
1. Boil water, add beef bones + mirepoix (celery, carrot, onion).
2. Add beef cube.
3. Simmer **at least 60–90 minutes** until tasty.

### Best version (source-style)
1. Boil water, add beef bones. Boil 5 minutes.
2. Bundle celery + carrot + onion in cheesecloth as a mirepoix bag; add to pot.
3. Add beef cube.
4. Simmer **up to 3 hours** (top up water if needed).
5. Strain and reserve stock.

**RiceMap24 tip:** Make stock as a weekly batch and portion into containers.

---

## 2) Cook Noodles + Toppings (Wok)
1. Heat **3 Tbsp oil** in a wide pan/wok.
2. Sauté cubed red onion until soft.
3. Add minced beef; sauté 3–5 minutes.
4. Stir in sliced liver; sauté 3 minutes.
5. Add bean sprouts, green onions, cabbage, carrots; sauté 2–3 minutes.
6. Pour in **4–5 cups** beef stock and bring to a boil.

---

## 3) Cook Noodles in the Broth
1. Push meat/veg to one side of wok.
2. Place fresh miki noodles into the broth side.
3. Toss noodles while cooking.
4. Add **3 Tbsp soy sauce** and **1/4 tsp black pepper**.
5. Cook 2–3 minutes until noodles are hot and coated.

---

## 4) Poach Egg (Classic topping)
1. Push noodles to one side to make a small “pool” of broth.
2. Crack **1 egg** gently into the broth.
3. Poach until cooked to your preference.
4. Remove poached egg and set aside.

---

## 5) Make the “Batil” Egg-Drop Soup
1. Remove noodles from wok and plate them.
2. Scoop the remaining broth from wok into a small saucepan.
3. Bring to a boil.
4. Crack **1 egg** into the boiling broth and **stir quickly** to create egg ribbons.
5. Cook 1–2 minutes while stirring until it looks like an egg-drop soup.
6. Pour into serving bowls/cups (one per customer).

---

## 6) Assemble (RiceMap24 Service)
Per bowl:
1. Noodles base
2. Poached egg on top
3. Meat + veg topping
4. Optional: lechon carajay on top
Serve with:
- A side cup/bowl of batil (egg-drop stock)
- Dip: chopped onion + soy sauce + vinegar (+ chili)

**How customers eat it:** They gradually pour the batil over the noodles and add dip to taste.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Stock:** savory and beefy (not bland) — the batil makes the dish.
- **Noodles:** coated, not watery.
- **Egg-drop:** visible ribbons, not “scrambled lumps.”
- **Veg:** still crisp-tender.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Suggested)
- Noodles: 250–300 g cooked
- Topping: 180–250 g
- Batil: 250–300 ml side cup
- Lechon add-on: 40–60 g

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
- Multiply noodle/toppings by **2.5×**.
- For batil stock: make **at least 4–5 liters** so you can use:
  - 4–5 cups for cooking noodles/toppings
  - 10 × 250 ml for side batil cups
  - extra for top-ups

**Ops tip:** Pre-portion onions/veg; pre-cook minced beef and liver mix in advance, then finish quickly to order.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
This dish is best with components separated:

- Container A: noodles + toppings (no batil poured yet)
- Cup B: batil (egg-drop stock) sealed
- Cup C: dip (onion + soy + vinegar) sealed
- Optional cup: lechon topping
- Optional: chili slices

Label: “Pour batil over noodles gradually. Add dip to taste.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Stock holds 3–4 days refrigerated; freeze well.
- Noodles best day-of; if holding, keep broth separate.
- Reheat batil to a boil; make egg-drop fresh if possible.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Egg
- Liver (if customers have dietary restrictions)
- Gluten possible (depending on noodle brand)
