# Mayak Eggs (Korean Soy-Marinated “Addictive” Eggs) — RiceMap24 Side Order Standard

A perfect **side order / add-on**: jammy eggs marinated in a sweet-salty soy bath with garlic, onion, scallion, and chili.  
**Why it sells:** looks gorgeous (amber eggs + green onions + chili), keeps well, batch-friendly, and pairs with rice bowls, ramen, bibimbap, fried rice, and more.

**Reference recipe:** My Korean Kitchen — “Mayak Eggs”  
https://mykoreankitchen.com/mayak-eggs/

- **Category:** Korean • Side order / add-on
- **Yield:** 10 eggs (adjustable)
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** 8–12 hours marinate (overnight best)
- **Skill level:** Easy

## Ingredients (10 eggs)

### A) Eggs
- **10 large eggs**

### B) Marinade base
> The My Korean Kitchen version uses a soy-based sauce with aromatics (onion, green onion, garlic, chili) and sweeteners.  
Use the exact balance from the reference recipe if you want it identical; below is a practical, repeatable kitchen standard that matches the style.

- **240 ml** soy sauce (light/regular)
- **240 ml** water (or kelp-water if you do the kelp soak step)
- **60 ml** mirin
- **35–45 g** honey (or rice syrup) + **20–30 g** sugar (adjust sweetness)
- **12 g** garlic, minced (about 3–4 cloves)
- **80–120 g** onion, very finely diced
- **50–70 g** green onion, sliced
- **1–2** red chili, chopped (optional)
- Optional: **1 tsp** ginger powder or 5 g fresh ginger, grated
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** sesame seeds
- Optional: **1 tsp** sesame oil (add at the end for aroma)

> **Taste target:** sweet–salty with a fresh onion/garlic bite. It should taste a bit strong by itself.

---

## Eggs: Jammy Yolk Timing (Highly Recommended)
For that “premium photo” center:
- **6 min 30 sec** (jammy)
- **7 min 30 sec** (slightly firmer jam)

### Foolproof method
1. Bring water to a gentle boil.
2. Lower eggs in carefully (room temp eggs reduce cracking).
3. Stir gently for the first 30–60 seconds (helps center yolks).
4. Boil for your chosen time.
5. Transfer immediately to **ice water** for 10 minutes.
6. Peel under a thin stream of water for best results.

---

## Marinade Method (10 minutes)
1. In a bowl/container, mix soy sauce, water, mirin, honey/syrup, and sugar until dissolved.
2. Add diced onion, sliced green onion, garlic, and chili.
3. Optional: add sesame seeds.  
4. Optional: add sesame oil right before serving (not required).

---

## Marinate Eggs (Overnight)
1. Place peeled eggs in a container.
2. Pour marinade over eggs until fully covered.
3. Refrigerate:
   - **Minimum:** 6 hours
   - **Best:** 8–12 hours
4. If eggs aren’t fully submerged, flip them halfway.

---

## Serving (Side Order / Add-on)
### Standard side order (best presentation)
- Cut eggs in half.
- Spoon a little marinade with onion/scallion over the eggs.
- Add sesame seeds + a few chili slices for color.

### “Over rice” mini add-on
- 1 whole egg or 2 halves
- 1 tbsp marinade drizzled
- Optional: sprinkle of sesame seeds

---

## Quality Checks
- **Yolk:** jammy, not chalky.
- **Whites:** clean, not rubbery.
- **Marinade:** balanced, not overly salty (adjust with a splash of water if needed).

---

## Portioning + Cost Control
- **1 egg add-on:** 1 egg + 10–15 ml marinade
- **2-egg side:** 2 eggs + 20–30 ml marinade
- **4-egg family:** 4 eggs + 40–60 ml marinade

---

## Scaling (50 eggs batch)
Multiply by **5×**:
- Soy sauce: 1.2 L
- Water: 1.2 L
- Mirin: 300 ml
- Honey/syrup + sugar: 5×
- Aromatics: 5×

**Ops tip:** Use a hotel pan + small wire rack to keep eggs submerged evenly.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best for delivery:**
- Cup A: eggs (halved)  
- Cup B: marinade (separate) OR spoon a small amount over eggs and include extra cup  
- Keep onion/scallion mostly in the marinade cup (prevents soggy look)

Label: “Pour marinade over eggs (or rice) right before eating.”

---

## Storage & Food Safety
- Keep refrigerated at all times.
- Best within **3 days** for peak texture and freshness.
- Discard if left at room temp > 2 hours.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Sesame (if used)
