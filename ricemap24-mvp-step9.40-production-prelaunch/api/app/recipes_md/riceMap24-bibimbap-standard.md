# Bibimbap (Korean Mixed Rice Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Korean bowl built from warm rice + seasoned vegetables + optional beef + gochujang sauce, finished with an egg. Highly customizable and perfect for “choose your toppings” upsells.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Bibimbap”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/bibimbap

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Rice Bowl • Build-Your-Own
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min (multi-component prep)
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (separate veg prep + assembly)

## Equipment
- Large pot or rice cooker
- 2–3 skillets (or cook components in batches)
- Cutting board + knife
- Mixing bowls
- Tongs/spatula
- Measuring spoons

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Bibimbap is component-based. The exact amounts and seasoning ratios (soy/sesame/garlic) vary by style.  
> Use the linked Maangchi recipe for **exact measurements** for each vegetable and the optional beef.

### Base
- Steamed short-grain rice (enough for 4 bowls)

### Vegetables (classic set)
- Spinach
- Bean sprouts
- Carrot (julienned)
- Zucchini (julienned or sliced)
- Mushrooms (shiitake or other, sliced)
- Cucumber (optional)
- Korean radish (optional)
- Kimchi (optional side)

### Protein (optional)
- Thinly sliced beef (or ground beef), marinated per source

### Egg
- 4 eggs (fried sunny-side or soft yolk)

### Seasoning set (used across components; per source)
- Soy sauce
- Minced garlic
- Sesame oil
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Sugar (small amount, if specified)
- Salt
- Neutral oil (for cooking)

### Sauce (gochujang-based; per source)
- Gochujang
- Sesame oil
- Sugar or honey
- Vinegar/water (if specified)
- Toasted sesame seeds

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1. **Cook rice first** and keep warm.
2. Prepare and cook vegetables **one at a time** (fast, clean flavors).
3. Cook beef (if using).
4. Fry eggs last (for best texture).
5. Mix sauce and portion into cups for service.

---

## Component Methods (Easy-to-Follow)

## A) Spinach (blanch + season)
1. Blanch spinach briefly in boiling water until just wilted.
2. Rinse under cold water; squeeze out excess water.
3. Season with a little soy sauce/salt, garlic, sesame oil, and sesame seeds (per source).

## B) Bean sprouts (blanch + season)
1. Blanch sprouts until just tender.
2. Drain well.
3. Season similarly (salt/garlic/sesame oil/seeds per source).

## C) Carrot + zucchini (quick sauté)
1. Heat a pan with a little oil over medium-high.
2. Sauté carrots briefly (keep crisp-tender). Season lightly.
3. Sauté zucchini briefly in the same way.

## D) Mushrooms (sauté)
1. Sauté mushrooms until they release water and begin to brown.
2. Season lightly per source.

## E) Beef (optional)
1. Marinate beef per source.
2. Cook quickly over medium-high until just done.

## F) Sauce (gochujang)
Mix the sauce ingredients per source. Taste and adjust:
- More sweet: add a little sugar/honey
- More nutty: add sesame oil
- More heat: add more gochujang

---

## Assembly (Bowl format)
1. Add hot rice to each bowl.
2. Arrange vegetables in sections around the bowl (colorful “wheel”).
3. Add beef (optional).
4. Top with a fried egg.
5. Add a spoonful of sauce (or serve sauce in a side cup).
6. Garnish with sesame seeds.

**Serving instruction:** “Mix well before eating.”

---

## Quality Checks
- Vegetables should be **distinct** (not all tasting the same).
- Sauce should be **balanced** (spicy-sweet-salty).
- Egg yolk (if soft) adds richness when mixed.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply ingredients by **2.5×**.
- Cook vegetables in **larger batches** but keep them separate.
- Sauce: mix a larger batch and portion into cups.

**Batch note:** Bibimbap is ideal for a “topping bar” workflow.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best:** 2-compartment or salad-bowl style:
- Rice at bottom
- Toppings arranged on top (keep egg separate if possible)
- Sauce in a **mini cup**

Label: “Mix everything + add sauce to taste.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Vegetables:** Refrigerate up to 2 days (best day-of for crispness).
- **Rice:** Best fresh; reheat with a splash of water covered.
- **Sauce:** Keeps 5–7 days refrigerated.
