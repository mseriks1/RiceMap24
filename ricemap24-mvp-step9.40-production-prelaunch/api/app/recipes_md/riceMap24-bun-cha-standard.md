# Bun Cha (Vietnamese Grilled Pork & Noodle Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Hanoi classic: caramelized grilled pork served with rice vermicelli, fresh herbs, pickled veg, and a bright dipping sauce (nuoc cham).  
Designed for home kitchens: **exact batch weights, per-bowl portions, grill/pan workflow, and delivery packaging.**

**Source recipe:** RecipeTin Eats — “Bun Cha (Vietnamese Meatballs!)”  
https://www.recipetineats.com/bun-cha-vietnamese-meatballs/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Vietnamese • Noodle Bowl • Grill / Pan Sear
- **Yield:** **4 portions**
- **Active time:** ~25 min  
- **Total time:** ~55–75 min *(includes 30 min marinate; longer is better)*
- **Skill level:** Medium (browning + sauce balance)

---

## Equipment
- Grill / grill pan / skillet (cast iron works great)
- Mixing bowls (marinade, pork, sauce)
- Tongs
- Knife + cutting board
- Small saucepan (optional for warming sauce)
- Digital scale (recommended for repeatability)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Pork (2 textures: slices + meatballs)
### Pork slices
- **400 g** pork shoulder or pork neck, sliced thin (4–6 mm)

### Pork meatballs
- **400 g** ground pork (20–30% fat preferred)

### Marinade (for BOTH pork types)
- **30 ml** fish sauce (2 Tbsp)
- **30 ml** soy sauce (2 Tbsp)
- **30 g** brown sugar (2 Tbsp packed)
- **15 ml** lime juice (1 Tbsp)
- **15 ml** neutral oil (1 Tbsp)
- **10 g** garlic, minced (about 2 cloves)
- **10 g** lemongrass, very finely minced *(or lemongrass paste; keep consistent)*  
- **2 g** ground black pepper (about 1/2 tsp)

> Note: Exact marinade ratios can vary by version; keep aligned with the source recipe and adjust only after testing.

---

## B) Nuoc Cham (dipping sauce)
- **120 ml** fish sauce (1/2 cup)
- **120 ml** water (1/2 cup)
- **60 ml** lime juice (1/4 cup)
- **50 g** sugar (about 1/4 cup)
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1** red chili, sliced (optional)

---

## C) Bowl base + veg
- Rice vermicelli (bun): **300 g dried** (≈ 75 g dry per bowl)  
- Lettuce: **1 small head** or equivalent
- Cucumber: **1**, sliced
- Fresh herbs (big flavor): mint + cilantro + Thai basil (or regular basil)

### Quick pickles (optional but recommended)
- **200 g** carrot, julienned
- **200 g** daikon (or extra carrot), julienned
- **120 ml** rice vinegar (1/2 cup)
- **25 g** sugar (2 Tbsp)
- **5 g** salt (1 tsp)

---

## Prep (Do this before cooking)

### 1) Make quick pickles (15–30 min)
1. Mix vinegar + sugar + salt until dissolved.
2. Add carrot/daikon. Press down to submerge. Rest **15–30 minutes**.

### 2) Make nuoc cham (10 min)
1. Stir fish sauce + water + sugar until sugar dissolves.
2. Add lime juice, garlic, and chili. Rest **10 minutes** (flavor improves).

### 3) Marinate pork (30+ min)
1. Mix marinade ingredients.
2. Toss pork slices with half the marinade.
3. Mix ground pork with remaining marinade. Form **12 meatballs** (~65–70 g each).
4. Marinate covered:
   - **Minimum:** 30 minutes  
   - **Best:** 2–6 hours refrigerated

### 4) Cook noodles (last-minute or prepped)
1. Cook vermicelli per package instructions (often soak/boil briefly).
2. Drain, rinse cold, drain well.
3. Portion into **4 nests**.

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Repeatable)

## 1) Grill / pan-sear pork
### Heat setup
- Grill/grill pan/skillet: **medium-high** (hot enough to caramelize sugar without burning instantly).

### Pork slices
1. Lay slices in a single layer.
2. Cook **2–3 minutes** per side until caramelized and cooked through.
3. Rest 2 minutes.

### Meatballs
1. Cook meatballs **6–8 minutes total**, turning to brown all sides.
2. Check doneness: **74°C / 165°F** internal temp.

> Batch rule: Cook in **batches**. Crowding = steaming, no char.

---

## Bowl Assembly (RiceMap24 portion control)

### Per bowl targets
- **75 g** dried vermicelli (or ~180–220 g cooked; depends on brand)
- **200 g** cooked pork total (mix of slices + meatballs; adjust as you like)
- **120–150 ml** nuoc cham (served as “soup-dip” bowl or cup)
- Handful lettuce + herbs + cucumber + pickles

### Assembly flow
1. Noodle nest in main bowl.
2. Add lettuce/herbs/cucumber/pickles on the side/top.
3. Add pork (slices + 3 meatballs) on top.
4. Serve nuoc cham in a separate cup/bowl (or poured partly into bowl per your style).

**Customer note:** “Dip pork in sauce, or pour sauce over noodles. Add herbs for freshness.”

---

## Quality Checks
- **Pork:** Caramelized edges, juicy inside (don’t overcook).
- **Sauce:** Sweet–salty–sour balance; lime should be bright.
- **Noodles:** Well-drained (watery noodles dilute sauce).

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply everything by **2.5×**.

### Targets (10 bowls)
- Pork slices: **1000 g**
- Ground pork: **1000 g**
- Vermicelli: **750 g dried**
- Nuoc cham: **2.5×** all amounts
- Pickles: **2.5×** all amounts

### Ops tips
- Form meatballs ahead and portion into trays.
- Keep nuoc cham in a squeeze bottle or jug; stir before service.
- Grill/pan in batches; hold cooked pork warm (covered loosely).

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality (3-part):**
- Container A: noodles + veg + herbs (dry)
- Container B: pork (separate so heat doesn’t wilt herbs)
- Container C: nuoc cham (sealed cup)

Label: “Add pork to noodles. Pour/dip with nuoc cham. Toss with herbs.”

**Holding time:** Best within **2 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Pork keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated; reheat in hot pan for best texture.
- Nuoc cham keeps **5–7 days** refrigerated.
- Noodles best day-of (can clump; rinse and drain if needed).
