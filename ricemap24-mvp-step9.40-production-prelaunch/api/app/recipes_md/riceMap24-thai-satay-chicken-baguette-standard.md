# Thai Satay Chicken Baguette (Peanut-Lime Sauce) — RiceMap24 Standard (Breakfast/Lunch)

A premium, super-sellable baguette: satay-style chicken + peanut-lime sauce + crunchy slaw.  
Fusion (not strictly traditional), designed for pickup quality and strong repeat orders.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Baguette sandwich (Asian twist)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Thai satay profile
- **Yield:** 4 baguettes
- **Active time:** ~20–25 min
- **Total time:** ~30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Peanut sauce sells** (high-margin, addictive).
- **Crunchy slaw** looks premium and balances rich sauce.
- **Fast production** and easy batch scaling.
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra peanut sauce cup, spicy version.

---

## Ingredients (4 baguettes)

## A) Chicken
- **700 g** boneless chicken thighs (preferred), sliced
- Salt + pepper
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil

### Quick satay-style seasoning (fast)
- **1 tsp** curry powder
- **1 tsp** brown sugar
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **1 clove** garlic, grated
- Optional: 1/2 tsp turmeric (color)

---

## B) Peanut-lime sauce (the identity)
- **120 g** peanut butter
- **2 Tbsp** hoisin sauce *(or 1 Tbsp soy + 1 Tbsp sugar if no hoisin)*
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1 Tbsp** brown sugar (adjust)
- **1 tsp** grated garlic
- **150–200 ml** warm water (to loosen to “spreadable drizzle”)
- Optional: **1–2 tsp** sriracha or chili flakes

**Delivery tip:** keep sauce slightly thicker than dipping sauce so it doesn’t run.

---

## C) Crunch slaw
- **250 g** shredded cabbage
- **1 carrot**, julienne
- **1 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** sugar
- Pinch salt
- Optional: chopped cilantro

Toss lightly (don’t overdress).

---

## D) Bread + finish
- **4** baguettes (15–18 cm)
- Cucumber sticks (optional)
- Crushed peanuts (premium look)
- Cilantro (optional)
- Optional: fried shallots (tiny, premium)

---

## Equipment
- Skillet
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make slaw (5 minutes)
- Toss cabbage + carrot + vinegar + sugar + salt (+ cilantro).

---

## 2) Make peanut-lime sauce (5 minutes)
1. Mix peanut butter, hoisin, soy, lime, sugar, garlic.
2. Add warm water gradually until smooth and drizzle/spread thickness.
3. Add chili if using.

---

## 3) Cook chicken (8–10 minutes)
1. Toss chicken with curry powder, sugar, soy, ginger, garlic.
2. Sear in hot skillet with oil until browned and cooked through.

---

## 4) Build baguettes
Per baguette:
1. Split baguette.
2. Spread 1 Tbsp peanut sauce inside (or drizzle).
3. Add chicken portion (160–190 g cooked).
4. Add slaw.
5. Finish with crushed peanuts + optional cilantro.

---

## Portioning (Per baguette)
- Chicken: 160–200 g cooked
- Slaw: 60–90 g
- Peanut sauce: 30–40 g

---

## Batch for 10 baguettes
- Chicken: 1.8 kg
- Peanut sauce: 2.5×
- Slaw: 600 g cabbage + 4 carrots

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Wrap in parchment.
- If pickup is long: pack slaw in a cup and customer adds (keeps crunch).
- Offer extra peanut sauce cup as upsell.

Label: “Add slaw last for maximum crunch.”

---

## Storage
- Peanut sauce: 3–5 days refrigerated
- Slaw best same day
- Cooked chicken: 2 days refrigerated

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Wheat (baguette)
- Gluten possible (hoisin/soy brands)
