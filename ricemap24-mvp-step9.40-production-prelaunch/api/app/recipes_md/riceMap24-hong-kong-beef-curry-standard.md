# Hong Kong Beef Curry (咖喱牛腩) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Hong Kong–style curry with **tender beef chunks**, potatoes, carrots, and a curry gravy mellowed with coconut milk.  
Designed for home kitchens: **exact quantities, step timing, and delivery-ready packaging**.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Hong Kong Beef Curry”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/beef-curry/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Hong Kong / Cantonese-inspired
- **Style:** Beef chunks braised in curry gravy
- **Yield (source):** **8 servings**
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~2 hr 15 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (spice blooming + braise)

---

## Equipment
- Heavy-bottomed pot / Dutch oven (6–8 L)
- Wok or skillet (for quick onion sauté; optional but recommended)
- Knife + cutting board
- Wooden spoon
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale optional)
- Colander/strainer (for blanch step)

---

## Ingredients (8 servings) — from source
- **3 lb (1.3 kg)** boneless beef chuck, cut into **large 1½-inch cubes**  
- **1/4 cup oil** *(plus 1 Tbsp, divided)*  
- **4 cloves** garlic, minced  
- **5** shallots, finely chopped  
- **One 6-inch** section of lemongrass  
- **3** bay leaves  
- **1/3 cup** good curry powder  
- **1 tsp** turmeric  
- **4 cups** chicken broth *(or water)*  
- **4 tsp** sugar  
- **2 Tbsp** tomato paste  
- **2 medium onions**, cut into large chunks  
- **3 large potatoes**, cut into large chunks  
- **2 carrots**, cut into large chunks  
- **1½ cups coconut milk (355 ml)**  
- **1½ tsp salt**, or to taste  

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Beef:** Cut beef chuck into **1½-inch (4 cm)** cubes.
2. **Aromatics:** Mince garlic; finely chop shallots; cut lemongrass into a 6-inch section.
3. **Vegetables:** Cut onions, potatoes, carrots into **large chunks** (so they hold shape).
4. Measure curry powder + turmeric, broth, coconut milk, tomato paste, sugar, salt.

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Detailed)

### 1) Blanch the beef (clean flavor)
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
2. Add beef chunks and bring back to a boil.
3. **Drain immediately** and set beef aside.

> This removes impurities and keeps the curry gravy cleaner.

---

### 2) Infuse oil + bloom spices (do not burn!)
1. In a heavy pot over **medium-low** heat, add **1/4 cup oil**.
2. Add **garlic, shallots, lemongrass, bay leaves**.
3. Cook a few minutes to infuse oil.
4. Turn heat to **lowest setting**.
5. Add **curry powder + turmeric** and stir until well combined.

**Critical:** If the curry powder starts sticking, add a little more oil. Do not burn the spices.

---

### 3) Start the braise (60–75 minutes)
1. Add:
   - blanched beef  
   - **4 cups broth (or water)**  
   - **4 tsp sugar**  
   - **2 Tbsp tomato paste**
2. Turn heat up and bring to a boil.
3. Once boiling, stir, then reduce to **medium**.
4. Cover and simmer **60–75 minutes**, stirring occasionally to prevent burning.

---

### 4) Quick-sauté onions (optional but recommended)
While beef simmers:
1. Heat **1 Tbsp oil** in a wok/skillet over **medium** heat.
2. Cook onions until transparent (~2 minutes).
3. Set aside.

---

### 5) Add coconut milk + potatoes + carrots (20 minutes)
1. After the initial simmer, add:
   - **1½ cups coconut milk**
   - potatoes
   - carrots
2. Turn heat up and bring to a boil.
3. Reduce to **medium-low**, cover, and simmer **20 minutes**.

---

### 6) Finish + thicken
1. By now, beef and vegetables should be tender.
2. Add sautéed onions and **salt to taste** (start with **1½ tsp**, adjust).
3. If sauce is too thin: uncover and simmer to reduce until thick enough to coat beef but still pourable over rice.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef:** Fork-tender (if not, simmer longer; add a splash of water if needed).
- **Sauce:** Thick curry gravy, not watery.
- **Veg:** Tender but intact (large chunks help).

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Curry beef in leak-proof container.
- **Rice separate** (prevents sogginess, improves presentation).
- Include note: “Reheat gently. Sauce thickens when chilled—add a splash of water if needed.”

**Holding time:** Excellent; flavors deepen after resting.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **3 days**.
- Reheat gently on stovetop or microwave; stir well.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes
- **Contains:** Coconut milk
- **May contain:** Gluten (some curry powders/stock brands vary—check labels)
