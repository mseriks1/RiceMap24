# Fu Qi Fei Pian (夫妻肺片) — Sichuan Sliced Beef in Chili Sauce — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A real Sichuan classic that’s **rarely done well outside Chinese communities**: tender sliced beef (often mixed cuts) dressed in a **chili-oil–forward sauce** with garlic, ginger, vinegar, and Sichuan peppercorn.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 as a **premium “spicy beef plate”** or a **rice-bowl topper**.

**Source recipe (reference):** Omnivore’s Cookbook — Fu Qi Fei Pian (Updated Oct 25, 2022)  
https://omnivorescookbook.com/fu-qi-fei-pian/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Sichuan)
- **Style:** Braised beef → thin slices → chili oil sauce (serve room temp or slightly warm)
- **Yield (source):** 6–8 servings
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 5 min (pressure) or ~2+ hr (stovetop)
- **Skill level:** Medium (braise + thin slicing + sauce balance)

---

## Equipment
- Big pot **or** pressure cooker/Instant Pot
- Fine mesh skimmer/strainer (helpful)
- Cutting board + sharp slicing knife
- Mixing bowls (sauce + assembly)
- Optional: mandoline or deli slicer (for ultra-thin slices)

---

## Ingredients (6–8 servings)

## A) Meat (choose cuts)
- **3–3.5 lb (1.3–1.5 kg)** beef brisket, chuck, tongue, and/or tripe  
  *(Source uses a mixture of brisket and tongue.)*

> RiceMap24 tip: brisket + chuck is easiest for most home kitchens. Tongue/tripe can be offered as an optional “authentic upgrade.”

---

## B) Braising Seasoning (source)
- **1/4 cup** light soy sauce (or soy sauce)
- **1/4 cup** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- **1 Tbsp** sugar
- **4** dried chili peppers
- **3** green onions, halved lengthwise
- **2** large slices ginger
- **1/2** cinnamon stick
- **1** bay leaf
- **1 tsp** Sichuan peppercorns
- **1 tsp** salt
- **1/4 tsp** nutmeg powder
- Water: as needed (see method)

---

## C) Sauce (source)
- **4 Tbsp** chili oil **with** **2 Tbsp** chili flake residue (or to taste)
- **3 Tbsp** braising liquid (from the pot)
- **2 Tbsp** flavored sweet soy sauce (see note for substitute)
- **2 tsp** Chinkiang vinegar
- **1/4 tsp** salt
- **1/4 tsp** ground toasted Sichuan peppercorns (or to taste)
- **3 cloves** garlic, grated
- **2** green onions, chopped
- **1 tsp** ginger, grated

### Sweet soy substitute (if you don’t have it)
- Use **2 Tbsp soy sauce + 2 tsp sugar** (sauce will be thinner).

---

## D) Toppings (choose)
- Chopped cilantro
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Chopped toasted peanuts

---

## Method

# Part 1 — Braise the Meat

## Option 1: Stovetop (source method)
1. Put meat in a large pot and add water to cover by about **1 inch**.
2. Bring to a boil over medium-high, then boil **10 minutes**.
3. Skim foam until the broth looks clear.
4. Add all braising seasoning ingredients.
5. Cover and simmer on medium-low until meat is tender **but not falling apart**, about **2 hours** (time varies by cut).

## Option 2: Pressure Cooker / Instant Pot (source method)
1. Put meat in the cooker.
2. Add all braising seasoning ingredients **plus 1 cup water**.
3. Cook on **High Pressure 35 minutes**.
4. Natural release.

---

# Part 2 — Slice the Meat (presentation is everything)
1. Remove meat and cool until safe to handle.
2. For tongue: peel off and discard the outer tough skin after cooking.
3. Slice **against the grain** as thin as possible (aim for deli-style thin slices).

> Pro tip: chill the cooked meat 1–2 hours before slicing for cleaner, thinner slices.

---

# Part 3 — Make the Sauce
1. In a bowl, mix all sauce ingredients until combined.
2. Best flavor: make sauce **a few hours ahead** and let it sit.

---

# Part 4 — Assemble and Serve
1. Arrange beef slices on a deep plate (or portion into bowls).
2. Spoon a generous amount of sauce over the beef.
3. Top with cilantro, sesame seeds, and/or peanuts.

Serve at room temp as a cold appetizer, or slightly warm (many people prefer a gentle warm-up).

---

## Quality Checks
- **Meat texture:** tender but still sliceable (not shreddy).
- **Slices:** thin and even (this dish is about texture + sauce cling).
- **Sauce:** chili oil fragrance up front, balanced by vinegar and peppercorn “tingle.”

## RiceMap24 Serving Formats (Very Sellable)

### Format A — Spicy Beef Plate (appetizer / share box)
- Beef slices + sauce + peanuts + cilantro
- Optional: cucumber side

### Format B — Rice Bowl Topper
- 250 g cooked rice + 150–200 g beef + sauce cup
- Add-on: extra chili oil, extra peanuts

---

## Portioning (Per order)
- As appetizer: **120–160 g** beef
- As rice bowl: **180–220 g** beef
- Sauce: **25–40 ml** (or more for “extra saucy” version)

---

## Scaling for Production (10 rice bowls)
Plan **~2.0 kg cooked sliced beef** (raw weight depends on cut and trimming).
- Cook a full **1.5 kg** batch as written, then portion.
- Make sauce in bulk and bottle it for fast service.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: sliced beef (cool or room-temp safe)
- Cup B: sauce (sealed) OR sauce already poured (stronger look but messier)
- Optional cup: peanuts + sesame (keep crunchy)
- Rice bowl version: rice separate, sauce separate.

Label: “Spicy & numbing (Sichuan peppercorn).”

---

## Storage & Food Safety
- Cooked meat holds **3–4 days** refrigerated (source guidance).
- Sauce holds several days refrigerated (best 2–3 days).

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce / sweet soy)
- Peanuts (if used)
- Alcohol (Shaoxing, cooked down)
