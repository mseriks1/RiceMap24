# Pad Thai (Thai Stir-Fried Rice Noodles) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Thai icon: rice noodles stir-fried with tamarind sauce, egg, tofu (optional), and crunchy peanuts.  
Designed for home kitchens: **exact weights per portion, sauce pre-batch, and a no-fail workflow**.

**Source recipe:** Serious Eats — “Pad Thai”  
https://www.seriouseats.com/pad-thai-recipe

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai • Stir-Fry • Noodles
- **Yield:** **4 portions** (best cooked as **2 batches of 2 portions** for wok heat)
- **Active time:** ~25 min  
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min *(includes noodle soak)*
- **Skill level:** Medium (timing + sauce balance)

---

## Equipment
- Wok or large skillet
- Large bowl (noodle soak)
- Mixing bowls (sauce + mise en place)
- Colander/strainer
- Tongs/chopsticks + spatula
- Digital scale (recommended)
- Optional: small pan (to toast peanuts)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Noodles
- **300 g** dried flat rice noodles (banh pho-style), ~5 mm width *(≈ 75 g per portion)*  
- Cold water for soaking

## B) Pad Thai sauce (mix once; portion per batch)
- **60 g** tamarind concentrate/paste *(check strength; see notes)*
- **50 g** palm sugar (or light brown sugar)
- **45 g** fish sauce
- **10 g** soy sauce *(optional; helps round saltiness)*
- **20 g** water (as needed to loosen)

> Sauce should taste **strong** (sweet–sour–salty); it mellows when absorbed by noodles.

## C) Stir-fry components
- **30 ml** neutral oil (2 Tbsp), divided
- **12 g** garlic, minced (about 3 cloves)
- **8 g** shallot, minced (optional but recommended)
- **4** eggs (1 per portion)
- **200 g** firm tofu, cut into 1 cm cubes *(optional; very common)*
- **200 g** cooked shrimp **or** chicken *(optional protein; see notes)*
- **80 g** bean sprouts (about 2 cups loosely packed)
- **40 g** garlic chives (or scallions), cut into 4–5 cm lengths
- **60 g** roasted peanuts, crushed

## D) To serve (required for “authentic feel”)
- Lime wedges (at least 1 per bowl)
- Extra bean sprouts (optional)
- Chili flakes (optional)

---

## Prep (Do this before heating the wok)

### 1) Soak noodles (critical)
1. Put dried noodles in a large bowl.
2. Cover with **room-temperature water** and soak **30–45 minutes** until pliable but still slightly firm in the center.
3. Drain thoroughly and shake dry.

> Don’t over-soak. Noodles finish cooking in the wok.

### 2) Make sauce (5 minutes)
1. Warm palm sugar with tamarind and a small splash of water until dissolved (or whisk vigorously if using soft sugar).
2. Whisk in fish sauce and soy sauce.
3. Taste: it should be **punchy** (a bit too strong is right).

**Portioning:** Divide sauce into **2 equal bowls** (each bowl = 2 portions).

### 3) Prep stir-fry mise en place (10 minutes)
- Mince garlic + shallot.
- Cube tofu.
- Portion sprouts, chives, peanuts.
- Crack eggs into 4 small cups (1 egg each) to speed service.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, Home-Kitchen Friendly)

> Cook **2 portions at a time** for better heat and less clumping.

## Batch #1 (2 portions)
1. Heat wok/skillet over **high heat** until very hot (light smoke).
2. Add **1 Tbsp oil (15 ml)**.
3. Add half the garlic + shallot; stir **10–15 seconds** (don’t burn).
4. Add **half the tofu** and stir-fry **60–90 seconds** until lightly golden.
5. Add noodles (half the soaked noodles). Toss **30 seconds**.
6. Pour in **half the sauce**. Toss continuously **1–2 minutes** until noodles absorb sauce and soften.
7. Push noodles aside. Add **2 eggs** into open space; scramble lightly **10–15 seconds**.
8. Fold eggs into noodles.
9. Add **half the sprouts** + **half the chives**. Toss **20–30 seconds** (keep crisp).
10. Add **half the peanuts**. Toss and plate immediately.

## Batch #2
Repeat with remaining ingredients.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Noodles:** Tender but chewy; not mushy.
- **Sauce:** Coats noodles; no watery puddle.
- **Aroma:** Tamarind + fish sauce balance; lime brightens at the table.
- **Veg:** Sprouts still crisp.

---

## Protein Notes (Choose ONE style)
- **Shrimp:** Add cooked shrimp near the end (with sprouts) so it doesn’t overcook.
- **Chicken:** Use fully cooked sliced chicken; add with tofu step to warm through.
- **No protein:** Offer as vegetarian with tofu (but note fish sauce is not vegetarian unless swapped).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl)
- Dried noodles: **75 g**
- Sauce: **~35–40 g** (adjust after first test batch)
- Tofu/protein: **optional** (80–120 g)
- Peanuts: **15 g**
- Sprouts/chives: to preference

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply ingredients by **2.5×**.

### Operational tips
- Pre-soak noodles and portion into 10 nests (well-drained).
- Pre-batch sauce into **5 cups** (each cup = 2 portions).
- Cook in **5 wok batches** of 2 portions.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Use a wide container (noodles clump in deep cups).
- Include **lime wedge** in a small cup.
- Optional: peanuts in a small cup to keep crunch maximum.

**Holding time:** Best within **1 hour**; noodles continue absorbing sauce.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Pad Thai is best fresh.
- If reheating: add a splash of water and toss in a hot pan.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Fish (fish sauce), egg, peanuts, soy (tofu/soy sauce)
- **Gluten:** Usually no (check soy sauce brand)
- **Spicy:** Optional

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Noodle soak is the #1 success factor—aim “pliable, still firm.”
- Cook in 2-portion batches for proper wok heat.
- Plate fast—stir-fry noodles degrade if they sit in the pan.
