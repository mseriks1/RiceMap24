# Thịt Nướng Sả (Vietnamese Lemongrass Grilled Pork) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Vietnamese “banker” that sells everywhere: fragrant **lemongrass grilled pork** served over rice (cơm) or vermicelli (bún), with pickles and a dipping sauce.  
For RiceMap24, it’s perfect because it’s **fast to cook**, easy to scale, highly photogenic (char + herbs), and works great for delivery (protein + rice separate).

**Reference recipe (recommended):** Hungry Huy — Vietnamese Grilled Pork (Thịt Nướng) / Lemongrass Pork  
https://www.hungryhuy.com/vietnamese-grilled-pork-thit-nuong/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Marinated grilled pork + rice + pickles + sauce
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~25 minutes
- **Total time:** ~1.5–4 hours (marinate time)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (marinade + high-heat cook)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **Broad appeal:** sweet-salty grilled pork with lemongrass aroma.
- **Fast production:** grill/broil/pan-sear in batches.
- **Great delivery:** pork stays flavorful; easy plating with rice + pickles.
- **Upsells:** extra pork, extra sauce, add egg roll, add extra pickles, spicy chili cup.

---

## Choose Your Pork (Pick ONE)
### Option A (best banker): Pork shoulder / butt
- **1.5 kg** pork shoulder, thinly sliced (3–5 mm)

### Option B (leaner): Pork loin
- **1.2–1.4 kg** pork loin, thinly sliced (watch cook time)

> Banker choice: **pork shoulder** (juicier, forgiving, great char).

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Marinade (lemongrass-forward)
- **4 stalks** lemongrass (white/tender part only), very finely minced  
  *(or 3 Tbsp lemongrass paste if needed)*
- **6 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2 Tbsp** shallot (or red onion), minced
- **3 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **2.5 Tbsp** sugar (or 2 Tbsp brown sugar)
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce (optional, adds depth and color)
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1 Tbsp** oyster sauce (optional, adds gloss)
- **1 tsp** black pepper
- Optional for color: **1 tsp** annatto powder or a small spoon of caramel color (nước màu)

## B) Pork
- **1.5 kg** thin-sliced pork (above)

---

## C) Serving set (RiceMap24)
- Steamed jasmine rice: **250 g cooked** per order
- Quick pickles (đồ chua): carrot + daikon (recommended add-on)
- Cucumber slices + herbs (mint/cilantro) optional but photogenic
- Dipping sauce (nuoc cham) cup (see below)

---

## D) Nuoc Cham (dipping sauce)
- **90 ml** fish sauce
- **180 ml** water
- **70–80 g** sugar
- **60 ml** lime juice
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1–2** chilies, sliced (optional)

Taste target: sweet–salty–sour, bold but balanced.

---

## Equipment
- Grill / grill pan / oven broiler (best for volume: broiler + rack)
- Mixing bowl + gloves (mixing marinade)
- Tongs
- Sheet pan + rack (for broiler)
- Knife + cutting board
- Small cups for sauce

---

## Prep (15 minutes)

### 1) Slice pork thin
- Aim for **3–5 mm** slices so it cooks fast and chars nicely.

### 2) Mix marinade
1. Combine lemongrass, garlic, shallot, fish sauce, sugar, oil, pepper.
2. Add soy sauce and oyster sauce if using.
3. Mix well.

### 3) Marinate
1. Add pork and coat thoroughly.
2. Cover and refrigerate:
   - **Minimum:** 1 hour
   - **Best:** 3–4 hours

> Don’t marinate overnight if you use a lot of fish sauce; it can get overly cured/salty.

### 4) Make nuoc cham
- Stir sugar into water, then add fish sauce, lime, garlic, chili. Portion into cups.

---

## Cooking Method (3 reliable options)

## Option 1 (Best for volume): Oven broiler
1. Preheat broiler to high.
2. Line a sheet pan with foil and place a rack on top.
3. Lay pork slices in a single layer.
4. Broil **3–5 minutes**, flip, broil **2–4 minutes** until charred edges and cooked through.
5. Rest 3 minutes.

## Option 2: Grill / grill pan
- Medium-high heat; grill slices **1–2 minutes per side** depending on thickness.

## Option 3: Skillet sear (batch)
- Hot pan + small oil; sear slices quickly, avoid overcrowding.

**Doneness check:** pork reaches **63°C** and rests (carryover) OR no pink remains for thin slices.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Char:** caramelized edges without burning.
- **Texture:** juicy, not dry (don’t overcook).
- **Aroma:** lemongrass and garlic prominent.
- **Balance:** sweet-salty, not overly fishy.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Grilled pork: **180–220 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Pickles: **60–90 g**
- Nuoc cham: **30–40 ml** cup

**Upsells**
- Extra pork (double)
- Extra nuoc cham
- Extra pickles
- Add fried egg
- Add spring roll (side order)

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Pork: ~2.5 kg
- Lemongrass: 7 stalks
- Fish sauce: ~5 Tbsp
- Sugar: ~4 Tbsp
- Oil: ~3–4 Tbsp
- Sauce: 1.7×
Cook in broiler batches for fastest service.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: pork (vent 1 minute before sealing to reduce steam)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Cup C: nuoc cham (sealed)
- Cup D: pickles (optional)
- Optional herb/cucumber pack (dry)

Label: “Pour nuoc cham over pork/rice. Add pickles last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Pork keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat quickly (hot pan or brief microwave).
- Sauce keeps 3–5 days refrigerated.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Soy (if using soy sauce)
- Shellfish (if using oyster sauce)
- Gluten possible (some sauces)
