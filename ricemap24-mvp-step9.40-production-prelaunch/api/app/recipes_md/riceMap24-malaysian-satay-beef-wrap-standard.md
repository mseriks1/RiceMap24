# Malaysian Satay Beef Wrap (Peanut + Lime + Cucumber) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A Malaysian-inspired wrap built around classic satay flavors: grilled/seared beef, peanut sauce, cucumber crunch, and fresh herbs.  
Fusion (wrap format), designed for lunch pickup and high repeat orders.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Malaysian-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Satay (Malaysia/SE Asia)
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~30 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Satay flavor sells** and is easy to recognize.
- **Peanut sauce is high-margin** and supports upsell cups.
- **Pickup-friendly:** sauce separate option keeps wrap clean.
- **Upsells:** extra beef, extra sauce cup, spicy version.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Beef
- **650 g** beef strips (sirloin/strip/flat iron), thin slices
- Salt + pepper
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil

### Satay-style marinade (quick)
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** brown sugar
- **1 tsp** curry powder
- **1 tsp** ground coriander
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **2 cloves** garlic, grated
- Optional: 1/2 tsp turmeric (color)
- Optional: 1 tsp chili flakes (heat)

Marinate 15–30 minutes.

---

## B) Peanut sauce (Malaysian satay vibe)
- **120 g** peanut butter
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** brown sugar (or palm sugar)
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1 tsp** grated garlic
- **150–200 ml** warm water (to loosen)
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** tamarind water (adds Malaysian tang)
- Optional: 1–2 tsp chili sauce

**Target texture:** thick drizzle / spreadable.

---

## C) Crunch + freshness
- Lettuce leaves (barrier)
- **1 cucumber**, thin sticks
- **1 small red onion**, thin slices (optional)
- Fresh cilantro or mint (optional)
- Optional: crushed peanuts (finish)

---

## D) Wraps
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps

---

## Equipment
- Skillet/wok or grill pan
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (recommended)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Marinate beef (15 minutes)
- Mix marinade and toss beef. Rest 15–30 min.

---

## 2) Make peanut sauce (5 minutes)
1. Mix peanut butter, soy, sugar, lime, garlic.
2. Add warm water gradually until smooth.
3. Optional: tamarind water + chili for deeper Malaysian profile.

---

## 3) Cook beef (6–8 minutes)
1. Heat skillet high with oil.
2. Sear beef in batches until browned.
3. Reduce any watery juices quickly.

---

## 4) Assemble wraps
Per wrap:
1. Warm tortilla briefly.
2. Add lettuce barrier.
3. Add beef (160–200 g cooked).
4. Add cucumber (and onion/herbs).
5. Drizzle peanut sauce (or keep sauce in cup).
6. Finish with crushed peanuts.

Roll tight.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- Beef: 160–200 g cooked
- Sauce: 30–40 g
- Cucumber/veg: generous handful

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Beef: 1.6 kg
- Sauce: 2.5×
- Cucumbers: 3

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- Wrap A: beef + veg wrap (rolled)
- Cup B: peanut sauce (customer drizzles/dips) for clean pickup
- Optional: crushed peanut packet

**Label:** “Sauce on the side (recommended).”

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Wheat (tortilla)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce brands)
