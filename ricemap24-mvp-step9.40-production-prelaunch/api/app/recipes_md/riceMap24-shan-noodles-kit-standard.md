# Shan Noodles (Shan Khauk Swe / ရှမ်းခေါက်ဆွဲ) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Myanmar regional favorite (Shan State): rice noodles topped with a savory **tomato-garlic chicken (or pork) sauce**, finished with crunchy peanuts, scallions, and optional pickled mustard greens.  
This is a fantastic **home-kitchen kit**: sauce + noodles + toppings pack separately and assemble fast.

**Reference recipe (inspiration):** Myanmar Cooking / Shan Noodles style overview  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shan_noodles  
(Shan noodles vary by family/region; this card standardizes a reliable “RiceMap24” version with common flavor structure: tomato + garlic + lightly oily sauce + toppings.)

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Burmese / Myanmar (Shan)
- **Style:** Rice noodles + tomato meat sauce + toppings (warm or room temp)
- **Yield:** 4 meal bowls
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~45 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (sauce reduction + noodle assembly)

---

## Equipment
- Medium pot (sauce)
- Pot/kettle for noodles
- Knife + cutting board
- Mixing bowl (seasoning noodles)
- Ladle
- Small cups for toppings

---

## Ingredients (4 bowls)

## A) Noodles
- **300 g** dried rice noodles (medium thickness)
- Warm water + boiling water (cook per package)

## B) Protein (choose one)
### Option A — Chicken (recommended)
- **500 g** ground chicken *or* finely chopped chicken thigh

### Option B — Pork
- **500 g** ground pork

### Option C — Vegetarian
- **400–500 g** finely chopped mushrooms + 200 g firm tofu (crumbled)

---

## C) Shan tomato-garlic sauce base
- **3 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1** onion, finely diced
- **6 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** grated ginger (optional but good)
- **2 Tbsp** tomato paste
- **400 g** canned crushed tomatoes *(or 4 ripe tomatoes, grated)*
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce *(or soy sauce for veg version)*
- **1 Tbsp** light soy sauce *(optional for depth; skip if you want “less soy”)*
- **1–2 tsp** sugar (balance tomatoes)
- **1 tsp** paprika (optional, color)
- **1/2 tsp** turmeric (optional, Myanmar-friendly warmth)
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper
- **120–200 ml** water or light stock (as needed)
- Salt to taste

---

## D) Noodle seasoning (simple, essential)
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil *(or garlic oil if you have it)*
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce *(optional; small amount)*
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce *(or soy for veg)*

> This light seasoning makes noodles flavorful even before sauce hits.

---

## E) Toppings (photogenic + sales)
Pick 5–7:
- Roasted peanuts, chopped (2–4 Tbsp)
- Scallions, sliced (2–3 Tbsp)
- Cilantro (optional)
- Fried garlic chips or fried shallots (optional)
- Pickled mustard greens (optional, very Myanmar)
- Lime wedges
- Chili oil (optional but great)
- Hard-boiled eggs (halved) — premium add-on

---

## Prep (10–12 minutes)
1. Dice onion; mince garlic; (optional) grate ginger.
2. Prep toppings (peanuts, scallions, lime, chili oil cups).
3. If using pickled mustard greens, chop small and keep in a cup.
4. Get noodle water ready (cook noodles close to service).

---

## Sauce Method (Repeatable, “RiceMap24”)

### 1) Build the base (8 minutes)
1. Heat **3 Tbsp oil** over medium.
2. Add onion; cook 3 minutes until softened.
3. Add garlic (and ginger); cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
4. Stir in tomato paste; cook 30 seconds.

### 2) Add tomatoes + season (15–20 minutes)
1. Add crushed tomatoes.
2. Add fish sauce, soy sauce (if using), sugar, paprika/turmeric (if using), and pepper.
3. Simmer uncovered 10–15 minutes until thickened and glossy.

### 3) Cook protein in sauce (10 minutes)
1. Add ground chicken/pork (or veg mix).
2. Break up well and simmer until cooked through and sauce is thick:
   - Ground meat: ~8–10 minutes
   - Veg version: 6–8 minutes

**Texture target:** not soupy. It should sit on noodles like a topping.

### 4) Final balance
- If sauce is too thick: add 2–3 Tbsp water.
- If too thin: simmer longer.
- Taste and adjust salt/fish sauce/sugar.

---

## Noodles + Assembly

### 1) Cook noodles
- Cook per package, then drain well.
- Rinse briefly with warm water if very starchy.
- Drain thoroughly (wet noodles dilute sauce).

### 2) Season noodles
In a bowl, toss hot noodles with:
- 2 Tbsp oil
- 1 Tbsp fish sauce (or soy)
- Optional 1 Tbsp soy sauce

### 3) Build bowls (best look)
Per bowl:
1. Noodles (base)
2. 3–5 heaping Tbsp meat sauce on top
3. Peanuts + scallions + optional greens
4. Chili oil drizzle (optional)
5. Lime wedge on the side

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sauce:** thick, tomato-garlic forward, lightly oily sheen.
- **Noodles:** coated lightly, not watery.
- **Toppings:** crunchy element (peanuts) present.
- **Balance:** lime at the end brightens everything.

## Scaling (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Noodles: 750 g dry
- Protein: 1.25 kg
- Tomatoes: ~1 kg crushed tomatoes
- Oil: 7–8 Tbsp
- Garlic: 15 cloves
- Onion: 2–3
- Toppings: pre-portion into 10 cups

**Ops tip:** Make a big pot of sauce; portion it. Cook noodles closer to service for best texture.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
Best “kit” format:
- Container A: noodles (seasoned, not wet)
- Cup B: sauce (sealed)
- Cup C: toppings (peanuts + scallions)
- Cup D (optional): pickled mustard greens
- Cup E (optional): chili oil
- Lime wedge in small bag/cup

Label: “Pour sauce over noodles. Add toppings. Squeeze lime.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Sauce: 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Noodles: best same day; if needed, store separately and refresh with hot water.
- Reheat sauce gently.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Soy (if using soy sauce)
- Peanuts (topping)
