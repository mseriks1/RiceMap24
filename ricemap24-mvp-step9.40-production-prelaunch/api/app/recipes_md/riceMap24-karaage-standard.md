# Japanese Karaage (Crispy Fried Chicken) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Ultra-crispy Japanese fried chicken with a soy-ginger-garlic marinade and a light starch coating.  
Designed for home-kitchen repeatability: **weights, times, oil temp, batch sizes, and packaging tips**.

**Source recipe:** Serious Eats — “Karaage (Japanese Fried Chicken)”  
https://www.seriouseats.com/karaage-japanese-fried-chicken-recipe

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Fried • Snack / Main
- **Yield:** **4 portions** (or ~20–24 pieces depending on cut size)
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~60–90 min (includes marinating)
- **Skill level:** Medium (frying temperature control)

## Equipment
- Heavy pot or deep skillet (for frying)
- Thermometer (highly recommended)
- Wire rack + sheet pan (best for draining)
- Mixing bowls (marinade + coating)
- Tongs or spider strainer
- Paper towels (backup only)
- Digital scale (recommended)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Chicken
- **900 g** boneless, skinless chicken thighs  
  *(Thighs are strongly recommended: juicier and more forgiving.)*

### Marinade
- **60 ml** soy sauce (1/4 cup)
- **30 ml** sake (2 Tbsp)
- **30 ml** mirin (2 Tbsp)
- **15 ml** fresh ginger, finely grated (1 Tbsp)
- **15 ml** garlic, finely grated (1 Tbsp)

### Coating
- **120 g** potato starch (katakuriko)  
  *(If unavailable, cornstarch works but texture changes.)*

### Frying
- Neutral oil (canola/peanut), enough for **4–5 cm** depth in your pot

### Optional (service)
- Lemon wedges
- Japanese mayo (optional dip)
- Shichimi togarashi (optional sprinkle)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Cut chicken
1. Trim excess fat if desired.
2. Cut thighs into **bite-size pieces** (~4–5 cm chunks).  
   Aim for consistent size so frying time is consistent.

### 2) Marinate (minimum 30 min)
1. Combine soy sauce, sake, mirin, ginger, garlic in a bowl.
2. Add chicken and mix until fully coated.
3. Cover and marinate:
   - **Minimum:** 30 minutes  
   - **Best:** 1–2 hours (refrigerated)

> Tip: Don’t marinate overnight unless your source method explicitly suggests it—texture can change.

### 3) Set up coating station
- Put potato starch in a wide bowl or tray.
- Prepare a wire rack on a sheet pan for draining.

---

## Frying Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### Oil temperature targets
- **First fry:** **160°C** (320°F) — cooks through gently
- **Second fry:** **180°C** (356°F) — crisps and colors

> Double-frying is the “pro” karaage texture: juicy inside, crackly outside.

---

## Step-by-step

### 1) Coat chicken
1. Take chicken out of marinade and let excess drip off (don’t wipe clean).
2. Toss pieces in potato starch until fully coated.
3. Shake off excess starch (too much = gummy crust).

### 2) First fry (cook through)
1. Heat oil to **160°C**.
2. Fry in **small batches** (don’t crowd): add pieces one by one.
3. Fry **~3–4 minutes** until outside is pale-golden and chicken is mostly cooked.
4. Remove to wire rack and rest while you fry remaining batches.

### 3) Second fry (crisp finish)
1. Increase oil to **180°C**.
2. Refry each batch **~45–75 seconds** until deep golden and very crisp.
3. Drain on rack.

### Doneness check
- Target internal temperature: **74°C** (165°F) at thickest piece.
- If you don’t have a thermometer: cut one piece—juices should run clear, no pink.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Exterior:** Crackly crisp, not dusty.
- **Interior:** Juicy, not stringy.
- **Color:** Deep golden (not dark brown).

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Target amounts (10 portions)
- Chicken thighs: **2250 g**
- Soy sauce: **150 ml**
- Sake: **75 ml**
- Mirin: **75 ml**
- Ginger: **37.5 ml** (≈ 2.5 Tbsp)
- Garlic: **37.5 ml** (≈ 2.5 Tbsp)
- Potato starch: **300 g** (start here; add more if needed)

### Batch notes
- Fry in batches of **8–12 pieces** depending on pot size.
- Keep first-fried chicken on racks; refry in the same batch order.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
Karaage loses crispness when sealed hot. Use packaging strategy:

- Use a **vented** container or crack the lid for 2–3 minutes before sealing.
- Keep lemon wedge in a small side cup.
- If offering dip (mayo), keep in a sealed cup.

**Holding time:** Best within **45–60 minutes** for peak crispness.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to 2 days.
- **Reheat for crispness:** Oven/air fryer at **190–200°C** for **6–10 min**.  
  Microwave softens the crust.
