# Hainanese Chicken Rice — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Southeast Asian classic: tender poached chicken, fragrant rice cooked in chicken fat/stock, and a trio of sauces. Excellent for premium meal boxes.

**Source recipe:** Rasa Malaysia — “Hainanese Chicken Rice”  
https://rasamalaysia.com/hainanese-chicken-rice/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Singapore/Malaysia • Poached Chicken • Rice Plate
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~25 min  
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 10 min *(includes poach + rest)*
- **Skill level:** Medium (poaching + rice timing)

---

## Equipment
- Large pot with lid (fits whole chicken or large pieces)
- Instant-read thermometer (recommended)
- Large bowl with ice water (for ice bath)
- Fine strainer (optional)
- Rice cooker or pot for rice
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Chicken + Broth
- **1 whole chicken** (about **1.3–1.6 kg**) *(or equivalent bone-in pieces)*
- Water (enough to submerge chicken in pot)
- **Ginger** (sliced)
- **Scallions** (cut into lengths)
- **Salt** (for seasoning)
*(Use the exact aromatics and quantities as specified in the source recipe.)*

## B) Fragrant Rice
- Jasmine rice (uncooked) *(amount per source recipe)*
- Chicken fat / oil *(per source)*
- Garlic + ginger *(per source)*
- Chicken stock/broth from poaching *(per source)*

## C) Sauces (serve as add-on cups)
- Ginger-scallion sauce *(per source)*
- Chili sauce *(per source)*
- Dark soy (optional) *(per source)*

## D) Sides
- Sliced cucumber
- Fresh cilantro (optional)

> **Important:** Hainanese chicken rice is a “system recipe” (broth → rice → sauces).  
> Follow the linked source recipe for **exact measurements** for rice ratios and sauces.

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Clean chicken:** Pat dry. (If the source includes a salt rub step, do it.)
2. **Aromatics:** Slice ginger; cut scallions into long pieces.
3. **Ice bath:** Prepare a bowl of ice water (for crisp skin and stopping carryover cooking).
4. **Rice:** Rinse jasmine rice until water runs mostly clear; drain well.

---

## Method (Process Flow)

## 1) Poach the chicken (gentle, not boiling)
1. Bring a pot of water to a **gentle boil**, then reduce to **low simmer**.
2. Add ginger + scallions (and salt per source).
3. Lower chicken into the pot. Return to a **gentle simmer** (no rolling boil).
4. Poach until chicken is cooked through per source timing.  
   - **Target internal temp:** **74°C / 165°F** at thickest part.
5. Remove chicken and immediately place into the **ice bath** for several minutes (per source).
6. Drain, then rest chicken. (Rub with a little oil if the source instructs to keep skin glossy.)

## 2) Make the rice (the signature step)
1. Render chicken fat / heat oil (per source) with garlic + ginger until fragrant.
2. Add drained rice and stir to coat.
3. Cook rice with measured poaching broth/stock (per source ratio) in rice cooker or pot.

## 3) Sauces
Prepare ginger-scallion sauce and chili sauce per the source recipe. Portion into small cups for service.

## 4) Slice + plate
1. Chop/slice chicken into serving pieces.
2. Serve with fragrant rice, cucumber, and sauce cups.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Chicken:** Tender, juicy; skin smooth (not torn).
- **Rice:** Fragrant, not wet; grains distinct.
- **Sauces:** Bright and punchy; should lift the mild chicken.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Chicken: **2–3 whole chickens** depending on size/yield  
- Rice + sauces: multiply exact source measurements by **2.5×**

### Batch notes
- Poach chickens in batches to keep water temp stable.
- Keep poaching broth—use for rice and optional soup cup upsell.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Best:** 3-compartment box:
  1) rice  
  2) sliced chicken  
  3) cucumber + sauce cups
- Add a small label: “Sauces: ginger • chili • soy (optional). Mix as you like.”

**Holding time:** Very good. Chicken stays tender; sauces keep fresh in cups.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Chicken:** Refrigerate up to **2 days**. Reheat gently (steam/microwave medium).
- **Rice:** Best same day; reheat with a splash of water covered.
- **Sauces:** Keep 3–5 days refrigerated (perishable ingredients—use good hygiene).

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Keep the poach at a **gentle simmer** to avoid tough chicken.
- Ice bath improves skin texture and stops overcooking.
- The rice is where customers feel the “authentic” difference—don’t rush it.
