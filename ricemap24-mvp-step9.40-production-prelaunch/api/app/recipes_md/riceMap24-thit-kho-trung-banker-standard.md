# Thịt Kho Trứng (Vietnamese Caramelized Braised Pork & Eggs) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A true Vietnamese “banker”: tender pork braised in a savory-sweet caramel coconut broth with eggs.  
It’s famously made for family meals and holidays because it **keeps well**, reheats perfectly, and tastes even better the next day — ideal for RiceMap24 home kitchens.

**Reference recipe (recommended):** Vicky Pham — “Thịt Kho Trứng (Braised Pork Belly with Eggs in Coconut Juice)”  
https://vickypham.com/blog/braised-pork-and-boiled-eggs-in-coconut-juice-thit-kho-tau/

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Caramelized braise (pork + eggs) served with rice
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 45 min to 2 hr 30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (caramel + gentle simmer)

- **Broad appeal:** sweet-salty comfort braise.

## Choose Your Pork (Pick ONE)
### Option A (classic): Pork belly
- **1.5 kg** pork belly, cut into **4–5 cm** chunks

### Option B (leaner + still great): Pork shoulder/butt
- **1.5 kg** pork shoulder, cut into 4–5 cm chunks

> Banker choice: **pork shoulder** (less fatty, still tender, often preferred by wider audiences).  
> Premium choice: **pork belly** (most traditional).

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Main
- 1.5 kg pork (above)
- **8 eggs** (chicken eggs), hard-boiled and peeled

## B) Braise liquid + seasoning
- **2–3 cups (500–750 ml)** coconut water (unsweetened)  
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce (to start; adjust to taste)
- **1 Tbsp** sugar (for seasoning; caramel sugar is separate)
- **1 tsp** salt (optional; depends on fish sauce brand)
- **1 tsp** black pepper (plus more to finish)

## C) Aromatics (recommended)
- **1 small onion** (or 2 shallots), sliced
- **5 cloves** garlic, smashed or minced (optional but common)

## D) Caramel color (Nước màu)
- **3 Tbsp** sugar
- **2 Tbsp** water

> Caramel gives the signature amber color and deeper flavor.

---

## Equipment
- Large heavy pot (5–6 L) with lid
- Saucepan or the same pot (for caramel)
- Slotted spoon
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Cut pork into 4–5 cm chunks.
2. Hard-boil eggs (see timing below) and peel.
3. Slice onion (and prep garlic if using).
4. Measure coconut water, fish sauce, sugar, pepper.

### Hard-boiled egg timing (clean peel)
- Simmer 10 minutes → ice bath 10 minutes → peel under running water.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Optional “clean boil” (recommended for a clearer braise)
1. Put pork in a pot and cover with water.
2. Bring to a boil for **3–5 minutes**.
3. Drain and rinse pork quickly.

> This reduces scum and makes a cleaner sauce (better for photos).

---

## Step 2 — Make caramel (Nước màu) (3–5 minutes)
1. In a pot, combine **3 Tbsp sugar + 2 Tbsp water**.
2. Heat over medium until it turns deep amber (like cola).
3. As soon as it’s amber, carefully proceed to the next step.

**Warning:** caramel is extremely hot. Don’t touch/splash.

---

## Step 3 — Build the braise (5 minutes)
1. Add onion (and garlic) into the pot briefly.
2. Add pork and stir to coat in caramel.
3. Add coconut water (start with 500 ml).
4. Add fish sauce + 1 Tbsp sugar + black pepper.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.

---

## Step 4 — Gentle simmer until tender (60–90 minutes)
1. Simmer partially covered until pork is fork-tender.
2. Stir occasionally and skim excess fat if you want a cleaner finish.
3. If liquid reduces too much, add a splash of coconut water or hot water.

**Tenderness target:** pork should be soft but still hold shape.

---

## Step 5 — Add eggs (final 20–30 minutes)
1. Add peeled eggs to the pot.
2. Simmer gently **20–30 minutes** so eggs absorb flavor and color.

**Optional premium look:** lightly score eggs with 2–3 shallow cuts so sauce penetrates and eggs look darker.

---

## Step 6 — Final seasoning
Taste and adjust:
- Needs more salty depth → a little more fish sauce
- Too salty → add coconut water or a splash of water + a pinch of sugar
- Needs more “roundness” → a pinch more sugar
- Finish with freshly cracked black pepper

---

## Quality Checks
- **Color:** glossy amber-brown.
- **Pork:** tender, not dry.
- **Eggs:** lightly stained by sauce, flavorful.
- **Sauce:** savory-sweet, not watery (should lightly coat pork).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Braise (pork + egg + sauce): **450–550 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Egg: **1–2 halves** (or 1 whole egg for premium)

**Upsells**
- Extra egg
- Extra pork
- Extra sauce cup
- Add pickled veg (đồ chua) as a side

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Pork: ~2.5 kg
- Eggs: 14
- Coconut water: 1–1.2 L (adjust)
- Fish sauce: start 3–4 Tbsp, then balance at end

**Ops tip:** Make one big pot; portion with a ladle so each box gets equal sauce.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: thịt kho (leak-proof)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Optional cup: extra sauce
- Optional side: pickles/greens

Label: “Reheat gently. Pour sauce over rice.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps **3–4 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently to a simmer (stovetop or microwave).
- Flavor improves on day 2.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Egg
- Coconut water (coconut)
