# Dorayaki (Japanese Honey Pancake Sandwiches) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

Soft, fluffy **honey pancakes** sandwiched with sweet **anko (red bean paste)**.  
Perfect for **pickup/takeaway**: no melting, travels well, and sells beautifully as **2-pack / 4-pack**.

## Ingredients (Makes 6 dorayaki) — from reference
- **1⅓ cups (160 g)** all-purpose flour
- **4 large eggs**
- **140 g** sugar (⅔ cup + ½ Tbsp)
- **2 Tbsp** honey
- **1 tsp** baking powder
- **1–2 Tbsp** water (adjust batter consistency)
- Neutral oil (for greasing the pan)
- **1.1 lb (500 g)** sweet red bean paste (anko) — store-bought or homemade

---

## Prep Notes (For a perfect “no-spot” golden color)
- Dorayaki look best when the pan is **almost dry** (just a film of oil).
- Always **wipe the pan with a paper towel** between pancakes.
- Cook over **medium-low** heat. Too hot = uneven spots + tough pancakes.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

### 1) Make batter (5 minutes)
1. In a large bowl, whisk **eggs + sugar + honey** until well combined and slightly foamy.
2. Sift in **flour + baking powder**.
3. Whisk just until smooth (do not overmix).
4. Add **1 Tbsp water** and mix. If batter is thick and doesn’t pour smoothly, add the remaining **1 Tbsp**.

**Target batter texture:** thicker than crepe batter, thinner than cookie dough—should flow slowly from a spoon.

### 2) Rest batter (15 minutes)
- Cover and rest **15 minutes** at room temperature.  
This helps the pancakes become fluffier and more even.

### 3) Cook pancakes (15–20 minutes)
1. Heat a nonstick skillet over **medium-low**.
2. Lightly oil the pan, then **wipe off excess** with a paper towel.
3. Pour batter to make ~8–9 cm pancakes (use a ladle/measuring cup for consistency).
4. Cook until bubbles appear on the surface and edges look set, about **1–2 minutes**.
5. Flip and cook the other side **about 1 minute**.
6. Move to a rack to cool.
7. Repeat, **wiping the pan** between pancakes.

**Consistency tip:** If pancakes brown too fast, reduce heat and give the pan 30–60 seconds to cool before the next one.

### 4) Assemble dorayaki (5 minutes)
1. Pair pancakes into 6 “tops” and 6 “bottoms” (match similar sizes).
2. Spread **anko** on one pancake (about **30–40 g** per dorayaki, depending on your pricing).
3. Sandwich with the second pancake and press gently.

- **Filling:** centered and not leaking from the sides.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Cool pancakes fully before boxing to avoid condensation.
- Use parchment squares between pieces to prevent sticking.
- Best boxes: small bakery clamshell or paper dessert box.

**Label suggestion:** “Best at room temperature. Eat within 24 hours for peak softness.”

---

## Storage
- **Best same day**.
- Assembled dorayaki: keep in a cool place or fridge up to **2 days** (bring to room temp before eating).
- Pancakes (unfilled): airtight, room temp 1 day or freeze.
- Freeze assembled pieces (wrap tightly) up to **1 month**; thaw at room temp.
