# Chai-Spiced Mango Rice Pudding Cups (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

Comfort rice pudding in single-serve cups, upgraded with **chai spices** and a bright **mango** layer.  
Not traditional Asian dessert—more like “global fusion” with South Asian tea-spice vibes. Perfect for pickup/delivery (chilled cups, no melting).

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Rice pudding cups + mango layer
- **Asian twist:** Chai spices (cardamom/cinnamon/ginger) + optional rose water
- **Yield:** 10 cups (150–180 ml each)
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Total time:** ~45 min + chill
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup-friendly:** cup dessert, stable and clean.
- **Great margins:** rice + milk base.
- **Premium look:** white pudding + orange mango layer + pistachio garnish.
- **Upsells:** extra mango, pistachios, “rose version,” larger cup.

---

## Equipment
- Saucepan
- Whisk/spatula
- 10 cups with lids
- Optional: thermometer (to avoid scorching)

---

## Ingredients (10 cups)

## A) Rice pudding base
- **180 g** short-grain rice (or arborio)
- **1 L** whole milk (or 750 ml milk + 250 ml cream for richer)
- **80–100 g** sugar (adjust)
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **1 tsp** vanilla

## B) Chai spice pack
- **6** green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- **1** cinnamon stick
- **3** cloves
- **1 tsp** fresh grated ginger (or 1/2 tsp ground)
- Optional: 1/4 tsp black pepper (tiny warmth)

## C) Mango layer
- **400 g** mango purée
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1–2 Tbsp** sugar (if needed)

## D) Finish (premium look)
- Chopped pistachios or toasted almonds
- Optional: a few saffron strands (very premium)
- Optional: 1/2 tsp rose water (stir into cooled pudding, not while boiling)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Cook rice pudding (35–40 minutes)
1. Rinse rice briefly (1–2 rinses).
2. In a saucepan, combine milk, rice, sugar, salt, and all chai spices.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium, then reduce to low.
4. Cook, stirring often, until rice is tender and pudding is thick:
   - **35–40 minutes** (varies by rice)
5. Remove from heat.
6. Remove whole spices (cardamom pods, cinnamon, cloves).
7. Stir in vanilla.
8. Optional: once cooled a bit, stir in rose water.

**Texture target:** thick enough to mound slightly; it thickens more when chilled.

---

## 2) Prepare mango layer (2 minutes)
- Mix mango purée with lime juice and sugar to balance.

---

## 3) Assemble cups (5 minutes)
Per cup:
1. Spoon rice pudding to fill 2/3 of the cup.
2. Add 1–2 Tbsp mango purée on top.
3. Swirl lightly or keep a clean layer.

---

## 4) Chill
- Refrigerate **2–4 hours** until set.

---

## Quality Checks
- Pudding thick and creamy, not runny.
- Chai aroma present but not overpowering.
- Mango bright and fresh.
