# Chicken Inasal (Bacolod-Style Filipino Grilled Chicken) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Smoky, juicy Filipino grilled chicken with a tangy marinade and a bright orange annatto basting oil.  
**European-friendly:** familiar “BBQ chicken” form, balanced acidity, and a beautiful color/shine for photos.  
Serve with rice + a small cup of vinegary dip (“sawsawan”).

**Source recipe (reference):** Serious Eats — Chicken Inasal (Filipino Grilled Chicken)  
https://www.seriouseats.com/chicken-inasal-recipe

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino (Visayas / Bacolod-style)
- **Style:** Grilled chicken + annatto basting oil + vinegar dip
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~2–6 hours (includes marinating; 2 hours minimum recommended)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (grilling + basting)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24 (Home Kitchen + Sales)
- **Looks amazing**: orange gloss from annatto oil + grill marks.
- **Smells “BBQ-good”**: no strong fermented fish aromas.
- **Easy upsells**: add extra basting oil, extra dip, garlic rice, extra chicken leg, spicy dip.

---

## Equipment
- Grill / grill pan / oven broiler (best: charcoal or gas grill)
- Tongs
- Small saucepan (for basting oil)
- Mixing bowls (marinade + dip)
- Knife + cutting board
- Optional: instant-read thermometer (recommended)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Chicken
- **1.2–1.5 kg** chicken thighs/drumsticks/leg quarters (bone-in, skin-on recommended)

---

## B) Marinade (tangy + aromatic)
> Use calamansi if possible; otherwise lime + a little orange works well.

- **120 ml** vinegar (cane/coconut vinegar preferred; white wine vinegar works)
- **120 ml** calamansi juice (or 80 ml lime + 40 ml orange)
- **240 ml** lemon-lime soda (yes—this is classic; it helps tenderness + caramelization)
- **30 g** brown sugar (about 2 Tbsp packed)
- **10 g** salt (about 2 tsp)
- **5 g** black pepper (about 1 tsp)
- **20 g** garlic, minced (about 5–6 cloves)
- **20 g** ginger, minced (about 1 Tbsp)
- **30 g** lemongrass, very finely minced (white/light-green parts only)
- Optional (adds “Filipino” depth but still EU-friendly):
  - **1–2 tsp** Maggi seasoning OR soy sauce (small amount)

---

## C) Annatto (Achuete) Basting Oil
This is the signature look + aroma.

- **120 ml** neutral oil (canola)
- **30 g** butter (or margarine; butter tastes better)
- **2 Tbsp** annatto seeds *(or 1–2 tsp annatto powder)*  
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** minced garlic (for “garlic chicken oil” finish)

---

## D) Sawsawan (Vinegar Dip)
- **80 ml** vinegar (same as above)
- **40 ml** soy sauce (optional; keeps it familiar for EU palates)
- **1–2** chilies, sliced (optional)
- **1 Tbsp** chopped onion or scallions
- **1 tsp** sugar (optional balance)
- Black pepper to taste

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Prep chicken for even cooking
- Pat chicken dry.
- If using thick leg quarters: make **2–3 shallow slits** in the thickest meat areas (helps marinade penetration and faster cooking).

### 2) Mix marinade
1. Whisk all marinade ingredients until sugar dissolves.
2. Add chicken and coat well.
3. Marinate refrigerated:
   - **Minimum:** 2 hours  
   - **Best:** 4–6 hours

> Tip: If marinating overnight, reduce soda slightly and keep chicken very cold.

---

## Make Annatto Basting Oil (10 minutes)
1. In a small saucepan over **low heat**, warm oil and add annatto seeds.
2. Gently heat until oil turns a deep orange-red (do not fry hard).
3. Strain out seeds.
4. Stir in butter until melted.
5. Optional: add garlic and warm 30 seconds.

Keep warm (not hot) for basting.

---

## Cooking Method (Grill + Baste)

### Temperature targets
- Grill: **medium heat** (you want caramelization without burning sugar)
- Internal temp: **74°C** in thickest part

### 1) Grill (25–35 minutes)
1. Preheat grill to medium.
2. Place chicken skin-side down first.
3. Grill, turning every 4–6 minutes:
   - Start basting with annatto oil after first turn.
   - Baste every time you turn.

### 2) Finish and rest
1. Continue until chicken reaches **74°C** and skin is browned.
2. Rest 5 minutes before packing.

> Don’t grill too hot—the sugar can scorch. If flare-ups occur, move chicken to a cooler zone.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per meal)
- Chicken: **250–350 g cooked** (1 leg quarter or 2 thighs/drumsticks)
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Dip: **30–40 ml** cup
- Optional: cucumber + tomato side (simple, fresh, photo-friendly)

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Chicken: **3.0–3.75 kg**
- Marinade: multiply all ingredients by 2.5
- Basting oil: multiply by 2.5 (keep warm in a small slow cooker if available)
- Dip: multiply by 2.5 and portion into 10 cups

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: chicken (vented 2–3 minutes before sealing to protect skin)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Cup: sawsawan dip
- Optional small cup: extra annatto oil (premium upsell)

**Holding time:** Best within 1–2 hours. Reheat chicken in oven/air fryer for crisp skin.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate cooked chicken up to 2 days.
- Reheat: oven/air fryer 180–200°C until hot; microwave softens skin.

---

## Allergen Notes
- Possible soy (if using soy/Maggi)
- Dairy (butter in basting oil)
