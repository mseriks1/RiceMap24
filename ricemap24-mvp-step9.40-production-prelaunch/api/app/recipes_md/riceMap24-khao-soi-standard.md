# Khao Soi (Northern Thai Curry Noodle Soup with Chicken) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A rich, creamy Northern Thai curry noodle soup served with tender chicken, fresh egg noodles, crispy fried noodles on top, and bright condiments (lime, red onion, pickled mustard greens, fried chili flakes).

**Source recipe:** Stefan Gourmet — “Khao Soi (Northern Thai Curry Noodle Soup with Chicken)”  
https://stefangourmet.com/2025/11/25/khao-soi/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai (Northern) • Curry • Noodles
- **Yield:** 4–8 portions *(portion size depends on your bowl and noodle amount)*
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min (paste + assembly)
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 15 min (chicken simmer is the long step)
- **Skill level:** Medium (paste + gentle simmer + noodle timing)

---

## Equipment
- Heavy pot / casserole with lid (drumsticks in a single layer)
- Small pan (to toast spices + fry chili flakes)
- Spice grinder (or mortar & pestle / small blender)
- Large pot (boiling noodles)
- Frying pot/pan for noodle frying
- Strainer + tongs
- Cutting board + knife
- Thermometer (optional)

---

## Ingredients (4–8 portions)

### A) Curry paste (make first)
- **2** black cardamom
- **1 1/2 Tbsp** coriander seeds
- **20 g** ginger, thinly sliced
- **5 g** fresh turmeric, thinly sliced
- **60 g** shallots, sliced
- **20 g** mild dried chilies
- **1 tsp** coarse salt

### B) Curry noodle soup
- **500 ml (2 cups)** coconut milk
- **8** chicken drumsticks
- Water, **as needed** (to just cover chicken)
- **3 Tbsp** Thai soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** Thai black soy sauce
- **2–3 tsp** fish sauce
- **1 Tbsp** minced palm sugar

### C) Fresh egg noodles (recommended by source)
- Fresh pasta dough from **3 eggs + 300 g flour**  
  *(Portion into “soup noodles” + “crispy noodles” — see below.)*

### D) Condiments (for service)
- Lime wedges
- Sliced red onion
- Pickled mustard greens
- Chili flakes + neutral oil (to fry them)
- Cilantro or green onion (garnish)

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Make curry paste  
2) Start soup simmer (1 hour)  
3) While simmering: prep condiments + noodles + fried chili flakes  
4) Fry crispy noodles + boil soup noodles just before serving  
5) Assemble bowls

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Make the curry paste
1. **Prep chilies:** Remove stems and most seeds from **20 g dried chilies**. Cut into pieces.
2. **Grind chilies:** Grind into a fine powder (spice grinder is easiest).
3. **Cardamom:** Remove seeds from **2 black cardamom** pods.
4. **Toast:** Toast cardamom seeds + **1 1/2 Tbsp coriander seeds** in a dry pan until fragrant. Cool briefly.
5. **Toast aromatics:** Toast **ginger (20 g)**, **turmeric (5 g)**, and **shallots (60 g)** in a dry pan until charred but not burnt on both sides.
6. **Grind spices:** Grind toasted coriander + cardamom seeds to a powder.
7. **Blend/pound:** Combine chili powder, ground spices, toasted aromatics, and **1 tsp coarse salt**. Blend or pound until smooth-ish paste.

---

## 2) Cook the curry soup + chicken
1. **Separate coconut cream:** Do *not* shake the coconut milk. Scoop the thick cream from the top.
2. In a casserole over **medium heat**, heat the coconut cream with the curry paste. Stir until it sizzles and becomes very fragrant.
3. Add the remaining coconut milk and stir until dissolved.
4. Add:
   - **8 drumsticks**
   - **3 Tbsp Thai soy sauce**
   - **1 Tbsp Thai black soy sauce**
   - **1 Tbsp minced palm sugar**
   - **2 tsp fish sauce** *(start here; add more later if needed)*
5. Add **just enough water to cover** the drumsticks.
6. Cover and bring to a boil, then reduce to a **gentle simmer**.
7. Simmer until chicken is tender and nearly falling off the bone, about **1 hour**.

---

## 3) Fried chili flakes (optional, classic side heat)
1. Put chili flakes in a small pan.
2. Add just enough neutral oil to moisten.
3. Stir over **medium heat** until flakes darken and smell smoky (do not burn). Set aside.

---

## 4) Noodles (two portions per serving: soup + crispy)
### Portioning rule (from source)
- Make **two noodle portions per serving**:
  - **3/4** for boiling into the soup
  - **1/4** for frying as crispy topping

### A) Crispy fried noodles (do just before serving)
1. Heat ~**250 ml (1 cup)** neutral oil to **180°C / 350°F**.
2. Deep-fry individual noodle portions until golden, about **1 minute**.
3. Drain on paper.

### B) Soup noodles (boil last-minute)
1. Bring a pot of water (at least 1 litre) to a rapid boil.
2. Boil individual noodle portions for about **30 seconds** (fresh egg noodles cook fast).
3. Drain well.

---

## 5) Chicken handling + broth adjustment (service-ready)
1. Remove drumsticks from broth.
2. Pull meat off bones and chop into bite-size pieces.
3. Keep broth warm on very low heat.
4. Taste broth and adjust:
   - **Salt first:** add fish sauce as needed
   - **Then sweetness:** add palm sugar as needed

---

## Bowl Assembly (RiceMap24 service flow)
1. Add boiled soup noodles to bowl.
2. Add chicken pieces on top.
3. Ladle hot curry broth over.
4. Top with crispy fried noodles.
5. Serve with condiments: pickled mustard greens, red onion, lime wedge, fried chili flakes, herbs.

**Customer note:** “Squeeze lime + add mustard greens + chili to taste.”

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Broth:** Creamy, aromatic, balanced sweet–salty.
- **Chicken:** Tender, not dry (gentle simmer).
- **Noodles:** Soup noodles not overcooked; crispy noodles still crunchy.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
This recipe is written for **4–8** portions. For **10 bowls**, plan:
- **1.5× to 2×** batch depending on your bowl size and noodle portioning.

**Batch notes**
- Keep broth in one pot (holds well).
- Fry crispy noodles *to order* (they lose crispness fast).

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality:** Keep components separated:
- Box A: boiled noodles + chicken
- Cup: curry broth (hot)
- Bag/cup: crispy noodles + condiments (dry)
- Chili flakes cup (optional)

Label: “Pour broth over noodles + chicken. Add crispy noodles last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Broth + chicken:** Refrigerate up to **2 days** (flavor improves).
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer; add water if reduced too much.
- **Crispy noodles:** Fry fresh for best texture (do not refrigerate).
