# Salmon Teriyaki (Japanese Teriyaki Salmon Rice Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A reliable Japanese favorite: pan-seared salmon glazed in a classic teriyaki sauce (soy + sake + mirin + sugar) and served over rice with optional greens. Great for “premium bowl” positioning.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Salmon Teriyaki”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/salmon-teriyaki/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Glazed Fish • Rice Bowl
- **Yield:** 2 portions
- **Active time:** ~10 min  
- **Total time:** ~15–20 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (glaze timing)

---

## Equipment
- Nonstick skillet or well-seasoned pan
- Spatula/fish turner
- Small bowl (for sauce)
- Measuring spoons
- Paper towels

---

## Ingredients (2 portions)

### Salmon
- **2 salmon fillets** (about **170 g / 6 oz each**)
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (or as needed for the pan)

### Teriyaki sauce (classic Just One Cookbook style)
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** sake
- **2 Tbsp** mirin
- **1 Tbsp** sugar

### Optional (recommended for bowls)
- Cooked short-grain rice: **~250 g cooked rice per bowl**
- Blanched or sautéed greens (bok choy, spinach, or broccoli)
- Sesame seeds (garnish)

---

## Prep (5 minutes)
1. **Pat salmon dry** (better browning).
2. **Make sauce:** Mix soy sauce + sake + mirin + sugar until mostly dissolved.
3. **Rice:** Keep hot and ready.
4. Optional: Prepare greens (quick blanch or pan sauté).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Sear the salmon
1. Heat pan over **medium heat**.
2. Add oil.
3. Place salmon in the pan and cook **skin-side down first** (if skin-on) until nicely browned, about **3–4 minutes**.
4. Flip and cook the second side **2–3 minutes**, until salmon is nearly cooked through.

> **Doneness target:** Salmon should flake easily; avoid overcooking before glazing.

### 2) Glaze with teriyaki
5. Reduce heat slightly to **medium-low** if needed (to prevent sugar burning).
6. Pour in the teriyaki sauce.
7. Spoon/tilt-pan baste for **30–90 seconds** until sauce reduces and turns glossy, coating the salmon.
8. Remove salmon as soon as glaze is sticky and shiny.

> **Stop point:** Teriyaki goes from glossy to burnt quickly—stay at the pan.

---

## Serve (Bowl format)
1. Add hot rice to 2 bowls/containers.
2. Add greens (optional).
3. Top with glazed salmon.
4. Drizzle a little extra sauce (if any remains), garnish with sesame.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Glaze:** Shiny, sticky, not scorched.
- **Salmon:** Juicy, not dry.
- **Balance:** Sweet-salty; adjust sugar slightly next batch for your audience.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 2 portions. Multiply by **5×**.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Salmon fillets: **10** (≈ 170 g each)
- Oil: **5 Tbsp** (or as needed)
- Soy sauce: **10 Tbsp** (≈ 150 ml)
- Sake: **10 Tbsp** (≈ 150 ml)
- Mirin: **10 Tbsp** (≈ 150 ml)
- Sugar: **5 Tbsp** (≈ 60–65 g)
- Cooked rice: **~2500 g cooked** (≈ 250 g per bowl)

### Batch notes
- Sear salmon in **batches** to avoid crowding (crowding = steaming).
- Reduce sauce **per batch** (don’t try to glaze 10 fillets at once in one pan).

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Best with **two compartments**: rice separate from salmon to reduce steaming.
- Sauce cup as an upsell (“extra teriyaki”).

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** for best salmon texture.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle microwave (medium power). Avoid overheating (dries salmon).

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)

- **Alcohol:** Sake/mirin (typically cooked off)
