## Core concept (Soboro)
Soboro is a Japanese “crumbled” topping usually made with meat.  
This version uses **tofu crumbles** cooked down with soy, mirin, sugar, and ginger for the same satisfying texture.

---

## Ingredients (4 bowls)

## A) Tofu soboro (main topping)
- **600 g** firm tofu
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (optional; tofu can dry-fry)
- **4 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **3 Tbsp** mirin *(or 2 Tbsp sugar + 1 Tbsp rice vinegar)*
- **2 Tbsp** sugar
- **1 Tbsp** grated ginger
- **1 tsp** sesame oil (finish, optional)

## B) Rice
- Cooked short-grain or jasmine rice for 4 bowls  
  - Portion target: **250 g cooked** per bowl

## C) “Tri-color” premium toppings (choose 2–3)
### Option 1 (vegan yellow)
- **Turmeric egg-style tofu scramble** (simple):  
  - 300 g soft tofu + 1/4 tsp turmeric + pinch salt + 1 tsp oil (quick stir)

### Option 2 (green)
- Blanched spinach with sesame (quick gomaae style) OR edamame

### Option 3 (fresh)
- Cucumber sticks + pickled ginger

### Option 4 (umami)
- Nori strips + toasted sesame

> The more color you add, the more premium it looks.

## Equipment
- Skillet
- Spatula
- Bowl for mixing sauce
- Rice cooker/pot

---

## Prep (5 minutes)
1. Drain tofu and pat dry.
2. Mix soy sauce, mirin, sugar, ginger in a bowl.
3. Prep garnish: nori strips, sesame, cucumber.

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## Step 1 — Crumble tofu (2 minutes)
1. Use hands or fork to crumble tofu into small pieces.
2. Aim for “ground meat” size pieces.

---

## Step 2 — Cook tofu down (8–10 minutes)
1. Heat skillet over medium-high.
2. Add tofu crumbles (with or without oil).
3. Cook, stirring, until moisture evaporates and tofu starts to brown slightly.

**Key:** cook until tofu is fairly dry before adding sauce for best “soboro” texture.

---

## Step 3 — Season and glaze (3–4 minutes)
1. Pour in sauce mixture.
2. Stir constantly as it reduces.
3. Cook until tofu is glossy and the pan is not watery.

Finish with a tiny sesame oil drizzle (optional).

---

## Step 4 — Build bowls (RiceMap24 service)
Per bowl:
1. Rice base (250 g cooked)
2. Tofu soboro (150–200 g)
3. Add 2–3 toppings:
   - yellow tofu scramble
   - spinach/edamame
   - cucumber/pickled ginger
4. Finish with sesame + nori.

---

## Quality Checks
- Soboro is crumbly and glazed, not wet.
- Sauce is savory-sweet, ginger-forward.
- Bowl has visible color contrast (premium signal).

---

## Portioning (Per bowl)
- Rice: 250 g cooked
- Soboro: 150–200 g
- Toppings: 60–100 g total
- Optional sauce cup (upsell): 20 ml of soboro glaze

---

## Scaling (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Firm tofu: 1.5 kg
- Soy sauce: 10 Tbsp
- Mirin: 7.5 Tbsp
- Sugar: 5 Tbsp
- Ginger: 2.5 Tbsp

Cook soboro in batches for better browning.

## Storage & Reheat
- Soboro keeps 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat gently in pan or microwave.
- Assemble toppings fresh for best crunch.

---

## Allergens

- Gluten possible (soy sauce brand)
