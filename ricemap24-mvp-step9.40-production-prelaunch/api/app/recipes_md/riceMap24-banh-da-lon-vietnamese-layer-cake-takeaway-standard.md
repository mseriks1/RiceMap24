# Bánh Da Lợn (Vietnamese Pandan & Mung Bean Steamed Layer Cake) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A classic Vietnamese steamed dessert with **chewy pandan layers** and a soft **mung bean layer**.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** no melting, slices into clean cubes, looks amazing in a box, and holds well chilled.

**Reference recipe:** Vicky Pham — “Vietnamese Steamed Layer Cake with Pandan and Mung Bean Paste (Banh Da Lon)”  
https://vickypham.com/blog/vietnamese-steamed-layer-cake/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Steamed layered cake (pandan + mung bean)
- **Yield:** 1 x 9-inch / 23 cm round cake (cut 16–25 pieces)
- **Active time:** ~30 minutes
- **Total time:** ~60–80 minutes (plus cooling/chill for best cuts)
- **Skill level:** Medium (steaming layers + batter consistency)

---

## Equipment
- Steamer setup (large pot/wok + rack + tight lid)
- 9-inch round pan (or 8-inch square pan)
- Mixing bowls + whisk
- Blender or food processor (for pandan and mung bean layer)
- Fine mesh strainer/sieve (recommended)
- Ladle
- Foil or towel under lid (prevents condensation dripping)
- Cooling rack

---

## Ingredients (1 cake)

## A) Pandan batter (green layers)
- **200 g** tapioca starch
- **40 g** rice flour
- **300 g** sugar
- **400 ml** coconut milk
- **600 ml** water (use pandan water if you make it)
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **1–2 tsp** pandan extract/paste (or fresh pandan water)

### Optional pandan water (more authentic color/aroma)
- Blend **8–10 pandan leaves** with **250 ml** water, strain, then use as part of the 600 ml water.

---

## B) Mung bean layer (yellow)
- **150 g** dried split mung beans (yellow), rinsed
- Water to boil/steam beans
- **80–120 g** sugar (to taste)
- **200 ml** coconut milk
- **1/4 tsp** salt

> Some families add a little tapioca/rice flour to the mung layer for extra set; this card keeps it simple and sliceable when chilled.

---

## Prep Notes (Critical)
- **Steam lid condensation** will pit your cake surface. Wrap lid with a towel or place foil under it.
- Batters **settle quickly**. Whisk/stir before every pour.
- Thin, even layers = better honeycomb-like texture and cleaner look.

---

## Step 1 — Make the Mung Bean Paste (20–30 minutes)
1. Boil or steam mung beans until very soft (fully mashable).
2. Drain well.
3. Blend mung beans with coconut milk, sugar, and salt until smooth (thick paste).
4. Cook the paste gently in a saucepan 2–3 minutes to dry slightly (optional but helps set).
5. Cool to room temperature.

**Texture target:** smooth and spreadable, not watery.

---

## Step 2 — Make the Pandan Batter (10 minutes)
1. In a bowl, whisk tapioca starch + rice flour + sugar + salt.
2. Add coconut milk and water gradually, whisking until completely smooth.
3. Add pandan extract (or pandan water).
4. Strain through a sieve into a clean bowl (recommended for smooth layers).
5. Rest 10 minutes, then whisk again.

**Batter target:** pourable like thin pancake batter.

---

## Step 3 — Steam Layers (35–45 minutes)
### Steamer setup
- Bring steamer water to a steady steam (not raging boil).
- Lightly oil the cake pan.

### Layer plan (simple and pretty)
- **Green (pandan)** base layer
- **Yellow (mung bean)** middle layer
- **Green** top layer

You can also do 5–7 thinner alternating layers if you want a more “premium” look.

---

### 1) Steam first pandan layer
1. Whisk pandan batter again.
2. Pour a thin layer (~120–150 ml depending on pan size).
3. Steam **5–7 minutes** until set (surface no longer wet).

**Set check:** surface is glossy and firm; gentle jiggle is ok.

---

### 2) Add mung bean layer
1. Spoon mung bean paste onto set pandan layer.
2. Spread gently into an even layer (use a lightly oiled spoon).
3. Steam **8–10 minutes** to heat through and set.

---

### 3) Steam final pandan layer
1. Whisk pandan batter again.
2. Pour over mung layer gently.
3. Steam **8–12 minutes** until fully set.

> If you do multiple thin layers: each layer is typically 4–6 minutes.

---

## Cooling (For clean takeaway cubes)
1. Cool at room temperature **30–45 minutes**.
2. Refrigerate **2–4 hours** (or overnight) for the cleanest cuts.

---

## Cutting (Clean “bakery cubes”)
1. Lightly oil a sharp knife or dip it in warm water and wipe dry.
2. Cut into:
   - **4×4 grid** = 16 premium cubes
   - **5×5 grid** = 25 snack cubes
3. Wipe knife between cuts.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Texture:** chewy and bouncy, not grainy.
- **Layers:** distinct and clean.
- **Surface:** smooth (no condensation pits).
- **Cut:** cubes hold shape without tearing.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Keep chilled for best structure.
- Place cubes in paper cups or a divider tray so they don’t smear.
- Include label: “Keep chilled. Best within 3 days.”

---

## Storage
- Refrigerated: **3–4 days** best texture.
- Do not freeze (texture changes).

---

## Allergen Notes
- Coconut
- Gluten-free (tapioca + rice flour; verify flour brands)
