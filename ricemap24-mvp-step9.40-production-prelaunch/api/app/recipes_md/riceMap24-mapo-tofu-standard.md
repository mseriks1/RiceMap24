# Mapo Tofu (Sichuan-Style Tofu with Pork) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A bold Sichuan classic: silky tofu in a spicy, savory chili-bean sauce, often with minced pork. Excellent with rice and easy to portion into bowls.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Mapo Tofu”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/ma-po-tofu-real-deal/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Chinese (Sichuan) • Spicy • Sauce Bowl
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15 min  
- **Total time:** ~25–30 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (heat + sauce control; gentle tofu handling)

---

## Equipment
- Wok or deep skillet
- Small pot (for blanching tofu; recommended)
- Spatula (wood or silicone)
- Mixing bowls
- Measuring spoons

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Main
- **450 g** soft tofu *(silken/soft per source; handle gently)*
- **225 g** ground pork *(optional; can be omitted for vegetarian variant)*
- **2–3** scallions, chopped
- **2–3** cloves garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** ginger, minced

### Sauce & seasoning (use the source recipe for exact amounts)
- Doubanjiang (Sichuan fermented chili bean paste)
- Sichuan peppercorns (ground or toasted + crushed)
- Chili oil (optional but common in the source)
- Light soy sauce
- Shaoxing wine
- Chicken stock or water
- Cornstarch slurry (cornstarch + water)
- Sugar (small amount, per source)
- Neutral oil

> **Important:** Mapo tofu depends on **specific Sichuan ingredients and ratios**.  
> Follow the linked source recipe for **exact measurements** and brand notes.

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Tofu:** Cut into 2–3 cm cubes.
2. **Blanch tofu (recommended):** Simmer in salted water for a few minutes, then drain.  
   This firms the tofu slightly and reduces “raw” tofu smell (as commonly done in the source approach).
3. **Aromatics:** Mince garlic + ginger; chop scallions.
4. **Slurry:** Mix cornstarch with water (per source) and set aside.

---

## Method (High-level but precise process flow)
> Use the exact ingredient amounts from the source recipe linked above.

### 1) Build flavor base
1. Heat wok/skillet over **medium heat** with neutral oil.
2. Add ground pork and cook until browned and crumbly.
3. Add garlic + ginger; stir briefly until fragrant.
4. Add doubanjiang (and chili oil if used) and fry gently to release red oil.

### 2) Simmer the sauce
5. Add Shaoxing wine, then stock/water and soy sauce (per source). Bring to a simmer.
6. Season with sugar and Sichuan peppercorn (per source).

### 3) Add tofu gently
7. Slide blanched tofu into the sauce. Use a spoon/spatula to gently nudge—avoid breaking cubes.
8. Simmer a few minutes so tofu absorbs flavor.

### 4) Thicken + finish
9. Stir cornstarch slurry, then add gradually until the sauce coats tofu and is glossy.
10. Finish with chopped scallions and serve with rice.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce:** Glossy, coats tofu, not watery; not pasty.
- **Tofu:** Cubes remain mostly intact.
- **Heat/numbing:** Spicy + “ma” (Sichuan pepper) balanced.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Tofu: **1125 g**
- Ground pork: **560 g** (optional)

For sauce ingredients, multiply **exact source measurements** by 2.5×.

### Batch notes
- Cook in **2 batches** to keep tofu intact and sauce consistent.
- Keep sauce at a gentle simmer (avoid hard boil).

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Best:** Rice in base + mapo tofu in separate compartment or on top (sauce can soak rice).
- Include a note: “Stir before eating.”

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle microwave or low simmer; avoid vigorous stirring (breaks tofu).

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Soy (doubanjiang, soy sauce)
- **May contain:** Gluten (some doubanjiang/soy sauces), sesame (if chili oil contains it)
- **Spicy:** Yes

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Keep the simmer gentle and stir carefully to avoid broken tofu.
- Doubanjiang quality matters—use a good brand as the source recommends.
- Add slurry gradually; you can always add more, but you can’t take it out.
