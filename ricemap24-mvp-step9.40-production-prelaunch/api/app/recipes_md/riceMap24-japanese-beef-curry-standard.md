# Japanese Beef Curry (ビーフカレー) — Beef Chunks — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Japanese curry with **tender beef chunks**, carrots, potatoes, mushrooms, and a thick curry-roux gravy.  
Designed for home kitchens: **exact amounts, clear timeline, batch scaling, and delivery packaging**.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Japanese Beef Curry”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/japanese-beef-curry/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Japanese
- **Style:** Low-and-slow curry stew (beef chunks)
- **Yield:** **8 servings**
- **Active time:** ~25–35 min  
- **Total time:** ~3 hours (includes onion caramelization + long simmer)
- **Skill level:** Medium (caramelized onions + roux handling)

---

## Equipment
- Heavy Dutch oven / pot (5–6 L)
- Cast iron or carbon-steel skillet (for searing beef) — optional but recommended
- Fine-mesh skimmer (for skimming scum) — optional
- Cutting board + knife
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale optional)

---

## Ingredients (8 servings) — from source recipe

### A) Onions (caramelized base)
- **2 onions**, thinly sliced
- **2 Tbsp unsalted butter**, divided
- **2 Tbsp neutral oil**, divided

### B) Vegetables + aromatics
- **1 russet potato**, peeled and cut into chunks (soak in water 15 min to remove starch)
- **1 carrot**, peeled and cut (rangiri/random cuts)
- **6 cremini mushrooms**, sliced
- **½ tsp** grated ginger (with juice)
- **1 clove garlic**, minced (or pressed)

### C) Beef (chunks)
- **1¼ lb (567 g)** boneless beef chuck roast, cut into **1½-inch (3.8 cm)** cubes
- **¼ tsp** kosher salt
- **¼ tsp** black pepper
- **1 Tbsp** all-purpose flour (light coat)

### D) Deglaze + braise
- **½ cup (120 ml)** red wine
- **½ Tbsp** Japanese curry powder
- **1 Tbsp** tomato paste
- **4 cups (960 ml)** beef stock/broth *(or half stock/half water)*

### E) Flavor finish (during simmer)
- **⅛ apple**, grated
- **1 Tbsp** Worcestershire sauce
- **2 Tbsp** milk
- **1 bay leaf**

### F) Curry roux
- **Up to 1 package** Japanese curry roux blocks *(add to taste; you may not need all)*

### To serve
- Cooked Japanese short-grain rice (enough for 5–8 servings)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. Slice onions thinly.
2. Cut beef into 3.8 cm cubes; season with salt/pepper; lightly coat with flour.
3. Cut potato; soak in water (15 min).
4. Cut carrot and slice mushrooms.
5. Grate ginger; prep garlic.
6. Measure curry powder, tomato paste, stock, wine, Worcestershire, milk.
7. Grate ⅛ apple (do near the time you’ll add it so it stays fresh).

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Caramelize onions (20–25 minutes)
1. Preheat Dutch oven over **medium heat**.
2. Add **½ butter + ½ oil**, then add onions.
3. Sauté, stirring occasionally, about **20–25 minutes**.
4. When onions are wilted, reduce heat to **medium-low** to prevent burning.
5. When tender and lightly caramelized, remove pot from heat and set onions aside (in the pot).

> Tip: While onions cook, prep beef/veg and start searing beef.

---

## 2) Sear beef (two batches, 6–10 minutes total)
1. Preheat a skillet over **medium-high heat**.
2. Add remaining **½ butter + ½ oil**.
3. Sear beef in **2 batches** (single layer; don’t crowd):
   - Brown 3–4 minutes on one side, then turn to brown other sides.
4. Transfer browned beef to a plate.

### Deglaze skillet
5. Pour **½ cup red wine** into the hot skillet.
6. Scrape browned bits (fond) with a wooden spatula.
7. Turn off heat; keep this deglaze liquid.

---

## 3) Start curry base (3–5 minutes)
1. Reheat the onion pot over **medium heat**.
2. Add garlic + grated ginger; stir 30 seconds.
3. Add **½ Tbsp curry powder** and **1 Tbsp tomato paste**; sauté **1 minute**.

---

## 4) Add beef + simmer (1 hr 45 min)
1. Add browned beef, any juices, and the red-wine deglaze liquid to the pot.
2. Stir and cook **3 minutes** to evaporate alcohol.
3. Add carrots and mushrooms.
4. Add **4 cups beef stock/broth** (liquid should just cover ingredients).
5. Cover and bring to a boil, then skim any scum from the surface.
6. Add:
   - grated **⅛ apple**
   - **1 Tbsp Worcestershire**
   - **2 Tbsp milk**
   - **1 bay leaf**
7. Cover and simmer on **low** for **~1 hour 45 minutes** until beef is tender.

> If your lid doesn’t seal well, add more stock/water to keep ingredients barely covered.

---

## 5) Add potato (15 minutes)
1. Drain soaked potato.
2. Add potato chunks to the pot.
3. Cover and simmer **15 minutes** until potatoes are tender.

---

## 6) Add curry roux (off heat) + thicken (3–5 minutes)
1. Turn off heat.
2. Add curry roux **1–2 cubes at a time**:
   - Put cubes in a ladle with hot liquid, dissolve with a spoon/chopsticks, then stir into pot.
3. Taste as you go; you may not need the full package.
4. Turn heat to **lowest** and simmer **3–5 minutes**, stirring often, until thick and velvety.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef:** Fork-tender (if chewy, simmer longer).
- **Sauce:** Thick, smooth, coats a spoon (not watery).
- **Potato:** Tender but intact.
- **Balance:** Mild-sweet-savory (adjust with roux amount and salt at the end if needed).

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Recipe yields up to 8. For ~10 portions, multiply by **1.25×**.

### Quick scale targets (10 portions)
- Beef chuck: **~710 g**
- Stock: **~1200 ml**
- Onions: **2–3** (keep onion-heavy for sauce body)
- Roux: **1.25×** (buy extra; add to taste)

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Curry in leak-proof container.
- **Rice separate** (best texture and presentation).
- Note: “Reheat gently. Sauce thickens when chilled—loosen with a splash of water if needed.”

**Holding time:** Excellent; curry is often better after resting.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **3 days**.
- Freeze up to **2–3 months**.
- Reheat gently; stir well.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes
- **Contains:** Dairy (butter, milk), gluten (flour, many roux brands), soy (many roux brands)
- **Alcohol:** red wine (cooked down)
