## 1) Vietnamese Lemongrass Beef Rice Paper Rolls (Bò Nướng Cuốn Bánh Tráng) — Premium Fresh Rolls

### Why it’s premium
- Beef strips look amazing through rice paper
- Big aroma (lemongrass + garlic)
- Works with **two dips**: nuoc cham + peanut sauce

### Yield
- 10–12 rolls

### Ingredients (core)
- **500 g** thin-sliced beef (sirloin/strip/flat iron)
- Lemongrass marinade: minced lemongrass + garlic + fish sauce + sugar + oil + pepper
- Rice vermicelli (cooked, cooled)
- Lettuce + herb mix (mint/basil/cilantro)
- Rice paper sheets
- Optional premium: cucumber sticks + pickled carrot/daikon

### Beef workflow (fast, consistent)
1. Marinate beef **15–30 min**.
2. Sear hot pan **60–90 sec per side** (don’t overcook).
3. Cool slightly; slice for neat assembly.

### Packaging tip
- Parchment dividers + keep herbs dry.
- Add sauce cups: **peanut** + **nuoc cham**.

## 2) Lemongrass Chicken Rice Paper Rolls — High-Volume, EU-Friendly

### Why it’s premium
- Familiar protein (chicken), fresh roll format
- Great for customers who want “lighter” meals
- Peanut sauce upsell is a strong match

### Yield
- 10–12 rolls

### Ingredients (core)
- **600–700 g** chicken thigh (juicy) OR breast (lean)
- Lemongrass marinade (same concept as beef)
- Vermicelli + lettuce + herbs
- Rice paper sheets

### Chicken workflow
1. Marinate **20–30 min**.
2. Pan-sear or grill until cooked.
3. Slice thin for tight rolls.

### Packaging tip
- Offer a “**double-dip**” pack: peanut + sweet chili-lime.

## 3) Cantonese Crispy Spring Rolls — Chicken or Shrimp (Dim Sum Style)

### Why it’s premium
- Classic crisp “dim sum” look
- Super sellable as **side order** and party box
- Can do **chicken version** or **shrimp version** (or combo)

### Yield
- 18–24 rolls (small-medium)

### Filling (premium, not watery)
- Shredded cabbage + carrot
- Mushrooms (shiitake)
- Bamboo shoots (optional, very “dim sum”)
- Protein choice:
  - **350–450 g** ground chicken **OR**
  - **300 g** chopped shrimp
- Soy sauce + sesame oil + white pepper
- Cornstarch slurry (tiny) to bind

### Crisp workflow (best for pickup)
1. Stir-fry filling to drive off moisture.
2. Cool completely before rolling.
3. Roll tight; seal edge.
4. Fry or air-fry:
   - Deep fry: 180°C until golden
   - Air fryer: brush oil, 200°C until crisp, flipping once

### Packaging tip
- Vent container 30–60 sec before closing.
- Sauce cups: sweet chili, soy-vinegar, or chili crisp mayo.

## 4) Thai “Green Curry” Spring Rolls — Premium Flavor Twist (Chicken)

### Why it’s premium
- Unique, recognizable Thai flavor
- Feels “special” compared to standard spring rolls
- Great upsell with coconut-lime dip

### Yield
- 16–20 small rolls

### Filling idea (fast home-kitchen)
- Cook a thick green curry chicken mixture (minimal liquid)
- Cool fully
- Roll in spring roll wrappers
- Fry until crisp

### Packaging tip
- Sauce cup: coconut-lime + sweet chili.

## 5) Premium Sauce Line-Up (Sell 1–2 cups per box)
Offer 2 sizes: **30 ml standard** and **60 ml premium**.

### A) Peanut sauce (classic)
- Peanut butter + hoisin + water/stock + garlic, adjusted thick for delivery

### B) Nuoc cham (fresh rolls)
- Fish sauce + water + sugar + lime + garlic + chili

### C) Chili crisp mayo (crispy rolls)
- Mayo + chili crisp + lime juice + pinch salt

### D) Coconut-lime dip (Thai twist)
- Coconut milk + lime + sugar + pinch salt (reduce slightly to thicken)
