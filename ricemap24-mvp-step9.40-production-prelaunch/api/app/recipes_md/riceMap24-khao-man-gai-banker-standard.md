# Khao Man Gai (Thai Hainanese Chicken Rice) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A true Thai “banker”: tender poached chicken, fragrant ginger-garlic chicken rice, cucumber, and a punchy fermented soybean/ginger sauce.  
For RiceMap24, it’s perfect because it’s **batch-friendly**, portions cleanly, and travels extremely well for pickup/delivery.

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Poached chicken + seasoned rice + dipping sauce
- **Yield:** 6 meal portions
- **Active time:** ~30 minutes
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (poach + rice + sauce)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **Broad appeal**: mild, comforting, super “repeat order” friendly.
- **Great operations**: cook chicken + stock once; rice cooked in chicken fat; sauce made ahead.
- **Delivery winner**: components stay good; cucumber keeps it fresh.
- **Upsells**: extra chicken, extra sauce cup, add soup cup, add egg.

---

## What makes it “Khao Man Gai”
1) Poached chicken (juicy, not roasted)  
2) Rice cooked in chicken stock + garlic + ginger (often with chicken fat/oil)  
3) Iconic sauce (ginger + fermented soy bean paste + chili + vinegar)  
4) Cucumber + optional soup on the side

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Chicken + poaching broth
- **1 whole chicken** (1.6–1.9 kg) OR **2.0 kg** bone-in thighs/drumsticks
- **2 L** water (enough to cover; adjust)
- **8 slices** ginger
- **6 cloves** garlic, smashed
- **2 scallions**, smashed
- **1.5 Tbsp** salt
- Optional: **1 tsp** white peppercorns (nice)

> Using a whole chicken makes a richer broth and better margins (use the meat + broth + fat for rice).

---

## B) Ginger-garlic chicken rice
- **600 g** jasmine rice (dry) *(100 g per portion)*
- **2 Tbsp** chicken fat or neutral oil *(rendered from skin if whole chicken)*
- **6 cloves** garlic, minced
- **25 g** ginger, minced
- **900 ml** chicken stock (use poaching broth)
- **1 tsp** salt (adjust)
- Optional: pandan leaf (adds aroma)

---

## C) Sauce (the identity)
Make a bowl of sauce and portion it into cups.  
This is a standardized “home kitchen” version that stays authentic and sellable.

- **80 g** fresh ginger, grated
- **6 cloves** garlic, grated
- **4 Tbsp** fermented soybean paste *(tao jiew; use “yellow bean sauce” if needed)*
- **4 Tbsp** light soy sauce
- **3 Tbsp** rice vinegar (or mild white vinegar)
- **2 Tbsp** sugar
- **2–4 Tbsp** chicken broth (thin to pourable)
- **2–3** Thai chilies, minced (adjust heat)
- Optional: **1 tsp** sesame oil (small, for aroma)

**Sauce taste target:** salty-sour-sweet with ginger heat; pourable but not watery.

---

## D) Fresh sides (photogenic + essential)
- **2 cucumbers**, thin slices
- Cilantro sprigs (optional)
- Optional soup cup: a ladle of clear poaching broth per order

---

## Equipment
- Large pot (8–10 L)
- Skimmer/ladle
- Rice cooker or pot with lid
- Mixing bowl (sauce)
- Knife + cutting board
- Fine grater/microplane (best for ginger/garlic)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Rinse rice 2–3 times until water runs mostly clear.
2. Smash ginger/garlic/scallions for poaching.
3. Prep sauce ingredients (grate ginger/garlic).
4. Slice cucumbers and keep chilled.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Poach chicken (45–60 minutes)
1. Put chicken into a pot with water to cover.
2. Add ginger slices, smashed garlic, scallions, salt, and optional peppercorns.
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a **gentle simmer**.
4. Simmer until chicken is cooked through:
   - Whole chicken: **45–55 minutes**
   - Pieces: **30–40 minutes** (depends on size)
5. Turn off heat and let chicken rest in broth **10 minutes** (juicier).

**Doneness check:** thickest part reaches **74°C**.

### Cool chicken for slicing
- Remove chicken and cool 10–15 minutes, then slice.
- Optional (best look): chill 30 minutes before slicing for clean, neat cuts.

---

## Step 2 — Make chicken rice (25–30 minutes)
1. Render 2 Tbsp chicken fat:
   - If using whole chicken: skim some fat from broth surface OR render chopped skin in a pan.
2. Sauté garlic + ginger in the fat/oil until fragrant (30–60 seconds).
3. Add rinsed rice and stir 1 minute to coat grains.
4. Add 900 ml broth + salt.
5. Cook in rice cooker or pot until done.

**Rice check:** fluffy, fragrant, slightly glossy.

---

## Step 3 — Make sauce (5 minutes)
1. Mix grated ginger + garlic + tao jiew (or yellow bean sauce).
2. Add soy sauce, vinegar, sugar.
3. Add broth to thin to a pourable consistency.
4. Add chilies to taste.

**Rest 10 minutes** for flavors to meld.

---

## Step 4 — Slice and assemble (RiceMap24 service)
Per order:
1. **Rice:** 250–280 g cooked
2. **Chicken:** 180–220 g sliced (or 2–3 pieces)
3. **Cucumber:** 5–8 slices
4. **Sauce cup:** 30–40 ml
5. Optional: **soup cup** (150–200 ml broth)

---

## Quality Checks
- **Chicken:** juicy, not stringy (gentle simmer + rest).
- **Rice:** aromatic, not bland (use broth + fat).
- **Sauce:** punchy ginger with balanced salt/sour/sweet.
- **Presentation:** clean slices + cucumber fans.

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×**:
- Chicken: 3 whole chickens or ~3.4 kg pieces
- Rice: ~1.0 kg dry
- Broth: 3–4 L (pot big enough)
- Sauce: 1.7×

**Ops tip:** Make chicken + broth in one big pot. Cook rice in two batches if your cooker is small.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: chicken (sliced, fanned for presentation)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Cup C: sauce (sealed)
- Cup D (optional): broth soup cup
- Small bag: cucumber slices (or in chicken container if dry)

Label: “Pour sauce over chicken. Sip broth on the side.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Chicken and broth: 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat chicken gently (steam or low microwave).
- Rice: best same day; can be reheated with a splash of water.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce/bean paste)
