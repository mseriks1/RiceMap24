# Sichuan Boiled Beef (Shuizhu Niurou / 水煮牛肉) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A bold, authentic Sichuan dish: tender beef slices “poached” in a chili-bean-paste broth, poured over vegetables, then finished with a sizzling hot oil pour-over.  
**Great for RiceMap24:** high visual impact (red oil + aromatics), strong identity, and it sells well as a premium spicy beef bowl.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Sichuan Boiled Beef (Shuizhu Niurou)”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/sichuan-boiled-beef/

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Sichuan)
- **Style:** Spicy broth + beef slices + hot oil finish
- **Yield (source):** 4 servings
- **Active time:** ~25 min (plus 1 hour tenderize step)
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (heat control + hot oil pour)

- Wok or large deep skillet + spatula
- Small saucepan (for hot oil pour)
- Mixing bowl (beef)
- Large deep serving bowl (or 4 bowl portions)

- Measuring cups/spoons

## Ingredients (Serves 4)

### A) Beef (tenderize + velvet)
- **283 g (10 oz)** flank steak, sliced **6 mm (¼-inch)** thick **against the grain**
- **¼ tsp** baking soda
- **60 ml (¼ cup)** water
- **2 tsp** cornstarch

### B) Vegetables (bottom layer)
- **170 g (6 oz)** soybean sprouts, washed/drained
- **200 g (7 oz)** enoki mushrooms, trimmed/washed/drained

> Vegetable swaps that still work: napa cabbage, romaine lettuce, leeks, sliced mushrooms.  
> Always quick-stir-fry first so they don’t water down the broth.

### C) Aromatics + spices
- **Ginger:** 3 slices
- **Scallions:** 3 (white parts only), cut into 1.2 cm (½-inch) pieces
- **Sichuan peppercorns:** 1 Tbsp

### D) Broth base
- **Spicy bean sauce/paste (doubanjiang):** 3 Tbsp
- **Low-sodium chicken stock:** 360 ml (1½ cups)
- **Sugar:** 1½ tsp

### E) Finish (toppings + hot oil)
- **Garlic:** 1 Tbsp, minced
- **Chili flakes:** ½ tsp
- **Cilantro:** 1 Tbsp, chopped
- **Ground Sichuan peppercorn:** ½ tsp

### F) Oils (three stages, total from source)
- **1 Tbsp** peanut/canola oil (veg stir-fry)  
- **60 ml (¼ cup)** peanut/canola oil (broth aromatics)  
- **3 Tbsp** peanut/canola oil (hot oil pour-over)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Slice beef correctly
- Slice **against the grain** to keep it tender.
- Aim for even thickness so all slices cook at the same speed.

### 2) Tenderize + wash (key technique)
1. Toss beef with baking soda + ¼ cup water.
2. Rest **1 hour**.
3. “Wash” beef under running water for **5 minutes** in the bowl (gentle stream).
4. Drain well and toss beef with **2 tsp cornstarch**. Set aside.

> This is a restaurant-style technique that keeps beef very tender in spicy dishes.

### 3) Prep vegetables
- Keep sprouts and enoki ready to stir-fry quickly.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

### Step 1 — Quick stir-fry vegetables (3–4 minutes)
1. Heat wok over **high heat**.
2. Add **1 Tbsp oil**.
3. Stir-fry sprouts ~**2 minutes**.
4. Add enoki and stir-fry until wilted.
5. Spread vegetables in the bottom of a **deep serving bowl** (or divide into 4 bowls).

### Step 2 — Clean the wok (important)
- Rinse/wipe out the wok so the broth stays clean and bright.

### Step 3 — Build chili oil base (5–7 minutes)
1. Heat **¼ cup oil** over **medium heat**.
2. Add ginger slices; cook until lightly browned.
3. Add scallion whites + Sichuan peppercorns; cook **2 minutes** (don’t burn).
4. Add doubanjiang; cook **3–4 minutes** until oil turns **bright red**.

> Heat control matters:
> - Too low = dull color
> - Too high = burnt aromatics

### Step 4 — Add stock + cook beef (2–3 minutes)
1. Add stock + sugar; bring to a boil on **high**.
2. Add beef slices and stir gently to separate.
3. Turn off heat as soon as beef turns opaque (a hint of pink is OK for tenderness).
4. Pour everything over the vegetables in the serving bowl.

### Step 5 — Toppings + hot oil pour (2 minutes)
1. Sprinkle over top: minced garlic + chili flakes + cilantro.
2. Heat **3 Tbsp oil** in a small saucepan until shimmering.
3. Pour hot oil in a thin stream over the toppings (should sizzle).
4. Finish with a pinch of ground Sichuan peppercorn on top.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Beef:** tender, not chewy (do not overcook in boiling broth).
- **Oil color:** bright red (not brown).
- **Aroma:** numbing peppercorn + chili bean paste fragrance.
- **Veg:** still has texture, not soggy.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
To keep it looking great:
- Container A: beef + broth + vegetables (sealed, leak-proof)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Optional small cup: extra chili oil / extra peppercorn sprinkle

Label: “Stir before eating. Spicy & numbing.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- Reheat gently to a simmer; do not hard-boil (toughens beef).
- Add fresh cilantro after reheating if possible.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (doubanjiang often contains soy/wheat)
- Gluten possible (paste/stock brands)
- Chili & peppercorn (spicy/numbing)
