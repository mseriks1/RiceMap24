# Cumin Beef (孜然牛肉 / Ziran Niurou) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A bold **Northern Chinese / Xinjiang-style** dry stir-fry: beef slices fried until **crispy on the edges**, then tossed with **cumin**, chilies, garlic, ginger, and red onion.  
This is **not** the typical “Chinese restaurant saucy beef”—it’s dry, aromatic, and addictive. Great as a rice bowl or flatbread plate.

**Source recipe:** Omnivore’s Cookbook — Cumin Beef (published Nov 13, 2025)  
https://omnivorescookbook.com/cumin-beef/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Northern/Xinjiang-inspired)
- **Style:** Dry stir-fry (crispy beef, spice coating)
- **Yield:** 4 servings
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~40 min (includes marinating)
- **Skill level:** Medium (batch frying for crisp texture)

---

## Equipment
- Wok or large skillet (wide surface helps crisp beef)
- Mixing bowls (beef + spice mix)
- Tongs/spatula
- Knife + cutting board
- Plate + paper towels/rack (drain beef)

---

## Ingredients (Serves 4)

## A) Beef + marinade
- **1 lb (450 g)** flank steak
- **1/4 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** baking soda
- **1 Tbsp** vegetable oil

## B) Spice mix
- **2 Tbsp** cumin powder
- **2 tsp** Sichuan chili flakes *(or Korean chili flakes for milder heat)*
- **1/2 tsp** sugar
- **1/4 tsp** freshly ground Sichuan peppercorns *(or black pepper)*
- **1/4 tsp** salt

## C) Stir-fry
- **1/4 cup** cornstarch
- **3 Tbsp** peanut oil *(or vegetable oil)*, divided
- **8** dried Chinese chili peppers, halved crosswise and seeds removed
- **1 small** red onion, sliced into **1 cm (1/2")** thick pieces
- **1 Tbsp** ginger, minced
- **5 cloves** garlic, sliced
- **1 Tbsp** toasted white sesame seeds (garnish)
- Optional garnish (highly recommended): **cilantro**

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Slice beef correctly (important)
1. Cut the flank steak **along the grain** into two even pieces.
2. Slice each piece **against the grain** into **5 mm (1/4")** thick slices.

> Tip: Partially freeze beef 20–30 minutes to make slicing easier.

### 2) Marinate (30 minutes)
1. In a bowl, combine beef with **salt, baking soda, and 1 Tbsp vegetable oil**.
2. Mix until evenly coated.
3. Rest **30 minutes** at room temperature.

### 3) Mix spice blend
- Combine all **spice mix** ingredients in a small bowl. Set aside.

### 4) Cornstarch coat (right before frying)
1. Drain off any extra liquid from the beef bowl.
2. Add cornstarch a few tablespoons at a time, mixing until all slices are evenly coated.

---

## Cooking Method (Crispy “Dry Stir-Fry”)

> **Key rule:** fry beef in batches so slices don’t overlap. Overcrowding = steaming = no crisp.

### 1) Fry beef until crisp (2–3 batches, 6–10 minutes total)
1. Heat **2 Tbsp oil** in a wok/skillet over **medium-high** until hot.
2. Add beef in a single layer (no overlap).
3. Fry until bottom turns golden and crispy, **2–3 minutes**.
4. Flip and fry the other side until golden-brown and crisp, **1–2 minutes**.
5. Transfer to a plate lined with paper towels (or a rack).
6. Repeat batches. Add a bit more oil if the pan gets dry.  
   - If it starts smoking too much, reduce to medium heat.

**Crisp target:** some pieces can go darker brown—this means the surface is dehydrated and crispy (that’s the signature).

---

### 2) Aromatics + onion (1–2 minutes)
1. Add the remaining **1 Tbsp oil**.
2. Add dried chilies, ginger, and sliced garlic; stir a few times until fragrant.
3. Add red onion and stir-fry **about 1 minute**—you want it just starting to soften but still crisp.

---

### 3) Toss with spice mix (20–30 seconds)
1. Return beef to the pan.
2. Sprinkle spice mix evenly over everything.
3. Toss immediately to coat all beef slices.
4. Taste 1 piece and adjust with a tiny pinch of salt if needed.
5. Turn off heat.
6. Add sesame seeds and cilantro (if using). Toss once and plate immediately.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Beef:** crisp edges, tender inside (not chewy).
- **Spice coating:** dry and clingy, not wet.
- **Onion:** still has crunch (not fully soft).
- **Aroma:** cumin-forward with chili warmth and a hint of peppercorn tingle.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Beef: **1.125 kg**
- Spice mix: **2.5×**
- Cornstarch: **~150 g**
- Oil: plan **~200–250 ml** total depending on pan and batches
- Onion: **2–3** small red onions
- Dried chilies: **20**

**Ops tip:** Fry beef in steady small batches, then do one final toss with aromatics + spice for each batch (keeps it hottest and crispest).

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh (crispness fades).
- Reheat quickly in a hot pan or air fryer (190°C, 3–5 min) to re-crisp.

---

## Allergens
