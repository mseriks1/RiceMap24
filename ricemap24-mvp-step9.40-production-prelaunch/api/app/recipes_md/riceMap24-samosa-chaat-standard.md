# Samosa Chaat (Indian Street-Food Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A bold Indian street-food “chaat” bowl built on crispy samosas topped with warm chickpeas, tangy-sweet chutneys, yogurt, and crunchy garnishes. High-impact as a snack bowl or “share” item.

**Source recipe:** Swasthi’s Recipes — “Samosa Chaat”  
https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/samosa-chaat-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Indian • Street Food • Snack Bowl
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~20–25 min  
- **Total time:** ~30–40 min *(faster if using ready-made samosas)*
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (multiple toppings + assembly)

---

## Equipment
- Saucepan (for chickpeas / gravy)
- Small bowls (chutneys/yogurt)
- Cutting board + knife
- Ladle/spoon
- Optional: air fryer/oven to crisp samosas

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Chaat relies on **component balance** (spicy + sweet + tangy + creamy + crunchy).  
> Use the linked source recipe for **exact measurements** for the chickpea base and chutneys.

### Base
- Samosas (store-bought or homemade), per source guidance
- Cooked chickpeas (or chana) base/gravy per source

### Sauces (per source)
- Green chutney (mint-coriander)
- Tamarind chutney (sweet-tangy)
- Yogurt (whisked, lightly salted)

### Seasonings + crunch
- Chaat masala
- Roasted cumin powder
- Red chili powder (optional)
- Salt
- Onions (finely chopped)
- Tomatoes (optional)
- Cilantro
- Sev (crispy chickpea noodles) or crushed papdi (crunch)

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Crisp samosas (air fryer/oven)  
2) Warm chickpea gravy/base  
3) Prep chutneys + whisk yogurt  
4) Chop onions/cilantro + ready sev  
5) Assemble bowls quickly

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Crisp the samosas
1. Heat samosas in an oven/air fryer until crispy and hot (time/temp per package or source).
2. Cut into halves or bite-size pieces for bowls.

## 2) Warm chickpea base
1. Prepare or warm the chickpea gravy/base as per source recipe.
2. Keep warm at low heat.

## 3) Assembly (the chaat “build”)
1. Add samosa pieces to each bowl.
2. Spoon warm chickpeas/gravy over.
3. Add whisked yogurt.
4. Drizzle green chutney + tamarind chutney.
5. Sprinkle chaat masala + roasted cumin powder (and chili powder if using).
6. Add onions + cilantro.
7. Finish with sev/papdi crunch on top.

**Serve note:** Best served immediately while samosas are crisp.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Texture:** Hot/crisp + creamy + crunchy layers.
- **Balance:** Tangy-sweet-salty-spicy in one bite.
- **Freshness:** Chutneys bright; yogurt smooth (not watery).

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best:** Keep crunchy parts separate:
- Bowl: samosa + chickpea base
- Cups: chutneys + yogurt
- Bag/cup: sev/papdi

Label: “Pour sauces and add crunch just before eating.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Chickpea base:** Refrigerate 3–4 days; reheat gently.
- **Chutneys:** Refrigerate 3–5 days (varies by ingredients).
- **Samosas:** Re-crisp in oven/air fryer.
- **Assembled chaat:** Best immediately.
