# Bánh Mì-Style Pork Baguette (Lemongrass Pork + Pickles) — RiceMap24 Standard (Breakfast/Lunch)

A top-tier “banker” baguette: Vietnamese-style pork, crunchy pickles, herbs, and a creamy-spicy mayo.  
Designed for **home kitchens**, **pickup**, and **repeat orders**.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Baguette sandwich (Asian twist)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Vietnamese bánh mì
- **Yield:** 4 baguettes (with batch scaling included)
- **Active time:** ~25 min
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min (includes quick pickles)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Big flavor + crisp texture** (pickles + herbs)
- **Photogenic** (color + visible fillings)
- **Scales well** (pork batch + pickles batch)
- **Great upsells:** extra pickles, extra sauce cup, add chili

---

## Ingredients (4 baguettes)

## A) Pork (lemongrass marinade)
- **600 g** pork shoulder (preferred) or pork belly (richer), thin slices (3–5 mm)

### Marinade
- **2 stalks** lemongrass (white part), very finely minced *(or 2 Tbsp lemongrass paste)*
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** sugar (or brown sugar)
- **1.5 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce (optional, adds color)
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1 tsp** black pepper

> Marinate **20–30 min** (or up to 4 hours chilled).

---

## B) Quick pickles (Đồ Chua)
- **1 medium carrot** (about 150 g), julienne
- **200 g** daikon radish (or kohlrabi as substitute), julienne
- **120 ml** rice vinegar
- **120 ml** water
- **35 g** sugar
- **1 tsp** salt

---

## C) Baguettes + veg
- **4** baguettes (15–18 cm) or 2 long baguettes cut in half
- **1 cucumber**, thin sticks/slices
- Fresh herbs: cilantro (and/or mint), 1 bunch
- Optional: sliced jalapeño or chili

---

## D) Sauce (spicy mayo)
- **120 ml** mayo
- **1–2 tsp** sriracha (adjust)
- **1 tsp** lime juice (optional but great)
- Pinch salt

### Optional “traditional” add-on (if you want)
- Chicken liver pâté (thin smear) — offer as **optional premium** (some customers love it, some don’t)

---

## Equipment
- Skillet or grill pan
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (optional)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make quick pickles (10 minutes + rest)
1. Mix vinegar, water, sugar, salt until dissolved.
2. Add carrot + daikon. Toss.
3. Rest **20–30 minutes** (longer = better).

---

## 2) Marinate pork (20 minutes)
1. Mix marinade ingredients.
2. Toss pork slices to coat.
3. Rest while pickles sit.

---

## 3) Cook pork (8–10 minutes)
1. Heat pan medium-high with a tiny oil.
2. Cook pork in batches (don’t crowd) until browned:
   - **60–90 sec per side** for thin slices
3. Rest 2 minutes.

**Quality target:** browned edges, still juicy.

---

## 4) Mix spicy mayo (1 minute)
- Stir mayo + sriracha + lime + pinch salt.

---

## 5) Build baguettes (RiceMap24 assembly)
Per baguette:
1. Split baguette (don’t cut fully through).
2. Spread **1–2 Tbsp** spicy mayo (and pâté if using).
3. Add cucumber + herbs.
4. Add pork (150 g cooked target per sandwich).
5. Top with a generous pinch of drained pickles.
6. Optional chili slices.

---

## Portioning (Per baguette)
- Pork: **140–170 g cooked** (from ~150 g raw depending on cut)
- Pickles: **40–60 g**
- Mayo: **25–30 g**
- Cucumber/herbs: as needed

---

## Batch for 10 baguettes
- Pork: **1.5 kg**
- Lemongrass: 5 stalks
- Garlic: 7–8 cloves
- Pickles: 4 carrots + 800 g daikon
- Spicy mayo: 300 ml mayo + sriracha to taste

**Ops tip:** Cook pork ahead and keep warm; build sandwiches fast at pickup.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
Best practice to avoid sogginess:
- Wrap in parchment + tight paper wrap.
- If pickup time is long: pack pickles in a small cup and customer adds (premium option).
- Offer sauce cup as upsell.

**Label:** “Add pickles last (optional).”

---

## Storage
- Pickles: 5–7 days refrigerated
- Cooked pork: 2–3 days refrigerated (reheat gently)

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Egg (mayo)
- Wheat (baguette)
- Soy (optional)
