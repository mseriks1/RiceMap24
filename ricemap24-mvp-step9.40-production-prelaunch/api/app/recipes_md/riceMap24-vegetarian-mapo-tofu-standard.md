# Vegetarian Mapo Tofu (Sichuan-Style, Premium) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card (Non-Indian Vegetarian)

A premium vegetarian main: silky tofu in a spicy, aromatic, slightly numbing Sichuan sauce.  
This version uses **mushrooms** (and optional fermented black beans) for “meaty” depth while staying meat-free.

**Reference (for grounding):** Omnivore’s Cookbook — Vegetarian Mapo Tofu  
https://omnivorescookbook.com/vegetarian-mapo-tofu/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Sichuan)
- **Type:** Vegetarian main (vegan-possible)
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15 minutes
- **Total time:** ~25 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (gentle tofu handling, sauce balance)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Premium look:** glossy red-brown sauce + tofu cubes + scallion.
- **High repeat-order potential:** spicy, savory, aromatic.
- **Delivery-friendly:** sauce travels well; tofu stays tender.
- **Upsells:** extra tofu, extra sauce cup, chili oil, steamed rice.

---

## Key Flavor Notes (Sichuan “Mapo”)
- **Doubanjiang** (Sichuan chili bean paste) = core flavor + color  
- **Sichuan peppercorn** = “tingly” fragrance (optional but highly recommended)  
- **Mushrooms** = umami depth for vegetarian version  
- **Fermented black beans** (optional) = extra savory punch  

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Main
- **450 g** tofu (medium-firm or firm), cut into **1.5 cm / 1/2-inch** cubes  
  - Medium-firm = more tender, but handle gently  
  - Firm = easiest for first-time sellers
- **225 g** mushrooms (shiitake + button mix works well), sliced or finely chopped
- **2–3 Tbsp** neutral oil (peanut/vegetable)

## B) Aromatics
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** ginger, minced
- **2 green onions/scallions**, sliced (white/green separated)

## C) Sauce base
- **2 cups (480 ml)** vegetable stock *(or water in a pinch)*
- **2–3 Tbsp** doubanjiang (Sichuan chili bean paste)  
  - Start with 2 Tbsp, adjust heat/salt later
- Optional (recommended): **1–2 tsp** fermented black beans, rinsed and chopped
- **2 tsp** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry) *(optional but good)*
- **1 tsp** sugar (balance)
- **1/8 tsp** five-spice powder *(optional, tiny)*
- Optional: **1/2–1 tsp** ground Sichuan peppercorn (finish)

## D) Slurry (thickener)
- **1 Tbsp** cornstarch
- **2 Tbsp** water

## E) Finish
- Homemade chili oil (optional drizzle)
- Extra scallion greens

---

## Equipment
- Wok or deep skillet
- Spatula + spoon (gentle mixing)
- Small bowl (slurry)
- Pot for blanching tofu (recommended)

---

## Prep (5–7 minutes)
1. Cut tofu into cubes.
2. Slice mushrooms.
3. Mince garlic + ginger.
4. Slice scallions (separate whites and greens).
5. Mix cornstarch + water slurry.

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Repeatable, Home Kitchen Friendly)

## Step 1 — Blanch tofu (recommended for best texture)
1. Bring a small pot of water to a gentle boil.
2. Add tofu cubes and simmer **1 minute**.
3. Drain carefully.

**Why:** helps tofu hold shape and removes raw-bean smell.

---

## Step 2 — Start the sauce (2 minutes)
1. Heat oil in wok/skillet over **medium**.
2. Add scallion whites, garlic, ginger.
3. Stir **30–45 seconds** until fragrant (don’t burn).

---

## Step 3 — Bloom doubanjiang (1 minute)
1. Add doubanjiang (and optional black beans).
2. Stir-fry **45–60 seconds** until the oil turns red and fragrant.

**This step builds the “restaurant” aroma.**

---

## Step 4 — Cook mushrooms (3–5 minutes)
1. Add mushrooms.
2. Stir-fry until they release moisture and start to brown slightly.

---

## Step 5 — Add stock + season (3 minutes)
1. Pour in vegetable stock.
2. Add Shaoxing wine (optional), sugar, and optional five-spice.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer.

---

## Step 6 — Add tofu + simmer gently (3–4 minutes)
1. Slide in tofu cubes.
2. Simmer gently **3–4 minutes** to flavor tofu.
3. Stir very gently (shake pan if possible) to avoid breaking tofu.

---

## Step 7 — Thicken (30–60 seconds)
1. Stir slurry again (cornstarch settles).
2. Slowly pour into simmering sauce while stirring gently.
3. Cook until sauce is glossy and coats a spoon.

---

## Step 8 — Finish + taste balance (30 seconds)
Taste and adjust:
- Too salty → add a splash of stock/water
- Too flat → tiny pinch of sugar or a touch more doubanjiang
- Want more “mapo” → add ground Sichuan peppercorn at the end

Finish with scallion greens and optional chili oil drizzle.

---

## Quality Checks
- Sauce is **glossy** and clings lightly to tofu.
- Tofu cubes mostly intact (not broken into mush).
- Aroma: chili bean paste + garlic/ginger + (optional) tingly pepper note.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning
Per order:
- Mapo tofu: **450–550 g**
- Rice (recommended): **250 g cooked**

**Upsells**
- Extra tofu (premium)
- Extra sauce cup (high-margin)
- Chili oil add-on cup
- Add a veg side (cucumber salad / pickles)

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Tofu: ~1.1 kg
- Mushrooms: ~560 g
- Stock: ~1.2 L
- Doubanjiang: 5–7 Tbsp (taste-adjust)
- Slurry: 2.5×

**Ops tip:** Blanch tofu in batches. Keep sauce gently simmering; add tofu near service time for best texture.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
- Use leak-proof container.
- Rice in separate container.
- Optional garnish cup (scallion + pepper) for fresher look.

Label: “Stir gently. Best with rice.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **2 days**.
- Reheat gently (simmer); add splash of water if thick.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (tofu, doubanjiang often contains soy)
- Gluten possible (some doubanjiang brands contain wheat)
