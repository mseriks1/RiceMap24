# Thai Red Curry Chicken (Gaeng Phed Gai / แกงเผ็ดไก่) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Thai red curry is a classic “repeat-order” dish: creamy coconut curry with a warm red-chili profile, great with chicken and vegetables.  
For RiceMap24, it’s a strong **banker** because it’s fast, scalable, photogenic, and delivers/reheats extremely well.

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Coconut red curry + chicken + vegetables
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~35–45 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (fry paste + balance seasoning)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **High demand:** red curry is a global favorite.
- **Great delivery:** sauce protects chicken; reheats well.
- **Consistent:** easy to portion and price.
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra sauce cup, add vegetables, spice levels, add rice/noodles.

---

## Choose Your Chicken (Pick ONE)
### Option A (recommended banker): Boneless chicken thigh
- **1.2 kg** boneless chicken thighs, sliced into bite-size pieces

### Option B (leaner): Chicken breast
- **1.0–1.1 kg** breast, thin slices (cook gently to avoid dryness)

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Curry base
- **100–140 g** Thai red curry paste *(about 6–8 Tbsp; varies by brand)*
- **800 ml** coconut milk (full-fat)
- **200–300 ml** chicken stock or water (adjust thickness)
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil **OR** “crack coconut cream” method (below)

## B) Seasoning (Thai balance)
- **2–3 Tbsp** fish sauce (to taste)
- **1–2 Tbsp** palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- **1 Tbsp** tamarind paste *(optional, adds depth and gentle tang)*
- Optional: **4–6** makrut/kaffir lime leaves (very nice)
- Optional: **1 tsp** shrimp paste (kapi) *(only if your curry paste is mild/doesn’t contain it)*

## C) Vegetables (choose 2–4)
Classic red curry often includes bamboo shoots and bell pepper.

- **200 g** bamboo shoots, sliced (very Thai)
- **1 red bell pepper**, thin strips (photo-friendly)
- **250 g** green beans, cut 4–5 cm
- **1 small eggplant** or **1 zucchini**, half-moons
- Optional: pineapple chunks (red curry + pineapple is popular and sells well)

## D) Finish (important)
- **1–2 cups** Thai basil leaves (or sweet basil if needed)
- **2** red spur chilies, thin slices (garnish)
- Optional: lime wedges for serving

## To serve
- Jasmine rice: **250 g cooked** per order

---

## Equipment
- Large pot/Dutch oven (5–6 L)
- Knife + cutting board
- Ladle
- Small bowl for balancing seasoning

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Slice chicken.
2. Prep vegetables.
3. Tear makrut lime leaves (optional) to release aroma.
4. Pick basil leaves and keep dry.
5. Have fish sauce + sugar ready for balancing.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

### Step 1 — Fry curry paste for “restaurant” flavor (3–5 minutes)
**Option 1 (best): crack coconut cream**
1. Scoop **~200 ml** thick coconut cream from the top of the coconut milk (or use separate coconut cream).
2. Add to pot over medium heat.
3. Cook 3–5 minutes, stirring, until it thickens and looks slightly oily (“cracks”).
4. Add red curry paste and fry 2–3 minutes until very fragrant.

**Option 2 (simple): oil method**
1. Add **2 Tbsp oil** to pot over medium.
2. Add red curry paste and fry 2–3 minutes until fragrant.

### Step 2 — Build curry sauce (5 minutes)
1. Add remaining coconut milk.
2. Add **200 ml stock/water** to start.
3. Add makrut lime leaves (optional).
4. Bring to a gentle simmer (avoid hard boil).

### Step 3 — Cook chicken (8–12 minutes)
1. Add chicken.
2. Simmer gently until just cooked:
   - Thigh: **8–10 minutes**
   - Breast: **6–8 minutes**
3. Stir occasionally so chicken cooks evenly.

### Step 4 — Add vegetables (5–10 minutes)
Add in order of cook time:
1. Eggplant/zucchini first: 5–8 min until tender
2. Green beans: 3–4 min
3. Bamboo shoots + bell pepper: last 2–3 min

### Step 5 — Balance seasoning (2 minutes)
Start with:
- **2 Tbsp fish sauce**
- **1 Tbsp sugar**
- **1 Tbsp tamarind** (optional)

Taste and adjust:
- Too salty/strong → add a splash of coconut milk or water
- Too flat → a bit more fish sauce
- Needs roundness → a bit more sugar
- Needs depth → tamarind (or a tiny pinch MSG if you use it)

### Step 6 — Finish with basil (30 seconds)
1. Turn off heat.
2. Stir in Thai basil leaves (they wilt quickly).
3. Top with red chili slices.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sauce:** smooth, creamy (not split), rich red-orange.
- **Chicken:** tender, not dry.
- **Veg:** cooked but not mushy; still colorful.
- **Flavor:** spicy + sweet + salty in balance.

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6 portions).
- Chicken thigh: ~2.0 kg
- Red curry paste: ~170–240 g (adjust heat)
- Coconut milk: ~1.35 L
- Stock/water: ~350–500 ml
- Veg + garnishes: scale 1.7×

**Ops tip:** Make curry base + chicken in a big pot. Add vegetables in a finishing batch so veg stays bright for photos.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: red curry (leak-proof)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Optional cup: extra sauce
- Optional garnish cup: basil/chili (add after reheat)

Label: “Reheat gently (don’t hard-boil). Add basil garnish last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently to a simmer; avoid hard boil (coconut can split).
- If thick, loosen with a splash of water/stock.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Coconut
- Shellfish (if curry paste contains shrimp)
