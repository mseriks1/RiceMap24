# Gochujang Caramel Brownies (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

Classic fudgy brownies with a subtle Korean twist: **gochujang caramel** (sweet, salty, gently spicy).  
Not a traditional Asian dessert—just a familiar “banker” dessert with an Asian flavor signature that sells well and travels perfectly.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Fudgy brownies + gochujang caramel swirl/drizzle
- **Asian twist:** Gochujang (Korean chili paste) used in caramel
- **Yield:** 16 brownies (20×20 cm pan) or 12 larger brownies
- **Active time:** ~25–30 min
- **Total time:** ~55–65 min + cool
- **Skill level:** Medium (caramel + brownie bake)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup-friendly:** bars, no melting, easy to box.
- **High “wow” factor:** glossy caramel swirl + deep chocolate.
- **Broad appeal:** chocolate first; spice is subtle and optional.
- **Upsells:** extra caramel cup, “mild” vs “spicy,” add sesame brittle.

## Equipment
- 20×20 cm (8×8) pan
- Parchment paper
- Saucepan (caramel)
- Mixing bowl
- Whisk/spatula
- Oven

---

## Ingredients

## A) Brownie base (fudgy)
- **170 g** unsalted butter
- **200 g** sugar
- **2 large eggs**
- **1 tsp** vanilla
- **60 g** cocoa powder
- **90 g** all-purpose flour
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- Optional: **100 g** dark chocolate chips (extra fudgy)

---

## B) Gochujang caramel (swirl + drizzle)
- **150 g** sugar
- **60 ml** water
- **90 ml** heavy cream (warm)
- **30 g** butter
- **1–2 Tbsp** gochujang *(start with 1 Tbsp for mild)*
- **1/2 tsp** salt (adjust)

### Optional premium finish
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Flaky salt

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make gochujang caramel (10 minutes)
1. Warm cream in a small pot or microwave (hot, not boiling).
2. In a saucepan, combine sugar + water and cook over medium-high until amber (cola color).
3. Turn off heat and carefully whisk in warm cream (it will bubble hard).
4. Add butter and whisk smooth.
5. Whisk in gochujang and salt until fully dissolved.
6. Cool 10 minutes (thickens slightly).

**Caramel safety:** caramel is extremely hot—avoid splashes.

---

## 2) Make brownie batter (10 minutes)
1. Preheat oven to **175°C**.
2. Line pan with parchment.
3. Melt butter and let cool 2 minutes.
4. Whisk in sugar.
5. Whisk in eggs and vanilla.
6. Fold in cocoa, flour, and salt (and chocolate chips if using).

**Do not overmix**—stop when no dry streaks remain.

---

## 3) Assemble + bake (25–30 minutes)
1. Spread brownie batter into pan.
2. Spoon caramel on top in lines/dollops.
3. Swirl gently with a skewer (don’t over-swirl).
4. Bake **25–30 minutes** until edges set and center is still slightly soft.

**Doneness target:** toothpick comes out with moist crumbs, not wet batter.

---

## 4) Cool + slice (important)
1. Cool completely in pan (45–60 minutes).
2. Lift out and slice into brownies.
3. Optional: drizzle extra caramel and sprinkle sesame/flaky salt.

---

## Quality Checks
- Brownies are fudgy, not dry.
- Caramel swirl is glossy and visible.
- Heat level is balanced (sweet-salty with a gentle chili warmth).

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Cool fully before boxing (caramel sets).
- Use parchment between layers.
- Label: “Best within 3 days. Keep cool.”

---

## Storage
- Room temp (airtight): 3 days
- Refrigerated: up to 1 week (bring to room temp for best chew)
- Freeze: 1 month

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (butter, cream)
- Egg
- Wheat (flour)
- Gluten possible in gochujang brand
