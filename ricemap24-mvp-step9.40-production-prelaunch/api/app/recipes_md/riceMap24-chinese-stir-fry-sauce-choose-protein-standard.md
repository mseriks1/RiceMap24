# Chinese Stir-Fry Sauce (万能酱汁) — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A **universal Chinese stir-fry system**: one master sauce that works with **any protein + any vegetables**.
Perfect for RiceMap24/home kitchens because it’s fast, consistent, and easy to scale.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Chinese Stir-Fry Sauce: For Any Meat/Vegetables!” (Updated Feb 5, 2023)

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (general)
- **Style:** Wok stir-fry system (sauce + protein + veg)
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min
- **Total time:** ~25–30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (batch cooking + slurry thickening)

---

## Choose Your Protein (Pick ONE)
Target: **~500 g protein** total for 4 bowls (≈ 125 g per bowl).

- **Chicken:** thigh or breast, thinly sliced
- **Beef:** sirloin/flank, thin strips (across the grain)
- **Pork:** loin/shoulder, thin strips
- **Shrimp:** peeled, raw
- **Tofu:** firm tofu, pressed and cubed
- **Mixed protein:** half chicken + half shrimp (popular)

> Keep protein weight consistent so your pricing stays predictable.

## Master Stir-Fry Sauce (Make 1 bowl; serves 4)
Use The Woks of Life sauce recipe for exact measurement and approach.
To make it **RiceMap24-repeatable**, do this:

1) Make the sauce exactly as in the source recipe.
2) Portion into **4 cups** (1 cup per bowl), OR into **2 cups** if you cook 2 bowls per wok batch.

> **Operational tip:** keep the sauce in a jar; shake before use because cornstarch settles.

---

## Quick Velvet Marinade (Optional, but makes it “restaurant”)
Use this for chicken/beef/pork (skip for tofu; reduce for shrimp):

- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 Tbsp water
- 1 tsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- 1 tsp neutral oil
- 2 tsp cornstarch

**Marinate 10 minutes.**
Shrimp: reduce water to 1 tsp.
Tofu: toss in 1 tsp oil + 2 tsp cornstarch (no water).

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Slice protein** thin and evenly (3–4 mm) for fast cooking.
2. **Marinate** (optional) 10 minutes.
3. **Prep vegetables** (uniform sizes for even cooking).
4. **Make sauce** (exact method from source) and shake/whisk.
5. Put everything next to the stove (stir-fry moves fast).

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable Workflow)

> Best practice: cook **2 bowls at a time** for heat control.

### 1) Sear protein (2–4 minutes)
1. Heat wok over **high** until very hot.
2. Add a little oil.
3. Add protein in a single layer.
4. Cook until **80% done**, then remove to a plate:
   - Chicken/beef/pork: lightly browned edges
   - Shrimp: just turning pink/opaque
   - Tofu: lightly golden edges

### 2) Stir-fry vegetables (2–4 minutes)
1. Add a touch more oil.
2. Add hard veg first (carrot, broccoli stems), then softer veg (mushrooms, peppers).
3. Stir-fry until crisp-tender.

### 3) Sauce + finish (1–2 minutes)
1. Shake sauce again (cornstarch settles).
2. Pour sauce around the wok edge and bring to a strong simmer.
3. Return protein; toss until sauce thickens and coats everything.
4. Add quick veg last (sprouts, snow peas) for 10–20 seconds.
5. Remove from heat.

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
- Multiply sauce by **2.5×** and portion into **10 cups**.
- Protein: **~1.25 kg**
- Veg: **~2.0 kg**
- Cook in **5 batches** (2 bowls each) for consistent wok heat.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Stir-fry in leak-proof container.
- **Rice separate**.
- Note: “Toss before eating. Best within 1–2 hours.”
