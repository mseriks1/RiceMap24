# Miang Kham (Thai One-Bite Leaf Wraps) — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Thai “flavor bomb” snack/appetizer: peppery leaves filled with toasted coconut, peanuts, lime, ginger, shallot, chili, and a sweet-salty sauce.  
**Not typical takeaway** in most places, but it photographs beautifully as a **premium platter** or “party box.”

**Source recipe (reference):** Hot Thai Kitchen — Miang Kham  
https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/miang-kham/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Build-your-own leaf wraps + sauce
- **Yield:** 20–30 wraps (depends on leaf size)
- **Active time:** ~25–35 min  
- **Total time:** ~35–45 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (knife work + sauce simmer)

## Choose Your Protein (Optional Add-on)
Miang Kham traditionally uses small dried shrimp, but you can offer protein options while keeping the core Thai identity.

Pick ONE (or offer a “mix”):
- **Classic:** 80–120 g small dried shrimp (soaked briefly, drained)
- **Shrimp:** 250 g cooked shrimp, chopped small
- **Chicken:** 300 g cooked chicken thigh, chopped small
- **Tofu (veg):** 300 g firm tofu, pressed and diced small
- **No protein:** keep it “royal snack” style (still authentic in many contexts)

> Keep protein pieces SMALL (5–7 mm) so wraps remain one-bite.

## Equipment
- Small saucepan (sauce)
- Dry pan (toast coconut)
- Cutting board + sharp knife
- Small bowls/ramekins (mise en place)
- Optional: microplane (for lime zest) and mortar for grinding

---

## Ingredients (20–30 wraps)

## A) Leaves (wrapper)
- 20–30 **chaphlu/cha-plu leaves** (wild betel / Piper sarmentosum)
  - Substitute if unavailable: baby spinach + Thai basil combo, or small lettuce leaves

## B) Filling components (classic set)
- 3/4 cup toasted coconut (see toasting method)
- 1/2 cup roasted peanuts (chopped)
- 1 lime (cut into tiny dice **including peel**; see safe cutting method)
- 1 (3-inch) piece ginger (peeled, small dice)
- 2 shallots (small dice)
- 5–6 bird’s eye chilies (thin slices) — adjust heat

## C) Sauce (nam miang)
A typical Miang Kham sauce is sweet-salty with a deep caramel note.
Use the Hot Thai Kitchen method for exact ratios. Core components usually include:
- Palm sugar
- Water
- Fish sauce (or soy for vegetarian)
- Shrimp paste (optional, small amount)
- Tamarind (optional)
- Aromatics (often lemongrass/galangal/ginger)

> RiceMap24 tip: Make sauce ahead and bottle it. It’s your “signature.”

## D) Optional garnish (for photos)
- Thin ribbons of kaffir lime leaf
- Extra toasted coconut sprinkle
- Chili slices on top

---

## Prep (20 minutes)

### 1) Toast coconut (3–5 minutes)
1. Add unsweetened coconut flakes/shreds to a **dry pan** over medium heat.
2. Stir constantly until golden and nutty.
3. Cool completely (stays crisp).

### 2) Lime dicing (important safety method)
1. Cut lime into 8 wedges.
2. Trim away the hard white core (bitter).
3. Dice wedges into very small pieces **including the peel** (this is key to Miang Kham flavor).

### 3) Dice everything small
- Ginger, shallot: 5–7 mm dice.
- Peanuts: chop.
- Chilies: thin slices.

### 4) Leaves
- Rinse and dry leaves well (wet leaves weaken the wrap).

---

## Sauce Method (High-level; follow source for exact amounts)
1. Simmer palm sugar + water until dissolved.
2. Add fish sauce (and any optional components like shrimp paste/tamarind).
3. Simmer briefly until slightly thickened and glossy.
4. Cool to warm/room temp before serving.

**Consistency target:** pourable but slightly syrupy.

---

## Serving / Assembly (How customers eat it)
1. Take 1 leaf.
2. Add small pinches of each filling component.
3. Add a spoon of sauce (or drizzle).
4. Fold into a small parcel and eat in **one bite**.

## Portioning Guide (Per 10 wraps)
- Toasted coconut: 2–3 Tbsp
- Peanuts: 2–3 Tbsp
- Lime dice: 2 Tbsp
- Ginger dice: 2 Tbsp
- Shallot dice: 2 Tbsp
- Chili: to taste
- Sauce: 3–5 Tbsp
- Protein (optional): 2–3 Tbsp

---

## Scaling for Production (10 boxes)
- Multiply components by **10×**.
- Pre-dice and store components separately:
  - Peanuts/coconut: room temp (airtight)
  - Lime/ginger/shallot/chili: refrigerated (airtight)
  - Sauce: refrigerated in squeeze bottle

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Keep everything separated:
  - Leaves in a dry bag/container with paper towel
  - Fillings in small cups
  - Sauce in a sealed cup/bottle
- Label: “Build 1-bite wraps. Add sauce last.”

**Holding time:** Great for 3–6 hours if kept cool and dry.

---

## Storage
- Prepped fillings: 1 day refrigerated (best fresh).
- Toasted coconut: 3–5 days airtight.
- Sauce: 3–5 days refrigerated (depends on recipe acidity/salt).

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Fish (fish sauce, shrimp paste if used)
- Shellfish (if shrimp/dried shrimp option)
