# Pho Ga (Vietnamese Chicken Pho) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A fragrant Vietnamese chicken noodle soup with clear spiced broth, rice noodles, tender chicken, and fresh herbs. Ideal as a premium soup bowl with “condiment kit” upsell.

**Source recipe:** RecipeTin Eats — “Chicken Pho (Vietnamese Chicken Noodle Soup)”  
https://www.recipetineats.com/vietnamese-pho-recipe/

## Summary
- **Category:** Vietnamese • Noodle Soup • Clear Broth
- **Yield:** 4–6 portions (depending on bowl size)
- **Active time:** ~25 min  
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min (broth simmer + assembly)
- **Skill level:** Medium (broth clarity + spice control)

> Follow the linked RecipeTin Eats recipe for **exact measurements** (especially spices, fish sauce, sugar, and salt).

### A) Broth base (per source)
- Chicken (whole or pieces) as specified
- Onion (charred) + ginger (charred)
- Water

### B) Pho spices (toast; per source exact amounts)
- Star anise
- Cinnamon stick
- Cloves
- Cardamom pods

### C) Seasoning (per source exact amounts)
- Fish sauce
- Sugar
- Salt (or seasoning method per source)

### D) Noodles + bowl components
- Flat rice noodles (banh pho), amount per portion preference

- Fresh herbs: cilantro + Thai basil (or regular basil) + mint (optional)
- Lime wedges
- Sliced chili (optional)

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Char onion + ginger  
2) Toast spices  
3) Simmer broth (keep it gentle for clarity)  
4) Prep noodle + herb kit  
5) Strain + season broth  
6) Assemble bowls to order

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Char aromatics (flavor + color)
1. Place halved onion and ginger (cut side down) in a dry pan over high heat.
2. Char until blackened in spots and fragrant. Set aside.

## 2) Toast spices
1. In a dry pan, toast pho spices briefly until fragrant (do not burn).
2. Transfer spices to a tea infuser/cheesecloth pouch if you use one (optional but tidy).

## 3) Simmer broth (gentle, not boiling)
1. Add chicken + water to a stockpot.
2. Add charred onion + ginger and toasted spices.
3. Bring to a boil, then immediately reduce to a **gentle simmer**.
4. Simmer until chicken is cooked and broth is fragrant (timing per source).
5. Remove chicken, shred/slice for serving.
6. Continue simmering broth as needed per source.

## 4) Strain + season
1. Strain broth through a fine mesh strainer for clarity.
2. Season with fish sauce + sugar + salt per the source recipe.
3. Keep broth hot on low.

## 5) Noodles
1. Prepare rice noodles per package instructions (often soak/boil briefly).
2. Drain well and portion into bowls.

## 6) Assemble bowls (to order)
1. Add noodles to bowl.
2. Add chicken.
3. Ladle boiling-hot broth over.
4. Top with herbs, sprouts, chili, and lime.

**Customer note:** “Squeeze lime + add herbs. Hoisin/sriracha to taste.”

---

## Quality Checks (Before Service)
- **Broth clarity:** Clear, not cloudy → keep simmer gentle; skim foam early if needed.
- **Aroma:** Distinct star anise/cinnamon note, not overpowering.
- **Seasoning:** Balanced savory-sweet-salty; tastes “light but complete.”

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Because yield is 4–6, scale by bowl size:
- For 10 standard soup bowls, plan **2×** batch (or **1.75×** if your bowls are smaller).

**Batch notes**
- Prepare a “herb + lime + chili kit” assembly line.
- Keep noodles separate until service to prevent sogginess.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best (high quality):** 3-part system
- Container A: noodles + chicken  
- Container B (sealed): hot broth  
- Container C: herb/condiment kit (dry)

Label: “Pour hot broth over noodles + chicken. Add herbs last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Broth:** Refrigerate up to 3 days (skim fat layer if desired).
- **Chicken:** Refrigerate 2–3 days.
- **Reheat:** Bring broth to a boil for service. Avoid microwaving in one container with noodles.
