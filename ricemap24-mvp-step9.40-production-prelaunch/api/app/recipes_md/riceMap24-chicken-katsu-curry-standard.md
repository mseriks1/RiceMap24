# Chicken Katsu Curry (Japanese Curry Rice) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Crispy panko chicken cutlet served with Japanese curry sauce and rice. A high-demand “comfort premium” bowl/box with excellent upsell potential.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Chicken Katsu Curry”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/chicken-katsu-curry/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Curry • Crispy Cutlet
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~25 min  
- **Total time:** ~50–60 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (frying + curry timing)

---

## Equipment
- Pot or deep skillet (for curry)
- Frying pan/pot (for shallow or deep frying)
- Thermometer (recommended)
- Wire rack (keeps katsu crisp)
- Cutting board + knife
- Mixing bowls (breading station)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Chicken Katsu
- **4** boneless, skinless chicken thighs *(or breasts; thighs stay juicier)*  
- Salt + pepper
- Flour (for dredging)
- **2** eggs, beaten (for breading)
- Panko breadcrumbs (for coating)
- Neutral frying oil (enough for shallow fry)

## B) Curry Sauce (Japanese-style)
- **1** onion, sliced
- **1** carrot, sliced
- **2** potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks *(use waxy potatoes if possible)*
- Neutral oil (for sautéing)
- Water/stock (amount per source)
- Japanese curry roux blocks (amount per source) *(or homemade roux per source option)*

## C) To serve
- Steamed rice: **~250 g cooked rice per portion** (4 portions)
- Optional: Fukujinzuke (Japanese pickles)

> **Important:** Curry roux brands/blocks vary in salt and thickness.  
> Use the linked source recipe for the **exact roux amount and water ratio**.

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Vegetables:** Slice onion, carrot; cube potatoes.
2. **Chicken:** Pat dry, trim, season with salt + pepper.
3. **Breading station:** Set up 3 steps:
   - Plate 1: flour  
   - Bowl: beaten eggs  
   - Plate 2: panko  
4. **Rice:** Cook and keep hot.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

# Part 1: Make the Curry Sauce
1. Heat a pot over **medium heat** with a little oil.
2. Add onion and cook until softened.
3. Add carrots and potatoes; stir briefly.
4. Add water/stock per source recipe and bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Simmer until vegetables are tender (time per source; usually ~10–15 min).
6. Turn heat to low and add curry roux blocks. Stir until fully dissolved.
7. Simmer gently to thicken, stirring so it doesn’t stick.

**Quality target:** Sauce should be smooth and spoon-coating, not watery.

# Part 2: Fry the Chicken Katsu
8. Coat each chicken piece: flour → egg → panko (press crumbs in firmly).
9. Heat frying oil to **170–175°C / 340–350°F**.
10. Fry chicken until golden and cooked through (time varies by thickness).
11. Rest on a **wire rack** (not paper towels).
12. Slice into strips.

**Doneness target:** **74°C / 165°F** internal temperature.

# Part 3: Serve
13. Portion rice into 4 bowls/boxes.
14. Add sliced katsu.
15. Ladle curry sauce beside or over rice (best: curry separate from katsu to keep crisp).
16. Add pickles (optional).

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Katsu:** Crisp exterior, juicy inside (rack-resting helps).
- **Curry:** Smooth, rich, not grainy; vegetables tender.
- **Balance:** Curry is mild and savory; pickles add brightness.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Katsu (10 portions)
- Chicken: **10 pieces**
- Eggs: **5**
- Flour + panko: scale as needed (use batching practice)
- Frying oil: maintain same depth; fry in batches

### Curry (10 portions)
- Vegetables: **2.5×** (onion, carrot, potato)
- Water/stock + roux: multiply **exact source measurements** by **2.5×**

### Batch notes
- Cook curry in a larger pot (holds well).
- Fry katsu in batches and hold on racks. Re-crisp briefly if needed.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best (preserves crispness):**
- Compartment 1: rice + curry (or curry in cup)
- Compartment 2: katsu slices
- Optional: pickle cup

Label: “Pour curry over rice; keep katsu crisp by adding last.”

**Holding time:** Curry holds great. Katsu best within **1–2 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Curry:** Refrigerate up to **3 days**; reheat gently (may thicken—add water).
- **Katsu:** Refrigerate up to **2 days**; reheat in oven/air fryer to restore crispness.
