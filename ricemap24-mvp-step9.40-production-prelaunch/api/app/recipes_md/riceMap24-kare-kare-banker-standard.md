# Kare-Kare (Filipino Peanut Stew) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Kare-kare is one of the most loved Filipino “special-occasion but still everyday” dishes: tender meat + vegetables in a rich **peanut sauce**, served with rice and (optionally) sautéed shrimp paste (bagoong) **on the side**.  
For RiceMap24, it’s a great **banker** because it’s hearty, memorable, scalable, and delivers well.

**Reference recipes:** Kawaling Pinoy (Kare-Kare) and Panlasang Pinoy (Oxtail Kare-Kare).

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino
- **Style:** Peanut stew + vegetables + optional bagoong side
- **Yield:** 6 generous portions (or 8 lighter portions)
- **Active time:** ~25 minutes
- **Total time:** 2.5–3.5 hours (depends on meat cut)
- **Skill level:** Medium (stew timing + sauce balance)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **Broad appeal:** creamy, savory, peanut-forward (like a comfort stew).
- **Upgrade path:** offer “Beef Kare-Kare” (easy) and “Oxtail Kare-Kare” (premium).
- **EU-friendly option:** serve bagoong **separately** (customers choose).
- **Great in photos:** orange-gold sauce + colorful veg + rice + garnish.
- **Delivery-friendly:** stew in one container + rice separate; veg stays intact if blanched/added at the end.

---

## Choose Your Meat (Pick ONE)
### Option A (Recommended banker): Beef chuck / stew beef
- **1.2 kg** beef chuck (or stew beef), cut into 4–5 cm chunks  
**Cook time:** ~1.5–2 hours simmer

### Option B (Premium): Oxtail
- **1.5 kg** oxtail pieces  
**Cook time:** ~2.5–3.5 hours simmer (or pressure cooker)

### Option C (Budget): Beef shank or brisket mix
- **1.2–1.5 kg** total

> For the easiest “home kitchen banker,” Option A (beef chuck) is the best balance of cost, speed, and tenderness.

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Aromatics + base
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1 large onion**, chopped
- **6 cloves garlic**, minced
- **1.5–2.0 L** water or beef stock (enough to cover meat while simmering)
- **2–3 Tbsp** fish sauce (or salt to taste)

## B) Peanut sauce (core)
- **250 g** peanut butter (smooth)
- **80–120 g** ground peanuts (optional but very authentic; adds texture)
- **3–4 Tbsp** toasted rice flour *(see quick method below)*  
  - Substitute: plain rice flour (works, less nutty)

## C) Color (annatto)
- **2 Tbsp** annatto powder **OR**
- **Annatto water:** soak **2 Tbsp annatto seeds** in **250 ml hot water** for 10 minutes, then strain

## D) Vegetables (classic set, choose 3–5)
- **1 large eggplant**, sliced into 2.5 cm pieces
- **250 g** long beans (sitaw) or green beans, cut into 5 cm pieces
- **2 bunches** bok choy/pechay (or napa cabbage), separated
- Optional: banana blossom (puso ng saging), sliced (more “authentic”)
- Optional: okra

## E) Optional side: Bagoong (served separately)
- **150–250 g** bagoong alamang (shrimp paste), sautéed
- Optional: a little sugar + chili for balance

> EU-friendly approach: label it as “shrimp paste relish (optional).”

## Equipment
- Large heavy pot (6–8 L)
- Strainer + bowl (optional)
- Whisk
- Small pan (for toasted rice flour and bagoong)
- Knife + board
- Ladle

---

## Prep (10–12 minutes)
1. Chop onion, mince garlic.
2. Cut meat into chunks (or rinse oxtail).
3. Prepare annatto water (or set annatto powder aside).
4. Prep vegetables (keep leafy greens separate; add at the end).

---

## Toasted Rice Flour (3–5 minutes, worth it)
1. Add **4 Tbsp rice flour** to a dry pan over medium heat.
2. Stir constantly until it smells nutty and turns very light beige (don’t burn).
3. Use **3–4 Tbsp** in the stew; store the rest airtight.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Brown aromatics (5 minutes)
1. Heat pot over medium.
2. Add oil.
3. Sauté onions until soft (2–3 minutes).
4. Add garlic and cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.

## Step 2 — Simmer meat until tender
1. Add meat.
2. Add water/stock to cover by 2–3 cm.
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer.
4. Skim foam if needed (cleaner sauce).
5. Simmer covered until tender:
   - Beef chuck: **1.5–2 hours**
   - Oxtail: **2.5–3.5 hours**
6. Season the broth lightly with fish sauce (start with 2 Tbsp).

> The broth you create here becomes your peanut sauce base.

---

## Step 3 — Build the peanut sauce (10 minutes)
1. Scoop out **1 liter** of hot broth into a bowl (estimate; you can add more later).
2. Whisk peanut butter into the hot broth until smooth.
3. Add annatto water (or annatto powder) for color.
4. Whisk in toasted rice flour (3 Tbsp to start).
5. Return this peanut mixture to the pot and stir.

### Sauce thickness targets
- Should coat a spoon and feel like a stew, not soup.
- If too thin: add 1 Tbsp toasted rice flour (whisk first in a little hot broth), simmer 5 minutes.
- If too thick: add hot broth or water in small splashes.

### Add ground peanuts (optional)
- Stir in ground peanuts for extra body and authentic texture.

---

## Step 4 — Cook vegetables (keep them beautiful)
**Best practice for delivery:** blanch veg separately or add late so they stay intact.

### Method A (fastest): add to pot near the end
1. Add long beans first; simmer **5–7 minutes**.
2. Add eggplant; simmer **5 minutes** (until tender but not mushy).
3. Add bok choy/pechay last; simmer **1–2 minutes** just to wilt.

### Method B (best texture): blanch separately
1. Blanch beans and bok choy briefly, drain well.
2. Pan-sear eggplant or blanch it.
3. Add vegetables to the stew right before service, just to heat through.

---

## Step 5 — Final seasoning
Taste and adjust:
- Needs more savory depth → a little more fish sauce
- Too salty → add a splash of water + a pinch of sugar
- Too “flat” → a squeeze of calamansi/lime at service (optional)

Simmer 3–5 minutes after final adjustments.

---

## Optional: Sautéed Bagoong (served separately)
1. In a small pan, add 1 tsp oil.
2. Sauté bagoong 2–3 minutes.
3. Optional: add a pinch of sugar and chili to round it out.
Serve in a small cup on the side.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Meat:** fork-tender, not chewy.
- **Sauce:** peanut-forward, smooth, orange-gold, not grainy.
- **Veg:** still has shape and color.
- **Serving:** bagoong is optional and separate.

## Scaling for Production (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (since base makes ~6 generous portions).
- Beef chuck: ~2.0 kg (or oxtail ~2.5 kg)
- Peanut butter: ~425 g
- Toasted rice flour: ~6–7 Tbsp
- Veg: scale 1.7×
- Broth: plan 3–3.5 L total

**Ops tip:** Cook meat + sauce in a large pot. Prep veg separately and add per batch for best look.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: Kare-kare (stew)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Cup C: bagoong (optional)
- Optional: lime/calamansi wedge in small bag

Label: “Peanut stew. Add shrimp paste relish if you want it more traditional.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Stew keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently (do not hard boil).
- Add a splash of water/stock if sauce thickens overnight.

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Fish/shellfish (if fish sauce and/or bagoong is used)
- Soy (some peanut butter brands; check label)
