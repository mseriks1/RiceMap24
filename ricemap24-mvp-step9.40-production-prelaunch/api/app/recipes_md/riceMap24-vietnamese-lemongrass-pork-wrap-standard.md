# Vietnamese Lemongrass Pork Wrap (Pickles + Herbs) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A premium wrap with a bánh mì vibe: lemongrass pork, crunchy pickles (đồ chua), cucumber, herbs, and a light spicy mayo.  
Fusion (wrap format), designed for pickup with “pickles separate” option.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Asian-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Vietnamese bánh mì profile
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~20–25 minutes
- **Total time:** ~40–50 minutes (includes quick pickles)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Fresh + aromatic** (lemongrass + herbs).
- **Crunch factor** from pickles makes it feel premium.
- **Scales well** (pork + pickles batch).
- **Upsells:** extra pork, extra pickles, extra sauce cup.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Pork (lemongrass marinade)
- **700 g** pork shoulder (preferred) or pork belly, thin strips/slices
- **2 stalks** lemongrass (white part), very finely minced *(or 2 Tbsp lemongrass paste)*
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** sugar
- **1.5 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1 tsp** black pepper
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** soy sauce (adds color)

> Marinate 20–30 minutes.

---

## B) Quick pickles (Đồ Chua)
- **1 carrot** (~150 g), julienne
- **200 g** daikon (or kohlrabi), julienne
- **120 ml** rice vinegar
- **120 ml** water
- **35 g** sugar
- **1 tsp** salt

---

## C) Sauce (spicy mayo)
- **120 ml** mayo
- **1–2 tsp** sriracha (adjust)
- **1 tsp** lime juice (optional)
- Pinch salt

---

## D) Wrap build
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps
- Lettuce leaves (recommended barrier)
- **1 cucumber**, thin sticks
- Fresh herbs: cilantro (and/or mint)
- Optional: sliced chili/jalapeño

---

## Equipment
- Skillet or grill pan
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (optional)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make quick pickles (10 minutes + rest)
1. Mix vinegar, water, sugar, salt until dissolved.
2. Add carrot + daikon; toss.
3. Rest 20–30 minutes, then drain well.

---

## 2) Mix spicy mayo (1 minute)
- Stir mayo + sriracha + lime + pinch salt.

---

## 3) Cook pork (8–10 minutes)
1. Heat skillet medium-high with a little oil.
2. Cook pork in batches until browned and cooked through.
3. Reduce any watery juices quickly (avoid soggy filling).

---

## 4) Assemble wraps
Per wrap:
1. Warm tortilla briefly.
2. Add lettuce barrier.
3. Spread 1–2 Tbsp spicy mayo.
4. Add pork portion (160–200 g cooked).
5. Add cucumber + herbs.
6. Add a generous pinch of drained pickles.
7. Roll tight.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- Pork: 160–200 g cooked
- Pickles: 40–60 g
- Mayo: 25–30 g
- Herbs/cucumber: as needed

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Pork: 1.8 kg
- Pickles: 4 carrots + 800 g daikon
- Mayo: 300 ml mayo + sriracha to taste

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- Wrap A: tortilla + mayo + pork + cucumber/herbs (rolled)
- Cup B: pickles (customer adds) for max crunch
- Optional: extra mayo cup

**Label:** “Add pickles last for maximum crunch.”

---

## Storage
- Pickles: 5–7 days refrigerated
- Cooked pork: 2 days refrigerated
- Mayo: 3 days refrigerated

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Egg (mayo)
- Wheat (tortilla)
- Soy optional
- Gluten possible (soy sauce)
