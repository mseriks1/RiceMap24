# Hong Shao Rou (红烧肉) — Chinese Red-Braised Pork Belly — “Banker Dish” — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A true Chinese **banker**: pork belly (or shoulder) braised “red-cooked” until tender in a glossy, savory-sweet sauce.  
For RiceMap24, this is ideal because it’s **batch-friendly**, **reheats perfectly**, and is a guaranteed **rice seller**.

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Shanghai-style red braise)
- **Style:** Caramelized braise (pork + glossy sauce) served with rice
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~2 hours
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (caramel + gentle braise)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **High repeat-order potential:** savory-sweet comfort flavor.
- **One-pot batch cooking:** easy to portion consistently.
- **Delivery-friendly:** sauce protects meat; tastes better day 2.
- **Upsells:** extra pork, extra sauce cup, add egg, add greens.

---

## Choose Your Cut (Pick ONE)
### Option A (most classic): Pork belly
- **1.5 kg** pork belly, cut into **4–5 cm** cubes

### Option B (leaner + still great): Pork shoulder/butt
- **1.5 kg** pork shoulder, 4–5 cm cubes

> If your market prefers less fat, do a **50/50 belly + shoulder** blend.

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Pork + quick blanch (recommended)
- 1.5 kg pork (above)
- Water to cover

## B) Braise base
- **60 ml** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- **90 ml** light soy sauce
- **30 ml** dark soy sauce *(optional but recommended for color)*
- **600–750 ml** water (start with 600 ml)
- **30–40 g** rock sugar *(or 3 Tbsp brown sugar)*
- **6 slices** ginger
- **3 scallions**, smashed (white parts)
- Optional spices (keep minimal for “Shanghai banker”):
  - **2** star anise (optional)
  - **1 small** cinnamon stick (optional)

## C) Finish
- Freshly cracked black pepper (optional)
- Optional: blanched bok choy or greens (for the plate)
- Steamed jasmine rice (about **250 g cooked** per order)

---

## Equipment
- Large heavy pot/Dutch oven (5–6 L)
- Skimmer/ladle
- Tongs
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Cut pork into 4–5 cm cubes.
2. Slice ginger; smash scallion whites.
3. Measure soy sauces, wine, sugar.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Blanch pork (5 minutes) *(recommended for clean sauce)*
1. Put pork in pot, cover with water.
2. Bring to a boil and boil **3–5 minutes**.
3. Drain and rinse quickly to remove scum.

> This makes the final sauce clearer and more “premium” looking.

---

## Step 2 — Start the red braise (10 minutes)
1. Return pork to pot.
2. Add ginger, smashed scallions, and optional spices.
3. Add rock sugar, Shaoxing wine, light soy, and dark soy (if using).
4. Add **600 ml water** (pork should be mostly submerged).
5. Bring to a boil.

---

## Step 3 — Gentle simmer (60–75 minutes)
1. Reduce to a gentle simmer (small bubbles).
2. Cover partially (leave a small gap).
3. Simmer **60–75 minutes**, stirring gently every 15–20 minutes.

**Tenderness check:** pork should be tender but still hold shape.

If liquid reduces too fast:
- add 50–100 ml hot water as needed.

---

## Step 4 — Reduce to glossy sauce (15–25 minutes)
1. Uncover fully and increase heat to medium.
2. Simmer until sauce reduces and becomes glossy and slightly sticky.
3. Stir more often near the end to prevent scorching.

**Sauce target:** coats the back of a spoon and clings to pork.

---

## Step 5 — Final taste and balance
Taste and adjust:
- Too salty → add a splash of hot water and simmer 2 minutes.
- Too sweet → add a tiny splash of soy or a pinch of salt.
- Needs more aroma → a little cracked black pepper at the end.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Color:** deep red-brown glaze (dark soy helps).
- **Pork:** tender, not dry.
- **Sauce:** glossy and clingy, not watery.
- **No bitterness:** don’t burn sugar during reduction.

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Pork: ~2.5 kg
- Light soy: ~150 ml
- Dark soy: ~50 ml
- Wine: ~100 ml
- Sugar: ~60–70 g
- Water: 1.0–1.2 L (adjust)
Cook in a wider pot so reduction is easier.

- Optional veg container

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate **3–4 days** (often tastes better on day 2).
- Reheat gently to a simmer; add a splash of water if sauce is very thick.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce, Shaoxing wine brands)
