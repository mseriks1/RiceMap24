# Injeolmi (Korean Sweet Rice Cake with Soybean Powder) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

Chewy Korean rice cake squares coated in nutty **roasted soybean powder** (“konggaru” / “kinako”).  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** no melting, stable when boxed, looks premium in bite-size cubes, and sells well as **6-pack / 12-pack / family box**.

**Reference recipe:** Maangchi — Injeolmi (Korean rice cake)  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/injeolmi

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Korean
- **Style:** Steamed sweet rice cake, cut into cubes, coated in soybean powder
- **Yield:** 1 tray (about 20–28 cubes depending on cut size)
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~40–50 min (including steaming + cooling)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (sticky dough handling)

---

- **No melt / no mess:** travels well, no sauces needed.
- **Photogenic:** clean cubes with a “snowy” soybean coating.
- **Fast to produce:** steam, cut, coat.

## Equipment
- Steamer (pot + rack + lid)
- Heatproof bowl or tray (metal pan works well)
- Parchment paper (or lightly oiled tray)
- Rubber spatula
- Sharp knife or bench scraper
- Small sieve (optional; for fine coating)
- Gloves (recommended; dough is sticky)
- Kitchen scale (recommended for consistent packs)

---

## Ingredients (1 tray)

## A) Rice cake base
- **500 g** sweet rice flour (glutinous rice flour)
- **1 tsp** fine salt
- **2–3 Tbsp** sugar (optional; mild sweetness)
- **350–380 ml** hot water *(start with 350 ml; add more if needed)*
- **1 Tbsp** toasted sesame oil *(for aroma + anti-stick)*

## B) Coating (“Konggaru” / Soybean powder)
- **250–300 g** roasted soybean powder (konggaru / kinako)
- **2–3 Tbsp** sugar (optional)
- **1/2 tsp** fine salt

## Optional premium add-ons
- Toasted sesame seeds (sprinkle)
- Black sesame powder (mix into coating for a “two-tone” box)

---

## Prep (5 minutes)
1. Line your steaming bowl/tray with parchment (or brush lightly with neutral oil).
2. Prepare a coating tray: mix soybean powder with sugar + salt.
3. Set up steamer and bring water to a steady boil.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Mix the rice cake dough (5 minutes)
1. In a mixing bowl, combine sweet rice flour + salt (+ sugar if using).
2. Pour in **hot water** gradually while mixing with a spatula.
3. Mix until you get a thick, sticky dough that holds together.

### Dough texture check
- If it looks dry and crumbly → add hot water **1 Tbsp at a time**.
- If it’s very runny → add a little more flour (small amount) and mix.

---

## 2) Steam (20 minutes)
1. Transfer dough into the parchment-lined steaming bowl/tray.
2. Smooth the top with a wet spatula (prevents sticking).
3. Steam over boiling water for **20 minutes**.

**Doneness check:** dough turns glossy and fully opaque; no raw flour smell.

---

## 3) Pound/mix + season with sesame oil (2–3 minutes)
1. Carefully remove the hot tray.
2. Add **1 Tbsp sesame oil** on top.
3. Using a spatula (or pestle), fold and “pound” the rice cake a bit until smooth and elastic.

> This improves chew and gives a glossy, premium texture.

---

## 4) Shape and coat (10 minutes)
1. Spread some soybean coating on your work surface or in a shallow tray.
2. Tip the hot rice cake onto the coating.
3. Dust the top heavily with more soybean powder so it’s fully covered (this is your anti-stick layer).
4. Press gently into a rectangle about **2–3 cm** thick.

---

## 5) Cut into cubes (5 minutes)
1. Coat your knife/bench scraper with soybean powder (repeat often).
2. Cut into clean cubes:
   - **Large cubes:** 4×5 grid (20 pieces)
   - **Snack cubes:** 5×6 grid (30 pieces)

**Consistency tip:** Weigh 1 cube and match your pack sizes by weight for predictable pricing.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Chew:** bouncy and elastic, not crumbly.
- **Coating:** dry and nutty, not damp.
- **Shape:** clean edges; cubes hold together.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Box with dividers or paper cups to prevent cubes sticking together.
- Dust a little extra soybean powder in the box.
- Add label: “Best the same day. Keep cool and airtight.”

---

## Storage
Injeolmi hardens over time (normal for rice cakes).
- **Best same day.**
- Airtight at room temp: **6–10 hours** (good for pickup)
- Refrigeration makes it firmer; bring to room temp before eating.
- Freezing works best for longer storage: thaw at room temp, then re-soften briefly (microwave 10–15 sec) and re-coat lightly.
