# Vietnamese Lemongrass Chicken Rice Bowl — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Char-grilled-style Vietnamese lemongrass chicken served over rice with bright Nuoc Cham sauce. High-demand, easy to batch, and perfect for “spicy level” add-ons.

**Source recipe:** RecipeTin Eats — “Vietnamese Noodles with Lemongrass Chicken” (use the *Chicken & Marinade* + *Nuoc Cham* sections)  
https://www.recipetineats.com/vietnamese-chicken-noodle-bowl/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Vietnamese • Rice Bowl • Grill / Pan Sear
- **Yield:** 4 portions (rice bowls)
- **Active time:** ~15 min prep + ~10 min cook
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min *(includes 1 hr marinade; longer is better)*
- **Skill level:** Easy (timing + browning)

---

## Equipment
- Large skillet / grill pan / BBQ
- Mixing bowl (marinade)
- Small bowl or jar (Nuoc Cham)
- Tongs
- Cutting board + knife

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Lemongrass Chicken + Marinade
- **600–800 g** chicken thigh fillets, skinless & boneless *(breast also works)*  
- **1 stalk** lemongrass, **white part only**, bruised then sliced into pieces (easy to pick out later)  
- **2 cloves** garlic, finely chopped or minced  
- **2 Tbsp** lime juice  
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce  
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce *(all purpose or light; not dark soy)*  
- **2 Tbsp** brown sugar  
- **1 Tbsp** vegetable oil  

## B) Nuoc Cham (Vietnamese Sauce)
- **1/4 cup** fish sauce  
- **4 Tbsp** rice vinegar  
- **2 Tbsp** white sugar  
- **1/2 cup** water  
- **2 cloves** garlic, finely chopped  
- **1** red bird’s eye chilli, finely chopped *(or substitute another hot chilli to taste)*  
- **3 Tbsp** lime juice  

## C) Bowl base
- Cooked rice: **~250 g cooked rice per bowl** (4 bowls)

---

## Prep (Do this before cooking)
1. **Lemongrass prep:** Peel tough outer layers to expose the softer white base (bottom ~1/3). Bruise with the back of a knife, then slice into pieces (not too fine—so you can remove it later).
2. **Marinade:** Combine all chicken + marinade ingredients in a bowl. Toss well.
3. **Marinate:** Cover and refrigerate **at least 1 hour**, up to **24 hours**.
4. **Nuoc Cham:** Mix all Nuoc Cham ingredients until sugar dissolves. Rest **20 minutes** for best flavor.
5. **Rice:** Cook and keep hot (or reheat to order).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Cook the chicken
1. Heat a skillet over **medium heat** (or preheat BBQ/grill).
2. Remove chicken from marinade, shaking off **large** lemongrass pieces (small bits usually fall off during cooking).
3. Add chicken to the pan and cook until **dark golden brown** and **just cooked through**, about **6–8 minutes total** (flip once).
4. Rest chicken **5 minutes**, then slice thinly.

> **Tenderness rule:** Stop as soon as chicken is cooked through. Overcooking dries it out.

## 2) Assemble bowls
1. Add hot rice to 4 bowls/containers.
2. Top with sliced lemongrass chicken.
3. Drizzle generously with **Nuoc Cham** (offer extra on the side).

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Color:** Dark golden browning (caramelization from sugar).
- **Chicken:** Juicy; no pink center.
- **Sauce:** Sweet/salty/tangy balance; adjust next batch (lime/sugar/chilli) to your audience.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### A) Chicken + Marinade (10 portions)
- Chicken: **1500–2000 g**
- Lemongrass: **2–3 stalks** (white part only), bruised + sliced
- Garlic: **5 cloves**
- Lime juice: **5 Tbsp**
- Fish sauce: **5 Tbsp**
- Soy sauce: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Brown sugar: **5 Tbsp**
- Vegetable oil: **2.5 Tbsp**

### B) Nuoc Cham (10 portions)
- Fish sauce: **0.625 cup** *(= 1/2 cup + 2 Tbsp)*
- Rice vinegar: **10 Tbsp** *(= 1/2 cup + 2 Tbsp)*
- White sugar: **5 Tbsp**
- Water: **1.25 cups**
- Garlic: **5 cloves**
- Bird’s eye chilli: **2–3** (to taste)
- Lime juice: **7.5 Tbsp**

### C) Rice (10 portions)
- Cooked rice: **~2500 g cooked** (≈ 250 g per bowl)

### Batch notes
- Cook chicken in **batches** to maintain browning (don’t overcrowd).
- Make Nuoc Cham in a **jar** and shake before service.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rice in base, chicken on top.
- **Nuoc Cham in a separate mini cup** (best) to keep rice from getting soggy.
- Offer “Spice level” by adding extra chilli to the sauce cup, not the whole batch.

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Chicken:** Refrigerate up to **3 days**. Reheat gently (microwave medium or quick pan warm).
- **Nuoc Cham:** Keeps **5–7 days** refrigerated (shake/stir before use).
- **Rice:** Best fresh; reheat with a splash of water and cover.

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- This marinade is very forgiving—**thighs** give best juiciness.
- Browning is flavor: give the chicken space and time to caramelize.
- Nuoc Cham is the “wow”: keep it fresh and don’t be shy with it.
