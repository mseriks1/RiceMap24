# Oyakodon (Japanese Chicken & Egg Rice Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic donburi: chicken and onions simmered in a savory-sweet dashi sauce, finished with softly set eggs over rice. Fast, comforting, and ideal for to-go bowls.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Oyakodon (Chicken & Egg Bowl)”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/oyakodon/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Donburi • Simmer + Egg Finish
- **Yield:** 2 portions
- **Active time:** ~10–15 min  
- **Total time:** ~20–25 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (egg timing)

---

## Equipment
- Medium skillet with lid (20–24 cm / 8–10 in works well)
- Small bowl (eggs)
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring spoons/cups

---

## Ingredients (2 portions)

### Main
- **340 g** boneless, skinless chicken thighs *(recommended)*  
- **1/2** onion *(thinly sliced)*
- **2** large eggs
- **2 servings** cooked short-grain rice

### Sauce (mix in pan)
- **120 ml** dashi
- **1 1/2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 1/2 Tbsp** mirin
- **1 1/2 tsp** sugar
- **1 Tbsp** sake *(optional, but included in many versions; follow source)*

### Optional garnish
- Mitsuba (Japanese parsley) or sliced scallions
- Shichimi togarashi (Japanese seven-spice) at service

*(Ingredient set and approach follow the linked source recipe.)*

---

## Prep (5–10 minutes)
1. **Onion:** Slice thinly.
2. **Chicken:** Cut into bite-size pieces (2–3 cm). Keep pieces consistent for even cooking.
3. **Eggs:** Lightly beat eggs in a bowl—don’t overmix (some streaks are fine).
4. **Rice:** Keep hot.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Simmer onions in sauce
1. In a medium skillet, combine **dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sugar, (sake)**.
2. Add onion and bring to a gentle simmer over **medium heat**.
3. Simmer until onion softens, about **3–5 minutes**.

### 2) Cook chicken
4. Add chicken pieces in a single layer. Keep at a gentle simmer (avoid hard boiling).
5. Cook until chicken is just cooked through, about **5–7 minutes** (depends on thickness).

### 3) Egg finish (soft set)
6. Reduce heat slightly if bubbling is aggressive.
7. Pour beaten eggs evenly over the chicken and onions in a thin stream.
8. Cover with lid and cook until eggs are **just set but still slightly glossy**, about **30–90 seconds**.
9. Turn off heat and rest **30 seconds** (residual heat finishes the eggs).

> **Stop point:** Eggs should be soft and tender, not fully firm.

### 4) Serve
10. Portion hot rice into 2 bowls.
11. Slide the chicken/egg mixture on top and spoon a little sauce over.
12. Garnish (optional) and serve immediately.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Eggs:** Soft-set, not rubbery → shorten covered cook time.
- **Chicken:** Tender and juicy → keep simmer gentle, stop early.
- **Sauce:** Balanced savory-sweet; adjust sugar slightly next batch if needed.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 2 portions. Multiply by **5×**.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Chicken thighs: **1700 g**
- Onion: **2.5 onions** (or ~500–600 g), thinly sliced
- Eggs: **10**
- Cooked rice: **10 servings**

### Sauce (5×)
- Dashi: **600 ml**
- Soy sauce: **7.5 Tbsp**
- Mirin: **7.5 Tbsp**
- Sugar: **7.5 tsp**
- Sake: **5 Tbsp** *(if using)*

### Batch notes
- For best egg texture, cook **2 portions at a time** in a skillet.
- Keep sauce at a gentle simmer; avoid rolling boil.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Best:** Two compartments (rice separate from topping) to preserve texture.
- If single bowl: rice base + topping; include note “Stir gently before eating.”

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** for soft egg texture.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **1 day** (egg texture degrades).
- **Reheat:** Gentle microwave; avoid overheating.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Egg, soy (soy sauce)
- **May contain:** Fish (if dashi is fish-based)
- **Alcohol:** Mirin/sake (typically cooked off)
