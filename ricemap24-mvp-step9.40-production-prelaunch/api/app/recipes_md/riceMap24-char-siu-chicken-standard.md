# Char Siu Chicken (Chinese BBQ-Style Chicken) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Chinese BBQ-inspired chicken dish with a glossy, sweet-savory char siu-style glaze. Great for rice boxes and meal prep.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Char Siu Chicken”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/char-siu-chicken/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Chinese • BBQ-Style • Glazed Chicken
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15 min  
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min *(includes bake + glaze steps; optional marinating improves flavor)*
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (glaze timing)

---

## Equipment
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Baking sheet + foil/parchment
- Wire rack (recommended for even cooking)
- Saucepan (small) for reducing glaze (optional but useful)
- Instant-read thermometer (recommended)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Chicken
- **900 g** boneless, skinless chicken thighs *(preferred for juiciness)*  
  *(Breasts also work but are less forgiving.)*

### Marinade / Glaze (mix in a bowl)
*(Use the exact ingredient set and ratios from the source recipe linked above.)*
- Char siu sauce base (typical components in the source):  
  - hoisin sauce  
  - soy sauce  
  - honey or sugar  
  - Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)  
  - five-spice powder  
  - garlic  
  - red coloring option (e.g., red food coloring or fermented red bean curd), optional  
  - plus any additional items specified in the source recipe  

> **Important:** The precise amounts vary by recipe version; follow the linked source for the **exact measurements**.

### To serve
- Cooked rice (or noodles)
- Optional sides: cucumber salad, steamed bok choy, pickled vegetables

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Trim chicken:** Remove excess fat; keep pieces similar thickness for even cooking.
2. **Mix marinade:** Whisk until smooth.
3. **Marinate (recommended):** Coat chicken and marinate **at least 2 hours**, ideally **overnight** (covered, refrigerated).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Bake
1. Preheat oven to **220°C / 425°F**.
2. Line a baking sheet with foil/parchment. Place a **wire rack** on top if available.
3. Arrange chicken in a single layer (leave space between pieces).
4. Bake until cooked through, turning once if the recipe instructs, and basting as directed by the source.

> **Doneness target:** **74°C / 165°F** internal temperature at the thickest part.

### 2) Glaze & finish
5. During the last phase of cooking, brush with extra marinade/glaze as directed by the source recipe.
6. Optional (recommended for “BBQ shine”): switch to **broil/grill** briefly to caramelize the surface—watch closely to avoid burning sugar.

### 3) Rest & slice
7. Rest chicken **5 minutes** before slicing.
8. Slice into strips or bite-size pieces for rice boxes.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Glaze:** Glossy, sticky, slightly charred edges—not burnt.
- **Chicken:** Juicy; not dry (thighs help).
- **Balance:** Sweet-salty; if too sweet, serve with a crisp, fresh side.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base recipe is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

- Chicken: **2250 g**
- Marinade/glaze: **2.5×** (use exact amounts from the source and multiply)

### Batch notes
- Bake on **multiple trays** to avoid steaming.
- Rotate trays for even browning.
- Finish with a quick broil per tray for consistent caramelization.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Option A:** Rice base + sliced char siu chicken + sauce drizzle.
- **Option B (best):** Chicken separate compartment; sauce in mini cup; rice stays fluffy.

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours** for glossy glaze and texture.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3 days**.
- **Reheat:** Medium power microwave or quick hot-pan toss.  
  Add a teaspoon of water to prevent glaze from drying out.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Soy (soy sauce, hoisin)  
- **May contain:** Gluten (most soy sauce/hoisin)  
- **Alcohol:** Shaoxing wine (usually cooked off)

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Sugar glazes burn quickly—broil only briefly and watch closely.
- Thighs stay juicy and reheat better than breasts.
- A wire rack improves airflow and glaze texture.
