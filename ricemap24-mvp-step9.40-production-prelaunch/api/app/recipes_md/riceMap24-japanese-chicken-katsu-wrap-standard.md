# Japanese Katsu Wrap (Chicken Katsu + Tonkatsu Sauce + Cabbage) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A premium crispy wrap: chicken katsu, shredded cabbage, tonkatsu sauce, and kewpie-style mayo.  
Fusion (wrap format), optimized for pickup with “sauce separate” to keep crispness.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Asian-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Japanese katsu
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~25 minutes
- **Total time:** ~35 minutes
- **Skill level:** Medium (crispy workflow)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Crispy katsu sells** (restaurant feel).
- **High perceived value** (golden cutlet + sweet-savory sauce).
- **Pickup-friendly** when vented and sauce is separated.
- **Upsells:** extra katsu, extra sauce cup, spicy mayo, add fries.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Chicken katsu
- **600–700 g** chicken breast cutlets (or thigh fillets flattened)
- Salt + pepper

### Breading station
- **80 g** flour
- **2 eggs** beaten
- **200 g** panko breadcrumbs

### Frying
- Oil for frying  
  *(or air fryer method below)*

---

## B) Sauces
### Tonkatsu sauce (the identity)
- Store-bought tonkatsu sauce (easy and authentic vibe)
  - Portion: **30–40 ml** per wrap (cup)

### Kewpie-style mayo
- **120 ml** mayo (kewpie if available)
- Optional: 1 tsp rice vinegar + pinch sugar

---

## C) Crunch + veg
- **300 g** shredded cabbage (very thin)
- **1 cucumber**, thin sticks/slices (optional)
- Optional: pickled ginger (tiny adds premium)

---

## D) Wraps
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps

## Finish
- Sesame seeds (optional)
- Scallions (optional)

---

## Equipment
- Frying pot/skillet
- Tongs
- Thermometer (optional)
- Knife + cutting board
- Paper towels / rack

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Prep cabbage (2 minutes)
- Shred very thin. Keep dry and crisp.

---

## 2) Bread chicken (6–8 minutes)
1. Season chicken with salt/pepper.
2. Dredge in flour → egg → panko (press panko firmly).

---

## 3) Fry (6–8 minutes)
### Fry method (best)
1. Heat oil to **175–180°C**.
2. Fry cutlets **3–4 minutes per side** until golden and cooked.
3. Drain on rack.

**Doneness:** 74°C internal.

### Air-fryer method (easier)
1. Spray breaded chicken lightly with oil.
2. Air fry **200°C** until crisp and cooked, flipping once.

---

## 4) Slice + assemble wraps
Per wrap:
1. Warm tortilla briefly.
2. Spread thin mayo layer.
3. Add shredded cabbage (and cucumber if using).
4. Add sliced katsu (1 cutlet per wrap).
5. Add tonkatsu sauce:
   - either drizzle lightly
   - or keep in cup for pickup crispness
6. Roll tight.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- 1 chicken cutlet (~150–180 g cooked)
- Cabbage: 60–90 g
- Tonkatsu sauce: 30–40 ml (cup)
- Mayo: 20–25 g

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Chicken: **1.6–1.8 kg**
- Panko: **500 g**
- Eggs: 5–6
- Cabbage: 750 g
- Tonkatsu sauce cups: 10× 40 ml

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- Wrap A: katsu + cabbage wrap (tight parchment)
- Cup B: tonkatsu sauce (customer drizzles)
- Optional: vent 30–60 sec before sealing to protect crispness

**Label:** “Add sauce last for maximum crisp.”

---

## Storage
- Katsu best same day.
- Re-crisp in air fryer/oven before serving if needed.

---

## Allergens

- Egg

- Gluten possible (brands)
