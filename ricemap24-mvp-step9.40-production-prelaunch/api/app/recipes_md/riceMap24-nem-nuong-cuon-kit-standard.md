# Nem Nướng Cuốn (Nha Trang Grilled Pork Paste Rice Paper Rolls) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A regional specialty from **Nha Trang**: springy charcoal-grilled pork paste (nem nướng) rolled with rice paper, herbs, cucumber, and a crunchy element—served with a signature **orange pork-peanut sauce**.  
Not the “default” Vietnamese takeaway like phở/bánh mì, but extremely photogenic and perfect as a **DIY roll kit** for home-kitchen sellers.

**Primary reference recipe:** Serious Eats — “Nem Nướng Cuốn (Vietnamese Grilled Pork Paste Rice Paper Rolls)”  
https://www.seriouseats.com/grilled-pork-paste-rice-paper-rolls-recipe-8415789

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese (Nha Trang)
- **Style:** Rice paper roll kit + grilled pork paste skewers + dipping sauce
- **Yield:** 4 meal kits (or 6 snack kits)
- **Active time:** ~35–50 min
- **Total time:** ~60–90 min (includes chilling the pork paste)
- **Skill level:** Medium (pork paste texture + grilling)

## Equipment
- Food processor (or stand mixer) for springy pork paste
- Mixing bowl
- Bamboo skewers (soaked 20 minutes)
- Grill / grill pan / oven broiler (grill best for char)
- Small saucepan (sauce)
- Knife + cutting board
- Shallow bowl of warm water (for rice paper)
- Optional: thermometer (pork to 71–74°C)

---

## Ingredients (4 meal kits)

## A) Nem Nướng (grilled pork paste)
- **900 g** ground pork (20–30% fat preferred for juiciness)
- **20 g** garlic, minced
- **25 g** sugar (about 2 Tbsp)
- **30 ml** fish sauce (2 Tbsp)
- **1 tsp** white pepper (or black)
- **1 tsp** baking powder *(helps bounce; optional but common in “springy” texture)*
- **20 g** cornstarch or potato starch (about 2 Tbsp)
- **80–120 g** crushed ice (cold helps emulsify and create springy texture)

> Texture note: nem nướng is about a **smooth, sticky, springy** paste. Keep it cold.

---

## B) “Crunch” component (choose one)
This is what makes Nha Trang rolls special and photogenic.

### Option 1 — Fried egg roll wrapper strips (classic crunch)
- 6–8 egg roll wrappers
- Oil for frying

### Option 2 — Crispy rice paper sheets (lighter crunch)
- 6–8 rice paper sheets
- Oil for frying (shallow)

---

## C) Rice paper + noodles + herbs (kit base)
- Rice paper sheets (bánh tráng), **16–24** depending on size
- Rice vermicelli (bún), **250 g cooked** (or ~100 g dry)
- Lettuce leaves
- Herbs: mint + cilantro (and Vietnamese perilla if available)
- Cucumber, cut into batons
- Pickled carrot/daikon (optional but makes it look “complete”)

---

## D) Signature orange dipping sauce (pork-peanut style)
**EU-friendly “orange” sauce that still feels authentic:**
- **2 Tbsp** annatto oil *(or 1 tsp annatto powder + 2 Tbsp oil)*
- **1 Tbsp** garlic, minced
- **2 Tbsp** tomato paste
- **300 ml** water or light pork/chicken stock
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1–2 Tbsp** sugar (to taste)
- **60 g** roasted peanuts (finely ground)
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** hoisin (rounds flavor; not required)
- Optional: chili oil or chili flakes (for spicy version)

> This sauce should be **thick enough to cling** to rolls.

---

## Prep (20–30 minutes)

### 1) Soak skewers
- Soak bamboo skewers in water for **20 minutes** to prevent burning.

### 2) Mix pork paste (springy texture)
1. Put ground pork in a chilled bowl.
2. Add garlic, sugar, fish sauce, pepper, baking powder (optional), and starch.
3. Mix (hand or mixer) until the mixture becomes **sticky**.
4. Add crushed ice gradually while mixing until paste is smooth and tacky.

**Chill:** Refrigerate **20–30 minutes** (firms up and improves texture).

---

## Make the Crunch (10 minutes)

### Option 1 — Fried egg roll wrapper strips
1. Slice wrappers into thin strips.
2. Fry at **175–180°C** until golden and crisp.
3. Drain well.

### Option 2 — Crispy rice paper
1. Heat a thin layer of oil.
2. Fry rice paper briefly until puffed and crisp.
3. Drain and break into shards.

---

## Make the Orange Sauce (10–12 minutes)
1. Heat annatto oil in a small saucepan over medium.
2. Add garlic, cook 15–20 seconds (don’t burn).
3. Stir in tomato paste; cook 30 seconds.
4. Add water/stock, fish sauce, and sugar; simmer 3–4 minutes.
5. Whisk in ground peanuts.
6. Simmer until thick and glossy (add water if too thick; it thickens as it cools).

---

## Grill the Nem Nướng (12–15 minutes)

### Shape
- Wet hands lightly.
- Form pork paste into small logs and wrap onto skewers (or form small patties).

### Cook
1. Grill over **medium-high** heat.
2. Turn frequently until browned with light char and cooked through.
3. Target internal temp: **71–74°C**.

> If no grill: broil on a rack and rotate for even browning.

## Assemble RiceMap24 Kits (Best for Delivery)

### Meal Kit (recommended)
- **8 skewers** (or 6 larger skewers) total per kit depending on size
- Rice paper stack
- Vermicelli nest
- Herb + lettuce pack (dry)
- Cucumber + pickles
- Crunch pack (separate, keep dry)
- Orange sauce cup (sealed)

### Snack Kit
- Half size: 4 skewers + smaller herb/sauce packs

---

## How Customers Eat It (Instruction Card Text)
1. Dip rice paper in warm water for 2–3 seconds.
2. Add lettuce + herbs + noodles + cucumber + nem nướng.
3. Add crunchy strips.
4. Roll tightly and dip in orange sauce.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Nem nướng:** springy bite, juicy, lightly charred.
- **Sauce:** thick, orange, peanutty, not watery.
- **Kit:** herbs dry and crisp; crunch stays crunchy (separate packing).

---

## Portioning Guide (Per meal kit)
- Nem nướng: **180–250 g cooked** (depends on skewer size)
- Noodles: **60–80 g cooked**
- Sauce: **60–80 ml**
- Herbs + veg: generous (this sells the photo)

---

## Scaling (10 meal kits)
- Pork paste: **~2.5×** batch (about 2.25 kg pork)
- Sauce: **2.5×** batch
- Prep crunch in a large batch and keep airtight

**Ops tip:** Pre-shape skewers and chill; grill in batches close to pickup time.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Keep **all components separated**:
  - Container A: grilled skewers
  - Container B: herbs/lettuce (dry + paper towel)
  - Container C: noodles + cucumber/pickles
  - Bag D: rice paper stack
  - Cup E: orange sauce
  - Bag F: crunch (dry)

Label: “Build-your-own rolls. Add crunch last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Skewers: refrigerate up to 2 days; reheat in oven/air fryer for best texture.
- Sauce: 3–5 days refrigerated.
- Herbs: best day-of.

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Gluten possible (some sauces; egg roll wrappers)
