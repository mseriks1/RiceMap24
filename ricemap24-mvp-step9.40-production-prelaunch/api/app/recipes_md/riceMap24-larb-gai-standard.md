# Larb Gai (Thai Chicken Larb Salad) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A bright, savory Thai minced chicken salad with lime, fish sauce, toasted rice powder, and fresh herbs.  
This is a **fast, high-margin** dish that scales well and works as a “light bowl” (with rice) or as a salad box.

**Source recipe:** Hot Thai Kitchen — “Larb Gai (Thai Chicken Salad)”  
https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/larb-gai/

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Larb is all about balance (salty–sour–spicy–toasty) and the **exact fish sauce + lime + sugar ratio**.  
> Use the linked Hot Thai Kitchen recipe for **exact measurements**, then follow the workflow below for consistent results.

### Core
- Ground chicken (or finely minced chicken), amount per source
- Shallots, thinly sliced (per source)
- Thai chilies (or other hot chilies), to taste (per source)
- Fresh herbs: cilantro + mint (per source)
- Optional: scallions, per source

### Toasted rice powder (khao khua)
- Uncooked jasmine rice (small amount per source)

### Dressing / seasoning (per source)
- Fish sauce
- Lime juice (fresh)
- Sugar (usually small amount)
- Optional: chili flakes / roasted chili powder (if source uses)
- Optional: stock/water (small amount) to keep larb juicy (per source)

### To serve (RiceMap24-friendly)
- Steamed jasmine rice (recommended as separate container)
- Lettuce leaves or cabbage (for wrapping)
- Cucumber slices (fresh side)

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Toast rice and grind into powder  
2) Prep shallots + herbs + chilies  
3) Cook chicken (keep it juicy)  
4) Season + toss with herbs at the end  
5) Portion + pack with rice and fresh sides

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Detailed Workflow)

## 1) Make toasted rice powder (khao khua) — 5 minutes
1. Add uncooked jasmine rice to a **dry pan** over medium heat.
2. Toast, stirring constantly, until rice turns **golden brown** and smells nutty.
3. Cool 2 minutes.
4. Grind into a coarse powder (mortar & pestle or spice grinder).
5. Set aside. *(This is a signature larb flavor—don’t skip.)*

## 2) Prep aromatics + herbs — 5 minutes
1. Slice shallots thin.
2. Chop cilantro + mint (rough chop; keep them fresh and green).
3. Prepare chilies (thin slices or minced).

## 3) Cook the chicken — 6–8 minutes
> Larb is not “browned meat.” It’s gently cooked minced chicken that stays moist.

1. Add ground chicken to a skillet over **medium heat**.
2. Add a small splash of water/stock if the source uses it (helps keep texture tender).
3. Break up chicken and stir until it’s **just cooked through** (no pink).
4. Remove from heat **as soon as** it is cooked—overcooking makes it dry.

## 4) Season while warm — 3 minutes
1. While chicken is warm (not scorching hot), add dressing ingredients per source:
   - fish sauce
   - lime juice
   - sugar
   - chilies
2. Add toasted rice powder gradually.
3. Taste and adjust:
   - **Too salty:** more lime or a tiny splash of water
   - **Too sour:** a pinch more sugar
   - **Not “toasty” enough:** more rice powder
   - **Need more heat:** add chili

## 5) Add herbs last (off heat) — 1 minute
1. Add shallots + herbs (cilantro + mint) and toss quickly.
2. Serve immediately.

> Herbs go in last so they stay fragrant and bright.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Moisture:** Larb should be juicy, not dry.
- **Balance:** Salt (fish sauce) + sour (lime) + slight sweet + toast (rice powder).
- **Herbs:** Bright green and aromatic (not wilted).

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Practical batch tips
- Toast rice once in a larger batch and store powder airtight (1–2 weeks).
- Cook chicken in **2 batches** to keep it moist and evenly cooked.
- Toss herbs **per batch** right before packing for best aroma.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality:**
- Container A: larb (cool it slightly before sealing to protect herbs)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Container C: lettuce/cabbage + cucumber side (fresh)
- Optional: extra lime wedge

Label: “Eat as a bowl with rice, or wrap in lettuce.”

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours** (herbs fade over time).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Larb is best fresh; refrigeration dulls herbs.
- If storing: keep larb up to **1 day** refrigerated; add extra fresh herbs at serving.
- Do not reheat aggressively (it’s intended as a warm/room-temp salad).
