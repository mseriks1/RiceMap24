## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Filling:** Mix filling ingredients per source until sticky and cohesive.
2. **Sauce:** Mix chili oil sauce per source. Portion into serving bowls (or into a batch container).
3. **Wrapper station:** Set out wrappers + small bowl of water for sealing.
4. **Folding plan:** Keep wrappers covered with a damp towel to prevent drying.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Wrap wontons
1. Place 1 wrapper on your hand/board.
2. Add a small spoon of filling (don’t overfill).
3. Moisten edges lightly with water and fold/seal (follow source fold style).
4. Place finished wontons on a tray; cover lightly to prevent drying.

## 2) Boil wontons
1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil.
2. Add wontons in batches (avoid crowding).
3. Boil until wontons float and the filling is cooked through (timing per source).
4. Remove with a skimmer and drain briefly.

## 3) Sauce + serve
1. Spoon hot wontons into bowls with the pre-mixed chili oil sauce.
2. Toss gently to coat.
3. Garnish with scallions/sesame if desired.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Wrappers:** Tender, not broken.
- **Filling:** Juicy, fully cooked.
- **Sauce:** Spicy-sour-salty balance; glossy and aromatic.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is ~4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** by **2.5×** for filling and sauce.

### Batch notes
- Wrap wontons ahead and refrigerate covered for same-day service.
- Boil to order for best wrapper texture.
- Portion sauce into cups for consistent cost and spice level.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended

## Storage & Reheat
- **Uncooked wontons:** Freeze on tray, then bag; cook from frozen (add time).
- **Cooked wontons:** Best fresh; wrappers soften quickly.
- **Sauce:** Keeps 3–5 days refrigerated (stir before serving).
