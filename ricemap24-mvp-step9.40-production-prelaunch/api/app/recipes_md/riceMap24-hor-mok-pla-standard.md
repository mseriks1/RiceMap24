# Hor Mok Pla (Thai Steamed Fish Curry Custard) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Thai classic that **looks premium**: silky curry custard steamed in banana-leaf cups, topped with coconut cream, sliced chili, and kaffir lime leaf ribbons.  
Not a typical “street takeaway” item in many European cities—great as a standout **signature special** for home kitchens.

**Source recipe (reference):** Asian Inspirations — “Steamed Thai Fish Curry Custard (Hor Mok)”  
https://asianinspirations.com.au/recipes/steamed-thai-fish-curry-custard-hor-mok/

## Equipment
- Steamer (bamboo/metal) with lid
- Blender/food processor
- Knife + cutting board
- Mixing bowl
- Toothpicks (for banana leaf cups)
- Optional: ramekins if you don’t want banana leaves

---

## Ingredients (Base recipe)
### Main custard
- 400 g white fish, finely chopped
- 2 Tbsp red curry paste
- 1 egg
- 1/2 cup coconut milk (chilled)
- 1 tsp palm sugar
- 1 Tbsp fish sauce
- 1/2 cup Thai basil leaves
- 6 kaffir lime leaves, finely sliced
- 1 red Thai chili, finely sliced

### Topping
- 1/4 cup coconut cream
- 1 Tbsp rice flour

### Wrapping
- Banana leaves + toothpicks

---

## Protein Notes (Home-kitchen friendly)
The base recipe is fish. If you want variety without changing the look:
- **Seafood mix:** replace up to half the fish with chopped shrimp or scallops (keep total 400 g).
- **Chicken version:** replace fish with very finely chopped chicken thigh (texture changes—steam a test batch first).
- **Vegetarian:** use finely chopped mushrooms + firm tofu; reduce fish sauce and swap to soy + salt.

Keep the total “protein” weight the same (**400 g**) for consistency.

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Make banana-leaf cups (or use ramekins)
1. Cut banana leaves into ~20 cm circles (trace a plate).
2. Stack **two circles** per cup, shiny sides facing out, veins perpendicular.
3. Make 4 folds to form a cup shape and secure each fold with a toothpick.

**Shortcut:** Use small ramekins—still photogenic if you top nicely.

### 2) Slice garnishes
- Thinly slice chili.
- Finely slice kaffir lime leaves.
- Pick Thai basil leaves.

### 3) Preheat steamer
- Get the steamer water **boiling** before adding cups.

---

## Method (Precise, Repeatable)

### Step 1 — Blend half the fish into a paste
1. Put **half the fish** into blender with:
   - red curry paste
   - egg
   - palm sugar
   - fish sauce
2. Blend until smooth and paste-like.

### Step 2 — Add coconut milk + lime leaves
1. Add chilled coconut milk and sliced kaffir lime leaves.
2. Blend briefly to combine.

### Step 3 — Fill cups
1. Place Thai basil leaves at the bottom of each banana-leaf cup.
2. Divide the **remaining chopped fish** among cups (this gives nice texture).
3. Pour the blended curry custard over fish.

### Step 4 — Coconut cream topping (no lumps)
1. Mix coconut cream + rice flour until smooth.
2. Spoon a small amount on top of each cup.
3. Add a few chili slices on top for color.

### Step 5 — Steam
1. Place cups in steamer **only when water is boiling**.
2. Steam **15–20 minutes** until custard is set.
   - It should jiggle slightly but not look liquid.

### Serve
- Serve hot with steamed jasmine rice.

---

## Doneness Check
- Center should be set (no wet liquid).
- A skewer inserted into the center should come out mostly clean.

## Scaling (10 servings guideline)
The source recipe serves 1–2. For consistent production, decide your “cup size” and scale accordingly.

A practical home-kitchen batch for **10 servings**:
- Multiply the recipe by **6×** (gives ~2.4 kg fish-based custard mix before steaming).
- Portion into 10–12 cups depending on cup volume.

**Ops tip:** Blend in batches if your blender is small.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
Hor mok is delicate and can leak if tipped.

- Use a **flat, stable container** that fits the banana-leaf cup snugly.
- Keep rice **separate**.
- Add extra chili and lime leaf on top right before packing for best looks.

**Holding time:** Best within 1–2 hours.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to 1 day (best same day).
- Reheat by steaming gently or microwave at low power (avoid drying).

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish, fish sauce)
- Coconut
- Egg
- Gluten possible (some curry pastes contain additives—check brand)
