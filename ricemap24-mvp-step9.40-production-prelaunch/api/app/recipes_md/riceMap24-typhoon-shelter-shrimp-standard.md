# Hong Kong Typhoon Shelter Shrimp (避风塘炒虾) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A bold Hong Kong specialty: **crispy fried shrimp** tossed with a **garlic-ginger-chili breadcrumb “crunch”** (often with fermented black beans).  
This is not typical “Chinese restaurant saucy shrimp”—it’s dry, aromatic, and extremely addictive. Perfect as a premium add-on, sharing box, or rice-bowl topper.

**Source recipe (reference):** The Woks of Life — “Typhoon Shelter Shrimp”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/typhoon-shelter-shrimp-recipe/

## Ingredients (Serves 4)

## A) Shrimp
- **450–500 g** large shrimp/prawns (shell-on preferred for best flavor; peeled is fine)
- **1 Tbsp** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** white pepper (or black)
- **60 g** cornstarch (for coating; adjust as needed)
- Neutral oil for frying (canola/peanut), **2–3 inches depth**

## B) Typhoon “Crunch” (breadcrumbs + aromatics)
- **80–100 g** panko breadcrumbs (about 1.5–2 cups)
- **30 g** garlic, minced (about 8–10 cloves)
- **15 g** ginger, minced (about 1 Tbsp)
- **2–3** red chilies, finely chopped (adjust heat)
- **2 Tbsp** fermented black beans (optional but very authentic), rinsed and chopped
- **2** scallions, thinly sliced (finish)
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil (for toasting panko)

## C) Optional finish (highly recommended)
- Lime wedges (brightens and looks great)
- Extra chopped scallions
- Optional: a pinch of sugar (balances bitterness if garlic runs strong)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Prep shrimp
- If shell-on: devein (leave shell for crunch and flavor).
- If peeled: pat very dry (dry shrimp fries crisp).
- Toss shrimp with Shaoxing wine, salt, and pepper. Rest 5–10 minutes.

### 2) Prep aromatics
- Mince garlic and ginger.
- Chop chilies.
- Rinse and chop fermented black beans (if using).
- Slice scallions for finish.

### 3) Set up frying + draining
- Heat oil in wok/deep skillet to **180°C / 350°F**.
- Prepare rack/paper towels for draining.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable Workflow)

## 1) Fry shrimp (6–10 minutes total)
1. Toss shrimp in cornstarch until lightly and evenly coated (shake off excess).
2. Fry in **small batches** (do not crowd; oil temp must stay stable):
   - Shell-on shrimp: **2–3 minutes** until crisp and golden.
   - Peeled shrimp: **60–90 seconds** until just golden and cooked.
3. Drain on rack/paper towels.
4. Reserve **2 Tbsp of shrimp frying oil** in a clean skillet (optional but adds flavor to the crunch).

> Crisp rule: if oil temp drops, shrimp turns oily. Wait for temp to recover between batches.

---

## 2) Make the breadcrumb “crunch” (5–8 minutes)
1. In a clean skillet over **medium** heat, add **2 Tbsp neutral oil**  
   - Optional: substitute **2 Tbsp shrimp frying oil** for extra aroma.
2. Add minced garlic and ginger. Stir constantly until fragrant and just turning pale-golden.
3. Add chilies and optional black beans; stir 15–30 seconds.
4. Add panko breadcrumbs.
5. Toast, stirring constantly, until panko turns **evenly golden** and dry-crisp (about **3–5 minutes**).

> Do not burn the garlic. If it browns too fast, lower heat immediately.

---

## 3) Toss and finish (30 seconds)
1. Add fried shrimp to the skillet with the crunchy topping.
2. Toss quickly to coat.
3. Turn off heat.
4. Add scallions and toss once.
5. Serve immediately.

## Quality Checks
- **Shrimp:** crisp outside, not greasy.
- **Crunch:** golden, fragrant, not burnt.
- **Balance:** garlicky, savory, chili warmth; optional black beans add depth.

- Extra crunch add-on: **30–40 g** (small cup)

---

## Scaling for Production (10 orders)
- Shrimp: **~1.25–1.5 kg**
- Cornstarch: **~180 g**
- Panko: **~250 g**
- Garlic: **~75 g** (lots—this is the identity)
- Ginger: **~35 g**
- Chili: to taste
- Oil: plan enough for consistent frying (keep oil fresh)

**Ops tip:** Fry shrimp in batches and keep warm on a rack. Make crunch in two batches to avoid burning.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended (Critical)
Crispness dies in steam—pack like fried chicken:

- Use a **vented** container or crack lid 2–3 minutes before sealing.
- Keep a lime wedge in a small bag/cup.
- For rice bowl: keep rice separate; put shrimp+crunch on top (not under sauce).

Label: “Best eaten right away for maximum crunch.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- Re-crisp in air fryer **190°C** for **3–5 minutes** (works surprisingly well).

---

## Allergens
- Shellfish (shrimp)
- Gluten (panko)
- Soy (if black beans are salted/fermented; check label)
