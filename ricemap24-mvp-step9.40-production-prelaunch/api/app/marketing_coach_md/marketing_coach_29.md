# Marketing Coach – Month 29  
## Focus: Customer list as a “repeat engine” (tags + small segments + WhatsApp/call – tidy and personal)

This month you do something that gives results for the rest of the year: you turn your **customer list** into a simple system that creates repeat orders. No spam. No big campaigns. Just a tidy routine where you:

- collect customers (name + phone)  
- tag them smartly (so you can filter in 2 seconds)  
- follow up small groups in a friendly way  
- use WhatsApp/calls/copy buttons to save time  
- get more people to order again with the same CTA every time  

This is one of the cheapest growth moves you can make, because you build on customers you already have. Customers also like it when it feels personal and organized.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What this means in practice (simple)
The customer-list engine is about three things:

1) **You add customers consistently** (after each order)  
2) **You tag them** (so you can find the right group in 2 seconds)  
3) **You send a short, friendly “new round” message** to small groups (5–15 people)

You make it easy to order again, without pressure.

⸻

## Why this works (and why customers like it)
People order again when they:
- are recognized (“hi!” / favorite)  
- get a simple nudge (“new round this week”)  
- don’t have to ask “what do you have?” every time  
- get the same link, same CTA, same safety  

You build a habit. And habit beats everything.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Make your customer list “complete enough” (add after every order)  
2) Create 8–12 simple tags (so you can segment fast)  
3) Create 4 mini segments (small groups you can message)  
4) Run a weekly follow-up rhythm (one short batch per week)

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You add at least **25 customers** to your list (or whatever you have — the point is consistency)  
2) You create **8–12 tags** and use them actively  
3) You create **4 segments** and do follow-ups weekly  
4) You keep your routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week  
5) You track: which segments create repeat orders?

⸻

## Fixed routine (baseline this month)
- 2 photo posts per week (FB + IG)  
- 2 videos per week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)  
- Customer-list follow-up: 1 batch per week (10–20 minutes)

⸻

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Customer list engine (60%)**: customers added + tags + segments + follow-up  
- **Content routine (40%)**: weekly posts/videos completed

# Part A: Add customers consistently (the “after every order” rule)

## The rule (non-negotiable if you want repeat)
After each order, you add/update:
- name  
- phone  
- 1–2 notes (favorite, spice level, allergies)  
- tag(s)

If you do this for 30 days, you build an asset that keeps paying you back.

⸻

# Part B: Tags (8–12 tags that cover 80% of your customers)

## Tag categories (simple and useful)
Choose tags you can actually use. Here are examples:

### Preference tags
- spicy  
- mild  
- “extra sauce”  
- “no pork” / “halal” (if relevant)  
- vegetarian / vegan (if relevant)

### Behavior tags
- repeat customer  
- new customer  
- “orders for family”  
- “orders for work/lunch”  

### Dish tags
- signature lover  
- noodle lover / rice lover (if relevant)  
- “cold-ready buyer”  
- “finish-at-home buyer”

**Rule:** tags must be fast to apply and fast to filter.

⸻

# Part C: Mini segments (4 groups you can message)
You build 4 simple segments. The point is that you can message a small group, not everyone.

Examples:
1) **Repeat customers** (warm)  
2) **New customers (last 30 days)**  
3) **Lunch/office interest**  
4) **Signature lovers**  

Each segment should be 5–15 people when you message it.

⸻

# Part D: The weekly “new round” message (copy/paste)
This is the message you send once per week to one or two segments.

**Template (short):**
> Hi! 😊 New cooking round this week.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]  
> If you want the same as last time, just write “same as last time” + preferred time.

**Template (more personal):**
> Hi [name]! 😊 I’m cooking again this week.  
> Your favorite [dish] is available.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

**Rule:** keep it warm, short, and never spam large lists.

⸻

# WhatsApp / call / copy (time saver)
Use the tool that makes it easiest for you:
- WhatsApp broadcast (small groups)  
- direct messages  
- quick calls to warm leads (only if you like it)  
- copy/paste messages from templates

The point is speed and consistency.

⸻

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Set up tags + start adding consistently
### Mini lesson (tags create speed)
If you tag right, you can market in 10 minutes instead of 2 hours.

## Week 2 — Create 4 segments + send your first “new round” message

Small segments feel friendly. Large blasts feel like spam.

### Tasks
1) Create your 4 segments (based on tags)  
2) Send one message to one segment (5–15 people)  
3) Keep the routine: 2 photos + 2 videos

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now repeat becomes a weekly habit.”
- “Small segments increase response.”

## Week 3 — Improve: make it more personal (favorites + “same as last time”)
### Mini lesson (personal beats promotional)
A small personal note increases conversion.

### Tasks
1) Pick 5 customers and use a personal variant:
- “Your favorite is back”
- “Same as last time?”
2) Send one “new round” message to one segment  
3) Track responses/orders

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Personal follow-up increases repeat.”
- “You’re building loyalty, not just sales.”

## Week 4 — Stabilize + track what segments work best
### Mini lesson (measure lightly, learn a lot)

- which message style worked best?
3) Decide your monthly rhythm:
- which 1–2 segments will you contact weekly?
