# Marketing Coach – Month 37  
## Focus: The spillover effect (when one marketing action creates more marketing)

This month we focus on something that can change your growth curve: **the spillover effect**. Spillover effect means that one marketing action leads to more marketing — without you having to “do more.” It’s when:
- one post gets shared
- one story creates DMs
- one local placement creates conversations
- one happy customer triggers new customers

In other words: your marketing starts to **multiply itself**.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What spillover effect means (simple)
Spillover effect happens when your marketing creates a second step automatically:

1) You post or place something  
2) People react (comment/share/ask)  
3) Their reaction becomes new visibility  
4) New people see you  
5) More people order

This is the difference between marketing that “just exists” and marketing that spreads.

⸻

## Why this matters now
You already have:
- routines (2 photo posts + 2 videos)
- systems (menu, drop, windows, repeat, VIP)
- offline touchpoints
- customers who know you

Now you’re in the phase where you can create more growth by making your marketing **more shareable and more talkable**, not by posting more.

Spillover effect is one of the most effective ways to grow locally without ads.

⸻

1) Build 3 “spillover triggers” (things that invite sharing/talking)  
2) Publish content designed for comments and shares (not only views)

4) Track what creates second-order reach (shares, DMs, mentions)

# Part A: The 3 spillover triggers (choose and define)
Pick 3 triggers that fit your style. Examples:

### Trigger 1: Simple question (comments)
- “Spicy or mild?”
- “Which dish should I cook next?”
- “Lunch or dinner?”

### Trigger 2: Tag/share invitation (shares)
- “Tag a friend who would love this”
- “Share this with someone local”
(Keep it gentle and not spammy.)

### Trigger 3: Local pride / neighborhood
- “Local kitchen in [area]”
- “This week’s pickup in [area]”
People share local pride content more than generic food posts.

**Rule:** choose triggers you can repeat monthly.

⸻

# Part C: Offline “talk triggers” (2 actions this month)
