# Marketing Coach – Month 38  
## Focus: Brand lift (increase perceived value without changing the food)

This month you focus on **brand lift**. Brand lift means that the customer experiences you as:
- more professional  
- more trustworthy  
- more “worth it”  
- more consistent  

…without you necessarily changing the food itself. You raise perceived value through:
- cleaner presentation
- more consistent visuals
- clearer language
- more “organized” feeling

This often leads to:
- higher conversion (more people dare to order)
- higher willingness to pay (less price sensitivity)
- more repeat customers (it feels like a real brand)

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What brand lift means in practice (simple)
Brand lift is about three areas:

1) **First impression** (profile + hero + “start here” clarity)  
2) **Consistency** (photos, copy, tone, packaging)  
3) **Professional small details** (predictability, clean execution, good routines)

It is not about looking “corporate.” It is about looking safe and worth supporting.

⸻

## Why this focus now
When you grow, you meet customers who:
- are more selective
- compare you with restaurants
- want safety and professionalism

Brand lift makes you more competitive without needing ads. It also makes your content work better, because the same photo and the same post convert better when the profile and menu feel “clean.”

⸻

1) Upgrade your first impression (profile, hero, pinned/overview)  
2) Create visual consistency guidelines (same style)  
3) Upgrade your message (one positioning line)  
4) Add one offline brand touch (poster quality + placement)

# Part A: Upgrade your first impression (profile + hero + pinned)
Checklist:
- clear kitchen name + area
- strong hero image (clean, appetizing)
- short bio (2–3 lines)
- one clear link (RiceMap24)
- pinned “start here” post with CTA

**Rule:** new customers decide in seconds.

⸻

# Part B: Visual consistency (simple rules)
Choose 3–5 “rules” for your photos:
- same angle type (45° or top-down)
- clean background
- same light (near window / daytime)
- minimal clutter
- consistent plate/packaging look

**Rule:** consistency looks premium.

⸻

# Part C: One positioning line (repeat everywhere)
Examples:
- “Small-batch, real ingredients, cooked fresh locally.”
- “Authentic [cuisine] made in small batches.”
- “Fresh, homemade food — safe and real.”

Choose one and use it in:
- bio
- pinned post
- posters
- menu overview posts

⸻

# Part D: Offline brand touch (poster quality + placement)
A clean poster in a good place creates brand lift because it feels established.

Do:
- print fresh posters (not faded)
- place them in tidy, relevant locations
- take a photo for your records (optional)

⸻
