# Marketing Coach – Month 8
## Focus: Increase order value with add-ons and bundles (more dishes per order)

This month we focus on **increasing order value** — meaning the customer buys **a little more per order**, without it reducing the number of customers or the experience. This is one of the most profitable growth methods because you can often earn more **without** doing a corresponding amount of extra marketing work. You already have visibility and are starting to get repeat. Now you build further by making the order feel “more complete” and more tempting.

### Why order value is a smart growth lever
When you increase order value, two good things happen:
1) You earn more per customer (more revenue per order)  
2) You can often keep costs relatively stable (same delivery/pickup, same communication)

That means you can increase profit without having to increase pace as much as when you chase new customers.

### What “add-ons” and “bundles” mean in practice
**Add-ons** = small extras the customer can add (often sides/add-ons)  
Examples: extra rice, extra sauce, drink, dessert, spring rolls, salad, pickles.

**Bundles** = ready-made combinations that make choosing easy  
Examples:
- “Meal set”: main dish + side  
- “2 mains + 2 sides” (sharing for 2–3 people)  
- “Family pack” (larger order, fewer choices)

The key is that this should not feel “pushy.” It should feel like help:
> “Want to make the meal complete? Here’s an easy combo.”

### Why this focus comes now
When you’re new, the most important thing is to get started and build credibility and trust. When you have a basic rhythm and some customers, the next step is to make each order a little more profitable — and at the same time make ordering easier for the customer. Bundles do exactly that.

## This month’s strategy (3 simple steps)
1) Choose **2–3 add-ons** that fit your menu and are easy to deliver  
2) Create **2 bundles** (one small and one a bit bigger)  
3) Market bundles as **“an easier choice”** (not as “buy more!”)

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Choose add-ons that actually fit (and are profitable)
### Mini lesson: add-ons should be easy to deliver
An add-on should:
- be easy to produce
- fit multiple dishes
- not create chaos in the kitchen
- ideally give good margin

### Tasks
1) Choose 2–3 add-ons you want to standardize  
2) Decide how they are shown together with the dishes (same place, same language)  
3) Update dish text so the add-on feels natural:
   - “Add-on: extra rice / extra sauce / [side]”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. Simple add-ons often increase order value without extra stress.”
- “Standardization makes delivery easier and more professional.”

### This week’s posting
- Photo post: “Make the meal complete” (dish + add-on) + CTA  
- Video: plating/packing with the add-on visible (short)

---

## Week 2 — Create 2 bundles: small and big
### Mini lesson: bundles sell because they remove choices
Many customers like to be helped. Bundles make ordering easier:
- they avoid choosing too much
- they feel they get a “complete solution”
- you can steer orders toward what is efficient for you

### Tasks
1) Create “Bundle 1” (simple): 1 main dish + 1 add-on  
2) Create “Bundle 2” (for sharing): 2 main dishes + 2 sides/add-ons  
3) Give them a simple naming style:
   - “Meal Set”
   - “For 2”
   - “Family/Sharing Pack” (if it fits)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. Bundles help customers buy more easily — and often more.”
- “You build income without chasing more customers.”

### This week’s posting
- Photo post: “Bundle 1” (simple and clear) + CTA  
- Photo post (bonus): “Bundle 2” for 2 people + CTA  
- Video: “what you get” (bundle shown fully packed)

---

## Week 3 — Market bundles as a “smart choice” (not pushy)
### Mini lesson: language that feels helpful
People don’t like being sold to, but they like being helped.
That’s why we use language like:
- “easy choice”
- “complete meal”
- “perfect for sharing”
- “no need to think”

### Tasks
1) Create 2–3 caption templates you can reuse:
   - “If you want an easy choice…”
   - “Perfect when you want a complete meal…”
2) Post 1–2 times with a bundle angle
3) If you have repeat customers: send one gentle message about bundles:
   > “This week I also have an easy meal set / for 2 option. Menu: [link]”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. Helpful language builds credibility and trust.”
- “You increase order value without it feeling like nagging.”

### This week’s posting
- Video: close-up + packing of bundle (proof)  
- Photo post: “for 2” angle (sharing)

## Week 4 — Stabilize: make bundles a fixed habit
### Mini lesson: repeat what works
When something produces more income, it should be repeated regularly. Don’t reinvent the wheel every week.

### Tasks
1) Choose 1 “fixed bundle” you can always offer  
2) Update menu/text so the bundle is always easy to understand  
3) Quick look at orders:  
   - Which add-ons are chosen most?  
   - Which bundle works best?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a system that increases income without increasing stress.”
- “This can become a standard part of your operation.”

### This week’s posting
- Photo post: “This week’s bundle” + CTA  
- Video: “fresh today + bundle ready” (short)

### Offline (execution)
- Put up the poster (1–2 locations) where allowed (or ask for permission)
