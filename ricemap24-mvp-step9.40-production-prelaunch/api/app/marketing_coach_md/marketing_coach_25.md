# Marketing Coach – Month 25  
## Focus: Office lunch & “small catering” that creates larger orders, repeat customers, and a predictable week

This month you build a simple, repeatable office lunch model: small companies and teams that order for several people at once. This is one of the most profitable “shortcuts” for a home kitchen, because you can get:  
- larger orders (5–25 portions in one order)  
- more predictability (the same company can order weekly)  
- better production flow (batch cooking + packing in a system)  
- strong word of mouth (co-workers see, ask, and share)

You don’t need to “become a catering company.” You are simply creating an office-lunch version of what you already do: same food, same style — just packaged and presented as a solution for teams.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post, always the same:  
“Menu and ordering: [link]”

⸻

## What office lunch means in practice (simple)

Office lunch is about three things:  
1. Clear package: “Lunch for 6 / 10 / 15 people”  
2. Simple order window: “Order by X the day before” (or “24 hours before”)  
3. Delivery/pickup with structure: fixed time, clear labeling, easy to share

You make it easy to say yes, because the company doesn’t have to think.

⸻

## Why this works (and why companies buy)

Companies buy office lunch because it solves small, important needs:  
- “We want something nice at work without managing it”  
- “We have a meeting / workshop / training”  
- “We want to celebrate a colleague / a milestone”  
- “We want some ‘real food’ in a busy week”

When you hit this, you become more than “food”: you become a regular solution.

# Part A: Create your office lunch packages (2–3)

## Package structure (keep it very simple)
Create packages based on number of people:

- **Package 1: Lunch for 6**  
- **Package 2: Lunch for 10**  
- **Package 3: Lunch for 15** (optional if you have capacity)

Each package should state:
- what dishes are included (simple)
- portion logic (how many boxes/trays)
- allergens note (short + “see menu text”)
- pickup/delivery window
- order cutoff

**Rule:** fewer choices = more sales.

⸻

# Part B: Define order window + pickup/delivery window

## Example (simple and professional)
- **Order cutoff:** 24 hours before (or 10:00 the day before)  
- **Delivery/pickup window:** 11:00–13:00 (lunch window)

You can choose your own times, but they must be clear and repeatable.

⸻

# Part C: Outreach (the simple way that actually works)
Office growth is not “posting more.” It is “touching the right places.”

### 3 simple outreach channels
1) **Coworking / office hubs** (boards + managers)  
2) **Local businesses** (DM/email + reception desks)  
3) **Local business groups** (Facebook groups, LinkedIn, community boards)

⸻

# Templates (copy/paste)

## 1) Office message template (DM/email)
> Hi! I run a small home kitchen in [area] and offer simple office lunch orders.  
> Lunch for 6 / 10 / 15 people — easy pickup/delivery in the lunch window.  
> Menu and ordering: [link]  
> Order cutoff: [time]. Pickup/delivery: [time window].  
> Let me know if you want a weekly lunch solution.

**Office Lunch in [area]**  
Lunch for teams (6 / 10 / 15)  
Order via QR: [link]  
Order cutoff: [time] • Pickup/delivery: [time window]
