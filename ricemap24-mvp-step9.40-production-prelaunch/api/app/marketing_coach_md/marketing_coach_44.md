# Marketing Coach – Month 44  
## Focus: Intensive offline (bold local visibility across many touchpoints — for a short window)

This month you do a bold move: **intensive offline marketing**. Instead of 1–2 posters, you create a short period where you become noticeably visible locally by placing posters in many places — in a structured, organized way.

The goal is not to “spam the neighborhood.” The goal is to create local repetition fast, so people start thinking:
> “I keep seeing this kitchen.”

This is the offline version of a campaign week.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per poster/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What intensive offline is (simple)
Intensive offline means:
- you plan a list of 10–25 locations  
- you execute in waves (so it’s manageable)  
- you track which clusters produce customers  
- you refresh and maintain lightly

It is not random. It is a structured visibility push.

⸻

## Why this works (repetition and credibility)
It works because:
- repetition is created quickly
- offline visibility feels “real”
- people see you while living their normal life
- the QR makes ordering easy

One strong offline month can create a long tail of new customers.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Create a location list (clusters) of 10–25 places  
2) Print enough posters and keep the message extremely simple  
3) Execute placements in 3 waves  
4) Track customer sources and learn which clusters worked

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You place posters in at least **10 locations** (goal: 15–25)  
2) You use clusters (not random)  
3) You take quick notes (location + date)  
4) You do one supporting social post (“we’re local”)  
5) You track how new customers found you  
6) You keep routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week

⸻

## Fixed routine (baseline this month)
- 2 photo posts per week (FB + IG)  
- 2 videos per week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)  
- Offline: placements every week (this is the focus month)

⸻

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Placement execution (70%)**: posters placed in 10–25 locations + notes  
- **Tracking & support (30%)**: one SoMe support post + customer source tracking  

⸻

# Part A: Location list (clusters) — plan 10–25
Do not scatter randomly. Create clusters, for example:
- one neighborhood cluster  
- one shopping area cluster  
- one gym cluster  
- one campus/community cluster  
- one coworking/office cluster  

Write the list:
- location name
- type
- permission needed? (yes/no)

⸻

# Part B: Poster message (extremely simple)
Your poster must communicate in 2 seconds:
- local kitchen
- QR to order

Poster text example:
Local home kitchen in [area] 😊  
Menu and ordering via QR

Optional line:
Pickup: [time window]

**Rule:** one sentence + QR.

⸻

# Part C: The permission line (copy/paste)
> Hi! I run a small local home kitchen in [area].  
> Could I put up a small QR menu poster here for a while? Thanks 😊

⸻

## Week 3 — Wave 2 placements (5–8 more) + refresh
### Mini lesson (repetition increases impact)
More touchpoints = more “I keep seeing them.”

### Tasks

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you are building strong local repetition.”

## Week 4 — Final wave + tracking habit

Just ask customers.

### Tasks

- “You can find us locally around [area]” + CTA  
3) Ask new customers:
- “How did you hear about us?”
4) Note which clusters worked best

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you know which offline clusters produce customers.”
- “Intensive offline becomes a repeatable tool.”
