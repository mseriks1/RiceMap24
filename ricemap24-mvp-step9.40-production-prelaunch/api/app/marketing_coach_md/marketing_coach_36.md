# Marketing Coach – Month 36  
## Focus: “VIP Early Access” (turn your best customers into a stable core — and fill slots before you post publicly)

This month you build a very powerful system for a home kitchen: **VIP Early Access**. It means your best customers get early access to your menu/drop before you post publicly. This creates:
- predictability (orders come in early)
- repeat (VIP customers order more often)
- loyalty (people feel special and included)
- less stress (you know demand earlier)

The goal is not to create “exclusive elitism.” The goal is to reward your best customers and make your weeks easier.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per message/post, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What VIP Early Access means in practice (simple)
VIP Early Access is a simple 3-step system:

1) You create a VIP list (10–30 people)  
2) You send them the menu first (4–12 hours before public posting)  
3) You give a small VIP benefit:
- priority pickup slot
- first access to limited quantity
- occasional small add-on

Then you post publicly.

This makes your week start with demand.

⸻

## Why this works (and why customers love it)
VIP works because:
- people like to feel seen and appreciated
- early access creates a natural “now” feeling
- you reduce uncertainty and waste
- customers order faster when they know there are limited slots

It is one of the best “repeat engines” because it uses psychology in a friendly way.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Define who qualifies as VIP (simple rule)  
2) Build your VIP list and mark them in the customer list  
3) Create VIP message templates (copy/paste)  
4) Run VIP early access weekly and measure the effect

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You define a VIP rule (clear)  
2) You build a VIP list of 10–30 customers

# Part A: Define who is VIP (simple rule)
Choose one rule:
- “Ordered 3+ times”
- “Repeat customers in the last 60 days”
- “Customers who refer friends”
- “Top 10–30 customers”

**Rule:** VIP must be clear and easy to apply.

⸻

# Part B: Build the VIP list (10–30 people)
You create a list of people you can message easily:
- phone list
- WhatsApp list
- Customer List with tags

Add a tag:
- “VIP”

Optional: add notes:
- favorite dish
- pickup preference
- allergies

⸻

# Part C: VIP message templates (copy/paste)

## 1) VIP early access (main template)
> Hi! 😊 You’re on my VIP early access list.  
> Orders open now before I post publicly.  
> Pickup window: [time].  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]  
> VIP benefit: [priority slot / first access / small bonus]

## 2) VIP reminder (short)
> Hi! 😊 Quick reminder — VIP early access is open now.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

## 3) VIP thanks (after order)
> Thank you so much for ordering again 😊  
> I really appreciate you supporting the kitchen.

⸻

# Part D: VIP benefit (keep it small and sustainable)
VIP benefits that work without destroying margin:
- priority pickup slot
- first access to limited batch
- occasional free add-on (not every time)
- small extra sauce

**Rule:** VIP is recognition, not heavy discounts.

⸻
