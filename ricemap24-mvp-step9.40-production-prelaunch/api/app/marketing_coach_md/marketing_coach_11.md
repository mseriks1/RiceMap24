# Marketing Coach – Month 11  
## Focus: Market full menus (not just single dishes) — on social media and offline

This month we focus on marketing **full menus**, not just single dishes. When you’ve come far enough that you have both a routine, a content library, bundles, and different concepts (add-ons, cold-ready, finish-at-home), it becomes smart to zoom out: the customer should not only be tempted by one dish — they should understand that you have a **whole menu** that fits different needs.

This often increases:
- the number of orders (the customer sees more relevant choices)
- order value (the customer sees “combinations” and the full picture)
- repeat (the customer experiences variation over time)

---

## Natural transition from Months 8–10
Months 8–10 were about increasing order value with smarter offers: bundles, cold-ready, and finish-at-home.  
Now you will present this as a whole: “Here is what you can order from me,” in a tidy and tempting way — and make it easy for the customer to choose.

---

## What “market full menus” means in practice
It means you create content that:
- shows 3–6 things at once (not 1)
- explains the menu simply (categories, “best sellers,” “for 2,” “for later”)
- makes the customer think: **“I can always find something here”**

### Important: menu content must be clear (not messy)
Menu content works best when you:
- have few categories
- have clear photos (ideally the same style)
- have short, understandable copy
- have one clear CTA

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Decide your menu structure (categories + what you show in an overview)  
2) Create one “menu overview format” you can reuse  
3) Post menu overview weekly and support with one featured dish + one proof video

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Build your menu structure (simple categories)
### Mini lesson: categories remove decision stress
Customers hesitate when it feels messy. Categories help them decide quickly.

### Tasks
1) Decide 3–5 simple categories (examples):
- “Main dishes”
- “Add-ons / sides”
- “For 2 / bundles”
- “For later (cold-ready)”
- “Finish at home”

2) Choose 3–6 items you will show in your first menu overview  
3) Write a short “menu line” you can reuse:
- “This week’s menu is open”
- “Menu + ordering: [link]”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your menu feels easier to understand.”
- “Categories make ordering feel safer.”

---

## Week 2 — Create a reusable menu overview format (3–6 items)
### Mini lesson: one format saves you time every week
If you have an overview template, you can repeat it with minimal effort.

### Tasks
Choose one overview format:
- **A) Collage / grid (3–6 photos in one image)**  
- **B) Simple list post with 3–6 items + one photo**  
- **C) “Menu card” style (simple graphic with names)**

Create:
1) One menu overview post in your chosen format  
2) One pinned or saved version you can reuse

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a menu format you can repeat.”
- “This reduces weekly decision stress.”

---

## Week 3 — Weekly rhythm: menu overview + one featured dish
### Mini lesson: overview gives clarity, featured dish gives appetite
The overview helps people choose. The featured dish makes them crave.

### Tasks
1) Post the menu overview (weekly)  
2) Post one featured dish (signature/banker)  
3) Video: proof clip (packing/ready for pickup) or “what you get”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now the customer sees both options and desire.”
- “This is a simple weekly system you can keep.”

---

## Week 4 — Offline menu push (poster + local board)
### Mini lesson: offline works best when it’s simple
A menu poster works when people instantly understand:
- what you offer
- how to order (QR)

### Tasks
1) Download/print a menu poster (QR)  
2) Place it in 1–2 locations (where allowed / ask politely)  
3) Optional: post one photo of the poster in place (social proof)
4) Ask one new customer:
- “How did you hear about us?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your menu is visible locally as well.”
- “Offline strengthens trust and familiarity.”
