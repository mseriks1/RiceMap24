# Marketing Coach – Month 27  
## Focus: Meeting food & “small catering” that is ready to serve (packages for meetings, courses, and workshops)

This month is about **meeting food**: food ordered for a planned meeting, a workshop, or a course day — where the customer doesn’t want “normal lunch,” but a **ready-to-serve package** that can be placed directly on the table.

Here is the key: Office lunch is often about **portions per person**. Meeting food is about **food for the table**. You are not just selling food — you are selling a solution that is:  
- easy to serve  
- easy to share  
- neatly packed  
- predictable in time  

You don’t need to “become a catering company.” You are simply making a **meeting-food version** of what you already do: same food, same style — but packed as **meeting packs** that look professional and feel easy to order.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post, always the same:  
“Menu and ordering: [link]”

⸻

## What meeting food means in practice (simple)
Meeting food is about three things:

1) **Package by number**  
“Pack for 6 / 10 / 15 / 20”

2) **Ready-to-serve format**  
Trays/boxes that can be placed directly on the table (no plating, no extra hassle).

3) **Fixed order window + fixed delivery/pickup**  
Meetings are planned. You make it easy to order in advance and get delivery/pickup at the right time.

You make it easy to say yes, because the organizer doesn’t have to think.

⸻

## Why this works (and why they buy)
Meeting food is bought because it solves small, important needs:
- “We want the meeting to feel a bit nice”
- “We have a workshop and need food that is easy to share”
- “We have visitors and want to serve something proper”
- “We want to avoid sending someone out to buy food”

When you hit this, you become more than “food”: you become a safe solution for the meeting organizer.

⸻

## Payment & receipt (tidy and realistic)
Meeting food is easiest when one person coordinates:

- One contact person orders  
- One contact person pays as a single order (card/Vipps/etc. depending on setup)  
- You provide a receipt (clean and simple)

**Important:** You are not building a complex invoicing system here. You keep it realistic and tidy.

# Part A: Create your meeting packs (2–3)

## Pack structure (simple)
Create packs based on number of people:

- **Pack for 6**  
- **Pack for 10**  
- **Pack for 15** (or 20 if relevant)

Each pack should state:
- what is included (simple list)
- ready-to-serve format (trays/boxes)
- allergies note (short + “see menu text”)
- pickup/delivery window
- order cutoff

**Rule:** fewer choices = easier to order.

⸻

# Part B: Order window + delivery/pickup window
Meeting food works best with clear windows.

Example:
- Order cutoff: 24 hours before (or 10:00 the day before)  
- Delivery/pickup: fixed time slot for meetings

⸻

# Part C: Outreach (meeting environments)
