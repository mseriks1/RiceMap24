# Marketing Coach – Month 21  
## Focus: Relational marketing (build loyalty, trust, and long-term profitability)

This month we focus on **relational marketing** (relationship marketing). That means you build your business by building **trust, loyalty, and lasting connections** — not just by “posting and hoping for sales.” When you run a home kitchen, relational marketing is especially powerful, because customers often buy because of:
- safety (credibility and trust)
- recognition
- the feeling of supporting a real local person

Relational marketing is about making it easy to come back, and making the customer feel: “this is my place.”

---

## Why this focus now
You already have:
- visibility and routines
- libraries and reuse
- earlier focus on repeat customers and customer experience

Now we take it one level up: you make relationship a conscious system, not just “be nice.”

When relationship becomes a system, three things happen:
1) more repeat customers (stable income)
2) more word of mouth (new customers through recommendations)
3) less need for constant “new customer hunting”

---

## What relational marketing is in practice (simple)
Relational marketing means that you:
1) recognize your customers (or at least customer groups)
2) communicate warmly and clearly
3) follow up in a way that feels like service, not nagging
4) give small reasons to come back (without dumping price)

You are not going to become a “chat buddy” with everyone. You are simply building a professional, warm relationship that makes people choose you again.

---

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Build a simple “customer base” in the RiceMap24 Customer List (if not already)  
2) Create 2–3 message templates (thank-you, re-order, service clarification)  
3) Create “return triggers” that feel helpful (bundles, favorites back, VIP touch)  
4) Make it a weekly habit: small touches that compound

---

## Goals this month
1) You register at least **10 customers** in the Customer List (or upgrade your existing list)  
2) You send a standard thank-you message after orders  
3) You send 1–2 relevant follow-ups per week (gentle, not spam)  
4) You keep your weekly content routine stable (2 photos + 2 videos)  
5) You collect at least 3 “proof elements” (positive messages/reviews) you can reuse

# The 3 core templates (copy/paste)

## 1) Thank-you message (after order)
> Thank you so much for your order! 😊  
> I hope you enjoy the food. If you want to order again, the menu is here: [link]

## 2) Re-order message (gentle, relevant)
> Hi! Just a quick note — I’m cooking again this week.  
> Menu + ordering via RiceMap24: [link].  
> If you want the same as last time, just write “same as last time” in the request 😊

## 3) Service clarity message (pickup/time)
> Hi! 😊 Pickup window this week is [time].  
> Please write your preferred pickup time in the request. Menu: [link]

**Rule:** Warm + short + clear.

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Upgrade the customer base (Customer List)
### Mini lesson (system beats memory)
If you track customers and notes, follow-up becomes easy and relevant.

### Tasks
1) Register customers after orders:
- name + contact
- favorite dish
- mild/spicy preference
- allergies
- small note (“loves extra sauce”, etc.)

2) Goal this week: add/update at least 3 customers (build toward 10)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. Now your relationship work becomes a system.”
- “Notes make follow-up feel like service.”

---

## Week 2 — Follow-up habit + collect proof
### Mini lesson (proof lowers risk)
New customers order faster when they see others are happy.

### Tasks
1) Use the thank-you message after orders  
2) Send 1–2 gentle re-order messages to relevant customers  
3) Save 1–2 positive messages (screenshots for your own library)  
4) Optional: ask one repeat customer for a short review

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you’re building trust and repeat at the same time.”
- “Proof makes conversion easier.”

---

## Week 3 — Create return triggers (reasons to come back)
### Mini lesson (return triggers are about timing)
People often need a reason *now*.

Choose ONE trigger this week:
- “Customer favorite is back”
- “Bundle for 2”
- “Limited batch”
- “VIP early access” (simple message first)

### Tasks
1) Choose one trigger  
2) Post once with that trigger + CTA  
3) Send one message to relevant customers (gentle)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you’re creating repeat behavior without discounts.”
- “Helpful triggers feel natural, not pushy.”

---

## Week 4 — Stabilize: turn relationship into a weekly system
### Mini lesson (small weekly touches compound)
You don’t need big campaigns. You need small habits.

### Tasks
1) Define your weekly rhythm:
- after each order: update Customer List
- 1 follow-up batch per week (10 minutes)
- 1 proof/review collection habit

2) Quick review:
- which messages got response?
- which trigger worked best?
- what will you repeat next month?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now relational marketing is built into your workflow.”
- “This increases long-term profitability.”
