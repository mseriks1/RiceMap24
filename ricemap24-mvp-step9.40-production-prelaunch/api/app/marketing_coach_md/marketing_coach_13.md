# Marketing Coach – Month 13  
## Focus: More offensive local marketing (with confidence and credibility)

This month we focus on **more offensive local marketing**. Over time you have done a lot right: you have a content library, routines, bundles/menu logic, and you have practiced both social media and offline. Now it’s about using that confidence to be a bit more visible locally — in a way that still feels easy and natural.

The goal is not to become “pushy.” The goal is to be clear and consistent, so more people in your local area discover you, remember you, and actually order.

### Why this focus now
When you have some experience, three things happen:
1) You know what sells (best sellers, bundles, “for later/lunch,” menu)  
2) You have more content to reuse (lower workload)  
3) You have more reason to be proud: you deliver something people actually want

That means you can increase local visibility without increasing stress. You build more “local touchpoints” where people see you again and again.

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Increase local visibility through **more offline touchpoints** (still low threshold)  
2) Use social media as support: “we’re here in the neighborhood” + clear CTA  
3) Test one “mini-offensive” method that creates more traffic than a normal poster

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Plan your 4 offline actions (and choose locations)
### Mini lesson (the plan makes execution easy)
Offensive local marketing works when it becomes a routine. You don’t improvise — you choose 4 small actions and just do them.

### Tasks
1) Choose 6–10 possible locations/channels (use your “location bank”)  
2) Decide your 4 weekly actions:
- Week 1: placement/check
- Week 2: placement/check
- Week 3: mini-offensive push
- Week 4: placement/check + tracking

3) Prepare a short micro script (copy/paste)
> Hi! I run a small home kitchen in [area] and sell via RiceMap24.  
> Could I put up a small QR menu poster here for a while?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a plan that removes stress.”
- “A script makes it easy to act consistently.”

---

## Week 2 — Offline action #1: place in a strong location (and document)
### Mini lesson (one strong location beats many random ones)
Choose places where your ideal customers already are.

### Tasks
1) Place a poster in 1–2 locations (or refresh existing)  
2) Take one photo of the poster in place (for your records)  
3) Post one optional story/post:
- “You can find us locally at [place]” + CTA

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Local touchpoints create credibility.”
- “Documentation makes it repeatable.”

---

## Week 3 — The “mini-offensive” method (more traffic than a normal poster)
### Mini lesson (a small push can create a spike)
A mini-offensive push is something slightly more active than “hang a poster.”

Choose ONE:

**A) Ask a local group admin for a pinned menu post (one week)**  
- “Can I pin my menu post for one week?”

**B) Two-location combo**
- Place a poster in 2 new locations on the same day (creates more repetition)

**C) Partner micro-share**
- Ask a small local business to share your menu link once (you give them a share kit)

### Tasks
1) Choose A, B, or C  
2) Execute it this week  
3) Track reactions:
- clicks
- messages
- questions

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you did an action that creates more traffic than usual.”
- “This helps you learn what produces growth.”

---

## Week 4 — Offline action #4 + simple measurement
### Mini lesson (measurement makes you smarter)
You don’t need analytics. You need simple notes.

### Tasks
1) Do one more offline action (placement/refresh/share)  
2) Ask new customers:
- “How did you hear about us?”
3) Note:
- which location/channel produced response

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you know what works locally.”
- “This turns local marketing into a system.”
