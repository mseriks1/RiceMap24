# Marketing Coach – Month 35  
## Focus: “Demand & Frequency System” (find the best selling rhythm — and make customers expect you)

This month you build a system that makes your sales more predictable: you learn **how often** you should open, and **which rhythm** gives the best demand without burning you out. Many home kitchens guess. Here you test and choose a rhythm based on reality.

A strong rhythm creates:
- more repeat orders (customers expect you)
- less stress (you plan your weeks)
- fewer “random” marketing pushes
- higher trust (“they are stable and organized”)

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What “demand & frequency” means in practice (simple)
It means you answer these questions with real testing:

1) How often do customers actually want to order from you?
- weekly?
- twice a week?
- every other week?

2) Which day/time creates the best response?
- Thursday pickup?
- weekend?
- lunch?

3) Which rhythm fits your life and production capacity?

You test a simple rhythm for 4 weeks and track the signals.

⸻

## Why this matters (and why it increases sales)
Customers buy more when they can **predict** you:
- “They open every Thursday”
- “They do a drop every other week”
- “Orders open on Monday, pickup Thursday”

Predictability creates habit. Habit creates repeat. Repeat creates stable income.

Without rhythm, customers forget you. With rhythm, customers start to plan around you.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Choose a frequency hypothesis (your best guess)  
2) Communicate the rhythm clearly (same message every week)  
3) Track simple demand signals (orders, messages, “missed it”)  
4) Decide your best rhythm and lock it in for the next months

⸻

# Part A: Choose your rhythm to test (one hypothesis)
Pick ONE of these to test:

### Option 1: Weekly rhythm
- Open every week on the same day (e.g., Thursday pickup)

### Option 2: Twice per week
- Two fixed days (e.g., Tuesday + Thursday)

### Option 3: Every other week (biweekly)
- Larger drops, more batch cooking, less frequent

**Rule:** Choose what fits your life and your capacity.

⸻

# Part B: The “availability message” (copy/paste)
This is the message you repeat every week, so people learn the rhythm.

**Template:**
> Orders open for [day].  
> Pickup window: [time].  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

Optional:
> If you want the same as last time, write “same as last time” + preferred time.

⸻

# Part C: Demand signals (what you track)
You track simple signals:
- number of orders
- messages/questions
- “I missed it” messages
- which dishes/bundles sell
- time preferences

You can track it in:
- notes
- spreadsheet
- customer list notes

- “Small changes teach you more than big changes.”

## Week 4 — Decide the winning rhythm + plan next month

### Tasks
1) Review the 4 weeks:
- which week had the best demand?
- which schedule felt easiest?
- did customers start to expect you?

2) Choose:
- keep the rhythm
- or switch to a better one (based on data)

3) Write your next-month plan in 3 lines:
- opening day(s)
- order cutoff
- pickup window

✅ Done confirmations:

- “You chose a rhythm that supports long-term growth.”
