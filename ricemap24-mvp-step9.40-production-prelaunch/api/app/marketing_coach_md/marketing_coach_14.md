# Marketing Coach – Month 14  
## Focus: New and bigger customer groups (without losing the local feeling)

This month we focus on reaching **new and bigger customer groups**. You now have a solid base: good routines, content you can reuse, and you have practiced local visibility. Now it’s about expanding a bit beyond “the closest people” and getting more orders from people who may not know you yet — but who have needs that fit perfectly with what you offer.

The goal is not to become “big and impersonal.” The goal is to become more visible in **more relevant environments**, and make it easy for them to order in a way that fits their everyday life.

---

## Why this focus now
When you want to grow further, you often don’t need “more random people.” You need more people in **situations where they actually buy food**:
- work/lunch
- families/weekdays
- small groups/friends
- local environments with steady activity

This is often better than just posting more on social media. You want to reach people with a concrete reason to order.

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Choose 2–3 **customer groups** you want to reach (not all at the same time)  
2) Create 2–3 **offer angles** that fit them (bundle / “for 2” / lunch / for later)  
3) Combine social media + local distribution (offline actions) so you actually get discovered

---

## Goals this month
1) You choose 2 target groups and create a clear message for each  
2) You do 2 small “channel tests” per target group (social media + offline)  
3) You complete 4 offline actions (1 per week) in environments where the target group exists  
4) You keep the routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week

---

## Fixed posting routine (this month)
This month you run the **challenge routine** as a baseline:

- **2 photo posts per week** (FB + IG, same content)  
- **2 videos per week** (TikTok + Reels + Shorts where you can)  
- + **1 offline action per week** (4 total)

---

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Targeting (50%)**: target groups + offer angles + messages created  
- **Execution (50%)**: weekly posts/videos + offline actions completed  

---

# Step 1: Choose your target groups (2)
Choose 2 groups (not more) this month.

Examples of target groups:
- Office lunch / workday lunch
- Families (weekday dinner)
- Couples / “for 2”
- Friends / small groups
- Students (if relevant)
- Gym / health-focused customers
- Community groups (church/volunteer/club)

**Rule:** choose groups where you can actually reach them locally.

---

# Step 2: Create offer angles that fit each group (2–3 angles)
You don’t need to invent new dishes. You angle what you already have.

Examples:
- **For 2** bundle (easy and clear)
- **Lunch** option (portion + easy pickup)
- **For later** (cold-ready) add-on
- **Finish-at-home** (restaurant at home)
- **Family pack** (weeknight solution)

---

# Step 3: Combine social media + offline distribution (so you get discovered)
You want “touchpoints” where the group actually is.

Offline ideas (depending on the group):
- coworking / office hub boards
- gyms
- apartment building boards
- schools / daycare boards (where allowed)
- community centers / libraries
- sports halls / clubhouses
- church/volunteer boards

---

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Pick groups + write the messages
### Mini lesson (clarity beats “marketing”)
If your message is simple and relevant, the right people react.

### Tasks
1) Choose your 2 target groups  
2) Write 2 short messages (one per group), 2–3 lines each:
- who you are
- what you offer that fits them
- how to order (CTA)

**Message template (office lunch):**
Local home kitchen in [area].  
Lunch orders available this week (easy pickup).  
Menu + ordering: [link]

**Message template (families):**
Dinner is handled this week — small-batch home kitchen in [area].  
For 2 / bundles available.  
Menu + ordering: [link]

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your marketing becomes relevant, not generic.”
- “Clear messages increase conversion.”

---

## Week 2 — Channel tests for each group (SoMe + offline)
### Mini lesson (test small and learn fast)
You don’t need huge campaigns. You need small tests.

### Tasks
For each target group:
1) 1 social media post angled to the group  
2) 1 offline action in an environment where they are

Examples:
- Office lunch: post “Lunch this week” + poster at coworking
- Families: post “For 2 / family pack” + poster at apartment board

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you’re meeting people where their need exists.”
- “Small tests show what works.”

---

## Week 3 — Repeat the best angle + second offline action
### Mini lesson (repetition creates familiarity)
People often need to see you more than once.

### Tasks
1) Repeat the best-performing message/angle for each group  
2) Do one offline action (or refresh one) in the same environments  
3) Ask 1–2 customers:
- “How did you hear about us?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Repetition increases trust and response.”
- “Tracking helps you double down on winners.”

---

## Week 4 — Finish strong: 4th offline action + simple review
### Mini lesson (review turns effort into a system)
A small review prevents wasted work.

### Tasks
1) Do the 4th offline action (placement/refresh/share)  
2) Review:
- which group responded more?
- which angle worked best?
- which locations/channels produced messages/orders?

3) Decide what to continue next month:
- keep one group
- or swap one group

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a data-informed direction for growth.”
- “This makes scaling feel simpler.”

---

## Month 14 done: you’re “ready”
You’re ready when:
- you selected 2 target groups and wrote clear messages ✅  
- you tested channels (SoMe + offline) for both ✅  
- you completed 4 offline actions ✅  
- you maintained 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week ✅  
- you reviewed what worked and what to continue ✅
