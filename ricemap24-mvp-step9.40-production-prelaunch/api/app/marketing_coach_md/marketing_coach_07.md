# Marketing Coach – Month 7 (v2)
## Focus: Get more repeat customers (customer experience that creates stable income)

This month we focus on getting **more repeat customers**. When you already have a steady marketing rhythm, the next big lift is to make your customers more loyal — so they order more often and you get more stable income. Repeat means you don’t start from zero every week.

### Why repeat is the key to stable income
Getting a new customer usually requires much more work than getting a customer back. New customers must:
- discover you
- build credibility and trust
- understand the menu and ordering method

Repeat customers have already done this. They order faster, they trust more, and they can become your stable base. When repeat increases, the business becomes more stable and predictable. You get a “base” that means every week doesn’t start from zero.

### What “customer experience” means in practice for a home kitchen
Customer experience means the total experience the customer is left with:
- the food tastes good (of course)
- it was easy to order
- it was clear what they would get
- communication was short, friendly, and professional
- the customer felt seen (a thank you, a small “hi”)

Small details create credibility and trust — and that’s often what decides whether people order again.

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Build the repeat system in the Customer List (RiceMap24)
### Mini lesson: “system beats memory”
Repeat happens more often when you remember people and preferences. You don’t need an advanced CRM — just that you actually register the customer and a couple notes. That makes follow-up relevant and professional.

### Tasks
1) **After each order:** register the customer in the **Customer List in RiceMap24**  
   - Note favorite dish / mild-spicy / allergies / “liked extra sauce”
2) Goal this week: **at least 2 customers registered** (you build toward 5+ total)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. Now you’re building your repeat system inside RiceMap24.”
- “Notes make follow-up relevant and more credible.”

### This week’s posting (routine + repeat angle)
- 1 post: “This week’s favorite is back” (signature/banker) + CTA  
- 1 video (if you follow standard): “fresh today” / “ready to pick up”

---

## Week 2 — Make it easy to order again (lower friction)
### Mini lesson: “people repeat when it’s easy”
Many customers like the food, but “don’t think of it” or postpone. A simple reminder + easy ordering method makes a big difference.

### Tasks
1) Create a standard **thank-you message** (copy/paste)
> Thank you so much for your order! I hope you enjoyed the food  
> If you want to order again later, you can find the menu here: [link]

2) Create a “quick order” text
> Hi! If you want to order again, you can just reply with:  
> “Same as last time” or “[dish] + [add-on]” + your preferred time

✅ Done confirmations:
- “A thank-you message builds credibility and trust.”
- “Quick ordering removes friction and increases repeat.”

### This week’s posting
- Post: “Want the same as last time?” + CTA  
- Post (bonus): “Dish + add-on” bundle + CTA  
- Video: “ready to pick up” / packing (proof)

---

## Week 3 — Give a reason to come back (without dumping price)
### Mini lesson: “return trigger”
Repeat increases when the customer feels it’s “worth it” to order again now. It can be:
- a new small add-on
- a bundle
- a “limited batch”
- a small surprise

It doesn’t have to be a discount.

### Tasks
1) Choose one “repeat trigger” for the week:
- Bundle: dish + side (in the same post)
- “Limited batch” (limited quantity)
- “New variant” of a known dish

2) Post one social media post with a repeat angle:
- “This week’s favorite is back”
- “Want the same as last time?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You give a natural reason to order again.”
- “Bundles often increase both repeat and average order value.”

---

## Week 4 — Build credibility and trust with small VIP habits
### Mini lesson: “the little extra that gets remembered”
Credibility and trust are built not only by food — but by the experience:
- clarity
- friendliness
- predictability
- small positive surprises

### Tasks
1) Choose one VIP habit (realistic)
- “fixed update” one day a week: “I’m cooking this this week”
- small extra (e.g., extra sauce) for repeat customers (if it’s profitable)
- “priority slot” with early ordering

2) Send one last re-order message this week (gentle and short)
> Hi! I’m opening orders this week as well  
> Menu and ordering: [link]

3) Summarize the Customer List:
- how many repeat customers do you have now?
- which dishes repeat most?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a repeat system that makes income more stable.”
- “You’re building loyalty without wearing yourself out.”
