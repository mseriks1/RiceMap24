# Marketing Coach – Month 32  
## Focus: “Menu Focus Month” (optimize the menu so it sells more — without more marketing)

This month you do one of the highest-leverage improvements in the whole program: you make the **menu** your strongest sales tool. Many sellers spend a lot of time on marketing, but lose sales because the menu is confusing, the photos are uneven, or the ordering flow feels unclear.

Menu Focus Month is about:
- clarity
- appetite
- trust
- “easy to choose”

When the menu is strong, marketing becomes easier because the conversion is higher. You don’t need to drive as much traffic when the page sells better.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What this means in practice
This month you do three things:
1) You clean up the menu so it is easy to understand  
2) You ensure your top items look great (photos + copy)  
3) You create a weekly “menu marketing” rhythm where the menu is always visible

You are not making a new menu from scratch. You are improving what already exists.

⸻

## Why this matters (and why it increases sales)
A strong menu:
- reduces customer questions  
- reduces hesitation  
- increases first orders  
- increases order value (people add extras when they understand the menu)  
- increases repeat (people know what to expect)  

A messy menu does the opposite.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Define your menu structure (simple categories + best sellers)  
2) Upgrade photos and copy for top dishes (the “hero set”)  
3) Add clear ordering language and pickup/delivery clarity  
4) Market the menu weekly as an overview (not just single dishes)

⸻

1) You simplify the menu to 3–6 main items + clear add-ons  
2) You create a hero set for the top 3 items (photos + clips)  
3) You reduce top 3 customer questions by fixing the menu text

5) You do 2 offline actions (Week 2 and Week 4) supporting the menu

# Part A: Menu structure (simple categories)
Choose 3–5 categories that make it easy to decide:
- Main dishes  
- Add-ons / sides  
- Bundles / For 2  
- For later (cold-ready)  
- Finish-at-home  

**Rule:** fewer categories = less confusion.

⸻

# Part B: The “hero set” for your top 3 dishes
A hero set is:
- 5–10 strong photos per dish
- 2–4 short clips per dish
- consistent style (angle/light)

This is content you can reuse for months.

⸻

# Part C: Copy clarity (reduce questions)
Every repeated customer question is a menu fix.

Common questions to eliminate:
- “How big is it?”  
- “What’s included?”  
- “When can I pick up?”  
- “How spicy is it?”  

Fix by adding 1–2 lines in the menu text.

⸻

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Simplify and restructure the menu (remove friction)
### Mini lesson (clarity increases conversion)
People order when they understand.

### Tasks
1) Choose your categories (3–5)

3) Ensure add-ons are connected clearly to dishes  
4) Add clear ordering language:
- pickup/delivery window

### Tasks
