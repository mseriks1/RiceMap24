# Marketing Coach – Month 40  
## Focus: Video library + storytelling (make content easier and make people feel connected)

This month you build a stronger **video library** and use it for **storytelling**. Not “cinematic storytelling.” Simple, real storytelling that makes people feel:
- this is a real kitchen  
- this is safe  
- this is worth ordering from  
- this is a local person with a real process  

A good video library also makes marketing easier because you can:
- reuse clips
- cut new videos quickly
- keep the 2-video-per-week routine without stress

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/video, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What a video library is (simple)
A video library is just a folder of short clips that you collect over time. When you have:
- cooking clips
- ingredient clips
- packing/proof clips
- “you” clips (hands/hello)
- portion/what you get clips

…you can combine them into new videos anytime.

The goal this month is to expand the library so content becomes easy.

⸻

## Why storytelling works (for food)
People don’t only buy food. They buy a feeling:
- trust
- comfort
- appetite
- identity (“this is my kind of food”)

Storytelling creates that feeling. And it increases:
- first orders
- repeat
- word of mouth

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Set up your library structure (folders)  
2) Capture a lot of short clips (20–40+)  
3) Create 2 repeatable story formats  
4) Publish weekly and save everything back to the library

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You capture **40 clips** (minimum 20)  
2) You create **2 story formats** you can reuse  
3) You publish at least **4 videos** this month  
4) You keep routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week  
5) You organize your library so reuse becomes easy

⸻

## Fixed routine (baseline this month)
- 2 photo posts per week (FB + IG)  
- 2 videos per week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)  
- Library session weekly (capture + organize)

Offline:
- optional this month (only if it fits)

⸻

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Video library (60%)**: clips captured + organized + formats defined  
- **Execution (40%)**: weekly videos posted + routine completed  

⸻

# Part A: Library structure (folders)
Create folders:
- Ingredients  
- Cooking / prep  
- Plating  
- Packing / proof  
- “You” / behind the scenes  
- Menu / overview  
- Bundles / for 2  

**Rule:** don’t overcomplicate. You need to find clips fast.

⸻

# Part B: The 2 story formats (repeatable)

## Story format 1: “Ingredients → cooking → finished meal”
A simple transformation story (before/after).  
Clips:
- ingredients
- cooking
- final plate
- CTA

## Story format 2: “Pack with me / ready for pickup”
A proof story.  
Clips:
- packing
- labels
- finished boxes
- CTA

These two formats alone can cover most weeks.

⸻

# Part C: Text overlay / voice (keep it simple)
You don’t need to talk. Text overlay is enough:
- “Fresh today”
- “Small batches”
- “Ready for pickup”
- “Menu + ordering: [link]”

If you like voice: keep it short and friendly.

⸻

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Set up folders + capture the first clips
### Mini lesson (capture beats perfection)
If you wait for perfect, you don’t build a library.

### Tasks
1) Create the folder structure  
2) Capture 10 clips this week (5–10 sec each):
- 3 ingredients
- 3 cooking
- 2 plating
- 2 packing/proof
3) Keep routine: 2 photos + 2 videos

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your content engine has a structure.”
- “Clips today = easier marketing next month.”

---

## Week 2 — Story format #1 video + capture more clips
### Mini lesson (transformation stories perform well)
Ingredients → finished meal is a strong story.

### Tasks
1) Capture 10 more clips (total 20)  
2) Create and post one video using Story format 1  
3) Save the finished video and clips back into the library

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a repeatable story format.”
- “Saving makes reuse easy.”

---

## Week 3 — Story format #2 video + build the habit
### Mini lesson (proof reduces risk)
Packing/ready clips build trust fast.

### Tasks
1) Capture 10 more clips (total 30)  
2) Create and post one video using Story format 2  
3) Organize clips and label best ones

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a second repeatable format.”
- “Proof increases first orders.”

---

## Week 4 — Stabilize: reach 40 clips + decide what to repeat
### Mini lesson (systems beat creativity)
You don’t need new ideas. You need a library and two formats.

### Tasks
1) Capture 10 more clips (reach 40)  
2) Post one video using the format you like best  
3) Review:
- which format got best response?
- which clips are “top 10” for reuse?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your library supports your weekly routine.”
- “You built a system you can keep.”
