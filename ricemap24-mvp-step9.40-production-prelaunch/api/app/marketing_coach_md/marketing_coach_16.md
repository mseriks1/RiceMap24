# Marketing Coach – Month 16 (updated)  
## Focus: Use network and relationships to build the business (without being pushy)

This month we focus on using **network and relationships** as a conscious part of marketing. When you have a good product and a routine that works, your network is often the fastest path to more customers — because relationships already contain something that is absolutely crucial in the beginning: **credibility and trust**.

This is not about “selling to friends.” It’s about making it easy for people around you to:
- understand what you offer
- know how to order (RiceMap24)
- recommend you to others in a natural way

---

## What “network” means (beyond family)
Network is not just “friends.” It’s all the people and environments you already have contact with, directly or indirectly:

- **Friends** and acquaintances  
- **Co-workers** and former co-workers  
- **Friends of friends** (through introductions)  
- **Neighbors** and local community  
- Communities you are part of:  
  - **church/congregation**  
  - **gym** / sports / hobbies  
  - **school/PTA** (parents, if you have children)  
  - **volunteering** / local groups  
  - **coworking/office hubs**

You don’t need a “big network.” You need a network that is **locally relevant** and where people can order.

---

## Why network marketing works so well for home kitchens
Because it solves the biggest barrier: trust. A new customer often thinks:
- “Is this safe?”
- “Is it real?”
- “Do I dare to order?”

If they hear about you from someone they already trust, that barrier drops dramatically. That means:
- more people try you
- more people order again
- word-of-mouth grows faster

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Build a **simple message** you can reuse (what you offer + link)  
2) Do **small network actions weekly** (not big pushes)  
3) Turn it into a habit: “1 touchpoint per week” (so it becomes a system)

# The “network message” (copy/paste template)
This is the message you can reuse everywhere (DM, chat, email, group posts).

**Network message (copy/paste):**
Hi! I run a small home kitchen in [area] and cook [cuisine/style] in small batches.  
Menu + ordering via RiceMap24: [link]  
Pickup in [area]. Allergens listed in the menu text.

Optional line:
- “If you know someone local who would enjoy this, feel free to share.”

**Rule:** Keep it short, warm, and clear.

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Map your network (where can you show up naturally?)
### Mini lesson (network is not “selling,” it’s being visible)
You don’t need to pitch. You need to show up with a clear message and a link.

### Tasks
1) Make a list of 20 possible network nodes (people or groups), for example:
- 5 friends who live locally
- 5 co-workers (or former co-workers)
- 5 local community nodes (church, gym, volunteer group, neighborhood group)
- 5 “connector people” (people who know many others)

2) Choose 5 of them to start with this month  
3) Save your network message as a note on your phone

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you know where you can show up naturally.”
- “Saving the message removes awkwardness.”

---

## Week 2 — Network action #1: soft share (no pressure)
### Mini lesson (the best network marketing is low pressure)
People share when it feels easy and natural.

### Choose ONE action:
**A) Share on your personal profile (once)**
- share your menu link + 2 lines

**B) Send the network message to 3 people**
- friends/co-workers who are local

**C) Share in one community channel you are already part of**
- church group, neighborhood group, gym community (if allowed)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You created visibility through trust.”
- “This is how word-of-mouth begins.”

---

## Week 3 — Network action #2: ask for a simple share (not a big favor)
### Mini lesson (people often want to help — they just need clarity)
The ask should be small and easy.

### Action (choose one):
**A) Ask one connector person**
> “Hi! If you know anyone local who would enjoy this, feel free to share my menu link. It helps a lot.”

**B) Ask a group admin (light)**
> “Is it okay if I share a weekly menu post here?”

**C) Ask one local business contact**
- coworking manager, gym receptionist, etc.  
- ask if you can place a QR flyer

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You made it easy for someone else to spread the word.”
- “One connector can create many customers.”

---

## Week 4 — Network action #3 and #4: repeat + track
### Mini lesson (repeat creates results)
Network marketing compounds when you repeat small actions.

### Tasks
1) Do 2 small actions this week:
- one share
- one message
- one intro ask

2) Ask new customers:
- “How did you hear about us?”

3) Note which network channel created response

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you see what network channels create orders.”
- “This becomes a repeatable system.”
