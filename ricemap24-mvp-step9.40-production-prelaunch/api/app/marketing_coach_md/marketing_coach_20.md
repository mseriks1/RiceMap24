# Marketing Coach – Month 20  
## Focus: You can always start again — a steady rhythm that matches your ambitions (long-term growth)

This month we focus on something that separates those who succeed over time from those who stall: **the ability to keep going steadily**, no matter how the previous month went. Many home kitchen sellers have periods where they don’t market as much as they wanted. That does not mean you “failed.” It just means that life and capacity vary.

The goal this month is to build a marketing routine that:
- is realistic over time
- matches your ambitions (part-time vs full-time)
- gives steady visibility and sales
- makes it easy to “come back” without stress

This is not about short bursts. This is about a rhythm that fits you.

---

## Why this focus now
When you’ve kept a routine for a while, sooner or later you will meet periods of:
- busy food production
- low energy
- less time
- a “I should post more” feeling

If you stop completely in those periods, you lose momentum. But if you have a “minimum plan” that is always possible, the engine keeps running. This is one of the most important long-term skills in business.

---

## Ambition level = marketing level (you decide)
There is a big difference between whether you want to:
- just sell a little on the side (part-time)
- build a stable part-time income
- build a full-time / growth business

This month you therefore create two plans:

1) **Minimum plan** (always doable)  
2) **Growth plan** (when you have capacity)

Week by week you choose which plan you follow, without guilt.

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Create a “minimum plan” and a “growth plan”  
2) Create a simple system for reuse and scheduling (so the rhythm becomes easy)  
3) Build a routine that supports your sales goals (not just posting)

---

## Goals this month
1) You define your ambition level (part-time / growth / full-time) in 2 sentences  
2) You create a minimum plan and a growth plan  
3) You schedule at least 7 days ahead (at the level you choose)  
4) You keep steady marketing all month by choosing the right plan  
5) You do 2 offline actions (Week 2 and Week 4)

---

## Fixed posting routine (always included)
This month, “fixed routine” is defined as following **one of two plans**:

### Plan A: Minimum plan (always doable)
- **1 photo post per week** (FB + IG)  
- **1 video per week** (TikTok/Reels/Shorts where you can)  
- **1 local action every other week** (optional this month)  
- Scheduling: at least **7 days ahead** (1 week)

### Plan B: Growth plan (when you have capacity)
- **2 photo posts per week** (FB + IG)  
- **2 videos per week** (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)  
- **1 local share / offline action per week** (or every other week if needed)  
- Scheduling: **7–14 days ahead**

**Rule:** You choose Plan A or Plan B each week.  
The goal is that you never stop completely.

---

## CTA (repeated)
**CTA = Call To Action** = one clear sentence that says what the customer should do now.

Examples:
- “Menu + ordering via RiceMap24: [link]”
- “See the menu and order here: [link]”

Mini rule: one CTA per post.

---

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Rhythm (70%)**: you followed Plan A or Plan B every week  
- **Scheduling + reuse (30%)**: content planned ahead + reuse system active

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Define your ambition + create your two plans
### Mini lesson (decide once, then execute)
The biggest stress comes from deciding every day. Decide once per week.

### Tasks
1) Write 2 sentences:
- “My ambition level right now is …”
- “This month I will follow Plan A / Plan B most weeks.”

2) Write your Minimum plan and Growth plan (copy/paste above and adjust)  
3) Prepare the week’s content list:
- 1 appetite dish
- 1 proof/trust post or clip
- 1 menu overview (optional)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a plan without guilt.”
- “A steady rhythm beats intensity.”

---

## Week 2 — Scheduling week + offline action #1
### Mini lesson (scheduling protects you)
If you schedule ahead, you don’t lose momentum when life is busy.

### Tasks
1) Schedule 7 days ahead (Plan A or Plan B)  
2) Offline action #1 (Week 2):
- place/refresh one poster
- or share your weekly menu in one strong local group

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Scheduling keeps your visibility alive.”
- “Small local actions compound over time.”

---

## Week 3 — Reuse week (same content, new angle)
### Mini lesson (reuse prevents burnout)
Reuse is not laziness. It is a strategy.

### Tasks
1) Reuse one photo or video:
- new hook text
- new angle (for 2 / lunch / for later)
2) Keep your plan (A or B) steady

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you can keep going without creating everything new.”
- “Angles create variety.”

---

## Week 4 — Stabilize + offline action #2 + review
### Mini lesson (review keeps you honest and sustainable)
A small review helps you choose the right rhythm next month.

### Tasks
1) Offline action #2 (Week 4)  
2) Review:
- What felt easy this month?
- What felt heavy?
- Which plan (A/B) worked best for your life?

3) Decide your next month rhythm:
- mostly Plan A
- or mostly Plan B
- or A most weeks and B in campaign weeks

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your marketing is built for long-term growth.”
- “You can always come back without stress.”

---

## Month 20 done: you’re “ready”
You’re ready when:
- you defined your ambition level in 2 sentences ✅  
- you created a minimum plan and a growth plan ✅  
- you scheduled at least 7 days ahead ✅  
- you followed a steady plan every week ✅  
- you completed 2 offline actions ✅
