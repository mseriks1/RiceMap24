# Marketing Coach – Month 5
## Focus: Local growth — offline marketing that supports social media and brings local customers

This month we focus on **local growth**, meaning local growth through **offline marketing**. You continue your social media routine, but add one smart offline action that makes more people in your local area discover you — and get an easy path to ordering.

### Offline vs online — what’s the difference (and why you need both)?
**Online marketing (social media)** is when people see you on a screen: Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, etc. It’s great for:
- reach (many people can see you)
- repetition (people meet you multiple times)
- content that creates appetite and trust

**Offline marketing** is when people meet you in physical everyday life: in stores, on bulletin boards, at workplaces, in local environments. It’s great for:
- the right local people (those who actually live/work nearby)
- credibility (it feels real and close)
- fewer distractions (they see the message in calmer situations)

The most important thing is that offline and online point to the same place:
**Offline creates discovery → QR/link → RiceMap24 → order.**  
Offline is therefore not an “alternative” to social media, but a **booster** that can give you new customers without you having to post more.

### What local growth means in practice
Local growth is not about “being everywhere.” It’s about choosing one place where your target group already is — and making it easy for them to try you.

This month we therefore do:
- **Main action (1):** one concrete offline action everyone can do
- **Bonus (1):** if you have capacity
- + you create a list of places/ideas you can use later (so it gets easier month by month)

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Understand the offline role and find good categories
### Mini lesson (why “the right place” beats “many places”)
Offline works best when the place matches people who can actually become customers. One good place can beat ten random ones.

### Tasks
1) Create a **place list bank** (10–20 ideas) you can reuse later  
   Examples:
   - local grocery / kiosks / deli
   - gym
   - hair salon / barbershop / nail salon
   - coworking / office hub
   - bulletin board in an apartment building
   - school/PTA / sports club
   - church/volunteer communities
   - local Facebook groups (counts as a “local channel”)

2) Choose **3 candidates** that feel realistic to contact

✅ Confirmation: *“Great. Now you’re building a bank that makes offline easier every month.”*

---

## Week 2 — Choose the main action (and make it easy to execute)
### Mini lesson (why one good action is enough)
Many people fall off because they try too much. You’re not going “on tour.” You’re doing one thing that has impact and that you can repeat later.

### Main action (choose one)
**A) QR poster in a strategic place**  
– fits if you want a fixed touchpoint that works for you

**B) Local sharing in one relevant group/channel** (neighborhood/workplace)  
– fits if you already have a network to start with

### Tasks
1) Choose A or B  
2) Prepare a “micro-intro” (20 sec / 2 sentences), so it doesn’t feel awkward

**Micro-intro (to the place/person):**  
> “Hi! I run a small home kitchen in [area]. I cook [type of food].  
> I have a QR poster that shows the menu and how to order via RiceMap24.  
> Would it fit to hang it here for a while?”

**Micro text (to a group/channel):**  
> “Hi! I’ve started a small home kitchen in [area] and now sell via RiceMap24.  
> Menu and ordering: [link]. Thanks for taking a look ”

✅ Confirmation: *“Great. When you have a ready sentence, it becomes easy to actually do it.”*

---

## Week 3 — Complete the main action (1 thing — done)
### Mini lesson (why execution beats planning)
This week there’s only one job: execute. Even if it feels a bit new, this is exactly how local growth is built.

### Tasks (depending on A or B)
**If A (poster):**
1) Place the poster (QR + name + signature dish)  
2) Take one photo of the poster in place (for possible social media later)

**If B (local sharing):**
1) Post/send the message in the chosen channel  
2) Reply kindly if anyone asks (it builds trust)

✅ Confirmation: *“Strong. This creates a new local meeting point between you and customers.”*

### Bonus (optional, small)
- If you have capacity: do one extra small action (e.g., one extra place, or one extra local channel)

---

## Week 4 — Make this a habit: find 2 places for next month
### Mini lesson (how you build local growth over time)
Offline becomes truly powerful when you do a little every month. Then you get more “touchpoints” in your local area, and you become a known local option.

### Tasks
1) Choose **2 new places/channels** you want to try next month  
2) Note:
   - who you should talk to
   - where you’ll place the poster / which group
   - when you will do it (a specific day next month)

✅ Confirmation: *“Now you have a plan forward. That makes local growth a system, not a one-time stunt.”*

---

Keep the routine you already have:
- 1–2 posts per week (FB/IG)
- video if you have capacity (but don’t increase requirements now)
- ideally one post that supports offline: “You can find us locally at [place]” (optional)
