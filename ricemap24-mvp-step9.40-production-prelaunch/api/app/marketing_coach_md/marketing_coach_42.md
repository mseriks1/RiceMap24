# Marketing Coach – Month 42  
## Focus: Neighborhood Week (7-day hyper-local campaign that makes you “the local kitchen” in one area)

This month you run a campaign that is extremely effective for local growth: **Neighborhood Week**. This is a 7-day campaign where you focus on ONE neighborhood/area and create so many local touchpoints that people start to think:

> “Oh yeah — that kitchen is in our area.”

Neighborhood Week works because it creates:
- strong local repetition (people see you multiple times)
- higher trust (it feels local and real)
- easier first orders (the barrier drops)
- more word of mouth (neighbors talk)

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What Neighborhood Week is (simple)
Neighborhood Week is just this:

1) You choose one neighborhood focus area  
2) You run a 7-day campaign where you:
- post locally (same message)
- share in local groups/channels
- place posters in that neighborhood
3) You track: what creates orders?

You do not try to cover the whole city. You do one area well.

⸻

## Why Neighborhood Week works (and why it can create fast growth)
Local campaigns work because:
- people respond to relevance (“this is in my area”)
- repetition creates trust and memory
- offline + online together feels more real
- neighbors share with neighbors

When you focus, you get more impact than when you spread thin.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Choose your neighborhood and make a location plan  
2) Prepare assets (poster + share kit + simple posts)  
3) Run the 7-day week (offline + online together)  
4) Follow up, track results, and decide what to repeat next month

⸻

During the 7-day Neighborhood Week you add:

# Part A: Choose the neighborhood (one focus)
Choose one area where:
- people are actually local customers
- pickup/delivery makes sense
- you can place posters and reach local groups

**Rule:** choose one area only. Focus creates impact.

⸻

# Part B: Location plan (5 realistic touchpoints)
Pick 5 touchpoints in that neighborhood. Examples:
- grocery/convenience store bulletin board  
- gym  
- apartment building board  
- laundromat / shared laundry room (where allowed)  
- café/coffee shop board (if allowed)  
- community center/library board  
- sports hall/clubhouse  
- local Facebook group / neighborhood group

**Rule:** choose places where people pass by repeatedly.

⸻

# Part C: Neighborhood message (copy/paste)
Use one simple message everywhere:

> Neighborhood Week in **[area]** 😊  
> Local home kitchen — pickup this week: [time window].  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

Optional:
> For 2 / bundles available (choose one angle).

⸻

# Part D: Poster headline (keep it simple)
Poster text example:
**Neighborhood Week – [area]**  
Local home kitchen • Order via QR  
Pickup: [time window]  
Menu and ordering: [link]

⸻

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Choose neighborhood + make the location plan
### Mini lesson (focus makes you memorable)
One neighborhood done well beats many areas done weakly.

### Tasks
1) Choose the neighborhood focus area  
2) Choose 5 touchpoints (location plan)  
3) Prepare the neighborhood message template (copy/paste)  
4) Prepare poster draft (QR + headline)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now the campaign has focus and structure.”
- “A location plan makes execution easy.”

---

## Week 2 — Prepare assets + warm-up
### Mini lesson (warm-up makes the week feel natural)
If people see you before the 7-day week, it feels less pushy.

### Tasks
1) Prepare:
- posters (print/download)
- share kit (image + text + link)
- 3–5 photos/clips from your library  
2) Post one warm-up:
- “Neighborhood Week starts on [date] in [area]” + CTA  
3) Optional: ask one local group admin if you can share during the week

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now the week will run smoother.”
- “Warm-up increases campaign response.”

---

## Week 3 — Run Neighborhood Week (7 days)
### Mini lesson (repetition inside one area creates trust)
During the 7 days you do daily light touches.

### Tasks (during the 7 days)
1) Place posters in 3–5 locations in the neighborhood  
2) Share in local groups/channels (not spam — 1–2 strong channels)  
3) Post daily light touch:
- story
- short post
- “menu open”
- “fresh today”
4) Reply quickly and warmly  
5) Ask new customers:
- “How did you hear about us?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you created strong local awareness.”
- “Offline + online together creates fast trust.”

---

## Week 4 — Follow-up + review + decide what to repeat
### Mini lesson (campaigns become systems when you reuse them)
A good neighborhood week can be repeated in a new area next month.

### Tasks
1) Thank people who ordered:
> Thank you so much for your order! 😊  
> Menu for next week: [link]

2) Review:
- which locations worked best?
- which channel gave messages/orders?
- what copy/angle performed best?

3) Decide:
- repeat in the same neighborhood
- or run in a new neighborhood next month

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have a repeatable local campaign model.”
- “You learned what creates local growth.”
