# Marketing Coach – Month 26  
## Focus: Local “Business Lunch” agreement that repeats (weekly rhythm + simple standard order)

After the office lunch month you now have a big advantage: you already have language, packages, and content that show you can deliver to multiple people. Month 26 is about making this even more stable by creating **a small, local “business lunch” rhythm** that repeats.

This does not mean big contracts. It means you create a **simple, fixed solution** for 1–3 nearby workplaces that order again and again because it’s easy and nice.

The goal this month is to turn office lunch into a “weekly habit.” You make it so easy that the customer doesn’t have to think: they know what they get, when they must order, and how it is delivered/picked up.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post, always the same:  
“Menu and ordering: [link]”

⸻

## What a “business lunch” agreement means in practice (simple)
You create a fixed structure that can be repeated:

1. **Fixed order deadline** (same every week)  
2. **Fixed delivery/pickup time** (same every week)  
3. **A “lunch of the week” variant** (1–2 dishes)  
4. **A contact person** (one person who coordinates and pays as a single order at delivery/pickup)

You make it easy to say yes, because the workplace doesn’t have to plan every time.

⸻

## Why this works (and why they order again)
Workplaces order again when it feels like:
- a small luxury in a busy week
- a nice team thing without hassle
- a solution that “runs by itself”

When you make it stable and easy, you get:
- repeat orders
- higher trust
- word of mouth into other departments and other workplaces

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Define your weekly lunch rhythm (deadline + time window)  
2) Create a standard “lunch of the week” offer (1–2 dishes + 1 add-on option)  
3) Convert 1–3 workplaces into a repeating habit (simple follow-up and reminders)  
4) Build a standard order flow that takes 1 minute to execute

⸻

# Part A: Define your weekly business lunch rhythm (the stable schedule)

## Choose your weekly structure (example)
- **Order deadline:** Wednesday 10:00  
- **Delivery/pickup window:** Thursday 11:00–13:00  

Or choose a rhythm that fits you.

**Rule:** The same rhythm every week is what creates habit.

⸻

# Part B: Create “Lunch of the Week” (1–2 dishes)
This should be simple and predictable.

Example structure:
- Option 1: main dish (standard portion)
- Option 2: main dish (alternative)
- Add-on: one simple side (optional)

**Rule:** fewer choices = higher conversion.

⸻

# Part C: Standard order flow (1 minute)
The customer should be able to send one message and you know what to do.

### Standard order message (template)
> Hi! Business Lunch is open for this week.  
> Order deadline: [time]. Delivery/pickup: [time window].  
> Lunch of the Week: [option 1] / [option 2].  
> Menu and ordering: [link]

### Standard follow-up message (template)
> Hi! Just a reminder — Business Lunch order deadline is [time].  
> Menu + ordering: [link]

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Define rhythm + build Lunch of the Week offer
### Mini lesson (habits are created by repetition)
A fixed schedule is what turns “one order” into “weekly repeat.”

### Tasks
1) Choose your weekly schedule (deadline + window)  
2) Decide Lunch of the Week (1–2 dishes)

## Week 2 — Convert leads into repeat (touchpoints + follow-up)
### Mini lesson (repeat is created by follow-up)
Many workplaces need a second touch to act.

### Tasks
1) Send “order open” message to 3–5 workplaces/leads  
2) Post one business lunch post on FB/IG (simple)  
3) Put a business lunch flyer/poster in 1–2 office-related locations (coworking, office hub)  
4) Send one reminder before the deadline

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you are building B2B discovery and habit.”
- “Follow-up is what creates repeat.”

---

## Week 3 — Run the weekly cycle again (and make it easier)
### Mini lesson (make the process lighter each week)
The second cycle should feel easier than the first.

### Tasks
1) Repeat the weekly schedule (order open + reminder)  
2) Keep Lunch of the Week simple  
3) Ask the workplace:
- “Would you like this weekly?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you are turning one order into a weekly habit.”
- “Simple cycles create predictable income.”

---

## Week 4 — Stabilize + choose your “default” business lunch package
### Mini lesson (default packages create scale)
When you know your default, everything becomes faster.

### Tasks
1) Decide your default:
- Lunch for 6 / 10 (the most realistic)
2) Keep the weekly rhythm and messages  
3) Review:
- which workplaces responded?
- which message worked best?
- how will you continue next month?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now business lunch can become a recurring revenue stream.”
- “A default offer makes scaling easier.”
