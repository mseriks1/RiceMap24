# Marketing Coach – Month 31  
## Focus: “Repeat Order Week” (7-day push that brings customers back — without feeling pushy)

This month you run a simple campaign that can create a clear boost without needing new customers: **Repeat Order Week**. This is a 7-day push where you invite previous customers back in a warm, tidy way — with one clear message and one clear CTA.

The key is that this is not “spam.” It is service and reminder:
- people liked your food
- they just forgot or didn’t think of you this week
- a simple reminder + easy ordering can bring them back

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What Repeat Order Week is (in practice)
Repeat Order Week is a small campaign with three parts:

1) You choose a 7-day window (Mon–Sun, or any week)  
2) You message previous customers in small batches (not everyone at once)  
3) You support it with 3–5 posts during the week (same theme)

You are not trying to “sell hard.” You are inviting people back.

⸻

## Why Repeat Order Week works
Repeat Order Week works because:

- **Trust is already there** (they have tried you)  
- **The barrier is low** (they know what to expect)  
- **You can personalize lightly** (“your favorite is back”)  
- **A short window creates action** (“this week”)  

And importantly: repeat customers often order more easily and are more profitable than new customers.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Build your Repeat List (who you will invite)  
2) Choose one small “repeat trigger” (bonus, bundle, favorite back)  
3) Run the 7-day week with a tidy message rhythm  
4) Capture repeat behavior and learn what worked

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You choose a 7-day Repeat Order Week window  
2) You create a repeat list (at least 25 people, or as many as you have)  
3) You send messages in small batches (5–15 at a time)  
4) You post 3–5 times during the week (same theme)  
5) You track:
- how many came back
- which message angle worked best

⸻

## Fixed routine (baseline this month)
Outside the campaign, you keep the normal routine:
- 2 photo posts per week (FB + IG)  
- 2 videos per week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)

During the 7-day campaign you add:
- extra posts and reminders (light)
- more direct follow-up to customers (small batches)

⸻

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Campaign execution (70%)**: repeat list + messages + posts completed  
- **Capture & learning (30%)**: customer list updated + notes on what worked  

⸻

# Part A: Build the repeat list (simple)
Your repeat list can be:
- your Customer List in RiceMap24  
- a phone note  
- WhatsApp contacts  
- a simple spreadsheet  

What matters is that you can send messages in small batches.

Add notes if you can:
- favorite dish
- mild/spicy
- lunch interest
- “for 2” buyer

⸻

# Part B: Choose one repeat trigger (so it feels like a reason)
You need one simple trigger. Examples:
- “Favorite is back this week”
- “For 2 bundle available”
- “Small bonus add-on this week” (profitable)
- “Priority pickup slot for repeat customers”

**Rule:** keep it small and sustainable.

⸻

# Part C: The message templates (copy/paste)

## 1) Repeat Order Week invite (short)
> Hi! 😊 It’s Repeat Order Week — I’m cooking again this week.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]  
> If you want the same as last time, just write “same as last time” + preferred time.

## 2) Personal variant (favorite)
> Hi [name]! 😊 Your favorite [dish] is available this week.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]  
> (Write preferred time in the request.)

## 3) Gentle reminder (mid-week)
> Hi! Just a quick reminder — Repeat Order Week is still open 😊  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

## 4) Last call (end of week)
> Last days of Repeat Order Week 😊  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

⸻

# Part D: Optional “thank-you bonus” (only if profitable)
You can add a small bonus to make repeat feel special:
- free extra sauce
- small dessert
- free add-on after X orders (loyalty)
- priority pickup slot

**Rule:** don’t destroy your margin.

⸻

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Prepare: repeat list + trigger + assets
### Mini lesson (preparation removes stress)
A repeat week works when it is simple and planned.

### Tasks
1) Choose your 7-day window  
2) Build your repeat list (25+, or as many as you have)  
3) Choose your one repeat trigger  
4) Prepare assets (reuse library):
- 5–8 photos (menu + favorites)
- 3–5 clips (proof + appetite)
- templates saved in Notes

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now Repeat Order Week is ready to run smoothly.”
- “A clear trigger makes it feel like service, not spam.”

---

## Week 2 — Warm-up: announce that Repeat Order Week is coming
### Mini lesson (a heads-up increases response)
A small announcement makes it natural when you message customers.

### Tasks
1) Post one warm-up post:
- “Repeat Order Week starts on [date]”
2) Send a small heads-up message to the warmest segment (5–10 people)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Warm-up increases response during the campaign.”
- “Small batches feel personal.”

---

## Week 3 — Run Repeat Order Week (7 days)
### Mini lesson (repeat + clarity creates action)
During the week you repeat the same theme and make ordering easy.

### Tasks (during the 7 days)
1) Message customers in small batches (5–15/day or every other day)  
2) Post 3–5 times during the week:
- menu overview
- favorite dish
- proof/packing
- reminder
3) Reply quickly and warmly  
4) Track simple numbers:
- how many came back?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you created a boost using your existing customer base.”
- “Small batches make it personal and effective.”

---

## Week 4 — Follow-up + capture repeat behavior
### Mini lesson (repeat is built after the week)
The week creates the boost. Follow-up creates the habit.

### Tasks
1) Send a thank-you message to those who ordered:
> Thank you so much for your order! 😊  
> Menu for next week is here: [link]

2) Update Customer List:
- who returned
- what they ordered
3) Review:
- what message angle worked best?
- what will you repeat next time?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now Repeat Order Week becomes a repeat system.”
- “You learned what creates the best response.”
