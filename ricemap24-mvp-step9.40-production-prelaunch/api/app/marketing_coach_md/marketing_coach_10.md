# Marketing Coach – Month 10  
## Focus: Finish At Home Menu System (increases order value with “restaurant at home”)

This month we focus on the **Finish At Home Menu System**. That means you offer 1–2 dishes or packages the customer can easily **finish at home** in a few minutes. This is a smart way to increase order value because the customer gets a better experience (more “restaurant at home”), and you can sell larger and more complete orders without everything needing to be delivered piping hot.

---

## Natural transition from Month 9
In Month 9 you worked with **cold-ready/chilled**: products meant to be served cold, stored cold, and eaten cold (with light heating only if you say so).  
Now we take the next step: instead of “ready cold,” you offer something that is **almost finished**, and the customer does the last 5–10% at home. That often creates higher willingness to pay, because quality and “freshness” is perceived as better.

---

## What “finish at home” means in practice (very simply)
Finish-at-home means the customer gets:
- main components **ready** (sauce, protein, base)
- any toppings/herbs **separate** (for crunch/freshness)
- a very simple **2–4 step** instruction
- the customer finishes by **heating / assembling / finishing** at home

### Important: it must be clear and credible
You must explain clearly:
- what is included
- what the customer does at home
- how long it takes (honest minutes)

That builds **credibility and trust**.

---

## Why this can increase order value (without becoming pushy)
Finish-at-home sells because:
1) **The experience feels more valuable** (restaurant at home)  
2) **The customer can order bigger** (for 2 / for 4) without everything needing to be eaten right now  
3) **Quality holds up better** (less “soggy food” during delivery)  
4) **You can standardize** and make production more efficient

It also becomes easier to sell bundles:
- “For-2 kit”
- “Weekend kit”
- “Family pack”

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Choose 1–2 finish-at-home products that work well with logistics and are easy to explain  
2) Create one clear **For-2 bundle** (high order value)  
3) Market it as “easy restaurant at home” + clearly show *what you get* in photos/video

---

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Choose 1–2 finish-at-home products (start simple)
### Mini lesson: simplicity sells best
One or two clear finish-at-home choices are enough to test demand and raise average order value.

### Tasks
1) Choose 1–2 products that are truly easy to finish at home  
2) Write a short 2-line explanation for each:
- **What it is**
- **What the customer does** (2–4 steps) + time

3) Take the first visual round (minimum requirement):
- 3 photos per product
- 1 short video per product

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. One clear finish-at-home option can raise order value without more stress.”
- “Now you have visuals that make the customer understand it in seconds.”

### This week’s posting (suggestion)
- Photo post: finished result + “5–8 minutes at home” + CTA  
- Video: quick “what you get” (unpack + show components)

---

## Week 2 — Create a “For 2” finish-at-home bundle (high order value)
### Mini lesson: bundles remove friction
When you package the choice, it becomes easier to say yes. Customers don’t want too many decisions.

### Tasks
1) Create one “For 2” bundle:
- 2 main dishes, or 1 main + 1 finish-at-home kit (depending on your menu)

2) Give it a simple name:
- “For 2 – Restaurant at Home”
- “Date Night Kit”
- “Weekend Kit”

3) Create bundle visuals:
- 2 photos (everything included in one frame)
- 1 short video: “unpack → finish → final plate”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. You made it easy to order more without pressure.”
- “Bundle visuals do the selling for you.”

### This week’s posting (suggestion)
- Photo post: “For 2 kit” + what’s included + CTA  
- Video: “unpack → finish → plate” (short is fine)

---

## Week 3 — Market the value (experience, not “buy more”)
### Mini lesson: sell the result, but show the process
Finish-at-home sells best when you both:
- promise an easy experience
- show that it truly is easy

### Tasks
1) Create 2 caption templates:
- “Everything is ready — you do the final finish at home”
- “Perfect if you want that restaurant feeling at home”

2) Post at least once with a finish-at-home angle  
3) Optional: a gentle message to repeat customers:
> “This week I also have a ‘For 2 finish-at-home’ pack. Menu: [link]”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Credible language + clear visuals = higher willingness to pay.”
- “You’re selling an experience, not just food.”

### This week’s posting (suggestion)
- Video: “unpack → finish → final plate” (can be short)  
- Photo post: finished result + “5–8 minutes” + CTA

---

## Week 4 — Offline poster + stabilize as a standard offer
### Mini lesson: offline works when the message is simple
A poster works best when it communicates one clear idea. “For 2 – restaurant at home” is easy to understand offline.

### Offline (low threshold): choose 1–2 locations
Start with your location list. If you need 5 new ideas (variety):
1) Bulletin board at a **local grocery/convenience store**  
2) **Gym**  
3) Bulletin board in an **apartment building/HOA** (where allowed)  
4) **Laundry room / common area** (where allowed)  
5) **Coworking / office hub**

**Timing (as a plan):**
- Week 3: download poster + choose locations  
- Week 4: put it up (1–2 locations)

### Tasks
1) Download a finish-at-home promo poster (QR)  
2) Put it up in 1–2 locations (where allowed / ask politely)  
3) Mini evaluation:
- What questions do you get? (improve the text/steps)
- Which sells best: single product or For 2?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You now have an offer that can steadily raise your average order.”
- “Offline creates local touchpoints without more posting.”
