# Marketing Coach – Month 34  
## Focus: “Pickup & Delivery Windows” (clear time windows that reduce chaos and make you look professional)

This month you build one of the most important “operations + trust” systems in a home kitchen business: **clear pickup and delivery windows**. Many sellers get stressed because every customer asks for a custom time. That creates:
- chaos
- too many messages
- unpredictability
- burnout risk

A clear window system creates:
- calmer workdays
- better customer experience
- higher professionalism
- easier scaling

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What pickup/delivery windows mean (simple)
A pickup/delivery window is a fixed time range, for example:
- Pickup window: 16:00–18:00  
- Delivery window: 18:00–19:30  

Customers choose a time inside the window (or you assign a slot inside it). The point is that you **don’t promise any time** — you promise a window. This protects your time and creates predictability.

⸻

## Why this is important (and why customers like it)
Customers like clear windows because:
- they know what to expect
- it feels organized and safe
- they don’t have to negotiate
- they can plan their day

You like clear windows because:
- fewer messages
- less stress
- smoother delivery/pickup flow
- easier batching and packing

This is a win-win.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Choose your windows (pickup and/or delivery) that fit your life  
2) Communicate them clearly in menu, posts, and messages  
3) Use polite but firm enforcement (protect the system)  
4) Review and adjust after 4 weeks

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You define 1–2 pickup windows (and delivery windows if you offer it)  
2) You update menu text + pinned post with the windows  
3) You reduce time-related questions (customers understand)  
4) You keep the routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week  
5) You do 2 offline actions (Week 2 and Week 4) using the same window language

⸻

## Fixed routine (baseline this month)
- 2 photo posts per week (FB + IG)  
- 2 videos per week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)  
- Window clarity: mention windows in posts weekly  
- Offline actions: Week 2 and Week 4 (optional but recommended)

⸻

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Window system (60%)**: windows defined + communicated + used  
- **Execution (40%)**: weekly routine + reduced friction tracked

# Part A: Choose your windows (realistic)
Choose windows that fit your life, not just “ideal customers.”

Examples:
- Pickup: Thursday 16:00–18:00  
- Pickup: Saturday 12:00–14:00  
- Delivery: Friday 18:00–19:30  

**Rule:** start with fewer windows. Add later if demand is high.

⸻

# Part B: Put the windows everywhere (menu + pinned + templates)
This is where the system becomes real.

Add the window line to:
- RiceMap24 menu overview  
- pinned post on FB/IG  
- message templates  
- posters (offline)

Example window line:
> Pickup window: 16:00–18:00.  
> Please write your preferred pickup time in the request.

⸻

# Part C: Polite enforcement (copy/paste)
When someone asks outside the window:

> Thanks! 😊 My pickup window is 16:00–18:00 this week.  
> Could you do pickup within that window?

Keep it warm, but protect the rule.

⸻

- place/refresh a poster that includes the window line

## Week 3 — Reduce friction: fix the top time questions
### Mini lesson (every question is a system improvement)
If customers ask the same thing, your text is missing one line.

### Tasks
1) Note the top 3 time questions  
2) Update menu/pinned post to answer them  
3) Post:
- menu overview (include windows)
- one proof/packing video (include windows)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now the system gets smoother.”
- “Clarity increases trust.”

---

## Week 4 — Offline action #2 + review and adjust
### Mini lesson (small tweaks make it fit your life)
The best system is the one you can keep.

### Tasks
1) Offline action #2 (Week 4): placement/refresh

- did you get fewer time messages?
- did the window feel too wide/narrow?

- shift by 30 minutes
