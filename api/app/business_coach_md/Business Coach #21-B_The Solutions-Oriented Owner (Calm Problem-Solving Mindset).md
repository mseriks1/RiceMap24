# Business Coach #21-B — The Solutions-Oriented Owner (Calm Problem-Solving Mindset)
## How to respond to problems like a professional—without losing energy or pride

Every business has problems.

Ingredients change.
Packaging fails.
A customer is late.
A sauce leaks.
A busy day becomes chaotic.
A supplier runs out.

The difference between stressed owners and strong owners is not that strong owners have fewer problems.

It’s that strong owners solve faster—without panic.

This month is about building a solutions-oriented mindset that fits RiceMap24 owners:
- calm
- practical
- optimistic
- results-oriented
- proud

Because your job is not to avoid every problem.
Your job is to build a business that can handle problems without losing quality.

---

## 1) Solutions-oriented means “I see a lever”

A problem is rarely a wall.
It is usually a lever.

Examples:
- Spill problem → sealing standard
- Soggy texture problem → vent/separate packaging design
- Ingredient waste problem → menu focus + storage rule
- Transport chaos → supply rhythm + consolidate runs
- Customer confusion → clear boundary script
- Net flat → drift diagnosis + one lever

Solutions-oriented owners always ask:
**“What is the lever?”**

One lever is enough.

---

## 2) The calm problem-solving loop (simple steps)

When a problem happens, use this loop:

1) **Name the problem clearly** (no drama)  
2) **Find the cause** (usually one)  
3) **Choose one fix** (a standard)  
4) **Protect it for 30 days**  
5) **Compare results** (Now vs Prev)  

This turns problems into upgrades.

Upgrades compound.
Compounding is how businesses grow.

---

## 3) The “standard beats mood” mindset

Many owners solve problems emotionally:
- “Today was bad, I must change everything.”
- “That customer annoyed me, so I’ll create ten rules.”
- “One complaint means the business is failing.”

Solutions-oriented owners do the opposite:
- one change
- one standard
- one measured improvement

Because standards stay.
Mood changes.

---

## 4) The 5 problem categories owners face (and the mindset for each)

### A) Product problems (taste/texture)
Mindset:
- protect repeatability
- protect the eating moment
- redesign the product, not the customer

### B) Packaging problems (leaks, mess, soggy)
Mindset:
- “packaging is product design”
- separate, vent, seal
- choose one standard per dish type

### C) Customer problems (late, changes, misunderstandings)
Mindset:
- warm boundaries
- clarity is kindness
- protect your process to protect quality

### D) Operations problems (time, chaos, supply runs)
Mindset:
- rhythm reduces stress
- batch runs
- packaging station
- simplify menu

### E) Money problems (net flat, drift, uncertainty)
Mindset:
- diagnose drift
- protect net with one lever
- allocate cash like an owner (pay/reinvest/reserve)

Every problem has a lever.
That is the key.

---

## 5) Being solutions-oriented is also an identity

Identity matters.

If you tell yourself:
“I’m not good at business,”
then every problem feels personal.

If you tell yourself:
“I’m a solutions-oriented owner,”
then problems feel like:
**normal business upgrades.**

This identity creates calm confidence.

Solutions-oriented is not toxic positivity.
It is realistic optimism.

It says:
- “This is normal.”
- “We can fix it.”
- “One lever at a time.”
- “We learn fast.”

That is the mindset that creates long-term success.

## A practical example: turning one problem into a system upgrade

Problem:
“Customers say the crispy topping is soggy.”

Non-owner reaction:
“I should remove the dish.”

Solutions-oriented owner reaction:
- Separate the topping
- Use vented packaging where needed
- Add a simple assembly standard
- Protect the change for 30 days
- Compare repeat behavior and complaints

Now the problem becomes:
an upgrade.

That is how craft and business grow together.

## 7) The solutions-oriented language that changes everything

Owners who solve well use a different language:

Instead of:
- “This always happens.”
They say:
- “We need a standard.”

Instead of:
- “Customers are difficult.”
They say:
- “Our process needs clarity.”

Instead of:
- “I’m not good at business.”
They say:
- “This is normal. We’ll fix one lever.”

Language shapes identity.
Identity shapes action.

---

## 8) The “two-minute fix” mindset

Many problems don’t need a big meeting.
They need a small decision.

Ask:
- “What is the simplest standard that prevents this next time?”

Examples:
- spills → one cup + lid standard
- soggy crunch → separate + vent standard
- late pickup chaos → clear window + message rule
- ingredient waste → menu focus + label/date rule
- transport chaos → one supply run rhythm

One standard is a two-minute fix.
Two-minute fixes create long-term calm.

---

## 9) Solutions-oriented does not mean you do everything yourself

Being solutions-oriented also means:
- you simplify so you can delegate later
- you standardize so a helper can learn
- you build systems so the business is not trapped in your head

That is real leadership.

This is how owners last.
