## 1) Monthly overview = your “owner dashboard” for the month

In **Accounting & Insights → Monthly overview**, you select:
- Month (e.g., March)
- Year (e.g., 2027)

You’ll see the three core numbers:
- **Income**
- **Expenses**
- **Net**

This view is intentionally simple. It’s the cleanest scorecard possible.

### Month-to-date vs full month
If it’s the current month, the checkbox like **“Month-to-date (current month)”** helps you see progress without waiting until month end.

This is useful when you want to keep your hands on the wheel:
- “Are we on track?”
- “Did expenses jump early?”
- “Is net steady?”

Then you can click **Refresh** when you’ve added entries and want the newest totals.

### What “good” looks like
A simple owner goal is not “big numbers.”
A simple owner goal is **stability**:
- income isn’t random
- expenses are visible
- net becomes predictable

Predictability is what lets you plan upgrades, save money, and grow calmly.

---

## 2) “Open details” = where your month becomes understandable

The overview is the headline.  
**Open details** is the story.

When you open details, you can usually see:
- more breakdown
- **trend within the month** (when there’s enough data)
- **top dishes / top customers** (when linked)
- comparisons to the previous month

If the system says “Not enough data for a chart yet,” that’s simply a signal:
**the system is ready; it needs consistent entries.**

This is a good moment to remember the real rule:
You don’t need perfect entries—just consistent ones.

### How to “feed” the details view (without complexity)
To make the details view come alive faster, aim for this pattern:
- Log income consistently
- Log expenses consistently
- Add simple categories for expenses
- Link a customer for income when it fits
- Add dish lines for income when it fits

You can do this lightly (not for every entry), and it still becomes useful.

## 3) Comparison mode = learn faster with “Now vs Prev”

One of the most powerful parts of your Accounting & Insights module is the ability to compare periods.

In comparison view, you can choose time windows like:
- Today
- This week
- Last 30 days
- This month
- This quarter
- This year
- Custom
- All time

Then you’ll see columns like:
- Now
- Prev
- Change
- %

This is where you can start thinking like a real operator:

- “Last 30 days vs previous 30 days — did we grow?”
- “This month vs last month — what changed?”
- “This quarter vs last quarter — are we becoming stable?”
- “This year vs last year — are we building something real?”

### Why custom ranges matter (practical examples)
Custom ranges are perfect for real business moments:
- You launched a new signature dish (compare before/after)
- You switched packaging (did expenses rise, did net change?)
- You ran a special event weekend (was it worth the effort?)
- You tested an office-lunch push (did income stabilize?)
- You changed pickup windows (did it affect repeat behavior?)

Custom range comparisons help you learn fast—without emotion.

## 4) Ledger: how to enter data in a way that unlocks insights

The Ledger is where you add entries. This is the “source of truth.”

When you add a new entry, you’ll typically see fields like:

- **Date** (dd.mm.åååå)
- **Type:** Income or Expense
- **Customer (optional)**
- **Category (optional)**
- **Note (optional)**
- **Multi-line (sum to total)**
- **Lines (summed to total)** with Dish / Qty / Amount

Here is a calm way to understand each field:

### A) Type: Income or Expense
This one choice powers almost everything.

- Choose **Income** when money comes in.
- Choose **Expense** when money goes out.

Income builds your sales story.  
Expenses build your cost story.  
Net is the result.

### B) Customer (optional) — connects to your Customer List
When you choose a customer for an income entry, you link money to a real person.

This unlocks:
- top customers
- future stability patterns
- smarter decisions about who drives your business

You don’t have to link a customer every time.  
But doing it often makes your business overview stronger.

### C) Dish lines — connects money to product strategy
The “Dish (line)” section is how your ledger becomes a product compass.

When you use dish lines:
- top dishes becomes meaningful
- trends inside the month become clearer
- you learn what to cook more (and what not to overproduce)

This is especially helpful when an order includes multiple items.

### D) Multi-line (sum to total) — built for real orders
Many orders include more than one thing.

With Multi-line:
- you add multiple dish lines
- each line has dish, quantity, amount
- the system sums the total automatically
- it’s stored as one income entry

This keeps your accounting realistic and clean.

### E) Category (optional) — makes expenses readable
For expenses, simple categories make month-to-month learning easier.

A practical set:
- Ingredients
- Packaging
- Transport
- Equipment
- Other

Even if you only use categories for the big three (ingredients/packaging/transport), you’ll already get strong clarity.

### F) Note (optional) — turns numbers into memory
Notes help you remember context:
- “New dish test batch”
- “Extra packaging for event”
- “Fuel for delivery run”
- “Bought labels for packaging station”

Short notes are enough. One line is plenty.

## 5) The monthly review rhythm (light, but powerful)

Here is a clean monthly rhythm that keeps you in control without feeling heavy:

### A) Read the month like a headline
- Income
- Expenses
- Net

### B) Ask three owner questions
1) “What made this month different?”
2) “Which cost moved the most?”
3) “What should I repeat next month?”

### C) Choose one improvement (only one)
A strong owner improves one thing at a time:
- a packaging standard
- a waste reduction move
- a smoother pickup window
- one dish that becomes the anchor

Small improvements compound.

## 6) Exports (CSV) = your business memory and future-proofing

Exports are not just “files.”
Exports are confidence.

When you export your data, you do two owner moves:

1) **You build a business archive** (month by month)
2) **You keep ownership of your data** (portable, secure)

Typical exports:
- Customers CSV (your customer asset)
- Transactions CSV (your money timeline)

A practical approach is to save one folder per month:
- 2026-01
- 2026-02
- 2026-03
…and place exports there.

Even if you never open the CSV, having it saved creates a strong signal:
**“My business is real. My business is trackable.”**

## 7) Being “tax-ready” without getting complicated (and thinking across countries)

Rules differ across countries, but strong business hygiene is similar almost everywhere:

- record income
- record expenses
- store documentation where relevant (receipts, purchase confirmations)
- keep a consistent timeline

RiceMap24 supports that calm approach:
- ledger entries for income/expense
- categories to track cost drivers
- receipts feature for customer professionalism
- exports for archiving

### If you later establish a company (or operate in different VAT regimes)
When that time comes, the owners who already have:
- consistent entries
- basic categories
- an export archive
- receipts when needed

…are the ones who feel calm.

Because they don’t need to reconstruct the past.

They already have the story.

And that story is what professionals (accountants, banks, partners) understand instantly.
