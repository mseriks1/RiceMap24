# Business Coach #06-A — Accounting & Insights: Turn Entries Into Real Business Clarity
## How your ledger becomes trends, top dishes, top customers, and calm decisions

At some point, every serious home kitchen owner hits the same moment:

You’re working hard. You’re getting orders.  
But you still want to *see* what’s happening.

Not in a complicated way—just in a way that gives you calm confidence.

That’s what **Accounting & Insights** is built for in RiceMap24.

It’s not there to make you “do accounting.”  
It’s there to turn small entries you already understand into **real clarity**:

- Income vs expenses
- Net profit
- Trends inside a month
- Comparisons across time
- Top dishes
- Top customers
- Receipts (professional trust)

The best part is this:

**The insights only need simple inputs.**  
You don’t need perfect entries. You need consistent entries.

This guide shows you what you can do inside the module and how the pieces connect.

---

## The two main views: Ledger vs Monthly overview

Inside **Accounting & Insights**, you’ll see two tabs:

### 1) Ledger
Think of Ledger as your **business timeline**.

It’s where you add entries and see them as a list:
- incomes
- expenses
- (and any notes or categories you included)

Ledger is where your business truth is captured.

### 2) Monthly overview
Think of Monthly overview as your **business dashboard**.

It shows:
- **Income**
- **Expenses**
- **Net**
…for a selected month and year, and it also compares against previous periods.

Monthly overview is where your entries become meaning.

---

## Monthly overview: how to read it (and why it matters)

When you open **Monthly overview**, you’ll see:

- A month selector (e.g., January)
- A year selector (e.g., 2027)
- A checkbox like **“Month-to-date (current month)”**
- Buttons like **Refresh** and **Open details**

Then you’ll see three big boxes:

- **Income**
- **Expenses**
- **Net**

This is the cleanest “owner view” possible:
**What came in, what went out, what stayed.**

If your month is still early, Month-to-date helps you see the current month progress without waiting until the month ends.

### Compared with previous month
Below the boxes you’ll see a comparison section showing **current vs previous month**, with a simple difference view.

This is where you start thinking like an owner:
- “Is my income growing?”
- “Are my expenses creeping up?”
- “Is my net improving?”

Not emotionally. Factually.

---

## Comparison view: time periods that unlock real insight

RiceMap24 also supports comparisons across time periods.

In the Comparison view you’ll see something like:

- **Now:** a date range (e.g., 2027-02-05 → 2027-03-06)
- **Prev:** a previous range (e.g., 2027-01-06 → 2027-02-04)

And you’ll see columns like:
- **Now**
- **Prev**
- **Change**
- **%**

This is powerful because it lets you compare:
- today vs yesterday
- this week vs last week
- last 30 days vs previous 30 days
- this month vs last month
- this quarter vs last quarter
- this year vs last year
- custom ranges (when you want to compare a campaign week or a special event)

In the time range dropdown you can choose:
- Today
- This week
- Last 30 days
- This month
- This quarter
- This year
- Custom
- All time

This turns your business into something measurable—something you can improve.

## How to add entries in the Ledger (the practical part)

When you add a new entry, you’ll typically see fields like:

- **Date** (dd.mm.åååå)
- **Type:** Income or Expense
- **Customer (optional)**
- **Category (optional)**
- **Note (optional)**

And you may also see:
- **Multi-line (sum to total)**
- A section called **Lines (summed to total)** with:
  - Dish (line)
  - Qty
  - Amount
  - Total (calculated)

Here’s how this connects:

### 1) Income vs Expense (your foundation)
Choose **Income** when money comes in.
Choose **Expense** when money goes out.

That one choice powers:
- monthly totals
- comparisons
- net profit
- trends

### 2) Customer (optional) — this connects your ledger to your Customer List
When you choose a customer, you’re doing something valuable:

You’re linking income to a real person in your customer register.

That makes it possible to show:
- top customers
- repeat behavior patterns (later)
- clearer understanding of who drives stability

You don’t need to choose a customer every time.
But the more often you do, the more the system can show you.

### 3) Dish (line) — this connects your income to product strategy
The “Dish (line)” field is not just a detail.

It’s how your business starts learning which products are your true winners.

When you use dish lines, the system can build:
- top dishes lists
- trends within a month
- better clarity on what to cook more of

### 4) Multi-line (sum to total) — perfect for real-life orders
Many orders include more than one thing.

With **Multi-line** you can add multiple dish lines:
- each line has a dish, quantity, and amount
- the total is summed automatically
- it’s stored as one income entry

This is a calm owner feature:
It keeps your entries realistic without being complicated.

### 5) Category and Note (optional) — small details that improve clarity
Categories can be simple:
- Ingredients
- Packaging
- Transport
- Equipment
- Other

Notes are best when short:
- “Extra packaging for event”
- “Fuel for delivery run”
- “Office order”
- “New dish test batch”

These optional fields help you remember *why* an expense happened, or what a special income was related to.
