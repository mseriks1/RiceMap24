## Big idea (positive and powerful)

The easiest customer to sell to is the one who already bought from you.

Repeat orders are like a secret superpower:
- less marketing stress
- more stable weekly income
- customers trust you faster
- your kitchen feels “real” and consistent

You don’t need hundreds of new customers.
You need a small group of people who love your food and come back.

**Everything becomes possible when you build repeat customers.**

## Why repeat orders work so well (simple)

New customers need:
- discovery (they must find you)
- trust (they must feel safe)
- courage (first order)

Repeat customers already have trust.
So they buy with less thinking.

That means:
- fewer questions
- faster decisions
- more consistent sales

It’s also cheaper:
- you don’t need ads
- you don’t need viral videos
- you just need good food + good follow-up

---

## The “repeat order engine” (simple funnel)

Repeat orders happen when you do 3 things:

1) **Great first experience**  
   Taste + clean packing + smooth pickup

2) **Friendly connection**  
   A small thank you message makes people feel valued

3) **Simple reminder at the right time**  
   People are busy. A reminder makes them remember you

That’s the entire engine.

---

## The mindset: follow-up is service, not spam

Many sellers fear follow-up.
They think:
- “I don’t want to bother people.”

But friendly follow-up is normal in business.
It is not spam when:
- it is respectful
- it is occasional
- it helps people

Think of it like this:
> “I’m letting you know we’re cooking again, in case you want to reserve.”

Most people appreciate it.

## Step 1 — Build your VIP list (the easy way)

A VIP list is simply:
- people who bought before
- people who liked your food
- people who are likely to order again

You don’t need 200 VIPs.
Start with **10**.

### How to build it (simple)
After every order, save the customer:
- name
- message channel (WhatsApp/IG/FB)
- one note (optional): spicy/mild, favorite dish

If you have a customer list in your dashboard, great.
If not, use your phone contacts or a Notes list.

### VIP categories (optional, very helpful)
Tag people simply:
- **VIP** (orders often)
- **Big orders** (family packs)
- **Office** (team orders)
- **Spicy lovers** (helps marketing)
- **New customer** (first time)

Even 2 tags make a big difference.

---

## Step 2 — The 3-message follow-up system (simple timing)

This is the core system. It is friendly and works globally.

### Message 1: Thank you (same day)
Purpose: connection + good feeling.

### Message 2: Next cooking reminder (2–3 days before)
Purpose: reserve early and avoid “last minute”.

### Message 3: Reserve again (7–10 days after)
Purpose: repeat habit.

You can run this system even if you cook only once per week or twice per month.
Just adjust the “reminder” message to your schedule.

---

## Step 3 — Copy/paste messages (ready to use)

### Message 1 — Thank you (same day)
“Thank you for ordering ❤️  
Hope you enjoy it!  
If you want to order again next time, just DM **ORDER** 😊”

### Message 2 — Reminder (2–3 days before)
“Hi 😊 We’re cooking again on **[Day]**.  
Want me to reserve **[Dish]** for you?  
Reply **YES** or DM **ORDER** ❤️”

### Message 3 — Reserve again (7–10 days later)
“Hi! Quick question 😊  
Do you want **[Dish]** again for the next pickup?  
DM **ORDER** and I’ll reserve a portion ❤️”

### Optional VIP message (early access)
“VIP heads-up ❤️ We’re cooking again **[Day]**.  
Want an early reservation?  
DM **ORDER** 😊”

**Why these work:** short, friendly, and easy to answer.

---

## How to avoid being spammy (simple rules)

Follow-up is powerful when it is calm.

Use these rules:
- Don’t message people every day.
- 1–2 messages per week max.
- Always be polite and easy.
- If someone doesn’t reply, don’t chase.

**A respectful business always wins.**

## The “repeat order calendar” (simple)

Here is a very easy rhythm you can follow:

- **Cooking day (Day 0):** Thank you message
- **Day 4–5:** reminder for next cooking day
- **Day 7–10:** reserve again message

If you cook on weekends, this fits well.
If you cook every 2 weeks, send the reminder earlier.

## How to increase repeat orders with tiny upgrades

Repeat orders grow when the first experience feels great.

Here are the best upgrades:

### 1) Clean, consistent packing
- clean box
- neat label (optional)
- sauce cup closed properly
This creates trust.

### 2) Portion consistency
If the portion is consistent, customers feel safe ordering again.

### 3) One add-on remembered
If a customer loves extra rice or sauce, remember it.
Then next time you can ask:
“Want extra rice again?”  
That feels personal and premium.

### 4) Save the best customers
Your best customers are your business foundation.
Treat them well.

VIP means:
- people who buy again

### How to make VIP feel special (easy)

## Turning repeat customers into referrals (bonus)

Repeat customers can bring you new customers.

A simple message you can use sometimes:

## What to do when you are sold out (useful)

Being sold out is a good sign.
But it can create disappointment.

Use a simple waitlist message:
“Hi 😊 Today we are fully booked.  
Want me to put you on the list for the next pickup? ❤️”

This protects future sales.

## The “office repeat” secret (big boost)

Office orders can become weekly habits.
If you get one office customer:
- ask them gently: “Want to order together next week too?”
- offer a simple group pack

Office repeat orders create stability fast.

## A simple “after pickup” message that feels premium

Right after pickup (same day), send:

“Thank you ❤️ Hope you enjoy!  
If anything is not perfect, tell me — I want to improve 😊”

- builds trust (you care)

Most customers will reply with something positive.

## The 3 best moments to ask for a review (without pressure)

Reviews are fuel for growth.
But asking at the wrong time can feel awkward.

Ask at these moments:

1) When a customer says “so good”  
Reply:
“Thank you ❤️ If you have 10 seconds, a 1-line review helps a lot 😊”

2) After a repeat order  
They already trust you, so it feels natural.

3) After a “campaign week”  
Customers expect you to be more professional, so reviews fit well.

Keep the review ask small and optional.

You don’t need complicated CRM.
Just these 4 segments are enough:

- **VIP Regulars:** order often
- **Family Pack:** buy bigger packs

This is pro-level thinking, but very simple.

## Reorder timing: how to choose the best day

Different kitchens cook on different schedules.
So here is an easy rule:

### If you cook weekly (example: weekends)
- Thank you same day
- Reminder midweek (Wed/Thu)
- Reserve again 7 days later

### If you cook every 2 weeks
- Thank you same day
- Reminder 4–5 days before next cooking
- Reserve again around day 10–14

### If you cook only sometimes
Use the VIP list when you decide to cook:
- send one “we’re cooking again” message
- reserve early
- then thank you

The system is flexible. You control it.
