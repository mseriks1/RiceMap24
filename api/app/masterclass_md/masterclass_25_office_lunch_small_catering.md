## Why offices buy (simple psychology)

Offices buy food when:
- it solves a problem (“What do we eat today?”)
- it feels easy and safe
- it arrives on time (or pickup is clear)
- it looks professional
- it feeds many people

They are not looking for the cheapest.
They are looking for:
- convenience

## The simple catering model (home kitchen friendly)

To keep it realistic, use this model:

- **One signature tray** (your hero dish)
- **One safe tray** (banker dish: noodles/wok/rice)
- **Two sides** (spring rolls, rice, sauce cups, dessert)

That’s a full catering pack.
Simple. Repeatable.

---

## Step 1 — Choose your “catering-friendly” dishes

Catering dishes must be:
- batch-friendly
- stable (still tastes good later)
- easy to portion
- safe to transport

### Great catering dish examples (adapt to your cuisine)
- fried rice / noodle trays
- curry trays
- stir-fry trays
- dumplings / spring rolls
- adobo / braised dishes
- bun bowls (components packed separately)
- pho broth (separate containers)

Avoid dishes that die fast (unless you are confident):
- super crispy items without proper packing
- delicate plating-only dishes

You can still include crispy items as sides if packed well.

---

## Step 2 — Create 3 simple offers (the “menu”)

This is the most important part.
Offices don’t want a long menu.
They want clear packages.

### Offer A: Team Lunch Pack (6–10 people)
Includes:
- 1 main tray (hero or banker)
- 1 side tray (rice/noodles)
- 1 side order (spring rolls / dumplings)
- sauce cups

### Offer B: Office Party Pack (10–20 people)
Includes:
- 2 main trays (hero + banker)
- 2 sides (rice + rolls)
- sauce cups

### Offer C: Event Tray Pack (20–30 people) (optional)
Includes:
- 2–3 main trays
- 2–3 sides
- optional dessert tray

You can start with only Offer A and B.
That is enough to build a business.

---

## Step 3 — Price it simply (no stress)

Pricing catering can feel scary.
So use a simple rule:

### Rule 1: Price per person
Example:
- 6–10 people: price per person = [X]
- 10–20 people: price per person = [X] (same or slightly lower)

Then:
Total price = price per person × number of people

This is easy for office buyers to understand.

### Rule 2: Add a “packing + handling” buffer
Catering needs extra packing and planning.
Add a simple buffer:
- +5–10% on the total
or
- +[Fixed amount] for packing

### Rule 3: Keep the offer premium and clean
Offices pay more if:
- packaging is neat
- trays are clean
- ordering is simple

You are selling convenience.

---

## Step 4 — Make ordering extremely easy

Office buyers are busy.
So make ordering feel like one message.

### Best ordering method
- DM “ORDER”
or
- send a simple form message (if you later build it)

For now, keep it simple:
“Tell me: date, number of people, spicy level.”

### The 3 questions you must ask
1) What date / time?
2) How many people?
3) Mild or spicy?

That’s enough to confirm.

## Step 6 — Delivery vs pickup (keep it realistic)

Delivery can be great, but only if you can do it calmly.

### Option A: Pickup only (simple)
- one pickup time window
- office sends one person

### Option B: Limited delivery window (advanced)
- deliver only once per week
- only within a small area
- add a delivery fee

Start with pickup only if you want simplicity.
You can add delivery later.

## Step 7 — Make it repeat (the real magic)

One office order is nice.

How to make it repeat:

“Thank you ❤️ If your team wants another lunch pack next week, just tell me the day and number of people 😊”

That’s it.

## “Portion math” for trays (so you feel confident)

Many kitchens worry:
“How do I know how much to cook?”

Use this simple guide (adjust to your dish):

- **10 people:** plan for 10 “normal portions”
- **20 people:** plan for 20 “normal portions”
- **30 people:** plan for 30 “normal portions”

If your cuisine is usually served with rice/noodles:
- rice/noodles are your “filler” and can be cooked in volume easily
- this makes trays more profitable

### Simple tray breakdown (example for 10 people)
- Main tray: 6–7 portions of protein dish
- Rice/noodles tray: 10 portions
- Side tray: 10–20 pieces (rolls/dumplings)
- Sauces: 10 cups

Why this works:
- people mix
- everyone feels full
- cost stays controlled

You can adjust: more main tray if your customers expect more protein.
