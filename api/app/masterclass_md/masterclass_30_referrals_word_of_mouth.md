The fastest marketing is not always social media.

- trust spreads
- customers feel safe
- repeat orders increase

## Why word-of-mouth works better than ads (simple)

People trust people.

A friend’s recommendation feels:
- safe
- honest
- local
- personal

Ads can be ignored.
But a friend’s message gets attention.

This is why “micro networks” are powerful:
- families
- friends
- coworkers
- neighborhood groups
- diaspora communities (Filipino/Thai/Vietnamese/Chinese communities abroad)

If one person shares you inside a community, you can get many orders quickly.

## The referral funnel: Delight → Share → New customer → Repeat

Word-of-mouth has a simple funnel:

1) Customer has a great experience (taste + clean packing + smooth pickup)
2) Customer feels proud and happy
3) Customer shares you (story, group, friend message)
4) A new customer orders
5) You convert them into repeat customers (VIP system)

This is a loop.
The goal is to create the loop on purpose.

---

## Step 1 — Make your kitchen easy to share (very important)

People share when it is easy.

Your “share kit” should include:
- your kitchen name (simple)
- your city/area
- your “DM ORDER” line
- your RiceMap24 link or QR (optional)

If people need to explain too much, they won’t share.

### The simplest share line
“Local home kitchen in [Area]. DM ORDER ❤️”

### The simplest share asset
A screenshot of your RiceMap24 listing or your best food post.
Screenshots are how people share in real life.

---

## Step 2 — The “share moments” (when sharing happens naturally)

Don’t ask for referrals randomly.
Ask at the best moments:

### Best moment A: right after pickup
Customer is happy and excited.

### Best moment B: after they say “so good!”
They are already praising you.

### Best moment C: after a repeat order
They trust you and want you to win.

These are the moments where sharing feels natural.

---

## Step 3 — How to ask for referrals (without being pushy)

A good referral ask is:
- short
- optional
- grateful

### The golden referral line
“If you know someone who would love this food, feel free to share ❤️ Thank you!”

That’s it.

Avoid pressure.
Pressure kills sharing.

---

## Copy/paste referral messages (use these)

### A) After pickup (simple)
“Thank you ❤️ Hope you enjoy!  
If you know someone who loves [Cuisine] food near [Area], feel free to share 😊”

### C) After repeat order (VIP)

### D) Share message customer can forward (very useful)

Homemade [Cuisine] food — super delicious.  
DM ORDER ❤️”

### E) Story caption (for customers to post)
“Local home kitchen ❤️ DM ORDER  
(RiceMap24)”

Simple and shareable.

---

## Step 4 — Create a “mini referral reward” (optional)

You don’t need big discounts.
But small rewards can increase sharing.

### Referral rewards that work well
- Free add-on for the referrer (next order)
- Free sauce cup / extra rice
- VIP early reservation
- “Thank you gift” once in a while

Keep it simple and controllable.

Example:
“Refer a friend who orders once → you get a free spring roll add-on next time.”

This is easy and doesn’t destroy profit.

## Step 5 — Community loops (how kitchens spread fast)

Word-of-mouth spreads fastest in communities.

Examples:
- apartment buildings
- gyms
- churches/faith groups
- sports clubs
- ethnic communities abroad
- parent groups (if allowed)

The key is: **one champion inside the group**.

One person posts:
“Try this kitchen.”
Then others follow.

### How to find a champion (simple)
Ask a happy customer:
“Do you have a group chat in your building/workplace? If yes, feel free to share 😊”

Many people will share if you ask kindly.

## Step 6 — Facebook/WhatsApp group strategy (referral version)

This is not your own posting.
This is customers sharing.

### The best way
- give customers a short message they can copy/paste
- give them a photo or screenshot
- give them the link/QR

## Why “warm referrals” beat cold marketing (psychology)

Cold marketing is when strangers see you for the first time.
They have doubts:
- “Is this safe?”
- “Is it worth it?”
- “Will it taste good?”

Warm referrals skip most doubts because trust is transferred.

A referral is like a bridge:
- the friend is the bridge
- the new customer crosses the bridge
- ordering feels safer

This is why referral customers often:
- order faster
- ask fewer questions
- reorder sooner

Word-of-mouth is not only growth.
It is also **better quality customers**.

## The “share friction” rule (the #1 reason referrals fail)

Referrals fail when sharing feels hard.

Common friction points:
- customer must explain too much
- customer doesn’t know your pickup area
- customer doesn’t know how to order
- customer doesn’t want to write long messages

Your solution is always:
**short forward pack + one photo + one action**

Make sharing easier than doing nothing.

## Group etiquette (how to get referrals without annoying people)

Groups can be sensitive.
Even when customers share, some groups dislike “selling”.

So keep it respectful:

- share rarely (not every week)
- use friendly tone
- use one photo
- keep message short
- respect group rules

If your customer shares and someone complains, that’s okay.
Over time, happy customers and good food win.

A good group message feels like a recommendation, not an ad.

Example:
“Hi 😊 I tried this local home kitchen and it was really good.  
If anyone wants homemade [Cuisine] food, DM ORDER ❤️”

That feels like a friend, not a seller.

## What to do when referrals bring many new DMs (good problem)

- limit portions if needed

Selling out is also a marketing signal.
