## Big idea (positive and powerful)

Marketing is not about posting.
Marketing is about **being remembered**.

The problem is:
most home kitchens are busy.
So marketing becomes random:
- one week you post a lot
- then you disappear
- then you feel guilty
- then you try again

AI can help, but only if you use it the right way.

Because AI can create a lot of content.
But “a lot” is not the goal.

The goal is:
**simple, consistent, human content that connects to orders.**

When you use AI well, it becomes:
- your idea generator
- your script drafter
- your caption assistant
- your repurposing engine
- your planning buddy

So you can spend more time cooking and less time staring at your phone.

This masterclass will show you a clean system.

---

## What AI should NOT do in your marketing

This is important.

AI should not:
- write fake emotional stories
- invent customer reviews
- pretend you are someone you are not
- create spam captions with 20 hashtags and hype language
- push discounts all the time

If your content feels fake, trust goes down.
And in food business, trust is everything.

So we use AI like this:

**AI builds structure. You bring the human.**

## The AI Marketing System (simple model)

Use this 5-step pipeline:

1) **Offer** (what you are selling this week)  
2) **Story** (why it matters / why it’s special)  
3) **Content** (video/photo formats)  
4) **CTA** (how to order, pickup window, limited slots)  
5) **Reuse** (turn 1 idea into 5 pieces of content)

This pipeline keeps you focused on sales, not random posting.

## Step 1 — Define your weekly offer (the anchor)

Before you make content, define the offer.

A weekly offer can be:
- your signature dish + pickup window
- a family pack / tray
- a limited drop
- a “new customers welcome” simple message
- an add-on bundle

### Prompt: Weekly offer clarity (copy/paste)
> Using my Business Brief, help me define a simple weekly offer for this week.  
> Include: menu focus, pickup window, max orders, and one short “why now” line (limited slots or weekly pickup).  
> Keep it warm and simple.

If the offer is clear, content becomes easy.

---

## Step 2 — Build your content pillars (so you never run out of ideas)

Most kitchens need only 4 pillars:

1) **Food close-ups** (taste + quality)
2) **Behind the scenes** (trust)
3) **How it works** (clarity)
4) **Happy customers / proof** (trust signals)

### Prompt: Content pillars (copy/paste)
> Create 4 content pillars for my home kitchen brand.  
> For each pillar, give me 10 video ideas that fit TikTok/IG Reels.  
> Keep ideas simple and realistic for a busy person.

---

## Step 3 — Turn ideas into short scripts (15–30 seconds)

You don’t need long videos.
Short, clear videos work best.

A great short video usually has:
- a hook
- 2–3 quick scenes
- a simple CTA

### Prompt: Short video script generator (copy/paste)
> Write 5 short video scripts (15–30 sec) for my weekly offer.  
> Each script should include: hook, 3 shots, and a simple CTA (pickup window + how to order).  
> Tone: warm, human, local. No hype.

## Step 4 — Make a filming plan (so content fits real life)

Filming becomes easy when it is attached to what you already do:
- shopping
- prep
- cooking
- packing
- pickup

### The “film while doing” rule
Don’t create extra work.
Film 5 seconds here and there.

### Prompt: Filming plan (copy/paste)
> Create a filming plan for one cooking day.  
> Suggest 10 quick shots I can film while I cook and pack (no extra setup).  
> Then map those shots into 3 short videos + 5 story posts.

This creates a week of content from one day.

## Step 5 — Captions that sound human (not AI)

Captions should be short and clear.
And they must match your voice.

### Prompt: Human captions (copy/paste)
> Write 10 caption options for my weekly offer.  
> Tone: warm, simple, local, confident.  
> No corporate language. No hype.

## Step 6 — CTAs that convert (without being pushy)

A CTA can be simple:

- “Pickup Saturday 16–18”
- “Limited to 25 orders”

Clarity is not pushy.
Clarity is helpful.

### Prompt: CTA library (copy/paste)

> Tone: warm and helpful.

## Step 8 — AI for brand consistency (so everything feels like YOU)

This is the secret.

You need a “Brand Voice Brief” like you have a Business Brief.

### Brand Voice Brief (copy/paste template)

> **My brand vibe:** warm, local, trustworthy, simple.  
> **My language:** simple English, short sentences.  
> **My values:** quality, cleanliness, consistency, respect.  
> **My style:** helpful, not pushy. No hype.  
> **My audience:** local customers who want easy meals.  
> **My CTA style:** clear pickup window + simple ordering.  
> **Avoid:** corporate buzzwords, fake emotions, exaggerated claims.

Paste this when AI writes captions and scripts.

## Step 9 — Connect content to real sales (the sales loop)

Your sales loop should be:

1) Post the offer  
2) Explain how ordering works  
3) Remind before cutoff  
4) Show behind the scenes  
5) Pickup day updates  
6) Reviews / thank you  
7) Repeat next week

AI helps you build that loop.

> Keep it realistic.

## Common AI marketing mistakes (and quick fixes)

1) **Posting too much**
Fix: 3 reels + stories is enough.

2) **No offer clarity**
Fix: define weekly offer first.

3) **Captions feel fake**
Fix: use Brand Voice Brief + edit lightly.

4) **No CTA**
Fix: clarity is friendly.

5) **No reuse**
Fix: repurpose from one cooking day.

6) **Chasing trends only**
Fix: trends can help, but your system must work without them.

If you want better output from AI, your prompt matters.

> Goal: orders for Saturday pickup.

## How to avoid AI content that feels fake (the trust checklist)

Before you post, do a quick trust check.

Ask:
- Is this true?
- Is this my voice?
- Would I say this in real life?
- Does it feel helpful or pushy?
- Does it make ordering clearer?

If the answer is no, edit it.

### Simple edit rules
- remove hype words (“amazing”, “best ever”, “unbelievable”)
- replace claims with specifics (“slow cooked”, “packed fresh”, “limited slots”)
- keep it short
- keep it human
