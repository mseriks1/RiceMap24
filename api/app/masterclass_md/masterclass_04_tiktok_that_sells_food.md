## Why TikTok works for home kitchens (simple explanation)

TikTok is powerful because it can show your video to people **near you** who do not follow you yet.

For home kitchens, that is perfect:
- You want local customers.
- You want people who discover you for the first time.
- You want “new regulars” (repeat customers).
- You want to grow without spending money on ads.

TikTok rewards videos that:
- make people watch longer (satisfying cooking)
- make people rewatch (quick loops)
- make people comment (simple question)
- make people share (friends love sharing food videos)

You don’t need 1 million views.  
You need the right people in your area to see you consistently.

## TikTok algorithm (simple) — and why it’s a great platform for home kitchens

TikTok is different from Instagram. On TikTok, you don’t need a big follower count to get results.
TikTok can show your video to people who do not follow you yet. That is why TikTok is a **discovery platform**.

Here is the simple idea:
TikTok tests your video with a small group of people. If they react well, TikTok shows it to more people.

### What TikTok usually “likes” (in simple words)

1) **Watch time (most important)**  
If people watch your video longer (or watch twice), TikTok thinks: “This is interesting.”

2) **Completion rate**  
If many people watch to the end, TikTok is more likely to push it.

3) **Replays (loops)**  
Food videos are perfect for this. When the end looks like the beginning, people rewatch without thinking.

4) **Saves and shares**  
When someone saves your video (“I want to order later”) or shares it to a friend, TikTok sees strong value.

5) **Comments + replies**  
A simple question like “Mild or spicy?” can create comments. Comments tell TikTok the video is engaging.

6) **Consistency (account health)**  
If you post regularly (even once per week), your account looks active. Active accounts often get more chances.

### Why TikTok is extra interesting for RiceMap24 kitchens

- **Local discovery:** Many viewers are nearby (especially when your content is local and your profile says your area).  
- **Food performs well:** People love watching cooking, sauce pours, sizzling pans, and close-ups.  
- **Trust building:** Home kitchens can show clean packing and real cooking — it feels authentic.  
- **Fast feedback:** You learn quickly what people like because views and comments come fast.  
- **One video = many platforms:** TikTok videos can be reused on Reels and Shorts. This saves time.

### A simple TikTok strategy that works (no stress)

Think in 3 lanes. Rotate them:

**Lane A — Hunger (make people crave):**  
Close-ups, steam, sauce pour, crunch, “my favorite bite”.

**Lane B — Trust (make people feel safe):**  
Clean counter, packing orders, portion proof, hands cooking.

**Lane C — Action (make ordering easy):**  
DM “ORDER”, simple menu photo in another post, quick replies.

If you rotate these three lanes, your content stays fresh, and customers get the full picture:
**delicious + trustworthy + easy to order**.

Remember: You don’t need viral. You need consistent discovery in your area.

## Hashtags (simple) — and how to reach people near you (RiceMap24 style)

Hashtags are not magic, but they help TikTok understand **what your video is about** and **who might like it**.
Think of hashtags like “labels” on your content.

**Important:** Hashtags alone won’t make you viral.  
Your hook + food visuals matter most.  
But good hashtags can help you get discovered — especially by people who are searching.

### How TikTok discovery really happens (easy)
People find your video through:
- the For You feed (TikTok testing your video)
- searches (“pad thai”, “dumplings”, “homemade food”)
- watching similar videos (TikTok groups food content)
- local interest (when your content + profile signals your area)

Hashtags help with **search** and **topic grouping**.

### The best hashtag strategy (simple and safe)
Use **6–12 hashtags**. Not 30.  
Pick a mix from these groups:

1) **Cuisine (1–3 hashtags)**

2) **Dish (1–2 hashtags)**

3) **Home kitchen / homemade (1–2 hashtags)**  
Examples: #HomeCooking #HomemadeFood #HomeKitchen #SmallBusiness

This is the most important for local sales.
Examples (choose your city/area):  
#Oslo #OsloFood #OsloEats #Bergen #Trondheim #Stavanger  
Or: #[YourArea] #[YourArea]Food (if people use it)

5) **General food TikTok (1–2 hashtags)**  
Examples: #FoodTok #Foodie #DinnerIdeas

That’s enough.

## The simple framework: TikTok in 5 steps (the RiceMap24 way)

### Step 1) Hook (0–2 seconds)
Start with the best moment:
- finished dish close-up
- steam
- sauce pour
- crunchy bite
- sizzling pan

**Rule:** never start with an empty table.

### Step 2) Satisfy (2–12 seconds)
Show the most satisfying cooking moments:
- stir, flip, chop, simmer
- sauce mixing
- noodles lifting
- dumplings steaming

### Step 3) Proof (12–20 seconds)
Show trust:
- clean hands
- clean counter
- packing boxes
- portion shot

### Step 4) Reward (20–28 seconds)
Show the final beauty shot again:
- close-up finished dish
- slow pan
- sprinkle topping

### Step 5) Action (last 2 seconds)
Make ordering simple:
- on-screen: **DM “ORDER”**
- caption ends with: **DM “ORDER”**

That’s the full system.

## How to create a “loop” (TikTok secret for food)

A loop is when the end of the video feels like the beginning.  
People rewatch without thinking. That increases views.

Simple loop tricks:
- start with the final dish close-up
- end with the final dish close-up
- keep clips short (1–3 seconds)
- use a steady rhythm (not chaotic)

**Food loops are powerful because they feel satisfying.**

## A deeper guide: what makes a food video “sell” (simple, but powerful)

Think of your video like a tiny restaurant window.

If the window looks good, people come inside.
If the window looks unclear, people keep walking.

A selling food video does 3 jobs:

### Job 1: Create hunger
Hunger comes from:
- close-up texture (crispy, juicy, saucy)
- steam (fresh)
- movement (stir, flip, pour)
- contrast (bright herbs, sauce, toppings)

### Job 2: Create trust
Trust comes from:
- clean and calm visuals
- neat packaging
- a small “human” moment (hands, smile)
- consistency (same style often)

### Job 3: Create a next step
A next step must be easy.
That is why **DM “ORDER”** is perfect for beginners.

## How to use your phone camera (simple settings)

You do NOT need to change many settings.

If you want an easy upgrade:
- wipe your camera lens before filming
- use 1x lens (not wide 0.5x, it can distort food)
- tap the food on screen to focus
- lower brightness a little if highlights look white

If your video looks too yellow:
- film closer to a window
- turn off strong ceiling lights (if possible)

This alone can make your food look 30% better.
