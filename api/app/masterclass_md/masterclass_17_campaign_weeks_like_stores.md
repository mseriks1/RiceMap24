## What is a campaign week? (simple)

A campaign week is a one-week “special push”.
It can be:
- a signature dish week
- an office lunch week
- a party pack week
- a free taste/sample week
- a spice choice week

The key is:
**one clear theme + one clear action**.

Campaign weeks work best when you keep the menu simple and repeat one message.

---

## Why campaigns work (simple psychology)

Campaigns work because they create:

1) **Urgency**
“Only this week.”

2) **Clarity**
“This is the offer.”

3) **Excitement**
“Something special is happening.”

4) **Social sharing**
People share specials with friends.

Campaigns give customers a reason to act now, not later.

---

## The campaign week funnel: See → Desire → DM → Order → Repeat

A campaign week should:
- grab attention (video/photo)
- build desire (close-up, steam)
- build trust (packing proof)
- make action easy (DM ORDER)
- create repeat (thank you + reminder)

Campaigns are not separate from business.
They connect to repeat orders.

---

## The 7-day campaign structure (use this every time)

This structure is simple and repeatable.

### Day 1 — Launch (announce)
- announce the theme
- show hero dish photo/video
- DM ORDER

### Day 2 — Reach (share / group / partner)
- post in one local group OR
- ask one partner OR
- send to friends

### Day 3 — Trust (packing / portion proof)
- show portion in box
- show clean packing line

### Day 4 — Upgrade (add-on / bundle)
- promote one add-on or family pack

### Day 5 — Reminder (keep it alive)
- story reminder
- “still available”

### Day 6 — Last call (urgency)
- “last chance this week”
- DM ORDER

### Day 7 — Thank you (repeat engine)
- thank customers
- ask for review
- invite reorder next week

This is the full campaign system.
You can repeat it monthly.

## How to run campaigns without burnout (the “minimum viable campaign”)

Many people think campaigns mean a lot of work.
But your best campaign is simple and realistic.

If you are busy, your minimum campaign is:

- Day 1: one launch post
- Day 3: one trust post (packing/portion)
- Day 6: one last call post
- Day 7: thank you message

That’s only 4 posts.
And it can still boost sales.

Campaigns are about clarity, not volume.

---

## The campaign “offer” secret (avoid discounts)

Stores use discounts, but home kitchens don’t have to.

Instead of discounts, use:
- limited time
- limited quantity
- bundles (bigger orders)
- add-on spotlight
- VIP early access

These feel special and protect profit.

### Example: limited quantity
“Small batch — limited portions available.”

This creates urgency without lowering price.

### Example: VIP early access
“VIP heads-up: reserve early.”

This creates loyalty and fast orders.

## “Free mini taste” campaign (how to do it safely and smart)

This campaign can be powerful if done correctly.

### The safe way
- only small sample (1 bite / small cup)
- only for first-time customers
- limited quantity

### Why it works
- lowers fear (“let me try first”)
- creates conversation and trust

### How to message it
“First-time customers: free mini taste (limited) ❤️ DM ORDER”

Do not make it too big.
Keep it controlled.

## Troubleshooting (quick fixes)

### “Campaign didn’t get many orders”
Fix:
- choose a clearer offer next time (family pack or add-on)
- add more trust posts (packing/portion)
- make DM action clearer

### “People like the post but don’t DM”
Fix:
- DM ORDER on screen + caption
- reply faster
- offer 1–2 clear options only

### “I felt stressed during the week”
Fix:
- run the minimum viable campaign (4 posts only)
- prepare posts in advance (save captions)
- keep menu simple

Campaigns should feel energizing, not draining.

## How to measure a campaign (simple, no spreadsheets)

You don’t need analytics tools.
Just track these 3 numbers:

1) How many DMs did you get?
2) How many orders did you get?
3) Did any new customers reorder after?

That’s enough to know if the campaign worked.

## 5 more campaign types (easy and effective)

You already have many campaign themes in your system.
Here are 5 more that are easy to execute:

### 1) “Spice Choice Week”
Offer mild / medium / hot.
Ask a question in captions:
“Mild or spicy?”
This creates comments and more reach.

### 2) “Add-on Upgrade Week”
Spotlight one add-on that increases order value:
- spring rolls / dumplings
- extra rice
- dessert
Keep it visible in photos.

### 3) “Culture Spotlight Week”
Introduce one traditional dish with one simple line:
“[Dish] — traditional [Cuisine] comfort food.”
This makes your kitchen unique and memorable.

### 4) “Review Harvest Week”
Ask for reviews politely.
Post one review screenshot and say thank you.
This campaign builds trust for months after.

### 5) “Partner Takeover Week”
Focus on one partner spot.
Place QR cards and also shout them out in your story:
“Thank you [Partner] ❤️”
Partners often appreciate this and keep your cards longer.
