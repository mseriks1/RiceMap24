# Marketing Coach – Month 28  
## Focus: Gift cards + loyalty cards (punch card) printed from the dashboard that creates repeat, referrals, and higher order value

This month you build a simple system that makes your customers come back — and bring new customers — without you having to “push marketing.” You do it with two local classics that always work:

1) **Gift cards** (private + business)  
2) **Loyalty cards / punch cards** (bonus/reward after X purchases)

This fits perfectly for home kitchens because it is:
- easy to understand for customers  
- easy to use in practice (paper/QR)  
- easy to sell both online and offline  
- easy to implement without payment inside the order itself  

You don’t have to change the food you make. You simply build a smart “return system” around the food you already sell.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post, always the same:  
“Menu and ordering: [link]”

⸻

## What gift cards mean in practice (simple)
Gift cards are an easy way for others to give “food as a gift” — privately or in a company.

**Private gift cards** are typically used for:  
- birthday, thank-you, “I thought of you”  
- help for new parents  
- “dinner as a gift” to family/friends  

**Business gift cards** are typically used for:  
- thank-you to employees  
- small prize/bonus  
- celebrating a milestone  
- customer/partner thank-you  

You make it easy to say yes because the recipient doesn’t have to choose in the moment — they can choose when it fits.

⸻

## What loyalty cards mean in practice (simple)
Loyalty cards are “come back and get a bonus.” It’s one of the most effective local ways to create habits.

What makes loyalty cards extra smart in RiceMap24 is that you can run it in two ways at the same time:
- **In the system** (customer list / history) for verification when needed  
- **In the hand** (the customer’s card) as a reminder and habit builder  

The goal is not control. The goal is a friendly habit that creates repeat.

⸻

# Part A: Gift cards (private + business)

## Step 1: Choose 3 gift card amounts (simple)
Pick 3 price points that match your menu level, for example:
- $25  
- $50  
- $100  

(Adjust to your market and pricing.)

**Rule:** Keep it simple. Too many options reduces buying.

## Step 2: Decide how gift cards are delivered (realistic)
Since you don’t have payment built into the order itself, you keep it simple:
- customer pays via your standard method (Vipps / etc.)  
- you send a gift card code/message  
- or you hand over a printed card

**Gift card “code” can be simple:**
- KitchenName-25-001  
- KitchenName-50-014  

(You track it in your notes/customer list.)

## Step 3: Gift card copy (copy/paste)
**Short message (private):**
> Gift cards are now available 😊  
> Perfect as a small thank-you or “dinner as a gift.”  
> Menu and ordering: [link]

**Short message (business):**
> Business gift cards available for employees and teams.  
> A simple local thank-you gift.  
> Menu and ordering: [link]

⸻

# Part B: Loyalty card / punch card (repeat engine)

## Step 1: Choose one loyalty rule you can afford
Choose a rule that protects your margin. Examples:
- “Buy 5 times → get a free add-on”  
- “Buy 6 times → get $10 off next order”  
- “Buy 8 times → get one dish at 50%”  

**Rule:** Keep it easy to understand and easy to deliver.

## Step 2: Make the loyalty card feel “friendly”
This must feel like a positive bonus, not a strict control system.

Example line:
> Loyalty bonus: after X orders you get [reward]. Thank you for supporting a small kitchen 😊

## Step 3: How to verify (system + hand)
- Customer has the physical card (stamps/marks)  
- You also note it in the Customer List (as backup)

The point is not to “catch people.” The point is that you can keep it tidy if needed.

⸻

# Part C: Printing and use (dashboard routine)

## Print routine (simple)
1) Print a small stack of gift cards + loyalty cards  
2) Keep them ready for every pickup/delivery  
3) Hand them out with one sentence:
> “Here’s a loyalty card — after X orders you get a small bonus 😊”

## One “in-order” habit (fixed)
After each order:
- send thank-you message  
- mention gift card/loyalty lightly (one line)

Example:
> Thank you so much for your order! 😊  
> If you want to order again, the menu is here: [link]  
> (PS: Gift cards and loyalty bonus are available.)

2) Mention gift cards in one story/post

## Week 4 — Offline action #2 + review and tighten the system

If the reward is too expensive, adjust. If it’s too weak, simplify the message.

### Tasks

2) Review:

- is the reward sustainable?

- reward size
- threshold (X purchases)

- “Now you have a system that creates repeat and referrals.”
- “You tightened it so it stays profitable.”
