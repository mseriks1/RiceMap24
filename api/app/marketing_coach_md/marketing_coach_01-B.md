# Marketing Coach – Month 1
## Focus: Make your RiceMap24 page sales-ready

This month we focus on making your **RiceMap24 page sales-ready**. Before you market widely on social media or with offline actions, your “store” on RiceMap24 must create two things in seconds: **trust** and **desire to buy**. The goal right now is not perfection — the goal is to get started and put everything in place so that a customer who clicks in actually understands what you sell, feels tempted, and sees that it’s easy to order.

### Why this focus now (and why it leads to more sales)
When a customer scrolls and sees your kitchen, the evaluation happens fast:
- **Does this look real and safe?**
- **Does the food look tempting?**
- **Do I understand what I’m getting?**
- **Do I understand how to order?**

If one of these is missing, you can lose orders even if the food is amazing. That’s why the first step is to make the page clear and “sales-friendly” — so all later marketing points to a place that actually converts.

### Goals this month
1) **First impression:** a hero/front-page image that makes people click and stay  
2) **Desire:** tempting food photos (real photos, not AI)  
3) **Clarity:** simple, good food copy that describes correctly + ingredients/allergens  
4) **Low friction:** the customer understands ordering without having to ask much

### Minimum vs Bonus
**Minimum (for brand-new sellers):**
- 1 hero/front-page image
- 1 signature dish
- 1 “banker”/easy-selling main dish
- 1 side (listed as an **add-on together with the dish**)
- Text on these dishes (tempting + ingredients/allergens)

**Bonus (if you have capacity):**
- 4 main dishes (including the signature dish)
- 2 sides (as add-ons on the dishes)
- Text on everything (same format)

### Progress in the dashboard
Two tracks (two colors):
- **Setup (profile/menu/text): 50%**
- **Photos (hero + dishes): 50%**
After each task you press “Done” and get a short confirmation of why it helps.

## Week 1 — Get started: a starter menu that sells
### Mini lesson (why a simple menu wins in the beginning)
When you’re new, the most important thing is that you can deliver consistently and well. A big menu often creates stress, uneven quality, and unclear photos/text. A simple menu makes it easier to succeed quickly.

### Tasks (Done per step)
1. **Choose a starter menu (Minimum or Bonus)**
   - Minimum: signature + banker + side (add-ons on the dishes)
   - Bonus: 4 main dishes + 2 sides (add-ons on the dishes)
2. **Choose your signature dish**
   - The signature dish is “what you want to be known for” right now (it can be changed later).
3. **Write one simple ordering line**
   - Example: “Order via RiceMap24. Write your desired time in the request. Pickup in [area].”

✅ Confirmations (examples):
- “Great. A small menu gives better quality and a safer start.”
- “A signature dish makes customers remember you.”
- “A clear ordering line removes friction.”

## Week 2 — Hero/front-page image: the first impression that gets clicks
### Mini lesson (what customers actually look for)
On the front page/Explore people scroll fast. Your hero image is the front door. It must signal: **clean, real, tempting** — and ideally a little human warmth.

### Hero styles that work
- **Food in focus:** the signature dish as a “scroll-stopper”
- **Kitchen atmosphere:** tidy, warm, and safe vibe
- **The cook/person + food:** a nice smile together with the food can increase trust (“who is behind this?”)

### Tasks
1. **Choose and upload a hero/front-page image**
2. **Write a short profile text (2–3 lines)**
   - Who you are + what you cook + area for pickup/delivery

✅ Confirmations:
- “The hero image increases clicks and makes people stay on the page.”
- “A short profile text builds trust without a lot of reading.”

---

## Week 3 — Food photos that sell (real photos)
### Mini lesson (appetite + correct expectations)
Photos must do two jobs: create desire and show correctly what the customer will get. Use **real photos**. Avoid AI photos, because it can be perceived as false marketing and create distrust.

### Tasks
1. **Signature dish: choose your best photo**
2. **Other dishes: at least one clear photo per dish**
3. **Overall check:** does the profile look “clean and real”?

✅ Confirmations:
- “A strong signature photo increases first-time purchases.”
- “Clear photos reduce questions and increase ordering.”
- “Real photos build trust faster than ‘perfect’.”

---

## Week 4 — Food copy that tempts and makes the customer feel safe
### Mini lesson (copy that sells correctly)
Good copy is not decoration. It makes the customer understand, feel tempted, and dare to order. It also saves you time because you get fewer questions.

### Text template (4 short lines per dish)
1) **What is it?** (name + style)  
2) **Flavor profile** (2–3 words)  
3) **What do you get?** (portion / sides / add-ons mentioned on the dish)  
4) **Ingredients/allergens** (short and clear)

### Tasks
1. **Write the full text for the signature dish**
2. **Write the full text for at least two other dishes**
3. **Choose and download the poster**
   - Poster with name, hero image, and photo of the signature dish (including QR code)

✅ Confirmations:
- “Now the copy sells for you when you’re not present.”
- “Clear ingredients/allergens build trust.”
- “The poster is ready to use when you want offline visibility.”
