# Marketing Coach – Month 12
## Focus: Consolidation + reuse (with a “challenge”: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week + local sharing)

This month we focus on **consolidation**: you connect the dots, reuse what you have already created, and build a more stable “pro routine.” At the same time, we add a clear **challenge**: you follow a fixed weekly plan that gives high visibility with low extra work — because you use your library smartly.

### Why this focus now
When you have worked through several focus areas (page, photos/copy, video, offline, repeat, order value, menus), it’s smart to make it simple and tidy: you repeat what works, plan better, and make marketing flow as a natural part of operations. This is also an important milestone: you move from “I’m trying a bit” to “I have a routine I can maintain.”

---

## This month’s challenge (fixed plan)
**Challenge routine (every week):**
- **2 photo posts per week** (FB + IG, same content)  
- **2 videos per week** (TikTok + Reels + Shorts where you can)  
- **1 local share per week** (Facebook groups or a local channel)  
- **Reuse**: at least 70% of the content must come from your library

> You are not going to create twice as much new. You are going to post more by reusing smarter.

---

## CTA (repeated)
**CTA = Call To Action** = one clear sentence that says what the customer should do now.  
Without a CTA, people can see your content and like it, but not order.

Examples:
- “See the menu and order here: [link]”
- “Menu + ordering via RiceMap24: [link]”

**Mini rule:** one CTA per post.

---

## What “reuse” means this month (very concrete)
Reuse means that you:
- use previous photos again with new text/angle
- cut the same video into a short/medium version
- post the same dish again, but with a new purpose: “for 2,” “for lunch,” “for later”
- show “menu/overview” instead of only a single dish

---

## Goals this month
1) Complete the month’s challenge (consistency)  
2) Build a better content library habit (so reuse becomes easy)  
3) Make “local sharing” a steady habit (so local discovery increases)  
4) Increase visibility without increasing stress

---

## Minimum vs Bonus
**Minimum (if you have low capacity):**
- 1 photo post per week
- 1 video per week
- 1 local share every other week
- reuse: at least 50%

**Bonus (challenge full):**
- 2 photo posts per week
- 2 videos per week
- 1 local share per week
- reuse: at least 70%

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Challenge execution (70%)**: weekly posts + videos + local share completed  
- **System & library (30%)**: content organized + reuse becomes easier week by week

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Organize the library + prepare the week’s content
### Mini lesson (a system beats motivation)
When you know where your content is, posting becomes easy.

### Tasks
1) Create/clean 4 folders:
- Food photos
- Proof/trust (packing, clean kitchen, ready to pick up)
- Menu/overview
- You/behind the scenes

2) Pick 10 “best photos” and save them as favorites  
3) Prepare the week’s plan (challenge):
- 2 photo posts
- 2 videos
- 1 local share

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now reuse becomes easy.”
- “A structured library saves you time every week.”

---

## Week 2 — Run the challenge week (reuse 70%)
### Mini lesson (repeat what works)
People need to see you multiple times. Reuse is not spam — it’s smart repetition.

### Tasks
1) Publish 2 photo posts (reuse photos + new angle text)  
2) Publish 2 videos (reuse clips, cut into short/medium)  
3) Do 1 local share (group or local channel)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You increased visibility without creating everything new.”
- “Local sharing increases discovery.”

---

## Week 3 — Improve speed with batching + scheduling
### Mini lesson (batching makes it sustainable)
If you batch content in one session, you avoid daily stress.

### Tasks
1) Do one batching session (60–90 min):
- prepare 2 photo posts
- prepare 2 videos
- schedule them

2) Local share (keep it simple):
- weekly menu post in one group
- or share your pinned “how to order” post

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Scheduling protects consistency.”
- “Batching makes the challenge easy to maintain.”

---

## Week 4 — Evaluate + lock in what you will repeat
### Mini lesson (small reviews create big improvements)
You don’t need complex analytics — just a quick review.

### Tasks
1) Identify your top performers this month:
- which post got the most response?
- which video got the most attention?
- which local share produced messages/orders?

2) Decide what you repeat next month:
- 2 content angles that worked best
- 1 local channel that worked best

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now growth becomes more predictable.”
- “You’re building a routine you can keep.”

---

## Month 12 done: you’re “ready”
You’re ready when:
- you completed the challenge (or close) ✅  
- you reused most content from your library ✅  
- you did local sharing as a habit ✅  
- you evaluated what worked and decided what to repeat ✅
