# Marketing Coach – Month 33  
## Focus: “Pre-Order Drop System” (sell before you cook — more predictability, less stress, higher order value)

This month you build a system that is extremely powerful for home kitchens: **Pre-Order Drops**. A drop system means you open orders in a defined window, customers pre-order, and you cook in a batch based on real demand.

This changes everything because:
- you cook with less uncertainty  
- you plan portions and ingredients better  
- you reduce waste  
- you can increase order value with bundles  
- you feel calmer because the week is structured  

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per post/message, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What a pre-order drop is (simple)
A pre-order drop is just this:

1) You announce: “Orders open now”  
2) Customers order inside a time window  
3) You close orders at a clear cutoff  
4) You cook in a batch  
5) You deliver/pick up in a fixed window  

**Key rule:** the customer understands exactly:
- when orders open  
- when orders close  
- when they receive the food  

⸻

## Why this works (and why it increases profitability)
Drops work because:
- customers act faster when there is a deadline (“this week”)  
- you can plan production (better efficiency)  
- you can batch cook (saves time)  
- you can sell bundles naturally (“for 2”, add-ons, cold-ready)  
- customers get a more “organized” and professional experience  

A drop system makes the business feel more serious and reliable — and customers like that.

⸻

# Part A: Choose your drop schedule (the structure)
Pick a schedule you can repeat. Example:

- **Announce/open:** Monday 18:00  
- **Cutoff:** Wednesday 10:00  
- **Cook:** Thursday  
- **Pickup window:** Thursday 16:00–19:00  

You can change the days, but the point is **consistency**.

⸻

# Part B: Drop menu (keep it simple)
Your drop menu should have:
- 2–4 main items  
- 1–2 add-ons/bundles  
- clear quantities if limited  

**Rule:** fewer choices = more orders.

⸻

# Part C: The 3-message sequence (copy/paste)
This is what makes drops work.

## 1) Orders open (announcement)
> Orders are open for this week’s drop 😊  
> Cutoff: [day/time]. Pickup: [day/time window].  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

## 2) Reminder (24 hours left)
> Reminder 😊 Orders close in 24 hours.  
> Cutoff: [day/time].  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

## 3) Last call (same day as cutoff)
> Last call 😊 Orders close at [time] today.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

Optional: personal version for repeat customers:
> Hi [name]! 😊 Orders are open — your favorite is available.  
> **Menu and ordering:** [link]

⸻

# Part D: Bundle inside the drop (increase order value naturally)
Drops are perfect for bundles:
- “For 2”  
- “Dinner + lunch” (cold-ready add-on)  
- “Weekend kit” (finish-at-home)  

**Rule:** keep it easy to understand.

⸻
