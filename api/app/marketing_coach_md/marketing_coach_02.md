# Marketing Coach – Month 2
## Focus: Social media marketing with the right copy and great photos

This month we focus on **social media marketing with the right copy and great photos**. Now that your RiceMap24 page looks safe and tempting, the next step is to get people in your local area to actually find you — and click into your page to order.

### Natural transition from Month 1
Month 1 made your “store” ready. Month 2 is about getting **traffic** into the store — in a way that is easy to execute for beginners. We therefore start with a low posting frequency that increases slightly toward the end, and at the same time we build a photo bank you can reuse all year.

### Why this focus now (and what “visibility” means in practice)
Visibility in practice means that people:
- see your food (appetite)
- see that you are real and safe (trust)
- understand how to order (action)

For a new home kitchen, visibility is almost always a “multi-touch game”: many people need to see you several times before they order for the first time. That’s why the goal this month is steady and simple visibility — not perfection.

### What is CTA (and why do you need it)?
**CTA = Call To Action** = one clear sentence that says what the customer should do now.  
Without a CTA, people can like your post, but not take the step to ordering.

Examples:
- “See the menu and order here: [link]”
- “Tap the link for menu and ordering: [link]”
- “Want to order? Send a request via RiceMap24: [link]”

### Goals this month
1) **Routine:** you can post steadily without stress  
2) **Traffic:** more people click to your RiceMap24 page via social media  
3) **Photo bank:** you collect photos you can reuse later

### Minimum vs Bonus
**Minimum:**
- Week 1–2: 1 post per week (same post on FB + IG)
- Week 3–4: 2 posts per week
- Photo bank: at least 30 photos total

**Bonus:**
- 2 posts per week all month
- Stories 2–3 times per week
- Photo bank: 50–80 photos

### Progress in the dashboard
Two tracks (two colors):
- **Posting routine (50%)** – this week’s posts published
- **Photo bank (50%)** – this week’s photo goal reached

## Week 1 — “I exist, and this is real”
### Mini lesson (trust before sales pressure)
When you are new, people need to see that you are real and that this is safe. That’s why we start with a simple intro that points to RiceMap24.

### This week’s posting (1 post)
**Post A (Intro + trust)**  
Content: you/kitchen vibe/hero image + (optional) a food photo in the same post.  
CTA: “See the menu and order here: [link]”

**Caption template (copy/paste):**
Hi! I run a small home kitchen in [area].  
I cook [type of food/culture] in small batches, fresh, with a focus on great flavor.  
Menu and ordering: [link] 

### Photo bank (weekly goal: 8–12)
Capture “the customer’s eye”:
- 2 trust photos (clean/tidy/real)
- 3 food photos (signature + banker + detail)
- 2 portion/serving photos
- 1 packing/pickup

✅ Done + confirmations:
- “Great. Now people know you exist and how they can order.”
- “A photo bank makes marketing easier week by week.”

## Week 2 — The signature dish: make people hungry
### Mini lesson (one dish at a time sells best)
When you show one clear dish, it becomes easy for the customer to want to try. This is “appetite marketing.”

### This week’s posting (1 post)
**Post B (Signature dish)**  
CTA: “Menu and ordering: [link]”

**Caption template:**
Today I want to show my signature dish: **[name]**.  
[1 tempting sentence about flavor/texture]  
See the menu and order here: [link]

### Photo bank (weekly goal: 8–12)
- 4 signature variations
- 2 banker
- 2 “add-ons together with the dish”
- 1 “ready for pickup”

✅ Done + confirmations:
- “Great. A signature post often creates the first ‘I want to try!’ reactions.”
- “You’re building content you can post again later.”

---

## Week 3 — The banker dish: low threshold
### Mini lesson (make the first order easy)
A familiar and easy-selling dish lowers the customer’s perceived risk. The banker dish is a bridge in.

### This week’s posting (2 posts)
**Post C (Banker dish)**  
CTA: “See the menu and order: [link]”

**Post D (Dish + add-on in the same frame)**  
The goal is to raise average order value without pushing.  
CTA: “Menu and ordering: [link]”

### Photo bank (weekly goal: 10–15)
- 3 full-portion shots
- 3 close-ups
- 2 “on the table as a meal”
- 2 “ready for pickup”
- 1–2 “behind the scenes” (clean/tidy)

✅ Done + confirmations:
- “Banker dish = more people dare to order for the first time.”
- “Add-ons in the same photo makes it natural to buy a little more.”

## Week 4 — “Weekly menu” + a simple habit you can continue
### Mini lesson (routine beats motivation)
You don’t need to be inspired every week. You need a simple formula you can repeat.

### This week’s posting (2 posts)
**Post E (This is what you can order now)**  
List 2–4 dishes + CTA: “Order here: [link]”

**Post F (Trust + thank you)**  
You/kitchen/“ready for pickup” + CTA: [link]

### Photo bank (weekly goal: 10–15)
- 3 signature
- 3 banker
- 2 “menu overview”
- 2 “packaging/ready”
- 2 “person/kitchen vibe”

✅ Done + confirmations:
- “A menu post makes it easy for the customer to decide.”
- “A thank-you/trust post builds relationship and repeat over time.”
