# Marketing Coach – Month 24 (updated)  
## Focus: “Real, fresh, and natural” positioning (from scratch vs ultra-processed) — and marketing that shows the ingredients

This month we focus on positioning you clearly as an alternative to industrial food and ultra-processed food: **real, fresh, and naturally made food**. This is not just “a nice story” — it is a market that has grown stronger because many customers are more aware of:
- health and everyday energy  
- less ultra-processed / more natural food  
- real ingredients and a “from scratch” feeling  
- local and human, not industrial  

You are not going to scare people or talk negatively about others. You are going to highlight what you offer: **food made from real ingredients, in small batches, with a visible process and a visible person behind it.**

---

## Why this works in the market (and why customers care)
Many people feel that industrial and ultra-processed food:
- feels “heavy” or “empty”
- gives less satiety or a worse feeling
- is full of things they don’t recognize
- makes them miss “real food”

At the same time, people have less time. They want to eat better, but they don’t always want to cook everything themselves. That’s where you can become a perfect alternative:
> “I want to eat better, but I don’t have time. I want something real.”

When you market this clearly, you can get:
- higher willingness to pay (quality feels valuable)
- more loyalty (people want “their regular” real food)
- more word of mouth (people recommend you because it feels healthy and real)

---

## Important clarification: you must be credible
This only works when you are credible:
- don’t exaggerate (“100% healthiest,” “health guarantee”)  
- don’t promise medical effects  
- show instead: ingredients, process, small batches, freshness  

You are not selling “health treatment.” You are selling real food that feels natural and homemade.

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Make your positioning clear in your profile and menu copy  
2) Build ingredient-focused content (photos + video)  
3) Repeat the message weekly so it sticks (without being negative)

---

## Goals this month
1) Update your profile/menu texts with a clear “real/fresh/from scratch” line  
2) Build an ingredient photo bank (at least 25 photos)  
3) Publish weekly with an ingredient/freshness angle (2 photos + 2 videos per week)  
4) Do 2 offline actions (Week 2 and Week 4) with the same positioning  
5) Track simply: which content creates response/orders?

- **2 photo posts per week** (FB + IG, same content)  
- **2 videos per week** (TikTok + Reels + Shorts where you can)  
- Offline actions: Week 2 and Week 4

# The message you repeat (one simple line)
Choose one line you can repeat everywhere:

Examples:
- “Small-batch, real ingredients, cooked fresh locally.”
- “From scratch, real ingredients — not industrial food.”
- “Fresh, homemade food made in small batches.”

**Rule:** Keep it positive and credible.

---

# Ingredient content (what you should actually show)
Ingredient marketing is not just “food photos.” It is proof of realness.

Show:
- colorful vegetables
- spices and sauces
- prep shots (clean cutting board)
- raw ingredients before cooking
- simple “from scratch” moments

**Rule:** Keep it clean and appetizing. No mess.

---

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Update your positioning + start the ingredient photo bank
### Mini lesson (ingredients create trust)
Ingredients are proof. They show authenticity.

### Tasks

- profile bio
