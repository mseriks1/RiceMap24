# Marketing Coach – Month 30  
## Focus: Referrals that actually get used (print “share cards” + a simple routine in the customer list)

This month you build something extremely valuable in a local “home kitchen” market: a **referral engine**. That means new customers come to you through customers who are already happy. This is one of the most profitable growth methods you can have, because you get new customers without having to post more than you already do.

Strategically, this is about three things:

### 1) Trust spreads (and it beats everything)
In local food and homemade food, **trust** is more important than “branding.” When a friend or colleague says “that food is really good,” the barrier drops a lot. People dare to order because someone they trust has already tested it.

### 2) Sharing must become a habit, not a campaign
Most people try referrals as a one-off thing: “Feel free to share us!” in a post. It’s often forgotten. Here you do the opposite: you build a **fixed routine** that repeats every week:
- share card in the bag  
- one short, friendly share sentence to small groups  
- tags in the customer list so you can see what actually happens

### 3) The system must be frictionless (and work without tracking in the link)
You don’t need a technical referral link to get started. The most practical thing is this one line that makes it “trackable enough” in real life:

**When you order: feel free to write the name of the person who tipped you 😊**

It’s simple, human, and works both online and offline.

Early important term:  
**CTA = Call To Action** (what you want the customer to do now). Use **one CTA per post**, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What referrals mean in practice (simple)
Referrals are just this:

1) A happy customer gets a small share card (QR)  
2) They share it with one person (friend, colleague, neighbor)  
3) The new customer orders via the link/QR  
4) When ordering, they can write who tipped them  
5) You register it simply in the customer list and build further

You make it easy to say yes, because the customer doesn’t have to explain everything — they just share a link.

⸻

## Why this works (and why people actually share)
People share when it is:
- **easy** (card/QR)  
- **safe** (they don’t feel “salesy”)  
- **useful** (“try this kitchen, it’s really good”)  
- **small** (one person, not “share to everyone”)

You build referrals best with small, steady nudges — not big campaigns.

# Part A: The share card (printable “del-kort”)

## What the share card must contain (simple)
Your share card should have:
- kitchen name  
- one strong food photo (signature/best seller)  
- QR code to your RiceMap24 link  
- one short line:
  - “Menu and ordering: [link]”
- the referral line:
  - “When ordering: write who tipped you 😊”

**Rule:** the card must be easy to understand in 2 seconds.

⸻

## Suggested share card text (copy/paste)
**Local home kitchen in [area]**  
Menu and ordering via QR  
When ordering: write the name of the person who tipped you 😊

⸻

# Part B: The share script (one sentence people can actually send)

## “Share sentence” (copy/paste)
This is what you want your happy customer to send to one person:

> I ordered from this local home kitchen and the food was really good 😊  
> Menu + ordering: [link]  
> (When ordering, write my name as the tip.)

**Rule:** one person at a time — not “send to everyone.”

⸻

# Part C: Tags in the customer list (tracking without tech)

## Create simple referral tags
You need tags you can apply fast:

- **referral – received** (new customer who came through someone)  
- **referral – giver** (customer who referred someone)  
- optional: **referral – 2+** (people who have referred multiple times)

## What you register (simple note)
When a new customer writes a name:
- tag new customer: “referral – received”
- tag the referrer: “referral – giver”
- add a note: “Referred [name] on [date]”

This is enough to track what happens over time.

⸻

# Part D: Optional “thank-you reward” (only if it fits)
You don’t need a reward to start. But a small thank-you can increase sharing.

Examples (keep it profitable):
- free add-on after 1 successful referral  
- priority slot once  
- “thank-you” dessert when you have extra capacity

**Rule:** don’t destroy margin. Keep it small.

⸻
