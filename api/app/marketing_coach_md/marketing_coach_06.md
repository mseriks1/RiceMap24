# Marketing Coach – Month 6
## Focus: Marketing discipline — a fixed weekly plan you can actually maintain (with scheduling)

This month we focus on **marketing discipline**: putting a **fixed, realistic weekly plan** in place for marketing that you can repeat again and again, without it taking over your life. You have already done the most important things:
- you have a sales-ready RiceMap24 page
- you have started with social media
- you have started with video
- you have done a bit of local growth/offline

Month 6 is about collecting all of that into a **stable routine**.

### Why discipline is what creates results
Most people who don’t succeed with marketing don’t lack ideas. They lack rhythm.  
Marketing often works like “small drips” that build up:
- people see you multiple times
- they feel safer
- they remember you when they get hungry
- they order

If you post a lot one week and nothing for three weeks, you don’t get this effect. Discipline therefore doesn’t mean “hard work.” It means **steady, small effort** that gives big impact over time.

### What a fixed weekly plan does in practice
A good weekly plan does three things:
1) It removes decision stress (“what should I post today?”)  
2) It makes marketing sustainable alongside cooking  
3) It builds an “engine”: content → visibility → order → repeat

### Why scheduling (planning ahead) is the key
Scheduling means you create and plan posts in advance, so marketing happens even when you have busy days. It makes you:
- less stressed
- more consistent in visibility
- more professional in expression

This is one of the easiest “pro habits” you can build.

## This month’s weekly plan (standard from now on)
This is the baseline plan you can continue with going forward:
- **2 photo posts per week** (Facebook + Instagram, same post)  
- **2 videos per week** (TikTok + Reels + Shorts when you can)  
- **Offline/local growth every other week** (1 small action every other week)  
- **Scheduling**: plan at least 3–7 days ahead

> Important: This is the goal. If you are part-time or brand new, you can start with “minimum” and build up.

1) You get a fixed weekly plan that matches your capacity  
2) You manage to plan ahead (scheduling) at least 1 week forward

**Minimum (for busy / part-time):**
- 1 photo post per week
- 1 video per week
- 1 offline action during the month
- scheduling 3 days ahead

**Bonus (for more growth):**
- 2 photo posts per week
- 2 videos per week
- offline every other week (2 total)
- scheduling 7–14 days ahead

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Set up the weekly plan + make the first “batch”
### Mini lesson (batching: do a lot in a short time)
Batching means you create multiple posts in one session. That’s how you save time.

### Tasks
1) Choose your plan: Minimum or Bonus  
2) Make a simple “content list” for the week:
   - 1 single dish (signature)
   - 1 single dish (banker)
   - 1 bundle angle (dish + add-on in the same post)
   - 1 “menu” post (what you’re cooking this week)
3) Create and schedule **at least 3 posts** in your calendar (scheduling)

✅ Confirmations:
- “Great. Now you don’t have to invent things every day.”
- “Planning gives calm and steady visibility.”

---

## Week 2 — Variety without stress: single dish + bundle
### Mini lesson (variety is about angles, not new dishes)
You don’t need new dishes every week. You need new angles:
- appetite
- portion/clarity
- trust/proof
- bundle/add-on

### Tasks
1) 2 photo posts (or 1 in minimum)
   - Post 1: signature dish (appetite)
   - Post 2: bundle (dish + add-on)
2) 2 videos (or 1 in minimum)
   - Video 1: close-up + finished result
   - Video 2: proof (packing/ready for pickup)
3) **Offline (every other week):** small action
   - choose 1 of your “two places” or a local channel and do a simple share/poster check

✅ Confirmations:
- “You keep the rhythm and build recognition.”
- “Bundle content often increases average order value without push.”
- “Small offline drips give local credibility.”

---

## Week 3 — Menu week: make it easy to order
### Mini lesson (clarity creates orders)
Many customers order when they see that it’s easy: “This is what I can get now.”

### Tasks
1) Photo post: “This is what I’m cooking this week” (mini menu) + CTA  
2) Photo post: one dish in focus + what the customer gets  
3) Video: “what you get” (portion + result)  
4) Video: “fresh today” (short and tempting)
5) Scheduling: plan at least 3 posts ahead

✅ Confirmations:
- “A menu post makes the decision easy.”
- “Planning makes you consistent, even when you’re busy.”

---

## Week 4 — System + offline: build the engine further
### Mini lesson (an engine means marketing becomes easier over time)
When you repeat the weekly plan, you build library and routine. Then everything gets easier.

### Tasks
1) Follow the weekly plan (photos + video)  
2) **Offline (every other week):** small action #2  
   - place/refresh poster or share in a relevant local channel  
3) Make the “next week batch” (at least 3 posts) and schedule them

✅ Confirmations:
- “You’re building a system that creates impact over time.”
- “When you plan, you avoid last-minute pushes.”
