# Marketing Coach – Month 22  
## Focus: Local growth and offline marketing (new places + a simple “poster routine” you can repeat)

This month we focus on **local growth through offline marketing**, but with a slightly new twist: you are not just going to “hang up a poster.” You are going to build a **repeatable poster routine** where month by month you find new places, test, and build more local touchpoints that work for you.

The goal is that offline marketing becomes as natural as your social media routine: a little each week, in a tidy way.

---

## Why offline is especially strong locally (and why it works even without “hype”)
Offline works because it reaches people while they are in their own local area. It also builds credibility and trust, because the message feels more “real” than a random ad.

And importantly: offline marketing does not need to be “aggressive” to work. It works best when:
- the message is simple
- ordering is easy (QR/link)
- it shows up in more places over time (repetition in the local community)

Offline gives you local visibility that is often more stable than social media algorithms.

---

## This month: build a “poster engine”
Think of it like an engine:
- You have finished posters (QR + name + signature dish / menu top)
- You place them in good spots
- You replace/refresh regularly
- You build a growing location bank

That means each month becomes easier than the previous one.

# The location bank (10 new places)
You need 10 new location ideas this month. They don’t all have to be used now. The point is to build a growing bank.

Examples (mix and match):
- grocery store / convenience store bulletin board
- gym / fitness center
- apartment building boards / HOA boards
- laundromat / shared laundry room (where allowed)
- hair salon / barbershop / nail salon waiting area
- library / community center board
- sports hall / clubhouse
- coworking / office hub board
- student house / campus board (if relevant)
- church / volunteer house board

**Rule:** choose places where:
- people are local
- they can actually order from you
- it’s allowed to post (or you ask politely)

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Build the location bank + choose your 4 test places
### Mini lesson (a bank makes offline easy)
When you have a location bank, you can take action fast without thinking.

### Tasks
1) Write down 10 new places (location bank)  
2) Choose 4 places to test this month (1 per week)  
3) Prepare posters (download/print):
- QR + kitchen name
- one clear headline
- one strong photo (signature/top dish)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your offline work becomes repeatable.”
- “A clear poster sells in seconds.”

---

## Week 2 — Test place #1 (new placement)
### Mini lesson (small weekly actions compound)
A small routine is stronger than big one-time efforts.

### Tasks
1) Place a poster in test place #1  
2) Take a photo for your records  
3) Optional: share on social media:
- “You can find us locally at [place]” + CTA

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you created a new local touchpoint.”
- “Documentation makes it easy to maintain.”

---

## Week 3 — Test place #2 (refresh/maintenance)
### Mini lesson (maintenance is what makes the engine work)
A poster routine works because you keep it alive.

### Tasks
1) Refresh/replace a poster at one location (clean and visible)  
2) If you can: add test place #2 as a new placement  
3) Ask one new customer:
- “How did you hear about us?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Refresh keeps the system effective.”
- “Tracking teaches you what works.”

---

## Week 4 — Test place #3 + #4 (placement + maintenance)
### Mini lesson (repeat creates stable local visibility)
The more local touchpoints you have, the more you become “known.”

### Tasks
1) New placement in test place #3  
2) Maintenance check in one existing place (test place #4 can be this)  
3) Note:
- which places you want to keep next month

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have multiple local touchpoints working for you.”
- “This becomes easier every month.”
