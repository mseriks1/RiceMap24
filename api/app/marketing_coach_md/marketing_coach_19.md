# Marketing Coach – Month 19  
## Focus: You as a person = your brand (credibility and trust through how you show up — both online and offline)

This month we focus on something many people underestimate: that **you as a person indirectly are your business**. Not because you have to be “visible all the time,” but because people buy food from a home kitchen when they feel:
- this is real
- this is safe
- this is a person I have a good feeling about

That means the impression people get of you — both on social media and in real life — affects how easily they dare to order, and whether they recommend you to others.

This is not about being “perfect.” It’s about being **consistent, warm, and professional**, so you build credibility and trust over time.

---

## Why this focus now
When you’ve come a bit further, the challenge is not only “being seen.” You *are* being seen. The challenge is:
- what people feel when they see you
- whether they feel like supporting you
- whether they perceive you as safe and serious

Especially for home kitchens, this matters more than for big restaurants, because customers often connect the experience directly to you as a person.

---

## What this means in practice (without getting private)
You don’t need to share your private life. You just need to make three things clearer:

1) **Who you are in the role as cook/operator**  
2) **What you stand for** (quality, flavor, hygiene, real food)  
3) **How you treat people** (kindness, respect, clarity)

---

## The 3 most important signals you send (often without thinking about it)

### 1) Tone and language (in text and messages)
- are you warm and simple in your wording?
- do you seem stressed, irritated, short?
- do you seem confident and organized?

**Rule:** Friendly + clear is always stronger than “clever.”  
Short and polite beats long and complicated.

**Mini template for replies (copy/paste):**
> Hi! Thanks for reaching out 😊  
> Yes — you can order via RiceMap24 here: [link].  
> Just write your preferred pickup time in the request.  
> Let me know if you have allergies or special preferences.

✅ Confirmation: “Clear and friendly communication lowers the threshold to order.”

---

### 2) Visual impression (profile, photos, and “realness”)
People judge quickly:
- Does this look clean and real?
- Does the person behind it feel trustworthy?
- Does the food look like what I’ll actually get?

**You don’t need perfect studio content.** You need:
- real photos
- consistent style
- clean, pleasant kitchen vibe
- clear menu and ordering flow

✅ Confirmation: “Consistency in visuals builds trust faster than perfection.”

---

### 3) Behavior in real life (how you show up locally)
Offline signals matter:
- Do you greet people politely?
- Is pickup easy and calm?
- Are you predictable and respectful?

If you’re friendly and clear, people will talk about you.

✅ Confirmation: “A calm and kind pickup experience creates word-of-mouth.”

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Make your “brand signals” clearer (tone, visuals, consistency)  
2) Build one simple “personal brand routine” you can repeat every week  
3) Create one small offline presence action that matches your vibe

---

## Goals this month
1) You define your “brand identity” in 3 short sentences  
2) You use a simple messaging template for customers  
3) You post weekly with at least one “you/behind-the-scenes” element  
4) You do 1–2 low-threshold offline actions

---

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Brand clarity (60%)**: identity sentences + templates + profile consistency  
- **Execution (40%)**: posts + offline action completed

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Define your “brand identity” (3 sentences)
### Mini lesson: clarity creates trust
If you can say who you are, people feel safer.

### Tasks
Write 3 sentences you can reuse everywhere:
1) Who you are + what you cook  
2) What you stand for (fresh, clean, real, flavor)  
3) How to order (simple steps)

Example:
> I run a small home kitchen in [area] and cook [style/culture].  
> I make small batches, real ingredients, and clean, safe food.  
> Menu and ordering via RiceMap24: [link] (write pickup time in the request).

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your brand is easy to understand.”
- “This creates a professional first impression.”

---

## Week 2 — Standardize your customer tone (messages)
### Mini lesson: tone is part of the product
People remember how you made them feel.

### Tasks
1) Create 2–3 message templates:
- order instructions
- thank-you message
- allergy/ingredients message

Thank-you template:
> Thank you so much for your order! 😊  
> I hope you enjoy the food. If you want to order again, the menu is here: [link]

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You reduce friction and sound professional.”
- “Small kindness creates repeat customers.”

---

## Week 3 — Add “you” into your content (without oversharing)
### Mini lesson: people buy from people
A small glimpse of the person behind the food builds trust.

### Tasks (pick 1–2)
- a short clip of you packing orders
- a photo of your hands cooking (no face needed)
- a simple “hello” story: “menu open this week”
- one line about why you love this dish

✅ Done confirmations:
- “This makes your kitchen feel real and safe.”
- “You build familiarity without becoming private.”

---

## Week 4 — Offline presence that matches your brand
### Mini lesson: offline visibility feels “real”
A poster or local mention makes you feel established.

### Tasks
1) Put up 1–2 posters (where allowed)  
2) Take one photo of the poster in place (optional for SoMe)  
3) Ask one new customer:
- “How did you hear about us?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your brand exists both online and offline.”
- “Tracking helps you repeat what works.”

---

You’re ready when:
- you have 3 identity sentences ✅  
- you use templates for messages ✅  
- you posted weekly with a small “you” element ✅  
- you did 1–2 offline actions ✅
