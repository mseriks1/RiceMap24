# Marketing Coach – Month 23  
## Focus: Local social media with Snapchat (fast, local, and “real” — for more reach in your area)

This month we focus on **local social media**, with special emphasis on **Snapchat**. The goal is not to become a “Snapchat influencer.” The goal is to use Snapchat (and similar fast formats) as a low-threshold channel to reach people locally — especially younger audiences and people who respond to short, real content.

Snapchat is useful because it is:
- fast
- informal
- local-feeling
- easy to keep consistent

And most importantly: it can create **many small touchpoints** without a lot of work.

---

## Why this focus now
You now have:
- a strong routine (2 photo posts + 2 videos per week)
- offline touchpoints and local growth habits
- menu logic (bundles, for 2, for later, finish-at-home)

Now you add a channel that is **lightweight** and can give you extra local visibility without having to “produce” more heavy content.

---

## What Snapchat marketing is (in practice)
Snapchat marketing for a home kitchen is not long stories. It is:
- short 5–15 second clips
- quick proof that you are cooking today / this week
- simple “fresh today” updates
- one clear CTA (link / “order on RiceMap24”)

The job is to make people in your local area think:
> “Oh yeah, this kitchen is active — I should order.”

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Create a simple Snap routine (repeatable clip types)  
2) Post lightly but consistently (2–5 days per week)  
3) Use Snap to support your main channels (FB/IG/TikTok) — not replace them

---

## Goals this month
1) You set up Snapchat correctly (profile + name + simple bio)  
2) You build a small Snap clip library (at least 20 snaps saved)  
3) You post consistently:
   - Minimum: 2–3 days per week
   - Bonus: 4–5 days per week
4) You keep the main routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week  
5) You track lightly: did Snap create replies/DMs/orders?

# The Snap content types (repeatable, low effort)
You will use 5 repeatable clip types. Keep it simple.

1) **Fresh today / this week**  
2) **Packing orders** (proof)  
3) **What you get** (portion + contents)  
4) **Menu open** (short text overlay)  
5) **Behind the scenes** (hands cooking, ingredients, clean kitchen)

**Rule:** No long talking required. Text overlay is enough.

---

# CTA (keep it simple)
Use one CTA line in the Snap text:
- “Menu + order on RiceMap24: [link]”
If links are not clickable in Snap, use:
- “Order link in bio” (and keep your bio link updated)

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Setup Snapchat + define your routine
### Mini lesson (Snap is a “proof channel”)
Snap works because it’s real and frequent.

### Tasks
1) Create/clean your Snapchat profile:
- kitchen name
- profile image
- short bio
- ordering link (if possible)

2) Write your 5 clip types (copy into Notes)  
3) Save 10 “Snap hooks” you can reuse:
- “Fresh today”
- “Menu open”
- “Packing now”
- “Ready for pickup”
- “For 2 available”
- “Lunch option”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now Snap becomes easy to execute.”
- “A small template bank removes friction.”

---

## Week 2 — Consistency week: 2–3 Snap days + build clip library
### Mini lesson (frequency builds familiarity)
Snap is not about one viral video. It’s about being present.

### Tasks
1) Post on 2–3 days this week:
- 1–3 snaps per day (short)
2) Save your best snaps into your library (camera roll)
3) Keep the main routine:
- 2 photo posts
- 2 videos

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you’re building local familiarity.”
- “Saving clips makes future Snap posting even easier.”

---

## Week 3 — Add one “menu open” story + one “what you get” story
### Mini lesson (clarity increases orders)
People order when they know what they get and how to order.

### Tasks
1) Make 1 Snap sequence (3 snaps):
- “Menu open”
- “What you get”
- “Order on RiceMap24” (CTA)

2) Post on 2–4 days this week  
3) Keep main routine stable

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now Snap supports conversion, not just views.”
- “Clarity reduces DMs and increases orders.”

---

## Week 4 — Evaluate + lock your Snap rhythm
### Mini lesson (Snap should stay lightweight)
If Snap feels heavy, you’ll quit. Keep it simple and sustainable.

### Tasks
1) Review:
- Which Snap type got the most views/replies?
- Which felt easiest?
2) Choose your ongoing rhythm:
- 2–3 days/week (minimum)
- 4–5 days/week (bonus)

3) Decide your “Snap combo” you will repeat:
- Fresh today + packing + CTA
- Menu open + what you get + CTA

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now Snap is part of your system, not a burden.”
- “You chose a sustainable rhythm.”
