# Marketing Coach – Month 9 (v3)  
## Focus: Increase order value with Cold-Ready / Chilled (make it easy to order more — also for lunch)

This month we focus on increasing order value with **Cold-Ready / Chilled**. That means you offer 1–2 products or add-ons the customer can **take with them and keep ready in the fridge**, so they get value both “now and later.” When it becomes easy to add something extra (without it needing to be eaten immediately), it also becomes easier to order a little more — in a natural way.

In addition, cold-ready can be a smart way to hit **other buying moments** than dinner, especially **lunch**: things that are already meant to be eaten cold are perfect “grab-and-go” choices.

---

## Natural transition from Month 8
In Month 8 you used add-ons and bundles to increase average order value. Now we take the same idea further, but with a new type of customer value: something that can be eaten later or at another time (for example lunch). When the customer thinks “I’m ordering today anyway,” it often becomes natural to add one more item when it also gives value tomorrow.

---

## Cold-Ready / Chilled — definition (completely clear)
Cold-ready items are designed to:
- **be served cold (on purpose)**  
- **be stored cold** (in the fridge)  
- **be eaten cold** *(or only lightly heated if you say it clearly)*

The point is that the customer should not be in doubt: this is meant to be cold and ready. If you offer “light heating,” write it briefly:  
“Can be lightly heated 1–2 min” (if that’s true).

---

## Examples of cold-ready / chilled items (easy to understand)
Cold-ready does not have to be “dinner.” It can be things people naturally buy cold:
- **salads**
- **dessert / cake / sweet** (dessert cups, cake pieces)
- **pancakes** (cold + toppings on the side)
- **baguettes / wraps** (meant cold, or “can be lightly heated” if you write that)
- **drinks**
- **small sides/snacks** (pickles, dips)

Always clear language: “served cold” + “store in the fridge.”

---

## This month’s strategy (3 steps)
1) Choose **1–2 cold-ready items** that are easy to deliver and easy to understand  
2) Connect them to a **bundle or add-on angle** (“add for tomorrow / for lunch”)  
3) Market it as **practical value**, not as “buy more”

---

## Goals this month
1) 1–2 cold-ready items clearly on the menu (with clear text)  
2) 1 simple “today + tomorrow” / “dinner + lunch” bundle (or add-on combination)  
3) Weekly posting with a cold-ready angle (at least once per week)  
4) New cold-ready items get **new photos and video** (fixed part of the routine)  
5) One low-threshold offline action (poster) in Week 4

---

## Fixed posting routine (always included)
Keep the same baseline rhythm you already have:

**Minimum**
- 1 photo post/week (FB/IG)
- 1 video/week (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)

**Bonus**
- 2 photo posts/week
- 2 videos/week
- Offline every other week (optional)

### Important rule this month: new offer = new visual content
When you introduce cold-ready, you have to show it. Otherwise it won’t be chosen.

For each cold-ready item:
- Take **at least 3–5 new photos** (alone)  
- Take **at least 1 short video** (5–10 sec)  
- Take **photos/video in a bundle** (cold-ready + main dish in the same frame)

### What you can post this month (connected to cold-ready)
**Photo/copy (FB/IG)**
- “Order today + have ready for tomorrow” (dish + cold-ready add-on)
- “Lunch ready in the fridge” (cold-ready alone)
- “Meal set + cold-ready side”

**Video**
- “Pack it” (packing main dish + cold-ready in the same frame)
- “What you get” (show both clearly)

CTA every time:
- “Menu and ordering: [link]”

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Choose 1–2 cold-ready items (start simple)
### Mini lesson: simplicity sells best
One or two clear cold-ready choices are enough to test demand and increase average order value.

### Tasks
1) Choose 1–2 cold-ready items (clearly “served cold”)  
2) Write a short 2-line explanation for each item:
- **What it is**
- **When the customer uses it** (e.g., “lunch tomorrow / ready in the fridge”)

3) Take the first visual round (minimum requirement):
- 3 photos per item
- 1 short video per item

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. One clear cold-ready choice can increase order value without more stress.”
- “Now you have visual content that makes the customer actually choose it.”

### This week’s posting (suggestion)
- Photo post: cold-ready alone (“perfect for lunch tomorrow”) + CTA  
- Video: short “ready in the fridge” / packing (5–10 sec)

---

## Week 2 — Connect cold-ready to a simple bundle (make the choice easy)
### Mini lesson: bundles remove friction
When you package the choice, it becomes easier to say yes. The goal is that the customer doesn’t have to think.

### Tasks
1) Create one simple bundle:
- “Main dish + cold-ready add-on”  
2) Give it a simple name that explains the point:
- “Today + tomorrow”
- “Dinner + lunch”
- “Extra for later”

3) Create bundle visuals:
- 2 photos of the bundle (both products in the same frame)
- 1 short video of the bundle (packing / “what you get”)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Great. Now you make it easy to order more without pushing.”
- “Bundle visuals makes the customer understand in 2 seconds.”

### This week’s posting (suggestion)
- Photo post: “Dinner + lunch” bundle + what you get + CTA  
- Video: “what you get” (both clearly)

---

## Week 3 — Market the value (practical, not pushy)
### Mini lesson: sell everyday value
Cold-ready works best when your language is helpful:
- “ready in the fridge”
- “perfect for lunch”
- “since you’re ordering anyway…”

### Tasks
1) Create 2 caption templates (copy/paste)
- “Since you’re ordering today…”
- “Perfect to have ready for lunch tomorrow…”

2) Post at least once with a cold-ready angle  
3) Optional: a gentle message to repeat customers (only if relevant):
> “This week I also have a cold-ready ‘dinner + lunch’ option. Menu: [link]”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You’re selling practical value — it feels natural to the customer.”
- “This increases average order value without requiring more posting.”

---

## Week 4 — Offline poster + stabilize cold-ready as a fixed add-on
### Mini lesson: offline works when the message is simple
A poster works best when it communicates one clear idea. “Dinner + lunch” is easy to understand and perfect offline.

### Offline (low threshold): choose 1–2 locations
Start with your location list. If you need suggestions, use these 5 (new for variety):
1) **Library / community center** (local bulletin board)  
2) **Coworking / office hub** (board/common area)  
3) **Sports hall / clubhouse** (info zone/board)  
4) **Student house / campus boards** (if relevant)  
5) **Church / volunteer house** (bulletin board)

**Timing (simple):**
- Download a finished promo poster with QR
- Put it up in 1–2 places (where allowed / ask politely)

### Tasks
1) Make cold-ready a fixed, clear add-on in menu text  
2) Put up the poster (1–2 places)  
3) Mini evaluation:
- Which item is chosen most?
- Which text/visual explains best?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have an add-on that can steadily increase income.”
- “Offline creates local touchpoints without more posting.”
