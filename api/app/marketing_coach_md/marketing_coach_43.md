# Marketing Coach – Month 43  
## Focus: Visibility in public spaces (be seen where people already are — and build “I keep seeing them” locally)

This month you focus on visibility in **public spaces** — places where people naturally pass by and see messages repeatedly. Public-space visibility is powerful because it creates:

- repetition (“I keep seeing them”)  
- credibility (“they’re real”)  
- local memory (people remember you)  

The goal is not to be everywhere. The goal is to be present in a few places where your ideal customers already are.

Early important term:  
CTA = Call To Action (what you want the customer to do now). Use one CTA per message/poster, always the same:  
**“Menu and ordering: [link]”**

⸻

## What “public space visibility” means (simple)
It means you place or refresh posters/QR materials in places like:
- grocery stores / convenience stores  
- libraries / community centers  
- gyms  
- laundromats / shared laundry rooms  
- sports halls / clubhouses  
- coworking/office hubs  
- campus boards (if relevant)  

The key is repeated exposure in everyday routines.

⸻

## Why this works (repetition beats everything)
Most customers don’t order the first time they see you. They order after:
- the 3rd time  
- the 5th time  
- the 10th time  

Public spaces create those repeated “touchpoints” without you posting more online.

⸻

## This month’s strategy (4 steps)
1) Choose 5 public-space placements (realistic)  
2) Keep the message extremely simple (QR + one sentence)  
3) Execute placements over the month (waves)  
4) Build a maintenance habit + track what works

⸻

## Goals this month
1) You choose 5 public-space locations  
2) You place or refresh posters in all 5 (over the month)  
3) You document placements (photo + note)  
4) You do weekly maintenance (quick check)  
5) You track customer discovery sources (“How did you hear about us?”)  
6) You keep routine: 2 photo posts + 2 videos per week

⸻

## Fixed routine (baseline this month)
- 2 photo posts per week (FB + IG)  
- 2 videos per week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)  
- Offline: public-space placements/maintenance every week (this is the focus month)

⸻

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Placements (60%)**: 5 locations placed/refreshed + documented  
- **Maintenance & tracking (40%)**: weekly checks + customer source notes  

⸻

# Part A: Choose your 5 public-space locations
Pick 5 places where:
- people are local
- people pass by repeatedly
- it’s allowed to post (or you ask politely)

Examples:
- grocery store board  
- library/community board  
- gym  
- laundromat/shared laundry room  
- sports hall  
- coworking board  
- campus board  
- church/community house board  

⸻

# Part B: Poster message (one sentence)
Keep the poster extremely simple:

**Poster headline example:**
Local home kitchen in [area] 😊  
Menu and ordering via QR

Add:
- QR code  
- pickup window (optional, if relevant)  
- one clear CTA line

**Rule:** “One sentence + QR.”

⸻

# Part C: Documentation (so you can maintain)
For each placement, write:
- location name
- date
- photo (for your records)

This turns it from “random” into a system.

⸻

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Choose locations + first placements
### Mini lesson (where people already are beats “more places”)
Your best places are part of people’s weekly routines.

### Tasks
1) Choose 5 public-space locations  
2) Place in 1–2 locations this week  
3) Take photos + notes (location + date)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now your public visibility has started.”
- “Documentation makes it repeatable.”

---

## Week 2 — Add 2 more locations + maintenance
### Mini lesson (maintenance is the real system)
Fresh posters get more attention.

### Tasks
1) Place in 2 more locations  
2) Refresh/straighten one existing poster  
3) Ask one new customer:
- “How did you hear about us?”

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you’re building local repetition.”
- “Tracking teaches you which places work.”

---

## Week 3 — Add final locations + improve clarity
### Mini lesson (clarity increases conversion)
If people see you offline but don’t order, the message may be too unclear.

### Tasks
1) Place in remaining locations (reach 5 total)  
2) Improve poster clarity if needed:
- stronger photo
- clearer headline
- clearer pickup window
3) Maintenance check of 1–2 places

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now you have 5 local touchpoints working.”
- “Clarity makes offline convert.”

---

## Week 4 — Maintain + track + lock the routine
### Mini lesson (offline becomes powerful when it’s maintained)
A poster engine grows month by month.

### Tasks
1) Maintenance check of all placements (quick)  
2) Replace any damaged/faded posters  
3) Review:
- which locations produced customers?
- which didn’t?
4) Decide your next month plan:
- keep the best 3
- test 2 new

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now public-space visibility is a system.”
- “You have a clear plan for what to repeat.”
