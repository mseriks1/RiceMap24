# Marketing Coach – Month 17 (updated)  
## Focus: Intensive campaign (7–10 days) that gives a clear “boost” — because something is actually new

This month we focus on an **intensive campaign**. An intensive campaign means that for a short period (7–10 days) you have a clear theme, clear message, and slightly higher visibility than normal — but in a tidy way. You use the campaign to create a feeling of:

- “something is happening now”
- “this is a reason to order now”
- “I want to try this this week”

### What an intensive campaign is (in practice)
An intensive campaign is **not** just “posting more.” It is a short period where you:
1) make the message extra clear  
2) make it easier to choose  
3) give the customer a concrete reason to order now

That’s why campaigns often work better than “normal posting” when you want a lift.

---

## Why an intensive campaign works (when it’s smart)
Intensive campaigns work because they combine four powerful things in marketing:

### 1) Repetition in a short time (more touchpoints)
Many customers need to see you several times before they order the first time. An intensive week creates more touchpoints in a short time, so you “get into people’s heads.”

### 2) Clarity + easier choice
When you collect the offer into one theme (“Best sellers week,” “For 2 week,” “Lunch week”), the customer doesn’t have to interpret everything themselves. They understand: “This is what they want me to order now.”

### 3) Timing and momentum
A campaign creates a psychological “now” feeling. Not because you nag, but because the message has a clear frame: “this week.”

### 4) This must be different from normal posting
If a campaign is just “more of the same,” it can feel pushy.  
**A campaign must therefore include something that stands out**, for example:
- a new angle (a new way to present it)
- a new dish (small novelty)
- a new bundle / “for 2” / “for 4”
- a special day (“Friday Night Pack,” “Sunday Lunch”)
- a new time frame (“pickup only 16–18,” or “extra pickup slot Friday”)
- a special offer (without destroying margin — can be a “bonus add-on,” not a discount)

1) You choose one theme that is clear and feels “new”  
2) You run the campaign for 7–10 days (with higher visibility)

4) You follow up after the campaign and turn it into repeat behavior

## Fixed posting routine (baseline + campaign boost)
Baseline (as you’re used to now):
- 2 photo posts/week (FB + IG)
- 2 videos/week (TikTok + Reels + Shorts)

During the campaign window (7–10 days), you add:
- extra repetition (the same message, same theme)
- 1–2 extra posts (light)  
- 2–4 local pushes (posters, pinned post, group share)

## Progress in the dashboard (two colors)
- **Campaign execution (70%)**: campaign run + posts + local pushes completed  
- **Repeat capture (30%)**: follow-up + customer list updates + notes

# Step 1: Choose your campaign theme (pick ONE)
Choose one theme that makes it easy to understand what is happening.

Examples:
- **Best Sellers Week** (simple, safe)  
- **For 2 Week** (bundles, higher order value)  
- **Lunch Week** (new buying moment)  
- **Finish-at-Home Week** (restaurant at home)  
- **Cold-Ready Week** (order now + have for later)

**Rule:** the theme must include something that feels different/new this week.

---

# Step 2: Decide “what is new” (so it doesn’t feel like nagging)
Pick one “new element” (you only need one):
- a new bundle name (“For 2 – Weekend Kit”)
- a new add-on (bonus item)
- a special day (Friday pack)
- a new pickup window/slot
- a limited batch (limited quantity)

---

# Step 3: Plan the 7–10 day campaign
A simple plan that works for most home kitchens:

**Day 1:** Campaign announcement (this week)  
**Day 2:** Feature dish/bundle (what you get)  
**Day 3:** Proof/trust (packing, fresh, ready)  
**Day 4:** Reminder (still open)  
**Day 5:** Social proof (messages/reactions)  
**Day 6:** “Last days” urgency (gentle)  
**Day 7:** Last call + thank you  
(If you run 10 days, repeat the best-performing content once.)

# Weekly plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1 — Choose theme + prepare assets
### Mini lesson (preparation makes campaigns easy)
Campaigns feel stressful only when you improvise. If you prepare, it becomes simple.

### Tasks
1) Choose the campaign theme + “what is new”  
2) Prepare assets (reuse your library):
- 5–8 photos (bundle/dish/menu)
- 3–5 short clips (proof/appetite/what you get)
- 1 poster (QR + theme)

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now the campaign is ready to run without chaos.”
- “Reusing library content makes this low effort.”

---

## Week 2 — Pre-campaign warm-up (make people aware)
### Mini lesson (people need a heads-up)
A small warm-up makes the campaign feel natural and not pushy.

### Tasks
1) Post one “coming this week” post  
2) Share once in one local channel (optional)  
3) Make sure your RiceMap24 menu is updated and clear

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Warm-up increases response during the campaign.”
- “A clear menu reduces friction.”

---

## Week 3 — Run the 7–10 day campaign window
### Mini lesson (repetition creates conversion)
During the campaign window you repeat the theme and keep it very clear.

### Tasks (during the 7–10 days)
1) Post according to the plan (announcement → what you get → proof → reminders)  
2) Do 2–4 local pushes:
- poster placements
- ask admin for a pinned post (one week)
- local group shares (not spam)
3) Reply quickly and warmly

✅ Done confirmations:
- “You created a clear boost without being messy.”
- “Local pushes increase discovery fast.”

---

## Week 4 — Follow up + turn the boost into repeat
### Mini lesson (repeat is the real profit)
A campaign is great — but the real win is capturing repeat customers.

### Tasks
1) Add new customers to your Customer List (notes)  
2) Send a short thank-you message:
> Thank you so much for your order! 😊  
> Menu for next week is here: [link]

3) Ask (optional):
- “How did you hear about us?”
4) Quick review:
- what post got the most response?
- which push produced orders?

✅ Done confirmations:
- “Now the campaign becomes a repeat engine.”
- “You learned what creates a boost — so you can repeat it later.”
