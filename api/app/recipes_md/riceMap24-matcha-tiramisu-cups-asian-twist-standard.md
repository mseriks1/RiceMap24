# Matcha Tiramisu Cups (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

A familiar dessert with an Asian upgrade: **matcha** + optional **yuzu** brightness, served as neat single-serve cups that travel well for pickup/takeaway.

No “traditional Asian dessert” requirement here—this is a crowd-pleasing Western classic with a clear Asian signature.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Matcha tiramisu (no-bake), single-serve cups
- **Asian twist:** Matcha (green tea) + optional yuzu / citrus
- **Yield:** 8 cups (200–250 ml each)
- **Active time:** ~25–30 min
- **Chill time:** 4–8 hours (best overnight)
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup/delivery friendly:** portioned cups, no slicing mess.
- **Looks premium:** clean layers + matcha dust top.
- **High-margin upsells:** “extra matcha,” “yuzu cream,” “strawberry layer,” larger cup.
- **Batch-friendly:** assemble trays of cups.

---

## Equipment
- Mixing bowls
- Whisk or hand mixer
- Fine sieve (for matcha dusting)
- Spatula
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale recommended)
- 8 dessert cups with lids (200–250 ml)

---

## Ingredients (8 cups)

## A) Matcha soak
- **240 ml** hot water (80°C is ideal; not boiling)
- **2–3 Tbsp** matcha powder (culinary grade)
- **2–3 Tbsp** sugar or honey (adjust)
- Optional: **1 tsp** vanilla

> Texture tip: whisk matcha with a little water into a paste first, then add the rest.

---

## B) Cream layer
- **500 g** mascarpone
- **350 ml** heavy cream (cold)
- **70–90 g** sugar (adjust)
- **1 tsp** vanilla extract
- **1/8 tsp** salt (tiny pinch helps)

### Optional “Asian brightness” (recommended)
- **1–2 Tbsp** yuzu juice OR lime juice
- Optional: **1 tsp** yuzu zest (if available)

---

## C) Ladyfingers / base
- **24–30** ladyfingers (savoiardi)  
  OR substitute: sponge cake cut into fingers (less brittle)

---

## D) Topping
- Matcha powder for dusting
- Optional: white chocolate shavings
- Optional: toasted sesame seeds (tiny sprinkle looks cool)

---

## Prep Notes (Success rules)
- Don’t soak ladyfingers too long—**quick dip only** or they go mushy.
- Chill cups at least **4 hours** so layers set for delivery.
- Dust matcha **right before pickup** for the cleanest look.

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make matcha soak (5 minutes)
1. Sift matcha into a bowl.
2. Add 2–3 Tbsp hot water and whisk into a smooth paste.
3. Add remaining hot water + sugar (and vanilla if using).
4. Cool to room temp (or chill 10–15 min).

---

## 2) Make cream (10 minutes)
1. In a bowl, whisk mascarpone briefly to soften.
2. Add sugar, vanilla, salt (and yuzu/lime if using). Mix smooth.
3. In a separate bowl, whip cold heavy cream to **soft peaks**.
4. Fold whipped cream into mascarpone mixture in 2–3 additions until smooth and fluffy.

**Texture target:** thick enough to hold layers, but spreadable.

---

## 3) Assemble cups (10–15 minutes)
For each cup:
1. **Quick-dip** 2–3 ladyfingers in matcha soak (1 second per side).
2. Break to fit cup and press a base layer.
3. Add a layer of cream (about 2–3 Tbsp).
4. Repeat: soaked ladyfingers + cream until cup is almost full.
5. Finish with a smooth top layer of cream.

**Standard build:** 2 cookie layers + 2 cream layers.

---

## 4) Chill (minimum 4 hours)
- Cover cups and refrigerate **4–8 hours** (best overnight).

---

## 5) Finish (service time)
- Dust matcha on top using a fine sieve.
- Optional: white chocolate shavings or a tiny sesame sprinkle.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Flavor:** matcha-forward but balanced (not bitter).
- **Texture:** creamy and set, not runny.
- **Layers:** clean, visible, not collapsed.

## Scaling (10 cups)
Multiply all ingredients by **1.25×**.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Use clear cups with tight lids.
- Keep cold.
- If pickup time is long: place cups in an insulated bag.

**Label suggestion:** “Keep chilled. Best within 48 hours.”

---

## Storage
- Refrigerated: **2–3 days** best texture (matcha color stays better if covered).
- Not freezer-friendly (texture changes).

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (mascarpone, cream)
- Egg/wheat (ladyfingers)
- Possible caffeine sensitivity (matcha)
