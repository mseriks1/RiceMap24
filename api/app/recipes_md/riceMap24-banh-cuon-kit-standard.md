# Bánh Cuốn (Vietnamese Steamed Rice Rolls) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Silky steamed rice rolls filled with savory pork + mushroom, finished with scallion oil, fried shallots, and a bright **nước chấm** dipping sauce.  
This is **very photogenic** and works great as a **kit** (rolls + toppings + sauce) for home-kitchen sellers.

**Reference recipe:** Hungry Huy — Bánh Cuốn / Bánh Ướt  
https://www.hungryhuy.com/banh-cuon/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Steamed rice sheets → filled rolls → toppings + dipping sauce
- **Yield:** 4 meal portions (or 6 lighter portions)
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min
- **Total time:** ~75–90 min (includes batter rest + steaming)
- **Skill level:** Medium (steaming thin sheets is the only tricky part)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24
- **Not typical takeaway** (compared to pho/bánh mì), but very “restaurant” in feel.
- **Looks premium:** glossy rolls, green scallion oil, golden fried shallots, herbs.
- **Batch-friendly:** batter + filling can be made ahead; steam/roll to order or in batches.
- **Great upsells:** extra filling, extra scallion oil, extra fried shallots, add chả lụa (Vietnamese ham).

---

## Equipment
- Wide nonstick pan + lid **OR** a steamer setup (cloth over pot)  
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Small skillet (filling)
- Small saucepan (scallion oil)
- Spatula (thin flexible)
- Ladle (for batter)
- Cutting board + knife

> **Home-kitchen shortcut:** nonstick pan + lid method is easiest to standardize.

---

## Ingredients (4 meal portions)

## A) Batter (silky + flexible)
- **220 g** rice flour
- **60 g** tapioca starch (for elasticity)
- **1 tsp** salt
- **900 ml** water
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil

**Optional (nice aroma):**
- **2 scallions**, finely sliced (stir in at the end)

> Rest batter 30–60 minutes. Stir well before each ladle (starch settles).

---

## B) Filling (pork + mushrooms)
- **450 g** ground pork
- **150 g** wood ear mushrooms (rehydrated, finely chopped)  
  *(or shiitake if wood ear is hard to find)*
- **1 small onion** or **2 shallots**, finely diced
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced (optional)
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1 tsp** sugar
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil

---

## C) Nước chấm (dipping sauce)
- **90 ml** fish sauce
- **180 ml** water
- **70–80 g** sugar
- **60 ml** lime juice
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1–2** chilies, sliced (optional)

Taste target: sweet–salty–sour, bold but balanced.

---

## D) Toppings (make it photogenic)
- **Fried shallots** (store-bought is fine)
- **Scallion oil:**  
  - 60 ml neutral oil  
  - 1/2 cup chopped scallions  
  - pinch salt
- **Cucumber slices** + **herbs** (mint/cilantro) optional
- **Chả lụa / Vietnamese ham** (optional premium add-on)

---

## Prep (20 minutes)

### 1) Batter
1. Whisk rice flour + tapioca + salt.
2. Add water gradually, whisking smooth.
3. Stir in 1 Tbsp oil.
4. Rest 30–60 minutes (room temp).  
5. Stir again right before steaming.

### 2) Filling
1. Heat 1 Tbsp oil in a pan over medium heat.
2. Sauté onion/shallot (and garlic if using) until soft.
3. Add ground pork; break up and cook until no pink remains.
4. Add chopped mushrooms, fish sauce, sugar, pepper.
5. Cook until mixture looks fairly dry (no watery pooling). Cool.

### 3) Sauce + scallion oil
- Mix nước chấm until sugar dissolves.
- Warm oil and pour over scallions + salt for scallion oil.

---

## Steaming + Rolling Method (Nonstick Pan + Lid)

### Pan setup
- Use a wide nonstick pan (26–30 cm).
- Lightly oil the pan (wipe with oiled paper towel).
- Heat to **medium** (not high—thin sheets set fast).

### 1) Steam one sheet
1. Stir batter well.
2. Ladle a small amount (start with **60–80 ml**) into the pan.
3. Swirl quickly to form a thin, even layer.
4. Cover with lid and steam **45–75 seconds** until set and glossy.
   - It should look translucent and not wet.

### 2) Fill and roll
1. Uncover.
2. Add **1–2 Tbsp** filling along one edge.
3. Use a spatula to lift and roll into a tight cylinder.
4. Transfer to a plate or tray.

Repeat, oil-wiping the pan lightly between sheets as needed.

> **Consistency tip:** keep sheets thin. Thick sheets look dull and feel heavy.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sheet:** silky, slightly elastic (doesn’t tear easily).
- **Filling:** savory, not wet (wet filling tears sheets).
- **Look:** glossy rolls, not matte (matte often means overcooked/dry pan).

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
**Meal box target:**
- 10–14 rolls (depends on roll size)
- Scallion oil: **15–20 ml** cup
- Nước chấm: **30–40 ml** cup
- Fried shallots: **1–2 Tbsp** cup

**Optional add-ons:**
- Chả lụa slices
- Extra filling cup
- Extra sauce cup

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Assume 1 order = 12 rolls average:
- Multiply batter + filling by **2.5×** for 10 orders.
- Pre-portion filling into 10 cups for speed.
- Bottle nước chấm; portion into cups.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
To keep it looking great:
- Container A: rolls (don’t drown in sauce)
- Cup B: nước chấm (sealed)
- Cup C: scallion oil (sealed)
- Cup D: fried shallots (sealed, dry)
- Optional: herbs/cucumber pack (dry, with paper towel)

Label: “Drizzle scallion oil, sprinkle shallots, dip in nước chấm.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best same day.
- Rolls can be gently reheated by steaming 1–2 minutes.
- Keep sauce refrigerated 3–5 days.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Gluten: none in base (verify sauces/toppings)
