# Vegan Thai Khao Soi (Northern Thai Coconut Curry Noodles) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card (Vegetarian, Non-Indian)

A premium vegetarian noodle soup: creamy coconut curry broth, noodles, and a crunchy topping.  
This version is designed for **home kitchens** and **pickup/delivery** with smart packaging (crispy topping kept separate).

**Reference (for grounding):** WoonHeng — Vegan Thai Khao Soi  
https://woonheng.com/vegan-thai-khao-soi/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai (Northern)
- **Type:** Vegetarian main (vegan)
- **Yield:** 3–4 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~40–50 minutes
- **Skill level:** Medium (broth balance + topping workflow)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Premium look:** creamy orange broth + noodle nest + crunchy topper + lime.
- **High perceived value:** “restaurant soup” experience at home.
- **Delivery-friendly:** pack crispy topping separately; broth stays great.
- **Upsells:** extra tofu/mushrooms, extra broth, chili oil cup.

---

## Components (what makes it “Khao Soi”)
1) Coconut curry broth (aromatic + creamy)  
2) Noodles (egg noodles are classic; use vegan noodles if needed)  
3) Crunch topper (crispy noodles or crispy wonton strips)  
4) Condiments (lime + pickles + chili)

---

## Ingredients (3–4 portions)

## A) Broth base
- **3 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **1 medium onion**, thin slices
- **4 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** ginger, minced
- **2–3 Tbsp** Thai red curry paste *(check vegan)*
- **5 cups (1.2 L)** vegetable stock
- **1 cup (240 ml)** coconut milk *(add more for extra creamy)*
- **15 g** palm sugar (or 1 Tbsp brown sugar)
- **3 Tbsp** vegan “fish sauce” *(or soy sauce; adjust to taste)*
- **1/4 tsp** mushroom seasoning *(optional; adds depth)*

## B) Protein + veg (pick 1–2)
- **250–300 g** mushrooms (king oyster/shiitake/button), sliced  
- **250–350 g** firm tofu, cubed (or crispy tofu)
- Optional veg: bok choy, mustard greens, or napa cabbage (adds volume)

## C) Noodles
- **3 servings** noodles  
  - Classic: egg noodles (not vegan)  
  - Vegan options: wheat noodles, ramen noodles (egg-free), or rice noodles  
- Reserve some noodles for “crispy topper” if doing crispy noodles method.

## D) Crunch topper (choose ONE)
### Option 1 (easiest + consistent): Crispy wonton strips
- 6–8 wonton wrappers *(check vegan)*
- Oil for frying or air-fryer method

### Option 2 (more classic): Crispy noodles
- 1 serving noodles, cooked and dried well, then fried

## E) Condiments (very important)
- Lime wedges
- Pickled mustard greens (if available) OR quick pickled red onion
- Chili oil or chili flakes
- Chopped cilantro
- Optional: sliced shallots

---

## Equipment
- Pot (5–6 L)
- Knife + cutting board
- Strainer
- Small pan or air fryer for crunchy topper
- Sauce cups / containers for packaging

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Slice onion, mince garlic + ginger.
2. Slice mushrooms; cube tofu.
3. Prepare noodle water pot.
4. Decide your crunchy topper method (wonton strips is fastest).

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## Step 1 — Make the broth (15–20 minutes)
1. Heat oil in a pot over **medium** heat.
2. Add onion and cook **3–4 minutes** until softened.
3. Add garlic + ginger; cook **30 seconds** until fragrant.
4. Add red curry paste; stir-fry **1 minute** to bloom aroma.
5. Add vegetable stock, coconut milk, palm sugar, vegan fish sauce (or soy), and mushroom seasoning (optional).
6. Bring to a gentle simmer.
7. Add mushrooms (and optional greens). Simmer **8–10 minutes**.
8. Add tofu cubes in the last **5 minutes** to warm through (or add crispy tofu at serve time).

**Taste balance (important):**
- Too salty → add more stock
- Too flat → add a little more curry paste or vegan fish sauce
- Too spicy → add coconut milk
- Needs brightness → lime at serving

---

## Step 2 — Cook noodles (6–8 minutes)
1. Cook noodles according to package.
2. Drain and rinse briefly if very starchy (depends on noodle).
3. Keep warm.

---

## Step 3 — Make the crunchy topper (pick one)

### Option 1: Crispy wonton strips (best for delivery)
1. Cut wrappers into thin strips.
2. Fry in 170–180°C oil for **30–60 seconds** until golden (stir constantly).  
   OR air fry at 190–200°C with a light oil spray until crisp.
3. Drain well and cool.

### Option 2: Crispy noodles (classic)
1. Cook 1 serving noodles, drain very well.
2. Spread on tray to dry 10–15 minutes (important).
3. Fry in hot oil until crisp.

---

## Step 4 — Assemble bowls (RiceMap24 service)
Per bowl:
1. Add noodles to bowl (or noodle compartment).
2. Ladle hot broth + mushrooms/tofu over noodles.
3. Add crunchy topper **separately** for delivery.
4. Pack condiments: lime wedge, pickles, chili oil cup, cilantro.

---

## Quality Checks
- Broth is creamy and aromatic, not watery.
- Noodles are not overcooked.
- Crunch topper stays crisp (packed separate).
- Lime/pickles included (makes it feel “restaurant”).

## Portioning (Per order)

- Broth: **350–450 ml**
- Mushrooms/tofu: generous scoop
- Crunch topper: small handful
- Condiments: 1–2 cups + lime wedge

**Upsells**
- Extra tofu
- Extra broth cup
- Extra crunchy topper
- Chili oil cup

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
- Stock: **12 L**
- Coconut milk: **2.4 L** (or more for creamier)
- Curry paste: **20–25 Tbsp** (brand strength varies)
- Mushrooms: **2.5–3 kg**
- Tofu: **2.5–3.5 kg**
- Crunch topper: make large batch and keep dry

**Ops tip:** Keep broth hot in one pot. Cook noodles in batches and portion quickly.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
- **Container A:** hot broth + mushrooms/tofu (leak-proof)
- **Container B:** noodles (separate)
- **Cup C:** crunchy topper (dry)
- **Cup D:** chili oil
- **Cup E:** pickles + cilantro
- **Lime wedge** in a small bag

Label: “Pour broth over noodles. Add crispy topping last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Broth keeps 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat broth to simmer.
- Cook noodles fresh if possible (best texture).

---

## Allergens
- Coconut
- Soy (if using soy-based sauce)
- Wheat (noodles/wonton strips) depending on products
- Always check curry paste ingredients for shrimp/fish if vegan.
