A fast, repeatable ramen bowl built on a rich chicken stock base + miso tare, finished with noodles and simple toppings.

## Core Concept (How this ramen works)
Ramen is built from:
1) **Broth** (hot liquid)  
2) **Tare** (seasoning concentrate)  
3) **Aromatic oil** (optional)  
4) **Noodles + toppings**

This card makes a **repeatable miso tare** and gives per-bowl weights for consistent flavor.

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### A) Broth base
- **1200 ml** good chicken stock (store-bought or homemade)
- **10 g** grated garlic (about 2 cloves)
- **10 g** grated ginger (about 2 tsp)
- **15 ml** sesame oil (1 Tbsp)

### B) Miso Tare (seasoning concentrate — makes 4 bowls)
- **80 g** miso paste *(white or awase; choose one and keep consistent)*
- **20 ml** soy sauce (4 tsp)
- **10 ml** mirin (2 tsp)
- **10 g** sugar (about 2 tsp)
- **10 ml** rice vinegar (2 tsp) *(optional brightness)*
- **10 ml** sesame oil (2 tsp)
- **2 g** ground white pepper (about 1/2 tsp) *(optional)*

> If you want a more “restaurant” miso depth, add **1 tsp doubanjiang** (Sichuan chili bean paste) as some miso ramen styles do, but keep it optional.

### C) Noodles
- Fresh ramen noodles: **4 portions** (follow package weight)  
  *(If dried, follow package weight + timing.)*

### D) Simple toppings (operator choice)
- Chashu slices (optional)
- Soft-boiled egg (ajitsuke tamago), optional
- Corn kernels
- Sautéed mushrooms
- Scallions
- Nori

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Tare:** Whisk all tare ingredients until smooth.  
   Divide into **4 equal portions** (about **30 g** per bowl).
2. **Broth aromatics:** Grate garlic and ginger.
3. **Toppings:** Prep and portion toppings.
4. Bring noodle water to a boil.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Heat broth
1. Add chicken stock to a pot.
2. Add grated garlic + ginger.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer for **5 minutes**.
4. Stir in **1 Tbsp sesame oil**.

## 2) Cook noodles (do this last-minute)
1. Cook noodles according to package timing.
2. Drain thoroughly (shake off water).

## 3) Build each bowl (repeatable per bowl)
**Per bowl:**
- **30 g miso tare**
- **300 ml hot broth**
- **1 portion noodles**
- Toppings

**Assembly:**
1. Add tare to bowl.
2. Add hot broth and whisk/stir to dissolve tare fully.
3. Add noodles.
4. Add toppings.

> Taste once: if your stock is saltier/less salty, adjust tare by ±5 g per bowl next batch.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Tare dissolved:** No miso clumps.
- **Broth:** Hot and aromatic; not greasy.
- **Noodles:** Springy; not overcooked.
- **Balance:** Savory miso with mild sweetness; soy supports, not dominates.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Quick scale targets (10 bowls)
- Chicken stock: **3000 ml**
- Garlic: **25 g**
- Ginger: **25 g**
- Sesame oil (broth): **37.5 ml**

**Miso tare (10 bowls):**
- Miso: **200 g**
- Soy sauce: **50 ml**
- Mirin: **25 ml**
- Sugar: **25 g**
- Rice vinegar: **25 ml**
- Sesame oil: **25 ml**
- White pepper: **5 g (optional)**

Portion tare into **10 cups of 30 g** each.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best ramen delivery method (non-negotiable):**
- Container A: hot broth (sealed)
- Container B: noodles (lightly oiled; separate)
- Container C: tare cup + toppings

Label: “Add tare to bowl, pour in broth, add noodles, then toppings.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Tare keeps **5–7 days** refrigerated (covered).
- Broth keeps **3 days** refrigerated or can be frozen.
- Noodles best cooked fresh; cooked noodles can clump.
