# Premium Spring Rolls (3 Sellable Formats) — RiceMap24 Home Kitchen Standard

Below are **three “premium” spring roll formats** that work extremely well for **pickup/delivery** and for **menu upsells**.  
They’re designed to look good, stay crisp/fresh, and be easy to portion into **4/6/8/10-pack** boxes.

# 1) Premium Fresh Spring Rolls (Gỏi Cuốn) — Shrimp + Pork + Herb Mix

## Why it’s “premium”
- Looks beautiful (shrimp visible through rice paper)
- Light, fresh, **high perceived value**
- Great upsell with **two dipping sauces** (peanut + nuoc cham)

## Yield
- 10–12 rolls (standard size)

## Ingredients
### Proteins
- **450 g shrimp**, peeled/deveined
- **450 g pork belly** (or pork shoulder for leaner)

### Noodles + veg
- **250 g** thin rice vermicelli (cooked, cooled)
- Lettuce leaves (soft, wide)
- Fresh herbs: mint + Thai basil + cilantro (mix)

### Wrap
- **Rice paper** (bánh tráng), medium thickness

### Optional “premium add-ons”
- Cucumber sticks
- Pickled carrot/daikon (small amount)
- Fried shallots (tiny sprinkle inside)

## Cooking: shrimp + pork
1. **Pork:** simmer pork belly gently until cooked/tender (45–60 min depending on cut). Cool and slice thin.
2. **Shrimp:** simmer 2–3 minutes until pink; chill and slice lengthwise (presentation).

## Assembly workflow (fast + consistent)
**Station setup:** bowl of warm water, noodles, sliced proteins, herbs, lettuce, trays.

1. Dip 1 rice paper **1–2 seconds** in warm water (don’t over-soak).
2. Lay on board, wait 10–15 sec to soften.
3. Add lettuce, herbs, noodles.
4. Add pork slices.
5. Place shrimp halves near the top so they show through when rolled.
6. Roll tight: fold sides in, then roll forward.

## Packaging (Pickup-friendly)
- Put each roll in a **paper divider** or parchment strip (prevents sticking).
- Include a **small damp paper towel** in the box corner (not touching rolls) to keep humidity stable.
- Sell as **4/6/8/10 pack**.

## Dips (sell 2 cups)
- Peanut sauce (see section 4)
- Nuoc cham (quick): fish sauce + water + sugar + lime + garlic + chili (cup)

# 2) Premium Fried Imperial Rolls (Chả Giò) — Pork + Shrimp + Crunchy Veg

## Why it’s “premium”
- Crispy, golden, “restaurant feel”
- Works as side order and as “add to rice/noodle bowl”
- Great for **party boxes**

## Yield
- 20–25 rolls (small/medium)

## Ingredients (classic premium filling)
- **450 g ground pork**
- **200 g shrimp**, chopped
- **Glass noodles** (mung bean/cellophane), soaked + chopped
- **Wood ear mushrooms**, soaked + chopped
- **Carrot**, grated/julienne
- **Jicama** (or kohlrabi), julienne (adds crunch)
- Shallot + garlic
- **Egg white or egg** (binder)
- Fish sauce, sugar, white pepper

## Wrapper
- Vietnamese spring roll wrappers (bánh tráng for frying) OR dedicated spring roll wrappers

## Filling rules (for crisp rolls)
- Drain noodles and veg **very well** (wet filling = soggy).
- Mix and rest 10 minutes to hydrate.
- Do not overfill.

## Rolling (tight = crisp)
1. Lay wrapper, add 1–1.5 Tbsp filling.
2. Roll tight, seal with a little water/egg wash.

## Frying (2-stage for best crunch)
- **First fry:** 160°C for 6–8 min (set structure)
- Rest 10 minutes
- **Second fry:** 180°C for 2–3 min (deep golden + crisp)

## Packaging (Pickup-friendly)
- Vent 30–60 seconds before sealing container (steam kills crisp).
- Include dipping sauce separately.

## Dip options (sell as upgrade)
- Nuoc cham cup (classic)
- Sweet chili + lime cup
- Peanut sauce cup (works surprisingly well)

# 3) Premium “Roll-It-Yourself” Nem Nướng Cuốn Kit (Nha Trang Style)

This is a **premium kit**: customers assemble at home.  
It’s not typical takeout — it’s an experience product (great for social media).

## Why it’s “premium”
- Feels like a “set meal” / tasting
- Very photogenic
- Higher ticket price than normal rolls

## What’s inside (per kit = 2 people)
- Rice paper sheets
- Cooked vermicelli
- Herb mix + lettuce
- Cucumber + pickles
- **Nem nướng** (grilled pork paste) slices
- Optional crunchy add: **fried egg roll skins** (cut into strips)
- Signature peanut sauce (pork-peanut style)

## Simple nem nướng approach (home-kitchen friendly)
- Use a seasoned pork paste mix (store-bought Vietnamese nem nướng mix) OR
- Make pork paste: ground pork + garlic + fish sauce + sugar + pepper + cornstarch; shape into logs and grill/pan-sear.

## Packaging (key)
- Separate compartments:
  - Herbs/lettuce (dry + crisp)
  - Noodles (cool)
  - Pork (warm or room temp)
  - Sauce cups
  - Rice paper (dry pack)
- Include a “how-to” card (1-minute assembly steps).

# 4) Peanut Dipping Sauce (Premium, Works for Fresh + Kits)

## Yield
- ~1.5 cups (enough for 15–20 rolls depending on dip size)

## Ingredients
- 1 Tbsp neutral oil
- 1 Tbsp garlic, minced
- 1 cup water (or stock)
- 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
- 1/2 cup peanut butter
- Optional: toasted peanuts
- Optional: sriracha

## Method (5 minutes)
1. Sauté garlic in oil 30 seconds.
2. Add hoisin + peanut butter and stir.
3. Add water gradually to desired thickness.
4. Finish with peanuts and/or sriracha.

**For delivery:** keep it thicker (less water) so it doesn’t separate.

# Operational Tips (Home Kitchen)
- Make **one sauce batch** and reuse across roll types.
- Pre-portion sauces into cups (30–40 ml standard).
- For crisp items: vent containers before closing.
- For fresh rolls: parchment dividers prevent sticking and keep the look clean.
