# Yum Som-O (Thai Pomelo Salad) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A premium Thai salad that **looks stunning**: juicy pomelo segments, toasted coconut, peanuts, herbs, fried shallots, and a bright sweet–salty–sour dressing.  
Not a “default takeaway” in many places, but perfect as a **signature salad box** or side dish for home-kitchen sellers.

**Source recipe (reference):** Hot Thai Kitchen — Pomelo Salad (Yum Som-O)  
https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/yum-som-o/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Salad (no cook; optional shrimp)
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~20–25 min
- **Total time:** ~25 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (knife work + dressing balance)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24
- **Photogenic:** pink pomelo + green herbs + golden coconut/shallots.
- **Great margins:** mostly produce + pantry items.
- **Delivery-friendly:** pack dressing separately; salad stays crisp.
- **Upsells:** add shrimp, extra fried shallots, extra toasted coconut, extra dressing.

## Equipment
- Mixing bowl
- Small bowl (dressing)
- Knife + cutting board
- Dry pan (toast coconut)
- Optional: mortar & pestle (for classic dressing)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Salad base
- **600–700 g** pomelo flesh (peeled; membranes removed), broken into bite-size chunks
- **1/4 cup** shredded coconut, toasted
- **1/4 cup** roasted peanuts, coarsely crushed
- **2 Tbsp** fried shallots (store-bought is fine)
- **2** makrut/kaffir lime leaves, finely slivered (optional but beautiful)
- **1–2** stalks lemongrass (bottom tender part), very thinly sliced (optional)
- **1/2 cup** mint leaves
- **1/2 cup** cilantro leaves
- Optional crunch: **1/2** cucumber, thin slices (nice for photos)

## B) Optional protein (add-on)
Pick ONE (keep small and light so salad stays “fresh”):
- **200–250 g** cooked shrimp, peeled (best add-on)
- **200 g** shredded cooked chicken (works, but shrimp is more “Thai”)

---

## C) Dressing (sweet–salty–sour, Thai style)
Use Hot Thai Kitchen’s method as the reference (mortar version is classic).  
For a consistent RiceMap24 batch, use these targets:

- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **3 Tbsp** lime juice
- **2 Tbsp** palm sugar (or light brown sugar)
- **1–2** Thai chilies (to taste), minced

**Optional depth (recommended if you have it):**
- **1 tsp** roasted chili paste (nam prik pao) OR a tiny pinch of chili flakes

> Dressing should taste bold; it mellows once tossed with pomelo.

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Toast coconut (3–4 minutes)
1. Add shredded coconut to a dry pan over medium heat.
2. Stir constantly until golden and fragrant.
3. Cool completely.

### 2) Prep pomelo (most important)
1. Peel pomelo, remove thick pith.
2. Separate segments and remove the membranes.
3. Break flesh into bite-size chunks (don’t mash).

### 3) Prep herbs + crunchy toppings
- Wash and dry herbs well.
- Crush peanuts.
- Measure fried shallots.

---

## Dressing Method (Repeatable)
1. If using mortar: pound chilies, then add palm sugar and pound until dissolved.
2. Add fish sauce and lime juice; stir to combine.
3. Taste and adjust:
   - Too salty → more lime
   - Too sour → a little more sugar
   - Not enough punch → more fish sauce (small amounts)

---

## Assembly (Best Practice)
1. In a large bowl, combine pomelo + herbs + peanuts + toasted coconut.
2. Add dressing and toss **gently** (use hands or a large spoon).
3. Add fried shallots and toss once more.
4. Top with makrut lime leaf slivers and extra coconut for presentation.

> Add fried shallots at the end so they stay crisp.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Pomelo:** juicy, not crushed.
- **Balance:** sweet–salty–sour with chili lift.
- **Texture:** crisp herbs + crunchy toppings; not watery.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning
- Salad: **220–280 g** per portion
- Optional shrimp add-on: **50–70 g** per portion
- Dressing: **20–30 ml** per portion (or toss before packing if eating soon)

---

## Scaling (10 portions)
Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Pomelo: ~1.5–1.8 kg flesh
- Dressing: 2.5× (bottle it; shake before use)
- Toppings: 2.5×

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended (Critical)
For best look + texture:
- Container A: pomelo + herbs + toppings (keep dry)
- Cup B: dressing (sealed)
- Cup C (optional): shrimp
- Cup D (optional): extra fried shallots

Label: “Pour dressing, toss gently, top with shallots.”

**Holding time:** Great for 3–6 hours chilled.

---

## Storage
- Best fresh. If needed, keep components separate and assemble within 24 hours.

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Shellfish (if shrimp)
