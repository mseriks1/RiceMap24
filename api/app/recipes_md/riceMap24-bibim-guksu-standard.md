# Bibim Guksu (비빔국수) — Korean Spicy Cold Noodles — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card (Vegetarian, Non-Indian)

A premium vegetarian cold noodle dish: vibrant red gochujang sauce, crisp vegetables, and sesame aroma.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 as a **summer bestseller** and a “fresh, spicy” option.

**Reference (for grounding):** Korean Bapsang — Bibim Guksu  
https://www.koreanbapsang.com/bibim-guksu-korean-spicy-cold-noodles/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Korean
- **Type:** Vegetarian main (vegan-possible)
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~25 minutes (plus optional chill)
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Premium look:** bright red sauce + colorful veg + sesame + nori.
- **Delivery/pickup friendly:** pack noodles + sauce + veg separately.
- **Fast production:** boil noodles, mix sauce, assemble.
- **Upsells:** extra sauce cup, extra veg, add tofu, extra sesame/nori pack.

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Noodles
- **400 g** somyeon (Korean thin wheat noodles)  
  - Substitute: thin wheat noodles or soba (texture differs)

## B) Sauce (the identity)
- **4 Tbsp** gochujang
- **2 Tbsp** gochugaru *(optional for more heat and color)*
- **3 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **3 Tbsp** sugar (or 2 Tbsp honey if not vegan)
- **3 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **2 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **2 Tbsp** water
- **2 cloves** garlic, grated/minced
- **1 Tbsp** toasted sesame seeds (plus more to finish)

**Flavor target:** spicy-sweet-tangy with sesame aroma.

## C) Vegetables (premium look)
- **1 cucumber**, julienne
- **1 carrot**, julienne
- **4–6** leaves napa cabbage (optional), thin slices
- Optional: shredded purple cabbage (adds color)
- Optional: bean sprouts (blanched 30 seconds)

## D) Garnish (highly recommended)
- Thin nori strips (or roasted seaweed)
- Extra sesame seeds
- Sliced scallions

## Optional add-ons (still vegetarian)
- Firm tofu cubes (cold or lightly pan-seared)
- Kimchi (if your customers want; not always vegetarian—check)

---

## Equipment
- Large pot for noodles
- Strainer
- Large mixing bowl
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups for packaging

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Julienne cucumber and carrot (keep crisp and dry).
2. Mix all sauce ingredients in a bowl until smooth.
3. Prepare garnish (nori strips, sesame).

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## Step 1 — Cook noodles (4–6 minutes)
1. Boil noodles per package.
2. Drain and rinse under **cold water** thoroughly to remove starch.
3. Rub noodles gently with your hand while rinsing (important for somyeon).
4. Drain very well.

**Texture tip:** after rinsing, shake and drain hard so noodles aren’t watery.

---

## Step 2 — Mix (service-ready)
Per portion:
1. Add noodles to a bowl.
2. Add **2–3 Tbsp** sauce (to start).
3. Toss quickly until coated.
4. Top with cucumber, carrot, and garnish.

**Taste check:** add more sauce if needed.

---

## RiceMap24 Pickup/Delivery Assembly (best practice)
To keep it premium and crisp:
- **Container A:** cooked noodles (plain, drained well)
- **Cup B:** sauce (40–60 ml)
- **Cup C:** veg pack (cucumber + carrot + optional cabbage)
- **Cup D:** garnish pack (sesame + nori)
Customer mixes at home.

**Label:** “Mix noodles + sauce first, add veg & garnish on top.”

---

## Quality Checks
- Noodles are cold and springy (not gummy).
- Sauce coats noodles (not watery).
- Veg is crisp and colorful.
- Sesame + nori visible (premium look).

---

## Portioning
Per order:
- Noodles: **100 g dry** (about 1 portion)
- Sauce: **40–60 ml**
- Veg: **100–150 g**

**Upsells**
- Extra sauce cup
- Add tofu (extra protein)
- Extra veg pack
- Extra nori/sesame garnish

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
- Noodles: **1 kg**
- Sauce: multiply by **2.5×**
- Veg: 2.5×
Prep sauce ahead and keep chilled.

---

## Storage & Reheat
This is meant to be served cold.
- Noodles: best same day (can hold chilled 1 day, but texture declines).
- Sauce: 3–5 days refrigerated.

---

## Allergens
- Wheat (somyeon)
- Soy (soy sauce, gochujang may contain soy)
- Sesame (sesame oil/seeds)
- Gluten possible (gochujang brand)
