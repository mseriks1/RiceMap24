# Thai Green Curry Chicken (Gaeng Keow Wan Gai) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A fragrant Thai green curry with coconut milk, tender chicken, and crisp-tender vegetables. A reliable “premium curry bowl” with spice-level upsells via extra curry paste.

**Source recipe:** Hot Thai Kitchen — “Thai Green Curry (Gaeng Keow Wan)”  
https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/green-curry-new-2/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai • Curry • Coconut
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min  
- **Total time:** ~30–35 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (paste frying + final seasoning balance)

---

## Equipment
- Medium pot or deep skillet
- Ladle
- Cutting board + knife
- Measuring cups/spoons

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Green curry strength and saltiness vary a lot by curry paste brand.  
> Use the linked Hot Thai Kitchen recipe for **exact measurements** (especially curry paste amount, fish sauce, sugar, and coconut milk ratio).

### Core
- Green curry paste (homemade or store-bought), amount per source
- Coconut milk (full-fat recommended), amount per source
- Chicken (thigh or breast), sliced, amount per source
- Thai eggplant / bamboo shoots / bell pepper (any of these per source options)
- Thai basil (finish)

### Aromatics & seasoning (per source)
- Fish sauce
- Palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- Kaffir lime leaves (optional)
- Optional: Thai chilies (heat control)

### To serve
- Jasmine rice (portion per bowl)
- Optional: cucumber salad / pickled veg

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Chicken:** Slice thin for quick, tender cooking.
2. **Vegetables:** Prep to similar size so they cook evenly.
3. **Basil:** Pick leaves; keep for the end.
4. **Paste readiness:** Have curry paste measured and ready (curry moves fast once hot).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Fry the curry paste (flavor foundation)
1. Spoon the thick coconut cream from the top of the can into a pot (don’t shake the can if possible).
2. Heat over **medium** until it starts to bubble and the oil begins to separate slightly.
3. Add green curry paste and fry gently until very fragrant (time per source).  
   Stir continuously—don’t let it scorch.

## 2) Build the curry
4. Add the remaining coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Add chicken and simmer gently until just cooked through.
6. Add vegetables and simmer until crisp-tender.

## 3) Finish seasoning (balance)
7. Season with fish sauce + palm sugar per source, tasting as you go.
8. Turn off heat and stir in Thai basil (and lime leaves if using).
9. Optional: add sliced chilies for heat or serve chili on the side.

> **Stop point:** Basil goes in at the end to keep aroma bright.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce:** Creamy, aromatic, not split/oily (gentle simmer).
- **Chicken:** Tender (thin slices + no hard boil).
- **Balance:** Spicy-salty-slightly sweet; adjust with fish sauce/sugar.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** by **2.5×** for coconut milk, paste, chicken, and seasonings.

### Batch notes
- Fry paste per batch for best aroma, or fry a larger paste base carefully and then build in a bigger pot (test first).
- Keep simmer gentle to avoid coconut splitting.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rice in base; curry in separate compartment or on top.
- Offer “spice level” by adding extra curry paste in a small sauce cup (or chili cup).

**Holding time:** Very good; flavors deepen after 30–60 minutes.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2–3 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer; stir well. Avoid hard boil.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Fish (fish sauce), coconut
- **May contain:** Shellfish (some curry pastes contain shrimp paste)
- **Gluten:** Usually no, but check paste brand
