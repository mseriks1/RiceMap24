# Roasted Barley Tea (Mugicha) — RiceMap24 Drink Standard (Zero/Low Sugar)

A classic Japanese everyday drink: **mugicha** (roasted barley tea).  
Great for RiceMap24 because it’s extremely food-friendly, very low cost, and offers a “no sugar” option that balances sweet drinks on the menu.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Iced tea (usually unsweetened)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Japanese
- **Yield:** 1 drink (with batch scaling included)
- **Active time:** 1 minute (if brewed)
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Ingredients (1 drink)
- **250–300 ml** chilled mugicha
- **180–220 g** ice
- Optional: lemon wheel (nice but not required)

---

## Flavor Options (menu toggles)
- **Unsweetened (standard):** no sugar
- **Lightly sweet:** add 10 ml honey syrup
- **Citrus:** add 5–10 ml lemon juice

---

## Step-by-Step (Single Drink)
1. Fill cup with ice.
2. Pour chilled mugicha.
3. Garnish (optional).

---

## Batch Setup (Recommended)

## A) Brew mugicha (for 10 drinks)
Two common formats: tea bags or roasted barley grains.
Use your product label if it differs.

### Tea bag method (most common)
- **3 L** water
- **6–8** mugicha tea bags

Method:
1. Bring water to a boil, turn off heat.
2. Steep bags **10 minutes**.
3. Remove bags, cool, chill.

### Cold-brew method (super easy)
- **3 L** cold water
- **6–8** tea bags
Steep in fridge **6–8 hours**, remove bags.

Portion: **250–300 ml** per drink.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Bottle is ideal.
- Cup with lid works too.

**Label suggestion:** “Unsweetened roasted barley tea.”

---

## Shelf Life
- Brewed mugicha: **2–3 days** refrigerated

---

## Allergens
- Barley (gluten)
