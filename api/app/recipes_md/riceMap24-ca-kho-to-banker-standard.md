# Cá Kho Tộ (Vietnamese Caramelized Braised Fish in Clay Pot) — “Banker Dish” for Home Kitchens — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A true Vietnamese “banker”: fish braised in a savory-sweet caramel sauce with fish sauce, aromatics, and black pepper.  
It’s a home-cooking staple because it’s **simple**, **reheats well**, and tastes amazing over rice — perfect for RiceMap24 pickup/delivery.

**Reference recipe (recommended):** Hungry Huy — Cá Kho Tộ (Vietnamese Caramelized & Braised Fish)  
https://www.hungryhuy.com/ca-kho-to-recipe-vietnamese-braised-fish/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese
- **Style:** Caramelized braise (fish) served with rice
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~60–90 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (caramel + gentle simmer)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **Broad appeal:** sweet-salty, peppery comfort flavor.
- **Fast operations:** one pot; easy portioning; minimal garnish.
- **Delivery winner:** braise holds heat and reheats perfectly.
- **Repeat orders:** sauce gets better after resting.
- **Upsells:** extra fish, extra sauce, add pickled veg, add greens.

---

## Choose Your Fish (Pick ONE)
You want firm fish that won’t fall apart.

### Option A (best banker): Catfish / basa / pangasius steaks
- **1.4–1.6 kg** catfish steaks, 2.5–3 cm thick

### Option B: Salmon (premium, more expensive)
- **1.2–1.4 kg** salmon fillets, cut into thick portions  
*(Shorter braise time; handle gently.)*

### Option C: Mackerel (authentic, stronger flavor)
- **1.4–1.6 kg** mackerel steaks

> Banker choice for consistent results: **catfish steaks**.

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Fish + quick marinade
- 1.4–1.6 kg fish (above)
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1 Tbsp** sugar
- **1 tsp** black pepper
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** soy sauce (adds depth and color)

## B) Aromatics
- **1 medium onion**, sliced
- **5 cloves** garlic, minced/smashed
- **2 Tbsp** shallots, minced (optional but very common)
- **2–3** green onions, cut into 5 cm pieces (optional)

## C) Braise liquid
- **300–450 ml** coconut water *(traditional and very good)*  
  - Substitute: water + 1 tsp sugar if needed
- Optional: **1–2** bird’s eye chilies (whole) for gentle heat

## D) Caramel color (Nước màu)
- **3 Tbsp** sugar
- **2 Tbsp** water

## E) Finish (signature)
- Extra cracked black pepper
- Optional: chili slices and scallions for a fresher look

---

## Equipment
- Clay pot (ideal) OR heavy pot/skillet with lid
- Saucepan or same pot for caramel
- Tongs/spatula
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Pat fish dry (dry fish browns better).
2. Mix marinade (fish sauce + sugar + pepper + optional soy).
3. Coat fish and rest 10–15 minutes while you prep aromatics.
4. Slice onion; mince garlic/shallots.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Make caramel (Nước màu) (3–5 minutes)
1. In your pot, combine **3 Tbsp sugar + 2 Tbsp water**.
2. Heat over medium until it turns deep amber (cola color).
3. As soon as it’s amber, move to next step.

**Safety note:** caramel is extremely hot — be careful.

---

## Step 2 — Build the base (3 minutes)
1. Add sliced onion (and shallots) into the caramel and stir quickly.
2. Add garlic and stir 20–30 seconds until fragrant.

---

## Step 3 — Add fish + braise liquid (45–70 minutes)
1. Lay fish in a single layer.
2. Pour in coconut water (start with **300 ml**).
3. Bring to a gentle simmer, then reduce to low heat.
4. Cover partially and simmer:
   - Catfish/mackerel steaks: **45–60 minutes**
   - Salmon: **20–30 minutes** (gentle heat; don’t overcook)

**During braise**
- Spoon sauce over fish occasionally.
- If sauce reduces too fast, add 50–100 ml coconut water/water.
- Turn fish carefully once halfway if needed (or don’t flip; just spoon sauce).

---

## Step 4 — Reduce and glaze (5–10 minutes)
1. Uncover and gently simmer until sauce becomes glossy and coats fish.
2. Add green onion pieces (optional) in the last 2 minutes.
3. Finish with lots of cracked black pepper.

**Sauce target:** not watery — it should cling and lightly pool.

---

## Final Seasoning (important)
Taste and adjust:
- Needs more savory depth → a splash of fish sauce
- Too salty → add a splash of coconut water + tiny pinch sugar
- Needs brightness → serve with lime wedge (optional)

---

## Quality Checks
- **Fish:** holds shape, flakes gently, not mushy.
- **Sauce:** glossy, caramel-brown, sweet-salty balanced.
- **Aroma:** pepper-forward (very Vietnamese).
- **Look:** fish glazed with sauce (photo-friendly).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Braised fish + sauce: **220–280 g** (1 portion steak + sauce)
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Optional side: pickled veg (đồ chua) 60–90 g

**Upsells**
- Extra fish piece
- Extra sauce cup
- Add pickles
- Add sautéed greens (bok choy/spinach)

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6):
- Fish: ~2.5–2.7 kg
- Coconut water: 600–750 ml
- Aromatics: 1.7×
- Caramel sugar: 5 Tbsp sugar + 3–4 Tbsp water

**Ops tip:** Use a wide pot so fish stays in one layer. Over-stacking breaks fish.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Container A: fish + sauce (leak-proof; keep fish flat)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Optional cup: extra sauce
- Optional side cup: pickles/greens

Label: “Reheat gently. Pour sauce over rice.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently to a simmer (avoid hard boil).
- Often tastes even better on day 2.

---

## Allergens
- Fish
- Fish sauce (fish)
- Soy (if using soy sauce)
- Coconut water (coconut)
