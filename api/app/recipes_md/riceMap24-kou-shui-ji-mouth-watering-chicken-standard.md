# Kou Shui Ji (口水鸡) — Sichuan “Mouth-Watering” Chicken in Chili Oil — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

An authentic Sichuan cold chicken dish with a **killer red-oil sauce** (chili oil + black vinegar + garlic + sesame paste option) and a **numbing Sichuan peppercorn finish**.  
This is **not** typical Chinese takeaway; it’s a premium “cold plate” / appetizer that photographs beautifully and sells well as a spicy add-on or rice-bowl topper.

**Source recipe (reference):** Omnivore’s Cookbook — “Saliva Chicken (口水鸡)”  
https://omnivorescookbook.com/saliva-chicken/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Sichuan)
- **Style:** Poached chicken served cold/room temp with chili-oil sauce
- **Yield:** 4 portions (as appetizer) or 2–3 (as main with rice)
- **Active time:** ~25 min
- **Total time:** ~60–90 min (includes chilling + sauce resting)
- **Skill level:** Medium (poaching technique + sauce balance)

---

- **Premium look:** glossy red oil, sesame, scallions, chili, cilantro.
- **Batch-friendly:** cook chicken ahead; sauce holds well; plate fast.
- **Great “mer-salg”:** add-on to rice bowls, noodle bowls, dumplings; “spicy cold plate” upsell.

## Equipment
- Pot with lid (for poaching)
- Bowl with ice water (to chill chicken)
- Knife + cutting board
- Mixing bowl (sauce)
- Small saucepan (optional, to warm aromatics)
- Containers + small sauce cups (for delivery)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Chicken (poached)
- **900 g** chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on recommended)  
  *(You can use chicken legs or a whole chicken; thighs are easiest and juicy.)*
- **1 Tbsp** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- **4 slices** ginger
- **3 scallions**, smashed (white parts)
- **1 tsp** salt
- Water to cover chicken

> Optional: keep 3–5 Tbsp hot poaching broth for the sauce.

---

## B) “Killer Sauce” (Sichuan red-oil sauce)
This sauce should be **bold** and slightly “too strong” by itself (it mellows on chicken).

- **3 Tbsp** chili oil (with some sediment/chili flakes)
- **1–1.5 Tbsp** Chinkiang black vinegar (or rice vinegar)
- **1 Tbsp** light soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** sugar (adjust)
- **1 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **2 Tbsp** Chinese sesame paste (optional but recommended for richness)  
  - Substitute: tahini (works), or 1 Tbsp tahini + 1 Tbsp peanut butter
- **2–3 Tbsp** hot chicken poaching liquid (optional; helps emulsify sauce)
- **2 cloves** garlic, grated
- **1 tsp** ginger, grated
- **1–1.5 tsp** ground toasted Sichuan peppercorn (hua jiao fen), to taste

---

## C) Garnish (makes it photogenic)
- **2 scallions**, thinly sliced
- **Handful cilantro**, chopped
- **Toasted sesame seeds**
- Optional: crushed roasted peanuts
- Optional: thin-sliced red chili

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Toast Sichuan peppercorns (if grinding fresh): dry-toast 1–2 Tbsp peppercorns until fragrant, cool, then grind.
2. Prepare ice bath (big bowl of ice water) for chilling chicken.
3. Mince/prepare garnishes.
4. Mix sauce ingredients (except poaching liquid) in a bowl; set aside.

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Poach chicken gently (25–35 minutes)
1. Put chicken in a pot with ginger, smashed scallions, Shaoxing wine, and salt.
2. Add water to cover chicken by about 2–3 cm.
3. Bring to a boil, then immediately reduce to a **gentle simmer** (small bubbles).
4. Simmer until chicken is cooked through:
   - Thighs/legs: **20–25 minutes** (varies by size)
5. Turn off heat, cover, and rest chicken in hot broth **10 minutes** (juicier).

**Doneness check:** thickest part reaches **74°C** and juices run clear.

---

## 2) Chill chicken (keeps it tender and sliceable)
1. Transfer chicken to ice water for **8–10 minutes** to stop cooking.
2. Pat dry.
3. Optional (best slicing): chill chicken in the fridge **20–30 minutes**.

> Cold chicken slices cleaner and looks more premium.

---

## 3) Make the sauce (emulsify)
1. If using sesame paste, stir it smooth with 1 Tbsp sesame oil first.
2. Add chili oil, vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, garlic, ginger, and ground Sichuan pepper.
3. Add **2–3 Tbsp hot poaching liquid** gradually while stirring to emulsify and thin to a pourable consistency.
4. Taste and adjust:
   - Too salty → add a little sugar or more poaching liquid
   - Too sour → add a little sugar
   - Not enough “punch” → more chili oil or peppercorn powder

**Rest 10 minutes** for flavors to meld (or make ahead and refrigerate).

---

## 4) Slice and plate
1. Remove bones (optional) and slice chicken into bite-size pieces.
2. Arrange in a shallow plate (or portion into bowls).
3. Spoon sauce generously over chicken.
4. Finish with scallions, cilantro, sesame seeds, and optional peanuts/chili.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Chicken:** juicy, not dry (gentle poach + rest is key).
- **Sauce:** glossy, red-oil-forward, balanced by vinegar and sweetness.
- **Peppercorn:** a pleasant numbing “tingle,” not bitter (don’t over-grind/overdose).

## Scaling (10 orders)
- Chicken: **~2.5×** (about 2.25 kg)
- Sauce: **2.5×**
- Make sauce in a squeeze bottle; keep chilled.

**Ops tip:** Cook chicken in the morning, chill, slice to order.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
To keep it looking great:
- Container A: sliced chicken
- Cup B: sauce (sealed) OR pour sauce right before sealing for best visual
- Cup C: garnish (sesame + scallion + cilantro) — keeps it fresh and bright
- For rice bowl: rice separate

Label: “Spicy & numbing (Sichuan peppercorn). Add garnish last.”

---

## Storage & Food Safety
- Cooked chicken: refrigerate and use within **2–3 days**.
- Sauce: refrigerate **3–5 days** (stir before use).

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Sesame (sesame oil/paste)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce brand)
