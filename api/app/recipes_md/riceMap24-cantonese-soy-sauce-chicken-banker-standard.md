# Cantonese Soy Sauce Chicken (豉油鸡 / See Yao Gai) — “Banker Dish” — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Cantonese “banker”: tender chicken gently braised in a fragrant soy sauce master stock with aromatics.  
For RiceMap24, this is ideal because it’s **batch-friendly**, **reheats well**, and sells perfectly with rice + blanched greens.

**Reference recipe (recommended):** The Woks of Life — Soy Sauce Chicken (豉油鸡)  
https://thewoksoflife.com/soy-sauce-chicken/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Cantonese)
- **Style:** Gentle braise (master stock) + sliced chicken + rice
- **Yield:** 6 meal portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 15 min (plus optional chill for clean slicing)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (gentle simmer + timing)

---

## Why this is a “Banker” Dish for RiceMap24
- **Broad appeal:** savory, aromatic, not overly spicy.
- **Operationally great:** one pot; sauce can be reused (master stock concept).
- **Delivery winner:** chicken stays moist; sauce + rice is always a hit.
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra sauce, add egg (tea egg style), add greens.

---

## Choose Your Chicken (Pick ONE)
### Option A (most authentic): Whole chicken
- **1 whole chicken** (1.6–1.9 kg)

### Option B (easier portioning): Bone-in thighs/drumsticks
- **2.0 kg** bone-in pieces

> Whole chicken gives the best texture and presentation. Pieces are easier for portion control.

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Braising liquid (master stock style)
- **250 ml** light soy sauce
- **80 ml** dark soy sauce *(color + depth; optional but recommended)*
- **200 g** rock sugar *(or 150–180 g brown sugar; adjust sweetness)*
- **1.8 L** water (enough to mostly cover chicken; adjust as needed)
- **120 ml** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)

## B) Aromatics + spices
- **8 slices** ginger
- **6 cloves** garlic, smashed
- **4 scallions**, smashed (white parts)
- **2 star anise** (optional but common)
- **1 small cinnamon stick** (optional)
- **1 tsp** whole peppercorns (optional)
- Optional citrus note: 1 strip dried tangerine/orange peel (optional)

## C) Finish (RiceMap24 plating)
- Jasmine rice: **250 g cooked** per order
- Blanched bok choy or gai lan (highly recommended)
- Extra scallions (thin sliced) for garnish

---

## Equipment
- Large pot (8–10 L for whole chicken)
- Tongs + ladle
- Thermometer (recommended for doneness)
- Knife + cutting board
- Bowl with ice water (optional, helps firm skin and stop cooking)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Smash ginger, garlic, scallion whites.
2. Measure soy sauces, sugar, wine, water.
3. Rinse chicken and pat dry.
4. Prepare an ice bath (optional, for best skin + slicing).

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Make the braising stock (10 minutes)
1. Combine water, light soy, dark soy, rock sugar, and Shaoxing wine in a pot.
2. Add ginger, garlic, scallions, and optional spices.
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a **gentle simmer** until sugar dissolves (3–5 minutes).

---

## Step 2 — Braise chicken gently (35–55 minutes)
### Whole chicken method
1. Lower chicken into the pot breast-side down.
2. Keep at a **gentle simmer** (do not hard-boil).
3. Simmer **20 minutes**, then flip chicken breast-side up.
4. Simmer **15–25 minutes** more until cooked through.

**Doneness check:** thickest part reaches **74°C**.

### Bone-in pieces method
- Simmer pieces gently **25–35 minutes** until cooked through.

> Gentle simmer = tender meat and clean presentation.

---

## Step 3 — Rest + optional chill (important for clean slicing)
1. Lift chicken out and let rest 10 minutes.
2. Optional: dip chicken briefly into ice water 1–2 minutes to firm skin, then pat dry.
3. For best slicing, chill 20–30 minutes.

---

## Step 4 — Reduce sauce (optional but recommended)
1. Simmer braising liquid uncovered 10–15 minutes to concentrate.
2. Skim some fat if desired for a cleaner finish.

---

## Step 5 — Slice and serve
1. Chop/slice chicken into serving pieces.
2. Spoon a little sauce over chicken (don’t drown).
3. Serve with rice and blanched greens.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Chicken:** juicy, not stringy (gentle simmer).
- **Color:** beautiful soy-brown glaze (dark soy helps).
- **Sauce:** savory-sweet, aromatic, not bitter.
- **Presentation:** clean slices/pieces, glossy finish.

- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Greens: 80–120 g
- Sauce: 25–40 ml (small cup) or drizzle over chicken

**Upsells**
- Extra chicken
- Extra sauce cup
- Add greens
- Add soy-braised egg (soak boiled eggs in cooled stock overnight)

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Use 3 whole chickens (or ~3.4 kg pieces).
- Make stock in a larger pot; braise chickens one at a time if pot is small.

**Ops tip:** This dish is perfect for “cook once, serve all day.” Keep stock and chicken chilled; reheat portions gently in sauce.

## Storage & Reuse (Master Stock concept)
- Refrigerate stock; fat will solidify on top (remove if desired).
- Stock can be reused 2–3 times if boiled and cooled properly each time (food safety matters).
- Chicken keeps 2–3 days refrigerated.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce, Shaoxing wine brands)
