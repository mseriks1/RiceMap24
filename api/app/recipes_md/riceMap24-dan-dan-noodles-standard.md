# Dan Dan Noodles (Sichuan-Style Spicy Noodles) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Sichuan classic: noodles tossed in a savory-spicy sauce with chili oil, Sichuan pepper, and a topping of minced pork. Big flavor, high demand, and great as a “spice level” menu item.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Dan Dan Noodles”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/dan-dan-noodles/

- **Category:** Chinese (Sichuan) • Noodles • Spicy

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Dan Dan depends on **specific Sichuan pantry items** (e.g., chili oil, Sichuan pepper, sesame paste, ya cai/preserved mustard greens in some versions).  
> Follow the linked source recipe for **exact measurements** and ingredient options.

### A) Noodles
- Chinese wheat noodles (fresh or dried), amount per source
- Optional: a handful of greens (bok choy/yu choy) blanched in the noodle water

### B) Pork topping (per source)
- Ground/minced pork
- Aromatics (garlic/ginger) as specified
- Seasonings (soy sauce/Shaoxing wine/sugar) as specified
- Preserved mustard greens (ya cai) if included in the source version

### C) Dan Dan sauce (per source)
- Chinese sesame paste or tahini (as an alternative per source)
- Soy sauce
- Black vinegar (Chinkiang)
- Chili oil
- Sichuan peppercorn (ground/toasted)
- Sugar (small amount)
- Stock or hot water (to loosen sauce)
- Optional: minced garlic, scallions, etc. per source

### Garnish
- Scallions (sliced)
- Crushed peanuts (optional, per source)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Sauce bowls:** Mix the Dan Dan sauce per source recipe (one big bowl or portion into 4 bowls).
2. **Pork mix:** Prepare the pork topping ingredients and have them ready.
3. **Noodle plan:** Boil noodles last so they’re hot when tossed.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Cook the pork topping
1. Heat a wok/skillet over **medium-high** with a little oil (per source).
2. Add pork and stir-fry until browned and crumbly.
3. Add aromatics and seasonings as directed by the source recipe.
4. Add preserved greens if using. Cook briefly until fragrant.
5. Set aside.

## 2) Boil noodles (and greens)
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
2. Cook noodles according to package timing.
3. Optional: Blanch greens in the same water for the last 30–60 seconds.
4. Drain noodles well.

## 3) Toss and assemble
1. Add drained hot noodles to the sauce bowl(s).
2. Toss thoroughly until evenly coated (add a splash of hot water/stock if needed).
3. Top with pork mixture.
4. Garnish with scallions and peanuts if desired.

**Serve note:** Dan Dan is best eaten immediately.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce:** Coats noodles and is glossy; not dry-pasty.
- **Heat/numbing:** Balanced spice + Sichuan pepper “tingle.”
- **Pork:** Browned and savory, not watery.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** for noodles, sauce, and pork topping by **2.5×**.

### Batch notes
- Make sauce in a large container and portion per bowl.
- Cook noodles in batches to keep timing tight.
- Keep a kettle of hot water/stock to loosen sauce quickly if needed.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best:** Keep components separate:
- Noodles in main box
- Sauce in a cup (or coating lightly)
- Pork topping in a cup  
This prevents noodles from soaking up all sauce before eating.

Label: “Mix sauce + noodles, then add pork topping. Add hot water if you want it looser.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Sauce:** 3–5 days refrigerated (shake/stir before use).
- **Pork topping:** 2–3 days refrigerated.
- **Noodles:** Best cooked fresh for service (texture changes after refrigeration).
