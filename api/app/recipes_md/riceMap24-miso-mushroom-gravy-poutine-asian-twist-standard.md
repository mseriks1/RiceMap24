# Miso Mushroom Gravy Poutine (Asian Twist Side or Main) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A familiar comfort dish (poutine-style fries + gravy) with a Japanese pantry twist: **miso mushroom gravy**.  
Not traditional Asian—this is fusion comfort food that sells well as a share box or a main bowl.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Poutine-style fries bowl
- **Asian twist:** White miso + mushroom umami gravy
- **Yield:** 6 side portions or 4 main portions
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~35–40 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **High-margin side/main:** fries + gravy + cheese.
- **Signature twist:** miso gravy is memorable and “premium.”
- **Pickup friendly:** pack gravy separately to keep fries crisp.
- **Upsells:** extra gravy cup, add chicken, spicy chili crisp drizzle.

---

## Equipment
- Oven/air fryer for fries
- Saucepan for gravy
- Whisk
- Knife + cutting board
- Containers + gravy cups

---

## Ingredients

## A) Fries base
- **1.2 kg** frozen fries (best for consistent home kitchens)
- Salt

## B) Cheese
- **250 g** cheese curds (ideal) OR mozzarella chunks (works)

## C) Miso mushroom gravy (the identity)
- **2 Tbsp** butter (or neutral oil)
- **300 g** mushrooms (shiitake + button mix), finely chopped
- **1 small onion**, minced
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2 Tbsp** all-purpose flour
- **700 ml** beef stock (or veg stock)
- **2 Tbsp** white miso (start with 1.5 Tbsp if strong)
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** rice vinegar (tiny brightness)
- Black pepper
- Optional: 1 tsp sesame oil (finish)

## D) Optional toppings (for sales)
- Sliced scallions
- Chili crisp drizzle
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Pickled jalapeño (if you want a “fusion” heat option)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Cook fries (20–25 minutes)
- Bake or air fry fries until very crisp.
- Salt immediately.

**Crisp rule:** crisp fries are everything. Undercooked fries become soggy fast.

---

## 2) Make miso mushroom gravy (10–12 minutes)
1. Melt butter in a saucepan over medium.
2. Add onion and mushrooms; cook 5–6 minutes until moisture evaporates and mushrooms brown.
3. Add garlic; cook 30 seconds.
4. Stir in flour; cook 1 minute (roux).
5. Slowly whisk in stock.
6. Simmer 4–5 minutes until thickened.
7. Turn heat low and whisk in miso + soy + vinegar.
8. Taste and adjust pepper/salt (miso + soy add salt).

---

## 3) Assemble (two pickup options)
### Option A (best for pickup)
- Pack fries + cheese in container.
- Pack gravy in a sealed cup.
- Customer pours gravy at home.

### Option B (best for immediate eat)
- Pour hot gravy over fries + cheese so cheese melts.
- Garnish scallions/sesame.

---

## Quality Checks
- Gravy thick enough to coat fries (not watery).
- Strong mushroom umami with miso depth.
- Fries crisp (pack gravy separately for delivery).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning
### Side portion
- Fries: **200–250 g**
- Cheese: **30–40 g**
- Gravy: **60–80 ml**

### Main portion
- Fries: **350–450 g**
- Cheese: **60–80 g**
- Gravy: **120–150 ml**

**Upsells**
- Extra gravy cup
- Add chicken
- Chili crisp drizzle
- Scallion garnish pack

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Always separate gravy for pickup delivery.
- Use vented container or crack lid 1 minute for fries.
- Label: “Pour gravy just before eating.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Fries best fresh.
- Gravy holds 2–3 days refrigerated; reheat gently.

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (cheese)
- Wheat (flour)
- Soy (miso, soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce, miso brand)
