# Nasu Dengaku (Japanese Miso-Glazed Eggplant) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card (Vegetarian, Non-Indian)

A premium vegetarian side or main set: tender eggplant with a glossy caramelized miso glaze.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 because it’s photogenic, feels “restaurant,” and travels well when packed correctly.

## Ingredients

## A) Eggplant
- **4 Japanese eggplants** *(or 2 large globe eggplants)*
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (for pan-sear) OR brush oil for broil method
- Pinch salt

## B) Miso glaze (the identity)
- **3 Tbsp** white miso (shiro miso)
- **2 Tbsp** sugar
- **2 Tbsp** mirin
- **1 Tbsp** sake *(or 1 Tbsp water + 1 tsp rice vinegar if needed)*
- Optional: 1/2 tsp grated ginger (tiny)

## C) Finish (premium)
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Sliced scallions
- Optional: shichimi togarashi (Japanese chili spice)
- Optional: yuzu kosho dot (very premium; optional)

---

## Equipment
- Oven broiler (best finish) OR skillet + oven
- Baking tray + parchment/foil
- Small saucepan (glaze)
- Knife + cutting board
- Brush or spoon for glazing

---

## Prep (5 minutes)
1. Split eggplants lengthwise.
2. Score flesh in a crosshatch pattern (don’t cut through skin).
3. Soak in water 5 minutes (optional; reduces bitterness), then pat dry.

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## Step 1 — Make miso glaze (3–5 minutes)
1. In a small saucepan on low heat, combine miso, sugar, mirin, sake.
2. Stir until smooth and glossy (do not boil hard).
3. Remove from heat.

**Glaze should be thick but spreadable.**

---

## Step 2 — Cook eggplant until tender (10–12 minutes)
### Option A (best for home kitchens): Pan + broil
1. Heat skillet with 1 Tbsp oil over medium.
2. Place eggplant cut-side down and cook **4–5 minutes** to brown.
3. Flip cut-side up and transfer to tray.

### Option B (all-oven): Roast then broil
1. Brush cut side lightly with oil.
2. Roast at **200°C** for 12–15 minutes until soft.

---

## Step 3 — Glaze and broil (3–5 minutes)
1. Spread miso glaze generously over cut side.
2. Broil on high **2–4 minutes** until caramelized and bubbling.
3. Watch closely—miso can burn quickly.

---

## Step 4 — Finish
- Sprinkle sesame seeds and scallions.
- Optional: add a tiny pinch of shichimi.

---

## Quality Checks
- Eggplant is soft and tender (spoon cuts easily).
- Glaze is caramelized and glossy, not burnt.
- Sesame/scallion visible.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning
### As side order
- 1 eggplant half per portion (or 2 halves as premium)

### As main set
- 2 halves + rice (250 g cooked) + optional veg side

**Upsells**
- Extra miso glaze cup
- Add rice
- Add tofu or mushrooms

---

## Scaling (10 side portions)
- Eggplant halves: 10–12 halves depending on size
- Glaze: multiply by 2.5×

Make glaze ahead and keep chilled.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
- Container A: eggplant halves (keep flat)
- Cup B: extra glaze (optional)
- Garnish pack: sesame/scallion (optional for freshest look)

Label: “Reheat in oven/air fryer for best glaze.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to 2 days.
- Reheat in oven/air fryer to restore caramelization.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (miso)
- Gluten possible (miso/mirin brands)
