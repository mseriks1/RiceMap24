# Dakdoritang / Dakbokkeumtang (닭도리탕) — Korean Spicy Braised Chicken Stew — “Banker Dish” — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean “banker” for home kitchens: chicken braised with potatoes, carrots, onions, and scallions in a gochujang-gochugaru sauce.  
It’s hearty, photogenic, and sells extremely well with rice. Great for delivery because it reheats beautifully.

**Reference recipe (recommended):** Maangchi — Dakbokkeumtang (Spicy braised chicken)  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/dakbokkeumtang

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Korean
- **Style:** Spicy braised chicken stew + vegetables, served with rice
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~60–75 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (balance sauce + gentle braise)

## Choose Your Chicken (Pick ONE)
### Option A (classic): Bone-in chicken pieces
- **2.0 kg** chicken thighs/drumsticks (bone-in, skin-on)

### Option B (faster): Boneless thigh
- **1.5 kg** boneless chicken thighs, large chunks (watch cook time)

> Banker choice: **bone-in** (more flavor, stays juicy after reheat).

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

## A) Vegetables (classic set)
- **700 g** potatoes, chunked (4–5 cm)
- **250 g** carrots, chunked
- **1 large onion**, thick slices
- **4 scallions**, cut 5 cm
- Optional: 1 chili pepper, sliced (for extra color)

## B) Sauce (the identity)
- **3 Tbsp** gochujang
- **2 Tbsp** gochugaru
- **4 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** sugar (adjust)
- **2 Tbsp** mirin or rice wine (optional, helps aroma)
- **6 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** grated ginger (optional but nice)
- **2 cups (480 ml)** water or light chicken stock
- **1 tsp** black pepper
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** sesame oil (finish)

## C) Optional add-ons (great for sales)
- **300 g** Korean rice cakes (tteok) — add in last 10 minutes
- Ramen noodles — add in last 4–5 minutes
- Mushrooms (enoki or shiitake) — add in last 10 minutes

## To serve
- Steamed rice: **250 g cooked** per order
- Optional: kimchi side (extra add-on)

---

## Equipment
- Large pot/Dutch oven (6–8 L)
- Ladle
- Knife + cutting board
- Mixing bowl (sauce)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Cut chicken into serving pieces if needed.
2. Chop potatoes/carrots/onion.
3. Mix sauce ingredients in a bowl until smooth.
4. Prepare rice.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable, RiceMap24-friendly)

## Step 1 — Combine and bring to simmer (10 minutes)
1. Add chicken, potatoes, carrots, and onion to a pot.
2. Pour sauce mixture over.
3. Add 2 cups water/stock.
4. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a **gentle simmer**.

---

## Step 2 — Braise until tender (35–45 minutes)
1. Simmer partially covered until chicken is cooked and tender and potatoes are soft.
2. Stir every 10 minutes to prevent sticking and to coat ingredients.

**Doneness check:** chicken reaches **74°C** at thickest point.

---

## Step 3 — Finish (10 minutes)
1. Add scallions (and optional mushrooms) and simmer 5 minutes.
2. Add optional tteok (rice cakes) in the last 10 minutes.
3. Taste and adjust:
   - Too salty → add a splash of water
   - Not sweet enough → pinch more sugar
   - Want more heat → more gochugaru
   - Want more depth → a small splash soy or mirin
4. Optional: finish with 1 Tbsp sesame oil.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sauce:** thick enough to cling, not watery.
- **Chicken:** tender, not dry.
- **Potatoes:** soft but not fully disintegrated.
- **Balance:** spicy + sweet + savory.

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **~1.7×** (base makes ~6).
- Chicken: ~3.4 kg bone-in
- Potatoes: ~1.2 kg
- Carrots: ~425 g
- Onion: ~2
- Sauce: 1.7×
Cook in a large pot or two pots for better control.

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps **2–3 days** refrigerated.
- Reheat gently to a simmer; add a splash of water if thick.
- Flavor often improves on day 2.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce, gochujang brand)
