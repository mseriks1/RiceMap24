# Onigiri (Japanese Rice Triangles) — 3 Fillings + Pickup Packaging — RiceMap24 Standard

A premium handheld Asian genre: **onigiri** (rice triangles) with savory fillings wrapped in nori.  
Perfect for **breakfast/lunch**, **high margin**, and **2/3-pack** combos.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Onigiri (handheld rice)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Japanese
- **Yield:** 8 onigiri (with scaling included)
- **Active time:** ~25 minutes
- **Total time:** ~40–50 minutes (includes rice cooking)
- **Skill level:** Medium (rice handling + shaping)

---

## Why onigiri works for RiceMap24
- **Very food-cost efficient** (rice = cheap, value feels high).
- **Great for breakfast** and “light lunch”.
- **Easy combos:** onigiri + miso soup + drink.
- **Batch-friendly** once rice is cooked.

---

## Key success rule
Use **Japanese short-grain rice** (sushi rice).  
Long-grain rice won’t hold shape the same way.

---

## Ingredients (8 onigiri)

## A) Rice base
- **500 g** Japanese short-grain rice (uncooked)
- Water per rice cooker instructions
- **1 tsp** salt (mix into rice after cooking)

## B) Nori
- **4 sheets** nori, cut into strips  
  (or use onigiri nori wrappers for premium packaging)

## C) Salted hands / shaping
- Bowl of water + pinch salt (wet hands prevent sticking)

## D) Fillings (choose 2–3)
Below are 3 classic + sellable fillings.

### Filling 1: Tuna Mayo (banker)
- 2 cans tuna (drained)
- 3–4 Tbsp mayo
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- Optional: 1 tsp sriracha (spicy version)

### Filling 2: Teriyaki Salmon Flakes (premium)
- 250–300 g salmon, cooked and flaked
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 Tbsp honey/sugar
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
Reduce quickly to coat flakes.

### Filling 3: Ume + Sesame (light, classic vibe)
- Umeboshi paste or chopped ume (as available)
- Toasted sesame seeds
Optional: add shredded shiso (if available)

---

## Equipment
- Rice cooker or pot
- Mixing bowls
- Plastic wrap (helps shaping)
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Cook rice (30–35 minutes)
1. Rinse rice until water runs mostly clear.
2. Cook in rice cooker (or pot).
3. Rest 10 minutes after cooking.
4. Mix in 1 tsp salt gently.

**Note:** rice should be warm for shaping.

---

## 2) Prep fillings (5–10 minutes)
Make 1–2 bowls of filling. Keep thick (not watery).

---

## 3) Shape onigiri (10–15 minutes)
### Best method (consistent): plastic wrap
1. Place a square of plastic wrap in hand.
2. Add ~90–110 g warm rice.
3. Make an indent; add 1–2 tsp filling.
4. Cover filling with a little rice.
5. Shape into triangle (press firm but not crushing).

Repeat.

---

## 4) Wrap with nori
Option A (immediate eat): wrap nori directly.  
Option B (pickup premium): use **separate nori wrapper** so it stays crisp.

---

## Portioning
- Rice: 90–110 g per onigiri
- Filling: 10–15 g per onigiri (1–2 tsp)

---

## Batch for 20 onigiri
- Rice: 1.25 kg uncooked
- Nori: 10 sheets
- Fillings: 2.5×

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery) — Critical
To keep nori crisp:
- Wrap rice triangle in plastic
- Pack nori strip separately (or use onigiri film wrappers)
- Include “wrap nori before eating” note.

**Sell formats:** 2-pack / 3-pack / 6-pack (party)

---

## Storage
- Best same day (rice dries in fridge).
- If needed: keep room temp a few hours; avoid long refrigeration.
- Offer “fresh made daily” positioning.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (tuna/salmon)
- Egg (mayo)
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce)
