# Singapore-Style Chicken Satay (with Peanut Sauce) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Tender grilled chicken skewers with a fragrant marinade and classic peanut sauce. Perfect as a “premium add-on” (satay pack) or as a rice box with cucumber + sauce cups.

**Source recipe:** Rasa Malaysia — “Chicken Satay”  
https://rasamalaysia.com/chicken-satay-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Singapore/Malaysia • Grilled • Peanut Sauce
- **Yield:** 4 portions *(e.g., 8–12 skewers depending on size)*
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~50–80 min *(includes marinating; longer is better)*
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (skewer + grill timing)

---

## Equipment
- Skewers (bamboo or metal)
- Grill / grill pan / broiler (oven grill)
- Mixing bowls
- Small pot (for peanut sauce)
- Tongs

## Ingredients (4 portions)

> **Important:** Satay flavor depends on the **marinade ratio** and the **peanut sauce balance**.  
> Use the linked source recipe for **exact measurements**.

### A) Chicken + Marinade (per source)
- Boneless chicken thighs or breast, cut into strips/cubes
- Lemongrass (or lemongrass paste), per source
- Garlic + shallots, per source
- Turmeric powder, per source
- Coriander powder, per source
- Cumin (if included), per source
- Brown sugar, per source
- Salt, per source
- Oil, per source

### B) Peanut sauce (per source)
- Roasted peanuts or peanut butter (depending on source method)
- Coconut milk (if used)
- Tamarind, if used
- Sugar, salt
- Chili (optional)
- Aromatics/spices per source

### C) To serve
- Cucumber slices
- Optional: ketupat (rice cakes) or steamed rice

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Skewers:** If using bamboo skewers, soak in water **30 minutes** to prevent burning.
2. **Chicken:** Cut into uniform strips/cubes for even cooking.
3. **Marinade:** Mix marinade ingredients per source recipe.
4. **Marinate:** Toss chicken with marinade and refrigerate **at least 30 minutes**, ideally **2–4 hours**.
5. **Peanut sauce:** Prepare sauce per source recipe and keep warm.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Skewer + grill
1. Thread chicken onto skewers (don’t pack too tight).
2. Preheat grill/grill pan to **medium-high**.
3. Grill skewers, turning every 1–2 minutes, until cooked through and lightly charred.
4. Rest 2 minutes before serving.

> **Doneness target:** Chicken cooked through (no pink), juicy. Don’t overcook.

## 2) Serve
- Serve skewers with peanut sauce and cucumber.
- Optional: serve with rice or rice cakes.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Char:** Light smoky marks, not burnt.
- **Juiciness:** Thighs stay juicy; breast requires careful timing.
- **Sauce:** Thick enough to cling; balanced sweet–salty–tangy.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** by **2.5×** for marinade and sauce.

### Batch notes
- Grill in batches; keep cooked skewers warm (covered) briefly.
- Portion peanut sauce into cups (consistent cost control).

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Skewers in main tray; cucumber in side compartment.
- Peanut sauce in **sealed cup** (essential).
- Offer as:
  - “Satay Skewer Pack” (8–10 skewers)
  - “Satay Rice Box” (rice + skewers + sauce)

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** for grilled texture.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Cooked skewers:** Refrigerate up to 2 days. Reheat gently (oven/air fryer) to preserve texture.
- **Peanut sauce:** Refrigerate 3–4 days; rewarm gently and stir (may thicken).
