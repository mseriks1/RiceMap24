# Ginataang Langka at Danggit (Filipino Jackfruit in Coconut Milk with Dried Fish) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Creamy coconut jackfruit stew with chili heat and crispy dried fish. This is **distinctly Filipino** but less “tourist-common” than adobo/sinigang, and it sells well as a comforting rice bowl.

**Source recipe:** Kawaling Pinoy — “Ginataang Langka at Danggit”  
https://www.kawalingpinoy.com/ginataang-langka-at-daing/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino
- **Style:** Coconut stew (ginataan)
- **Yield:** 6 servings
- **Active time:** ~10–15 min
- **Total time:** ~40 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (gentle simmer + reduction)

---

## Equipment
- Frying pan (for danggit)
- Wide pan or pot (for stew)
- Tongs / spatula
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring cups/spoons

---

## Ingredients (6 servings)

### Core
- **2 × 16 oz (454 g each)** frozen green jackfruit (langka), thawed and drained well (total **~900 g**)
- **1** onion, peeled and chopped
- **4 cloves** garlic, peeled and minced
- **3** Thai chili peppers, minced (adjust heat)
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1 cup (240 ml)** coconut milk
- **1 cup (240 ml)** coconut cream
- **Salt and pepper**, to taste

### Dried fish
- **8 pieces** danggit (dried fish)

### Oil (from the source method)
- **2 Tbsp** canola oil (for frying fish)
- **1 Tbsp** canola oil (for sautéing aromatics)

> Total oil: **3 Tbsp** (you may use slightly less depending on pan size).

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Jackfruit:** Thaw and drain very well (excess water dilutes coconut sauce).
2. **Aromatics:** Chop onion, mince garlic, mince chilies.
3. **Fish:** Keep danggit dry (helps it fry crisp).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Fry the danggit (3–5 minutes)
1. Heat **2 Tbsp oil** in a frying pan over **medium heat**.
2. Add danggit and lightly fry, turning once or twice, until crisp and lightly browned.
3. Remove and drain on paper towels. Set aside.

> You want it crisp, not dark-brown. It will soften slightly once added back to the stew.

### 2) Build the ginataan base (2–3 minutes)
1. In a wide pan over **medium heat**, heat **1 Tbsp oil**.
2. Add onions and garlic; cook until softened and fragrant.

### 3) Cook jackfruit (2–3 minutes)
1. Add drained jackfruit.
2. Cook, stirring occasionally, for **2–3 minutes**.

### 4) Season + simmer with coconut milk (10 minutes)
1. Add **fish sauce** and cook **1–2 minutes**.
2. Add **coconut milk** and minced chilies.
3. Bring to a gentle simmer.
4. Continue cooking about **10 minutes** or until jackfruit is tender.

### 5) Finish with coconut cream + thicken (5–8 minutes)
1. Add **coconut cream** and stir.
2. Simmer **5–8 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until sauce thickens and reduces.

### 6) Add fish + final seasoning (1–2 minutes)
1. Add fried danggit back into the pan.
2. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
3. Serve hot.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Sauce:** Thick, creamy, clings to jackfruit (not watery).
- **Jackfruit:** Tender but not mushy.
- **Fish:** Still has some crisp edges (best if added at the end).

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
Base serves 6. Multiply by **1.67×**.

### Quick scale targets (10 bowls)
- Jackfruit: **~1.5 kg**
- Onion: **2**
- Garlic: **6–7 cloves**
- Thai chilies: **5**
- Fish sauce: **1 Tbsp + 2 tsp**
- Coconut milk: **400 ml**
- Coconut cream: **400 ml**
- Danggit: **13–14 pieces**
- Oil: **5 Tbsp**

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Best quality:** pack fish separately to preserve crispness:
  - Container A: ginataang langka (stew)
  - Container B: danggit (dry, vented)
  - Container C: rice (separate)
- If not possible: add danggit at the very end before sealing.

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** (fish softens over time).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate stew up to **3 days**.
- Reheat gently; add a splash of water if too thick.
- Fish is best freshly fried.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes
- Fish (danggit, fish sauce)
- Coconut (coconut milk/cream)
