# Chicken Biryani (Indian-Style) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A deeply aromatic rice dish layered with spiced chicken and basmati rice. Great as a premium “family box” or individual rice bowls (portion-controlled).

**Source recipe:** Swasthi’s Recipes — “Chicken Biryani Recipe”  
https://www.indianhealthyrecipes.com/chicken-biryani-recipe/

## Ingredients (≈ 5 portions)

> **Important:** Biryani depends on **exact spice ratios and rice-water timing**.  
> Use the linked Swasthi recipe for **exact measurements** (especially rice, yogurt, aromatics, whole spices, and cook times).

### A) Chicken marinade (per source)
- Chicken (bone-in is common for best flavor), amount per source
- Yogurt
- Ginger-garlic paste
- Chili powder / green chilies
- Turmeric
- Garam masala / biryani masala
- Lemon juice (if included)
- Salt

### B) Rice (per source)
- Basmati rice (aged preferred)
- Whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, etc.)
- Salt
- Water

### C) Biryani base (per source)
- Onions (often fried/ browned)
- Mint leaves
- Cilantro
- Ghee/oil
- Optional: saffron milk / rose water / kewra water (if included)

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Wash + soak basmati rice  
2) Marinate chicken  
3) Brown onions  
4) Parboil rice  
5) Layer chicken + rice  
6) Dum cook (steam)  
7) Rest, fluff, portion

---

## Method (Process Flow — follow source for exact times/amounts)

## 1) Rice soak
1. Rinse basmati rice until water runs mostly clear.
2. Soak per source (typically 20–30 minutes). Drain.

## 2) Marinate chicken
1. Mix marinade ingredients per source.
2. Coat chicken thoroughly.
3. Rest 30 minutes (or longer as source suggests).

## 3) Brown onions
1. Fry sliced onions until deep golden (don’t burn).
2. Reserve some for topping and layering.

## 4) Parboil rice
1. Boil water with whole spices + salt per source.
2. Add drained rice and cook until partially cooked (grain should still have bite; timing per source).
3. Drain immediately.

## 5) Layer and dum cook
1. In a heavy pot, add marinated chicken base (and aromatics as directed).
2. Add a layer of parboiled rice.
3. Add herbs + fried onions + optional saffron milk (per source).
4. Repeat layers if needed.
5. Seal pot tightly (lid + towel or dough if used).
6. Cook on low heat (dum) for the time specified by the source recipe.
7. Rest 10–15 minutes off heat.

## 6) Serve
1. Fluff gently from the sides (don’t mash).
2. Portion into bowls/boxes.

## Quality Checks
- **Rice:** Long grains, separate, not mushy.
- **Chicken:** Fully cooked, tender.
- **Aroma:** Strong but balanced spice; herbs noticeable.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base yields ~5. Multiply by **2×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** by **2×** for chicken, rice, spices, and aromatics.

### Batch notes
- Use a larger heavy pot with a tight lid.
- Keep rice parboil timing consistent for repeatable texture.
- Portion control: weigh cooked biryani per box for consistent cost.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended

- **Reheat:** Microwave with a splash of water, covered, or steam gently.
