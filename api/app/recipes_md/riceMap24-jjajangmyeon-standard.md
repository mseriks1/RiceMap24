# Jjajangmyeon (Korean Black Bean Noodles) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean-Chinese comfort classic: chewy noodles coated in a glossy black bean sauce with pork and vegetables. Great as a premium noodle bowl with “extra sauce” upsell.

## Ingredients (≈ 4 portions)

> **Important:** Jjajangmyeon depends on **chunjang** (Korean black bean paste) and the **paste-frying step**.  
> Use the linked Maangchi recipe for **exact measurements** (paste, pork, vegetables, oil, slurry, sugar, and water/stock).

### A) Noodles
- Fresh jjajang noodles (or dried wheat noodles), amount per source

### B) Sauce base (per source)
- Chunjang (Korean black bean paste)
- Pork (typically pork belly/shoulder, diced)
- Onion (diced)
- Zucchini (diced)
- Potato (diced) *(common)*
- Cabbage (optional, diced)
- Garlic, minced
- Neutral oil

### C) Seasoning + thickener (per source)
- Sugar (small amount)
- Water/stock
- Cornstarch/potato starch slurry (starch + water)

### Optional garnish
- Cucumber (julienned)
- Green peas (optional)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Dice vegetables:** Keep dice size consistent (1–1.5 cm) for even cooking.
2. **Dice pork:** Small cubes cook fast and distribute evenly.
3. **Slurry:** Mix starch + water per source; set aside.
4. **Noodle water:** Put a pot of water on to boil (cook noodles near the end).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Fry the black bean paste (key step)
1. Heat oil in a wok/skillet over **medium heat**.
2. Add chunjang and fry gently, stirring constantly, until it loosens and becomes glossy (time per source).
3. Scoop the fried paste out and set aside (leave some oil in the pan if the source instructs).

> This step reduces bitterness and builds the signature flavor.

## 2) Cook pork + vegetables
4. In the same pan, cook diced pork until it starts to render and brown.
5. Add onions (and other veg) and stir-fry until onions soften.
6. Add remaining vegetables and cook until slightly tender.

## 3) Build sauce
7. Add the fried black bean paste back into the pan and mix well.
8. Add water/stock per source and bring to a simmer.
9. Add sugar per source and simmer until vegetables are cooked through.

## 4) Thicken
10. Stir slurry again and add gradually while stirring until sauce becomes thick and glossy.
11. Simmer 1–2 minutes to cook out starch taste.

## 5) Cook noodles + assemble
12. Boil noodles according to package timing.
13. Drain well (rinse briefly if instructed by your noodle type).
14. Portion noodles into bowls and ladle sauce on top.
15. Garnish with cucumber (optional).

**Serve note:** “Mix well before eating.”

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce:** Thick, glossy, coats noodles (not watery).
- **Bitterness:** Minimal (paste frying helps).
- **Veg:** Tender but not mushy.
- **Noodles:** Chewy, not overcooked.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is ~4. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** by **2.5×** for paste, pork, vegetables, and slurry.

### Batch notes
- Sauce holds well in a warm pot (stir occasionally).
- Cook noodles in batches close to service for best texture.
- Offer “extra sauce cup” upsell (easy margin).

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best:** Keep noodles and sauce separate:
- Container A: cooked noodles
- Container B (sealed): hot sauce
- Garnish cup: cucumber

Label: “Pour sauce over noodles and mix well.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Sauce:** Refrigerate up to 3 days; reheat gently (add a splash of water if too thick).
- **Noodles:** Best fresh; refrigerated noodles can clump and soften.
- **Reheat tip:** If noodles are pre-cooked, toss with a tiny bit of oil to reduce sticking.

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
