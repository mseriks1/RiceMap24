# Polvoron (Filipino Milk Powder Shortbread) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

A classic Filipino no-bake “shortbread” candy-cookie made from **toasted flour + milk powder + butter**.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** shelf-stable, doesn’t melt, giftable, and sells well as **2-pack / 6-pack / 12-pack**.

**Reference recipe:** Kawaling Pinoy — Polvoron  
https://www.kawalingpinoy.com/polvoron/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino
- **Style:** No-bake milk shortbread (pressed cookies)
- **Yield:** ~20–24 pieces (depends on mold size)
- **Active time:** ~25–35 min
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min (includes cooling/toasting flour)
- **Skill level:** Easy (toasting + pressing consistency)

---

## Why it’s great for RiceMap24
- **No melting, no refrigeration required.**
- **Premium look** when wrapped individually.
- **High-margin add-on**: easy multipacks and gift boxes.
- **Batch-friendly**: make once, sell for several days.

## Equipment
- Wide skillet or saucepan (toasting flour)
- Mixing bowl
- Measuring cups/spoons (scale recommended)
- Polvoron mold (best) OR small cookie scoop + firm pressing
- Parchment/wax paper (wrapping)
- Airtight container (storage)

---

## Ingredients (≈20–24 pieces)

### Base
- **2 cups (260 g)** all-purpose flour
- **1 cup (100 g)** powdered milk (full-cream milk powder)
- **3/4 cup (150 g)** granulated sugar *(adjust to taste)*
- **1 cup (225 g)** unsalted butter, melted and cooled slightly

### Optional “Premium” add-ins (choose 0–2)
- **1/2 cup** crushed roasted peanuts
- **1/2 cup** toasted sesame seeds
- **2–3 Tbsp** cocoa powder (chocolate version)
- **1/3 cup** malted milk powder (adds “milk tea” vibe)
- Pinch of salt (recommended if using unsalted butter)

---

## Step 1 — Toast the Flour (Most important for flavor)
1. Place flour in a wide skillet over **medium-low** heat.
2. Stir continuously with a spatula to prevent hot spots.
3. Toast **10–15 minutes** until it smells nutty and turns very light golden.
4. Remove from heat and cool to room temperature.

**Doneness target:** nutty aroma; pale golden.  
**Avoid:** dark brown (bitter) or under-toasted (raw flour taste).

---

## Step 2 — Mix Dry Ingredients (2 minutes)
1. In a large bowl, combine:
   - cooled toasted flour
   - milk powder
   - sugar
   - optional pinch of salt
2. Whisk or stir until evenly mixed.

---

## Step 3 — Add Butter and Form “Sand” (3–5 minutes)
1. Pour in melted butter gradually while mixing.
2. Mix until the texture becomes like **damp sand** that holds together when pressed.

### Texture test
- Grab a handful and squeeze: it should **clump** and hold shape.
- If it’s too dry/crumbly: add **1 Tbsp melted butter** at a time.
- If it’s too wet/greasy: add **1 Tbsp toasted flour** at a time.

---

## Step 4 — Press and Shape (10–15 minutes)
### Best method (mold)
1. Pack mixture firmly into polvoron mold.
2. Press hard so it compacts well.
3. Release onto parchment.

### No mold method (still works)
1. Use a tablespoon or small cookie scoop.
2. Pack and press tightly into a compact puck/brick.
3. Place on parchment.

**Consistency rule:** press firmly to prevent crumbling in packaging.

---

## Step 5 — Chill (Optional but improves sturdiness)
- Chill pieces in the fridge **15–30 minutes** to firm up before wrapping.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Wrap each piece in wax paper or parchment squares (very “bakery/gift” feel).
- Place in a small box with dividers or tight fit so pieces don’t bounce.
- Add label: “Keep airtight. Best at room temperature.”

---

## Storage
- Airtight at room temp: **5–7 days**
- Refrigerated: up to **2 weeks** (bring to room temp before eating)
- Freeze: up to **1 month** (wrap tightly; thaw at room temp)

---

## Quality Checks
- **Flavor:** nutty, milky, not raw flour.
- **Texture:** crumbly-shortbread but holds together when bitten.
- **Look:** clean edges; not greasy.

---

## Allergen Notes
- Wheat (flour)
- Dairy (milk powder, butter)
- Nuts (if using peanuts)
