## Prep Plan (Recommended order)

2) Blend spinach to puree

5) Add paneer + finish  
6) Portion with rice

---

## Method (Process Flow — follow source for exact times/amounts)

## 1) Blanch spinach (for color)
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
2. Add spinach and blanch briefly (time per source; usually 1–2 minutes).
3. Transfer immediately to ice water to preserve bright green color.
4. Drain well and blend to a smooth puree.

## 2) Build curry base
1. Heat oil/ghee in a pan over medium.
2. Add cumin (if using seeds), then onion and sauté until golden.
3. Add ginger-garlic and cook until fragrant.
4. Add tomatoes (or puree) and cook until thick and no longer raw.
5. Add spices per source and stir briefly.

## 3) Add spinach puree + simmer
1. Add spinach puree and simmer gently.
2. Adjust consistency with water as needed (per source).
3. Finish with cream/butter if using.

## 4) Add paneer
1. Add paneer cubes and simmer briefly (don’t overcook paneer).
2. Add garam masala and optional kasuri methi near the end.
3. Taste and adjust salt/spice.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Color:** Bright green (not dull) → blanch + ice bath is key.
- **Texture:** Smooth sauce, paneer tender.
- **Flavor:** Balanced spices, not bitter.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is ~4. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply all **exact source measurements** by 2.5× for spinach, paneer, aromatics, and spices.

### Batch notes
- Make spinach puree first, then cook in a big pot.
- Add paneer near the end for each batch to keep it soft.
