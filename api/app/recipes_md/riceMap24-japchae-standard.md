# Japchae (Korean Glass Noodles Stir-Fry) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean party classic: chewy sweet-potato glass noodles tossed with a savory-sweet soy-sesame sauce, vegetables, and optional beef. Great as a stand-alone bowl or as a side + protein add-on.

## Ingredients (6 portions)
> **Important:** Japchae quality depends on **noodle amount, sauce ratio, and vegetable balance**.  
> Use the linked Maangchi recipe for the **exact measurements** of noodles, seasonings, and optional beef.

### Core components (as in the source recipe)
- Sweet potato glass noodles (dangmyeon)
- Spinach
- Carrot (julienned)
- Onion (sliced)
- Mushrooms (shiitake/wood ear, per source)
- Scallions (optional)
- Optional protein: beef (thinly sliced) or other (per source)

### Seasoning set (per source)
- Soy sauce
- Sugar
- Garlic
- Sesame oil
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Black pepper
- Neutral oil (for stir-frying components)

---

## Prep (Do this before cooking noodles)
1. **Vegetables:** Slice everything evenly (thin strips cook fast and look best).
2. **Spinach:** Rinse and drain.
3. **Protein (optional):** Slice thin; marinate per source (soy/sugar/garlic/sesame oil/pepper).
4. **Sauce/mix:** Prepare the noodle seasoning mixture per the source recipe and set aside.
5. **Noodle plan:** Prepare a big mixing bowl for tossing noodles immediately after cooking.

---

## Method (Process Flow — follow source for exact quantities & times)

### 1) Cook noodles
1. Boil dangmyeon per package directions and Maangchi method.
2. Drain well. Rinse briefly if needed to stop cooking.
3. Toss noodles right away with sesame oil (per source) to prevent sticking.
4. Cut noodles with kitchen scissors into shorter lengths (common and helpful for bowls).

### 2) Cook each component (best texture)
Cook vegetables and optional beef **separately** (quick stir-fry), then combine:
- **Spinach:** blanch quickly, squeeze out water, season per source.
- **Carrot/onion/mushrooms:** quick stir-fry each until just tender (not soggy).
- **Beef (optional):** cook quickly until just done.

### 3) Combine + final season
1. Add noodles to a large mixing bowl.
2. Add cooked vegetables (and beef if using).
3. Add the sauce/seasoning mixture per source.
4. Toss thoroughly (tongs/chopsticks) until evenly coated.
5. Finish with sesame seeds and a drizzle of sesame oil (per source), then taste and adjust.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Noodles:** Chewy and separated (not clumped).
- **Vegetables:** Bright and crisp-tender (not watery).
- **Flavor:** Savory-sweet with sesame aroma; not overly salty.

- Multiply **all exact source measurements** by 1.67× for noodles, vegetables, and seasonings.

### Batch notes
- Cook components in batches to avoid steaming.
- Combine while noodles are still slightly warm for best absorption.
