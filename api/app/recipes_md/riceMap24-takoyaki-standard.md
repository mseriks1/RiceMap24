# Takoyaki (Japanese Octopus Balls) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Takoyaki are crispy-on-the-outside, soft-in-the-center batter balls filled with octopus and topped with takoyaki sauce, Japanese mayo, bonito flakes, and aonori. A strong “street food” add-on or snack box.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Takoyaki (Japanese Octopus Balls)”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/takoyaki-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Street Food • Snack
- **Yield:** ~24 takoyaki balls (portion as 6–8 per serving)
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~30–35 min

## Ingredients (≈ 24 balls)

> **Important:** Takoyaki texture depends on **batter ratio (dashi + flour)** and **fast turning technique**.  
> Use the linked Just One Cookbook recipe for **exact measurements**.

### Batter (per source)
- All-purpose flour
- Dashi (or dashi powder + water)
- Eggs
- Soy sauce (small amount, if used)
- Salt (if used)

### Fillings & mix-ins (per source)
- Boiled octopus, cut into bite-size pieces
- Green onion
- Tenkasu (tempura bits)
- Pickled red ginger (beni shoga)

### Toppings
- Takoyaki sauce
- Japanese mayo
- Katsuobushi (bonito flakes)
- Aonori (seaweed flakes)

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Octopus:** Cut into small cubes (1–1.5 cm).
2. **Green onion:** Slice thin.
3. **Beni shoga + tenkasu:** Set out in small bowls.
4. **Make batter:** Whisk batter ingredients per source until smooth.
5. **Heat pan:** Preheat takoyaki pan over medium-high and oil each well generously.

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Fill the pan
1. Oil every well thoroughly.
2. Pour batter into wells to the top; let batter overflow slightly over the pan surface (this helps forming spheres).
3. Drop in octopus + green onion + tenkasu + beni shoga into each well.

## 2) Turn into balls
4. As the edges set, use chopsticks/skewers to rotate each piece 90° and tuck cooked batter edges into the well.
5. Continue turning repeatedly until each becomes a round ball and the outside is golden-brown.

## 3) Crisp + finish
6. Cook until deeply golden and crisp outside, soft inside.
7. Remove to a tray and repeat with remaining batter.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Exterior:** Golden-brown and lightly crisp.
- **Interior:** Soft, custardy; not raw batter.
- **Shape:** Round balls; no leaking centers.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 servings)
If 1 serving = 6 balls, 24 balls = 4 servings.
- For 10 servings, plan **60 balls** (~2.5× the base recipe).

Multiply all **exact source measurements** by **2.5×**.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
Takoyaki are best eaten quickly, but you can package well:
- Use a vented or not fully sealed container to reduce steaming.
- Add sauces in squeeze cups or drizzle lightly and include extra cups.
- Dry toppings (aonori + bonito) in a small cup/sachet; add at service.

**Holding time:** Best within **30–60 minutes** (texture changes with steam).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- Reheat in air fryer/oven for crispness; microwave softens.
