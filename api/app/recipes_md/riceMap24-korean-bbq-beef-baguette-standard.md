# Korean BBQ Beef Baguette (Bulgogi-Style) — RiceMap24 Standard (Breakfast/Lunch)

A premium baguette built for sales: bulgogi-style beef + gochujang mayo + crunchy kimchi slaw.  
Not a traditional Korean sandwich—this is **fusion** designed for pickup and repeat orders.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Baguette sandwich (Asian twist)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Korean BBQ / bulgogi profile
- **Yield:** 4 baguettes (with batch scaling included)
- **Active time:** ~25 min
- **Total time:** ~40 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Big flavor:** sweet-salty-garlic beef + creamy spicy mayo
- **Premium crunch:** kimchi slaw looks and tastes “restaurant”
- **Scales well:** beef cooks fast; slaw can be prepped ahead
- **Upsells:** extra beef, extra sauce cup, add cheese, add kimchi

---

## Ingredients (4 baguettes)

## A) Beef (bulgogi-style quick marinade)
- **650 g** beef, thin sliced (ribeye/sirloin/strip; or frozen shaved beef)
- **1 small pear** (Asian pear best) grated *(or 1/2 pear + 1/2 apple)*
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** grated ginger
- **4 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** brown sugar
- **1 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (for cooking)
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** mirin (nice aroma)

> Marinate **15–30 minutes**.

---

## B) Kimchi slaw (premium crunch)
- **250 g** shredded cabbage (coleslaw mix works)
- **120 g** kimchi, chopped + 2 Tbsp kimchi juice
- **1 carrot**, julienne (optional)
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- **1 tsp** sugar (balances)
- **1 tsp** rice vinegar (optional)
- **1 Tbsp** toasted sesame seeds

**Texture rule:** keep slaw lightly dressed, not wet.

---

## C) Baguettes + veg
- **4** baguettes (15–18 cm) or 2 long baguettes halved
- **1 cucumber**, thin sticks
- Optional: lettuce leaves (helps moisture barrier)

---

## D) Gochujang mayo (the identity)
- **120 ml** mayo
- **1.5 Tbsp** gochujang
- **1 tsp** rice vinegar (or lime)
- **1 tsp** honey (or sugar)
- Pinch salt

---

## E) Finish
- Sliced scallions
- Optional: nori strips
- Optional: cheese slice (fusion upgrade)

---

## Equipment
- Skillet or wok
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (optional)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make kimchi slaw (5–7 minutes)
1. Mix cabbage + chopped kimchi.
2. Add kimchi juice, sesame oil, sugar, sesame seeds.
3. Toss lightly and set aside.

---

## 2) Mix gochujang mayo (1 minute)
- Stir mayo + gochujang + vinegar + honey + pinch salt.

---

## 3) Marinate beef (15 minutes)
1. Mix pear, garlic, ginger, soy, brown sugar, sesame oil, pepper (and mirin).
2. Toss beef to coat.
3. Rest 15–30 min.

---

## 4) Cook beef (6–8 minutes)
1. Heat skillet/wok high.
2. Add a little neutral oil.
3. Cook beef in batches (don’t crowd) until browned and glossy.
4. Reduce juices quickly so it’s not watery.

**Quality target:** caramelized edges, not boiled.

---

## 5) Build baguettes
Per baguette:
1. Split baguette.
2. Spread **1–2 Tbsp** gochujang mayo.
3. Add cucumber (and lettuce if using).
4. Add beef (about **160–190 g cooked**).
5. Top with kimchi slaw.
6. Finish with scallions + sesame.

---

## Portioning (Per baguette)
- Beef: **160–200 g cooked**
- Slaw: **60–90 g**
- Mayo: **25–30 g**
- Cucumber/greens: as needed

---

## Batch for 10 baguettes
- Beef: **1.6 kg**
- Pear: 2 pears
- Soy sauce: 10 Tbsp
- Gochujang mayo: 300 ml mayo + 4 Tbsp gochujang
- Slaw: 600 g cabbage + 300 g kimchi

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
To avoid sogginess:
- Wrap in parchment + paper.
- If pickup time is long: pack slaw in a cup and customer adds (premium option).
- Sauce cup upsell is easy.

Label: “Add slaw last for maximum crunch.”

---

## Storage
- Slaw best same day (or keep undressed and mix at pickup).
- Cooked beef: 2 days refrigerated.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce, gochujang may contain soy)
- Sesame (sesame oil/seeds)
- Egg (mayo)
- Wheat (baguette)
- Gluten possible (soy/gochujang brands)
