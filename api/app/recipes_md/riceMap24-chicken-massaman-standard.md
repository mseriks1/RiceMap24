# Chicken Massaman Curry (Thai-Style) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A rich, mildly spicy Thai curry with Persian/Indian spice influence: coconut milk gravy with warm spices, chicken thighs, potatoes, and peanuts. Perfect as a premium rice box.

**Source recipe:** The Guardian (Felicity Cloake) — “How to make the perfect chicken massaman” (Feb 15, 2026)  
https://www.theguardian.com/food/2026/feb/15/perfect-massaman-recipe-felicity-cloake

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai • Curry • Coconut
- **Yield:** 4–6 portions (operator choose portion size)
- **Active time:** ~30 min
- **Total time:** ~80 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (paste + simmer control)

## Equipment
- Large pot / Dutch oven
- Small dry pan (to toast spices)
- Mortar & pestle or blender/processor (for paste)
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring spoons/cups

---

## Ingredients (Serves 4–6)

## A) Curry paste (homemade)
- **1½ tsp** cumin seeds
- **2 tsp** coriander seeds
- **2** cloves
- **4** cardamom pods
- **½** nutmeg, grated
- **1** cinnamon stick, roughly broken
- **¼ tsp** white or black peppercorns
- **6** long dried chillies, seeded and roughly crushed *(soak 20 min if using mortar & pestle)*
- **4** Thai shallots *(or 2 round shallots)*, peeled and chopped
- **5** plump garlic cloves, peeled and chopped
- **2** lemongrass stems, trimmed; chop the purplish section only *(save trimmings)*
- **2 Tbsp** finely chopped galangal
- **2 Tbsp** finely chopped coriander root or stems
- **1 tsp** Thai shrimp paste (kapi)
- **25 g** unsalted roasted peanuts, roughly chopped

> Shortcut (from the same source): Use **6 Tbsp red curry paste** + the starred spices/peanuts if you want faster prep.

## B) Curry
- **8** Thai shallots *(or 4 round ones)*, peeled and halved or quartered
- Neutral oil, to fry
- **6** chicken thighs, bone-in
- Salt
- **1** cinnamon stick
- **3** cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- **3** bay leaves
- **800 ml** coconut milk
- **4** small waxy potatoes, peeled and cut into halves or quarters
- Salt
- **180 ml** coconut cream *(brands without emulsifiers work best)*
- Palm sugar, to taste
- Fish sauce, to taste
- Tamarind water or puree, to taste
- **30 g** roasted peanuts

## To serve
- Steamed rice (jasmine is typical)

---

## Prep (30 minutes)
1. **Toast whole spices for paste:** In a dry pan, toast cumin + coriander seeds, cloves, cardamom, and peppercorns until fragrant. Cool.
2. **Chillies:** Seed and crush; soak 20 minutes if needed for easy pounding.
3. **Build paste:** Pound/blend toasted spices with remaining paste ingredients until smooth-ish (it doesn’t need to be perfectly pureed).
4. **Potatoes:** Peel and cut (waxy potatoes hold shape).
5. **Chicken:** Salt lightly.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Fry aromatics
1. Heat a little neutral oil in a large pot over **medium heat**.
2. Add the curry shallots and fry until lightly golden at edges.

## 2) Start the curry base
3. Add the massaman paste and fry gently until aromatic and the oil starts to separate a little.
4. Add the coconut milk, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, and bay leaves. Bring to a gentle simmer.

## 3) Cook chicken + potatoes
5. Add chicken thighs and simmer gently until they’re cooked through and tender.
6. Add potatoes and simmer until potatoes are tender.

> Keep the simmer gentle (no rolling boil) for tender chicken and a smooth coconut sauce.

## 4) Finish + balance
7. Stir in coconut cream and simmer briefly to thicken.
8. Season to taste with palm sugar, fish sauce, and tamarind (aim for sweet–salty–sour balance).
9. Stir in roasted peanuts at the end.

## 5) Serve
10. Remove whole spices if you prefer. Serve hot with steamed rice.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce:** Rich and slightly thick; coats a spoon.
- **Chicken:** Tender, not stringy (gentle simmer).
- **Balance:** Sweet–salty–sour with warm spice notes (not overly spicy).

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
If you portion this as 1 bowl = 1 portion, target 10 bowls.
- Make **2×** the recipe for a comfortable 10 portions (depending on your bowl size).

**Batch notes**
- Cook in a wide pot for better reduction control.
- Let curry rest 30–60 minutes before service for deeper flavor.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer or microwave; stir well (coconut can separate slightly).

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)

- **Gluten:** Usually no, but check paste/condiments
