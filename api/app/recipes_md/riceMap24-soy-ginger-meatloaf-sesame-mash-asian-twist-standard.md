# Soy-Ginger Glazed Meatloaf Slices + Sesame Mash (Asian Twist Meal) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A familiar Western “banker” (meatloaf) upgraded with a glossy **soy-ginger glaze** and a sesame-infused mash.  
Not traditional Asian—this is fusion comfort food designed for home-kitchen sales, portion control, and delivery.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Meatloaf slices meal (main + side)
- **Asian twist:** Soy-ginger glaze + sesame mash
- **Yield:** 8 slices (≈8 portions) or 6 larger portions
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 10 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (bake + glaze)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Banker-friendly:** meatloaf is cheap, filling, and repeat-order.
- **Perfect portioning:** slice sizes are consistent.
- **Delivery winner:** reheats well; glaze stays glossy.
- **Upsells:** extra slice, extra glaze cup, add slaw/pickles.

---

## Equipment
- Loaf pan (or shape loaf on sheet pan)
- Mixing bowl
- Saucepan (glaze)
- Oven
- Potato masher (for mash)

---

## Ingredients

## A) Meatloaf
- **1 kg** ground beef (or 50/50 beef+pork)
- **2 eggs**
- **100 g** breadcrumbs (panko works)
- **120 ml** milk (or water)
- **1 small onion**, grated or finely minced
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- **1 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper

---

## B) Soy-ginger glaze (the identity)
- **90 ml** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** honey (or brown sugar)
- **1 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **1 clove** garlic, grated
- **60 ml** water
- **1 tsp** cornstarch + 1 Tbsp water (slurry)
- Optional: chili flakes

---

## C) Sesame mash (side)
- **1.2 kg** potatoes, peeled and chunked
- **60 g** butter
- **120–180 ml** milk (or cream), warm
- **1 tsp** salt
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- **1 Tbsp** toasted sesame seeds
- Optional: sliced scallions to finish

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Prep
1. Preheat oven to **190°C**.
2. Soak breadcrumbs in milk 2 minutes.

---

## 2) Mix meatloaf (8 minutes)
1. In a bowl, combine ground meat, eggs, soaked breadcrumbs, onion, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, pepper.
2. Mix gently until combined (don’t overwork).

---

## 3) Bake (45–55 minutes)
1. Press mixture into a loaf pan (or shape a loaf on a sheet pan).
2. Bake **45–55 minutes** until internal temp reaches **72°C**.

---

## 4) Make glaze (5 minutes)
1. In a saucepan, whisk soy, honey, vinegar, ginger, garlic, water.
2. Simmer 1 minute.
3. Add cornstarch slurry and stir until glossy.

---

## 5) Glaze and finish (10 minutes)
1. Brush glaze over meatloaf.
2. Bake 8–10 minutes more to set glaze.
3. Rest 10 minutes before slicing (clean slices).

---

## 6) Make sesame mash (20 minutes)
1. Boil potatoes in salted water until tender (15–18 min).
2. Drain and mash.
3. Mix in butter, warm milk, salt, sesame oil.
4. Finish with toasted sesame seeds (and scallions if using).

---

## Quality Checks
- Meatloaf slices cleanly (rested).
- Glaze glossy and clingy.
- Mash creamy with a subtle sesame aroma.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per order)
- Meatloaf: 1 slice (**150–200 g**)
- Sesame mash: **220–280 g**
- Optional: add pickles/slaw as a side cup

**Upsells**
- Extra meatloaf slice
- Extra glaze cup
- Add slaw/pickles
- Add spicy glaze

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Make **2 loaves** (2 kg meat total). Bake side-by-side.
Mash: 2× potatoes.
Glaze: 2×.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Container A: meatloaf + glaze
- Container B: sesame mash
- Optional: side cup (pickles/slaw)

Label: “Reheat gently. Extra glaze optional.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Keeps 3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat slices gently; add a splash of water if needed.
- Mash reheats with a splash of milk.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Sesame
- Egg
- Dairy (milk/butter)
- Wheat (breadcrumbs)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce brand)
