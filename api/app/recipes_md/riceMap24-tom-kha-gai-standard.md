# Tom Kha Gai (Thai Coconut Galangal Chicken Soup) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A creamy, fragrant Thai soup with coconut milk, galangal, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, chicken, mushrooms, and a bright sweet–salty–sour finish. Great as a premium soup bowl and easy to deliver with a “garnish kit”.

**Source recipe:** Hot Thai Kitchen — “Tom Kha Gai (Thai Coconut Chicken Soup)”  
https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/tom-kha-gai/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai • Coconut Soup • Mild–Medium
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min  
- **Total time:** ~30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (infusion + final seasoning balance)

---

## Equipment
- Medium pot (3–4 L)
- Ladle
- Fine strainer (optional, for extra-clear soup)
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring cups/spoons

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Aromatics (infusion)
- **2 cups (480 ml)** chicken stock
- **1 stalk** lemongrass (bottom ~1/3), bruised and cut into 5–7 cm pieces
- **6–8 slices** galangal *(or ginger if unavailable, but galangal is best)*
- **4–6** kaffir lime leaves, torn
- **2–6** Thai chilies (optional; adjust heat)

### Soup body
- **1 can (400 ml)** coconut milk *(full-fat for best body)*
- **250–350 g** chicken (thigh or breast), thinly sliced
- **200 g** mushrooms (straw mushrooms, oyster, or button), sliced

### Seasoning (add at the end; balance to taste)
- **2–3 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **2–3 Tbsp** lime juice (fresh)
- **1–2 tsp** sugar *(or 1–2 Tbsp Thai chili paste if your source version uses it)*
- Optional: **2–3 Tbsp** Thai chili paste (nam prik pao) for deeper color/flavor (if using per source)

### Garnish
- Cilantro (chopped)
- Optional: sliced scallions

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Lemongrass:** Trim and bruise (smash with the back of a knife), cut into short pieces.
2. **Galangal:** Slice thin.
3. **Lime leaves:** Tear to release oils.
4. **Chicken:** Slice thin so it cooks fast.
5. **Mushrooms:** Slice or halve.
6. **Limes:** Juice fresh (don’t bottle if possible).

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Infuse the broth (flavor base)
1. Add chicken stock to a pot. Bring to a **gentle simmer** over medium heat.
2. Add lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves (and chilies if using).
3. Simmer **5 minutes** to infuse.

> Keep it gentle—no hard boil needed.

## 2) Add coconut milk + mushrooms
4. Add coconut milk. Return to a gentle simmer.
5. Add mushrooms. Simmer **3–5 minutes** until tender.

## 3) Cook the chicken
6. Add sliced chicken and simmer gently until just cooked through, **2–4 minutes** depending on thickness.

## 4) Finish seasoning (off heat for best aroma)
7. Turn heat to low (or off briefly).
8. Season with fish sauce, sugar, and lime juice. Taste and adjust:
   - **More salty/umami:** fish sauce (½ tsp at a time)
   - **More sour/bright:** lime (½ tsp at a time)
   - **More sweet/round:** sugar (¼–½ tsp at a time)
9. If using Thai chili paste (nam prik pao), stir it in now and re-taste.

> **Rule:** Add lime juice at the end for the freshest flavor.

## 5) Serve
10. Ladle into bowls. Garnish with cilantro (and scallions if using).

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Broth:** Creamy and aromatic, not oily-separating.
- **Chicken:** Tender (don’t boil hard).
- **Balance:** Sweet–salty–sour with coconut richness; lime should be noticeable.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Chicken stock: **1200 ml**
- Coconut milk: **1000 ml** (2–3 cans depending on can size)
- Chicken: **625–875 g**
- Mushrooms: **500 g**
- Lemongrass: **2–3 stalks**
- Galangal: **15–20 slices**
- Kaffir lime leaves: **10–15**
- Fish sauce: **5–7.5 Tbsp** (then adjust to taste)
- Lime juice: **5–7.5 Tbsp** (then adjust to taste)
- Sugar: **2.5–5 tsp** (then adjust to taste)

### Batch notes
- Infuse aromatics first, then add coconut.
- Season lime at the end, per batch, for consistent brightness.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Soup in a leak-proof container.
- Include a **garnish kit** (cilantro + lime wedge) in a small bag/cup.
- Optional: “Spice cup” (chili paste or sliced chilies) for heat control.

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer. Avoid boiling hard (coconut can split, chicken toughens).
- **Finish:** Add a squeeze of fresh lime after reheating.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Fish (fish sauce), coconut
- **May contain:** Shellfish (if chili paste contains shrimp)
- **Gluten:** Usually no, but check condiments

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Thin-slice chicken for quick, tender cooking.

- Aromatics can be left in for authenticity, but consider removing lemongrass pieces before packing.
