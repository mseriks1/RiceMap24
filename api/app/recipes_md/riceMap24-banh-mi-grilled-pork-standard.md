# Banh Mi Thit Nuong (Vietnamese Grilled Pork Banh Mi) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Vietnamese street-food classic: caramelized grilled pork in a crispy baguette with quick pickles, herbs, and sauce. Great as a “lunch premium” item and easy to upsell with extra meat or extra pickles.

**Source recipe:** RecipeTin Eats — “Banh Mi” (use the pork + pickles + assembly framework)

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Mix pickles (needs 30–60 min)  
2) Marinate pork (30–60 min minimum; longer better)  
3) Prep herbs + cucumber + spreads  
4) Cook pork (quick high heat)  
5) Toast bread + assemble

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Quick pickles (Do Chua)
1. Julienne carrot (and daikon if using).
2. Mix pickling liquid per source; add vegetables.
3. Press down so veg is submerged. Rest **30–60 minutes**.

## 2) Marinate pork
1. Mix marinade per source.
2. Add pork and coat well.
3. Marinate **30–60 minutes** (or up to overnight refrigerated).

## 3) Cook pork (caramelize)
1. Preheat grill pan/skillet over **medium-high**.
2. Cook pork until caramelized and cooked through, flipping as needed.
3. Rest **5 minutes**, then slice thinly.

## 4) Assemble sandwiches
1. Warm/toast baguettes lightly.
2. Spread mayo/pate (optional).
3. Add cucumber + cilantro.
4. Add sliced pork.
5. Add drained pickles.
6. Add sauce (Maggi/sriracha) to taste.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Bread:** Crisp outside, light inside (avoid soggy).
- **Pork:** Caramelized edges; juicy.
- **Pickles:** Crunchy, not limp; sweet-sour balance.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Wrap tightly in paper + outer bag.
- Keep pickles in a small side cup if delivery time is long (prevents soggy bread).

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Pickles:** Refrigerate 5–7 days.
- **Cooked pork:** Refrigerate 2–3 days; reheat gently.
- **Sandwich:** Best assembled fresh.
