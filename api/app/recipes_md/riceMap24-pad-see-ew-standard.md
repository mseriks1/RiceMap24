# Pad See Ew (Thai Stir-Fried Wide Rice Noodles) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Thai street-food classic: wide rice noodles stir-fried with Chinese broccoli, egg, and pork in a savory soy-based sauce.  
**This version is designed for home‑kitchen production and clear repeatability (exact weights + per-batch workflow).**

**Source recipe:** Serious Eats — “Pad See Ew (Thai Stir-Fried Noodles)”  
https://www.seriouseats.com/pad-see-ew-recipe-8784741

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Thai • Stir-Fry • Noodles
- **Yield:** **4 portions** (Serious Eats cooks **1 portion per batch** for best caramelization)
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~45 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (high-heat timing + batch cooking)

## Equipment
- Wok or large carbon-steel / stainless-steel skillet
- Large pot (boiling water for blanching + par-cooking)
- Colander / strainer + spider (or noodle basket)
- Mixing bowls (noodles, sauces, pork)
- Digital scale (recommended for consistency)
- Tongs or chopsticks
- Paper towels

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Chile vinegar (condiment, optional but excellent)
- **240 ml** distilled white vinegar (1 cup)
- **Pinch** kosher salt
- **70 g** fresh red chiles, thinly sliced crosswise (about 4 chiles)

### Noodles
- **568 g** fresh rice noodle sheets **OR** **226 g** dried wide rice noodles  
- **30 ml** Thai black soy sauce (2 Tbsp)
- **30 ml** neutral oil (2 Tbsp)

### Marinated pork
- **225 g** pork shoulder, sliced very thin into ~2.5 x 4 cm pieces
- **5 ml** Thai oyster sauce (1 tsp)
- **5 ml** Thai thin soy sauce (1 tsp)
- **3 g** granulated sugar (1/2 tsp)
- **Pinch** ground white pepper
- **5 ml** sesame oil (1 tsp)
- **2 g** baking soda (1/2 tsp)
- **Small pinch** kosher salt
- **5 g** cornstarch (1 tsp)
- **5 ml** Shaoxing wine (1 tsp)

### Stir-fry sauce
- **45 ml** Thai oyster sauce (3 Tbsp)
- **50 ml** Thai thin soy sauce (3 Tbsp + 1 tsp)
- **15 ml** Thai black soy sauce (1 Tbsp)
- **1 tsp** granulated white sugar
- **1/4 tsp** ground white pepper

### Par-cooking + stir-fry components
- **240 g** Chinese broccoli (gai lan), sliced on bias into 1-inch pieces (about 4 cups)
- **60 ml** neutral oil (4 Tbsp), **divided**
- **50 g** garlic, smashed and roughly chopped (**10 cloves**), **divided**
- **4 large eggs**, **divided** (1 egg per portion)

---

## Prep (Do this before high-heat stir-frying)

### 1) Chile vinegar (optional)
1. Bring vinegar + salt to a boil, remove from heat.
2. Add sliced chiles. Steep **≥ 30 minutes** (best after 1 day). Store airtight.

### 2) Prepare noodles
**If using fresh noodle sheets:**
1. Cut sheets into **1½-inch (4 cm)** wide strips.
2. Toss noodles with **Thai black soy (30 ml)** + **neutral oil (30 ml)** until coated and not sticky.
3. Divide noodles into **4 equal portions** (by weight if possible).

**If using dried wide rice noodles:**
1. Put noodles in a heatproof bowl.
2. Cover with **1 quart (~1 liter) boiling water**. Hydrate **15–20 min** until pliable.
3. Drain and rinse **3 times** with cold water to remove surface starch.
4. Toss with **Thai black soy (30 ml)** + **neutral oil (30 ml)** until coated and not sticky.
5. Divide into **4 equal portions**.

### 3) Marinate pork (30 minutes)
1. Combine all pork marinade ingredients with the sliced pork.
2. Mix well by hand until evenly coated.
3. Marinate **30 minutes at room temperature**.

### 4) Mix stir-fry sauce
Whisk all stir-fry sauce ingredients until combined.  
Portion into **4 equal portions** (about **2 Tbsp / 30 g** per stir-fry batch).

### 5) Prep broccoli + garlic + eggs
- Divide broccoli into **4 equal portions** after blanching (below).
- Divide garlic into **4 equal portions** (~12 g per batch).
- Keep eggs whole; crack 1 egg per batch.

---

## Par-cook (Fast, keeps stir-fry crisp)

### 1) Blanch Chinese broccoli
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
2. Blanch broccoli **20 seconds** until crisp-tender.
3. Shock under cold water immediately; squeeze excess water out.
4. Divide into **4 portions**.

### 2) Water-velvet the pork (quick blanch)
1. Return water to a boil.
2. Add marinated pork; separate pieces with chopsticks.
3. Cook **~1 minute** until no pink remains.
4. Drain, rinse under cold water, then drain thoroughly.
5. Divide into **4 portions**.

---

## Stir-Fry (Cook in 4 separate batches — 1 portion per batch)

> **Why batches matter:** Overcrowding steams noodles and prevents the signature char/caramelization.

### For each 1-portion batch
1. Heat wok/skillet over **high heat** until lightly smoking (about **3 minutes**).
2. Add **1 Tbsp (15 ml) neutral oil** and swirl.
3. Add **1/4 of garlic (~12 g)**, lower heat to **low**, stir **~20 seconds** until fragrant.
4. Increase heat to **high**. Add **1 portion noodles (~142 g)**.  
   Stir carefully to avoid breaking; cook until slightly charred, about **1 minute**.
5. Add **2 Tbsp (30 g) sauce**. Stir well.
6. Add **1 portion broccoli**; stir-fry **30 seconds**.
7. Add **1 portion pork**; stir-fry **30 seconds**.
8. Push noodles to one side. Crack **1 egg** into empty side, scramble briefly, then fold through noodles.
9. Plate immediately and repeat for the remaining 3 batches.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Noodles:** Some browned/charred spots; not pale and not mushy.
- **Sauce:** Coats noodles; not watery at the bottom.
- **Broccoli:** Bright green, crisp-tender.
- **Egg:** Soft, mixed through (not overcooked).

## Scaling Notes (10 portions)
This recipe’s success comes from **small batches**. For 10 portions:
- Multiply ingredients by **2.5×**.
- Still cook as **10 individual wok batches**, or at least **5 batches** if your burner/wok is powerful.

**Operational tip:** Pre-portion noodles, sauce, garlic, broccoli, and pork into 10 “batch cups” for fast service.

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Thai black soy sauce is key for color and mild sweetness.
- Squeeze broccoli dry after shocking—water kills wok char.
- If noodles clump: toss with a bit more oil after rinsing (dried noodles).
