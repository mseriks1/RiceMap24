# Sundubu Jjigae (Korean Soft Tofu Stew) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean favorite: silky soft tofu in a spicy, savory broth with pork/seafood and an egg finish.  
Designed for home kitchens: **exact weights per bowl, spice-base batching, and delivery packaging**.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Sundubu-jjigae (Spicy soft tofu stew)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/sundubu-jjigae

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Stew • Spicy
- **Yield:** **4 portions**
- **Active time:** ~15 min  
- **Total time:** ~25–30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (spice base + final balance)

---

## Equipment
- Medium pot (3–4 L) or 2 smaller pots (best for tofu handling)
- Ladle
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring spoons + **digital scale** (recommended)
- Optional: small bowl for cracking eggs

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Core
- **800 g** soft tofu (sundubu), 2 tubes (about 400 g each)
- **300 g** pork belly or pork shoulder, sliced thin *(or seafood mix; see options)*
- **200 g** onion, sliced (1 medium)
- **10 g** garlic, minced (about 2 cloves)
- **10 g** scallions, chopped (about 2; reserve some for garnish)
- **800 ml** water or light stock *(about 3 1/3 cups)*

## B) Seasoning
- **30 g** gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) *(~3 Tbsp; adjust heat)*
- **15 ml** sesame oil (1 Tbsp)
- **30 ml** soy sauce (2 Tbsp) *(or use salt to taste)*
- **20 ml** fish sauce (4 tsp) *(optional but adds umami)*
- **5 g** sugar (1 tsp) *(optional, balances)*
- **2 g** kosher salt (start small; kimchi/seafood variations change saltiness)

## C) Optional add-ins (choose based on your menu)
- **150 g** mushrooms (enoki/oyster), optional
- **200 g** clams/shrimp/seafood mix, optional (swap for pork)
- **1 tsp** gochujang (optional depth)

## D) Egg finish
- **4 eggs** (1 per bowl; crack into the hot stew)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Onion:** Slice.
2. **Pork:** Slice thin (2–3 cm pieces).
3. **Garlic/scallions:** Mince/chop.
4. **Tofu:** Keep chilled; open just before adding so it stays intact.

---

## Core Technique: Make a Spicy Oil Base (Gochugaru-Gireum)
This step is the “restaurant” flavor shortcut and keeps the stew consistent.

### Spicy base (makes 4 portions)
1. Heat a pot over **medium heat**.
2. Add **sesame oil (15 ml)**.
3. Add **gochugaru (30 g)** and stir **20–30 seconds** until fragrant.

> Do not burn gochugaru. If it darkens fast, lower heat.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable Workflow)

## 1) Build broth
1. After making the spicy oil base, add onion and cook **2 minutes** (just to soften slightly).
2. Add pork and cook **3–4 minutes** until it loses raw color.
3. Add garlic and stir **15 seconds**.
4. Add water/stock (**800 ml**) and bring to a boil.
5. Reduce to a **gentle simmer** for **7–8 minutes**.

## 2) Season (taste-driven but consistent)
6. Add soy sauce (**30 ml**).
7. Add fish sauce (**20 ml**, optional) and sugar (**5 g**, optional).
8. Taste broth. Adjust salt gradually (pinches).

## 3) Add tofu (gentle)
9. Add soft tofu by squeezing/chunking it into the pot (or spooning in large pieces).
10. Simmer **3–4 minutes** to heat through.
11. Add mushrooms/seafood now if using (seafood cooks fast).

## 4) Egg finish (per bowl)
12. For each portion, ladle stew into a bowl or serve in the pot and crack **1 egg** on top.
13. Cover and let sit **1–2 minutes** until egg whites set and yolk stays soft.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Broth:** Spicy, savory, not bitter (gochugaru should smell toasted, not burnt).
- **Tofu:** Soft but not shredded to mush (stir gently).
- **Egg:** Soft-set for best richness.

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply everything by **2.5×**.

### Targets (10 bowls)
- Soft tofu: **2000 g**
- Pork: **750 g** (or seafood **500–750 g** depending on mix)
- Onion: **500 g**
- Stock/water: **2000 ml**
- Gochugaru: **75 g**
- Sesame oil: **37.5 ml**
- Soy sauce: **75 ml**
- Fish sauce: **50 ml**
- Eggs: **10**

### Ops tips
- Make spicy oil base in a larger pot, then portion into 2 pots for service control.
- Add tofu and eggs **late** (per batch) to keep texture premium.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality:**
- Stew in leak-proof container
- Rice **separate** (recommended)
- Egg: either cook in stew before packing or offer “egg add-on” only for dine-in (delivery risk)

Label: “Stir gently. Best with rice.”

**Holding time:** Very good for 2–3 hours; tofu stays soft.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **2 days**.
- Reheat gently (avoid hard boil; tofu can break down).
