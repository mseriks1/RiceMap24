# Korean BBQ Beef Wrap (Bulgogi + Kimchi Slaw) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A premium lunch wrap: bulgogi-style beef, gochujang mayo, and crunchy kimchi slaw.  
Fusion (not traditional), designed for **pickup/delivery** with a “slaw separate” option for maximum crunch.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Asian-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Korean BBQ / bulgogi
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~35–40 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **High repeat-order** flavor profile (sweet-salty beef + spicy mayo).
- **Photogenic** (red slaw + sesame + scallion).
- **Scales well** (fast beef cook; slaw can be prepped).
- **Upsells:** extra beef, extra sauce cup, add kimchi, add cheese.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Beef (bulgogi-style)
- **650 g** beef, very thin sliced (ribeye/sirloin/strip; or shaved beef)
- **1 small Asian pear** grated *(or 1/2 pear + 1/2 apple)*
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 Tbsp** grated ginger
- **4 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** brown sugar
- **1 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** mirin (nice aroma)

## B) Kimchi slaw (premium crunch)
- **250 g** shredded cabbage (coleslaw mix)
- **120 g** kimchi, chopped
- **2 Tbsp** kimchi juice (optional but great)
- **1 carrot**, julienne (optional)
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- **1 tsp** sugar
- **1 Tbsp** toasted sesame seeds
- Optional: **1 tsp** rice vinegar (brightens)

## C) Gochujang mayo (the identity)
- **120 ml** mayo
- **1.5 Tbsp** gochujang
- **1 tsp** rice vinegar (or lime)
- **1 tsp** honey (or sugar)
- Pinch salt

## D) Wrap build
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps
- Lettuce leaves (recommended moisture barrier)
- **1 cucumber**, thin sticks/slices (optional but great)
- Sliced scallions (finish)
- Optional: nori strips (premium)
- Optional: cheese slice (fusion upgrade)

---

## Equipment
- Skillet or wok
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (optional)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make kimchi slaw (5 minutes)
1. Mix cabbage + chopped kimchi (+ carrot).
2. Add kimchi juice, sesame oil, sugar, sesame seeds (and vinegar if using).
3. Toss lightly and set aside.

**Keep it light:** too wet = soggy wrap.

---

## 2) Mix gochujang mayo (1 minute)
- Stir mayo + gochujang + vinegar + honey + pinch salt.

---

## 3) Marinate beef (10–20 minutes)
1. Mix grated pear, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, pepper (and mirin).
2. Toss beef to coat.
3. Rest 10–20 minutes.

---

## 4) Cook beef (6–8 minutes)
1. Heat skillet/wok on **high**.
2. Cook beef in batches (don’t crowd) until browned and glossy.
3. Reduce any excess liquid quickly (avoid “boiled beef”).

**Quality target:** caramelized edges, not watery.

---

## 5) Assemble wraps (RiceMap24 build)
Per wrap:
1. Warm tortilla briefly (softens, prevents tearing).
2. Add lettuce barrier.
3. Spread **1–2 Tbsp** gochujang mayo.
4. Add beef (about **160–190 g cooked**).
5. Add a small handful kimchi slaw.
6. Add cucumber (optional).
7. Finish with scallions + optional nori.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- Beef: **160–200 g cooked**
- Slaw: **60–90 g**
- Mayo: **25–30 g**

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Beef: **1.6 kg**
- Marinade: 2.5×
- Gochujang mayo: 300 ml mayo + ~4 Tbsp gochujang
- Slaw: 600 g cabbage + 300 g kimchi

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
**Best practice (keeps crunch):**
- Wrap A: tortilla + lettuce + mayo + beef (wrapped tight)
- Cup B: kimchi slaw (customer adds)
- Optional cup: extra gochujang mayo

**Label:** “Add slaw last for maximum crunch.”

---

## Storage
- Cooked beef: 2 days refrigerated
- Slaw best same day (or keep undressed and toss at pickup)
- Gochujang mayo: 3 days refrigerated

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce, gochujang may contain soy)
- Sesame (sesame oil/seeds)
- Egg (mayo)
- Wheat (tortilla)
- Gluten possible (soy/gochujang brands)
