# Gyudon (Japanese Beef Bowl) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A fast, high-demand Japanese comfort bowl: thinly sliced beef and onions simmered in a savory-sweet dashi sauce and served over rice.

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Rice Bowl • Quick Simmer
- **Yield:** 2 portions (standard bowls)
- **Active time:** ~10 min  
- **Total time:** ~20 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (requires timing control to keep beef tender)

---

## Equipment
- Large skillet or sauté pan (26–30 cm / 10–12 in) with lid  
- Cutting board + knife  
- Measuring spoons/cups or scale  
- Fine mesh skimmer/strainer (optional, for skimming foam)

---

## Ingredients (2 portions)

### Main
- **225 g** thinly sliced beef (ribeye recommended; “shabu shabu/sukiyaki” cut)
- **113 g** onion (about **1/2** medium onion), thinly sliced
- **1** scallion (spring onion), thinly sliced on a diagonal (garnish)

### Sauce (add directly to pan)
- **120 ml** dashi
- **2 Tbsp** sake
- **2 Tbsp** mirin
- **3 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** sugar *(adjust to taste in future batches)*

### To serve
- Cooked short-grain rice: **~250 g cooked rice per bowl** (2 bowls)
- Pickled red ginger (beni shoga), optional

---

## Prep (5 minutes)
1. **Onion:** Slice thinly (the thinner, the faster and sweeter it cooks).
2. **Scallion:** Slice thinly; set aside.
3. **Beef:** If very floppy, chill **10 minutes** in freezer for easier handling. Cut wide slices into **~7–8 cm** lengths.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Build the pan (start cold)
1. **Cold pan:** Place a large skillet on the stove **without heat**.
2. Add **dashi, sake, mirin, soy sauce, sugar** to the pan. Stir until sugar dissolves.
3. Add the **onion slices**, separating layers so they spread evenly.
4. Lay the **beef on top**, separating slices as much as possible to avoid clumps.

### 2) Simmer (5–6 minutes total)
5. Cover with a **lid**. Turn heat to **medium**.
6. When it reaches a **gentle simmer**, reduce to **low** and simmer **3–4 minutes**.
7. Optional: **Skim foam** (grayish scum) once or twice for a cleaner sauce.
8. Sprinkle in **scallions**, re-cover, and simmer **1 minute**.

> **Stop point (tender beef rule):** The beef should be **just cooked through** (no longer pink).  
> Overcooking makes thin beef tough.

### 3) Serve
9. Spoon warm rice into two bowls.
10. Add beef + onions on top and drizzle with sauce.
11. Finish with optional beni shoga.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef tenderness:** Should be soft, not chewy → stop cooking earlier next time.
- **Onion:** Should be pliable and sweet (not raw-crunchy).
- **Sauce balance:** Savory-sweet. Fine-tune sugar ±1 tsp next batch if needed.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Use this when cooking for a small batch day. Cook in **2–3 pans** rather than one huge pot to keep beef tender.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Beef: **1125 g**
- Onion: **565 g**
- Scallions: **5**
- Dashi: **600 ml**
- Sake: **10 Tbsp** (≈ **150 ml**)
- Mirin: **10 Tbsp** (≈ **150 ml**)
- Soy sauce: **15 Tbsp** (≈ **225 ml**)
- Sugar: **5 Tbsp** (≈ **60–65 g**, depending on granule size)
- Cooked rice: **~2500 g cooked** (≈ 250 g per bowl)

### Batch method notes
- Assemble each pan **cold** with sauce + onions + beef, then heat.
- Keep simmer **gentle**; avoid rolling boil.
- Stop as soon as beef is just cooked.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
**Best quality:** Pack rice and toppings separately when possible.
- **Option A (simple):** Rice in bottom + beef/onion on top; sauce drizzled lightly.
- **Option B (premium):** Rice in main box; beef/onion in separate compartment; sauce in mini cup.

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** of cooking for tenderness.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Cool quickly; store up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle heat (microwave medium power or low pan simmer).  
  Avoid overheating (beef toughens).

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Soy (soy sauce)  
- **May contain:** Fish (if dashi is fish-based)  
- **Alcohol:** Sake + mirin (typically cooked off, but note for sensitive customers)

## Menu Copy (Ready to Paste)
**Gyudon — Japanese Beef Bowl**  
Thin-sliced beef and sweet onions simmered in a savory dashi soy sauce, served over warm rice.

---

## Variations (Keep the Core Recipe the Same)
- **Spicy:** Add chili flakes as a topping (not in the simmer).
- **Extra onion:** +20–30% onion for a sweeter bowl.
- **Protein swap:** Thin sliced chicken works, but cooking time changes—test separately.

---

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- The key is **thin beef + gentle simmer + short cooking time**.
- If your beef slices are thicker, reduce heat further and monitor doneness closely.
