# Spinach Gomaae (Japanese Spinach with Sesame Dressing) — RiceMap24 Side Order Standard

A classic Japanese side that sells well next to **any** bowl: nutty sesame dressing + bright green spinach.  
It’s cheap, fast, photogenic, and batch-friendly—perfect for add-ons.

**Reference recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Japanese Spinach Salad with Sesame Dressing (Gomaae)”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/spinach-with-sesame-sauce/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Side order / add-on
- **Yield:** 4 small sides (or 8 small add-on cups)
- **Active time:** ~10 min
- **Total time:** ~15 min
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Best Ways to Sell It (RiceMap24)
- **Add-on cup (small):** 80–100 g
- **Side order (standard):** 150–180 g
- **Family side:** 350–450 g
- **“Greens upgrade”** for any rice bowl (+1 side cup)

---

## Equipment
- Pot for blanching
- Bowl with ice water
- Colander
- Mortar & pestle (optional, best flavor) or mini food chopper
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Ingredients (4 sides)

### Spinach
- **1 bunch spinach (about 227 g)**
- **1 tsp kosher salt** (for blanching water)

### Sesame dressing (Goma-ae)
- **3 Tbsp toasted white sesame seeds**
- **1 Tbsp soy sauce**
- **1 Tbsp sugar**

> Flavor note: This dressing is intentionally simple. The quality and freshness of sesame seeds makes the biggest difference.

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Repeatable)

### 1) Make the sesame dressing (5 minutes)
1. Toast sesame seeds in a dry pan over medium-high, shaking/tossing until fragrant (30–60 seconds).  
2. Grind the seeds in a mortar (leave some texture; don’t turn it into paste).
3. Add soy sauce and sugar, mix until sugar dissolves.
4. Set aside.

**Shortcut:** If you don’t have a mortar, crush seeds in a zip bag with a rolling pin, or pulse a few times in a chopper.

---

### 2) Blanch spinach (2 minutes)
1. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
2. Add **1 tsp salt** to the water.
3. Add spinach stems first for 15–20 seconds, then submerge leaves.
4. Blanch total **~45–60 seconds** (spinach should stay bright green).
5. Transfer immediately to ice water to stop cooking.

---

### 3) Drain and cut (3 minutes)
1. Squeeze spinach gently but firmly to remove water.
2. Cut into **2.5 cm (1-inch)** lengths.

> Draining is key—wet spinach makes the dressing watery and dull.

---

### 4) Dress (1 minute)
1. Add spinach to dressing.
2. Toss until evenly coated.
3. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Color:** bright green (not olive).
- **Texture:** tender but not mushy.
- **Sauce:** nutty, lightly sweet, not watery.

---

## Scaling (10 side orders)
Multiply by **2.5×** (for 10 standard sides) or **5×** (for 10 large sides).
**Ops tip:** Make dressing in a bowl/jug and keep chilled; toss spinach close to service.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Pack in a wide cup/container (spinach compacts less).
- Optional: sprinkle a pinch of sesame seeds on top for “premium look.”
- Keep chilled.

---

## Storage & Food Safety
- Best within **24 hours** (keeps 2–3 days chilled, but brightest day-of).
- Keep refrigerated.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Sesame
