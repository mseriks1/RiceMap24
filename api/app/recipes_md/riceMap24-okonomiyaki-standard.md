# Okonomiyaki (Japanese Savory Cabbage Pancake) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Japanese street-food favorite: a savory cabbage pancake topped with okonomiyaki sauce, mayo, bonito flakes, and seaweed. Great as a shareable item or a “Japanese comfort” plate.

**Source recipe:** Just One Cookbook — “Okonomiyaki”  
https://www.justonecookbook.com/okonomiyaki/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Japanese • Street Food • Savory Pancake
- **Yield:** 2 large pancakes (≈ 2–4 servings depending on portioning)
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~30–35 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (batter consistency + flipping)

## Equipment
- Large nonstick skillet or griddle (with lid)
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Spatula(s)
- Measuring cups/spoons or scale
- Cutting board + knife
- Optional: squeeze bottles for sauce/mayo (service)

## Ingredients (2 large pancakes)

> **Important:** Okonomiyaki texture depends on **cabbage ratio**, **batter thickness**, and **cook time**.  
> Use the linked Just One Cookbook recipe for **exact measurements** for batter and toppings.

### A) Batter + mix-ins (per source)
- Cabbage, finely shredded
- Flour (often all-purpose; sometimes includes yam flour/nagaimo in Japanese style)
- Eggs
- Dashi (or water/stock as substitute per source)
- Green onions (optional)
- Optional add-ins: tenkasu (tempura bits), pickled ginger (beni shoga), per source

### B) Protein (operator choice; per source options)
- Pork belly slices (classic)
- Shrimp, squid, or other add-ins (optional)

### C) Toppings (classic)
- Okonomiyaki sauce
- Japanese mayo
- Katsuobushi (bonito flakes)
- Aonori (seaweed flakes)
- Optional: sliced green onion

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Cabbage:** Shred finely; keep fluffy (don’t compress too much).
2. **Optional add-ins:** Slice green onion; prepare any seafood; have pork belly ready.
3. **Mix batter:** Combine batter ingredients per source until smooth.
4. **Fold in cabbage:** Mix gently so cabbage is coated but batter isn’t overworked.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Cook pancake base
1. Heat skillet/griddle over **medium heat**. Lightly oil the surface.
2. Add half the mixture to form a thick round pancake (about 2–3 cm thick).
3. If using pork belly slices, lay on top in a single layer.
4. Cover with a lid and cook until underside is golden and set (timing per source; usually ~5 min).

## 2) Flip + finish
5. Flip carefully (use two spatulas if needed).
6. Cover and cook the second side until cooked through and golden (another ~5 min).
7. Optional: flip once more briefly to crisp both sides.

> **Doneness check:** Center should be hot and set, not wet batter.

## 3) Sauce + toppings
8. Transfer to a plate/box.
9. Drizzle okonomiyaki sauce and Japanese mayo.
10. Finish with bonito flakes + aonori (and green onion if using).

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Exterior:** Golden and lightly crisp.
- **Interior:** Moist but set; cabbage tender.
- **Toppings:** Sauce/mayo balance; bonito flakes “dance” from heat.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
This recipe makes 2 pancakes. For 10 portions:
- Decide your portioning:
  - If 1 portion = 1/2 pancake → 2 pancakes = 4 portions → multiply by **2.5×**
  - If 1 portion = 1 pancake → multiply by **5×**

**Batch notes**
- Cook pancakes to order when possible (best texture).
- Pre-shred cabbage and pre-mix batter base; fold cabbage in closer to cooking to keep it crisp.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Best as a flat container (keeps shape).
- Keep sauces in small cups or drizzle lightly and include extra cups (prevents sogginess).
- Bonito + aonori in a small dry sachet/cup for best aroma.

Label: “Add dry toppings last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Best fresh.**  
- Refrigerate up to 1 day; reheat in a pan or air fryer to restore crispness (microwave softens).
