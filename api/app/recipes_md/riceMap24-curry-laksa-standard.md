# Curry Laksa (Malaysian/Singaporean Coconut Curry Noodle Soup) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A spicy, creamy coconut curry noodle soup with shrimp (or chicken), tofu puffs, and a bold laksa paste base. Great as a premium soup bowl when packaged with a separate “broth cup” and garnish kit.

**Source recipe:** Rasa Malaysia — “Laksa Recipe (Curry Laksa)”  
https://rasamalaysia.com/laksa-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Malaysia/Singapore • Coconut Curry • Noodle Soup
- **Yield:** 4–6 portions (depending on bowl size)
- **Active time:** ~25 min  
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (paste frying + broth balance + noodle service)

## Ingredients (4–6 portions)

> **Important:** Laksa’s signature flavor depends on the **laksa paste** and the **coconut milk + stock ratio**.  
> Use the linked Rasa Malaysia recipe for **exact measurements** (paste, coconut milk, stock, fish sauce, salt, sugar).

### A) Broth base (per source)
- Laksa paste (store-bought or homemade per source)
- Coconut milk
- Chicken stock (or water/stock per source)
- Seasonings per source (salt/sugar/fish sauce/shrimp paste if included)

### B) Noodles (choose one or mix)
- Rice vermicelli and/or thick rice noodles (laksa noodles), per source amounts

### C) Proteins & toppings (classic set; per source)
- Shrimp/prawns (common) and/or chicken
- Tofu puffs (tau pok), halved (highly recommended)
- Bean sprouts (blanched)
- Eggs (boiled, optional)

### D) Garnish (serve as a kit)
- Fresh cilantro
- Sliced red chili
- Lime wedges

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Prep noodles + toppings (bean sprouts, tofu puffs, eggs)  
2) Fry laksa paste (build flavor)  
3) Simmer broth + season  
4) Cook proteins (shrimp/chicken)  
5) Assemble bowls to order

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Prepare toppings
1. **Tofu puffs:** Halve and set aside.
2. **Bean sprouts:** Blanch briefly, drain well.
3. **Eggs (optional):** Boil and halve.
4. **Noodles:** Set up a pot of water to boil for service.

## 2) Build the broth (paste frying matters)
1. Heat a pot over **medium heat** with a little oil (per source).
2. Add laksa paste and fry until very fragrant and oil starts to separate slightly (don’t rush).
3. Add stock and bring to a gentle simmer.
4. Add coconut milk and keep at a **gentle simmer** (avoid hard boiling to reduce splitting).
5. Season per source (salt/sugar/fish sauce etc.). Taste and adjust slowly.

## 3) Cook proteins
- **Shrimp:** Add near the end and cook just until pink (very fast).
- **Chicken:** Simmer until cooked through (time depends on cut; follow source guidance).

## 4) Noodles (cook last-minute)
1. Cook noodles per package instructions.
2. Drain thoroughly and portion into bowls.

## 5) Assemble bowls
1. Noodles in bowl.
2. Add tofu puffs + bean sprouts + protein.
3. Ladle hot laksa broth over.
4. Add egg (optional).
5. Finish with garnish kit (cilantro, chili, lime).

**Customer note:** “Squeeze lime and add chili to taste.”

---

## Quality Checks (Before Service)
- **Broth:** Creamy, spicy, deeply aromatic (paste should be “cooked”).
- **Coconut:** Not split/oily → keep simmer gentle.
- **Shrimp:** Just cooked (not rubbery).
- **Toppings:** Tofu puffs soak broth and feel premium.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Because yield is 4–6 portions, plan:
- **2×** batch for 10 standard bowls (or **1.75×** if bowls are smaller).

**Batch notes**
- Make broth in a big pot (holds well).
- Keep noodles separate until service.
- Portion tofu puffs and sprouts assembly-line style.

- Container A: noodles + toppings (shrimp/chicken + tofu puffs + sprouts)
- Container B (sealed): hot broth
- Container C: garnish kit (cilantro + chili + lime wedge)

Label: “Pour hot broth over noodles + toppings. Add garnish last.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Broth:** Refrigerate up to **2–3 days** (stir well when reheating).
- **Proteins:** 2 days (shrimp best day-of).
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer; avoid hard boil.
