# Lu Rou Fan (Taiwanese Braised Pork Rice) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Taiwan’s beloved comfort bowl: aromatic braised minced/chopped pork belly over steamed rice, often served with blanched greens and a braised egg. Great for meal prep and premium rice boxes.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Lu Rou Fan (Taiwanese Braised Pork Rice)”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/lu-rou-fan-taiwanese-braised-pork-rice/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Taiwanese • Braise • Rice Bowl
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min  
- **Total time:** ~1 hr (mostly hands-off braise)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (rendering + gentle simmer)

- Cutting board + sharp knife
- Spatula/wooden spoon
- Measuring spoons/cups

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Main
- **Pork belly**, finely chopped or minced *(use the cut/amount specified in the source recipe)*
- **Shallots** *(often fried shallots are used in Taiwanese versions; follow source)*
- **Garlic**, minced (if included per source)

### Braise liquids & seasonings *(use the source recipe for exact amounts)*
- **Soy sauce** (and/or light soy + dark soy if specified)
- **Shaoxing wine** (or equivalent per source)
- **Sugar** (rock sugar/brown sugar per source)
- **Water / stock**
- **Five-spice powder** *(if included)*
- **White pepper** *(if included)*
- **Star anise** *(commonly included; follow source)*
- Optional: **fried shallots** (a key flavor), per source instructions

### To serve
- Cooked rice: **~250 g cooked rice per bowl** (4 bowls)
- Optional sides: blanched bok choy/greens
- Optional topping: braised eggs (see below)

> **Important:** Lu Rou Fan flavor depends on the **exact soy/sugar/wine balance** and aromatics.  
> Follow the linked source recipe for **precise measurements**.

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Pork belly:** Chop very finely (small dice) so it becomes spoonable and coats rice well.
2. **Shallots:** Prep per source (fresh shallots to fry or use fried shallots).
3. **Aromatics/spices:** Measure seasonings and set near the stove.
4. **Rice:** Cook and keep hot.

---

## Method (Process Flow — follow source for exact quantities)

### 1) Render + build base
1. Add chopped pork belly to a pot over **medium heat** and cook until fat renders and pork begins to brown.
2. Add shallots/fried shallots as directed. Stir until fragrant and lightly caramelized.
3. Add aromatics and spices (e.g., star anise/five-spice) per source.

### 2) Braise
4. Add soy sauce, wine, sugar, and water/stock per source ratios.
5. Bring to a simmer, then reduce to **low**.
6. Simmer gently **45–60 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until:
   - pork is tender, and
   - sauce is thick, glossy, and spoonable (not watery).

### 3) Final adjustment
7. Taste and adjust seasoning per source guidance (small tweaks only).
8. Remove whole spices (e.g., star anise) before service if desired.

---

## Optional: Braised Eggs (Great add-on)
- Hard-boil eggs, peel, then simmer in the braise liquid for **10–20 minutes** to absorb flavor.  
*(Follow the source’s egg method if provided.)*

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Sauce texture:** Thick and glossy; clings to pork; spoon coats rice well.
- **Flavor:** Savory-sweet with warm spice; not overly salty.
- **Aroma:** Shallot-forward, with subtle spice notes.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Pork belly: **2.5×** source amount
- Braise liquids/seasonings: multiply **exact source measurements** by **2.5×**

### Batch notes
- Use a wide pot for better reduction control.
- Stir more often in the last 15 minutes to prevent sticking (sugar can burn).

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Rice in base; lu rou on top.
- Add **greens** in a side compartment if offering.
- Offer **braised egg** as a paid add-on.

**Holding time:** Excellent; flavors improve after resting.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3–4 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer or microwave; add a splash of water only if too thick.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Chop pork belly small—this dish is meant to be spooned over rice.
- Reduce slowly; the goal is a rich “lacquered” sauce.
- Great as a next-day dish: consider cooking ahead for peak flavor.
