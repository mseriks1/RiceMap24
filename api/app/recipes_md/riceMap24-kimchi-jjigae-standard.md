# Kimchi Jjigae (Korean Kimchi Stew with Pork) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean staple: rich, spicy kimchi stew with pork and tofu. Ideal as a comforting soup-bowl item (especially in cooler seasons) and easy to batch for home kitchens.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Kimchi Jjigae (Kimchi stew)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/kimchi-jjigae

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Stew • Spicy/Sour
- **Yield:** **4 portions**
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Total time:** ~35–45 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (simmer + seasoning balance)

---

## Equipment
- Medium pot or deep skillet with lid (3–4 L)
- Ladle
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring spoons/cups
- Optional: kitchen scale (for repeatability)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Core
- **450 g** well-fermented kimchi, chopped (about 4 cups loosely packed)
- **120–180 ml** kimchi juice (from the kimchi container) *(start with 1/2 cup; add more to taste)*
- **340 g** pork belly or pork shoulder, sliced into bite-size pieces
- **1** medium onion (about **200 g**), sliced
- **2** scallions, chopped (white/green separated if you want cleaner finish)
- **500 ml** water or light stock *(about 2 cups; adjust for your preferred stew thickness)*
- **300 g** firm tofu, cut into cubes (or thick slices)

### Seasoning (build in layers)
- **1 Tbsp** gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) *(add more for heat)*
- **1 tsp** sugar *(optional; balances sour kimchi)*
- **1 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **1 tsp** kosher salt *(or to taste)*
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce *(or to taste; optional but very common)*
- **1 tsp** minced garlic

### Optional (highly recommended if you have it)
- **1 tsp** gochujang (Korean chili paste) *(adds body and depth)*
- **150 g** mushrooms (enoki or oyster), optional

> Notes:
> - **Sour/aged kimchi** makes the best stew.
> - Saltiness varies by kimchi brand; season at the end.

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Kimchi:** Chop into bite-size pieces. Reserve kimchi juice.
2. **Pork:** Slice into bite-size pieces (2–3 cm).
3. **Onion/scallions:** Slice onion; chop scallions.
4. **Tofu:** Cube or slice; set aside on paper towel to reduce wateriness.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Start with pork + kimchi (foundation flavor)
1. Heat a pot over **medium heat**.
2. Add **pork** and cook 2–3 minutes to start rendering fat (no browning needed yet).
3. Add **kimchi** and stir-fry together **3–5 minutes** until kimchi softens and smells deep and “stew-ready.”

> This step concentrates flavor and reduces harsh raw kimchi notes.

## 2) Build the stew
4. Add **onion**.
5. Add **water/stock (500 ml)** + **kimchi juice (start 120 ml)**.
6. Add **garlic, gochugaru, sugar (optional), fish sauce (optional), and gochujang (optional)**.
7. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a **gentle simmer**.
8. Simmer **20–25 minutes** (lid slightly ajar if you want it to reduce).

## 3) Add tofu (and mushrooms) near the end
9. Add tofu (and mushrooms if using).
10. Simmer **5–7 minutes** so tofu heats through and absorbs broth.

## 4) Finish + balance (critical step)
11. Turn off heat and stir in **sesame oil**.
12. Taste and adjust:
   - **More sour/kimchi flavor:** add more kimchi juice (1 Tbsp at a time).
   - **More salty/umami:** add fish sauce or a pinch of salt.
   - **More heat:** add gochugaru (1 tsp at a time).
   - **Too sour:** add 1/2 tsp sugar (optional) and simmer 2 minutes.

13. Add scallions (green parts) as a fresh finish.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Broth:** Spicy-sour, deeply kimchi-forward; not bland.
- **Pork:** Tender, not chewy (gentle simmer helps).
- **Tofu:** Heated through, not broken apart (stir gently).

---

## Serving Suggestions (RiceMap24-friendly)
- Serve with **steamed rice** (separate container recommended).
- Optional add-on: **soft-boiled egg** or **extra tofu**.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Target amounts (10 portions)
- Kimchi: **1125 g**
- Kimchi juice: **300–450 ml** (taste-dependent)
- Pork: **850 g**
- Onion: **500 g** (2–3 onions)
- Water/stock: **1250 ml**
- Tofu: **750 g**
- Gochugaru: **2.5 Tbsp** (then adjust)
- Garlic: **2.5 tsp**
- Sesame oil: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Fish sauce: **2.5 Tbsp** (optional; adjust)
- Sugar: **2.5 tsp** (optional; adjust)

### Batch notes
- Simmer stew base first (kimchi+pork+broth), then add tofu per batch to keep it intact.
- Stir occasionally; avoid hard boiling.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Stew in leak-proof container**
- **Rice separate** (prevents sogginess and improves customer experience)
- Garnish kit: scallions in a small cup (optional)

**Holding time:** Very good; flavor often improves after 30–60 minutes.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3 days**
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer; avoid vigorous boiling (tofu breaks, pork dries)
- Add a splash of water if the stew reduces too much.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Soy (tofu), fish (fish sauce if used)
- **Gluten:** Possible (some gochujang brands)
- **Spicy:** Yes (adjustable)
