# Grilled Stuffed Thai Chicken Wings (Angel Wings) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A premium Thai-style appetizer: **deboned chicken wings stuffed** with a savory pork–glass noodle filling, **steamed**, then **grilled and glazed** until glossy.  
This is highly photogenic and not the “standard Thai takeaway” you see everywhere.

**Source recipe (reference):** TASTE — “Grilled Stuffed Thai Chicken Wings” (Leela Punyaratabandhu)  
https://tastecooking.com/recipes/grilled-stuffed-thai-chicken-wings/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai / SE Asian grill
- **Style:** Stuffed wings → steam → grill + glaze
- **Yield:** 4 servings (about 16 stuffed wings)
- **Active time:** ~45–60 min (first time deboning takes longer)
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min (plus chilling time)
- **Skill level:** Medium–Advanced (deboning is the hardest part)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24 (Home Kitchen + Sales)
- **Looks premium:** glossy glaze + “wow” stuffing reveal when cut.
- **Great upsells:** extra dip, spicy dip, extra wings, “party box”.
- **Batch-friendly:** stuff + steam in advance, grill to order.

## Ingredients (from source)

### Filling
- **1 oz** dried glass noodles
- **2 tsp** coconut oil
- **4 oz** white button mushrooms, finely chopped
- **4 oz** ground pork
- **2 Tbsp** oyster sauce
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **3 lg** garlic cloves, minced
- **2 Tbsp** finely chopped fresh cilantro leaves and stems
- **1 tsp** ground white or black pepper

### Wings
- **16 lg** chicken wings

### Glaze
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** honey
- **1 tsp** ground white or black pepper
- **1/4 cup** coconut oil, melted, or vegetable oil

## Prep Plan (Recommended Workflow)
1) Make filling (and chill)  
2) Debone wings (wingette + tip only)  
3) Stuff + toothpick  
4) Steam (cook through)  
5) Grill + glaze (golden + glossy)  
6) Pack with sauce cups

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Make the filling
1. **Soak noodles:** Soak glass noodles in warm water to cover until softened, about **15 minutes**. Drain, squeeze dry, and cut into **1/2-inch** lengths. Return to a mixing bowl.
2. **Sauté mushrooms:** Heat **2 tsp coconut oil** over medium-high. Add chopped mushrooms and sauté until soft and they release moisture, about **2 minutes**.
3. **Dry mushrooms:** Transfer mushrooms to a paper-towel-lined plate and press lightly to remove moisture.
4. **Mix filling:** Add mushrooms to noodles. Add ground pork, oyster sauce, fish sauce, garlic, cilantro, and pepper. Mix until sticky and well combined.
5. **Chill:** Cover and refrigerate while you prep wings (firms filling, easier stuffing).

---

## 2) Prep wings (separate drumette)
1. Cut through the joint connecting the **drumette** to the rest of the wing.
2. Reserve drumettes for another use.
3. You will stuff the **wingette + tip** portion (this is the classic “angel wing” shape).

---

## 3) Debone wingettes (the tricky part)
> If it’s your first time, buy a few extra wings to practice.

1. Break the partial wing at the joint where wingette meets tip **without breaking the skin**.
2. With a sharp paring knife, cut around the top where meat attaches to the joint bone.
3. Carefully pull the wingette meat toward the tip to expose the bones that run through the wingette.
4. Pull out those bones while keeping the meat/skin intact, creating an “empty pocket”.

Repeat until you have **16 boned wing pockets**.

---

## 4) Stuff and seal
1. Stuff each wing pocket with pork mixture (fill well but **don’t overfill**—it expands slightly).
2. Close opening with a **wooden toothpick**.
3. Place wings on a tray in a single layer.

---

## 5) Steam (fully cook filling)
1. Bring steamer water to a **gentle boil**.
2. Arrange wings in a single layer in steamer basket (not touching water).
3. Cover and steam until juices run clear when tested, about **10 minutes**.
4. Remove wings, blot dry, and cool on a sheet pan.
5. **Do not remove toothpicks yet.**

---

## 6) Make glaze
Whisk together:
- soy sauce
- honey
- pepper
- melted coconut oil (or vegetable oil)

---

## 7) Grill + glaze (finish for color)
### Grill setup
- Medium-high fire: **400–450°F / 200–230°C** (two-zone setup recommended)

### Grill method
1. Place steamed wings on the **cool side** of the grill.
2. Cover and cook, brushing glaze along the way.
3. Since wings are already cooked, you only need to heat through and brown: **10–12 minutes** total, until golden and glossy.

---

## Serve
1. Transfer to platter.
2. Remove toothpicks.
3. Cool to slightly warmer than room temperature (best texture) and serve.

---

## Doneness + Quality Checks
- **Filling:** no pink pork; juices run clear after steaming.
- **Skin:** glossy and lightly charred; not burnt.
- **Texture:** juicy, not dry (don’t over-grill).

## Scaling for Production (10 orders)
If 1 order = 4 wings → 10 orders = 40 wings:
- Multiply ingredient amounts by **2.5×** (since base is 16 wings)
- Plan extra practice wings if deboning is new

**Ops tip:** Stuff + steam all wings in advance. Grill in batches to order.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
These wings hold well, but crisp/gloss is best if not trapped in steam:
- Use a **vented** container or crack lid 2–3 minutes before sealing.
- Dip in separate cup(s).
- Include a small label: “Reheat briefly in oven/air fryer for best finish.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate (cooked):** up to 2 days.
- **Reheat best:** oven/air fryer **190–200°C** for **6–10 min**.
- Microwave works but softens skin.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce), shellfish (oyster sauce), fish (fish sauce), gluten possible (sauces)
