# Korean BBQ Meatball Bowl (Asian Twist, Banker-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A familiar Western format (meatballs + rice bowl) with a clear Korean twist: **gochujang-ginger glaze**, sesame, scallions, and a quick pickled side.  
Not a traditional Korean dish—this is fusion built for home-kitchen sales and repeat orders.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Meatball rice bowl (fusion)
- **Asian twist:** Gochujang + soy + sesame + ginger
- **Yield:** 4 bowls (about 16–20 meatballs)
- **Active time:** ~25 minutes
- **Total time:** ~35 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Banker-friendly:** meatballs are universally popular.
- **Batch-friendly:** bake a tray; sauce is quick; easy portioning.
- **Delivery winner:** sauce coats meatballs; rice separate; side in cup.
- **Upsells:** extra meatballs, extra sauce cup, add kimchi, add egg.

---

## Equipment
- Mixing bowl
- Sheet pan + parchment (or skillet)
- Small saucepan (glaze)
- Knife + cutting board
- Rice cooker/pot

---

## Ingredients (4 bowls)

## A) Meatballs
- **500 g** ground beef (or 50/50 beef+pork)
- **1 large egg**
- **40 g** breadcrumbs (or panko)
- **60 ml** milk (or water)
- **2 cloves** garlic, minced
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **2 scallions**, finely sliced
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper

> Texture tip: soak breadcrumbs in milk 2 minutes first for juicy meatballs.

---

## B) Korean-style glaze (the identity)
- **3 Tbsp** gochujang
- **2 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 Tbsp** honey (or brown sugar)
- **1 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- **1 tsp** grated garlic
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **60–90 ml** water
- **1 tsp** cornstarch + **1 Tbsp** water (slurry) *(for glossy cling)*

---

## C) Rice
- **4 bowls** cooked jasmine rice (about 250 g cooked per bowl)

---

## D) Quick pickled cucumber side (5 minutes)
- **1 cucumber**, thin slices
- **2 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 Tbsp** sugar
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- Optional: chili flakes + sesame seeds

---

## E) Finish (photogenic)
- Sesame seeds
- Sliced scallions
- Optional: shredded carrot or cabbage for extra color

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Quick pickle cucumber (5 minutes)
1. Mix vinegar + sugar + salt until dissolved.
2. Toss in cucumber slices.
3. Let sit while you cook meatballs.

---

## 2) Make meatballs (8 minutes)
1. Preheat oven to **210°C**.
2. Mix breadcrumbs + milk; rest 2 minutes.
3. Add ground meat, egg, garlic, ginger, soy, sesame oil, scallions, salt, pepper.
4. Mix gently until combined (don’t overwork).
5. Roll into 16–20 meatballs (25–30 g each).

---

## 3) Bake meatballs (12–15 minutes)
1. Place on parchment-lined sheet.
2. Bake **12–15 min** until cooked through.
   - Doneness target: **72°C** internal.

Alternative: pan-sear then finish on low heat.

---

## 4) Make glaze (5 minutes)
1. In a small saucepan, whisk gochujang, soy, honey, vinegar, sesame oil, garlic, ginger, and water.
2. Bring to a simmer.
3. Add cornstarch slurry and stir until glossy and thick enough to coat.

**Glaze target:** coats a spoon and clings to meatballs.

---

## 5) Toss meatballs in glaze (1 minute)
- Add baked meatballs to sauce and toss until coated.

---

## 6) Build bowls
Per bowl:
1. Rice
2. 4–5 glazed meatballs
3. Cucumber pickle side
4. Sesame + scallion garnish

---

## Quality Checks
- Meatballs juicy, not dense.
- Glaze glossy and balanced (sweet-salty-spicy).
- Pickles crisp and bright.

---

## Portioning (Per order)
- Meatballs: **4–5 pieces** (120–160 g cooked)
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Pickles: **70–100 g**
- Extra sauce cup: 20–30 ml (upsell)

---

## Scaling (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Meat: 1.25 kg
- Eggs: 2–3
- Glaze: 2.5×
Bake in two trays. Keep glaze warm and toss per batch.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Container A: meatballs + glaze
- Container B: rice
- Cup C: pickled cucumber
- Optional: garnish cup (sesame/scallion)

Label: “Add pickles after reheating. Extra sauce optional.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Meatballs keep 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat gently; add a splash of water to loosen glaze if needed.
- Keep pickles chilled.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce, gochujang)
- Sesame (sesame oil/seeds)
- Egg
- Wheat (breadcrumbs)
- Gluten possible (soy/gochujang brands)
