# Thai Fried Rice (Khao Pad) — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Street-food style Thai fried rice built around a simple soy–oyster seasoning and served with **lime** + **prik nam pla** (chilies in fish sauce).
This card adapts Mark Wiens’ “Thai Fried Rice with Shrimp” recipe into a **choose-your-protein** format for RiceMap24/home kitchens.

**Source recipe:** Eating Thai Food — “Thai Fried Rice Recipe with Shrimp (Khao Pad Goong)”
https://www.eatingthaifood.com/thai-fried-rice-recipe-shrimp/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Fast wok fried rice + prik nam pla condiment
- **Yield:** **4 portions**
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Total time:** ~20–25 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (high-heat timing)

---

## Choose Your Protein (Pick ONE for 4 portions)

Mark Wiens notes you can make this with shrimp or any protein you like.
Use one of these **RiceMap24-friendly** options:

### Option A — Shrimp (street-food style)
- **20–40** small/medium shrimp *(based on “5–10 shrimp per plate” in source)*
  - If using head-on shrimp: peel head/shell but try to keep head oil on the body (Thai trick).

### Option B — Chicken (diced)
- **500 g** chicken thigh, small cubes (1–1.5 cm)

### Option C — Beef (wok strips)
- **500 g** beef sirloin/flank, thin strips (slice 3–4 mm across the grain)

### Option D — Tofu (vegetarian)
- **600 g** firm tofu, pressed and cubed

> Target protein per bowl: **125–150 g**.

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Fried rice base (scaled from the source’s 1-plate recipe)
- **6 cups cooked jasmine rice, cooled/day-old** *(source: 1.5 cups per plate)*
- **1 onion**, sliced (source: ¼ onion per plate)
- **8 garlic cloves**, minced (source: 2 cloves per plate)
- **4 eggs** (source: 1 egg per plate)
- **4 leaves** Chinese broccoli (gai lan) *or* other crisp green (source: 1 leaf per plate)
- **2 Tbsp soy sauce** (source: ½ Tbsp per plate)
- **2 Tbsp oyster sauce** (source: ½ Tbsp per plate)
- **Pinch sugar** (optional, to balance; source uses a pinch)
- **4 Tbsp neutral oil** (source: 1 Tbsp per plate)
- **Black pepper**, to finish (source serving note)
- **Lime wedges**, to serve (source serves with lime)

## B) Prik Nam Pla (chilies in fish sauce) — for serving
- **20 Thai chilies**, sliced (source: 5 per plate)
- **12 Tbsp fish sauce** (source: 3 Tbsp per plate)
- **2 limes**, for squeezing (source: ½ lime per plate)

> This condiment is “essential” in Thai fried rice service according to the source.

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. **Rice:** Use cooled/day-old rice so it stays dry and doesn’t clump.
2. **Garlic:** Mince 8 cloves.
3. **Onion:** Slice.
4. **Chinese broccoli:** Slice into 1 cm strips (source method).
5. **Eggs:** Crack into 4 cups (or 2 cups if doing 2-bowl batches).
6. **Protein prep:**
   - Shrimp: peel; keep tail if you want “Thai style.”
   - Chicken/beef: small pieces for fast wok cooking.
   - Tofu: press and cube; pat dry.

---

## Prik Nam Pla (Make first)
1. Add **12 Tbsp fish sauce** to a bowl.
2. Add sliced chilies.
3. Add a squeeze of lime (optional) and stir.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable Workflow)

> Best practice: cook **2 portions at a time** in a wok for better heat control.

### Batch plan
- Split rice, aromatics, sauces, veg, eggs, and protein into **2 batches** (2 bowls each).

### 1) Aromatics (15 seconds)
1. Heat wok over **medium-high**.
2. Add **2 Tbsp oil** (half).
3. Add half the garlic; stir-fry ~**15 seconds** until fragrant.

### 2) Cook protein (timing by protein)
- **Shrimp:** add shrimp; fry ~**30 seconds** until starting to turn pink/firm.
- **Chicken:** stir-fry 2–3 minutes until mostly cooked.
- **Beef:** sear quickly 60–90 seconds; keep it medium for tenderness.
- **Tofu:** sear 2–3 minutes until edges lightly golden.

### 3) “Dry rice” trick (10 seconds)
Add **half the rice first**, stir-fry ~**10 seconds** (source trick to keep rice dry and absorb juices), then add the rest.

### 4) Egg (20 seconds)
1. Push rice aside.
2. Crack in **2 eggs** (per batch) and scramble briefly.
3. Mix egg through rice.

### 5) Season (20–30 seconds)
Add (per batch):
- **1 Tbsp soy sauce**
- **1 Tbsp oyster sauce**
- tiny pinch sugar (optional)

Stir-fry until evenly coated.

### 6) Vegetables + finish (30 seconds)
1. Add onion + Chinese broccoli strips.
2. Stir-fry ~**30 seconds** until vegetables wilt slightly but stay crisp.
3. Add scallions if you use them (optional; source uses green onions as part of prep steps).
4. Plate immediately.

### 7) Serve Thai-style
- Serve with lime wedge.
- Finish with black pepper.
- Serve **prik nam pla** on the side to spoon over bites.

## Quality Checks
- **Rice:** dry and separate, not mushy.
- **Sauce:** evenly coats rice, not watery.
- **Veg:** still crisp.
- **Thai signature:** lime + fish-sauce-chili condiment on the side.

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
Multiply everything by **2.5×**:
- Cooked rice: **15 cups**
- Soy sauce: **5 Tbsp**
- Oyster sauce: **5 Tbsp**
- Eggs: **10**
- Oil: **10 Tbsp**
- Protein: **~1.25–1.5 kg** (depending on option)
- Prik nam pla: chilies 50; fish sauce 30 Tbsp; limes 5

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Fried rice in main container.
- **Prik nam pla in a small cup** (critical; keeps the “Thai-ness” and controls salt/heat).
- Lime wedge in a small bag/cup.

**Holding time:** Best within 1–2 hours.
