# Pancit Bihon Guisado — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Filipino rice-noodle stir-fry that’s **very EU-friendly**: savory, lightly sweet, colorful, and photogenic (carrots + cabbage + scallions).  
Built for RiceMap24/home kitchens: **one noodle base + choose your protein**, consistent portioning, and delivery tips.

**Source recipe (reference):** Kawaling Pinoy — “Pancit Bihon Guisado”  
https://www.kawalingpinoy.com/pancit-bihon-guisado/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Filipino
- **Style:** Rice noodles (bihon) stir-fried with stock + soy
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~35–45 min (includes noodle soak)
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (noodle hydration + liquid absorption)

## Choose Your Protein (Pick ONE)
Target: **~500–650 g total protein** for a 6-portion batch.

### Option A — Chicken
- 600 g boneless chicken thigh, sliced thin

### Option B — Pork + Chicken
- 300 g pork (loin/shoulder), sliced thin
- 300 g chicken thigh, sliced thin

### Option C — Shrimp + Chicken
- 350 g shrimp, peeled
- 350 g chicken thigh, sliced thin

### Option D — Vegetarian (tofu)
- 700 g firm tofu, pressed and sliced/cubed  
  + optional: mushrooms for extra umami

> Keep protein amounts consistent so pricing stays predictable.

## Equipment
- Wok or wide sauté pan (large surface helps noodles stay fluffy)
- Mixing bowl (noodle soak)
- Tongs/spatula
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring cups/spoons

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

### Noodles + liquid base
- 225 g rice noodles (bihon) *(about 8 oz)*
- 1 liter chicken stock *(about 4 cups)*
- 60 ml soy sauce *(about 1/4 cup)*

### Aromatics
- 1 onion, sliced thin
- 2 cloves garlic, minced

### Vegetables
- 2 carrots, sliced thin on a bias
- 2 celery stalks, sliced thin on a bias
- 1 small cabbage, chopped (bite-size)

### Oil + finish
- 2 Tbsp neutral oil (plus more if needed)
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- Optional garnish: chopped scallions + lemon/calamansi wedges

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)

### 1) Soak noodles (critical)
1. Place bihon noodles in a bowl.
2. Cover with **warm water** and soak **8–10 minutes** until pliable but not mushy.
3. Drain well and set aside.

> If you soak too long, noodles break and turn soft.

### 2) Prep everything
- Slice protein thin (3–4 mm) for fast cooking.
- Prep vegetables (thin, even cuts cook quickly and look nice).

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable Workflow)

### 1) Cook protein
1. Heat wok over **medium-high**.
2. Add oil.
3. Add protein and stir-fry until mostly cooked:
   - Chicken/pork: no raw pink remains.
   - Shrimp: remove as soon as it turns pink.
   - Tofu: sear until lightly golden.

Remove protein to a plate (you’ll add back later).

### 2) Aromatics + veg
1. Add onion and garlic; stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
2. Add carrots + celery; stir-fry 1 minute.
3. Add cabbage and toss 30–60 seconds (just to soften slightly).

### 3) Add stock + soy and simmer
1. Pour in chicken stock and soy sauce.
2. Bring to a simmer.

### 4) Add noodles and absorb
1. Add drained bihon.
2. Toss continuously to distribute liquid.
3. Keep at a steady simmer until noodles absorb most of the liquid and become tender (about 5–8 minutes).
4. Return protein (and any juices). Toss to heat through.

### 5) Finish
- Taste and adjust with black pepper and a pinch of salt if needed.
- Optional: squeeze lemon/calamansi over bowls for brightness.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Noodles:** tender, separated, not soupy.
- **Veg:** still bright, not mushy.
- **Protein:** tender (shrimp not rubbery).
- **Flavor:** savory soy + stock, balanced (not too salty).

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply by **1.67×** (6 → 10 portions).
- Bihon noodles: ~375 g
- Stock: ~1.67 L
- Soy sauce: ~100 ml
- Protein: scale to ~1.0–1.2 kg total
- Veg: scale 1.67×

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Use a wide container so noodles don’t compress into a brick.
- Include lemon/calamansi wedge in a small bag/cup.
- For shrimp version: add shrimp at the end or pack separately to avoid overcooking.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- If needed: refrigerate up to 1 day; reheat gently with a splash of stock/water.

---

## Allergens
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Gluten possible (some soy sauces)
- Shellfish (if shrimp)
