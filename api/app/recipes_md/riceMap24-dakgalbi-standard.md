# Dakgalbi (Korean Spicy Chicken Stir-Fry) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Korean stir-fry: chicken and vegetables cooked in a spicy gochujang-based sauce. Great as a hot plate dish or as a spicy rice box.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Dakgalbi (Spicy stir-fried chicken)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/dakgalbi

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Stir-Fry • Spicy
- **Yield:** 4 portions *(easy to sell as 2–4 meal-prep boxes)*
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~40 min *(includes optional marinating)*
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (sauce mixing + timing)

---

## Equipment
- Large skillet, wok, or wide sauté pan (with lid if possible)
- Mixing bowl
- Cutting board + knife
- Measuring spoons/cups or scale
- Kitchen tongs/spatula

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

### Chicken
- **900 g** boneless chicken thighs or breast, cut into bite-size pieces

### Vegetables (classic combo)
- **300 g** cabbage, sliced
- **200 g** sweet potato, thinly sliced *(or potato)*
- **150 g** carrot, sliced
- **1** onion (about **200 g**), sliced
- **2–3** scallions, cut into 4–5 cm pieces *(optional)*

### Optional add-ins (popular)
- **1–2** rice cakes (tteok), sliced *(or store-bought tteokbokki tteok)*
- **Mozzarella** (for “cheese dakgalbi”)
- **Cooked rice** to serve

### Sauce / Marinade (mix in a bowl)
- **3 Tbsp** gochujang (Korean chili paste)
- **1 Tbsp** gochugaru (Korean chili flakes) *(adjust for heat)*
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** minced garlic
- **1 Tbsp** sugar
- **1 Tbsp** rice syrup, corn syrup, or honey
- **1 Tbsp** vegetable oil
- **1 tsp** toasted sesame oil
- **1 tsp** ground black pepper

*(Sauce composition is based on the Maangchi recipe linked above.)*

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Chicken:** Cut into bite-size pieces (roughly 2–3 cm chunks).
2. **Vegetables:** Slice everything thinly so it cooks evenly (sweet potato especially).
3. **Sauce:** Mix all sauce ingredients in a bowl until smooth.
4. **Marinate (recommended):** Toss chicken with the sauce and rest **10–30 minutes** for better flavor.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Start the pan
1. Heat a large pan over **medium-high heat**.
2. Add the marinated chicken and spread in an even layer.

### 2) Add vegetables and cook
3. Add cabbage, sweet potato, carrot, and onion on top (don’t worry if it looks crowded).
4. Cook **uncovered** for **5 minutes**, then stir well.
5. Continue cooking **10–15 minutes**, stirring every 2–3 minutes, until:
   - chicken is fully cooked, and
   - sweet potato slices are tender.

> If the pan looks dry, add **2–4 Tbsp water** (a little at a time) to prevent scorching and help steam-cook the vegetables.

### 3) Finish
6. Stir in scallions for the last **1–2 minutes** (optional).
7. Taste and adjust:
   - **More heat:** + gochugaru (½ tsp at a time)
   - **More sweetness:** + sugar or syrup (½ tsp at a time)
   - **More salt:** + soy sauce (½ tsp at a time)

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Chicken:** No pink center; juicy (avoid overcooking on very high heat).
- **Vegetables:** Sweet potato should be tender, not crunchy.
- **Sauce:** Coats everything, slightly glossy, not watery.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply by **2.5×** (since base recipe is 4 portions).

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Chicken: **2250 g**
- Cabbage: **750 g**
- Sweet potato: **500 g**
- Carrot: **375 g**
- Onion: **500 g** (≈ 2–3 onions)
- Scallions: **5–7**

### Sauce (2.5×)
- Gochujang: **7.5 Tbsp**
- Gochugaru: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Soy sauce: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Minced garlic: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Sugar: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Syrup/honey: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Vegetable oil: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Sesame oil: **2.5 tsp**
- Black pepper: **2.5 tsp**

### Batch notes
- Cook in **2 batches** to keep heat strong and prevent steaming into “boil”.
- Use a wide pan to maximize surface area and caramelization.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- **Option A (simple):** Rice in base + dakgalbi on top.
- **Option B (premium):** Dakgalbi + rice separate compartments to keep rice fluffy.
- **Cheese version:** Add mozzarella in a separate cup, instruct customer to melt after reheating.

**Holding time:** Best within **2–3 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Cool quickly; store up to **3 days**.
- **Reheat:** Microwave (medium-high) or quick hot-pan toss. Add 1–2 Tbsp water if thick.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Soy (soy sauce), sesame (sesame oil)
- **Spicy:** Yes (adjustable)
- **Gluten:** Likely (soy sauce + some gochujang brands)

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Slice sweet potato thin so it finishes with the chicken.
- If sauce starts sticking, add small splashes of water and keep stirring.
- Flavor improves after a short marination, even **10 minutes**.
