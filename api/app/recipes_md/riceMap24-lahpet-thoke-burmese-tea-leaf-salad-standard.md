# Lahpet Thoke (Burmese Tea Leaf Salad) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A signature Burmese dish built for “wow” plating: crisp greens + tomatoes + chilies + **lots of crunchy bits**, all pulled together by an earthy, savory **tea-leaf dressing**.  
Perfect for RiceMap24 as a **premium side**, **share platter**, or “add-on salad” next to grilled meats, curry bowls, noodles, and rice.

**Source recipe (reference):** Viet World Kitchen — “A Shortcut to Buzzy Burmese Tea Leaf Salad (Laphet Thoke)”

## Equipment
- Small saucepan (garlic chips + oil) OR use store-bought garlic chips
- Fine mesh strainer
- Small food processor (for dressing)
- Cutting board + knife

## Ingredients

## A) Quick Tea-Leaf Dressing (makes a scant 1/2 cup)
- **3 Tbsp** loose green tea leaves (sencha or Dragonwell)
- **3 cups** just-boiled water
- **1 garlic clove**, coarsely chopped
- **1 Tbsp** coarsely chopped peeled ginger
- **1/2 tsp** fine sea salt
- **1/2 tsp** fish sauce (optional, for a bit of funk)
- **1/4 cup** fried garlic oil (from below) OR canola/peanut oil
- **1 1/2 tsp** distilled white vinegar OR **2 tsp** apple cider vinegar

> Shortcut option: If you can buy **prepared fermented tea-leaf dressing** (e.g., from a Burmese restaurant/market), use that instead and skip making the dressing.

---

## B) Salad Base + Toppings
- **5–6 packed cups (8.5 oz / ~240 g)** thinly sliced crisp lettuce (Romaine/Little Gem) OR cabbage (green or Napa)
- **1 tsp** fish sauce OR 2–3 pinches fine sea salt
- **1 cup** diced tomatoes (Roma, Early Girl, or cherry)
- **1/4 cup** chopped, seeded moderately-hot chile  
  (1 large Fresno/jalapeño/long chile, or 2 small)
- **3–4 Tbsp** fried garlic chips (see below; store-bought works)
- **1/4 to 1/2 cup** unsalted roasted peanuts, finely chopped
- **1/4 cup** roasted chickpeas, soynuts, or fried yellow split peas (optional)
- **1/4 cup** roasted sunflower or pumpkin seeds
- **1 Tbsp** toasted sesame seeds
- **2 Tbsp** dried shrimp powder (optional)
- **1 whole juicy lime**, quartered

---

## C) Fried Garlic Chips (makes about 1/4 cup chips + garlic oil)
- **1/4 cup** thinly sliced garlic
- **About 1/3 cup** neutral oil (canola or peanut), enough to cover garlic

---

## Prep Plan (Fast workflow)
1) Make garlic chips (or open store-bought chips)  
2) Make tea-leaf dressing  
3) Slice greens + chop toppings  
4) Build a composed platter (best photos)  
5) Toss at the table (or customer tosses at home)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Fried Garlic Chips (6–10 minutes)
1. Put sliced garlic in a small saucepan and add enough oil to cover.
2. Heat over **medium-low** until gently sizzling.
3. Fry several minutes until **pale golden** (they won’t be crisp yet).
4. Lift pan and swirl to slow cooking and monitor color.
5. When slightly darker, pour through a mesh strainer into a bowl (save the oil).
6. Spread garlic on paper towel; it crisps as it cools.

**Fix if not crisp:** return to oil and refry briefly, then drain again.  
**Save the garlic oil**—you’ll use it in the dressing.

---

## 2) Make the Quick Tea-Leaf Dressing (8 minutes)
1. Steep tea leaves in just-boiled water for **3 minutes**.
2. Drain well, pressing out excess water. Cool tea leaves to room temp.
3. In a small food processor, whirl garlic + ginger + salt until minced.
4. Add tea leaves and whirl to a fine texture.
5. Add oil + vinegar + (optional) fish sauce, then pulse to combine.
6. You should have a **scant 1/2 cup** dressing.

**Make-ahead:** dressing keeps about 1 week refrigerated.

---

## 3) Assemble Salad (Best “RiceMap24 plating”)
### Composed platter method (recommended for photos)
1. Make a bed of lettuce/cabbage on a rimmed platter.
2. Drizzle fish sauce (or sprinkle salt) over the greens.
3. Arrange piles “like flower petals”:
   - tomatoes
   - chile
   - fried garlic chips
   - peanuts
   - optional chickpeas/soynuts
   - sunflower/pumpkin seeds
   - sesame seeds
   - optional shrimp powder
4. Scrape dressing into the center.

### Tossing
- At the table, squeeze **half** the lime wedges over the salad and toss.
- Let diners add more lime wedges to taste.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Crunch:** you should hear it—lots of crisp bits.
- **Balance:** earthy tea dressing + bright lime + tomato.
- **Not soggy:** dress/toss close to eating time.

---

## Scaling (10 side portions)
Multiply the salad base by **2× to 2.5×** depending on portion size.
**Ops tip:** Pre-portion crunchy toppings into small cups; they keep well and speed assembly.

---

## Storage & Food Safety
- Keep greens dry and chilled.
- Keep dressing chilled.
- Keep crunchy toppings dry/airtight.
- Best assembled same day.

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Sesame
- Fish (fish sauce / shrimp powder if used)
