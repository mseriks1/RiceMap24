# Miso-Butter Salmon Rice Bowl (Asian Twist, Banker-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A modern “not-traditionally Asian” dinner built for home-kitchen sales: **pan-seared salmon** glazed with **miso-butter**, served over rice with a quick cucumber side.  
It’s familiar (salmon + rice bowl) but has a clear Japanese-style twist (miso + sesame + scallion).

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Fusion rice bowl (salmon + miso butter glaze)
- **Asian twist:** White miso + soy + sesame + optional yuzu/lime
- **Yield:** 4 bowls
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~25 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (quick glaze timing)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Banker-friendly:** salmon bowl is easy to sell and repeat-order.
- **Fast production:** 1 pan + quick side.
- **Great pickup/delivery:** salmon stays good; rice separate; sauce cup option.
- **Upsells:** extra salmon, extra glaze, add avocado, add edamame, add spicy mayo.

---

## Equipment
- Nonstick or stainless skillet
- Small bowl (glaze)
- Knife + cutting board
- Rice cooker or pot
- Mixing bowl (quick cucumber)

---

## Ingredients (4 bowls)

## A) Salmon
- **600–700 g** salmon fillet, cut into 4 portions (150–175 g each)
- Salt + black pepper
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil (for searing)

## B) Miso-butter glaze (the identity)
- **60 g** unsalted butter, softened
- **2 Tbsp** white miso
- **1 Tbsp** honey (or brown sugar)
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** rice vinegar (or lemon juice)
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- Optional: **1 tsp** grated ginger
- Optional: **1 tsp** yuzu or lime juice (bright finish)

> Flavor target: savory-sweet, rich, slightly tangy, very glossy when melted.

## C) Rice
- **400 g cooked rice** (about 1.5–2 cups cooked)
- Optional: sesame seeds to finish

## D) Quick cucumber side (pickup-friendly, 5 minutes)
- **1 large** cucumber, thin slices
- **1 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** sugar
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- Optional: chili flakes
- Optional: sesame seeds

## E) Fresh finish (photogenic)
- **2 scallions**, thin slices
- Optional: nori strips
- Optional: edamame (easy add-on)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make cucumber side (5 minutes)
1. Mix rice vinegar + sugar + salt until dissolved.
2. Toss cucumber slices in the dressing.
3. Let sit while you cook salmon.

---

## 2) Mix miso-butter glaze (2 minutes)
1. In a bowl, mash butter + miso until smooth.
2. Stir in honey, soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil (and ginger if using).
3. Set aside (you’ll melt it in the pan).

---

## 3) Sear salmon (8–10 minutes)
1. Pat salmon dry; season with salt and pepper.
2. Heat skillet over medium-high with 1 Tbsp oil.
3. Place salmon skin-side down (if skin-on) and sear **3–4 minutes** until crisp.
4. Flip and cook **2–3 minutes** until almost done.

**Doneness target:** 52–55°C internal for juicy salmon (or cook longer if customers prefer).

---

## 4) Glaze (1 minute)
1. Reduce heat to medium-low.
2. Add miso-butter mixture to the pan.
3. Spoon the melted glaze over salmon for **30–60 seconds** until glossy.

> Don’t burn the miso/butter—low heat for the glaze step.

---

## 5) Build bowls (RiceMap24 plating)
Per bowl:
1. Rice (base)
2. Salmon on top
3. Spoon 1–2 Tbsp extra glaze from pan over salmon
4. Add cucumber side
5. Finish with scallions + sesame seeds (and optional nori)

---

## Quality Checks
- Salmon crisped on edges, still juicy.
- Glaze glossy and balanced (not too salty—adjust with a tiny squeeze of lemon).
- Cucumber side bright and crunchy.

---

## Portioning (Per order)
- Salmon: **150–175 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Cucumber side: **70–100 g**
- Glaze: **20–30 ml** (or drizzle + optional extra cup)

---

## Scaling (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Salmon: 1.6–1.8 kg
- Glaze: 2.5×
- Cucumbers: 2–3 large
Cook salmon in batches; keep glaze warm and spoonable.

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway) — Recommended
- Container A: salmon + glaze (or glaze cup separate for cleaner look)
- Container B: rice (separate)
- Cup C: cucumber side
- Optional: garnish cup (scallions/nori)

Label: “Add cucumber side after reheating. Extra glaze optional.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- Reheat salmon gently (avoid overcooking): low microwave or warm pan.
- Keep cucumber side chilled.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (salmon)
- Soy (miso/soy sauce)
- Dairy (butter)
- Sesame (sesame oil/seeds)
- Gluten possible (soy sauce brand)
