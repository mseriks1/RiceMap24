# Tteokbokki (Korean Spicy Rice Cakes) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Korean street-food staple: chewy rice cakes in a glossy gochujang-based sauce. Easy to batch, high margin, and perfect as a snack bowl or add-on side.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Tteokbokki (Spicy rice cake)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/tteokbokki

## Summary
- **Category:** Korean • Street Food • Spicy
- **Yield:** ~4 portions (snack bowls)
- **Active time:** ~10 min  
- **Total time:** ~25–30 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (simmer + reduction)

## Equipment
- Medium pot or deep skillet
- Spatula/wooden spoon

## Ingredients (≈ 4 portions)

> **Important:** Tteokbokki depends on the **broth + gochujang/gochugaru ratio** and simmer time.  
> Use the linked Maangchi recipe for the **exact measurements**.

### Core (per source)
- Korean rice cakes (tteok), cylinder style (amount per source)
- Korean fish cakes (eomuk), sliced (optional but classic), amount per source
- Water/stock (often anchovy-kelp stock), amount per source
- Gochujang (Korean chili paste), amount per source
- Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes), amount per source
- Sugar (amount per source)
- Green onion, chopped (finish)

### Optional add-ins (operator choice; test)
- Boiled egg (classic add-on)
- Ramen noodles (rabokki)
- Mozzarella (cheese tteokbokki)

---

## Prep (5–10 minutes)
1. **Rice cakes:** If using refrigerated/frozen tteok, soak in warm water to soften.
2. **Fish cakes:** Slice into bite-size pieces.
3. **Green onion:** Chop.
4. Optional: Prep boiled eggs or cheese.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Build the broth base
1. Add water/stock to a pot (per source).
2. Stir in gochujang, gochugaru, and sugar until dissolved.
3. Bring to a simmer over **medium heat**.

## 2) Simmer rice cakes
4. Add rice cakes and simmer, stirring gently so they don’t stick.
5. Continue simmering until rice cakes are tender and sauce begins to thicken.

## 3) Add fish cakes + reduce to glossy finish
6. Add fish cakes (if using) and simmer until heated through.
7. Keep simmering until sauce reduces to a **thick, glossy coating** (not watery). Stir frequently in the last few minutes.

## 4) Finish
8. Add chopped green onion, stir, and serve immediately.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Rice cakes:** Chewy and tender (not hard in the center).
- **Sauce:** Thick and glossy; clings to the tteok.
- **Heat level:** Spicy but balanced with slight sweetness.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is ~4 portions. Multiply by **2.5×**.
- Multiply **all exact source measurements** by **2.5×** (tteok, broth, gochujang/gochugaru, sugar, fish cakes).

### Batch notes
- Use a wider pan for faster reduction.
- Stir more often as it thickens to prevent scorching.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Best as a **snack bowl** with lid.
- If offering boiled egg, include it whole or halved on top.
- Offer “spice level” by adjusting gochugaru per portion (or offer mild batch + spicy add-on powder).

**Holding time:** Best within **1–2 hours** (sauce thickens as it cools).

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days** (tteok firms up).
- **Reheat:** Add a splash of water and reheat gently on stove or microwave; stir to restore sauce texture.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Likely fish (fish cakes; anchovy stock)
- **May contain:** Soy (some gochujang brands)
- **Gluten:** Possible (some gochujang/fish cakes)

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Reduction is flavor: don’t rush the final thickening step.
- If sauce is too thick after holding, loosen with a splash of hot water and stir.
