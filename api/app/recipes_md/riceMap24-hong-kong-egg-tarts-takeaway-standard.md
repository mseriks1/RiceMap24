# Hong Kong Egg Tarts (蛋挞) — RiceMap24 Takeaway Dessert Standard

Classic Hong Kong bakery egg tarts: flaky pastry (or shortcrust) filled with silky egg custard, baked until lightly caramelized.  
**Perfect for pickup/takeaway:** stable at room temperature, looks premium, sells well as **2-pack / 4-pack / 6-pack**.

**Reference recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Hong Kong Egg Tarts”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/hong-kong-egg-tarts/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Hong Kong/Cantonese bakery)
- **Style:** Baked custard tartlets
- **Yield:** ~12 tarts (depends on tart tin size)
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30 min (includes chilling dough)
- **Skill level:** Medium (pastry + custard)

## Equipment
- Muffin tin or egg tart tins (12-cup)
- Mixing bowls + whisk
- Saucepan (warm custard)
- Fine mesh strainer (important for smooth custard)
- Rolling pin
- Optional: kitchen scale (for consistent portions)

---

## Ingredients (≈12 tarts)

## A) Pastry (choose one)
### Option 1 — Shortcrust (easiest, very stable)
- 250 g all-purpose flour
- 125 g cold unsalted butter, cubed
- 60 g powdered sugar
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 1 large egg yolk
- 2–3 Tbsp cold water (as needed)

### Option 2 — Flaky pastry (more “HK bakery”)
Use the Woks of Life flaky crust method if you want the classic laminated-style shell.

---

## B) Egg Custard Filling (silky)
- 4 large eggs
- 200 g sugar
- 240 ml hot water
- 120 ml evaporated milk
- 120 ml whole milk
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- Optional: pinch of salt (rounds flavor)

---

## Prep Notes
- The **strainer step** is non-negotiable for a smooth custard.
- Chill pastry well so shells hold shape.
- Do not overbake: custard should be set but still slightly wobbly in the center.

---

## Step 1 — Make Shortcrust Dough (10 minutes)
1. Mix flour, powdered sugar, and salt in a bowl.
2. Rub in cold butter until sandy crumbs form (small pea-size pieces are fine).
3. Add egg yolk and 2 Tbsp cold water; mix until dough comes together.
4. If dry, add 1 Tbsp water at a time (don’t make it wet).
5. Shape into a flat disc, wrap, and chill **30 minutes**.

---

## Step 2 — Make Custard (10 minutes)
1. Dissolve sugar in hot water (stir until clear).
2. In a bowl, lightly beat eggs (don’t whip lots of air).
3. Add evaporated milk, whole milk, vanilla, and the sugar-water. Mix gently.
4. **Strain** through a fine mesh strainer into a jug/cup (removes bubbles and bits).

---

## Step 3 — Form Tart Shells (15–20 minutes)
1. Preheat oven to **200°C** (convection 190°C).
2. Roll chilled dough to ~3 mm thickness.
3. Cut circles and press into tart tins/muffin cups.
4. Trim edges neatly (clean edges look premium).
5. Chill formed shells **10 minutes** (helps hold shape).

---

## Step 4 — Fill and Bake (18–25 minutes)
1. Place tray on oven rack.
2. Pour custard into shells to about **85–90% full**.
3. Bake at **200°C** for **10 minutes**, then reduce to **180°C** and bake **8–12 minutes** until custard is set.

**Doneness target:** edges set, center still has a slight wobble. It firms as it cools.

---

## Cooling
- Cool 10 minutes in tray, then move to a rack.
- Best eaten **slightly warm or room temperature**.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Custard:** smooth, not bubbly; gentle jiggle.
- **Crust:** crisp, not soggy.
- **Top:** lightly caramelized speckles (beautiful in photos).

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Let tarts cool before boxing to avoid condensation.
- Use a cupcake insert/tray so tarts don’t slide.
- Add label: “Best same day. Reheat 3–5 min at 160–170°C for crisp.”

---

## Storage
- Best same day.
- Room temp: up to 1 day (cool, covered).
- Refrigerate: 2–3 days (reheat to restore crust).
- Freeze baked tarts: up to 1 month; reheat from frozen.
