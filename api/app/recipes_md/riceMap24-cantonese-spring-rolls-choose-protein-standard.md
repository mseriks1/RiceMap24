# Cantonese Spring Rolls — Choose Your Protein — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Crispy dim sum–style spring rolls with a classic vegetable filling and a **protein choice** (pork, chicken, shrimp, or tofu).  
Built for RiceMap24/home kitchens: **one master filling base**, consistent portioning, and freezer-friendly prep.

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Cantonese Spring Rolls” (adapted to protein-choice format)  
https://thewoksoflife.com/homemade-spring-rolls/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Cantonese)
- **Style:** Fried spring rolls (make-ahead friendly)
- **Yield:** ~18–20 spring rolls (8" wrappers)
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 30–2 hr (includes cooling/chilling for best wrapping)
- **Skill level:** Medium (rolling + frying)

## Protein Options (Pick ONE)
Keep the **same weight** for consistent costs and portioning:

### Option A — Pork (original)
- **8 oz / 225 g** finely shredded boneless pork (butt/shoulder/loin)

### Option B — Chicken
- **8 oz / 225 g** finely shredded chicken thigh or breast

### Option C — Shrimp
- **8 oz / 225 g** shrimp, peeled and chopped small *(or roughly minced)*

### Option D — Tofu (vegetarian)
- **8 oz / 225 g** firm tofu, pressed and finely crumbled *(or minced)*

## Ingredients (18–20 rolls)

## 1) Protein marinade (original amounts)
Mix with your chosen protein:
- **1/4 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** sesame oil
- **1 tsp** Shaoxing wine
- **1/2 tsp** cornstarch
- **1/4 tsp** white pepper

**Protein notes**
- **Shrimp:** same marinade works; keep shrimp small so it cooks quickly in the filling.
- **Tofu:** use the same amounts, but reduce Shaoxing wine to **1/2 tsp** (optional) if you want a cleaner tofu flavor.

---

## 2) Vegetables + seasoning (original set)
- **2 Tbsp** oil
- **1 clove** garlic, minced
- **10** dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked until soft and thinly sliced
- **2** medium carrots, julienned (about 1 cup)
- **1 cup** bamboo shoots, julienned
- **1** small napa cabbage, julienned (about 6 cups)
- **1 Tbsp** Shaoxing wine
- **2 Tbsp** light soy sauce
- **1 tsp** sesame oil
- **1/2 tsp** salt (or to taste)
- White pepper (to taste)
- **1/4 tsp** sugar

### Thickener (slurry)
- **2 Tbsp** cornstarch dissolved in **2 Tbsp** cold water

### Optional “umami boost”
- Up to **1/4 cup** reserved shiitake soaking water (decanted, no grit)

---

## 3) Wrapping + frying
- **1 package** spring roll wrappers (8" squares; makes ~20)
- **1 Tbsp** cornstarch dissolved in **1 Tbsp** boiling water (sealing paste)
- Neutral oil for frying (canola/peanut/vegetable), **2–3 inches deep**

---

## 4) Dipping sauce (simple, old-school)
- **2 tsp** sugar
- **2 tsp** hot water
- **1 tsp** Worcestershire sauce
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce

---

## Prep (15–20 minutes)
1. **Soak mushrooms** until soft; slice thin.
2. **Cut all vegetables** to similar thin size (key for a cohesive filling).
3. **Mix protein + marinade**; rest 20–30 minutes.
4. **Mix slurry** (cornstarch + cold water).
5. Make sealing paste (cornstarch + boiling water) and cool slightly.

---

## Make the Filling (Precise Workflow)

### 1) Cook protein (2–3 minutes)
1. Heat wok over **high heat**.
2. Add **2 Tbsp oil**.
3. Add marinated protein and stir-fry until mostly cooked:
   - Pork/chicken: no visible raw color
   - Shrimp: just turning opaque/pink
   - Tofu: heated through and slightly dry

### 2) Add aromatics + veg (2 minutes)
4. Add garlic, mushrooms, carrots; stir-fry **30 seconds**.
5. Add bamboo shoots, napa cabbage, and **1 Tbsp Shaoxing wine**.
6. Stir-fry **1 minute**, then reduce heat to **medium/low** as cabbage releases moisture.

### 3) Season + reduce (3–6 minutes)
7. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, white pepper, sugar.
8. Optional: add up to **1/4 cup** mushroom soaking water for deeper flavor, then simmer longer to reduce.
9. Simmer **~3 minutes** until cabbage is fully wilted and there’s **no standing liquid**.

### 4) Thicken + chill (critical for wrapping)
10. Stir slurry and add gradually while stirring until the mixture is thick and **dry enough to hold shape**.
11. Transfer filling to a shallow bowl/tray to cool.
12. Refrigerate **at least 1 hour** (cold filling wraps better and prevents wrapper tearing).

---

## Wrap Spring Rolls (Consistency Rules)
### Portioning
- Use **2 spoonfuls** filling per roll (about 35–45 g depending on spoon).

### Wrapping steps
1. Place wrapper like a diamond (corner toward you).
2. Add filling 2 inches from the bottom corner.
3. Roll once over filling, fold both sides in, roll tightly into a cigar.
4. Seal the final corner with cornstarch sealing paste.

### Tray rule
- Place sealed rolls on a tray with a little space between them (prevents sticking).

---

## Frying (Crispy, Not Greasy)
### Oil setup
- Oil depth: **2–3 inches**
- Temperature: **325°F / 165°C**

### Fry method
1. Heat oil slowly to 325°F.
2. Fry in small batches (don’t overcrowd).
3. Roll occasionally so they brown evenly.
4. Fry until golden brown (typically **3–5 minutes** per batch, depending on oil depth and roll size).
5. Drain on a rack or paper towel.

---

## Freezer Plan (Perfect for Home Kitchens)
1. Place wrapped (unfried) rolls on a tray.
2. Freeze overnight.
3. Transfer to a zip bag.
4. Fry from frozen at 325°F (do **not** thaw). Add ~1–2 minutes to fry time.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
Spring rolls lose crispness if sealed hot.
- Use a vented container or crack lid 2–3 minutes before sealing.
- Sauce in a small cup.
- Optional: include shredded cabbage + cucumber (fresh side).

**Holding time:** Best within **45–60 minutes** for peak crisp.
