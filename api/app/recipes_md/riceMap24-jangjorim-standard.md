# Jangjorim (Korean Soy-Braised Beef with Eggs & Peppers) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A classic Korean **beef-chunks** banchan: tender beef braised in soy sauce with whole garlic, dried kelp, eggs, and shishito peppers.  
Excellent for meal prep and rice bowls; keeps well in the fridge.

**Source recipe:** Maangchi — “Braised beef with eggs & peppers in soy sauce (Jangjorim)”  
https://www.maangchi.com/recipe/jangjorim-with-eggs

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Korean  
- **Style:** Soy-braised beef chunks (banchan / rice topping)
- **Yield:** ~4–6 portions (as a side) or ~3–4 rice bowls (as a main topping)
- **Active time:** ~20 min  
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 20 min (mostly simmer)
- **Skill level:** Easy (low simmer + timing)

---

## Equipment
- Thick-bottomed pot with lid (3–4 L)
- Strainer/colander
- Tongs
- Measuring cups/spoons
- Knife + cutting board

---

## Ingredients (from source)

### Core
- **1 pound (450 g)** beef (flank steak or round)
- **4 cups** water
- **¾ cup** soy sauce
- **½ cup** garlic cloves (peeled; keep whole)
- A few strips **(4×5 cm)** dried kelp
- **2 cups** shishito peppers *(kkwarigochu)*  
- **1 Tbsp** honey
- **3 eggs**

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Cut beef:** Cut **along the grain** into **2×3 inch** chunks (about 5×7.5 cm).
2. **Soak beef:** Soak chunks in cold water (removes blood/impurities). Drain.
3. **Peel garlic:** Keep cloves whole.
4. **Hard-boil eggs:** Cook eggs, peel, and set aside.
5. **Peppers:** Rinse shishito; keep whole.

---

## Step-by-step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Boil beef until tender (50–60 minutes)
1. Bring **4 cups water** to a boil in a thick-bottomed pot.
2. Drain soaked beef and add to the boiling water.
3. Boil **50 minutes to 1 hour** until beef is tender.

> Keep the boil steady (medium to medium-high). If water drops too low, add a little hot water to keep beef mostly covered.

## 2) Build the soy-braise (7 + 10 minutes)
1. When beef is tender, **remove beef chunks** and set aside.
2. Measure out **2 cups of the beef broth** from the pot (this becomes your braise liquid).
3. Return beef to the pot and add:
   - **2 cups broth**
   - **¾ cup soy sauce**
   - **½ cup garlic cloves**
   - dried kelp strips
   - **2 cups shishito peppers**
   - **1 Tbsp honey**
   - **3 peeled eggs** (rest them on top)
4. Bring to a boil and cook **7 minutes** over medium-high heat.
5. Open lid and **turn the eggs over** (for centered yolks).
6. Cook another **10 minutes**.

## 3) Finish + cool (important for flavor)
1. Turn off heat and let everything cool in the braising liquid.
2. Remove kelp strips (optional) and discard.
3. Slice or shred beef as desired for serving.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Beef:** Tender enough to pull apart with fingers.
- **Eggs:** Firm, soy-stained outside; yolk centered.
- **Sauce:** Salty-sweet; pepper aroma noticeable; not overly watery.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl)
If selling as a rice bowl topping:
- Beef: **120–160 g**
- Egg: **1** (halved)
- Peppers: **4–6**
- Sauce: **2–3 Tbsp**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**

As a side dish (banchan):
- Beef: **60–80 g** + a little sauce

---

## Scaling for Production (10 bowls)
For 10 rice bowls, make **3×** the recipe (approximate):
- Beef: **1.35 kg**
- Water: **12 cups**
- Soy sauce: **2.25 cups**
- Garlic: **1.5 cups**
- Kelp strips: ~3×
- Shishito: **6 cups**
- Honey: **3 Tbsp**
- Eggs: **9**

**Ops tip:** This dish scales well; keep eggs in whole form and slice at service.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Beef + eggs + peppers + sauce in one container.
- **Rice separate** (best texture).
- Include note: “Salty-sweet soy braise. Add sauce to taste.”

**Holding time:** Excellent; this dish is made for meal prep.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Refrigerate up to **5–7 days** (keeps very well).
- Reheat gently (microwave low or warm in a small pot).  
  Add a splash of water if sauce is too concentrated.

---

## Allergens & Dietary Notes
- **Contains:** Soy (soy sauce), egg
- **Gluten:** Likely (soy sauce unless GF)
- **Vegetarian:** No
