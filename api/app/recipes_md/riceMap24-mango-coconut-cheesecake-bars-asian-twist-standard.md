# Mango Coconut Sticky Rice Cheesecake Bars (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

A “fusion banker” dessert: classic cheesecake bars with an Asian-inspired **mango + coconut + sticky rice** vibe.  
Not a traditional Asian dessert, but it clearly nods to Thai mango sticky rice—designed for **pickup/takeaway** as clean bars.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Baked cheesecake bars with mango-coconut swirl + optional sticky rice layer
- **Asian twist:** Mango + coconut + glutinous rice (sticky rice)
- **Yield:** 16 bars (20×20 cm pan) or 12 larger bars
- **Active time:** ~30–35 min
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 10 min + chill
- **Skill level:** Medium (cheesecake set + chilling)

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup-friendly:** bars slice clean when chilled.
- **Premium look:** bright mango swirl + coconut white contrast.
- **High-margin packs:** 2-bar / 4-bar / tray.
- **Upsells:** extra mango swirl, toasted coconut, larger bar.

---

## Equipment
- 20×20 cm (8×8) pan
- Parchment paper
- Mixing bowls
- Hand mixer/whisk
- Saucepan (sticky rice)
- Oven
- Cooling rack

---

## Ingredients

## A) Crust
- **180 g** digestive biscuits / graham crackers
- **80 g** unsalted butter, melted
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** toasted coconut flakes

## B) Sticky rice layer (optional but very “signature”)
- **120 g** glutinous rice (sweet rice)
- **200 ml** water (to cook)
- **200 ml** coconut milk
- **30–40 g** sugar
- Pinch salt

> If you want a simpler workflow, skip this layer and just do mango-coconut cheesecake bars.

---

## C) Cheesecake layer
- **600 g** cream cheese, softened
- **120 g** sugar
- **2 large eggs**
- **150 ml** coconut milk
- **1 tsp** vanilla
- Pinch salt

## D) Mango swirl
- **250–300 g** mango purée (fresh blended or canned)
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1–2 Tbsp** sugar (if mango is not sweet)
- Optional: 1 tsp cornstarch + 1 Tbsp water (if purée is very thin)

## E) Finish
- Toasted coconut flakes
- Optional: lime zest

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Cook sticky rice (optional, 15–20 min)
1. Rinse glutinous rice until water runs mostly clear.
2. Cook with 200 ml water until tender.
3. Add coconut milk + sugar + pinch salt.
4. Simmer low 5–8 minutes until thick and sticky.
5. Cool to warm (spreadable).

---

## 2) Bake crust (10 min)
1. Preheat oven to **170°C**.
2. Line pan with parchment.
3. Mix crumbs + melted butter (+ toasted coconut).
4. Press firmly into pan.
5. Bake **10 minutes**. Cool slightly.

---

## 3) Add sticky rice layer (optional)
- Spread sticky rice evenly over the baked crust (thin layer).  
Chill 10 minutes to firm up slightly.

---

## 4) Mix cheesecake (8 min)
1. Beat cream cheese + sugar until smooth.
2. Add eggs one at a time (don’t overmix).
3. Add coconut milk, vanilla, salt.

---

## 5) Prepare mango swirl (3 min)
1. Mix mango purée + lime juice + sugar.
2. If very thin, whisk in cornstarch slurry and warm briefly to thicken (optional).

---

## 6) Assemble + bake (35–45 min)
1. Pour cheesecake batter over crust (or rice layer).
2. Spoon mango purée on top in dollops.
3. Swirl with a skewer.
4. Bake at **170°C** until edges are set and center has a slight wobble: **35–45 min**.

---

## 7) Cool + chill (essential)
1. Cool at room temp 45 minutes.
2. Refrigerate **4–8 hours** (best overnight) for clean cuts.

---

## 8) Slice + finish
1. Lift out using parchment.
2. Slice into bars.
3. Sprinkle toasted coconut and lime zest.

---

## Quality Checks
- Cheesecake set and sliceable.
- Mango swirl bright and fragrant.
- Sticky rice layer (if used) chewy but not wet.

## Packaging (Pickup/Takeaway)
- Chill fully before boxing.
- Use parchment between bars.
- Label: “Keep chilled. Best within 3 days.”

---

## Storage
- Refrigerated: 3–4 days
- Freeze bars: 1 month (thaw in fridge; add coconut after thaw)

---

## Allergens
- Dairy (cream cheese, butter)
- Egg
- Wheat (cookies)
- Coconut
