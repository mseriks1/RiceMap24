# Chinese Garlic Shrimp Clay Pot (蒜香虾煲) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

An impressive Chinese-style shrimp dish with a garlicky sauce and glass noodles, traditionally served in a clay pot (but a regular pot works too).  
**Not typical “takeaway shrimp”**—it looks premium (shrimp on top of noodles, glossy sauce, scallions) and works great for home-kitchen sales.

**Source recipe (reference):** The Woks of Life — “Shrimp Clay Pot with Garlic”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/shrimp-clay-pot-chinese/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese (Cantonese-style clay pot)
- **Style:** Shrimp + glass noodles + garlic sauce
- **Yield:** 3–4 servings
- **Active time:** ~20 min
- **Total time:** ~30–35 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (fast sauce + gentle simmer)

---

## Equipment
- Clay pot (ideal) OR a small Dutch oven / heavy pot with lid
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Tongs/spatula
- Kettle/pot for soaking noodles

---

## Ingredients (Serves 3–4)

### A) Glass noodles base
- **1 bundle** mung bean glass noodles (vermicelli)
- Warm water (to soak)

### B) Shrimp
- **450 g** large shrimp/prawns, shell-on preferred (peeled is fine)
- **1 Tbsp** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
- **1/2 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** white pepper (or black)

### C) Garlic sauce
- **20–25 g** garlic (about 6–8 cloves), finely minced
- **2 Tbsp** light soy sauce
- **1/2 Tbsp** oyster sauce
- **1 tsp** sugar
- **1/2 Tbsp** sesame oil
- **120–180 ml** chicken stock (or water) *(start with 120 ml; add more if needed)*
- **1 tsp** cornstarch + **1 Tbsp** water (slurry)

### D) Aromatics + finish
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **2** scallions, sliced
- Optional garnish: cilantro, chili slices

---

## Prep (10 minutes)

### 1) Soak noodles
1. Soak glass noodles in warm water **10–12 minutes** until pliable.
2. Drain well and cut once or twice with kitchen scissors (easier serving).

### 2) Season shrimp
1. Toss shrimp with Shaoxing wine, salt, and pepper.
2. Rest 5 minutes.

### 3) Mix sauce
1. In a bowl, mix garlic, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, sesame oil, and stock.
2. Mix cornstarch slurry in a separate small cup.

---

## Cooking Method (Clay Pot Workflow)

### Step 1 — Build the noodle base
1. Heat clay pot (or heavy pot) over **medium** heat.
2. Add **1 Tbsp oil**.
3. Add drained noodles and toss briefly (30–60 seconds).
4. Pour in **about half** of the garlic sauce mixture (save the rest).
5. Spread noodles evenly as a base layer.

### Step 2 — Cook shrimp on top (gentle steam-simmer)
1. Arrange shrimp on top of noodles in a single layer.
2. Pour remaining garlic sauce over shrimp.
3. Cover and cook on **medium-low** heat **6–8 minutes** until shrimp are just cooked through.

### Step 3 — Thicken
1. Uncover and bring liquid to a stronger simmer.
2. Stir in cornstarch slurry gradually until sauce is glossy and lightly thickened.
3. Turn off heat.

### Step 4 — Finish
- Top with scallions (and optional cilantro/chili).

## Quality Checks
- **Shrimp:** just cooked (pink/opaque), not rubbery.
- **Noodles:** fully softened, coated, not watery.
- **Sauce:** glossy garlic aroma, balanced savory-sweet.

## Portioning (Per order)
- Shrimp: **120–150 g**
- Noodles: **1/3–1/4 bundle** depending on serving size
- Sauce: enough to coat and lightly pool

---

## Scaling (10 orders)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Shrimp: **~1.1–1.25 kg**
- Noodles: **2.5 bundles**
- Sauce: **2.5×**
Cook in 2–3 pots for best results.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Use a wide container to keep shrimp visible (presentation).
- Optional: garnish pack (scallions/cilantro) in a small cup for bright finish.
- If delivering: keep sauce slightly thicker so it coats and doesn’t slosh.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- Reheat gently (do not boil hard; shrimp gets tough).
- Add a splash of stock/water if noodles tighten after chilling.

---

## Allergens
- Shellfish (shrimp)
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Shellfish (oyster sauce)
- Gluten possible (sauces)
