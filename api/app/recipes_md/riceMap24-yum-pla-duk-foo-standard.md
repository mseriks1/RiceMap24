# Yum Pla Duk Foo (Crispy Fluffy Fish with Green Mango Salad) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Thai “wow” dish: **cloud-like crispy fish** paired with a bright **green mango salad**.  
It photographs incredibly well and feels premium—rarely seen as typical takeaway, but perfect as a signature special for home kitchens.

**Source recipe (reference):** Hot Thai Kitchen — Yum Pla Duk Foo  
https://hot-thai-kitchen.com/yum-pla-duk-foo/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Thai
- **Style:** Crispy fish “floss” + mango salad + tangy dressing
- **Yield:** 4 portions (as appetizer) or 2–3 (as main with rice)
- **Active time:** ~30–40 min  
- **Total time:** ~45–60 min
- **Skill level:** Medium (drying fish + high-heat frying)

---

## Why this fits RiceMap24
- **High visual impact:** fluffy golden fish + vibrant green mango + red chili.
- **Premium feel:** plated like a restaurant.
- **Upsells:** add extra mango salad, “seafood” add-on, extra dressing, sticky rice.

## Equipment
- Deep pot or wok for frying (2–3 inches oil)
- Thermometer (strongly recommended)
- Wire rack + tray (best draining)
- Mixing bowls (salad + dressing)
- Knife + cutting board
- Optional: food processor (for shredding fish; or do by hand)

---

## Ingredients (4 portions)

## A) Crispy Fish Base
- **600 g** white fish fillet (catfish is classic; use tilapia/cod/haddock if easier)
- **1 tsp** salt
- **1/2 tsp** white pepper (or black)
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce (optional; adds Thai depth)
- **1–2 Tbsp** rice flour or cornstarch (helps dryness + crispness)

### Frying
- Neutral oil for deep-frying (canola/peanut)

> Key success factors: fish must be **very dry** before frying, and oil must be **hot enough**.

---

## B) Green Mango Salad (photogenic)
- **2** firm green mangoes, julienned (or use green papaya)
- **1/2** red onion or shallots, thinly sliced
- **1/4 cup** chopped cilantro
- **1/4 cup** chopped mint (optional but great)
- **2–4** Thai chilies, sliced (adjust heat)
- **2 Tbsp** roasted peanuts, chopped (optional)

---

## C) Dressing (sweet–salty–sour)
- **3 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **3 Tbsp** lime juice
- **2 Tbsp** palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- **1 Tbsp** water (to dissolve sugar)
- Optional: **1 tsp** tamarind concentrate (adds depth)

---

## Prep Plan (Recommended order)
1) Mix dressing (dissolve sugar)  
2) Prep mango salad (keep crisp)  
3) Prep and dry fish (most important)  
4) Fry fish hot + fast  
5) Plate immediately

---

## Step-by-Step Method (Detailed)

## 1) Make dressing (5 minutes)
1. Warm 1 Tbsp water and dissolve palm sugar.
2. Mix in fish sauce and lime juice.
3. Taste: it should be bold and slightly “too strong” (it mellows on salad).

Set aside.

---

## 2) Make mango salad (10 minutes)
1. Julienne green mango (thin matchsticks).
2. Add sliced onion/shallot, chilies, herbs, and peanuts (optional).
3. Keep undressed until service if possible (crisper + prettier).

---

## 3) Prepare fish for “fluffy” crisp (10–15 minutes)
1. Pat fish very dry.
2. Season with salt, pepper, lime juice, and optional fish sauce.
3. **Cook fish first** (choose one method):
   - **Steam** 6–8 minutes until just cooked, OR
   - **Poach** gently in simmering water 6–8 minutes, OR
   - **Bake** at 180°C for ~10–12 minutes

4. Cool slightly, then **shred** fish into fine strands (the fluffier, the better).
   - Use fingers or fork; for super fluffy, pulse briefly in a food processor.

5. Spread shredded fish on a tray and **air-dry 10–15 minutes** (or use a fan).  
6. Toss lightly with 1–2 Tbsp rice flour/cornstarch for extra crisp insurance.

> The drier the fish, the more it “puffs” and stays crisp.

---

## 4) Deep-fry (the make-or-break step)
### Oil temperature
- Target **190°C / 375°F**

### Fry method
1. Heat oil to 190°C.
2. Fry fish in **small batches**:
   - Drop in a loose handful and immediately stir to separate strands.
   - Fry **30–60 seconds** until golden and airy (do not over-brown).
3. Lift out to a wire rack to drain.
4. Repeat batches until done.

> If oil temperature drops, fish will absorb oil and turn heavy. Wait for temp to recover between batches.

---

## 5) Plate (restaurant-style)
1. Mound crispy fish into a tall “nest” (height = premium look).
2. Spoon dressed mango salad beside or lightly on top (don’t soak the fish).
3. Drizzle a little extra dressing around the plate.
4. Top with extra chili slices + herbs for color.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Fish:** airy and crisp, not oily/heavy.
- **Salad:** crunchy, bright, not watery.
- **Balance:** dressing hits sweet–salty–sour with chili heat.

## Portioning (Per order)
- Crispy fish: **120–160 g** cooked fish (pre-fry weight depends on moisture)
- Mango salad: **120–150 g**
- Dressing: **20–30 ml** cup

---

## Scaling for Production (10 orders)
- Fish: **~2.5–3.0 kg** raw fillet (depends on moisture loss)
- Mango: **8–10** green mangoes
- Dressing: multiply by 2.5×–3×

**Ops tip:** Pre-cook and shred fish ahead; store chilled. Fry to order for best crisp.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended (Critical)
Crisp + salad must be separated:

- Container A (vented): crispy fish (do not seal tightly when hot)
- Container B: mango salad (dry)
- Cup C: dressing (sealed)

Label: “Add salad + dressing just before eating.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Fried fish is best fresh; it softens over time.
- If needed: re-crisp in air fryer 190°C for 2–4 minutes (works fairly well).

---

## Allergens
- Fish
- Peanuts (if used)
- Fish sauce (fish)
