# Ginger Sesame Snickerdoodles (Asian Twist, Pickup-Friendly) — RiceMap24 Dessert Standard

A familiar cookie (snickerdoodle) with an Asian-adjacent profile: **ginger + toasted sesame**.  
Not traditional Asian dessert—just a mainstream cookie with a distinct “Asian pantry” aroma. Perfect for pickup multipacks.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Cookies (snickerdoodle-style)
- **Asian twist:** ginger + toasted sesame (and optional black sesame sugar)
- **Yield:** ~24 cookies
- **Active time:** ~15–20 min
- **Total time:** ~35–45 min
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Pickup-friendly:** cookies, no melting, long shelf life.
- **Unique but safe:** still a classic cookie, just more aromatic.
- **High-margin multipacks:** 4/8/12 cookie boxes.
- **Batch-friendly:** bake trays fast.

---

## Equipment
- Mixing bowl
- Hand mixer (optional)
- Baking tray + parchment
- Cookie scoop (consistent size)
- Cooling rack

---

## Ingredients (≈24 cookies)

## A) Cookie dough
- **170 g** unsalted butter, softened
- **200 g** sugar
- **1 large egg**
- **1 tsp** vanilla
- **240 g** all-purpose flour
- **1 tsp** cream of tartar *(classic snickerdoodle tang; optional but good)*
- **1/2 tsp** baking soda
- **1/2 tsp** salt

## B) Asian twist
- **2 tsp** ground ginger (or 1 Tbsp fresh grated ginger, squeezed dry)
- **2 Tbsp** toasted sesame seeds
- Optional: **1 Tbsp** black sesame powder (adds nutty depth)

## C) Rolling sugar
- **60 g** sugar
- **1 tsp** cinnamon
- Optional: **1 tsp** ground ginger (extra punch)
- Optional: 1 Tbsp black sesame seeds mixed in

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Prep
1. Preheat oven to **175°C**.
2. Line trays with parchment.

---

## 2) Make dough (10 minutes)
1. Cream butter + sugar until fluffy.
2. Add egg + vanilla and mix.
3. Whisk dry ingredients (flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, salt, ginger).
4. Mix dry into wet until just combined.
5. Fold in toasted sesame seeds (and optional black sesame powder).

Chill dough 10 minutes if very soft.

---

## 3) Roll + bake (12–14 minutes)
1. Scoop dough into balls (25–30 g).
2. Roll in cinnamon-sugar (and optional sesame mix).
3. Bake **12–14 minutes** until edges set but centers still soft.
4. Cool 10 minutes, then move to rack.

---

## Quality Checks
- Soft center, crisp edges.
- Ginger aroma present but not overpowering.
- Sesame nutty aroma visible in specks.
