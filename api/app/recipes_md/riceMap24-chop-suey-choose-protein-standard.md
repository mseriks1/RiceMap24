# Chop Suey (Choose Your Protein) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A flexible Chinese-American stir-fry built for home kitchens: **one master sauce + “odds & ends” vegetables + your choice of protein**.  
This card uses The Woks of Life’s Chop Suey recipe (updated 4/16/2025) and adapts it to a **protein-choice format** for RiceMap24. 

**Source recipe:** The Woks of Life — “Chop Suey”  
https://thewoksoflife.com/chicken-chop-suey/ 

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Chinese-American
- **Style:** Wok stir-fry with glossy sauce
- **Yield:** 4 portions
- **Active time:** ~25 min
- **Total time:** ~35 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (high heat + quick thickening)

---

## Protein Options (Pick ONE)
The Woks of Life notes chop suey is “odds and ends” and can use chicken, tofu, beef, leftover steak, char siu, etc.   
Use these **RiceMap24-friendly** options:

### Option A — Chicken (source standard)
- **12 oz / 340 g** boneless skinless chicken breast, sliced **¼-inch / 0.6 cm** thick 

### Option B — Beef (quick version)
- **340 g** beef sirloin/flank, sliced **¼-inch / 0.6 cm** thick

### Option C — Shrimp
- **340 g** peeled shrimp (raw), large size

### Option D — Tofu (vegetarian)
- **350–400 g** firm tofu, pressed and sliced (or cubed)

> Keep the protein quantity roughly the same (~340 g) for consistent portioning and cost.

## Equipment
- Wok or large skillet
- Mixing bowls (protein + sauce)
- Tongs/spatula
- Measuring spoons/cups
- Small bowl for slurry

---

## Ingredients (Serves 4)

## 1) Protein Marinade (velvet-style)
**Source chicken marinade:**   
- **3 Tbsp** water   
- **1 Tbsp** oyster sauce  
- **1 tsp** Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)  
- **1 tsp** vegetable oil   
- **2 tsp** cornstarch   

**How to apply to other proteins**
- **Beef:** same marinade works well.
- **Shrimp:** use the same marinade but reduce water to **1 Tbsp** (shrimp releases water).
- **Tofu:** skip water; toss tofu with **1 Tbsp oyster sauce (or veg oyster sauce)** + **1 tsp oil** + **2 tsp cornstarch**.

---

## 2) Master Sauce (mix in a bowl)
**Source sauce ingredients:**   
- **1/2 cup** low sodium chicken stock   
- **1/4 tsp** sugar (white or brown)   
- **1 1/2 Tbsp** soy sauce   
- **1 tsp** dark soy sauce   
- **1 1/2 Tbsp** oyster sauce   
- **1/2 tsp** toasted sesame oil   
- **1/8 tsp** white pepper   

---

## 3) Vegetables (source set; swap freely)
**Source vegetables:**   
- **3 Tbsp** vegetable oil (for wok)   
- **2 cloves** garlic, chopped  
- **4** white button mushrooms (or baby bella), sliced   
- **1/2** small carrot, thinly sliced   
- **2/3 cup** celery, thinly sliced   
- **6 oz / 170 g** bok choy, cut into 2×5 cm pieces   
- **1 Tbsp** Shaoxing wine (deglaze)   
- **3/4 cup** mung bean sprouts   
- **1 cup** snow peas  

**Thickener (slurry)**
- **1 1/2 Tbsp** cornstarch + **2 Tbsp** water 

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Slice protein** 0.6 cm thin (or cube tofu).  
2. **Marinate protein:** Mix marinade ingredients; massage until absorbed and coated; rest **10 minutes**.  
3. **Mix sauce** in a small bowl; set beside the stove.   
4. **Prep vegetables** (slice everything; keep sprouts/snow peas separate for the end).  
5. **Mix slurry** (cornstarch + water); set aside.

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Repeatable)

### 1) Sear protein (fast; don’t overcook)
1. Heat wok over **high heat** until lightly smoking.   
2. Add **2 Tbsp** vegetable oil around the perimeter.   
3. Spread protein in a single layer:
   - Chicken/beef: sear a few seconds, then stir-fry ~15 seconds until opaque/lightly golden; remove (about 80% cooked).   
   - Shrimp: stir-fry 20–30 seconds until just turning pink; remove early.
   - Tofu: sear 60–90 seconds per side until lightly golden; remove.

### 2) Stir-fry vegetables
1. Return heat to high; add **1 Tbsp oil** and garlic.   
2. Add mushrooms + carrot + celery; stir-fry **20 seconds**.   
3. Add bok choy; stir well.   
4. Deglaze: pour **1 Tbsp Shaoxing wine** around wok perimeter.   

### 3) Sauce + finish
1. Stir sauce bowl again and pour around perimeter to deglaze further.   
2. When sauce simmers, add bean sprouts + snow peas and return protein to wok.  
3. When sauce reaches a strong simmer, drizzle in slurry while stirring until thickened to your liking.  
4. Cook **10 seconds** more to coat everything; serve immediately. 

---

## Quality Checks
- **Protein:** tender, not overcooked (remove early; finish in sauce).
- **Veg:** crisp-tender, bright.
- **Sauce:** glossy, coats everything (no watery pooling).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl)
- Stir-fry: **300–350 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Optional: extra sauce cup (small upsell)

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply everything by **2.5×**.
**Ops tip:** Pre-portion sauce into 10 small cups; slurry added per batch.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Stir-fry in leak-proof container.
- **Rice separate**.
- Note: “Reheat gently. Toss before eating.”

## Allergen Notes
- Soy (soy sauce), shellfish (oyster sauce), gluten possible (sauces). Use GF versions if needed.
