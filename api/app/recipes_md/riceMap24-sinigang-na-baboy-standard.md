# Sinigang na Baboy (Filipino Sour Pork Soup) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A Filipino comfort soup known for its signature sour broth, typically from tamarind. Packed with vegetables and pork, it’s a great “hearty soup” menu item—especially in cooler seasons.

**Source recipe:** Panlasang Pinoy — “Sinigang na Baboy (Pork Sinigang)”  
https://panlasangpinoy.com/pork-sinigang-na-baboy-recipe/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Filipino • Soup • Sour
- **Yield:** 6 portions
- **Active time:** ~15 min  
- **Total time:** ~1 hr 15 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (simmer + vegetable timing)

---

## Equipment
- Large pot (6–8 L)
- Ladle
- Knife + cutting board
- Measuring cups/spoons

---

## Ingredients (6 portions)

> **Important:** Sinigang sourness is driven by tamarind.  
> Use the linked Panlasang Pinoy recipe for **exact ingredient amounts** and the recommended souring method (tamarind mix or fresh tamarind).

### Main (per source)
- Pork (belly/ribs/shoulder), cut into chunks
- Water
- Tomatoes
- Onion

### Souring
- Tamarind base (tamarind powder/mix or fresh tamarind) per source

### Vegetables (classic set; per source)
- Radish (labanos)
- String beans
- Eggplant
- Okra
- Water spinach (kangkong) or spinach substitute
- Green chilies (optional)

### Seasoning
- Fish sauce (patis) and/or salt per source

---

## Prep (10–15 minutes)
1. **Pork:** Cut into serving chunks.
2. **Vegetables:** Wash and cut according to source instructions (timing matters).
3. **Tamarind:** Prepare tamarind mix or extract if using fresh tamarind.
4. **Aromatics:** Quarter tomatoes; slice onion.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

## 1) Simmer pork until tender
1. Add pork + water to a pot and bring to a boil.
2. Skim off foam/impurities for a cleaner broth.
3. Add onion + tomatoes.
4. Reduce to a **gentle simmer** and cook until pork is tender (time per source; commonly ~45–60 min depending on cut).

## 2) Add souring + season
5. Add tamarind base per source. Stir and simmer 3–5 minutes.
6. Season with fish sauce (or salt) to taste.

## 3) Add vegetables in correct order (don’t overcook)
7. Add harder vegetables first (e.g., radish) and simmer until slightly tender.
8. Add remaining vegetables in stages per source timing (string beans, eggplant, okra, greens last).
9. Taste and adjust sourness and salt.

## 4) Serve
10. Serve hot as soup bowls (with rice as an optional add-on).

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Broth:** Clearly sour but balanced; not flat.
- **Pork:** Tender.
- **Veg:** Cooked but not mushy (greens added last).

- Keep greens separate and add right before serving for best color.

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Soup in leak-proof container.
- Optional: rice in separate container.

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **3 days**.
- **Reheat:** Gentle simmer. Avoid boiling hard once vegetables are in.
