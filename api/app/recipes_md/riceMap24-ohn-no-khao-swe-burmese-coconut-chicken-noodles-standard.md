# Ohn No Khao Swe (Burmese Coconut Chicken Noodle Soup) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A golden Burmese comfort bowl: chicken in a gently spiced coconut broth **thickened with chickpea flour**, served over wheat noodles with a “toppings bar” (egg, lime, chili, crispy onions/noodles).  
Great for RiceMap24 as a **signature soup kit** because components pack well and customers love customizing.

**Reference recipe:** The Burmalicious — “Burmese Coconut Noodle Soup”  
https://www.theburmalicious.com/blog/burmese-coconut-noodle-soup  
(Other good references exist; this card follows the common Myanmar-style structure: coconut milk + chickpea flour thickener + toppings.)

## Ingredients (4 bowls)

## A) Chicken + aromatics
- **600–700 g** chicken thigh (boneless), cut into bite-size pieces
- **1 medium** onion, diced
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2 tsp** grated ginger
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil

## B) Spices + seasoning
- **1 tsp** turmeric powder
- **1/2 tsp** paprika (optional, boosts color)
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce (or to taste)
- **1 tsp** sugar
- Salt to taste

## C) Broth base + thickener
- **2 cups (480 ml)** chicken stock
- **1 cup (240 ml)** coconut milk
- **2 Tbsp** chickpea flour
- **1/2 cup (120 ml)** water (for slurry)

## D) Noodles
- **2 bundles** egg noodles (or wheat noodles), cooked per package
  - Substitute: ramen-style noodles or fresh egg noodles

## E) Toppings (the “wow” + sales)
Pick 4–6 to offer:

- **2–4** hard-boiled eggs, halved
- Fried garlic or garlic chips (optional)
- Crispy fried noodles (optional but very popular)
- Sliced scallions
- Cilantro
- Lime wedges
- Chili oil or chili flakes
- Fried shallots (store-bought works great)
- Optional: chickpea fritters (advanced; can be a premium add-on)

---

## Prep (10–12 minutes)
1. Dice onion; mince garlic; grate ginger.
2. Cut chicken into bite-size pieces.
3. Whisk chickpea flour with 1/2 cup water until smooth (no lumps).
4. Cook noodles and drain well (toss with a tiny bit of oil so they don’t stick).
5. Prep toppings (eggs, lime wedges, scallions, cilantro, fried shallots).

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable)

### 1) Build the flavor base (5 minutes)
1. Heat **2 Tbsp oil** in a pot over **medium** heat.
2. Add onion and cook 2–3 minutes until softened.
3. Add garlic + ginger; cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
4. Stir in turmeric (and paprika if using) for 15 seconds (bloom spices).

### 2) Cook chicken (5 minutes)
1. Add chicken pieces and stir to coat in the aromatics.
2. Cook 3–5 minutes until the outside turns opaque.

### 3) Add stock + simmer (10 minutes)
1. Add chicken stock.
2. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook 8–10 minutes (until chicken is cooked through).

### 4) Thicken with chickpea slurry (2–4 minutes)
1. Turn heat to medium-low.
2. Whisk the chickpea slurry again (it settles) and pour in slowly while whisking.
3. Simmer 2–3 minutes until broth thickens slightly.

> If it thickens too much, add a splash of stock/water.  
> If it’s too thin, simmer a few minutes longer.

### 5) Add coconut milk + finish (3 minutes)
1. Stir in coconut milk.
2. Add fish sauce + sugar.
3. Heat gently 2–3 minutes (avoid hard boiling once coconut is in).

Taste and adjust:
- Need more depth → a little more fish sauce
- Too salty → add a splash of water or coconut milk
- Needs brightness → lime at service (don’t cook lime in)

---

## Optional: Crispy noodle topping (quick)
1. Take a small handful of cooked noodles.
2. Fry in a shallow pan with oil over medium-high until golden and crisp.
3. Drain well.

Store-bought fried shallots + a few crispy noodles makes the bowl look “restaurant”.

---

## Assemble (RiceMap24 Bowl Set)
Per bowl:
1. Add noodles to bowl (or pack separately for delivery).
2. Ladle hot coconut chicken broth over noodles.
3. Top with:
   - 1/2 egg
   - scallions + cilantro
   - fried shallots / crispy noodles
4. Serve with lime wedge and chili oil/chili flakes.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Broth:** golden, creamy, lightly thick (should coat a spoon slightly).
- **Chicken:** tender, not shredded.
- **Balance:** savory + coconut richness + lime brightness at the end.
- **Toppings:** crunchy element present (fried shallots/noodles).

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl targets)
- Noodles: 1/2 bundle (or ~70–90 g cooked)
- Broth + chicken: **350–450 ml**
- Chicken: **120–160 g**
- Toppings: small but colorful (they sell the photo)

---

## Scaling (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Chicken: 1.5–1.75 kg
- Stock: 5 cups (1.2 L)
- Coconut milk: 2.5 cups (600 ml)
- Chickpea flour: 5 Tbsp
- Noodles: 5 bundles
- Toppings: 10 eggs + extra lime/scallions/shallots

**Ops tip:** Make a big pot of broth. Cook noodles close to service, or pack noodles separately.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
To keep noodles perfect:
- Container A: broth + chicken (hot, sealed)
- Container B: noodles (separate)
- Cup C: toppings (shallots, herbs)
- Cup D: chili oil (optional)
- Lime wedge in a small bag/cup

Label: “Pour broth over noodles. Add toppings and squeeze lime.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Broth keeps 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat gently (avoid hard boil).
- Noodles best day-of.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Coconut
- Gluten (wheat noodles)
- Egg (topping)
