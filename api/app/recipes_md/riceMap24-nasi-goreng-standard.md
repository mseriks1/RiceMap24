# Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Indonesia’s iconic fried rice: deeply savory, slightly sweet, smoky, and fast to cook. Excellent as a high-margin bowl with optional egg and chicken/shrimp add-ons.

**Source recipe:** Rasa Malaysia — “Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice)”  
https://rasamalaysia.com/nasi-goreng-indonesian-fried-rice/

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Indonesian • Fried Rice • Quick Wok
- **Yield:** 2 portions *(scale easily)*
- **Active time:** ~10 min  
- **Total time:** ~15 min
- **Skill level:** Easy (best with cold rice)

---

## Equipment
- Wok or large skillet
- Spatula
- Small bowl (for sauce)
- Measuring spoons/cups

---

## Ingredients (2 portions)

### Core
- **2 cups** cooked rice *(preferably cold/day-old)*
- **2 Tbsp** vegetable oil
- **3 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2** shallots, minced
- **2** eggs *(optional but classic)*
- **2 Tbsp** sweet soy sauce (**kecap manis**)  
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** fish sauce *(optional in some versions; follow source)*
- **1/4 tsp** white pepper *(or to taste)*
- **1/2 tsp** sugar *(if needed; depends on how sweet your kecap manis is)*

### Optional protein / add-ins (operator choice)
- Cooked shredded chicken, shrimp, or diced leftovers (add during frying)
- Sliced cucumber + tomato (classic side)
- Fried shallots (garnish)

*(Ingredient list is based on the source recipe linked above; keep the sauce profile anchored to kecap manis.)*

---

## Prep (5–10 minutes)
1. **Rice:** Break up clumps with your hands or a fork. Cold rice gives best texture.
2. **Aromatics:** Mince garlic + shallots.
3. **Sauce bowl:** Mix kecap manis + soy sauce (+ fish sauce if using) and set aside.
4. **Eggs:** Lightly beat eggs if scrambling.

---

## Method (Precise, Easy-to-Follow)

### 1) Aromatics
1. Heat wok/skillet over **medium-high heat**.
2. Add **2 Tbsp oil**.
3. Add minced shallots + garlic; stir-fry **30–60 seconds** until fragrant (don’t burn).

### 2) Eggs (optional)
4. Push aromatics to one side. Add eggs to the empty side.
5. Scramble until just set, then mix through.

### 3) Fry the rice
6. Add rice. Increase heat to **high** if your stove can handle it.
7. Stir-fry **2–3 minutes**, pressing and tossing until grains separate and steam evaporates.

### 4) Season + glaze
8. Pour in the sauce mixture. Toss rapidly until rice is evenly coated, **60–90 seconds**.
9. Season with white pepper; taste and adjust:
   - **More sweet/deep:** +1 tsp kecap manis
   - **More salty:** +1 tsp soy sauce
   - **More umami:** + a few drops fish sauce (optional)

### 5) Serve
10. Portion into bowls. Optional: top with fried egg, cucumber/tomato, fried shallots.

---

## Quality Checks (Before Serving)
- **Texture:** Dry-fried, separated grains (not wet/mushy).
- **Color:** Even brown sheen from kecap manis (not patchy).
- **Aroma:** Sweet-savory, slightly smoky.

---

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Base is 2 portions. Multiply by **5×**.

### Ingredients (10 portions)
- Cooked rice: **10 cups**
- Vegetable oil: **10 Tbsp** (≈ 150 ml)
- Garlic: **15 cloves**
- Shallots: **10**
- Eggs: **10** (optional)
- Kecap manis: **10 Tbsp** (≈ 150 ml)
- Soy sauce: **5 Tbsp** (≈ 75 ml)
- Fish sauce: **5 tsp** (optional)
- White pepper: **1 1/4 tsp** (or to taste)

### Batch notes
- Cook in **2 batches** (5 portions each) to keep heat high and avoid steaming.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Single-compartment bowl works well.
- If adding a fried egg: pack egg on top or in a side compartment to preserve texture.

**Holding time:** Best within **2 hours**.

---

## Storage & Reheat
- **Refrigerate:** Up to **2 days**.
- **Reheat:** Microwave (medium-high) or quick hot-pan toss. Add a teaspoon of water only if very dry.

---

## Allergen & Dietary Notes (Operator Fill)
- **Contains:** Egg (if used), soy (soy sauce, kecap manis)
- **May contain:** Fish (fish sauce)
- **Gluten:** Likely (soy sauces unless GF)

## Internal Notes (For Your Kitchen)
- Kecap manis is the signature—don’t replace with regular soy + sugar unless you test.
- Cold rice is non-negotiable for the best “fried” texture.
