# Thai Satay Chicken Wrap (Peanut-Lime + Crunch Slaw) — RiceMap24 Standard Wrap Recipe

A premium wrap with a Thai satay vibe: juicy chicken, creamy peanut-lime sauce, and crunchy slaw.  
Fusion (not strictly traditional), optimized for pickup/delivery by offering sauce in a cup.

---

## Summary
- **Style:** Wrap (Asian-inspired)
- **Cuisine vibe:** Thai satay profile
- **Yield:** 4 large wraps
- **Active time:** ~20 minutes
- **Total time:** ~25–30 minutes
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Why this works for RiceMap24
- **Peanut sauce = repeat orders** (rich, addictive).
- **High-margin sauce** and easy upsell cups.
- **Crunchy slaw** keeps it feeling fresh.
- **Upsells:** extra chicken, extra peanut sauce, spicy option.

---

## Ingredients (4 wraps)

## A) Chicken
- **700 g** boneless chicken thighs (preferred), sliced strips
- Salt + pepper
- **1 Tbsp** neutral oil

### Quick satay-style seasoning
- **1 tsp** curry powder
- **1 tsp** brown sugar
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** grated ginger
- **1 clove** garlic, grated
- Optional: 1/2 tsp turmeric (color)

---

## B) Peanut-lime sauce (the identity)
- **120 g** peanut butter
- **2 Tbsp** hoisin sauce *(or 1 Tbsp soy + 1 Tbsp sugar if no hoisin)*
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1 Tbsp** brown sugar (adjust)
- **1 tsp** grated garlic
- **150–200 ml** warm water (to loosen)
- Optional: **1–2 tsp** sriracha or chili flakes

**Target texture:** thick-drizzle / spreadable (not watery).

---

## C) Crunch slaw
- **250 g** shredded cabbage
- **1 carrot**, julienne
- **1 Tbsp** rice vinegar
- **1 tsp** sugar
- Pinch salt
- Optional: chopped cilantro

---

## D) Wrap build
- **4 large** tortillas/wraps
- Lettuce leaves (recommended moisture barrier)
- Cucumber sticks (optional)
- Crushed peanuts (premium finish)
- Cilantro (optional)

---

## Equipment
- Skillet
- Mixing bowls
- Knife + cutting board
- Sauce cups (recommended)

---

## Step-by-Step Method

## 1) Make slaw (5 minutes)
- Toss cabbage + carrot + vinegar + sugar + salt (+ cilantro).

Keep it light—no heavy dressing.

---

## 2) Make peanut sauce (5 minutes)
1. Mix peanut butter, hoisin, soy, lime, sugar, garlic.
2. Add warm water gradually until smooth and spread/drizzle thickness.
3. Add chili if using.

---

## 3) Cook chicken (8–10 minutes)
1. Toss chicken with curry powder, sugar, soy, ginger, garlic.
2. Sear in hot skillet with oil until browned and cooked through.

---

## 4) Assemble wraps
Per wrap:
1. Warm tortilla briefly.
2. Add lettuce barrier.
3. Spread **1 Tbsp** peanut sauce (or keep sauce in cup for pickup).
4. Add chicken (about **160–190 g cooked**).
5. Add slaw + optional cucumber.
6. Finish with crushed peanuts + cilantro.

Roll tight.

---

## Portioning (Per wrap)
- Chicken: **160–200 g cooked**
- Slaw: **60–90 g**
- Peanut sauce: **30–40 g** (or sauce cup)

---

## Batch for 10 wraps
- Chicken: **1.8 kg**
- Peanut sauce: 2.5×
- Slaw: 600 g cabbage + 4 carrots

---

## Packaging (Pickup/Delivery)
Best practice:
- Wrap A: tortilla + lettuce + chicken (rolled)
- Cup B: peanut sauce (customer drizzles/dips)
- Cup C: slaw (optional separate for extra crunch)

**Label:** “Add sauce and slaw last (optional).”

---

## Storage
- Peanut sauce: 3–5 days refrigerated
- Cooked chicken: 2 days refrigerated
- Slaw best same day

---

## Allergens
- Peanuts
- Soy (soy sauce)
- Wheat (tortilla)
- Gluten possible (hoisin/soy brands)
