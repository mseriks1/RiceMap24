# Đồ Chua (Vietnamese Pickled Carrot & Daikon) — RiceMap24 Side Order Standard

A crisp, sweet-tangy Vietnamese pickle that instantly upgrades bowls, grilled meats, noodles, and bánh mì-style kits.  
Perfect “mer-salg” because it’s cheap to produce, looks fresh, and keeps well in the fridge.

**Reference recipe:** Serious Eats — “Đồ Chua (Vietnamese Pickled Daikon and Carrots for Bánh Mì)”  
https://www.seriouseats.com/pickled-daikon-and-carrot-do-chua

---

## Summary
- **Category:** Vietnamese • Side order / add-on / topping
- **Yield:** ~1 large jar (about 800–1000 g pickles, depending on cut)
- **Active time:** ~15 min
- **Total time:** 30 min quick-pickle (best after 4–12 hours)
- **Skill level:** Easy

---

## Best Ways to Sell It (RiceMap24)
- **Add-on cup (small):** 60–80 g (great with rice bowls)
- **Side order (standard):** 150–180 g
- **Family add-on:** 350–450 g
- **Topping pack:** “Add crunch” (included with grilled meats / roll kits)

---

## Equipment
- Cutting board + knife or mandoline
- Large bowl
- Colander (optional)
- Jar/container with lid

---

## Ingredients

### Vegetables
- **450 g** daikon, peeled and cut into matchsticks (or thin batons)
- **450 g** carrots, peeled and cut into matchsticks (or thin batons)

> A 50/50 mix is common. You can do all carrot if customers prefer milder flavor.

### Quick-pickle brine (classic profile)
- **240 ml** warm water
- **240 ml** white vinegar or rice vinegar
- **60–80 g** sugar (adjust sweetness)
- **8–10 g** salt (about 1½ tsp)

**Optional (premium aroma)**
- 1–2 star anise (very subtle, optional)

---

## Prep (10 minutes)
1. Cut daikon and carrots into even matchsticks/batons.
2. Keep cuts consistent so the jar looks professional (good for photos).

---

## Method (Repeatable)

### 1) “Massage” for quick crunch (optional but recommended)
1. Put carrots + daikon in a bowl.
2. Sprinkle with **1 tsp salt** and **1 tsp sugar** (taken from your totals).
3. Massage 1–2 minutes until vegetables soften slightly and release liquid.
4. Drain liquid (don’t rinse unless you oversalt).

> This step speeds pickling and gives that classic “snappy” texture.

### 2) Make brine
1. Whisk warm water + vinegar + remaining sugar + remaining salt until dissolved.
2. Taste: should be sweet-tangy and a touch salty (it mellows after soaking).

### 3) Pickle
1. Pack vegetables into a jar/container.
2. Pour brine over until fully submerged.
3. Refrigerate:
   - **Quick-pickle:** 30 minutes (works)
   - **Best:** 4–12 hours (more flavor)
   - **Peak:** day 2–4

---

## Quality Checks
- **Texture:** crisp, not limp.
- **Flavor:** sweet–tangy, not overly salty.
- **Look:** bright orange + white, clean brine (not cloudy from too much pulp).

---

## Portioning
- Add-on cup: 60–80 g
- Side: 150–180 g
- Family: 350–450 g

---

## Scaling (10 side orders)
If 1 side = 170 g, 10 sides ≈ 1.7 kg pickles.

A practical batch:
- Daikon: 1 kg
- Carrot: 1 kg
- Brine: 1 liter total (500 ml water + 500 ml vinegar)
- Sugar: 250–300 g (to taste)
- Salt: 30–40 g

**Ops tip:** Keep brine in a pitcher and pour as needed.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
- Cup A: pickles (drained lightly so they don’t slosh)
- Optional: small “extra brine” cup if the customer wants it punchier
- Label: “Crispy pickles. Best chilled.”

---

## Storage & Food Safety
- Refrigerate.
- Best within **7–10 days** for top crunch.
- Discard if it smells off or brine turns slimy.

---

## Allergens
- None (unless using special vinegar additives)
