# Cambodian Pepper Beef (Lok Lak) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

A fast, high-impact Cambodian stir-fry: **seared beef cubes** coated in a glossy peppery sauce, served with a lime–salt–pepper dipping sauce, salad, and rice.  
This card keeps Marion’s Kitchen’s exact ingredient list and adds **home-kitchen repeatability** (batching, doneness timing, packaging).

**Source recipe:** Marion’s Kitchen — “Cambodian Pepper Beef (Lok Lak)”

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Cambodian
- **Style:** High-heat stir-fry (beef cubes)
- **Yield:** **4 portions**
- **Active time:** ~15 min  
- **Total time:** ~25 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (high heat sear timing)

## Equipment

- Mixing bowls (marinade + sauces)
- Tongs or spatula

## Ingredients (Serves 4) — from source

### Main
- **500 g (1 lb)** sirloin/striploin steak, cut into roughly **3 cm / 1½ inch** cubes
- **1 Tbsp** vegetable oil
- **1** red onion, cut into wedges
- **1** tomato, sliced
- Lettuce leaves, to serve
- Steamed rice, to serve

### Marinade
- **1 Tbsp** oyster sauce
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **2 tsp** sweet dark soy sauce
- **1 tsp** ground Kampot pepper *(or half amount freshly ground black pepper)*
- **1 tsp** cornflour/cornstarch
- **½ tsp** baking soda
- **2** garlic cloves, finely grated

### Stir-fry sauce
- **2 Tbsp** oyster sauce
- **1 Tbsp** soy sauce
- **1 tsp** sugar

### Dipping sauce
- **3 Tbsp** lime juice
- **1 tsp** ground Kampot pepper *(or half amount black pepper)*
- **1 tsp** sea salt

---

## Prep (10 minutes)

### 1) Cut beef correctly
- Cut into **3 cm** cubes.
- Pat dry with paper towel (dry beef sears better).

### 2) Mix marinade (2 minutes)
1. Add beef to a bowl.
2. Add all **marinade** ingredients.
3. Mix thoroughly until cubes are evenly coated.
4. Rest **10 minutes** (or up to 30 minutes refrigerated).

> Baking soda + cornstarch help tenderness and “restaurant” sear.

### 3) Mix sauces (2 minutes)
- Stir-fry sauce: combine and set aside.
- Dipping sauce: combine and set aside.

### 4) Salad base
- Arrange lettuce leaves and tomato slices on serving plates/containers.

---

## Cooking Method (Precise, Repeatable)

> **Heat is everything:** You want a hot wok and fast searing. Crowding the pan will steam the beef.

### 1) Preheat
1. Heat wok/pan over **high heat** until very hot (light smoke).
2. Add **1 Tbsp vegetable oil** and swirl.

### 2) Sear beef in “shake” cycles (2 minutes total)
1. Add the marinated beef and spread into a single layer.
2. **Sear 30 seconds**, then toss.
3. Repeat **3 more times** (total ~2 minutes), until beef is starting to char.

**Doneness guide**
- Medium (recommended): pull after 4 shake cycles + sauce toss (below).
- Well done: cook ~30–60 seconds longer after adding sauce.

### 3) Add onion + sauce (60–90 seconds)
1. Add onion wedges and toss **a few seconds**.
2. Add the **stir-fry sauce** and toss **30 seconds**.
3. Turn heat off and rest beef in the pan **1 minute** (juicier beef).

---

## Serve
- Place beef over lettuce + tomato.
- Serve with rice and the lime–salt–pepper dipping sauce.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Beef:** Charred edges, juicy inside.
- **Sauce:** Glossy, coats cubes (not watery).
- **Onion:** Slightly softened but still crisp.

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl)

- Beef: **120–150 g**
- Rice: **250 g cooked**
- Salad base: lettuce + 2–3 tomato slices
- Dipping sauce: **1–2 Tbsp** in a small cup

## Scaling for Production (10 portions)
Multiply by **2.5×**.

### Quick scale targets (10 portions)
- Beef: **1.25 kg**
- Vegetable oil: **2.5 Tbsp**
- Onion: **2–3**
- Marinade + sauces: **2.5×** each item

**Ops tip:** Cook in **small batches** (2–3 portions per batch) to keep sear quality.

## Storage & Reheat
- Best fresh.
- If needed: refrigerate cooked beef up to 1 day and reheat quickly in a hot pan (microwave softens sear).
