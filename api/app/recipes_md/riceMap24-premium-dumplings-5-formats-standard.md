# Premium Dumplings (5 Sellable Formats) — RiceMap24 Home Kitchen Standard

Below are **premium dumpling formats** that work well for **pickup/delivery** and home-kitchen production.  
They’re designed for clean portioning (**6/10/12/20-pack**), strong “repeat-order” appeal, and smart packaging (steamed vs pan-fried vs boiled).

## How to sell dumplings on RiceMap24 (best formats)
### A) **Steamed** (most delivery-stable)
- Holds texture well
- No oil mess
- Best for “premium box” presentation

### B) **Pan-fried** (highest perceived value, but crisp can soften)
- Great “restaurant feel”
- For best results: **vent container** + include a note: “re-crisp 2 min in pan/air fryer”

### C) **Boiled** (fastest to cook in batches; sauce does heavy lifting)
- Great for big orders
- Add strong sauce + chili oil as upgrade

# 1) Premium Chinese Jiaozi — Pork + Chinese Chive (Banker)

## Why it’s premium
- The “classic” that people reorder
- Strong aroma (chive + sesame)
- Great boiled or pan-fried

## Yield
- ~40–50 dumplings (depends on wrapper size)

## Filling (high-level, consistent)
- Ground pork
- Chinese chives (or scallion + napa if chives unavailable)
- Soy sauce + sesame oil + egg + salt/pepper
- Optional: a splash of water to keep filling juicy

## Cooking options
- **Boil:** until dumplings float + skins look slightly translucent
- **Pan-fry:** sear bottoms → add water → steam covered → finish uncovered

## Packaging
- 10 or 20 dumplings per box
- 1–2 sauce cups (see sauce section)

**Upsell:** “Chili oil + black vinegar” combo cup.

---

# 2) Premium Japanese Gyoza — Pork + Cabbage (Potstickers)

## Why it’s premium
- Crispy bottom + juicy top
- Familiar Japanese “restaurant” item
- Great photo: golden lattice bottom

## Yield
- ~30–40 gyoza (depending on wrapper)

## Filling profile (authentic style)
- Ground pork
- Cabbage (squeezed dry; this is critical)
- Garlic + ginger
- Soy sauce + sesame oil + sake (optional)

## Cooking method (pan-fry/steam)
1. Sear in oil until bottoms are golden.
2. Add water, cover to steam.
3. Remove lid and crisp again.

## Packaging
- **Vent 45–60 seconds** before closing lid (steam kills crisp).
- Add **dipping sauce cup**.

**Upsell:** “Miso dipping sauce” (Japanese twist).

---

# 3) Premium Siu Mai (Shumai) — Pork + Shrimp (Steamed)

## Why it’s premium
- Looks like dim sum (instant premium signal)
- Steaming travels well
- Great for party boxes

## Yield
- ~20–24 pieces (wonton wrappers)

## Filling profile (classic)
- Ground pork + chopped shrimp
- A little pork fat (optional but makes it juicy)
- Ginger + sesame oil + Shaoxing wine (optional)
- Cornstarch as binder

## Cooking
- Steam until cooked through, serve hot.

## Packaging
- Use a **rigid tray** so tops stay pretty.
- Add 1–2 sauce cups:
  - soy-vinegar
  - chili oil
  - or “ginger soy” cup

**Upsell:** tobiko/sesame topping (tiny amount) for a “dim sum premium” look.

---

# 4) Premium “Dumpling Platter” (Mixed Box)

## Why it’s premium
- Gives customers variety
- Higher ticket price
- Perfect for couples/family

## Box formats
- **12-piece sampler:** 4 jiaozi + 4 gyoza + 4 siu mai
- **24-piece party box:** 8 + 8 + 8

## Sauce strategy (sell 2 cups)
- Cup A: soy-vinegar-garlic
- Cup B: chili oil / chili crisp
Optional: peanut sauce if you already offer it (works with gyoza surprisingly well)

---

# 5) Premium “Cook-at-Home Frozen Dumplings” (Best Margin)

## Why it’s premium
- Best quality control (customers cook fresh)
- Great for repeat subscriptions
- Minimizes delivery texture issues

## How to freeze correctly (important)
1. Place raw dumplings on a tray lined with parchment (not touching).
2. Freeze 1–2 hours until solid.
3. Bag them and label with cooking instructions.

## Cooking from frozen (simple)
- **Boil:** add frozen dumplings to boiling water; cook until they float + 2–3 min more.
- **Pan-fry:** sear bottoms, add water, steam covered, then crisp.

## Packaging
- 20 or 40 dumplings per bag
- Include sauce as optional add-on (bottled sauce sells well)

# Premium Dumpling Sauces (Sell 1–2 cups per order)

## 1) Classic soy-vinegar-garlic (fast)
Use: soy sauce + rice vinegar + minced garlic + sesame + chili oil + tiny sugar (Woks of Life style).

## 2) Sichuan-style (spicy)
Use: black vinegar + soy + chili oil + garlic (Red House Spice style).

## 3) Miso dipping sauce (Japanese)
Mix: miso + soy + rice vinegar + a little honey + sesame (small cup).

**Sauce sizing**
- Standard: 30–40 ml cup
- Premium: 60 ml cup

# Home Kitchen Ops (Quality = sales)
- Keep filling **dry-ish** (especially cabbage) or wrappers get soggy.
- If pan-frying for pickup: **vent** containers briefly before sealing.
- For steamed dumplings: rigid tray keeps shape and looks premium.
- Pre-portion sauce cups for speed.
