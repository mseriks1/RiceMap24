# Bánh Khọt (Vietnamese Mini Savory Coconut-Turmeric Pancakes) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Crispy-edged mini pancakes cooked in a special pan, topped with shrimp (or other options), finished with scallion oil, and served with lettuce/herb wraps + nuoc cham.  
**Why it’s great for RiceMap24:** it looks *amazing* (golden pancakes + green scallion oil + herbs), and it sells well as a **kit** (pancakes separate from herbs/sauce).

**Reference recipe:** Hungry Huy — Bánh Khọt  
https://www.hungryhuy.com/banh-khot/

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Vietnamese (Southern-style popular in Vũng Tàu)
- **Style:** Mini savory pancakes + wrap & dip set
- **Yield:** ~24–30 mini pancakes (aebleskiver/takoyaki pan)
- **Active time:** ~35–45 min  
- **Total time:** ~60–75 min (includes batter rest)
- **Skill level:** Medium (pan technique for crisp edges)

---

## Equipment
- Bánh khọt pan / aebleskiver pan / takoyaki pan (must have “cups”)
- Mixing bowl + whisk
- Small saucepan (scallion oil)
- Small bowl (nuoc cham)
- Ladle or squeeze bottle (batter)
- Brush (oil)
- Tongs/spoon/spatula

---

## Ingredients

## A) Batter (golden + crisp)
- **250 g** rice flour
- **30 g** cornstarch
- **4 g** turmeric powder (about 1 tsp)
- **6 g** salt (about 1 tsp)
- **400 ml** coconut milk (full fat)
- **450 ml** water (or sparkling water for extra lightness)
- **2** scallions, finely sliced (stir into batter)
- Optional (helps crisp): **1 Tbsp** neutral oil mixed into batter

> Rest the batter 30–60 minutes for better hydration and texture.

---

## B) Topping — choose your protein (keep it small)
Pick ONE topping option for a standard batch:

### Option A — Shrimp (classic)
- **350 g** shrimp, peeled and deveined
- **2** garlic cloves, minced
- **1 Tbsp** fish sauce
- **1/2 tsp** black pepper

### Option B — Shrimp + pork (party style)
- **250 g** shrimp + **200 g** minced pork (lightly seasoned)

### Option C — Vegetarian
- **300–400 g** mushrooms (finely chopped and sautéed) OR firm tofu (small cubes)

---

## C) Scallion Oil (for shine + aroma)
- **80 ml** neutral oil
- **1/2 cup** scallions, finely chopped
- **1/2 tsp** salt

Warm oil, pour over scallions + salt, stir, set aside.

---

## D) Nuoc Cham (dipping sauce)
- **90 ml** fish sauce
- **90 g** sugar (about 6 Tbsp)
- **180 ml** water
- **60 ml** lime juice (about 3–4 limes)
- **2** garlic cloves, minced
- **1–2** chilies, sliced

Stir until sugar dissolves. Taste and adjust (more lime for sour, more sugar for sweet).

---

## E) Wrap platter (photogenic and essential)
- Lettuce leaves (romaine/butter lettuce)
- Herbs: mint + cilantro (and Thai basil if available)
- Cucumber slices (optional)
- Pickled carrot/daikon (optional but very “complete”)

---

## Prep (15–20 min)
1. **Mix batter** (whisk smooth). Rest 30–60 min.
2. **Prep topping**:
   - Shrimp: chop into small pieces (5–7 mm) or keep small whole shrimp.
   - Season topping with garlic, fish sauce, pepper.
3. **Make scallion oil** and set aside.
4. **Make nuoc cham** and portion into cups.
5. Wash and dry lettuce/herbs thoroughly.

---

## Cooking Method (Crispy Bánh Khọt Workflow)

### Pan setup
- Heat pan over **medium to medium-high** until hot.
- Oil each cup generously (this is how you get crisp edges).

### 1) Fill cups
1. Stir batter (starch settles).
2. Pour batter into each cup about **3/4 full**.
3. Add a small amount of topping (e.g., 1–2 shrimp pieces per cup).

### 2) Steam-set, then crisp
1. Cover pan for **1–2 minutes** to set the top.
2. Uncover and continue cooking until edges are crisp and golden:
   - Total per batch often **4–6 minutes**, depending on pan heat.
3. If the tops look pale, spoon a tiny amount of hot oil around edges (old-school trick).

### 3) Finish
1. Lift pancakes out.
2. Brush or spoon a little scallion oil on top for shine and aroma.

Repeat until batter is used, oiling cups between batches as needed.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Edges:** crisp and browned, not soft.
- **Center:** cooked through but still tender (not dry).
- **Color:** golden yellow from turmeric.
- **Topping:** cooked, not rubbery (shrimp pieces small helps).

---

## RiceMap24 Serving Formats

### Format A — “Bánh Khọt Kit” (best for delivery)
- Container A: pancakes (vented or cracked lid 2–3 min to protect crisp)
- Cup B: nuoc cham
- Container C: lettuce + herb pack (dry, paper towel inside)
- Optional: pickles + cucumber

### Format B — Premium Platter (pickup / dine-now)
- Pancakes on a rack + herb board + sauce cup (very photogenic)

---

## Portioning (per order)
- **Meal kit:** 10–12 mini pancakes + herb pack + sauce
- **Snack kit:** 6–8 pancakes + sauce
- Nuoc cham: **30–40 ml** per meal kit

---

## Scaling (10 meal kits)
Assume 1 kit = 12 pancakes → 120 pancakes total.
- Multiply batter by **4×** (approx.) and cook in multiple batches.
- Pre-portion toppings and sauce cups for speed.

---

## Packaging Notes (To-Go)
- Crispness is the challenge: **vented container** and **dry packing** for herbs.
- Label: “Wrap pancake in lettuce + herbs. Dip in nuoc cham.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Best same day.
- Re-crisp pancakes in a hot dry pan 30–60 seconds per side (or air fryer 190°C for 2–4 minutes).

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish sauce)
- Shellfish (if shrimp)
