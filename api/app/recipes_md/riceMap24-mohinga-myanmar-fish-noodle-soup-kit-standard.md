# Mohinga (Myanmar Fish Noodle Soup) — RiceMap24 Standard Recipe Card

Myanmar’s national dish: rice noodles in a fragrant fish broth with lemongrass, turmeric, and a gentle thickener (toasted rice or chickpea flour).  
For RiceMap24, this is ideal as a **“soup kit”**: broth + noodles + toppings packed separately for best texture and presentation.

**Reference recipe:** LinsFood — Mohinga (last updated Aug 28, 2025)  
https://www.linsfood.com/mohinga/  

---

## Summary
- **Cuisine:** Burmese / Myanmar
- **Style:** Fish noodle soup + toppings bar
- **Yield:** 4 meal bowls
- **Active time:** ~25 min
- **Total time:** ~55–70 min
- **Skill level:** Easy–Medium (broth building + thickener + fish handling)

## Equipment
- Medium/large pot (4–6 L)
- Small skillet (toasting rice flour) OR buy rice flour and toast it
- Knife + cutting board
- Fine strainer (optional)
- Whisk (for thickener)
- Ladle
- Sauce cups for toppings

---

## Ingredients (4 bowls)

## A) Fish + broth base
- **600–700 g** firm white fish (catfish/tilapia/cod/haddock), cut into big chunks
- **1.2 L** water or light fish/chicken stock
- **2 stalks** lemongrass (white part only), thinly sliced or bruised
- **1 medium** onion, thinly sliced
- **5–6 cloves** garlic, minced
- **2 tsp** grated ginger
- **1 tsp** turmeric powder
- **2 Tbsp** neutral oil
- **2 Tbsp** fish sauce (to taste)
- **1–2 tsp** sugar (balance)
- Salt + black pepper to taste

## B) Thickener (choose ONE)
### Option 1 — Toasted rice powder (very traditional)
- **3 Tbsp** uncooked white rice (toast and grind)  
  OR **3 Tbsp** rice flour toasted in a dry pan

### Option 2 — Chickpea flour (easy and common)
- **3 Tbsp** chickpea flour + **120 ml** water (slurry)

> Use one thickener. Don’t over-thicken; Mohinga should be lightly creamy, not porridge.

## C) Noodles
- **300–350 g** rice vermicelli (thin rice noodles)

## D) Toppings (the “wow” + sales)
Pick 5–7:
- **4 eggs**, hard-boiled (halved)
- Fried shallots (store-bought is perfect)
- Chopped cilantro
- Sliced scallions
- Lime wedges
- Chili oil or chili flakes
- Crunch: crispy noodles or crackers (optional)

---

## Prep (10–12 minutes)
1. Slice onion, mince garlic, grate ginger.
2. Prep lemongrass (slice thin or bruise and knot).
3. Choose thickener method:
   - Toast rice or rice flour (see below).
4. Cook eggs; prep toppings.
5. Soak/cook noodles close to service (best texture).

---

## Thickener: Toasted Rice Powder (5 minutes)
### If using whole rice
1. Dry-toast **3 Tbsp rice** in a skillet over medium-low until light golden.
2. Cool, then grind to a fine powder (spice grinder or mortar).

### If using rice flour
1. Dry-toast **3 Tbsp rice flour** 2–3 minutes until lightly nutty.
2. Cool before adding to soup.

---

## Cooking Method (Repeatable)

### Step 1 — Build aromatics (5 minutes)
1. Heat **2 Tbsp oil** in a pot over medium.
2. Add onion and cook 2–3 minutes to soften.
3. Add garlic + ginger; cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant.
4. Add lemongrass + turmeric; stir 15 seconds (bloom).

### Step 2 — Simmer broth + fish (12–15 minutes)
1. Add **1.2 L** water/stock and bring to a simmer.
2. Add fish chunks and simmer gently until just cooked:
   - Usually **6–10 minutes** depending on thickness.
3. Remove fish carefully to a plate and cool slightly.
4. Flake fish into bite-size pieces; discard any bones/skin.

> Keep the broth at a gentle simmer, not a hard boil (fish stays tender).

### Step 3 — Thicken broth (3–6 minutes)
Choose one method:

**A) Toasted rice powder**
1. Whisk toasted rice powder into the simmering broth gradually.
2. Simmer 3–5 minutes to remove any raw taste.

**B) Chickpea slurry**
1. Whisk chickpea flour + 120 ml water until smooth.
2. Pour into simmering broth while whisking.
3. Simmer 2–3 minutes until lightly thickened.

### Step 4 — Season + add fish back (3 minutes)
1. Add fish sauce and sugar.
2. Taste and adjust salt/pepper.
3. Add flaked fish back into the pot.
4. Simmer gently 2 minutes (do not overcook fish).

---

## Noodles (Do close to serving)
1. Cook rice vermicelli per package (often a quick soak + brief boil).
2. Drain very well (watery noodles dilute the bowl).
3. Portion into 4 bowls.

---

## Assemble Bowls (RiceMap24 Style)
Per bowl:
1. Noodles
2. Ladle hot Mohinga broth + fish over noodles (350–450 ml)
3. Top with:
   - 1/2 egg
   - fried shallots
   - scallions + cilantro
4. Serve lime wedge + chili oil on the side.

---

## Quality Checks
- **Broth:** aromatic, lightly thick, golden, not watery.
- **Fish:** tender flakes, not dry.
- **Toppings:** crunchy element + lime brightness.

---

## RiceMap24 Portioning (Per bowl targets)
- Noodles cooked: **250–300 g**
- Broth + fish: **350–450 ml**
- Fish: **120–160 g**
- Toppings: small but colorful (sells the photo)

---

## Scaling (10 bowls)
Multiply by **2.5×**:
- Fish: 1.5–1.75 kg
- Liquid: 3 L
- Thickener: 7–8 Tbsp
- Noodles: 750–900 g
- Eggs: 10

**Ops tip:** Make broth in a large pot; cook noodles separately and pack separately.

---

## Packaging (To-Go) — RiceMap24 Recommended
Best “kit” format:
- Container A: broth + fish (hot, sealed)
- Container B: noodles (separate)
- Cup C: toppings (shallots + herbs)
- Cup D: chili oil (optional)
- Lime wedge in small bag/cup

Label: “Pour broth over noodles. Add toppings. Squeeze lime.”

---

## Storage & Reheat
- Broth holds 2–3 days refrigerated.
- Reheat gently (avoid hard boil).
- Noodles best day-of.

---

## Allergens
- Fish (fish + fish sauce)
- Gluten: none (verify fish sauce brand)
- Egg (topping)
